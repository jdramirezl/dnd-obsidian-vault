# 15. Forge of Spells

> **Part**: 4 — Wave Echo Cave | **Page**: 48

## Texto para Leer en Voz Alta 🎭

> Este gran taller fue gravemente dañado por la antigua batalla de hechizos que asoló la mina. Mesas de trabajo que ocupan dos esquinas de la sala están chamuscadas, y el yeso ha sido arrancado de las paredes de mampostería. En el centro de la sala, un pedestal de piedra sostiene un pequeño brasero en el que una inquietante llama verde danza y crepita. El brasero y su pedestal parecen no haber sido tocados por las fuerzas que destruyeron esta zona.

> Detrás del brasero de llama verde flota una criatura esférica de aproximadamente un metro de diámetro. Cuatro tallos oculares sobresalen de su masa central, dos a cada lado. En el centro del cuerpo hay un gran ojo que os mira fijamente.

> [!tip] Use the Brazier Before the Boss Fight
> The Brazier of Green Flame grants **temporary +1** to any nonmagical weapon or armor (1d12 hours). Encourage the party to enchant their gear here before heading to Nezznar in area 19. This is the adventure's primary objective — make it feel momentous.

## DM Notes

**This is the primary objective of the entire adventure** — the legendary Forge of Spells where human mages of old Phandalin enchanted dwarven weapons and gnome gadgets. Nezznar has been searching for this room but hasn't reached it yet.

### The Spectator

A **[[Beholders#Spectator|spectator]]** guards this room — summoned by one of the human wizards to protect the magic items created here. When the mine was sacked, the orcs disturbed the delicate magic, **unhinging the spectator's grip on reality**. It has become deranged and believes the mine is still in use, ignoring all evidence to the contrary.

**Behavior:**
- The spectator is not immediately hostile — it greets the party
- It believes the mine is operational and sees nothing strange about undead roaming around
- **If the party attempts to remove anything from this area**, the spectator attacks
> [!tip] Spectator Bypass — Three Ways to Avoid Combat
> The spectator can be dismissed without fighting: (1) DC 15 Deception — trick it into thinking it's released from duty, (2) blind it by any means — it returns to its home plane, (3) Mormesk in area 14 wants it killed and will trade treasure for the favor. Combat is Hard difficulty and unnecessary.

- [[14-Wizards-Quarters|Mormesk]] wants to drive off or kill the spectator but has failed — the creature easily handles [[Zombies#Zombie|zombie]] and [[Ghouls#Ghoul|ghoul]] assaults

**Ways to bypass combat:**
1. **Deception (DC 15 Charisma):** Trick the spectator into thinking party members are wizards or miners sent to terminate its employment. If successful, it believes it's released and **disappears to its home plane**.
2. **Blind it:** If the spectator is blinded by any means, it disappears back to its home plane, convinced it can no longer perform its duty.
3. **Brute force:** Fight it directly (Hard encounter).

### The Brazier of Green Flame 🔥

**DC 15 Intelligence (Arcana)** to identify the brazier as the source of the magic suffusing the surrounding caverns.

- The magic has **waned over the centuries** — it can no longer permanently enchant items
- Any **nonmagical weapon or armor** bathed in the green flame for at least **1 minute** becomes a **[[Magic-Items#+1 Weapon|+1 weapon]] or [[Magic-Items#+1 Armor|+1 armor]]** for **1d12 hours**
- The brazier **cannot be removed** from the Forge of Spells
- This is an incredible resource for the party before the boss fight — encourage them to use it!

### Northern Room

A separate workspace where items being prepared for enchantment were polished, lacquered, and finished. Almost completely destroyed. North door is scorched — **DC 15 Strength to force open**. Western double doors are damaged but stand slightly ajar.

## Monsters

- [[Beholders#Spectator|Spectator]] ×1 — deranged guardian
  - HP: 39 (6d8 + 12)
  - AC: 14
  - Eye Rays (2/turn, random): Confusion, Paralyzing, Fear, Wounding
  - Spell Reflection: If targeted by magic missile, line spell, or spell requiring attack roll, roll d6 — on 1–5, unaffected; on 6, reflected back at caster
  - **Can be bypassed without combat** (see above)

## Treasure

On the worktable in the southeast corner — the last items the spectator was charged to protect:

- ****Lightbringer**** — +1 mace, made for a cleric of Lathander
  - Head shaped like a sunburst, solid brass
  - Glows as bright as a torch on command
  - While glowing: deals an extra **1d6 radiant damage to undead**
  - *Perfect for the undead-heavy encounters in this dungeon*

- ****Dragonguard**** — +1 breastplate, gold dragon motif
  - Created for a human hero of Neverwinter named Tergon
  - Grants **advantage on saving throws** against breath weapons of dragon-type creatures
  - *Foreshadows future dragon encounters*

- **Brazier of Green Flame** — temporary +1 enchantment (1d12 hours) for any nonmagical weapon or armor
