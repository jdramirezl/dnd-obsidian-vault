# 14. Wizards' Quarters

> **Part**: 4 — Wave Echo Cave | **Page**: 47

## Texto para Leer en Voz Alta 🎭

> Polvo, ceniza, paredes ennegrecidas por el fuego y montones de escombros bajo el techo combado muestran que esta sala fue devastada por una explosión destructiva. El mobiliario — mesas, sillas, estanterías, camas — está carbonizado o astillado, pero por lo demás bien conservado. Un cofre de hierro chamuscado se encuentra cerca del pie de una de las camas.

> [!tip] Mormesk Can Be Negotiated With — Don't Fight Unnecessarily
> The wraith is evil but willing to bargain. Offer a magic item or scroll (DC 10 Persuasion), and he'll trade his coins/gems + let the party read his books (which contain a hidden adventure map). He wants the spectator in area 15 killed. His Life Drain permanently reduces max HP — avoid this fight if possible.

## DM Notes

The doors are cracked with partially melted iron hinges — **DC 15 Strength to wrench open**.

This building served as a guesthouse for visiting wizards working in the [[15-Forge-of-Spells|Forge of Spells]]. The furnishings are all human-proportioned.

**Mormesk the [[Wraith]]:** The restless spirit of the last wizard to die here. Not immediately visible — he **rises up out of the floor** when a living creature enters. Mormesk was a powerful mage transformed by centuries of anger into a hate-filled apparition. He **leads the undead** that haunt Wave Echo Cave.

### Roleplaying Mormesk

When Mormesk rises, he says:
> *"Your presence is offensive to me, your life forfeit. My treasures are mine alone, not yours to plunder!"*

If the characters make no attempt to reason with him, **he attacks**.

**Negotiation (if they haven't harmed him or taken his property):**
- Mormesk is irrevocably evil but can be bargained with
- He values: magic items (especially scrolls), spellbooks, arcane knowledge
- **DC 10 Charisma (Persuasion)** to convince him a gift is valuable
- He will **never** relinquish the wooden pipe in the chest
- **Deal:** He'll part with coins and gems if the party agrees to **kill the [[Beholders#Spectator|spectator]]** in [[15-Forge-of-Spells|area 15]] (he merely points toward it — doesn't explain what a spectator is)
- Once he receives a gift, he allows the party to peruse his books and keep the secret map

## Monsters

- [[Wraith]] ×1 — Mormesk
  - HP: 67 (9d8 + 27)
  - Resistance to acid, cold, fire, lightning, thunder; nonmagical bludgeoning/piercing/slashing
  - Immune to necrotic, poison; charmed, exhaustion, grappled, paralyzed, petrified, poisoned, prone, restrained
  - Life Drain: +6 to hit, 4d8+3 necrotic, target's max HP reduced by damage taken (dies if reduced to 0)
  - **Negotiable** — this encounter can be resolved without combat

## Treasure

Scorched iron chest (unlocked):
- 1,100 cp, 160 sp, 50 ep
- 3 diamonds (100 gp each)
- Wooden pipe with platinum filigree (150 gp) — **Mormesk will not part with this**

**Bookshelves:** Magically preserved tomes, mostly histories. One contains a **hidden map** sewn into its cover:
- DC 12 Intelligence (Investigation) to find
- Shows the location of a dungeon — **adventure hook for the DM's next campaign**
