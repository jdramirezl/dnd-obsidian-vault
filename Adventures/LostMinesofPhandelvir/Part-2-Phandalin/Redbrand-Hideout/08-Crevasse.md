# 8. Crevasse

> **Part**: 2 — Phandalin | **Location**: Redbrand Hideout | **Page**: 23–24

## Texto para Leer en Voz Alta 🎭

> Una brisa fría llena esta gran caverna natural, trayendo consigo el tenue olor de carne en descomposición. Una grieta divide la caverna y está flanqueada por dos columnas de piedra rugosa que sostienen el techo de seis metros de altura. Dos puentes de madera arqueados cruzan el abismo.

## DM Notes

Characters can arrive here by three routes: the tunnel from area 1, the storeroom at area 7, or the rough-hewn passage to the south (~100 feet to the forest south of Tresendar Manor). The southern passage is perfect for smuggling people and goods in and out of Phandalin unseen.

### The Nothic

> [!tip] DM Tip
> The nothic prefers to negotiate and will betray the Redbrands for food. It knows everything the Redbrands know. Talking earns the same XP (450) as fighting — and it's much safer.

The guardian of this cave is a [[Nothic]] — an insane subterranean monster that hungers for flesh. It was lured here by a faint magical effect from the crevasse. **Iarno** struck a bargain: guard the stronghold in exchange for treasure and fresh meat. The nothic is untrustworthy.

- **Lurks** near the west ends of the two bridges
- **Hides** behind a stone column if it notices intruders, using **Weird Insight** to discern their secrets
- **Communicates** via telepathy — speak in whispers, mad cackles, gibberish. It fills characters' heads with foul murmurings and demands for food
- **Prefers to negotiate** — will betray the Redbrands for the right incentive (promise of food)
- **Knows everything** the Redbrands know (see "What the Redbrands Know" in [[00-Overview]])

### Bridges

- Made of wooden planks, no rails
- **South bridge is rigged to collapse** when 50+ lbs moves across it
- **DC 15 Intelligence (Investigation)** to notice faulty construction (must be next to the bridge)
- Any creature can use an action to dislodge one end of either bridge

### Crevasse

- 5–10 feet wide, **20 feet deep**
- Rough walls — easily climbed without a check
- Fall: **2d6 bludgeoning**, land **[[Prone|prone]]** in rubble (difficult terrain)
- Bottom is unnaturally cold; faint **necromantic aura** ([[Detect Magic|detect magic]]). Organic matter decomposes at half the normal rate
- **Thel Dendrar's** half-eaten body lies at the bottom among broken, well-gnawed bones — murdered by the Redbrands, left for the nothic

### Nothic's Hoard

Hidden in a cubbyhole at the bottom of the crevasse, under the north bridge. Can't be seen from the edge — obvious to anyone who descends.

## Monsters

- [[Nothic]] ×1 (HP: 45)
  - **AC:** 15 (natural armor)
  - **Attacks:** Rotting Gaze (ranged, DC 12 Con save or 3d6 necrotic), Claws (+4, 2d6+1 slashing)
  - **Weird Insight:** Contested check — Nothic's Insight vs. target's Deception. Success = learns one fact/secret about the target
  - **Tactics:** Hide, observe, negotiate. Fights only if cornered or attacked. Can be bribed with food

**XP:** 450 (divided equally) — awarded for defeating OR negotiating a truce

## Treasure

- 160 sp, 120 gp
- 5 **Malachite** gems (15 gp each)
- [[Magic-Items#Potion of Healing\|Potion of Healing]] ×2
- **Scroll of Augury** ×1
- **[[Magic-Items#+1 Weapon|Talon (+1 longsword)]]** — a [[Magic-Items#+1 Weapon|+1 longsword]] in a silver-chased scabbard. Hilt shaped like a bird of prey with outspread wings. Inscribed with the name "Talon." Belonged to Sir Aldith Tresendar, "the Black Hawk." DC 15 Intelligence (History) to recognize.
