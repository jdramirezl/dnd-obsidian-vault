# 7. Dragon's Tower 🐉

> **Part**: 3 | **Location**: Ruins of Thundertree | **Page**: 32–33

## Texto para Leer en Voz Alta 🎭

> En la cima de la colina se alza una torre redonda con una cabaña adjunta. Ambas están en buenas condiciones, aunque la mitad del techo de la torre ha desaparecido. Una puerta conduce a la cabaña, y varias ventanas con aspilleras son visibles en la torre. No pueden evitar notar una quietud inquietante en el área y un extraño olor acre en el aire.
>
> Los cadáveres de dos arañas gigantes horribles yacen desparramados cerca del borde del sendero, aparentemente arrastrados hasta allí. Sus cuerpos hinchados están arrugados y ampollados, y parecen haber sido destrozados por un animal grande.

> [!warning] ☠️ TPK ALERT — Venomfang (Young Green Dragon, CR 8)
> This is the **most dangerous optional fight in the entire adventure**. Poison Breath (12d6, DC 16 CON) can **instantly kill** a level 3 character via massive damage. The open roof lets the dragon fly out of melee range. **Do not run this as a straight fight** — the party should negotiate, use clever tactics, or aim to drive Venomfang off at half HP (68 damage). The dragon flees at half health and does NOT fight to the death.

## DM Notes

### ⚠️ VENOMFANG — Young Green Dragon

**This is the most dangerous optional encounter in the entire adventure.** A [[Dragon-Green#Young Green Dragon\|young green dragon]] is CR 8 — potentially deadly for a level 3 party. The dragon's Poison Breath alone (12d6, DC 16 CON) can instantly down or kill multiple characters.

### Background
Venomfang recently claimed this tower, having passed over Thundertree while searching Neverwinter Wood for a suitable lair. The [[Appendix-A-Miscellaneous-Creatures#Giant Spider\|giant spider]] corpses outside are the former residents — killed by the dragon after it tore through the roof.

### Tower Layout
- **Single room** with a **40-foot-high ceiling**
- A **5-foot-wide staircase** spirals up the interior to the now-opened rooftop
- Heavy wooden beams and stair supports crisscross the interior
- The open roof gives the dragon **easy access** to fly in and out

### Cottage
- Contains dusty furniture draped in webbing — nothing of value
- If the party makes **loud noise** in the cottage, the dragon hears and **steels itself for a fight** (no surprise possible)

---

## Dragon Tactics 🎯

### Opening Move
- Venomfang does **not** want to give up this promising lair
- If alerted by noise in the cottage, the dragon is ready and cannot be surprised
- Otherwise, the dragon may be found greedily admiring its loot

### Combat Behavior
1. **Poison Breath** (Recharge 5–6): 30-ft cone, 12d6 poison damage, DC 16 CON save for half — Venomfang opens with this if possible
2. **Multiattack**: Bite (2d10+4) + 2 Claws (2d6+4) — devastating against level 3 characters
3. **Flight**: The open roof means Venomfang can fly up, breathe down, and stay out of melee range
4. **Staircase**: The narrow spiral staircase limits how many characters can engage at once

### Retreat Condition
> **At half hit points (68 HP)**, Venomfang climbs to the top of the tower and **flies off to fight another day**.

The dragon does NOT fight to the death. This is critical — it means the party can "win" by dealing 68 damage.

---

> [!tip] Driving Off vs. Slaying
> The **expected outcome** is driving Venomfang off (2,000 XP) — not killing it. Remind yourself: the dragon retreats at 68 HP. The *potion of flying* from Favric (area 13) and the *scroll of lightning bolt* from the treasure here are potential game-changers if the party gets them first.

## Negotiation Options 🤝

Venomfang is a green dragon — **the most cunning and manipulative of chromatic dragons**. It may:

- **Parley** if approached diplomatically — green dragons love to talk, deceive, and manipulate
- **Pretend to negotiate** while positioning for a breath weapon attack
- **Demand tribute** — the [[Appendix-B-NPCs#Cultist\|cultists]] in area 13 are already trying to broker an alliance
- **Offer false promises** — agree to leave, then ambush the party later

### Favric's Betrayal
If the party allies with the cultists from [[13-Dragon-Cultists|area 13]], Favric plans to **offer the characters to the dragon as tribute**. If a fight breaks out, the cultists side with the dragon.

### Driving Off vs. Slaying
- **Driving off** (reduce to half HP): 2,000 XP — the expected outcome
- **Slaying** (reduce to 0 HP): 3,900 XP — unlikely at level 3 but possible with clever tactics
- Driving off Venomfang fulfills Reidoth's condition for guiding the party to Wave Echo Cave

- **Condition Immunities**: [[Poisoned|Poisoned]]
- **Senses**: Blindsight 30 ft, Darkvision 120 ft, Passive Perception 17
- **Multiattack**: 1 Bite + 2 Claws per turn
  - **Bite**: +7, reach 10 ft, 15 (2d10+4) piercing + 7 (2d6) poison
  - **Claw**: +7, reach 5 ft, 11 (2d6+4) slashing

### ⚡ Danger Assessment
At level 3, a character with 25 HP hit by full Poison Breath damage (42) is **instantly killed** (massive damage exceeds HP maximum). Even on a successful save, 21 damage drops most characters. **This fight can TPK easily.**

### ⭐ **Hew** — +1 Battleaxe (Magic Item)

Beneath the coins lies a rusty old battleaxe of dwarven manufacture. Runes in Dwarvish on the axe head read "Hew." The rust is misleading.

- **+1 battleaxe**
- **Special**: Deals **maximum damage** when the wielder hits a plant creature or an object made of wood
- **Lore**: Created by a dwarf smith who feuded with the dryads of a forest where he cut firewood
- **Quirk**: Whoever carries the axe feels **uneasy** whenever traveling through a forest
- 💡 **Extremely useful** against the [[Blights#Twig Blight\|twig blights]] throughout Thundertree (auto-kills on hit: max damage of 1d8+1+STR vs 4 HP blights)

**Total treasure value**: ~470 gp + 2 spell scrolls + magic weapon
