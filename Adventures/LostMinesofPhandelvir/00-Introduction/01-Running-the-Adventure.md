# Running the Adventure

> **Source**: Lost Mines of Phandelver p.2–3

## Content

Lost Mine of Phandelver is an adventure for **four to five characters of 1st level**. During the course of the adventure, the characters will advance to **5th level**. The adventure is set a short distance from the city of [[Neverwinter]] in the Sword Coast region of the Forgotten Realms.

### The Dungeon Master

- **The DM is a referee.** When it's not clear what ought to happen next, the DM decides how to apply the rules and keep the story going.
- **The DM is a narrator.** The DM sets the pace of the story and presents the various challenges and encounters the players must overcome.
- **The DM plays monsters.** The DM plays the monsters and villains the adventurers battle against, choosing their actions and rolling dice for their attacks. The DM also plays all other NPCs.

### Rules to Game By

- **When in doubt, make it up!** Better to keep the game moving than to get bogged down in the rules.
- **It's not a competition.** The DM isn't competing against the player characters.
- **It's a shared story.** Let the players contribute to the outcome through the actions of their characters.
- **Be consistent.** If you decide a rule works a certain way, keep it that way.
- **Make sure everyone is involved.** Ensure every character has a chance to shine.
- **Be fair.** Treat the rules and the players in a fair and impartial manner.
- **Pay attention.** If the fun is waning, try a break or liven things up.

### Improvising Ability Checks

When adventurers try something the adventure doesn't anticipate:

| Difficulty | DC |
|---|---|
| Easy | 10 |
| Moderate | 15 |
| Hard | 20 |

If anyone should have an easy time doing it, don't ask for a check. If there's no way anyone could accomplish it, just say it doesn't work.

### Glossary

- **Characters** — The adventurers run by the players (the protagonists).
- **Nonplayer Characters (NPCs)** — Characters run by the DM.
- **Boxed Text** — Read-aloud text offset in boxes, used to describe rooms or present scripted dialogue.
- **Stat Block** — Game statistics for any monster or NPC likely to be involved in combat (see **Appendix B**).
- **Tenday** — A week in the Forgotten Realms is ten days long. Each month = three tendays (30 days).

### Roleplaying and Inspiration

Each pre-generated character has two personality traits, an ideal, a bond, and a flaw. When a player roleplays a negative personality trait or gives in to a drawback from a bond or flaw, award **one inspiration**. The player can spend it to gain **advantage** on one [[04-Ability-Checks|ability check]], attack roll, or [[11-Saving-Throws|saving throw]]. A character can have only one inspiration at a time.
