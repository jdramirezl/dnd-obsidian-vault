# Appendices - The 2016 D&D Dungeon Master Survey

## Original Text

This book makes frequent references to the 2016 Dungeons & Dragons Dungeon Master survey, conducted at the Sly Flourish website between October 28 and November 28, 2016. This appendix contains a summary of the results of that survey. You can find the full results, along with the raw data, at http://slyflourish.com/2016_dm_survey_results.html.

Survey respondents came from multiple online D&D communities, including the Dungeons & Dragons Google Plus community, the Reddit D&D Next community, the ENWorld forum for D&D, the Facebook D&D community, and on Twitter. There were a total of 6,600 responses. The results below have all been rounded to the nearest percent.

Here are the summarized results relating to games and game preparation.

### Frequency of Games

- More than twice weekly: 2%
- Twice a week: 6%
- Weekly: 43%
- Twice monthly: 26%
- Monthly: 13%
- Less than Monthly: 10%

### Length of Games

- About an hour: 1%
- About two hours: 5%
- About three hours: 28%
- About four hours: 44%
- About six hours: 17%
- About eight hours: 4%

- About 30 minutes: 10%

- About two hours: 24%

## Condensed Version

> **Survey:** 6,600 respondents from Google+, Reddit, ENWorld, Facebook, and Twitter (Oct–Nov 2016). Full data at [slyflourish.com](http://slyflourish.com/2016_dm_survey_results.html).

### Game Basics at a Glance

| Category | Top Result | Runner-Up |
|---|---|---|
| **Frequency** | Weekly (43%) | Twice monthly (26%) |
| **Session Length** | ~4 hours (44%) | ~3 hours (28%) |
| **Total Prep Time** | ~2 hours (24%) | ~1 hour (23%) |
| **Location** | Home (55%) | Roll20 (16%) |
| **Campaign World** | Personal setting (55%) | Forgotten Realms (38%) |
| **Adventure Source** | Personal adventures (64%) | Published (36%) |
| **Combat Style** | 5-foot grid (63%) | Abstract maps (19%) |

### Prep Time by Activity (Cumulative Percentages)

| Activity | None | ≤15 min | 30 min | 1 hr | 2+ hrs |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Story & Adventures** | 6% | 25% | 26% | 26% | 18% |
| **Campaign & World Building** | 8% | 23% | 24% | 23% | 22% |
| **Combat Encounters** | 9% | 34% | 26% | 21% | 8% |
| **NPC Development** | 10% | 48% | 24% | 13% | 5% |
| **Exploration & Roleplay** | 16% | 42% | 23% | 13% | 6% |
| **Treasure & Magic Items** | 23% | 56% | 13% | 5% | 2% |
| **Props & Handouts** | 45% | 35% | 11% | 6% | 4% |

### Key Takeaways for DMs

- **The typical D&D game is weekly, ~4 hours, with ~2 hours of prep.** That's a 2:1 play-to-prep ratio — a useful benchmark.
- **Most DMs play at home (55%) or online via Roll20 (16%).** Online play was already significant in 2016 and has only grown since.
- **Over half of DMs use personal settings (55%) and personal adventures (64%).** Homebrew dominates, though Forgotten Realms is the clear #1 published setting.
- **Grid combat is king (63%)**, but a combined 37% use abstract maps or theater of the mind — a sizable minority.
- **Story/adventures and world building eat the most prep time.** These are the activities where lazy DM techniques save the most effort.
- **Props and handouts are the most skipped activity** — 45% of DMs spend zero time on them. Don't feel guilty about skipping them.
- **Treasure and magic items are quick for most DMs** — 56% spend 15 minutes or less. Simple random tables can handle this.
- **NPC development is fast** — nearly half (48%) spend 15 minutes or less. This supports the book's advice to keep NPC prep lightweight and improvise at the table.
