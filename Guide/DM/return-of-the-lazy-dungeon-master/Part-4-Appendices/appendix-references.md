## Condensed Version

### RPG Systems Referenced

| System | Author(s) | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| *Apocalypse World* | D. Vincent Baker | Pioneered "fronts" and fiction-first design; directly influenced Lazy DM prep |
| *Dungeon World* | LaTorra & Koebel | Brought Apocalypse World's fronts to fantasy RPGs; Mike Mearls credits it for improving his D&D |
| *Fate: Core System* | Balsera et al. | Aspect-based narrative mechanics; useful for improvisation techniques |
| *13th Age* | Heinsoo & Tweet | Icon relationships and "one unique thing" — tools for player-driven story |
| *Numenera* | Monte Cook | Streamlined GM prep; *Weird Discoveries* pioneered instant-adventure format |
| *Pathfinder* | Bulmahn et al. | Major D&D alternative; context for the broader RPG landscape |
| *Starfinder* | Sutter et al. | Sci-fi Pathfinder variant |
| *D&D 5e DMG* | Crawford et al. | The official guide this book builds upon and supplements |

### GM Guides & Theory

| Title | Author | What It Offers |
|---|---|---|
| *Robin's Laws of Good Gamemastering* | Robin Laws | Classic short guide to running games for different player types |
| *Hamlet's Hit Points* | Robin Laws | Beat analysis applied to RPG pacing — tracks tension rises and falls |
| *Never Unprepared* | Phil Vecchione | Systematic approach to session prep (contrast with Lazy DM's minimalist approach) |
| *Unframed* | Martin Ralya (ed.) | Essay collection on improvisation from multiple experienced GMs |
| *Kobold Guide to Plots and Campaigns* | Michele Carter (ed.) | Practical advice on campaign-level storytelling |
| *Kobold Guide to Gamemastering* | Sean K. Reynolds (ed.) | Broad GM advice anthology |

| Title | Author | Relevance |
|---|---|---|

| *Zen in the Art of Writing* | Ray Bradbury | Joy of creative work; staying inspired |
