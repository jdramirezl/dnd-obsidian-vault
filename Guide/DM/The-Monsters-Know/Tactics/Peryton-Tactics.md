# Peryton Tactics

## Tactical Summary

Enormous bird-of-prey body, carnivorous stag head. Roosts in mountains/rocky hills near settlements. Favors humanoids (especially humans and elves). Single-minded and relentless when attacking humanoids. High STR, Dive Attack + Flyby combo. Resistance to nonmagical physical damage.

## Combat Behavior

- **Preferred pattern:** Half-move (30 ft dive) → Multiattack (Talons + Gore, both get Dive Attack bonus) → half-move (30 ft fly away). Repeat
- **Unconscious target + no magical weapon threat:** Keep attacking until dead, tear out heart, fly off
- **Unconscious Small target:** May pick up in talons and carry to nest
- **Magical weapon wielder appears:** Refocus attacks on that wielder. Fly up between attacks
- **Mated pairs:** Both ferociously focus on magical weapon wielder from two opposite directions (use Flanking rules)
- **Group behavior:** Each peryton picks a separate target — each wants its own meal. No cooperation unless focusing on a magical weapon threat

## Retreat Conditions

- Seriously wounded (≤13 hp) AND at least 10 hp of damage from magical weapons → driven off
- Even then: reconnoiters victim and returns when healed
- A peryton that gets a humanoid heart (or carries off a Small humanoid) departs satisfied. Mate leaves too

## Notes

- Keen senses, high Perception — hard to hide from
- No darkvision → daytime hunters. Good random road encounter
- Appear alone, in mated pairs, or rarely in groups of 4 (two pairs)

> [!tip] DM Tip
> Nonmagical weapons barely scratch them. The dive-bomb-and-flyby loop is extremely frustrating for melee-only parties without magic weapons. Great for teaching players to prepare ranged/magical options.
