# Inspired Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/inspired-tactics/) | **Date**: February 9, 2022

---

## Tactical Summary

- Humanoids possessed by quori (alien psionic minds). High DEX, very high INT and CHA → casters that avoid mano-a-mano fights.
- Expertise in Deception and Persuasion, proficiency in Insight. Can't be charmed or frightened. Equally puissant in social and combat arenas — can combine both, keeping a running patter throughout a fight.
- Psionic casting (no components) → effectively free Subtle Spell.

## Spell Analysis

| Spell | Verdict |
|---|---|
| *Hex* (bonus action, 90 ft) | Only useful if planning to stab with Crysteel Dagger in melee. Requires concentration. Cast from within 30 ft to close distance. |
| *Hold person* (1/day, 60 ft) | Powerful if it lands — advantage + auto-crit in melee. But DC 13 WIS save is anemic. Don't waste on clerics, druids, paladins, warlocks, wizards, gnomes, or anyone with positive WIS save mod. Only cast if target is displaying obvious daftness. |
| *Charm person* (1/day, 30 ft) | Boosts Deception/Persuasion. Best cast *before* combat (advantage on save during combat). Same low-DC problem. |
| *Dissonant whispers* (1/day, 60 ft) | Best damage of the WIS-save spells, decent rider, deals some damage even on success. Best use: **deterring pursuit**. |
| *Vicious mockery* (60 ft) | Terrible. 1d4 psychic, can't be enhanced by *hex*. Only use: against an armored nincompoop with a very big weapon — and even then, *hold person* as a Hail Mary + run is better. |

## Hex vs Hold Person

- *Hex*: Bonus action, immediate attack follow-up. Expected ~37 damage over rounds 1–2 with Multiattack.
- *Hold person*: Full action, no attack round 1. But if target fails save: expected ~42 damage round 2 (auto-crits), and another ~42 in round 3.
- **Judgment call**: If confident target will fail and no one will interrupt, *hold person* (especially for multi-round fights). Otherwise *hex* is faster and safer. Either way, cast from within 30 ft.

## Retreat

- Not suited for knock-down fights. Evacuates when **moderately wounded (≤28 hp)** — may not even wait that long depending on obvious threat level.
- WIS isn't quite high enough to recognize non-obvious threats.

## As Nobles/Rulers

- Rarely fight alone. Surrounded by elite guards, spellcasting advisers, early-warning systems. May play support, using 1/day abilities to set up allies' attacks (certainty of success matters less in this role).

## Quori Vessel Variants

- Each has one additional psionic "spell," but none alter tactics significantly — all still call for WIS saves at the same anemic DC.
