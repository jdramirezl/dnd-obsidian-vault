# Derro Tactics

## Tactical Summary

Derro are small Underdark humanoids — high DEX, above-average CON, average STR. Skirmishers with Stealth proficiency, Sunlight Sensitivity, and excellent darkvision. Paranoid and vicious, they prey on the weak and grovel before the strong. Low WIS means an underdeveloped survival instinct — they fight nearly to the death.

## Combat Behavior

### Basic Loop
1. Launch first crossbow volley from hiding (unseen attacker advantage)
2. Prefer **light crossbow** over spear (86% more damage on average)
3. Every derro carries both weapons — switch to spear only when rushed in melee
4. Neighbors of a rushed derro also switch to spears

### Hook-and-Trip (Advanced — 6+ Derro Only)
- First derro in each melee group attacks to knock prone (hook and trip)
- Remaining derro attack with advantage
- Only worthwhile with **6+ derro** attacking a single target against unarmored/lightly armored foes
- Crossbow derro fire *before* spear derro act (shoot first, then trip)
- At smaller numbers, just stab — the average damage difference is <1 point

### Retreat Behavior
| Condition | Response |
|---|---|
| Individual at 1–2 hp | Flees, baiting opportunity attacks — allies use this to Disengage and switch back to crossbows |
| Combat drags past 3 rounds | **All** derro Dash away — strategic retreat |
| After retreat | Gather reinforcements, stalk, and ambush again ASAP |

## Derro Savant (Sorcerer Variant)

- Below-average STR → always attacks from range; other derro run interference
- High CHA for spellcasting

### Spell Priority
| Spell | Usage |
|---|---|
| **Lightning bolt** (3rd) | Save slots for this — only cast when 3+ enemies are lined up; use *invisibility* to reposition |
| **Chromatic orb** (2nd) | Ranged spell attack against single targets |
| **Invisibility** (2nd) | Reposition for optimal *lightning bolt* angle |
| **Burning hands** (1st) | Self-defense only — when sacked in melee |
| **Sleep** (1st) | Doesn't scale well; skip past level 4 |
| **Ray of frost** (cantrip) | Default ranged cantrip after slots are spent |
| **Spider climb** | Escape in high-verticality environments only |

> [!tip] DM Tips
> - Derro never venture aboveground during the day (Sunlight Sensitivity)
> - Intensely territorial — they defend turf viciously but rarely leave it
> - Use "Handling Mobs" rule (DMG p.250) for large derro encounters; advantage = +4 to hit
> - Retreats are strategic, not permanent — they *will* come back with friends
> - Low WIS means they're easy to surprise but hard to scare off
