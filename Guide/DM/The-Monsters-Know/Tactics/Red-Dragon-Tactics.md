# Red Dragon Tactics

> [!summary]
> Red dragons are the most powerful and arrogant of the chromatic dragons. Wrathful, proud, and utterly convinced of their superiority, they fight head-on and refuse to back down until grievously wounded. They climb to ambush and use devastating Fire Breath in wide cones.

---

## Universal Dragon Traits (All Colors)
- Fly at 2× land speed; blindsight + darkvision
- High STR + CON (brute profile); proficient in Perception + Stealth
- Saves: DEX, CON, WIS, CHA
- Breath weapon recharges on 5–6 (avg every 3 rounds)
- **Legendary Resistance (adult+):** use every failed save without conserving

## Red-Specific Traits
- **Climb speed** — ambush by dropping from overhangs, cave ceilings, branches
- **Highest CR** among chromatic dragons at every age
- **Personality:** Wrathful, arrogant, supremely confident. Young reds are "arrogant punks"

---

## By Age

### Wyrmling
- Stealth-first: climb above prey, drop down to ambush
- **Fire Breath** first (cone), then Bite on subsequent turns
- Won't engage 2+ melee opponents — Dodge + reposition if ganged up on
- **Flee at ≤40% hp**

### Young
- Gains **Multiattack:** Claw/Claw/Bite
- Willing to engage **2 melee opponents** (not 3)
- Same ambush and breath-first tactics
- Adolescent personality: arrogant punk, spoiling for a fight

### Adult (Legendary)
- **Lair actions, legendary actions, Frightful Presence**
- Breath weapon is always the preferred attack — repositions to maximize targets (eats opportunity attacks without caring)
- If breath unavailable: **Frightful Presence** + Claw/Claw/Bite

#### Lair Actions (Pick 1 per round, never same twice)
| Action | Type | Best Against |
|---|---|---|
| **Tremor** | Movement restrictor (knocks prone) | Melee opponents — prone = disadvantage on attacks |
| **Magma Eruption** | Direct damage | Anyone in range — general softener |
| **Volcanic Gas** | Debilitator (poison + incapacitate) | PCs with rich action economy (bonus actions, Extra Attack) — incapacitation shuts them down completely |

#### Legendary Actions (3/round)
| Action | Cost | Use When |
|---|---|---|
| **Detect** | 1 | Any stealthy movement within line of sight |
| **Tail Attack** | 1 | PC behind the dragon; max once per round if 3+ melee attackers threaten |
| **Wing Attack** | 2 | **3+ PCs within 10 ft** — knocks prone + bonus fly to reposition. Also escape trigger at ≤40% hp |

### Ancient
- Same tactics as adult with bigger numbers
- Among the six highest-CR creatures in 5e (alongside archdemons and Tiamat)

## Red Dragon Combat Personality

> [!important] Key Difference
> Red dragons, like whites, are **too proud to retreat tactically**. When moderately wounded (≤70% hp), they keep fighting out of sheer wrath and arrogance. They only flee when seriously wounded (≤40% hp).

| Dragon Color | At Moderate Wounds |
|---|---|
| White | Keeps fighting (dense, truculent) |
| **Red** | **Keeps fighting (wrathful, arrogant)** |
| Blue | Disengages, takes potshots from range |
| Black | Skedaddles |
| Green | Stops fighting, parleys |

---

## Quick Reference: Combat Flow
1. **Pre-combat:** Stealth → climb ambush from above (rocky overhang, cave ceiling)
2. **Round 1:** Fire Breath (cone — reposition to catch max targets, ignore opportunity attacks)
3. **Breath unavailable:** Frightful Presence + Claw/Claw/Bite
4. **Lair action (init 20):** Volcanic Gas to incapacitate key PCs, Tremor to knock melee prone, or Magma for damage
5. **Legendary actions:** Wing Attack if 3+ in melee → reposition; Tail Attack for rear threats; Detect for sneakers
6. **≤40% hp:** Wing Attack (knock prone for disadvantage on opportunity attacks) → fly away

> [!tip] DM Note
> Red dragon Fire Breath is a **cone** — it gets wider the farther it extends. Spread-out parties still get caught. An adult red's cone is massive; an ancient's even more so. PCs need to be *very* far apart to avoid multi-target hits.
