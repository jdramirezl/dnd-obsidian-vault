# Corpse Flower Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/corpse-flower-tactics/) | **Date**: July 30, 2020

---

## Tactical Summary

- **Ambulatory scavenger plant** that collects dead humanoids and belches out zombies. Slow (20 ft) but can climb.
- **Mostly brute with skirmisher touch**: Peaks in Con/Wis, high Str/Dex. Barely sentient (Int 7) — instinct-driven and inflexible.
- **Preferred action is Harvest the Dead**: Absorbing corpses makes it stronger. Turn priority: move → snatch corpse.
- **Blindsight only**: Can't assess threats visually. Reacts to danger only after demonstration.
- **Stench of Death**: Clings to its zombies too.

## Combat Behavior

**Reactive tentacles**: Lashes out at anything within 10-ft reach. Three tentacles distributed among targets. Favors targets NOT incapacitated by Stench.

**Finishes downed enemies**: Keeps attacking unconscious humanoids to kill them (dead = useful, unconscious = not). Harvests the body next turn, even forgoing attacks on living enemies.

**Climbing trick**: Moves to nearest tall object and climbs 10 ft up — out of melee weapon reach while tentacles still reach down. Can snatch corpses from the ground while elevated.

**Ranged attack response**: Beyond blindsight → moves away (confused). Within blindsight → moves toward (angry).

## Bonus Action Economy

- **Above 88 hp**: Produces a zombie whenever enemies sensed within 120 ft.
- **At or below 88 hp**: Digests a corpse to regenerate if 11+ damage taken since last turn. Otherwise produces zombie.
- **Yes, zombies fall from trees**: 1d6 falling damage. Comedy gold.

## Encounter Foreshadowing

- Always preceded by seemingly random lone zombies in the area.
- Several existing zombies should be present when PCs find the flower.
- Cannot re-harvest its own zombies (zombies are undead, not humanoids).

## Retreat Conditions

- **Seriously wounded (≤50 hp)**: Dashes toward fewest enemies. Too dumb to Disengage.

## DM Tips

> [!tip] Running This Monster
> A noxious nuisance PCs are called to eliminate, not a predator. Turn priority: harvest corpse > attack. The climbing trick is its best move — 10 ft up, out of melee reach, tentacles still reach down. Seed the area with wandering zombies for atmosphere.
