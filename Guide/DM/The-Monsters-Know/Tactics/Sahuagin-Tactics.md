# Sahuagin Tactics

## Tactical Summary

Fierce amphibious fish-men. Raid coastal settlements at night (120 ft darkvision). Limited Amphibiousness = 4 hours air breathing max. INT and WIS above average — disciplined, coordinated. Looking for an easy fight on land; a hard one isn't worth the effort.

## Land Combat

- Multiattack: 1 weapon (spear, two-handed) + 1 bite
- **Blood Frenzy**: advantage vs any creature already damaged → press wounded targets, never switch
- Fight in cohesive units — split to flank/encircle
- Retreat (Disengage) if all group members ≤15 hp and enemies aren't hurt worse

## Underwater Combat

- Same attacks + two advantages:
  1. **Shark allies** via Shark Telepathy — sharks fight with sahuagin-level intelligence
  2. **Target spellcasters** — recognize non-aquatic creatures using magic; focus whoever they see cast a spell
- More tenacious: hang in until majority **seriously wounded** (≤8 hp)
- Fight to death at baron/priestess command (defending locations)

## Sahuagin Baron

- Stronger grunt — one extra melee attack, proficient in important saves
- Participates directly in coordinated attacks on spellcasters
- Rarely in land raids (only all-out conquest)
- Retreats when **seriously wounded** (≤30 hp)

## Sahuagin Priestess

- Support caster, no weapon, claw/bite Multiattack. **De facto leader.**
- Round 1: *spiritual weapon* (bonus) + Dodge
- *Guiding bolt*: when allies focus a spellcaster
- *Hold person*: when no water breathing to disrupt; targets Extra Attack users (not paladins)
- *Hold person* 3rd-level: two equally dangerous Extra Attack targets
- *Mass healing word*: when all sahuagin ≤15 hp
- *Tongues*: if PC initiates parley
- Retreats at ≤23 hp (or ≤13 hp if guarding something important)

> [!tip] DM Tips
> - *Water breathing* is non-concentration — sahuagin may or may not know this
> - Underwater: sahuagin can move-attack-move exploiting disadvantage on OAs from wrong weapon types
> - Priestess understands holy symbols and that *hold person* is weaker on the devout
