# Feyr Tactics

## Tactical Summary

Large hovering nightmare parasite. Very high STR/DEX, high INT/WIS. Expert Stealth, proficient Perception. 120 ft darkvision, 50 ft fly, Invisibility at will. Native to Wildspace, found anywhere dark.

## Combat Behavior

- **Primary Strategy — Target Sleeping Victims:**
  1. Approach under Invisibility
  2. Assess sleeping targets' WIS saves (high INT/WIS = accurate read)
  3. If no target has WIS save ≤ −2: **leave undetected** — odds aren't good enough
  4. If viable target: cruise to 10 ft, unleash Nightmare Fuel

- **Nightmare Fuel (1/day, unconscious only):** Massive psychic damage + temp HP on failed WIS save (DC 13). One shot — leave regardless of outcome

- **Multiattack (Frightful Bite + Tentacle):** Only if a second sleeper nearby might wake and interfere. Crits on unconscious within 5 ft (~42 avg damage) can drop most level 4 PCs. Only viable with exactly 2 sleepers

- **Withdrawal:** Disengage if in melee reach of enemies who see it; Dash otherwise. 50 ft fly = easy escape

## Key Decision Points

- Most likely outcome: feyr scouts, judges odds poor, leaves undetected
- After Nightmare Fuel (success or failure): depart immediately
- If discovered before acting: flee

## Retreat Conditions

Hit-and-run predator. Doesn't stay in fights it can't easily win. Flees immediately if detected before striking.

> [!tip] DM Tip
> Run as a serial killer mystery, not a combat encounter. Alien/Nightmare on Elm Street aboard a spelljammer — crew dying in sleep, party must detect and trap it. Pairs excellently with night hags (exhaustion → disadvantage on saves → easier Nightmare Fuel).
