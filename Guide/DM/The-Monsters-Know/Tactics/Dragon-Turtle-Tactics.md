# Dragon Turtle Tactics

> [!summary]
> Dragon turtles are aquatic brute combatants that love to get close and unload their full arsenal. They conceal themselves underwater until ready to strike, lead with Steam Breath, and are nearly impossible to escape at sea. The ancient dragon turtle is a mythic creature on par with greatwyrms — you have to kill it twice.

---

## Dragon Turtle Traits
- **Gargantuan** at adult size; younger variants are one size larger than dragon equivalents
- **Swim speed** is primary movement; can go on land but slower
- **Steam Breath** (cone, recharge) — preferred attack
- **No Stealth proficiency** — conceals itself only by staying underwater
- **No Siege Monster trait** — can't easily sink ships, but may try anyway
- **Nomadic** — no permanent lair (adult has no legendary actions)
- Speak Draconic and Aquan only (not Common)

---

## By Age

### Wyrmling
- Ambush predator — attacks to eat, no concept of extortion yet
- Stays underwater until ready to strike
- **Steam Breath** against 2+ targets; if one drops, try to gobble it up
- If no one drops: Bite the most wounded target
- **Retreat at ≤70% hp** (63 hp) — submerge and swim away

### Young
- Developing territorial awareness — bossy, boastful, demands deference
- **Round 1:** Surface → Steam Breath against 3+ targets → dive again
- **Round 2:** If boat looks vulnerable, first Multiattack (Claw/Claw/Bite) targets the **hull**
  - If hull doesn't lose ≥50% hp: switch to attacking passengers
- Targets whoever took most damage from Steam Breath first (identifies vulnerability)
- Then switches to attacking whoever is hurting it most (dominance display)
- Only ducks underwater between attacks if taking damage from 2+ sources
- **Retreat at ≤40% hp** (71 hp) — more truculent than wyrmlings

### Adult (MM Default)
- Gains **Tail Attack** and 60 ft breath weapon range
- **Not legendary** — no legendary actions, no lair
- Same tactical pattern: submerge → Steam Breath → Multiattack
- Extortion is now a viable strategy — demands tribute from passing ships

### Ancient (Legendary + Mythic)
- **Legendary actions** (3/round):

| Action | Cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Attack** | 1 | Extra attack — always choose **Tail** (more damage, 15 ft reach, knocks prone) |
| **Aquatic Move** | 1 | Move without opportunity attacks (swimming only) |
| **Boiling Aura** | 3 | Damages all creatures in melee range at start of their turns. **Use ASAP after own turn** to maximize affected creatures |

#### Mythic Trait: Blessing of the Sea
- **Must be killed twice** — when reduced to 0 hp, regains hp and unlocks mythic actions
- **Armor of Storms** (mythic action): 40 temp hp + retaliatory lightning jolt on melee hits

#### Tail vs Claw
- Tail: more damage, 15 ft reach, knocks prone (DC 24)
- Claw: less damage, 10 ft reach, no rider
- **Always choose Tail** for legendary actions — prone enables advantage on follow-up attacks before target can stand

#### Optimal Multiattack: Tail/Claw/Claw
- Tail first (knock prone) → Claw/Claw with advantage
- Then up to 3 more Tail legendary attacks with advantage before target stands

## Naval Combat Tactics

### Opening Sequence
1. Approach underwater (invisible — 60 ft swim speed)
2. Leap 12 ft from water (running high jump)
3. **Steam Breath** everyone on deck (90 ft cone — include ALL opponents)
4. Land back in water with colossal splash, vanish beneath sea-foam
5. Begin crunching hull from below while waiting for breath recharge

### Why Ships Are Hard to Sink
- No Siege Monster trait — hull damage is slow (7–8 rounds for a carrack)
- But the **psychological effect** of crunching slams is the point
- Only switches to attacking passengers directly if they fight back with real moxie

### Ranged Counterplay Is Limited
- Underwater combat rules: ranged weapons **auto-miss at long range**
- Dragon turtle stays submerged until close enough to attack
- You don't see it coming until it's too late

## Retreat and Parley

> [!important] First-Round Damage Check
> If PCs deal **≥123 damage in round 1**, the ancient dragon turtle stops — it has badly underestimated its opponents. It may attempt parley, but negotiation is difficult:
> - Speaks only Draconic and Aquan (not Common)
> - Doesn't want much beyond not being killed
> - Can't let anyone pass without taking *something* — dominance demands it

> [!warning] Once Committed, Fights to the Death
> If parley fails and the ancient dragon turtle re-engages, it fights to the bitter end — like greatwyrms, no further retreat.

---

## Quick Reference: Combat Flow
1. **Approach:** Submerged, invisible, 60 ft swim speed
2. **Round 1:** Breach → Steam Breath (catch ALL targets in 90 ft cone) → submerge
3. **Breath recharging:** Crunching hull attacks from below (psychological warfare)
4. **Breath available:** Surface → Steam Breath again
5. **Melee:** Tail/Claw/Claw (knock prone → advantage) + Boiling Aura legendary action ASAP
6. **Mythic phase:** Armor of Storms for 40 temp hp + lightning retaliation on melee hits

> [!tip] DM Note
> Dragon turtles are the ultimate naval encounter. PCs can't see them coming, can't easily escape (60 ft swim), and can't effectively use ranged weapons underwater. The ancient dragon turtle's mythic trait means the party thinks they've won — then it gets back up. Plan your naval encounters around the assumption that the dragon turtle controls the engagement.
