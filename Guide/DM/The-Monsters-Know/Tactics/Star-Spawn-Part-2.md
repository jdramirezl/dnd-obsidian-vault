# Star Spawn Tactics, Part 2

> **Source:** *The Monsters Know What They're Doing* — Keith Ammann
> **Bestiary:** Mordenkainen's Tome of Foes
> **Creatures:** Star Spawn Seer, Star Spawn Larva Mage

---

## Tactical Summary

This article covers the two boss-tier star spawn — the **seer** and the **larva mage** — plus a correction on the hulk's Psychic Mirror. The seer is a hybrid support/psychic brute that manipulates positioning through Bend Space and Collapse Distance. The larva mage is a legendary psychic brute that wants to be in the thick of enemies, dominating minds and unleashing Plague of Worms.

## Combat Behavior

### Star Spawn Hulk — Psychic Mirror (Addendum)

- Psychic Mirror doesn't affect the hulk's own tactics but affects allies fighting alongside it
- For every *x* psychic damage the hulk would take, every creature within 10 ft takes *x* damage — it's an amplifier, not a mirror
- Pairs devastatingly with mind flayers using Mind Blast, but the hulk's INT 7 means it will likely be stunned (losing Reaping Arms)
- Two hulks within 10 ft do NOT stack Psychic Mirror infinitely — Crawford ruled same-feature effects don't stack; additional hulks only extend the radius

### Star Spawn Seer

- **Ability contour:** INT/WIS primary, CON tertiary — hybrid support spellcaster / psychic brute
- **Weaknesses:** No CON save proficiency; vulnerable to effects requiring CON saves
- **Out-of-Phase Movement:** Phases through creatures dealing damage — small incentive to pass through opponents offensively
- **Bend Space (Reaction):** The seer's signature move. Not just an escape hatch — used defensively AND offensively simultaneously:
  - Move into a cluster of enemies, bait an attack, then swap places with a hulk (which then uses Reaping Arms)
  - Swap with a mangler to deliver Flurry of Claws to a target the mangler couldn't reach
- **Collapse Distance (Recharge 6):** Best used against a target with 2+ allies within 10 ft. Teleport the target somewhere dangerous (under a mangler, over a chasm). Aim at low-WIS targets; star spawn grues' Aura of Madness (disadvantage on saves) makes this nearly irresistible
- **Direct attacks:** Uses Psychic Orb at range vs. front-liners; closes with Comet Staff (two-handed) vs. ranged/casters. Can lob Psychic Orb directly at a hulk surrounded by enemies — hulk rebroadcasts the damage and takes none itself

### Star Spawn Larva Mage (Legendary)

- **Ability contour:** CON extraordinary, INT/STR exceptional — "psychic brute"
- Immune to paralysis and restraint; must be stunned to stop it
- Wants to be within 20 ft of 4+ enemies
- **Three primary attacks:** Slam (melee, 10-ft reach, poisons), *eldritch blast* (3 targets, 120 ft, 3× more damage than Slam), and Plague of Worms (recharge 6)
- **Plague of Worms:** The opening play if enemies are near each other. Ideally used from surprise, dropped into the middle of the party. Massive AoE effects
- ***Dominate monster* (3/day):** Better mind controller than a mind flayer thanks to high DC and 3 uses. Use as "C'm'ere, you!" to round up scattered enemies for Plague of Worms
- **Feed on Weakness:** Gains 10 temp HP whenever a creature within 20 ft fails a save. Synergizes with Slam (poison save), hulks forcing CON/DEX saves, and grues imposing disadvantage
- **Legendary actions (3/round):** All cost 2, so only 1 per round. Slam only for self-defense + temp HP. Feed when enemies are restrained by Plague. *Eldritch blast* as fallback

## Key Decision Points

| Situation | Decision |
|---|---|
| Seer is seriously wounded (≤61 HP) | Dash away via Out-of-Phase Movement through walls/floors |
| Seer has a hulk ally | Bait attacks → Bend Space swap with hulk for Reaping Arms |
| Larva mage opens combat, enemies clustered | Plague of Worms immediately |
| Larva mage opens combat, enemies scattered | *Dominate monster* to pull them together, then Plague |
| Larva mage reduced to ≤32 HP | Place self within 60 ft of all enemies → *circle of death* centered on self |
| Larva mage reduced to 0 HP | Return to Worms triggers — becomes swarm of insects, Dashes away across ceiling |

## Retreat Conditions

- **Star Spawn Seer:** Flees when seriously wounded (≤61 HP). Dashes via Out-of-Phase Movement, departing by the most direct route through walls, floors, and enemies
- **Star Spawn Larva Mage:** Never retreats. Fights to 0 HP, then self-nukes with *circle of death*. If reduced to 0 HP, transforms into a swarm of insects and skitters away across the ceiling to re-form the next day

> [!tip] DM Tip
> The star spawn are designed as a team. Grues impose disadvantage on saves, hulks amplify psychic damage, manglers deliver burst melee, and the seer/larva mage orchestrate positioning. Run them together for maximum lethality — the seer's Bend Space and Collapse Distance turn the battlefield into a shell game where every swap is a trap.
