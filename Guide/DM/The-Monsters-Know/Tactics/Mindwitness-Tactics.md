# Mindwitness Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/ulitharid-and-mindwitness-tactics/)

---

## Tactical Summary
- A beholder abducted and cerebromorphed by mind flayers—a shadow of its former self (CR 5)
- Loses Antimagic Cone, significant Con/Cha, and over half its HP vs. a standard beholder
- Gains Telepathic Hub (broadcasts telepathic messages to 10 creatures it can see) and a decent Multiattack (Bite + Tentacles)
- Retains 6 Eye Rays fired at random, but chooses targets intelligently via trial-and-error
- Subservient to its mind flayer colony—fights to the death while part of a hive

## Combat Behavior
- **Eye Ray targeting** (educated guesswork):
  - Aversion Ray / Psychic Ray → front-line warriors
  - Fear Ray → enemies on the periphery
  - Slowing Ray → front-liners and supporters
  - Stunning Ray / Telekinetic Ray → squishy backliners
- **Melee engagement**: Unlike a regular beholder, will float down to engage a single melee opponent. Tentacles first (stuns on failed save), then Bite with advantage
- **Synergy**: Stunned targets are ripe for allied mind flayers, but they must grapple with their *own* tentacles for Extract Brain
- **Action choice**: Multiattack OR Eye Rays each turn—not both. Eye Rays at range, Multiattack when a lone enemy closes in

## Retreat Conditions
- While part of a colony: fights to the death
- If colony destroyed and independent long enough: withdraws at ≤52 hp

> [!tip] Running This Monster
> The mindwitness is a force multiplier—its Telepathic Hub coordinates the colony while Eye Rays soften targets for Extract Brain. Don't run it alone; pair it with at least one mind flayer.
