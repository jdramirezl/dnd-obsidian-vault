# Bulette Tactics

> [!summary]
> The bulette ("land shark") is a low-intelligence burrowing predator that lives only to eat. It ambushes from below, leaps onto clustered prey, and fights with brute simplicity. It has no sophistication — just raw power, tremorsense, and hunger.

> [!warning] Source Note
> The Monsters Know What They're Doing does not appear to have a dedicated bulette article. These tactics are derived from the stat block using the same analytical methodology as the blog.

---

## Stat Block Profile
- **Type:** Monstrosity, Large, unaligned
- **STR 19 / DEX 11 / CON 21 / INT 2 / WIS 10 / CHA 5**
- Pure brute: extraordinary STR + CON, animal-level INT
- **Burrow 40 ft**, land 40 ft
- **Tremorsense 60 ft** — detects prey by vibration through the ground
- **Standing Leap** — long jump 30 ft, high jump 15 ft (no running start needed)
- **AC 17** (natural armor), **HP 94**

---

## Behavior
- INT 2 = pure instinct, no tactics beyond predatory cunning
- Hunts by tremorsense — detects footsteps, movement, vibrations
- Ambush predator: burrows beneath prey, erupts from below
- Targets the **easiest or closest prey first**
- Avoids elves (dislikes the taste); tolerates dwarves reluctantly
- Solitary — no pack tactics, no coordination

---

## Combat Sequence

### Ambush
1. Detects prey via **tremorsense** (60 ft) while burrowed
2. Burrows to position directly beneath the **most clustered group**
3. Erupts from the ground → **Deadly Leap** if possible

### Deadly Leap (Recharge 5–6)
- Jumps at least 15 ft as part of movement, lands on target(s)
- Each creature in its space: **DC 16 STR or DEX save**
- Fail: 3d6+4 bludgeoning + 3d6+4 slashing, knocked **prone**
- Success: pushed 5 ft out of the way, no damage
- Prone targets grant advantage on subsequent Bite attacks

### Standard Attack
- **Bite:** +7 to hit, 4d12+4 piercing damage
- Only one attack per turn — no Multiattack
- Targets prone creatures first (advantage)

### Decision Loop (Instinct-Level)
1. Deadly Leap available + 2+ targets clustered? → **Leap**
2. Prone target in reach? → **Bite** (advantage)
3. No prone target? → **Bite** nearest creature
4. Badly hurt (≤37 hp)? → **Burrow away** to escape

---

## Retreat
- At **≤40% hp (~37 hp)** — burrows underground and flees
- Tremorsense lets it navigate blind underground
- PCs without burrow speed or tremorsense cannot pursue
- May resurface later if still hungry and prey is still present

---

## DM Encounter Tips

> [!tip] Terrain Matters
> Bulettes can only burrow through **earth and stone** — not worked stone floors, metal, or solid rock (DM discretion). Encounters on natural ground (plains, hills, farmland) are ideal.

- **Standing Leap** means it can clear walls, pits, and obstacles up to 15 ft high
- Tremorsense is defeated by **flying** or **levitating** creatures (no ground contact)
- A bulette that can't detect prey will surface and hunt by sight — much less effective
- Multiple bulettes in an area is unusual but terrifying — treat as a natural disaster
- Halflings are a bulette's favorite food (per MM flavor text)
