# Drow Tactics: Inquisitors

> [Source](https://www.themonstersknow.com/drow-tactics-inquisitors/)
> Bestiary: [[Elves-Drow]]

---

## Tactical Summary

- High-level cleric; extraordinary WIS/CHA; quasi-shock attacker with ambush capability
- Magic Resistance + Fey Ancestry + CON/WIS/CHA saves — unafraid of enemy spellcasters
- Discern Lie trumps Deception; proficient in Insight for parley assessment
- Death Lance Multiattack (3 attacks, +10) does ~79.5 expected damage vs AC 15 — often better than casting

## Combat Behavior

- **Round 1**: *spiritual weapon* (bonus, 4th level) + Multiattack or close to melee; if can't reach, *poison spray* (3d12), Dodge, Search, or Help
- **Round 2+**: Multiattack preferred vs anything less armored than chain mail + shield
- **Spell priorities**:
  - *Insect plague* (conc.) vs 4+ grouped enemies
  - *Harm* vs DEX-dependent foes (unhealable damage)
  - *Bestow curse* (wasted action option) vs heavy-armor Extra Attack fighters
  - Reserve 6th slot: wait to see if *true seeing* needed; otherwise *harm*
  - Reserve 4th slot for *freedom of movement*
  - Save 3rd/4th slots for *dispel magic*
- **Targets**: worshipers of non-Lolth gods, non-drow elves, dwarves, most dangerous foes
- Shock attacker style: strikes hard, stays engaged, dares opponent to disengage (OA threat)

## Key Decision Points

- Won't start a fight it can't win — uses Insight to assess; will parley if ambush isn't enough
- Multiattack vs *insect plague*: Multiattack wins vs scattered/lightly armored; *insect plague* wins vs 4+ grouped
- *Silence* better pre-combat (conceal ambush) than during
- Cast *true seeing* and *freedom of movement* before combat if intel suggests need (1 hr, no conc.)

## Retreat Conditions

- Zealot among zealots — fights to the death once committed
- Simply knows better than to start a fight where death is likely

> [!tip] Death Lance Multiattack (~79.5 expected damage vs AC 15) is deceptively powerful. Don't default to spellcasting — the inquisitor is often better off stabbing things.
