# Demon Tactics: Type 4, 5 and 6

All upper-tier demons are immune to physical damage from normal weapons (resistant per errata), resistant to cold/fire/lightning, immune to poison. They don't fear death outside the Abyss and never retreat.

## Nalfeshnee (CR 13)

### Tactical Summary
Brute (extraordinary STR/CON) with extraordinary INT. Often commands lesser demons. Can plan, coordinate, adapt, and accurately assess enemy strengths/weaknesses. Flies.

### Horror Nimbus (Recharge)
- Part of Multiattack — usable ~1 turn in 3
- Like Frightful Presence: each enemy can only be affected once per encounter
- **Deliberately targets only its current melee opponent** — does NOT try to hit multiple foes. Positions to affect only the one it's about to engage

### Combat Pattern
1. Flies down toward an opponent
2. Uses Multiattack (including Horror Nimbus if available)
3. Flies back up out of reach
4. AC 18 + 184 hp — barely cares about opportunity attacks
5. If an enemy lands 2 opportunity attacks in a row: switches to fighting that enemy toe-to-toe on the ground

### Target Priority
- Uses Teleport + Magic Resistance to take out **spellcasters** giving its minions trouble (bite as opportunity attack if they flee)
- Prioritizes PCs using: magic weapons (especially anti-demon/evil), acid/thunder/radiant/necrotic/psychic damage, or DEX-save spells

## Marilith (CR 16)

### Tactical Summary
Six-armed Cuisinart. Skirmisher profile (high DEX) with 40 ft. speed. Reactive + Parry = effective **AC 23 vs. melee**. Exceptionally high INT and very high WIS — excellent planner and coordinator. Often commands demon troops.

### Combat Behavior
1. **First attack always Tail** — if it hits, grapples and restrains the target
2. All 6 longsword attacks go against the **restrained enemy** (advantage on all)
3. On subsequent turns with a grappled enemy: split attacks equally between grappled target and other melee engagers, with remaining attacks on the grappled target
4. Uses **Teleport** to reach enemy spellcasters — Tail attack to stop them fleeing, then longsword barrage
5. Same threat assessment criteria as nalfeshnee

## Balor (CR 19)

### Tactical Summary
General at the head of demon armies. Brute profile. **Fire Aura** burns every adjacent creature — *happy* to be surrounded. Fights on the ground (Fire Aura triggers at start of turn — needs adjacency).

### Combat Behavior
1. **Whip** (30 ft. reach) — snag an enemy and yank into Fire Aura hot zone
2. **Longsword** — follow up on the pulled-in enemy
3. Subsequent turns: sword the current target while whipping in more enemies, OR finish a wounded foe with sword and whip a new one, OR Teleport to a spellcaster and longsword opportunity attack if they flee
4. Generally fights on the ground, not flying (needs adjacency for Fire Aura)

### Death Throes
- Applies when physical form is destroyed on any plane
- When **seriously wounded (≤104 hp):** tries to position within 30 ft. of as many enemies as possible to take them with it

## Goristro (CR 17)

### Tactical Summary
Living siege engine. Brute profile but **stupid** — operates purely from instinct. Can recognize threats like an animal but can't assess PC abilities or adapt.

### Charge vs. Multiattack Decision
- **Charge** (15+ ft. straight movement → gore): 14d10+7 avg 84 damage, all-or-nothing
- **Multiattack** (fist/fist/hoof): 3d10+6d8+21 avg 64 damage, three chances to hit
- Instinctually prefers Charge when it has **≥67% chance to hit** (target AC ≤19)
- At AC 20+: hedges with Multiattack (three rolls better than one)
- With disadvantage: threshold drops to AC ≤16
- With advantage: threshold rises to AC ≤21
- Even when already in melee: **backs up 20 ft.** (doesn't care about opportunity attacks at AC 19/310 hp) and Charges again
- Failed Charge knockdown → target thrown 20 ft. → can Charge again next turn ("head golf")

> [!tip] DM Tips
> - Nalfeshnees fly-by attack: down, Multiattack, back up — track vertical positioning
> - Mariliths have effective AC 23 vs. melee (Reactive + Parry) — ranged/spell attacks are the counter
> - Balor Death Throes is confirmed to work on any plane — a seriously wounded balor is MORE dangerous, not less
> - Goristro "head golf" (Charge → knockdown → throw 20 ft. → Charge again) is hilarious and devastating
> - All upper-tier demons are excellent commanders — pair them with lesser demon troops
