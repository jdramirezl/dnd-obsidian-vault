# Hoplite Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/hoplite-tactics/) | **Date**: December 2, 2024

---

## Common Traits

- Good armor, DEX > CON (shock attacker contour), DEX save proficiency, solid melee. Designed for squads against mid/high-level parties under a powerful leader.

## Akroan Hoplite (Spartan)

- STR save proficiency resists formation disruption. **Hold the Line** (like Sentinel) punishes movement within reach.
- Tight formation, no gaps: approaching a non-end hoplite provokes **3 opportunity attacks** (target + both neighbors).
- **Multiattack**: 3 melee or 2 ranged. Melee: **Shield Bash** (knock prone) → 2 Spear attacks (advantage). Devastating: shield knock-down → double stab → punishing OA when they try to get up and flee.
- **Morale**: Undeterred by wounds. **Fights to the death.** Won't initiate against foolish odds if improvable. Encounters should be Deadly/High difficulty.

## Meletian Hoplite (Athenian)

- INT save proficiency, Perception proficiency. Hard to fool. Same weapons as Akroan but throws 3 spears at once.
- **Ray of frost** substitutes for one attack: less damage but no disadvantage at range. Superior to thrown spear beyond 20 ft vs AC 15+.
- **Key spells**: *Invisibility* (ambush setup), *blur* (pre-combat prophylaxis), *cloud of daggers* (consistent 10 dmg vs AC 17+; entire formation can cast it for a bargain-basement *blade barrier*), *expeditious retreat* (free bonus action for charging/withdrawing), *minor illusion* (squad makes scary sounds to unnerve opponents). *Color spray/sleep* — just stab 'em.
- **Retreat**: When ≥50% seriously wounded (≤19 hp). Less wounded delay pursuit; more wounded withdraw.

## Setessan Hoplite (Themiscyran)

- Fewer attacks but adds poison damage. Effective at very long range with longbows. **Pack Tactics** gives advantage on Scimitar attacks.
- WIS save proficiency; coordinate to take out enemy casters. At 125–150 ft, loose arrows without worrying about arcane reprisal.
- **Retreat**: When ≥50% seriously wounded (≤23 hp). Same withdrawal pattern as Meletians. Encounters should be Deadly/High difficulty.
