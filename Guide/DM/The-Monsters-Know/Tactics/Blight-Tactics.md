# Blight Tactics

Three types of evil awakened plants. They attack out of spite, not hunger. All have 60 ft. blindsight. No survival instinct — fight until destroyed.

## Twig Blights (CR ⅛)

- Small, scrappy; poor STR, good DEX/CON
- Found in forests and abandoned settlements; always in **large patches**
- **False Appearance** + Stealth proficiency = ambush from plain sight
- Inert until victims come within reach → attack with surprise
- Fight until destroyed; follow retreating foes but won't Dash after them
- Will surround if numbers allow
- **Vulnerable to fire** — Dash away from fire sources

## Needle Blights (CR ¼)

- Human-sized; sufficient STR/CON to fight alone (more in evil-suffused areas)
- **No Stealth** — not ambushers; don't care if seen
- Stationary until enemies approach within 30 ft. → needle volley (ranged)
- If enemy closes to melee → claw attack; make OAs on retreat but may not pursue
- Not afraid of fire; fight until destroyed

## Vine Blights (CR ½)

- Vaguely humanoid braided vines; some Stealth + False Appearance (dim light)
- **Entangling Plants** (15 ft. radius) → restrain targets → close for melee grapple/squeeze
- 10 ft. movement speed — very slow; wait for victims to come close
- Make OAs on retreating foes; pursue if Entangling Plants can catch them
- Fight until destroyed

## Gulthias Tree (Homebrew Boss)

The source of blights — a fiendish tree born from a vampire's staked corpse. Keith's homebrew version (CR 4):

- Huge plant; AC 13, 59 hp; blindsight 120 ft.
- **Creeping Fog** (500 ft.): blocks sunlight, dim light + lightly obscured
- **Regeneration:** 10 hp/turn unless in sunlight or hit with radiant/holy water
- **Slam** (10 ft. reach): 3d6+4 bludgeoning, or grapple (DC 16)
- **Impale** (grappled/restrained targets): 1d6+4 piercing + 3d6 necrotic; reduces max HP, heals the tree; humanoids killed and buried rise as vine blights
- Vulnerable to fire; resistant to bludgeoning, necrotic, piercing

> [!tip] DM Tips
> - Mixed groups work like 4e roles: twig blights = minions, needle blights = artillery, vine blights = controllers
> - Twig blight ambushes are Birnam Wood moments — the bushes come alive
> - Vine blights' 10 ft. speed means they're area-denial, not pursuers
> - The Gulthias tree is the real encounter — destroying it stops new blights from spawning
