# Felidar and Archon of the Triumvirate Tactics

## Felidar

### Tactical Summary

Big celestial lion. Pounce (20 ft charge → knock prone on failed STR save → bonus Claws). 40 ft speed, high AC. Some have wings (fly speed = land speed, pounce from above).

### Combat Behavior

- **Pounce loop:** Move 20 ft → Claws (prone) → bonus Claws + Bite with advantage. Allow OA to move 20 ft away, repeat next turn
- Keen Hearing and Sight prevents surprise

---

## Archon of the Triumvirate

### Tactical Summary

Azorius law enforcement. Extraordinary STR but even greater WIS — support/control role despite CR 14 melee power. Rides a winged felidar. Lawful neutral zealot.

### Combat Behavior

- **Pacifying Presence (120 ft, opening move):** DC 18 WIS save or charmed + drop weapons + lose concentration. Targets re-save each turn — act fast
- **Eye of the Law (bonus action):** Scan one creature/turn for crimes in last 24 hours. Recon before engagement
- **Hammer of Justice (Multiattack ×2):** 30 dmg/hit, can knock prone. Prefers nonlethal finishing blows
- **Smite (2 legendary actions):** Third Hammer attack + mount attack
- **Detention (3 legendary actions):** Banishment until end of next turn, no concentration. High cost, skip vs CHA save proficiency
- **Spells (at will):** *Calm emotions* (crowd control, 4+ targets), *command* (single fugitive), *compelled duel* (last resort, prevents fleeing)
- **Mount trait:** Bonus action teleport onto felidar. Archon at 0 hp = mount at 0 hp too

### Retreat Conditions

Archon never retreats. Orders troops to withdraw at 10%+ casualties. Covers retreat last.

> [!tip] DM Tip
> Run as a police operation: footsoldiers cordon, griffon hussars cover exits, archon descends with Pacifying Presence. The PCs aren't fighting a monster — they're resisting arrest. Reframe from combat to social/tactical problem-solving.
