# Medusa Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/medusa-tactics/) | **Date**: November 18, 2016
> **Related Bestiary**: [[Medusa]]

---

## Overview

- Infernal bargain for immortality — compulsion-driven. High DEX/CON (skirmisher), enough INT to plan traps, enough WIS to choose targets, high CHA for parley. Proficient in Deception, Insight, Stealth.
- Lair: shadowy ruins with obstructions and hiding places. Stays hidden, uses wiles to lure trespassers.

## Petrifying Gaze — Key Details

1. **Range 30 ft** — must draw opponents close. Don't start a sniper battle with the longbow.
2. **Not instant**: DC 14 CON save. Fail by 5+ → immediate petrifaction. Total chance: ~55% (average PC), ~30% (high-CON proficient PC).
3. **Restrained on partial fail**: advantage vs restrained, their attacks at disadvantage. Follow up with melee — lasts only one round.
4. **Avert eyes**: creatures can avert to avoid save — **unless surprised**. Surprise is essential.
5. **Needs dim light**: bright light risks self-petrifaction via reflection. Total darkness means targets can't see gaze.
6. **Affects all in range**: semicircular ~30-ft AoE (front-facing). Need ≥3 opponents in gaze to be worth unveiling.

## Combat Flow

1. Detect trespassers → hide where it can pop out and catch ≥3 by surprise within 30 ft but outside melee reach.
2. Surprise round: Petrifying Gaze hits all facing targets. Only unaffected opponents can attack after turn 1.
3. Turn 2+: focus attacks on **restrained opponents** first.
4. Prolonged combat benefits medusa — no immunity on successful save, repeated petrifaction chances.
5. Vs 2+ attackers: **Dodge** and move to concealment. If not chased → Hide, reposition, pop out again.
6. Vs averting opponents: unseen attacker → **advantage** on attacks against those looking away.

## Retreat

- Seriously injured (≤50 hp) → Dodge while retreating, hoping opponents carelessly expose themselves to gaze.
