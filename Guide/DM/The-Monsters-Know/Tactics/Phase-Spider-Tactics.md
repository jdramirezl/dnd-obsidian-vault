# Phase Spider Tactics

## Tactical Summary

Brute fighter. High STR + DEX, above-average CON, INT 6 (chimp-level). Web Walker but doesn't spin webs. Ethereal Jaunt lets it phase between material and ethereal planes. Stealth proficiency + darkvision = nighttime/underground ambush predator.

## Ecology

Likely evolved from giant spiders via magical mutation. Live alongside giant spider colonies. Act in conjunction with web-spinning sisters — take out moving targets while giant spiders ensnare prey. May target *larger* opponents (more venom delivery: 4d8 vs giant spider's 2d8, same DC).

## Combat Behavior

### Blinking In (Ethereal → Material)

1. Ethereal Jaunt (bonus action) — appear behind target
2. Bite (action) — gains advantage as unseen attacker
3. **Fairness check:** Phase spider makes DEX (Stealth) vs target's passive Perception. Fail = no advantage

### Blinking Out (Material → Ethereal)

1. Bite (action) — spite attack
2. Ethereal Jaunt (bonus action) — vanish

### After Paralyzing Prey

- Uses remaining movement to close on another opponent fitting the usual prey profile (old, weak, isolated, oblivious)
- Bigger opponents only engaged via stealth strikes

### Engagement Rules

- Stays engaged with one target until target is paralyzed or spider takes ≥10 damage in a single round
- If ≥10 damage in a round: Bite once, then blink out
- Seriously wounded (≤12 hp): blinks out and doesn't return

> [!tip] DM Tip
> Phase spiders are devastating ambush predators. The blink-in/blink-out cycle makes them nearly impossible to pin down. Readied actions are the counter — but the Stealth check determines who strikes first.
