# Fomorian Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/fomorian-tactics/) | **Date**: November 7, 2018

---

## Tactical Summary

- **Cursed giant brutes** — once noble and beautiful, now warped. Extremely strong and tough with hefty HP.
- **Low Int (9), high Wis (14)**: Good at judging if a fight is winnable, but lack tactical breadth. Once committed, behavior is simple.
- **Ambush capable**: Expertise in Perception, proficiency in Stealth. Trail groups before deciding to attack.
- **Darkness dwellers**: Long-range darkvision. Underground or dense gloomy forests. Don't attack in bright light.
- **Evil Eye (60 ft)**: Expected damage ~20.25 hp — better than greatclub's ~11.7 expected vs AC 15+shield.

## Combat Behavior

**Opening**: Attack from hiding for advantage + surprise. Lore suggests targeting elves/Fey Ancestry races first (optional).

**Tunnel vision**: Pick one target and keep whomping. Only switch to Multiattack (greatclub + Evil Eye split) after taking damage from a different source.

**Evil Eye usage**: Only against targets >15 ft away (out of club reach). If taking both melee and ranged/AoE damage, Evil Eye always targets the ranged/AoE attacker.

**Curse of the Evil Eye (1/combat, full action)**:
- Triggered when a single opponent deals ≥45 damage to the fomorian.
- Used against that specific opponent.
- If the curse fails → recognizes tide is turning → begins retreating while still attacking.

## Retreat Conditions

- **Curse fails**: Fighting withdrawal — retreats while continuing to attack.
- **Seriously injured (≤59 hp) without using Curse**: Dashes away. If pursued by one enemy, may slow to Evil Eye that pursuer.

## DM Tips

> [!tip] Running This Monster
> Tunnel vision is key — fomorians fixate on one target until hurt by someone else. The Curse is the "nuclear option" — save for when a PC deals serious damage. Place encounters in darkness to leverage darkvision.
