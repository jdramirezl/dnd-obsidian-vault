# Naga Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/naga-tactics/) | **Date**: December 19, 2016
> **Book**: Monster Manual

---

## Overview — All Nagas

- **Shock attackers**. Highest physical stats are Str and Dex; Con is lower → strike fast, limited staying power.
- **Bite** is default action — modest piercing + heavy poison damage. Immune to poison and charm.
- Strong mental abilities across the board → good combat sense, willing to parley.
- **Darkvision** → prefer nighttime / subterranean activity. Won't be encountered outdoors during the day.
- Living nagas (spirit, guardian) **can't truly die** without *wish* — they rejuvenate in days. No reason to flee.
- Living nagas have proficiency in all big three saves + Charisma; guardians also get Intelligence.

## Key Spell: Hold Person

- Only spell shared by both spirit and guardian nagas.
- "OK but not great" as a combo with bite — takes two turns.
- **Boost to 3rd or 4th level** to target 2–3 opponents. Worth trying against a party of 4; against 5+ opponents, probably not worth it.
- Never boosted to 5th level — those slots are too scarce.

## Spirit Naga (Evil)

- **Lightning bolt** for ranged damage (Dex save). Use when 3+ enemies line up. Better than *blight* unless enemies resist lightning.
- **Sleep** is unreliable at mid-levels — only useful as a late-fight cleanup spell against already-damaged foes.
- **Dominate person** — cast *before* combat, not during (damage breaks it).
- **Dimension door** — only if enemies reveal a *wish* spell before killing it.
- *Water breathing* is for servants/allies (naga doesn't need air). Subtract a 3rd-level slot if near water.
- *Detect thoughts*, *detect magic*, *charm person* — social interaction, not combat.

## Guardian Naga (Lawful Good)

- **Flame strike** for direct damage — worth casting if it hits 2+ enemies in the AoE.
- *Sacred flame* — only 3d8 radiant, weak fallback.
- **Spit Poison** — only against evil attackers who won't relent, and only at range (same poison damage as bite, minus piercing).
- Won't boost *flame strike* with its lone 6th-level slot — saves *true seeing* for *greater invisibility* counters.
- **Prefers negotiation** → uses *command* ("Begone!") before initiating combat. *Cure wounds* is a boon for supplicants.
- *Shield of faith* — bonus action but concentration; not worth it alongside *hold person*.

## Bone Naga (Undead)

- Created by yuan-ti. **Compulsion-driven** — obsessively serves its purpose, fights to destruction.
- Doesn't rejuvenate. More vulnerable to spellcasters → prioritizes caster targets.
- Still tries *hold person* + bite combo out of habit, even though lower DC makes it less effective.
- Former guardian nagas as bone nagas are "sad, defeated wrecks" — may not bother casting spells beyond *hold person*.
- *Sleep* may actually work at the lower levels bone nagas are encountered, but the naga may not realize this.

## General Behavior

- Both spirit and guardian nagas identify enemy weaknesses and avoid unfavorable battles.
- Spirit nagas use **deception and charm** to avoid fights.
- Guardian nagas use **negotiation, persuasion, intimidation**.
