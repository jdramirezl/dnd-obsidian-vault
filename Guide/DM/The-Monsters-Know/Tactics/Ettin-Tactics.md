# Ettin Tactics

> [!summary]
> Two-headed subgiants related to orcs. Pure brutes — extraordinary Str, exceptional Con, low Dex/Int. Charge the biggest enemy and bash. Nearly impossible to surprise thanks to Wakeful (one head always alert) and expertise + advantage on Perception. Often employed as sentries by orcs.

---

## Stat Block Profile
- **Type:** Giant, Large
- Extraordinary Str, exceptional Con, low Dex/Int
- Darkvision 60 ft
- **Wakeful:** one head sleeps while the other watches — can never be caught napping
- Expertise in Perception + advantage on Perception checks

---

## Combat Behavior

### Opening
- Spots foes (very likely — Perception expertise + advantage + darkvision)
- Charges the **biggest enemy** (perceived as greatest threat)
- Multiattack: battleaxe (left hand) + morningstar (right hand)

### Sustained Melee
- **One melee opponent:** both weapons on same target
- **Two melee opponents:** turns each head to face a different foe, divides attacks between them
- Stays in melee — won't disengage to chase ranged attackers while melee enemies remain
- Only chases archers/casters after dispatching melee foes

---

## Retreat
- **Seriously wounded (≤34 hp):** Dashes away
- Each head berates the other for fighting badly
- Not nimble enough to Dodge, not intelligent enough to Disengage

---

## Role as Sentry
- Wakeful + Perception expertise + advantage + darkvision = extremely hard to sneak past
- Orcs and other clever beings employ ettins specifically for this purpose

> [!tip] DM Tips
> Ettins are simple — charge biggest, bash, split attacks if flanked. The real value is as a sentry: nearly impossible to surprise. The retreat with both heads arguing is a fun roleplay moment.
