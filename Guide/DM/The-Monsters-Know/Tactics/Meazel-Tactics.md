# Meazel Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/meazel-tactics/) | **Date**: June 11, 2020

---

## Overview

- Shadowfell-warped humanoid kidnappers. Low STR/CON, very high DEX — not suited for attrition. High INT, above-average WIS. Proficient in Perception and Stealth. 120-ft darkvision.
- Always begin combat **hidden**. Core strategy: Garrote → Shadow Teleport (500 ft) to isolate victim.

## Core Combo: Garrote + Shadow Teleport

- Garrote grapples on hit → Shadow Teleport on subsequent turn spirits victim away.
- Target must be in dim light/darkness for advantage on first attack. Without advantage, +5 only reliable vs AC ≤12. With advantage: two-thirds+ chance up to AC 17.
- Own AC 13, 35 hp — can absorb a couple hits but no more.

## On a Mission (Working for Someone)

- Greater numbers with intelligence on targets.
- **Two meazels** double-team medium/heavy armor targets (one Help action for advantage on Garrote). Also double-team darkvision targets.
- **Threat priority**: darkvision users, tough warriors (especially barbarians), dragonborn (breath weapons vs low CON), anyone dealing ≥11 damage/round.
- **AC 18+ targets**: three-to-one with **Shortsword** (also vs slippery Acrobatics escapers).
- Employers: Strahd (patrol perimeter, yoink to castle spots), lich/shadow dragon (drop targets before higher-CR attackers), shadar-kai gloom weaver (*darkness* for free battlefield control).

## Freelance Meazels

- Fewer in number. Target easiest prey: low AC, not tough, isolated, oblivious.
- Spirit victims to **separate locations**. Keep garrotes on necks, switch to Shortsword if targets escape, or let other nasties finish.

## Retreat

- Flees at ≤14 hp. Shadow Teleport if available; otherwise Disengage → Hide → Dash.
