# Goblinoids Revisited

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/goblinoids-revisited/) | **Date**: January 6, 2017
> **Related Bestiary**: [[Goblins]], [[Bugbears]], [[Hobgoblins]]

---

## Tactical Summary

- Volo's Guide revisit of goblinoids. Reinforces existing tactics with deeper behavioral context.
- Goblins' Nimble Escape is innate slipperiness, not military discipline — they scamper out of reach too fast to react.
- Greater emphasis on **traps** for goblin encounters — PCs should walk into traps before seeing goblins.
- Bugbears confirmed as ambush-reliant, high-damage surprise attackers lacking intelligence and discipline.
- Hobgoblins confirmed as intelligent, disciplined soldiers. Volo's drops the elf-hatred from the MM.

## Key Updates from Volo's

### Goblins
- Ambush principle holds. Encounters should often begin with PCs triggering traps.
- Goblin lairs section provides a scaffold for building multi-encounter goblin dungeons.
- **Booyahg booyahg booyahg**: Rare goblin wild magic sorcerer using the mage stat block + darkvision + Nimble Escape.
- Follows basic mage combat principles (escape routes, avoid damage, maximize AoE) but may skip *mage armor* — goblins think "defense" means dodging or running, not durability.
- Wild magic surges on every spell including cantrips. Play for maximum comedy.

### Bugbears
- Volo's disagrees with the idea that captured bugbears cooperate fully. They'll offer to help track companions but plan to escape at the first opportunity.
- Still ambush-reliant, still lack discipline.

### Hobgoblins
- Volo's drops the elf-hatred entirely. Author sides with Volo's — the loathing is out of character for otherwise disciplined soldiers.
- "Reluctant Little Tyrants" section: subjugated goblins resign themselves to their fate (Stockholm syndrome). They won't desert.
- This means hobgoblin commanders no longer need to hang back watching for deserters — they fight on the front line alongside goblin minions.
- Two new variants introduced: Devastator (battle wizard) and Iron Shadow (rogue-monk-mage) — covered in a separate article.

## DM Tips

> [!tip] Running This Monster
> The biggest update is that hobgoblin commanders can now fight on the front line with their goblin troops — no need to babysit. Start goblin encounters with traps, not combat. The booyahg booyahg booyahg is pure chaos — wild magic surges on cantrips make every round unpredictable.
