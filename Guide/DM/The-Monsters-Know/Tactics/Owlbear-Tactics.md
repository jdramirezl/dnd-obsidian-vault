# Owlbear Tactics

## Tactical Summary

The owlbear is a dumb (INT 3), aggressive brute with bear-like temperament. It has no tactical sophistication — it charges the first thing it senses and Multiattacks until something dies or it does. High STR, very high CON, decent speed (40 ft.), keen sight and smell (Perception +3 with advantage). Active at night/underground (darkvision). Encountered in its lair during the day, it will be sleeping.

## Combat Behavior

### Engagement by Distance
| Distance | Action |
|---|---|
| >80 ft. | **Dash** toward prey (noisy — no surprise possible) |
| 40–80 ft. | Move 40 ft., then **Ready an Attack** against the first creature in reach |
| <40 ft. | Charge in and **Multiattack** (claw + beak) |

> **Note:** Multiattack cannot be used as a readied action — only a single Attack action can be readied.

### Target Selection
- Attacks the **first target it sensed** — no prioritization (INT 3)
- Switches focus to the **last PC who dealt melee damage** to it
- Does NOT understand ranged attacks — ignores archers
- A trained owlbear can be commanded to hold a target, but requires a WIS check (DC 10 or half damage taken, whichever is higher) when hit by a different melee attacker; on failure, it reverts to instinct; on natural 1, it turns on its handler

### Grab-and-Run
- If it drops a PC to 0 hp AND no one hits it with melee before its next turn:
  - Seizes the unconscious PC in its beak
  - **Dashes** back to its lair
- Only does this in open wilderness — not in enclosed spaces or if already in its lair

## Key Decision Points

| Situation | Response |
|---|---|
| Senses prey at distance | Dash or move + ready attack |
| In melee | Multiattack (claw + beak) every round |
| Hit by melee attacker | Switch focus to that attacker |
| Drops a PC, no melee retaliation | Grab body and flee to lair |
| Cornered in enclosed space | Fight to the death |

## Retreat Conditions

- **Open wilderness:** Flees when seriously wounded (≤23 hp)
- **Enclosed space / cornered:** Fights to the death — no retreat possible
- **In its lair:** Fights to the death

> [!tip] DM Tips
> - The owlbear is a great "animal encounter" — play it like a territorial bear, not a malicious monster
> - The grab-and-run creates urgency: the party has to chase it down or lose a companion
> - Keen Sight and Smell means stealth is very hard against an owlbear — Perception +3 with advantage
> - It's fast (40 ft.) — most PCs in heavy armor can't outrun it
> - A sleeping owlbear in its lair is a great tension-builder: can the party sneak past?
> - Trained owlbears exist in-lore — a bandit gang or noble house might use one as a guard animal
