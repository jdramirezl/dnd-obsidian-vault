# Gaj Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/gaj-tactics/) | **Date**: June 13, 2023

---

## Tactical Summary

- **CR 4 aberration** (originally from Dark Sun/Athas). Giant bug-like ambush predator with climbing speed. Found on planets, not in Wildspace.
- **Primary brute**: Very high Str, high Con. High Wis used offensively. Int 12 — smart enough to plan but prone to error.
- **Double proficiency** in Perception and Stealth. Buries under sand/silt, lurks in caves, perches on ledges.
- **Goal is interrogation, not eating**: Grapples with Mandibles, then uses Mind-Probing Antennae to extract intel.

## Combat Behavior

**Optimal Multiattack sequence**:
1. Round 1: Paralyze (Wis save) + Mandibles. Paralyze makes Mandibles easier to land.
2. Round 2: Mandibles (on grappled target) + Mind-Probing Antennae.

**Paralyze is not an attack or spell**: If target saves, gaj's position isn't revealed. Can continue lurking until recharge (6 only).

**Target selection**: Int 12 can't accurately read Wisdom saves. Instead selects based on *value of information* — may track and observe PCs beforehand. Interested in intel about its enemies, not just about the PCs.

**Not motivated by hunger**: Doesn't try to kill grappled targets or run away with them.

## Retreat Conditions

- **Moderately wounded (≤52 hp)**: Drops target, Disengages, scuttles away. Climbs out of reach if possible.

## Encounter Scaling

- CR 4 is weak solo. Best in groups: half party size at levels 6–8, three per PC at level 17+.
- Each gaj stays hidden until its Paralyze succeeds.

## DM Tips

> [!tip] Running This Monster
> Gaj are sapient spies, not mindless bugs. Have them observe the party for days before striking. The "not revealed on failed Paralyze" rule is key to their patience. Run multiple gaj as coordinated ambush — each targeting a different PC, all striking when Paralyze lands.
