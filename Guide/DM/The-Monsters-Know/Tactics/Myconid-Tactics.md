# Myconid Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/myconid-tactics/)

---

## Tactical Summary
- Subterranean fungus creatures (plants). Lawful neutral—not automatically hostile, default disposition is indifferent
- All share 120 ft darkvision, Sun Sickness (disadvantage on everything in sunlight, dies after 1 hour), Distress Spores (alerts other myconids within 240 ft when injured), Rapport Spores (telepathic thought-sharing with all living creatures)
- No verbal communication—Rapport Spores are their only way to "talk" to non-myconids
- The more chaos-muppety the PCs, the less myconids appreciate their presence

## By Type

### Myconid Sprout (CR 0)
- Fungus babies. Low Str, average Dex/Con, 10 ft speed
- Won't start a fight unless outnumbering PCs 4:1+
- If threatened: Dash directly away (20 ft/round). Use Rapport Spores only when within 10 ft of a larger myconid
- Distress Spores summon help from up to 240 ft away

### Myconid Adult (CR 1/2)
- 20 ft speed. Average Str/Dex, slightly above-average Con
- Prefer 2:1 numerical advantage before initiating a fight
- **First action**: Pacifying Spores (~25–50% chance to stun a single opponent)
- If attacked unprovoked and opponent is stunned: release Rapport Spores to ask *why* they're being attacked
- If myconids are the aggressors (responding to PC mayhem): attack stunned opponents with advantage
- Won't retry Pacifying Spores on a target that already resisted *its own* attempt—just punches
- Self-preservation at ≤8 hp: Dash away (but 20 ft speed makes escape unlikely)
- If outnumbered by casualties: stop attacking, Rapport Spores to parley. If parley fails: one last Pacifying Spores burst, then all Dash away

### Myconid Sovereign (CR 2)
- Above-average Str/Con, brute fighter style. Leads responses to PC provocation
- **Multiattack**: One spore attack (Pacifying or Hallucinating) + one Fist
- Pacifying Spores: Stuns (auto-fail Str/Dex saves, attacks have advantage)
- Hallucinating Spores: Poisons + incapacitates (but no auto-fail saves or advantage—strictly worse in combat)
- **Decision**: If responding to PC mayhem → Pacifying Spores. If PCs are aggressors and sovereign doesn't know why → Hallucinating Spores + attempt to talk them down
- **Animating Spores**: Creates spore servants (fungus zombies) from humanoid/beast corpses. Mindless brutes, no ranged attacks, no Dash/Disengage. Fight until destroyed. Aimed at stunned opponents for advantage

## Key Decision Points
- Myconids are not evil. Combat should feel like a misunderstanding escalating, not a monster attack
- Rapport Spores are the de-escalation tool—myconids will try to communicate before and during combat
- Spore servants are expendable drones thrown at stunned targets for advantage

> [!tip] Running This Monster
> Myconids are the rare monster that genuinely doesn't want to fight. Let the PCs stumble into a misunderstanding, have the myconids try to parley via Rapport Spores mid-combat, and make the players feel bad if they slaughter a bunch of sentient mushrooms who just wanted to be left alone.
