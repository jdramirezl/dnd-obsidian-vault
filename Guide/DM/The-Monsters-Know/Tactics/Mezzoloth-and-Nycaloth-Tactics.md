# Mezzoloth and Nycaloth Tactics

> [!info] Source Note
> From the original "Yugoloth Tactics" article which also covers arcanaloths and ultroloths.

## Yugoloth Shared Traits

All yugoloths: immune to acid/poison, resistant to cold/fire/lightning/physical from nonmagical weapons. Magic Resistance. Darkvision + enhanced sight. Innate *darkness*. Can Teleport. High STR + CON, lower DEX = brute fighters. Permanently killed only on Gehenna — no reason to retreat elsewhere.

---

## Mezzoloth (CR 5)

Low INT — operates on instinct. Blindsight + darkvision. Has *darkness* (2/day), *dispel magic* (2/day), *cloudkill* (1/day).

### Spell Priority: Cloudkill > Darkness

Both require concentration (can't stack). *Cloudkill* heavily obscures (same as *darkness*), darkvision doesn't penetrate, AND deals poison damage even on successful save. Strictly superior.

- Multiple mezzoloths: one casts *darkness*, another *cloudkill*
- Solo: *cloudkill* unless specifically needing total darkness

### Combat Sequence

1. **Cloudkill** toward the largest enemy cluster (≥4 preferred, 20 ft radius)
2. Scamper into the poison cloud — immune to poison, start stabbing
3. **Dispel magic** only vs spells causing severe inconvenience that can't be escaped by moving/Teleporting
4. If hit with radiant/necrotic/psychic/thunder: lash out at that opponent immediately. Not adjacent → **Teleport** behind them, Multiattack next turn. Claws for opportunity attacks

---

## Nycaloth (CR 9)

Stealthy flying brute. *Darkness* and *mirror image* at will. AC 18, 123 hp, wide resistances — doesn't fear opportunity attacks. Fights from the air: swoop, attack, swoop back up.

### Spell Priority

1. **Darkness** (default opener) — darkvision + blindsight = full advantage
2. **Mirror image** (fallback) — if *darkness* fails or enemies unaffected
3. **Dispel magic** — same as mezzoloth
4. **Invisibility → Teleport combo** — Teleport is an action, not a spell, so *invisibility* doesn't break. Cast *invisibility* → Teleport (still invisible) → attack with advantage AND surprise. Costs an extra round; use when payoff justifies delay (e.g., opponents have strong healers)

### Combat Behavior

- **Aerial skirmisher:** Fly down, Multiattack, fly back up. Opportunity attacks are acceptable cost
- **Greataxe** vs front-line fighters/barbarians (raw damage)
- **Claws** vs frailer targets with low CON saves (rogues, monks, skirmisher rangers, shapeshifted druids) — lingering damage keeps ticking after they flee

> [!tip] DM Tip
> Mezzoloths are instinct-driven grunts — *cloudkill* into the biggest cluster, wade in stabbing. Nycaloths are terrifying: invisible teleporting flying brutes that open with *darkness* and swoop from above. The invisibility → Teleport → surprise combo should freak players out. Use nycaloths as elite shock troops, mezzoloths as expendable infantry.
