# Death's Head and Boneless Tactics

## Death's Head (CR 1/2)

### Tactical Summary

Flying disembodied head — skirmisher with high WIS, very low INT. Thrives in difficult terrain (forests, swamps) where it can fly in/out of reach faster than grounded opponents. Can exploit favorable terrain but lacks the sense to seek it out.

### Combat Behavior
1. Flies in, **Bites** the frailest/least-armored target (high WIS = good target selection)
2. Keeps gnashing same target **until it takes damage**
3. After taking damage: **Dodges** (not smart enough to Disengage, but decent AC) and flies to safe distance
4. Re-engages a **different target**
5. If struck by melee: avoids that attacker going forward
6. If struck by ranged: may retaliate or pick someone else at random

### Monstrous Variants
- **Nothic's head:** After disorienting target, immediately flits to a new target
- **Medusa's head:** After restraining target, immediately flits to a new target

### Retreat: Never. Undead compulsion — fights until destroyed.

## Boneless (CR 1)

### Tactical Summary

Skin-sack brute. High STR/CON, abysmal INT. Zero tactical sophistication.

### Combat Behavior
1. Goes straight for the **nearest target**
2. Both **Slam** attacks must hit the **same creature** — never splits
3. Both Slams hit → **Crushing Embrace**: grapples, prevents breathing
4. **Suffocation starts immediately** — no chance to hold breath
5. Can't breathe = can't speak = can't cast verbal spells

### Retreat: Never. Fights until destroyed.

> [!tip] DM Tips
> - Death's heads pair well as reinforcements for a dullahan or undead boss
> - Have PHB ch. 8 "Suffocating" rules ready for Crushing Embrace
> - Don't let PCs hold breath before Crushing Embrace — start suffocation immediately
> - One corpse can yield: skull → death's head, skin → boneless, bones → skeleton, hands → crawling claws
