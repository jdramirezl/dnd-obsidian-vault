# Gold Dragon Tactics

> [!summary]
> Gold dragons are the most powerful metallic dragons — aloof, wise, and quick to detect insincerity. They use nonlethal Weakening Breath to subdue, fight with Frightful Presence and melee when needed, and reserve devastating Fire Breath for clearly evil foes. Their unique lair actions (Glimpse of the Future, Banishment) don't follow the standard metallic pattern.

---

## Gold-Specific Traits
- **Swim speed** — alternative movement
- **Fire Breath** (damaging, cone) + **Weakening Breath** (nonlethal, cone — imposes STR-based damage penalty)
- **Social skills:** conversation and bargaining, but **quick to detect insincerity and mixed motives**
- **Personality:** Aloof, a bit stuck-up, supremely wise. Young golds are aloof and snobbish
- Adult and ancient gold dragons can **Change Shape**
- **Highest CR** among metallic dragons at every age

---

## By Age

### Wyrmling
- Hides and observes watchfully — won't flee if approached unless PCs seem belligerent
- If attacked: **Weakening Breath once** → fly/swim away at max speed
- Only uses Fire Breath if already damaged; only Bites if cornered

### Young
- Aloof and stuck-up — won't initiate social interaction unless approached with nonhostile intentions
- Quick to pick up on insincerity and mixed motives
- If encounter turns hostile: **flies away immediately** — no hesitation
- Uses Weakening Breath as sucker punch before retreating

### Adult (Legendary)
- Would rather talk, but **will start a fight** if PCs seem to have evil intent
- Opens with nonlethal means: Glimpse of the Future (lair action) + Weakening Breath
- Uses **Multiattack (Frightful Presence + Claw/Claw/Bite) before Fire Breath**
- Fire Breath is a last-ditch trump card

#### Lair Actions — Unique Pattern (Not Cloud/Push)

| Action | Effect | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| **Glimpse of the Future** | Advantage on attack rolls, ability checks, and saving throws for the entire round | Default while no blood drawn |
| **Banishment** | Like the spell for one round — suspends one creature's fighting privileges | After blood drawn — remove **most threatening opponent** |

##### Fizban's New Lair Actions

| Action | Type | Best Against |
|---|---|---|
| **Divine Word** | Debilitator (powerful single-target) | Most dangerous enemy |
| **Golden Radiance** | Direct damage + healing (AoE) | Damages enemies while healing allies |
| **Suffocating Wealth** | Movement restrictor + debilitator (single target) | Greedy or treasure-focused enemies |

### Ancient
- Same tactics as adult; **Change Shape** available
- Among the six highest-CR creatures in 5e (tied with ancient red)

---

## Combat Personality

> [!important] Wise and Perceptive
> Gold dragons detect insincerity and mixed motives quickly. PCs trying to bluff a gold dragon face a very high bar.

> [!important] Retreat at ≤70% hp
> Unless defending their lair — Disengage → fly/swim away at max speed.

---

## Quick Reference: Combat Flow
1. **Pre-combat:** Observes from hiding or in disguise — assesses PCs' alignment and intentions
2. **No blood drawn:** Glimpse of the Future (lair) → Weakening Breath
3. **Blood drawn:** Switch lair action to **Banishment** on most threatening opponent
4. **If PCs persist:** Frightful Presence + Claw/Claw/Bite
5. **Last resort:** Fire Breath (cone)
6. **≤70% hp:** Disengage → fly/swim away
7. **In disguise:** Talks way out → Change Shape to escape

> [!tip] DM Note
> Gold dragons are the "final boss" of metallic dragons. Banishment removes one PC from the fight every round. Alternate Glimpse of the Future (advantage on everything) and Banishment (remove biggest threat). This makes gold dragon lairs extremely dangerous.
