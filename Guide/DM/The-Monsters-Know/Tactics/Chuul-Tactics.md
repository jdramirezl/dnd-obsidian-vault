# Chuul Tactics

> [!summary]
> Chuuls are enormous semi-uplifted crayfish — aboleth servitors that guard ancient sites and are drawn to magic. They're brute fighters that grapple with pincers, attempt to paralyze with tentacles, and get stuck in a loop of trying to poison resistant targets. They flee toward water when seriously hurt.

---

## Stat Block Profile
- **Type:** Aberration, Large
- **Exceptionally strong and tough** — brute fighter
- **INT 5** — operates strictly by instinct
- **AC 16** (natural chitin), darkvision 60 ft
- **Sense Magic:** 120 ft — drawn to spellcasters and magic items
- Highly proficient in Perception
- **10 ft reach** on Pincer attacks — stops moving at 10 ft from target

---

## Behavior
- Guards locations instinctually (not consciously) for ancient aboleth masters
- Drawn to magic — Sense Magic (120 ft) detects spellcasters/magic items before darkvision (60 ft)
- Indiscriminate in target selection, but magic-users and magic items catch attention first
- Amphibious — active on land only at night, otherwise underground or underwater

---

## Multiattack Sequence
- 2 Pincer attacks + 1 Tentacle attack (if creature grappled)
- Pincer hit = **automatic grapple** (target can try to escape on their turn)
- Tentacle = **no damage**, but injects paralyzing poison (DC 13 CON save)
- Paralysis: auto-fail STR/DEX saves, advantage on attacks against, auto-crits within 5 ft

### Attack Order Logic
1. **Pincer** → miss? → another **Pincer**
2. **Pincer** → hit (grapple)? → **Tentacle** (attempt poison)
3. **Tentacle** → poison fails? → next **Pincer** targets a **different creature** (try to grapple a second victim)
4. **Tentacle** → poison succeeds? → keep Pincer-attacking the **poisoned victim** (advantage + auto-crits)

### The Loop Problem
- If a grappled target resists poison, the chuul keeps trying Tentacle every round
- Too dumb (INT 5) to realize poison won't work on a resistant target
- Gets stuck: Tentacle on resistant grappled target, Pincers on others
- Meanwhile, grappled-but-not-restrained target can attack the chuul without penalty

---

## Retreat
- **Seriously injured (≤37 hp)** → flight instinct kicks in
- Too dumb to Disengage → uses **Dodge** while retreating toward nearest water
- Switches to **Dash** once swimming
- PCs without swim speed can't pursue effectively

---

## DM Encounter Tips

> [!tip] Sense Magic Inverts Expectations
> A Goliath fighter in full plate 65 ft away is invisible to Sense Magic, but a rogue sneaking in a Cloak of Elvenkind is lit up like a Christmas tree.

- 10 ft reach means the chuul stops at 10 ft — doesn't close to 5 ft
- Paralysis is devastating but short-lived (DC 13 CON save each turn to end)
- Chuuls may drag paralyzed victims into water to drown them (like crocodiles)
- Natural pairing with aboleths — chuuls are their servitors
- Multiple chuuls don't coordinate (no Pack Tactics, no social behavior)
