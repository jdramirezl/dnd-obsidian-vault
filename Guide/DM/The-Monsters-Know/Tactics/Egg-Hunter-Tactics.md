# Egg Hunter Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/egg-hunter-tactics/) | **Date**: September 20, 2022
> **Book**: Fizban's Treasury of Dragons

---

## Overview

- Parasites that feed on dragon embryos and lay eggs in emptied shells. Dragons will abandon ALL other threats to destroy an egg hunter on sight.
- **Multilateral combat catalyst**: PCs can punch above their weight against a dragon distracted by egg hunters. But egg hunters aren't friendly to PCs either.

## Egg Hunter Hatchling

- **Very high Dex, above-average Con, rock-bottom Int**. Pure instinct, two modes: "Get 'em!" and "Run!"
- Immune to frightened. Frightful Presence has no effect.
- **Hatching sequence**: Turn 1 — break into dragon egg (Egg Tooth easily smashes shell). Turn 2 — Multiattack the proto-wyrmling inside (almost certainly kills it). Turn 3 — devour.
- **If intervened upon by Small/Medium humanoid**: Bites back until seriously wounded (≤11 hp) or engaged by Large+ creature.
- **Rapid Movement (bonus action)**: Instinctively Disengages from any hostile creature within 15 ft (not just 5 ft — adult dragons have 15-ft reach). Wastes Disengage on enemies 10–15 ft away even if their reach is only 5 ft.

### Fleeing Priority
1. **Burrow** (unless solid rock) — squiggly path evades breath weapons (don't spread around corners). Only 3/10 common dragons can burrow.
2. **Swim** (if no burrowing possible) — only 4/10 common dragons can swim.
3. **Climb** into a crack (not smooth surfaces — no Spider Climb).
4. **Open terrain** — last resort.

## Egg Hunter Adult

- **Int 3** (dog/cat grade) but better instincts. Extraordinary Dex, very high Con, high Str, above-average Wis. Stealth +11, Perception proficiency.
- **Goal**: Sneak into dragon dens (ideally when dragon is out), deposit eggs, slink away. Conflict-averse — fighting gets in the way.
- **False Appearance**: Dragons have disadvantage to recognize stationary egg hunters. Once in the clutch, doesn't move until eggs are laid. Waits for dragon to leave before departing.

### Three Encounter Scenarios (Most to Least Likely)
1. **Lurking egg hunter discovered** by creatures rummaging through nest while dragon is absent/dead. Most probable.
2. **Egg hunter sneaking out** when interlopers arrive and distract the dragon. Less likely but more chaotic.
3. **Egg hunter sneaking in** during a fight. Risky (dragons have Detect legendary action). Generally contrary to behavior.

### Defenses
- **Torpor Spores**: AoE that compromises foes' ability to harm it.
- **Barbed Proboscis**: Nasty piercing + hp drain.
- **Rapid Adaptation (reaction)**: Halves the damage of the triggering attack (functions like Uncanny Dodge, not permanent resistance to a damage type).

### Combat Behavior
- Can't tell if a creature has seen it. If any creature gets within 5 ft: releases Torpor Spores, stabs twice with Barbed Proboscis, then skedaddles at full speed (Dashing on subsequent turns).
- Climbs walls to find cracks. Walking/climbing speed 40 ft.
- **Turns to fight** only if stronger than pursuer(s), a good distance from its eggs, and only until seriously wounded (≤20 hp).
- **Fight thresholds**: Stands against 1 PC up to level 11, 2 at level 8, 3 at level 6, 4 at level 5. Intuitive, not calculated.

## DM Tips

> [!tip] Running Egg Hunters
> The best encounter: PCs discover egg hunters while looting a dragon's hoard. Even better: the dragon comes back WHILE PCs are fighting the escaping egg hunter. If PCs finish off the egg hunter before threatening the dragon, it colors the relationship in a fascinating new way. Five hatchlings ≈ one adult in difficulty.
