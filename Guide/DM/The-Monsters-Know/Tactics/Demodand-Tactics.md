# Demodand Tactics

## Overview

Fiends native to the prison plane of Carceri. Unlike other fiends, a demodand destroyed outside Carceri re-forms there in 2d20 days via a torturous process — making them **far more willing to negotiate** when seriously wounded than any other fiend type.

## Farastu Demodand (CR 7)

### Tactical Summary
Fast-moving hunter and ambush predator (40 ft. speed, Perception/Stealth/Survival). 120 ft. darkvision — most active at night. Spider Climb lets it lurk on ceilings and in trees. Brute stats (extraordinary STR, very high CON). No ranged attack. INT 8 — relies on simple tricks: track, ambush, grab, drag away.

### Combat Behavior
1. Hides using at-will *invisibility* while lying in wait
2. Ambushes with **Claw/Claw/Bite** Multiattack (Claw first — successful hit grapples; Bite is damage only)
3. Grappled creatures have **disadvantage on escape** (tarry adhesive)
4. Casts *fog cloud* to haul victim away without opportunity attacks
5. Uses *invisibility* to vanish temporarily if foes block access to desired target
6. *Dispel magic* opportunistically vs. buffs/debuffs it can't ignore

### Summon Demodand
- **Don't roll.** 40% success is a pointless complication. Script the encounter — add a second farastu when narratively appropriate (e.g., when the first is dragging its victim away and needs to delay pursuers).

### Retreat: Seriously wounded (≤78 hp) → ceases hostilities, tries to make a deal using high CHA.

## Kelubar Demodand (CR 11)

### Tactical Summary
60 ft. flying speed (moves in flying leaps). Extraordinary STR, exceptional CON. Proficient in Insight. **Acidic Secretions** punish melee attackers — it *wants* enemies to engage in melee. Spit Acid (60 ft. line) for ranged opponents.

### Acid Fog (Recharge 6 — likely once/encounter)
The kelubar's signature move. 20 ft. radius sphere of heavily obscured, difficult terrain that poisons creatures who start their turn in it.

**Key tactic:** Drop Acid Fog on the enemy formation, then leap into the cloud. The kelubar is immune to poisoned condition and ignores difficult terrain (Boundless Movement).
- Heavy obscurity nullifies advantage/disadvantage on attack rolls for everyone inside
- But the kelubar's **Magic Resistance** (advantage on spell saves) is NOT suppressed
- Enemies who flee the cloud remain **poisoned** for the rest of that turn AND the next
- The kelubar pursues a fleeing enemy out of the cloud — now it can see, the enemy is poisoned (disadvantage), and allies are poorly positioned to help

### Spit Acid
- 60 ft. line — expect 2 targets; use vs. ranged opponents who refuse to close

### Retreat: Seriously wounded (≤74 hp) → may negotiate or cast *invisibility* and flee.

## Shator Demodand (CR 16)

### Tactical Summary
Same movement as kelubar (60 ft. fly). Brute stats with INT 21 and WIS 17 — pinpoint accuracy in identifying weaknesses. 120 ft. truesight. Perception/Stealth proficiencies. **Numbing Secretions** punish melee engagement — baiting foes into melee is a core strategy.

### Combat Behavior
- **Multiattack:** Bite (save-vs.-paralysis rider) then 2× Enervating Trident (no rider) — Bite first is preferred
- **Inhibitory Spray** (recharge 5–6, 100 ft. line): Imposes **paralyzed** on failed DC 16 DEX save. Best vs. front-line warriors and close-support casters (skip bards/monks/rangers/rogues — high DEX). Maneuver to hit from the side. Even 2 paralyzed targets is worth it for auto-crit melee follow-up
- At-will *invisibility* and *suggestion* (both concentration — one at a time)
- *Suggestion*: get a low-WIS opponent (mod ≤+3) to remove themselves from the fight
- INT 21: can identify weaknesses as if reading character sheets

### Jailer & Liquefaction Ritual
- Both take 1 minute — not combat actions, but encounter objectives ("stop the shator")
- Liquefaction Ritual implies throwing glass flasks to release additional demodands — more fun than Summon Demodand
- *Imprisonment* via Jailer: effective vs. targets with WIS mod ≤+3. If target has high WIS AND high CHA, render unconscious first. If target has high WIS, use *plane shift* to Carceri instead

### Retreat: Seriously wounded (≤78 hp) → calls time-out, negotiates. If refused, gathers demodand posse, casts *plane shift* back to Carceri to regroup.

> [!tip] DM Tips
> - Demodands are uniquely willing to negotiate among fiends — use this for roleplay
> - Farastus are abductors, not killers — the encounter is about rescue, not a slugfest
> - Kelubar Acid Fog + leap-in is a devastating combo — walk through it step by step
> - Shator Liquefaction flasks as environmental hazards ("don't break the jars!") make great encounter design
> - Don't roll Summon Demodand — script reinforcements for dramatic timing
