# Cave Fisher Tactics

> [!summary]
> The cave fisher is a human-sized arachnid ambush predator that lurks in lightless caves and reels in prey with an extremely sticky filament. It's dumb but has one excellent trick. Best position: on a 60-ft ceiling, yanking victims up where a fall means 6d6 damage.

---

## Stat Block Profile
- **Type:** Monstrosity, Medium
- **Very high STR, high CON** — brute
- **INT 3**, WIS 10
- **Spider Climb**, double Stealth proficiency
- **Adhesive Filament:** 60 ft range, 200 lb weight limit
- **Flammable Blood:** vulnerable to fire below half HP (no tactical impact)

---

## Optimal Positioning
1. **Best:** ceiling 60–65 ft up — beyond darkvision, devastating fall if prey escapes
2. **Good:** ground level 60 ft from traffic — beyond darkvision detection
3. **Acceptable:** high ledge with cover from below
- Prefers distance over height (distance defeats darkvision)

---

## Combat Sequence
1. Extends **Adhesive Filament** (action) toward prey lane
2. Prey touches filament → adhered
3. Next turn: **Filament action** → reels creature up
4. **Multiattack: Claws** until target is **dead** (wants to eat, not just KO)
5. Target breaks free and falls → extends new filament, retrieves (two-turn process)

### Important
- Extending filament = one action; reeling in = another action
- **Cannot extend and reel on the same turn**

---

## Target Selection
- Judges by **size** — smallest/weakest-looking
- Can feel mass from tug — won't try to reel in heavy/armored targets (>200 lb)
- Doesn't need isolated targets — filament *creates* isolation

---

## Retreat
- **Moderately wounded (≤40 hp)** → retreats using **Dodge**
- Charged by multiple foes → goes **straight up the wall** to ceiling
- Solitary by nature; multiple cave fishers fight over caught prey

---

## DM Encounter Tips

> [!tip] Ceiling Ambush
> Hiding on a 60-ft ceiling and yanking prey up is the dirtiest trick. If they break free: 6d6 fall damage, nowhere to go but down.

- Single cave fisher = combat encounter; multiple = obstacle or comedy
- Flying/levitating creatures are nearly immune
- Flammable Blood is irrelevant to tactics — ignore it
