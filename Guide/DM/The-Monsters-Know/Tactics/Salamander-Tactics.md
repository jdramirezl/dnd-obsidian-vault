# Salamander Tactics

## Salamander (CR 5)

### Tactical Summary

Fiery shock troops — exceptional STR, high DEX and CON. Favor melee over ranged (much more damage up close). Immune to fire, vulnerable to cold, resistant to nonmagical physical damage. Choleric temperament — caution around magic weapons and frost spells manifests as focused aggression, not avoidance. Speak Ignan.

### Weapon Use

- Spear wielded **two-handed** (no shield, no way to carry a second weapon)
- Will not throw spear — loses only weapon

### Attack Sequence

1. Close quickly with foes
2. **Tail first** — successful hit grapples + restrains → advantage on follow-up spear
3. Grappled target takes **automatic tail hits** on subsequent turns
4. **Heated Body**: creature touching salamander or hitting it in melee takes fire damage (once per turn)

### Heated Body Timing

- Start of grappled creature's turn: burned
- Touch or melee hit while within 5 ft: burned
- Both in same turn: **only once** (once-per-turn effect)

### Targeting Magic Weapons / Frost Spells

- Notices magic weapons and ice spells → calls out to allies in Ignan
- Charges spellslingers and ranged magic weapon users → tail grapple
- Magic melee weapon wielder: uses **Disarm** action (DMG p.271) — advantage if one-handed weapon
- After disarm: smacks weapon out of reach with tail (free object interaction)
- Ally may rush in to grab or hurl away the weapon (especially *frost brand*)

### Retreat

- Seriously wounded (≤36 hp): Dodge while retreating at full speed
- Not smart enough to Disengage, not dim enough to just flee

## Fire Snake (CR 1)

### Tactical Summary

Juvenile salamander. Same immunities/vulnerabilities/resistances. Not strong, tough, or bright. Fights by instinct — hit-and-run only.

### With Adult Salamanders

- Nip at heels of adults' opponents (Flanking)
- If targeted: Dash away (ignoring opportunity attacks)
- Returns when opponent's attention is on an adult again
- Seriously wounded (≤8 hp): flees permanently

### Without Adults

- Attack willy-nilly: half movement to close → Multiattack → half movement to back away
- No regard for opportunity attacks (Heated Body punishes melee OAs anyway)

> [!tip] DM Tips
> - Disarm action is brilliant for salamanders — gives a way to neutralize magic weapons
> - Salamanders are reluctant servants of efreets — on the material plane against their will
> - Fire snakes as flanking support make salamander encounters much more dynamic
