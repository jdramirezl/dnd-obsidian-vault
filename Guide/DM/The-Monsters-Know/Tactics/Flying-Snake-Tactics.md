# Flying Snake Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/flying-snake-tactics/) | **Date**: February 26, 2018

---

## Tactical Summary

- **Unaligned, solitary beast** — the cute wild version of a couatl. Very low Str, exceptional Dex, average Con, only 5 hp.
- **Avoids combat by default**. No evil intent. Predominantly solitary (no "Swarm of Flying Snakes" entry).
- **Shock striker**: One bite (1 piercing + 3d4 poison), then fly away. Legitimate threat to entry-level adventurers.
- **Flyby**: No opportunity attacks when flying out of reach.
- **Swimming speed** but not amphibious — slithers across water surface.

## Combat Behavior

**Wild / self-controlled**:
- Attacks only if surprised or nest of eggs is threatened.
- One hit → fly away at full speed. Doesn't wait to be injured.
- If guarding eggs: flees after taking 3–4 hp damage (any more kills it).

**Controlled by a more powerful being**:
- Hold station 20–30 ft in the air between attacks.
- Fly down, bite, fly back up (Flyby prevents OAs).
- Functions as aerial ranged weapon attack.
- Attacks until controller says stop — no survival instincts apply.

## Retreat Conditions

- **Wild**: Flees after one strike, or before striking at all. If guarding eggs, flees at 3–4 hp damage.
- **Controlled**: No retreat — fights until ordered to stop.

## DM Tips

> [!tip] Running This Monster
> A flock implies control by something with reptile affinity (yuan-ti, naga). Solo, they're a one-round hazard — bite and gone. The 3d4 poison on 1 piercing makes them a nasty surprise for level 1 parties.
