# Sea Spawn Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/sea-spawn-tactics/) | **Category**: Humanoids

---

## Tactical Summary
- Humanoids "lost to the sea" — transformed by maritime taboos or powerful underwater denizens; survive out of water for no more than a day
- Strength/Constitution peak — straightforward melee fighters; mix Piscine Anatomy types for variety
- 30 ft swim / 20 ft land speed — strong incentive to fight in water; 120-ft darkvision means attack at night or deep water
- Multiattack: two Unarmed Strikes + one Piscine Anatomy attack
- Intelligence 6 — one approach, mechanistically followed whether working or not

## Combat Behavior
- **Preferred terrain**: Water (2x speed advantage) or darkness
- **Piscine Anatomy options**:
  - *Bite*: Straight piercing damage, least interesting
  - *Poison Quills*: Poison damage + poisoned condition (disadvantage on attacks/checks); DC easy to beat
  - *Tentacle*: Most damage, 10-ft reach, grapples on hit — but Unarmed Strikes only 5-ft reach, so must close in for full Multiattack
- **Tentacle variant**: Waits for fleeing opponents to move beyond 10 ft, then lashes out with Tentacle (5.5 avg vs. 4.5 Unarmed Strike)
- **Mixed anatomy**: Can combine traits per turn — Poison Quills while target poisoned, Tentacle when foes retreat
- **Target selection**: Nearest humanoid not yet clawed; once all nearby clawed, Dash away seeking new targets
- Can instinctively drag targets into water with tentacles (not Intelligence-based)

## Retreat Conditions
- Seriously injured (≤12 hp) — Dashes away, diving into water at earliest opportunity
- Not smart enough to Disengage; Dodging insufficient with low AC
