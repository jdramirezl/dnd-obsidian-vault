# Merrenoloth Tactics

## Tactical Summary

CR 3 yugoloth. Lowest-level in MToF but unique: has lair actions with regional effects. Normally pilots ferryboats on the River Styx (Charon is head of their guild). Can be hired to captain vessels on other planes — the vessel becomes its lair.

Part spellslinger, part melee shock attacker. Wields an oversize oar (appears finesse). Has blindsight — critical for *darkness* strategy. **Teleport as a bonus action** (60 ft) — crucial extra mobility.

**Priority 1:** Protect its vessel. **Priority 2:** Protect itself. No third priority. Will never come to the aid of another creature.

## Lair Actions

- Restore HP to a damaged vessel
- Speed vessel away from/toward pursuers
- Interfere with flying attackers

Lair actions protect the *ship*, not the merrenoloth.

## Combat Behavior

### Opening Move

Cast **darkness** at the fore edge of the quarterdeck, engulfing most of the rear half of the ship. Merrenoloth has blindsight — enemies don't. Then **Teleport** (bonus action) into the sphere, or shift 10 ft within it to confuse attackers targeting its last known position.

### Standard Turn

1. **Oar attack** vs melee opponent (advantage — can sense them, they can't see it)
2. **Fear Gaze** — melee foes first, then ranged attackers
3. **Teleport** (bonus action) to another point in the *darkness* sphere

### Fear Gaze in Darkness

*Darkness* imposes disadvantage on attacks. Being frightened doesn't add more disadvantage (can't see the source), BUT foe can't willingly move closer to the merrenoloth — even though they can't see it. Fear Gaze is prophylaxis: keeps imposing disadvantage even if *darkness* is dispelled.

### Pièce de Résistance Combo

Watch for 4+ enemies lined up (with ≤1-2 friendly crew in the line):
1. Position (movement or Teleport)
2. **Gust of wind** — 60 ft × 10 ft. DC 13 (not great, but hits several)
3. If 4+ blown overboard: next turn → **control water** (Whirlpool) beneath them
4. Deck cleared → **strong wind lair action** to sail away

If concentration on *darkness* broken: recast immediately.

## Retreat Conditions

- **Ship seaworthy:** Fights to the death
- **Ship destroyed:** Released from contract. 40 ft swim speed — flees. Fear Gaze to dissuade swimmers, then Dash
- **Fight clearly hopeless:** Promptly parleys. Will bargain or buy its way out

> [!tip] DM Tip
> A merrenoloth's ship never sinks, even if hull is breached. It would rather not fight — it just wants to do its job. Build encounters around boarding actions where it defends helm and mainmast. On the River Styx, *gust of wind* knocking PCs overboard means Feeblemind-equivalent effects from touching the water.
