# Elder Elemental Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/elder-elemental-tactics/) | **Date**: November 8, 2018
> **Book**: Mordenkainen's Tome of Foes

---

## Shared Traits

- **Intelligence 2**: Bare-minimum sentience. Intuition without thinking, awareness without adaptability.
- Cannot distinguish individual PCs from one another. Acts according to nature.
- Proficiency in Wis/Cha saves. Resistance to nonmagical physical damage.
- Immune to poison, exhaustion, paralysis, petrification, poisoned, stunned.
- **Siege Monster**: Destroys cover before destroying you.
- **Legendary Resistance**: Primarily to avoid debilitating conditions, secondarily to avoid damage.
- **Neutral alignment**: Default attitude is indifference. Won't attack intentionally unless provoked.
- **No elder elemental ever retreats**. Being destroyed is ultimately a kind of freedom.

## Leviathan (CR 20, Water)

- **Melee brute** (Str/Con peak). Charges enemies, tries to stay on top of them (Water Form shares space).
- **Tidal Wave**: Hugely powerful (66 avg damage per target, persists 5 rounds). Use immediately on round 1 — elder elementals are creatures of impulse. Recharges on 6 only.
- **Siege Monster exception**: Tidal Wave damages objects/structures too — double damage to ships and coastal fortresses.
- **Subsequent rounds**: Tidal Wave again if recharged (from on top of foes). Otherwise, indiscriminate Multiattack.
- **Legendary Slam**: Roll d4/d6 per turn — on a 1, it Slams (caprice). Move legendary action to pursue if last enemy moved out of 20-ft reach.
- **Legendary Resistance**: vs being blinded, any cold damage, or ≥99 damage from single source.

## Phoenix (CR 16, Fire)

- **Skirmisher** (Dex/Con peak). Flyby — no opportunity attacks when flying out of reach.
- **Default behavior**: Stay 60 ft away, swoop in to Multiattack from target's space (Fire Form: auto 1d10 fire on entry + 1d10 if struck in melee), fly back out.
- **Fire Form**: Ignites flammable objects. Siege Monster doubles structural damage.
- **Legendary Move**: Stay out of melee reach. Peck/Swoop only vs inanimate structures or targets who've already used their actions.
- **Legendary Resistance**: vs being blinded or ≥53 damage from single source.

## Elder Tempest (CR 23, Air)

- **Single Dex peak**. No ranged attacks — relies on high-damage shock attacks + Flyby.
- **Multiattack**: Both attacks against same target to maximize damage.
- **Priority order**:
  1. Demolish structures sheltering ranged attackers from Living Storm winds
  2. Position within 120 ft of all opponents → Lightning Storm (recharge 6)
  3. Multiattack stunned targets (advantage) — unless Lightning Storm recharges immediately
  4. Demolish other cover
  5. Thunder-buffet non-stunned targets
- **Legendary Move**: Stay out of reach. Lightning Strike: roll a die for whimsy. Screaming Gale: only if ALL foes in 20-ft horizontal band.
- **Legendary Resistance**: vs being blinded or ≥80 damage from single source.

## Zaratan (CR 22, Earth)

- **Brute** like leviathan. Approaches structures at 10 ft/turn, using Earth-Shaking Movement (bonus) + Spit Rock to demolish cover (doubled by Siege Monster).
- **Once cover destroyed**: Resumes full speed advance.
- **Spew Debris (recharge 5–6)**: Doesn't affect objects — refrain until all cover is destroyed. Then use whenever available.
- **Default**: Indiscriminate Multiattack including against remaining cover.
- **Vulnerability to thunder**: Uses Legendary Resistance vs thunder damage. Reacts to thunder with Retract → Revitalize → Emerge sequence (2 legendary actions each, takes multiple turns).
- **While Retracted**: Restrained but can still act (with disadvantage). Can Spew Debris if available. Move/Stomp wasted.
- **After Emerge**: Spit Rock directly at whoever dealt thunder damage.
- **Legendary Stomp**: Default. Move if last opponent leaves reach. Spit only while demolishing structures.
- **Legendary Resistance**: vs blinded, prone, restrained, any thunder damage, or ≥93 other damage from single source.

## DM Tips

> [!tip] Running These Monsters
> Elder elementals are forces of nature, not tacticians. They act on impulse and temperament. Roll dice for their capricious legendary actions. They destroy cover first, then soft targets. None of them ever retreat — they have no attachment to the material plane.
