# Kobold Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/kobold-tactics/) | **Date**: August 15, 2016
> **Related Bestiary**: [[Kobolds]]

---

## Tactical Summary

- **Pack Tactics is everything**: Kobolds gain advantage on attacks when an ally is within 5 ft of the target. They never fight alone — swarming is the entire strategy.
- **Ambush with slings at 20–30 ft** to soften targets, then surge into melee when the advantage shifts.
- **Sunlight Sensitivity is absolute**: Kobolds will not pursue enemies into bright light, period. They must fight in darkness or underground.
- **Low individual damage (avg 4 hp/hit)** means every attack must count — Pack Tactics against AC 15 still lands ~66% of the time.
- **Numerical superiority required**: If kobolds no longer outnumber frontline PCs at least 3-to-1, they withdraw.

## Combat Behavior

**Opening**: Hidden kobolds (decent Dex, dark environment) ambush with sling stones from 20–30 ft. This continues until PCs close the gap or kobolds lose their positional advantage.

**Melee phase**: Kobolds surge forward and swarm, always ensuring at least one ally is adjacent to each target for Pack Tactics advantage. No kobold ever fights a foe hand-to-hand alone.

**Winged kobolds**: Extend the ranged phase by swooping down to grab rocks, flying back up, and throwing them. Limited by rock availability — if PCs block access to ammunition, the tactic collapses. Only 2 hp more than regular kobolds (9 vs 7 total).

## Key Decision Points

- **Ally adjacent to target?** → Attack with Pack Tactics advantage. If not, regroup with other kobolds fighting a different foe.
- **Outnumber frontline PCs 3:1?** → Continue fighting. Below that ratio → withdraw immediately.
- **Bright sunlight?** → Will not pursue under any circumstances. Enemies who retreat into light are safe.
- **Only kobold fighting a foe?** → Retreat and regroup with other kobolds on a different target.

## Retreat Conditions

- **1–2 hp remaining**: Turns and runs using Dash (not smart enough to Disengage — eats the opportunity attack).
- **Numerical advantage lost** (below 3:1 vs frontline PCs): Full withdrawal.
- **Retreating kobolds don't switch to ranged**: Sling range is too short to prevent PCs from closing again.
- **If they win**: Kobolds take prisoners for entertainment and taunting, not killing.

## DM Tips

> [!tip] Running This Monster
> Kobolds are simple to run: swarm with Pack Tactics, withdraw when outnumbered. The key constraint is Sunlight Sensitivity — design encounters in darkness or underground, and remember that kobolds will never chase PCs into daylight. Their simplicity makes them great for teaching players about tactical positioning.
