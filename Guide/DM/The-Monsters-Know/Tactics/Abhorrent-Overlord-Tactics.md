# Abhorrent Overlord Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/abhorrent-overlord-tactics/)

---

## Tactical Summary
- Greedy, cruel Theran fiend that opens with devastating Storm of Crows AoE, then hunts escapees with Claws
- High stats across the board; prioritizes martial foes over casters due to Magic Resistance
- 60-ft fly speed; uses altitude (15–20 ft) to stay out of melee between turns
- Claws steal vitality on hit, incentivizing attacks against low-AC targets for reliable healing
- Spellcasting (*confusion*, *crown of madness*, *suggestion*) is secondary to Storm of Crows and Claws

## Combat Behavior
- **Opening**: Storm of Crows (recharge 6, concentration) on 4+ grouped enemies. Delays if enemies are too spread out
- **Round 2+**: Claws against enemies who escaped the storm. Targets monks, rogues, valor bards first, then medium-armor fighters, saving heavy armor and casters for last
- **After Storm ends**: With minions, casts *confusion* on 2+ foes or *crown of madness* on low-Wis target. Without minions, retreats through a choke point and Readies *confusion*
- **Altitude**: Flies 15–20 ft up after knocking a foe unconscious

## Key Decision Points
- Storm of Crows is almost always the opener—only delay if enemies are too spread for the 20-ft radius
- Switch to spells only after Storm ends and all enemies have escaped it
- Target priority is reversed from typical: low-AC martial > heavy martial > casters (Magic Resistance)

## Retreat Conditions
- Flies away using Dash (120 ft/round) when seriously wounded (≤54 hp)
- Never willingly enters a fight below Deadly/High Difficulty

> [!tip] Running This Monster
> Open with Storm of Crows to split the party into "stuck in the storm" and "escaped but hunted"—two simultaneous problems with limited actions to solve them.
