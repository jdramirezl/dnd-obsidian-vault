# Hoard Scarab and Hoard Mimic Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/hoard-scarab-and-hoard-mimic-tactics/) | **Date**: September 9, 2022

---

## Hoard Scarab

- Teeny shock attacker (very high DEX). No tactical flexibility. Has tremorsense, burrow, and fly.
- **Best play**: Hides in the *middle* of a treasure pile. Bites whoever sticks their hand in — gains surprise + advantage (total cover).
- Successful Bite imposes disadvantage on target's attack rolls until start of the *target's* next turn (effectively only reactions/OAs).
- **If cover swept away**: Launches airborne, releases Scale Dust (bonus action), Bites nearest creature, flies up out of melee reach. Later turns: fly down, attack, fly back up.
- **If latched on**: Target may waste an action trying to fling it (STR Athletics contest). Scarab releases Scale Dust next turn. Keeps attacking that target unless another opponent lands a hit.
- One hit likely kills it. Seriously wounded (1–2 hp) → flies away using Dash.

## Swarm of Hoard Scarabs

- Must occupy same space as target. Can't hide in a pile — whole swarm descends on whoever disturbs it. Dispersed at ≤12 hp.

## Hoard Mimic

- **Huge** brute (extraordinary STR, very high CON). Disguises itself as the biggest pile of treasure anyone's ever seen — a decoy from the dragon's actual hoard.
- Can communicate verbally and telepathically. Proficiency in Persuasion.
- **If dragon is fighting nearby**: Stays out of the fight, waiting until the dragon weakens foes. Jumps in only if the dragon is significantly overmatched.
- **Opening**: Caustic Mist (recharge) if ≥3 enemies in a 30-ft cone. Uses again whenever it recharges.
- **Multiattack**: Pseudopods first (grapple/restrain chance), then Bite (5-ft reach — may need to close on grappled target).
- Never uses Shapechanger during combat.
- **Retreat**: Moderately wounded (≤86 hp) and opponents aren't equally hurt, or seriously wounded (≤49 hp) and opponents aren't seriously hurt → stops fighting and **negotiates**, using Persuasion to plead for its life. Fights to the death only if convinced foes will kill it regardless.
