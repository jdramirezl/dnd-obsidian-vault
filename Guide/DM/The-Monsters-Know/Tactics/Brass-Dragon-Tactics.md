# Brass Dragon Tactics

> [!summary]
> Brass dragons are talkative, sociable good dragons who love conversation and bargaining. They lead with nonlethal Sleep Breath to subdue without injury, fight with melee only when cornered, and use damaging Fire Breath only as a last resort when it's clear their opponents are evil and won't be deterred.

---

## Metallic Dragon Shared Traits
- Same brute profile as chromatic dragons: high STR + CON, proficient in all "big three" saves + CHA
- Blindsight, darkvision, flying + one alternative movement mode
- **Two breath weapons:** one damaging, one nonlethal (subdue without injury)
- **Social skill proficiencies** in addition to Perception + Stealth
- Adult/ancient: legendary actions (same set as chromatic), Legendary Resistance, Frightful Presence
- Adult/ancient: only **2 lair actions** (vs chromatic's 3)
- **Good-aligned** — encounters rarely begin with combat

## Brass-Specific Traits
- **Burrow speed** — alternative movement
- **Fire Breath** (damaging, line) + **Sleep Breath** (nonlethal, cone)
- **Social skills:** enjoy conversation, compliments, bargaining
- **Personality:** Prattling blabbermouths who love to talk. Young brass dragons are chatty and eager to engage
- Ancient brass dragons can **Change Shape**

---

## By Age

### Wyrmling
- Friendly and gentle, if aloof — may walk right up to adventurers and try talking in Draconic
- If attacked: **Sleep Breath once** to repel, then fly/burrow away at max speed
- Only uses Fire Breath if already damaged
- Only Bites if cornered

### Young
- Prattling blabbermouth — initiates social interaction, enjoys conversation and bargaining
- If encounter turns hostile: tries to **talk its way out** first
- If talk fails: **Sleep Breath** → flee
- Uses Fire Breath only if already hurt; melee only if cornered
- Repositions before breath weapons to catch max targets in cone AoE

### Adult (Legendary)
- Would rather talk than fight, but **will start a fight** if PCs seem to have evil intent
- Opens with nonlethal means: "cloud" lair action + Sleep Breath
- Uses **Multiattack (Frightful Presence + Claw/Claw/Bite) before Fire Breath** — Frightful Presence may keep opponents at bay
- Fire Breath is a **last-ditch trump card** when PCs are clearly evil and undeterred

#### Lair Actions (Pick 1 per round, never same twice)
| Action | Type | Best Against |
|---|---|---|
| **Cloud of Sand** | Cloud action (obscures vision) | Everyone — nonlethal opener |
| **Strong Wind** | Push action (no damage, inhibits movement) | Approaching melee fighters |

##### Fizban's New Lair Actions
| Action | Type | Best Against |
|---|---|---|
| **Animate Statues** | Direct damage (stationary) | Only useful if lair is full of statues or statues are the only cover |
| **Blast of Wind** | Upgraded push action (supersized AoE) | Approaching groups |
| **Obscuring Sand** | Upgraded cloud action (supersized AoE) | Everyone — better version of Cloud of Sand |

#### Legendary Actions (3/round)
| Action | Cost | Use When |
|---|---|---|
| **Detect** | 1 | Any stealthy movement within line of sight |
| **Tail Attack** | 1 | PC behind the dragon |
| **Wing Attack** | 2 | 3+ PCs within 10 ft — knocks prone + bonus fly to reposition |

### Ancient
- Same tactics as adult; gains **Change Shape**
- Prefers to go about in disguise outside its lair — blends in with surroundings
- If threatened in disguise: talks its way out, even surrenders to avoid revealing true nature
- Once eyes are off it: reverts to dragon form or assumes different form to escape

## Metallic Dragon Combat Personality

> [!important] Nonlethal First, Lethal Last
> All metallic dragons prefer nonlethal means first. They use Multiattack (with Frightful Presence) **before** damaging breath weapons, because Frightful Presence may deter opponents. Damaging breath is a last resort against clearly evil, undeterrable foes.

> [!important] Retreat Threshold
> Unless defending their lair, adult/ancient metallic dragons **retreat at ≤70% hp** — Disengage (action) → fly away at max speed (or burrow/swim/climb if attackers can't follow).

---

## Quick Reference: Combat Flow
1. **Pre-combat:** Social interaction — conversation, bargaining, compliments
2. **Hostile intent detected:** Cloud of Sand (lair action) → Sleep Breath (nonlethal)
3. **If PCs persist:** Frightful Presence + Claw/Claw/Bite Multiattack
4. **Last resort (clearly evil PCs):** Fire Breath
5. **≤70% hp:** Disengage → fly/burrow away at max speed
6. **Legendary Resistance:** use every failed save

> [!tip] DM Note
> Brass dragon encounters should start as social encounters. The dragon wants to talk, trade stories, and bargain. Combat only happens if PCs are hostile or clearly evil. Even then, the brass dragon tries nonlethal means first.
