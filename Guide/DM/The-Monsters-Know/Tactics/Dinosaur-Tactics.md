# Dinosaur Tactics

## Tactical Summary

Dinosaurs are dumb brutes — extraordinary STR/CON, rock-bottom INT. Two categories: **plant-eaters** (peaceful unless spooked) and **meat-eaters** (hunt anything smaller). All predators prefer the young, old, weak, sick, isolated, and oblivious.

## Plant-Eaters

### Hadrosaurus (CR 1/4, Large)
- Only defense: tail + 40 ft speed; **flees** using Dash; attacks only when cornered or ridden

### Ankylosaurus (CR 3, Huge)
- Armored, club tail, 30 ft speed; runs only from single predators its size or larger
- Smaller predators get the tail — never considers running even when seriously wounded

### Stegosaurus (CR 4, Huge)
- Plated back, spiked tail, 40 ft speed; won't yield to smaller creatures
- **Flees** (Dash) when seriously wounded (≤30 hp)

### Brontosaurus (CR 5, Gargantuan)
- Yields to none but T-Rex; **Stomp first** (knock prone) → **tail** on prone targets
- **Flees** (Dash) when seriously wounded (≤48 hp)

### Triceratops (CR 5, Huge)
- 50 ft speed; **Trampling Charge** → Gore + prone → **Stomp** on prone foe
- Runs enemy to enemy for repeated charges (ignores OAs) — only if enemies 20+ ft apart
- Clustered enemies: just Gores everything
- **Flees** (Dash) at ≤38 hp or upon seeing a T-Rex

## Meat-Eaters

### Dimetrodon (CR 1/4, Medium)
- Hunts rivers/coastlines; runs up and bites; **flees** at ≤13 hp

### Pteranodon (CR 1/4, Medium, Flying)
- Prefers fish — attacks Small creatures in water only
- **Flyby** + 60 ft fly → hover 30 ft, dive, fly back; **flees** at ≤9 hp

### Velociraptor (CR 1/4, Tiny)
- Hunts in flocks with **Pack Tactics**; won't attack without another velociraptor on same target
- **Flees** at ≤4 hp or when numerical advantage drops below 2:1

### Deinonychus (CR 1, Medium)
- Soloist; **Pounce** → prone → bonus bite → up to 2 claws + 2 bites/turn
- Assassin — aims for single shock kill; **flees** if prey deals ≥8 hp from one hit

### Allosaurus (CR 2, Large)
- Fast brute, same Pounce tactics; much tougher — **flees** only at ≤20 hp
- Always takes one more bite after knocking target prone; **Disengages** before retreating

### Plesiosaurus (CR 2, Large, Aquatic)
- Water ambush — Stealth proficiency, hides beneath surface
- At ≤47 hp: substitutes **grapple** for bite, drags prey underwater
- **Retreats** to deep water at ≤27 hp

### Quetzalcoatlus (CR 2, Huge, Flying)
- Targets Small creatures; **Dive Attack** (extra damage); Flyby dive-bomb
- **Flees** at ≤21 hp

### Tyrannosaurus Rex (CR 8, Huge)
- 50 ft speed; Multiattack on **two different targets**: bite (front) + tail (side/behind)
- Bite grapples and restrains; doesn't notice moderate wounds
- **Flees** (Dash) at ≤54 hp — carries restrained victim in jaws, biting as it runs

> [!tip] DM Tips
> - Most predators flee when wounded — realistic animal behavior
> - Plesiosaurus dragging a PC underwater is terrifying — maximize that drama
> - T-Rex Multiattack on two targets means positioning matters — you'd rather get the tail
> - Triceratops charging between enemies ignoring OAs is cinematic and effective
> - Velociraptors should always appear in packs — a lone one is just a cat with teeth
