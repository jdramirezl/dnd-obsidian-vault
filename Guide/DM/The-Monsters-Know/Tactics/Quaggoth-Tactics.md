## Tactical Summary

Savage, shaggy humanoids of the Underdark. High STR (17) and CON (16), low INT (6), moderate WIS (12). Darkvision 120 ft. Climbing speed 30 ft. Often enslaved by drow.

## Combat Behavior

- **Multiattack:** 2x Claw attacks per turn
- **Wounded Fury:** When reduced to ≤10 hp, gains advantage on attack rolls and deals extra 2d6 damage per hit. This makes wounded quaggoths *more* dangerous, not less
- **Climbing:** 30 ft climb speed allows ambush from ceilings and walls in Underdark caverns. Drop onto prey from above
- **Pack mentality:** Fight in groups. Swarm a single target when possible
- Low INT = no sophisticated tactics. Charge the nearest enemy and claw relentlessly
- Moderate WIS = basic survival instinct, can identify easy vs hard targets

## Retreat Conditions

- Wounded Fury incentivizes fighting to the death — they get *stronger* as they die
- Free quaggoths: may flee if badly outnumbered before Wounded Fury triggers
- Drow-enslaved quaggoths: fight to the death (no choice)
- Quaggoth thonot (psionic variant): fights to the death to protect the tribe

> [!tip] DM Tip
> Wounded Fury is the key feature. A quaggoth at 10 hp is scarier than one at full health. Don't let players assume low HP = safe. Use ceiling ambushes in Underdark settings — climbing speed is their real tactical edge.
