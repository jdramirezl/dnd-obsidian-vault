# Fastieth and Clawfoot Tactics

## Fastieth

### Tactical Summary

Fast skittish herbivorous dinosaur mount. Exceptional DEX, 50 ft speed, Quickness (Dodge as bonus action). INT 4 — smarter than a horse. Ideal Beast Master ranger companion (CR 1/4).

### Combat Behavior

- Only fights if cornered or attacked — never initiates
- Fleeing: Dash + Dodge same turn via Quickness
- Doesn't Disengage (lacks INT to understand OAs) — just Dashes
- Flees on 3+ damage in a single hit (unless controlled mount)

---

## Clawfoot

### Tactical Summary

Carnivorous pack-hunting dinosaur. DEX-primary, decent CON. Perception + Stealth proficiency = ambush attacker. 40 ft speed, Pounce, Pack Tactics, Multiattack. Great druid Wild Shape at level 8+ (CR 1).

### Combat Behavior

- **Optimal attack:** Target 25+ ft away with an ally adjacent → Pack Tactics + Pounce + full Multiattack
- **Attack order:** Claws first (Pounce → prone on failed save), then Bite ×2 with advantage. Pack Tactics more reliable than low Pounce DC
- **Pack hunting:** Targets weak/wounded/isolated prey. Eats kills on the spot, defends the meal

### Retreat Conditions

- Normal: Dashes at ≤13 hp
- Guarding meal: ≤7 hp

> [!tip] DM Tip
> Fastieths are flavor mounts for Talenta halfling encounters. Run 3–4 clawfoots together targeting an isolated PC — the Pounce → prone → advantage chain is their bread and butter.
