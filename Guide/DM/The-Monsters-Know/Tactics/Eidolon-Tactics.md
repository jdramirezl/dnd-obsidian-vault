# Eidolon Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/eidolon-tactics/) | **Date**: October 31, 2018
> **Book**: Mordenkainen's Tome of Foes

---

## Overview

- **Undead spirit honored by gods** for zealous devotion. Guards sacred places from defilers. Not necessarily evil — may even be good. Compulsion: protect.
- Can animate inanimate objects (sacred statues) to carry out its mission.

## Loose Eidolon (Spirit Form)

- Physical abilities all poor. Mental abilities robust — extraordinary Wis leads.
- **Massive condition immunities**: Can't be charmed, exhausted, frightened, grappled, paralyzed, petrified, poisoned, prone, or restrained. Vulnerable to: blinded, incapacitated, stunned.
- **Damage profile**: Resistant to acid, fire, lightning, thunder, nonmagical physical. Immune to cold, necrotic, poison. Vulnerable to: force and radiant (*sacred flame*, *magic missile*).
- Advantage on saves vs turning.
- **Only offensive ability**: Divine Dread — imposes frightened + must flee. DC not difficult; someone will make the save. Effect is temporary (1 min), success/expiration grants immunity. Only buys time to board a vessel.

## Animated Sacred Statue

- **Brute**: Extraordinary Str/Con, pitiful Dex. Retains all resistances/immunities but CAN be grappled, restrained, or knocked prone.
- **Slam/Slam Multiattack**: Avg 86 damage on two hits. Far superior to Rock (37 damage, ranged).
- **Rock attack**: Only to break a caster's concentration on a crucial spell. No other reason.
- Tactics are simply: "Rrrrahhhh, bash bash bash."

## Behavior

- **Never flees or retreats** while its protected place/thing is threatened, regardless of damage.
- Eidolon and statue have **separate hp pools**. When statue is destroyed, eidolon is forced out but retains its own hp.
- **After statue destroyed**: Virtually nothing it can do to opponents (especially if Divine Dread immunity has kicked in). Can only linger, impotently shouting in whatever language(s) it knew. The saddest combat dénouement of any published monster.

## Multiple Vessels

- Text implies multiple vessels are possible ("sacred vessels," "one of several statues"). Each additional vessel is like another CR 12 enemy. DM's discretion — dramatically increases difficulty.
- Three possible endings: PCs die, PCs leave voluntarily, or PCs destroy all vessels and desecrate the grounds while the eidolon watches in impotent rage-sadness.

## DM Tips

> [!tip] Running the Eidolon
> The eidolon itself is nearly unkillable but also nearly harmless without a vessel. The real fight is against the sacred statue. Consider multiple vessels in different rooms for a dungeon-crawl feel — the eidolon flees via incorporeal movement and re-animates in a new room. "This time it'll be your demise!" For higher-level parties, use an iron golem stat block for the final vessel.
