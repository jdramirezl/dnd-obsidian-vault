# Rust Monster Tactics

## Tactical Summary

Chitinous cave-dweller that eats ferrous metal. Not a fighter — wants to eat, not kill. Bold and instinct-driven but flees when threatened. Darkvision. CR 1/2 — negligible alone, encountered in nests. Outgrown by level 5+ parties.

## Detection and Approach

- Wanders aimlessly until detecting movement within 60 ft
- **Iron Scent** (30 ft): pinpoints ferrous metal by scent → moves directly toward **largest concentration of iron**

## Iron Points (FeP) System

Calculate per PC to determine targeting:
- Medium/heavy armor: AC − 12 FeP
- All-metal weapon: 2 FeP
- Wood-hafted weapon/tool: 1 FeP
- Shield: 1 FeP
- Quiver of 10+ arrows/bolts: 1 FeP

## Combat Behavior

- **Antennae** (primary): corrodes nonmagical ferrous metal within 5 ft — must see target
- **Bite** only when cornered (all escape routes blocked)
- Not aggressive — "moves toward" and waggles antennae; won't attack PCs, only their iron belongings

## Iron Scent vs Invisibility

- Can **smell** iron on invisible creatures but **can't use Antennae** (requires sight)
- Result: profoundly confused rust monster waving antennae at nothing

## Flee Threshold

- Flees (Dash) when damage taken ≥ target's FeP value
- Not nimble enough to Dodge, not smart enough to Disengage

## Group Behavior

- All target the **same PC** (highest FeP) — crowd around eagerly
- Overflow to second-best only when 6+ surround one PC
- Dropping iron items **attracts** them (like feeding raccoons) — need ~10 FeP to satiate

> [!tip] DM Tips
> - Don't describe as hostile — curious, even friendly-seeming, until they start corroding gear
> - Nighttime camp: rust monsters wander toward doffed armor while PCs sleep
> - Can't affect magical metal — Iron Scent detects but Antennae won't work
