# Hags Revisited, Part 1

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/hags-revisited-part-1/) | **Date**: March 15, 2017
> **Book**: Volo's Guide to Monsters

---

## Grandmother Lair Actions (All Types)

All grandmothers share two lair actions:
1. **Pass through solid walls**, doors, ceilings, floors.
2. **Open/close/magically lock doors and windows** at will. Use to trap enemies, separate pursuers, or cover escape.

### Sea Hag Grandmother

- **Simulacrum**: Seaweed doppelgänger of a non-frightened opponent (lasts 1 round). Target glass cannons. Or use as interference while fleeing.
- **Ink cloud** (underwater only): Obscures area she can see through. Useless in currents. Can cover escape if her turn is before initiative 10.

### Green Hag Grandmother

- **Illusory duplicate**: Can't take actions/reactions but CAN interact with physical objects. Must stay in hag's line of sight. Dispelled by 1 damage. Very situational — maybe misdirect pursuers momentarily.

### Night Hag Grandmother

- **Banish**: Send one creature to prison demiplane for 1 round. Target the opponent with best action economy. Use in round 2+ (needs 1 round to assess).
- **Fling**: Throw up to 3 creatures (Con save). Scatter melee opponents, finish fragile foes. Skip dwarves/barbarians. Fling out windows when feasible.
- Night hags are most likely encountered OUTSIDE their lair, making these lair actions very niche.

## Annis Hag

- Mental abilities like green hag, but **physically much stronger**. Large creature, AC 17 natural armor.
- Resistance to nonmagical physical damage + cold. Speed 40 ft.
- **Prefers fast violence**: Claw/claw/bite Multiattack over Crushing Hug.

### Crushing Hug

- Grapples (not restrains). Victim can fight back without disadvantage. Hag **can't attack while hugging** — can only continue the crush.
- Use as **surprise grab** against elusive/annoying opponents. Works on Large creatures. Will hug a rider's mount to kill it.
- Can Dodge or Disengage while hugging. Can carry victim 40+ ft away.

### Target Priority

- No fear of mundane fighters (high AC + resistance). **Targets spellcasters and paladins** out of spite and threat assessment.
- Not reckless despite being a brute. Wis 14 → knows when outmatched.
- Won't fight alone without favorable odds. Brings ogre/troll minions.
- **Retreat**: *Fog cloud* at ≤52 hp, then flee. Leaves minions behind.

## Bheur Hag

- **NOT a brute**. High Dex, above-average Str. No physical damage resistance. Prefers distance.
- Stealth proficient. Walks/climbs ice without penalty. Engages on treacherous icy ground.
- Flies on *graystaff* (50 ft) and can cast spells while flying (one hand on staff, one for somatic).

### Spell Priority

| Spell | When |
|---|---|
| *Cone of cold* | Gold standard. Use if ≥6 foes (or all) in AoE. |
| *Ice storm* | 5+ opponents, 4 catchable in 20 ft radius. |
| *Wall of ice* | Dome over 2 threatening melee opponents close together. |
| *Hold person* | Single dangerously chargy-bashy opponent. |

### Coven Bheur Hags

- *Eyebite*: Favor **panic** option (keep enemies at distance).
- One bheur casts *hold person* (boosted to 5th level) to pin clustered opponents → sisters pelt with *cone of cold* / *ice storm*.
- Generally prefer *graystaff* spells over coven spells (except *counterspell* and *hold person*).

### Maddening Feast

- Only on actually **dead** creatures (not 0 hp unconscious). Rarely used on PCs — more for NPC victims.

### Retreat

- Flees at ≤36 hp. Mounts *graystaff* and flies away using Dodge action.
