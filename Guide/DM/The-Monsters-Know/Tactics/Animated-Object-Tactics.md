# Animated Object Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/animated-object-tactics/) | **Related**: [[Animated-Objects]]

---

## Tactical Summary
- Constructs with no Intelligence, no self-preservation, no adaptation—they execute simple programmed behaviors until destroyed
- Animated Armor: melee slam robot; default slam/slam, shove/slam only in specific setups
- Flying Sword: hovers 10 ft up, swoops to attack, flies back; AC 17 makes OA risk acceptable
- Rug of Smothering: ambush predator (10 ft speed); one-shot grapple/restrain/blind, then damage sponge

## Combat Behavior
- **Animated Armor**: Slam/slam every round. Shove/slam only with many allied armors (exploit prone) or adjacent hazards (pits). Grapple/slam if mission is capture. Cannot differentiate targets
- **Flying Sword**: Hovers at 10 ft, swoops to attack, returns. Approaches from angles minimizing extra OAs from clustered enemies. No target differentiation
- **Rug of Smothering**: Lies in wait (False Appearance). Smother grapples, restrains, blinds. Can't attack others while smothering. Half damage passes through to victim. Best paired with allies that exploit restrained condition

## Key Decision Points
- Animated Armor: shove only if setup rewards it (gallery of armors, pit trap). Default slam/slam
- Flying Sword: always return to hover height after attacking
- Rug: first attack is the only one that matters—all-in on initial smother

## Retreat Conditions
- None. All animated objects fight until destroyed. Zero self-preservation.

> [!tip] Running This Monster
> Animated objects work best as environmental hazards—a hallway of armor shoving toward a pit, or a rug smothering the wizard while the real threat closes in.
