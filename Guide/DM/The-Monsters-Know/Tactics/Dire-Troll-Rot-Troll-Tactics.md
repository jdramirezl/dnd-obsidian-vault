# Dire Troll and Rot Troll Tactics

The tactics below are derived from the base troll tactics and the stat blocks in *Mordenkainen's Tome of Foes*.

## Dire Troll (CR 13)

A dire troll is a grotesque mutation of a standard troll — multiple arms, extra heads, and rampant regeneration. It follows the same basic behavioral loop as a regular troll but is far more dangerous.

### Combat Behavior
- Charges into melee immediately; Multiattack every round
- Regenerates **10 hp/turn** unless hit by acid or fire
- Multiple limbs grant additional attacks compared to a standard troll
- **Frightful Presence** — use on first Multiattack to impose frightened condition
- Targets nearest enemies indiscriminately (low INT)

### Reaction to Fire/Acid
- Same instinctive recoil as standard trolls — see [[Troll-Tactics]]
- Healthy: focuses attacks on fire/acid source
- Moderate wounds: repositions away
- Serious wounds: flees at top speed

## Rot Troll (CR 9)

A rot troll has lost its regeneration but gained necrotic energy. It is a troll that has been exposed to so much necrotic damage that its flesh withers and pulses with death energy.

### Combat Behavior
- Charges into melee like any troll — Multiattack every round
- **No regeneration** — but its attacks deal necrotic damage
- **Rotten Miasma:** When the rot troll takes damage, creatures nearby must save or take necrotic damage — punishes melee attackers for hitting it
- More desperate in combat than a standard troll since it cannot regenerate
- Less likely to flee — it has nothing to fear from fire/acid specifically

### Retreat Conditions
- Without regeneration, the rot troll is more vulnerable but also more reckless
- Flees only when seriously wounded (≤30 hp) — low WIS means poor survival instinct

> [!tip] DM Tips
> - Dire trolls are standard trolls turned up to 11 — run them the same way but with more attacks and Frightful Presence
> - Rot trolls punish melee attackers with Rotten Miasma — force PCs to choose between staying close and taking necrotic damage
> - Pair a rot troll with standard trolls for a nasty combo: PCs need fire/acid for the trolls but the rot troll doesn't care
