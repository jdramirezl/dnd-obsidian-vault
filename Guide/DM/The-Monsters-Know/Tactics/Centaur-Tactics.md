# Centaur Tactics

> [!summary]
> Centaurs are neutral good brute/shock hybrids that charge with pikes for devastating opening damage, then commit to melee with hooves. They fight to subdue when possible, rotate wounded fighters to archer roles, and retreat as a group when a majority are seriously hurt.

---

## Stat Block Profile
- **Type:** Monstrosity, Large, neutral good
- **STR 18**, DEX and CON above-average
- **Speed 50 ft**
- **Charge:** +3d6 piercing if moves 30 ft straight before pike attack
- **Multiattack:** one Pike (10 ft reach) + one Hooves (5 ft reach)
- **Longbow:** objectively inferior to melee (lower hit + damage)

---

## Combat Sequence

### Opening: Charge
1. Charges from **35–50 ft** → Pike with **Charge bonus (+3d6)**
2. Follows up with **Hooves** — stays in melee (does NOT strafe)
3. Hooves deal more than pike; Multiattack is always Pike + Hooves, never 2 Pikes

### Sustained Melee
4. Fights toe-to-toe while unhurt or lightly wounded
5. **Moderately wounded (≤31 hp)** → one final Multiattack, then moves away at full 50 ft speed

### Group Tactics
- ~1 in 4 centaurs serves as **archer** at 45–50 ft, flanking
- Wounded melee centaur retreats → archer charges in to replace
- Wounded centaur assumes archer role

---

## Context-Dependent Behavior
- **Evil invaders:** full force, archers provide fire support
- **Non-hostile trespassers:** no bows (risk of accidental kill), melee to subdue
- **Misunderstanding:** same as above — someone should offer parley before escalation

---

## Retreat
- **Majority seriously injured (≤18 hp)** → call retreat
- Uninjured centaurs act as **rearguard** for one round
- Injured centaurs **Disengage** and run; rearguard then **Dashes** to join

---

## DM Encounter Tips

> [!tip] Parley First
> Centaurs are NG — combat should rarely be the first resort. Give PCs a chance to talk.

- Charge + Hooves round 1 is devastating: 1d12+4+3d6 + 2d6+4
- Can't subdue with ranged weapons — another reason to prefer melee
