## Tactical Summary

Gargantuan monstrosity. Extraordinary STR (28) and CON (22), INT 1 — pure instinct, zero tactics. Burrows through solid rock. 60 ft tremorsense, blindsight 30 ft. No eyes — detects prey entirely by vibration.

## Combat Behavior

- **Multiattack:** Bite + Tail Stinger each turn
- **Bite:** On hit, Large or smaller target is swallowed (DC 21 STR save to avoid). Swallowed creatures take 6d6 acid damage/turn. Blinded, restrained, total cover from outside
- **Tail Stinger:** 3d6 piercing + 12d6 poison (DC 19 CON save for half). Devastating — avg 42 poison on a failed save
- **Tunneler:** Burrows at 30 ft through solid rock, leaving 10-ft-diameter tunnels
- **No intelligence:** Attacks nearest vibration source. Cannot be reasoned with, tricked, or intimidated
- Targets whatever is closest and making the most noise/movement
- If swallowed prey stops moving, may lose interest and burrow away — or may keep hunting remaining vibrations

## Retreat Conditions

- Effectively none. INT 1 means no self-preservation instinct beyond basic pain response
- May burrow away if seriously wounded and no vibrations detected nearby
- More likely to simply keep attacking until dead or prey is consumed

> [!tip] DM Tip
> Purple worms are environmental disasters, not tactical encounters. Run them like earthquakes with teeth. Tremorsense means "stand still" is a valid survival strategy — but only if the whole party commits. Pair with the Dune sandworm fantasy.
