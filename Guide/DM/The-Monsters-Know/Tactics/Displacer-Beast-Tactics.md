# Displacer Beast Tactics

## Tactical Summary

A panther-like creature on six legs with shoulder tentacles (10 ft reach). Its **Displacement** feature imposes disadvantage on attacks, giving an effective AC of 17–18 despite a base AC of 13. A single hit disrupts Displacement until end of its next turn. Intelligence 6 — instinct-driven, not strategic.

## Combat Behavior

### Positioning
- 10 ft tentacle reach means it **doesn't need to get adjacent** — stays as far as possible while attacking
- Opportunity attacks only matter if opponents have closed the gap

### After Taking a Hit — Response Depends on WHY It Was Hit
| Cause | Response |
|---|---|
| Opponents simply rolled well (still had disadvantage) | Stay and keep fighting — nothing needs to change |
| Single melee opponent attacking without disadvantage | Move out of reach (movement) toward a different opponent → attack that one (action) |
| Multiple melee opponents attacking without disadvantage | **Disengage** (action) → move out of reach, take cover from ranged, nullify advantage sources; returns to fight next turn |
| Seriously wounded (≤34 hp) | Dash or Disengage away (depending on # of melee opponents) and **flee the scene** |

### Sources of Advantage That Nullify Displacement
- Unseen attacker, inspiration, Help action, flanking (if used), class features (e.g., Samurai Fighting Spirit)
- Any advantage cancels Displacement — even if another source of disadvantage exists

### Limitations of Animal Intelligence
- Can grasp: being attacked from two directions, unseen ranged attacker, ferocious foe
- **Cannot** grasp: *faerie fire*, magical effects that grant advantage — reacts with confusion, doesn't know to target the caster

### Hunting Motivation Affects Behavior
| Motivation | Behavior |
|---|---|
| **Sport** | Attack and Disengage, attack and Disengage — toying with prey |
| **Food** | No messing around — attacks until quarry is dead; picks up unconscious target in jaws and Dashes away |

### Target Selection
- Prefers the young, old, sick, weak, isolated, oblivious
- Targets greatest **social** threat (highest WIS, most suspicious PC) — not greatest tactical threat
- A blunt "I don't believe you" or "Who are you really?" may trigger attack

## Retreat Conditions

- Seriously wounded (≤34 hp): Dash or Disengage and flee
- If engaged with 3+ melee attackers or flanked by 2+: Disengage and reposition closer to exit
- Always repositions to face all enemies at once and stay near the exit

> [!tip] DM Tips
> - Displacement disruption timing is key: a hit on PC turns opens the beast to *other* PCs' attacks, but by the beast's next turn it resets — so the beast has no reason to flee on its own turn
> - Consider giving Stealth proficiency (+4) despite it not being in the stat block — it's thematic and doesn't change CR
> - A food-motivated displacer beast grabbing an unconscious PC and Dashing away creates an instant chase scene
> - Don't turn Displacement into Battleship — the offset is only "several feet," not a whole square
