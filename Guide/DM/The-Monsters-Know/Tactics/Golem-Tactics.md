# Golem Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/golem-tactics/) | **Date**: April 24, 2017

---

## Tactical Summary

- All golems are straightforward brutes: exceptional Strength/Constitution, below-average Dexterity.
- Immune to normal weapons and many conditions (can be incapacitated, prone, restrained, stunned — but not charmed, frightened, paralyzed, petrified, or poisoned).
- No self-preservation instinct. Fight until destroyed or ordered to stop.
- Variation comes from special features, not decision-making. Intelligence 3 means behaviors are purely mechanical.

## Combat Behavior by Type

### Flesh Golem
- **Aversion to Fire**: Takes fire damage → disadvantage on attacks/checks until end of next turn. Compulsively moves away from any flame until 30+ ft away. This is nonnegotiable.
- Does NOT fear acid, lightning, or radiant damage — only fire.
- **Lightning Absorption**: Lightning heals it. Pursues sources of lightning damage.
- **Berserk**: At low HP, attacks the nearest creature regardless of allegiance. Creator can talk it down; alternatively, healing it to max HP ends the rage.

### Clay Golem
- **Acid Absorption**: Acid heals it. Compulsively pursues any foe dealing acid damage (closest one if multiple).
- **Berserk**: Same as flesh golem — goes haywire at low HP.
- **Haste** (action, recharge): Grants +2 AC, advantage on Dex saves, and a bonus Slam attack. Costs the Multiattack action on the turn it's used.
- With Int 3, the clay golem automatically uses Haste when available (except not two turns in a row — no payoff without a subsequent Multiattack round).
- Best used in round 1: sacrifice one attack now for AC bonus + three attacks next round.

### Stone Golem
- **Slow** (action, recharge): 10 ft radius. Affects all creatures in range. Worth using when 2+ enemies who are not already Slowed are within 10 ft.
- Against only one enemy, not worth the cost — gives up Multiattack for insufficient return.
- Literal in executing instructions. If guarding a dungeon and the enemy flees, considers the dungeon successfully guarded.

### Iron Golem
- **Fire Absorption**: Fire heals it. Compulsively pursues fire-damage sources.
- **Poison Breath** (recharge, 15 ft cone): Use on the most, nearest enemies. With Int 3, doesn't know to avoid using it on dwarves or stout halflings.
- **Sword over Slam**: Longer reach, more damage. Always prefers sword unless disarmed.

## Key Decision Points

- **Flesh golem: fire nearby?** → Must move away until 30+ ft distant. Nonnegotiable.
- **Clay golem: Haste available?** → Use it automatically (except not two turns in a row).
- **Stone golem: 2+ non-Slowed enemies within 10 ft?** → Use Slow. Otherwise, Multiattack.
- **Iron golem: Poison Breath recharged?** → Use on the most, nearest enemies. Indiscriminate.
- **Any golem: seriously damaged?** → Flesh/clay go Berserk. Stone/iron keep fighting normally.

## Retreat Conditions

- **Golems never retreat.** They have no self-preservation instinct. They fight until destroyed, their creator orders them to stop, or they complete their instructions.

## DM Tips

> [!tip] Running This Monster
> Golems are the rare monster where playing them *stupidly* is correct. The iron golem wastes Poison Breath on dwarves. The clay golem uses Haste mechanically. The flesh golem panics near torches. This is a feature, not a bug — it lets players feel clever for exploiting weaknesses. Balance smart enemies with dumb constructs.
