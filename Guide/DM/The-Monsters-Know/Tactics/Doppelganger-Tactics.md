# Doppelganger Tactics

## Tactical Summary

Shapeshifting humanoids (classified as monstrosities) that take on others' appearances. High CON, extremely high DEX (scrappy fighters), high CHA with Deception/Insight proficiency. Can't be charmed. Read Thoughts is unopposed. Strong self-preservation instinct. The doppelganger is a **sucker-puncher par excellence**.

## Pre-Combat: Social Manipulation

- Disguises as someone beyond suspicion or easily underestimated
- **Read Thoughts** (60 ft, unopposed): scans surface thoughts → grants advantage on Deception, Intimidation, Persuasion, and Insight checks against that target
- Favors **Deception** (bluff/seduce) over negotiation, demands, or threats
- May exploit Insight to shame someone or insult them to gain favor with others
- **Does not want to fight** — advances interests through misdirection as long as possible

## Target Selection

- Targets greatest **social** threat, not tactical threat
- Highest WIS character or whoever has shown suspicion
- A blunt "I don't believe you" or "Who are you really?" may be the spark that triggers attack

## Combat Behavior

### First-Round Nova (Surprise Attack)
- **Ambusher:** Advantage on attacks when target is surprised
- **Surprise Attack:** Extra 3d6 damage on first-round surprise hit
- Both bonuses apply to **both** Multiattacks in round 1
- Two attacks each dealing 4d6+4 bludgeoning (avg 18 per hit, 36 total) — can drop low-level PCs before anyone reacts

### After Round 1
- No way to regain the upper hand — just two attacks per action and a large HP pool
- Much less advantageous once PCs reach level 5 (Extra Attack)

### Fight-or-Flight Decision
| Situation | Response |
|---|---|
| Winning the fight | Continue until seriously injured (≤20 hp), then flee |
| Obviously losing | Evasive maneuvers at moderate injury (≤36 hp) |
| Ambiguous | Decision somewhere in between |
| Engaged by 3+ melee or flanked by 2+ | **Disengage** and reposition: face all enemies, closer to exit |

### Retreat
- Always **Disengage** (action) to avoid opportunity attacks
- Goal: vanish into a crowd and disguise as someone new
- Needs 1 action (one full turn unseen) to transform
- Next best: reach a less populated area and become someone who belongs (farmer, angler)
- Likely triggers a **chase** (DMG pp. 252–55)

## Key Decision Points

| Situation | Response |
|---|---|
| Pre-combat, target is suspicious | Read Thoughts → Deception to deflect |
| Suspicion becomes accusation | Surprise Attack nova on the accuser |
| Round 1 over, fight going well | Continue Multiattacking |
| 3+ melee opponents | Disengage, reposition toward exit |
| Seriously wounded | Disengage and flee; transform out of sight |

> [!tip] DM Tips
> - The first-round nova is the doppelganger's only trump card — make it count
> - A doppelganger that escapes and re-disguises creates paranoia that lasts the whole session
> - Doppelganger clothes/equipment aren't transformed by Shapechanger — they emulate clothing with their form; a doppelganger wearing a real magic item is a tell
> - Combat with a doppelganger will likely be followed by a chase — have the chase rules ready
> - In Lost Mine of Phandelver: Vhalak disguises as Nundro Rockseeker — use this to ambush a PC on watch during a long rest
