# Dragonnel and Liondrake Tactics

> [Source](https://www.themonstersknow.com/dragonnel-and-liondrake-tactics/)

---

## Tactical Summary

- **Dragonnel**: shock attacker with Flyby; nocturnal hunter (120 ft darkvision + blindsight); wants food, treasure, territory
- **Liondrake** (dragonne): brute that dives and mauls; Blood-Chilling Roar (30 ft effect, audible 300 ft) can paralyze/frighten; monstrosity — only wants territory and food

## Combat Behavior

- **Dragonnel**: stays airborne; swoops for melee (Flyby prevents OAs), flies back out; targets prey it can down in 1–2 rounds (AC ≤12, ~36 hp); prefers shiny targets (draconic instinct)
- **Liondrake**: closes to 30 ft, Blood-Chilling Roar; zeroes in on paralyzed targets; if none paralyzed, attacks weakest/most isolated; Multiattacks until unconscious, then Bites to kill; eats on the spot (can't carry prey); Dashes to chase fleeing prey (60 ft fly wins)

## Key Decision Points

- **Dragonnel**: chooses meal + shiny prize over easy pickings; competing predator in territory provokes a fight (three-way battle hook)
- **Liondrake**: paralyzed target overrides caution (attacks regardless of armor); otherwise won't attack creatures >~46 hp; too low INT to adapt pattern
- Both act independently when ridden (INT ≥6, understand language)

## Retreat Conditions

- **Dragonnel**: flees at ≤23 hp by Dashing (fights longer than wise — draconic arrogance)
- **Liondrake**: flees at ≤47 hp with a parting snarl

> [!tip] Blood-Chilling Roar's *effect* is only 30 ft despite being *heard* at 300 ft. The liondrake has no reason to use it until already close. Frightened creatures can't approach, isolating paralyzed prey from rescue.
