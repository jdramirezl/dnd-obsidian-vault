# Mimic Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/mimic-tactics/) | **Date**: September 23, 2016
> **Related Bestiary**: [[Mimic]]

---

## Overview

- Classic ambush predator disguised as objects (doors, treasure chests). Strong physical scores (especially STR/CON) — brute suited to close range. High Stealth proficiency + darkvision. Specialized intelligence (INT 5) for mimicking objects.

## Combat Sequence

1. Choose disguise (**Shapechanger**), lie in wait (**False Appearance**).
2. Attack with surprise using **Pseudopod** → grapples target (disadvantage on escape due to **Adhesive** surface).
3. Switch to **Bite** (action) vs grappled victim — has advantage (**Grappler**), more damage than Pseudopod.
4. Once victim at 0 hp → swallow and digest (1d8 acid/turn until dead — author's extrapolation).

## If Another Party Member Attacks

- Avoids attacker, moves away at half max speed (7 ft/turn for Small/Medium victims) while continuing to Bite grappled prey. INT 5 — zone of control concept is beyond it.

## Adhesive Property (Object Form Only)

- Anything that touches it sticks — including **melee weapons**. Freeing a stuck weapon: same escape check as grapple (DC 13 STR, disadvantage).
- Gives ranged fighters and spellcasters a leg up. Ammunition fired into mimic is not retrievable.
- **Note**: Adhesive only applies in object form, not creature form.

## Retreat

- Seriously injured (≤23 hp) → lets go of prey, reverts to amorphous form, backs away (effectively a Disengage).
- No longer Adhesive. Next turn: Bite anything in reach, or Dash away in reverse (mouth toward pursuers).
