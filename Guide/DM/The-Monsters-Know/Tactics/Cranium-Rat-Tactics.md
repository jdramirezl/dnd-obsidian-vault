# Cranium Rat Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/cranium-rat-tactics/) | **Date**: January 28, 2020

---

## Tactical Summary

- **Mind flayer minions** created by bombarding rats with psionic energy. Used as forward observers for illithid colonies.
- **Individual cranium rats are useless**: 2 hp, inconsequential Bite, 30-ft darkvision, 5-ft brain glow. Can only spy and die.
- **Swarms are the real threat**: Hive consciousness amplifies intelligence and grants spellcasting. Key spells: *confusion*, *command*, *dominate monster*, *detect thoughts*, *comprehend languages*.
- **Prefer confined spaces and darkness**: 30-ft darkvision means they avoid open areas and daylight.

## Individual Cranium Rat

- Spies for mind flayer colony via 30-ft telepathy (extended by mind flayer's 120-ft range or elder brain's 5-mile range).
- May be ordered to use Illumination for a brief look at something in the dark — effectively a suicide mission.
- If the illumination attack somehow misses, snuffs light and Dashes away.

## Swarm Tactics

**Luring**: Leads victims into tight spaces and narrow paths. Uses Illumination (15-ft radius in subterranean settings) to provoke curiosity, then snuffs light as a bonus action when targets reach 30 ft, overruns nearest victim and Bites.

***Confusion* (1/day)**: Saved for when maximum targets are grouped within the 10-ft-radius sphere. Minimum 2 targets. Lures victims into clustering before deploying.

**Shock attacks**: Happy to launch unexpected attacks but wants to finish quickly. If it doesn't do enough damage in one round to win in one more, pulls back using Disengage.

**Target priority**: Generally indiscriminate, but if hit with non-bludgeoning/piercing/slashing damage, turns fury on that attacker. Uses *command* to force distant attackers closer.

***Dominate monster* (1/day)**: The nastiest trick. Requires seeing the target (only 30-ft darkvision), correctly guessing which target will fail DC 13 Wis save, and maintaining concentration for up to 1 hour. The swarm can have telepathic conversations with the dominated target, inquiring about abilities. Control maintained as long as target takes no damage. Telepathic link persists across any distance on the same plane.

**Intelligence gathering**: Uses *comprehend languages* to translate conversations and *detect thoughts* to extend mind-reading range for its master.

## Retreat Conditions

- **Under elder brain/mind flayer orders**: Fights to the death if commanded.
- **Not in active contact**: When reduced to ≤14 hp, scatters into 6–8 individual rats that Dash in different directions, unless a mousehole is within 60 ft.

## Rogue Swarms

A swarm beyond elder brain influence retains its intelligence. Smart enough to guarantee food access and make territory uncontestable. Could be a worthy boss monster for level 3–4 characters in a one-shot.

## DM Tips

> [!tip] Running This Monster
> Individual cranium rats are spies, not combatants. Swarms are the encounter — lure PCs into tight spaces, then *confusion* or *dominate monster*. The *dominate monster* play requires patience: follow the party as normal-looking rats, observe, choose a low-Wis target, then dominate and maintain control silently for up to an hour. Remember that cranium rats look like normal rats (ignore the illustration) and won't chase anyone through open daylight.
