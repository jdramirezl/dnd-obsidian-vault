# Lizardfolk Tactics

> **Source:** [The Monsters Know — Lizardfolk Tactics](https://www.themonstersknow.com/lizardfolk-tactics/)
> **Bestiary:** [[Lizardfolk]]

---

## Tactical Summary

Lizardfolk are territorial brutes (high Str/Con, average Dex) proficient in Perception and Stealth. Semi-aquatic (hold breath 15 min, swim = land speed). They patrol territory borders, stalk intruders using Stealth, and attack with surprise. Low Intelligence means they fight simply — pick an enemy, bash until dead or wounded.

## Combat Behavior

### Regular Lizardfolk
- **Approach:** Use Stealth to close within 30 ft, then attack with surprise. In swamps, stalk underwater.
- **Multiattack:** One weapon strike (heavy club or javelin) + one shield bash per turn.
- **Simple fighters:** Pick an enemy, bash until it's dead or they're seriously wounded (≤8 HP).
- **Flanked/outnumbered:** Disengage (action) and back off if attacked by 2+ enemies. Don't flee — look for opportunity to re-engage 1-on-1.
- **Ranged attacks confuse them:** Move instinctively toward their territory regardless of shot direction.
- **Magic terrifies them:** Rush the caster (if visible, spell does ≤5 HP, no PCs in the way) OR run (if can't see caster, spell does ≥14 HP, too many enemies between). Having a shaman makes them bolder — more likely to rush enemy casters.
- **Positioning:** Always attack from the direction of their territory, backs toward it. May ambush but don't flank.
- **Never surrender voluntarily** (assume they'll be killed). Captured lizardfolk are impassive but will talk in Draconic. Will turn on captors instantly if chances look good.

### Lizardfolk Shaman (reskinned druid)
- **Turn 1:** *Conjure animals* — eight giant poisonous snakes (best), four crocodiles, or one swarm of poisonous snakes. This is the shaman's primary contribution.
- **Subsequent turns:** *Produce flame* (2d8 fire, 30 ft range) at enemies. Stays out of melee to protect concentration.
- **Thorn whip:** If enemy caster comes within 30 ft, yank them forward for other lizardfolk to pound.
- **If concentration broken by ranged attacker:** In swamp, Change Shape to crocodile and swim after shooter. In jungle (croc too slow on land), cast *fog cloud* to cover repositioning.
- **Covering retreat:** *Fog cloud* to help lizardfolk escape. If dispelled, *plant growth* to slow pursuers.
- **Pre-combat (in territory):** *Fog cloud* on PCs first, allowing lizardfolk to sneak up quickly.

### Lizard King/Queen
- Bigger, badder lizardfolk. **Two trident attacks** (two-handed, no shield) using Skewer feature on first hit.
- **Immune to frightened** — ain't afraid of nothin'. Will aggressively single out enemy casters for special attention to show the tribe why it's the boss.
- Never runs from a caster.

## Key Decision Points

1. **Shaman's conjure animals:** Eight giant poisonous snakes for maximum encounter multiplier impact. Four crocodiles for moderate freakout. One swarm for icky-creepy.
2. **Territory matters:** Outside territory = retreat when losing. Inside territory = fight to the death.
3. **Shaman's concentration:** Protect at all costs. If broken, the shaman's main contribution is gone.

## Retreat Conditions

- **Outside territory:** Disengage + retreat toward territory when ≤8 HP or battle going badly (≥half seriously wounded).
- **Inside territory:** Fight to the death.
- **Shaman covers retreat** with *fog cloud*, then *plant growth* if fog is dispelled.

> [!tip] DM Tip
> The shaman's *conjure animals* is the encounter-defining spell. Eight giant poisonous snakes will bump the adjusted XP by two encounter multiplier levels. The lizard king/queen's immunity to frightened and aggressive targeting of casters makes it a great foil for parties that rely on magical intimidation.
