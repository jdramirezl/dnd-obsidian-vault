# Drider Tactics

## Tactical Summary

The drider is a drow-spider hybrid built for combat — high STR, high DEX, exceptional CON. It excels at ranged ambush tactics, using Spider Climb to fight from walls and ceilings while peppering targets with longbow fire. It opens with *faerie fire* for sustained advantage, then maintains distance to exploit its ranged superiority. Strictly nocturnal/subterranean (120 ft. darkvision, Sunlight Sensitivity).

## Combat Behavior

### Encounter Formats
- **Solo drider:** Ambush predator, starts hidden (Stealth proficiency)
- **Drider + giant spiders (4+):** The drider commands while spiders web the area

### Opening Round
1. **Surprise round:** Cast *faerie fire* — grants advantage on ALL subsequent attacks (better than one surprise attack with advantage)
2. Begin longbow attacks from 30–120 ft. range

### Solo Drider
- Fights at range (longbow does more damage than longsword)
- Maintains 30–120 ft. distance — at range, it can attack anyone while not everyone can attack it
- Uses Spider Climb to run along walls and ceilings, staying out of melee reach
- If a PC closes to melee: switches to longsword (two-handed, d10+3) without hesitation

### Drider + Giant Spiders
- Drider casts *faerie fire* while spiders blanket the area with webs
- **Always attacks unrestrained targets first** — spiders instinctively attack restrained ones
- If all targets are restrained: switches to melee, substituting a bite for one longsword attack (bite delivers poison)

### Target Priority (Hatred-Based)
1. Dwarves and rock gnomes
2. Other elves
3. Clerics and paladins of non-Lolth deities
4. Everyone else

### Multiattack
- 3 attacks per turn with longsword OR longbow
- Can substitute one attack for a poisonous bite (primarily against webbed/restrained targets)

### Spellcasting Variant
- *Freedom of movement* — frees itself or a spider from restraint/paralysis
- *Dispel magic* — removes inconvenient PC spells
- *Silence* or *darkness* — shuts down enemy spellcasters (but requires dropping *faerie fire* concentration)
- *Hold person* — paralyzes a target who keeps escaping webs
- *Sanctuary* (bonus action) — free defensive buff after attacking; break it next turn, recast
- *Poison spray* cantrip is inferior to longbow — don't bother

## Key Decision Points

| Situation | Response |
|---|---|
| Combat starts (hidden) | *Faerie fire* on surprise round |
| Targets at range | Longbow from walls/ceiling |
| PC closes to melee | Switch to two-handed longsword |
| With spider allies, all targets webbed | Move to melee, bite + longsword |
| Enemy spellcaster causing problems | *Silence* or *darkness* (drops *faerie fire*) |

## Retreat Conditions

- ~2 in 3 driders **fight to the death** (cursed by Lolth, may have a death wish)
- ~1 in 3 values survival — flees when seriously injured (≤49 hp)
- A drider with a **vow or vendetta** to fulfill will generally flee to preserve itself for that purpose
- Fleeing drider: Dodge action + full movement along walls/ceilings

> [!tip] DM Tips
> - *Faerie fire* on round 1 is non-negotiable — it's the drider's best tactical move
> - Use vertical space aggressively: a drider on the ceiling with a longbow is extremely hard to reach
> - Giant spider webs + *faerie fire* = advantage on attacks against restrained, glowing targets — devastating combo
> - Driders cannot create webs themselves — they need spider allies for web control
> - The hatred priority list adds great roleplay flavor; the drider snarls at the dwarf and ignores the human
> - Web Walker lets the drider ignore its own spiders' webs — it can wade through webbed terrain freely
