# Elite Giant Tactics

> [!summary]
> Volo's Guide elite giant variants. Most add toughness without tactical depth, but the Cloud Giant Smiling One (rogue-like trickster) and Storm Giant Quintessent (legendary living storm) are genuinely complex.

---

## Mouth of Grolantor (Elite Hill Giant)
- Less tactically sophisticated than a regular hill giant
- Entirely random and mechanistic behavior — wind it up and let it go

## Frost Giant Everlasting One
- Extra-tough frost giant that ate a troll → gained regeneration
- Vaprak's Rage: barbarian Rage with fixed bonus damage
- Fights to the death; acid/fire just makes it angrier. "Rrrrahhhh, bash bash bash."

## Fire Giant Dreadnought
- Stupid but strong front-line defender; dual shields
- Shield Charge: multiple Dreadnoughts synchronize to clear enemy front line
- Provides cover for rock-hurling allies (can't throw rocks itself — no free hands)

## Stone Giant Dreamwalker
- Holy fool on a broken vision quest — irrational, not hostile
- **Dreamwalker's Charm is PASSIVE** — triggers on anyone within 30 ft
- Encounter: wanders by → fixates on PC/object → Charm triggers → Petrifying Touch → picks up victim → tries to leave
- If attacked: may ignore, swat, or throw a rock at nothing. Play for maximum oddity.

---

## Cloud Giant Smiling One
- Trickster/manipulator motivated by acquiring rare treasure
- Bard spells + cloud giant innate spells; key: *suggestion*, *Tasha's hideous laughter*, *telekinesis*
- **Has Sneak Attack** (hidden in small print): extra damage with advantage on attack

### Gaining Advantage
1. *Telekinesis* → restrain target
2. *Tasha's hideous laughter* → prone
3. *Invisibility* → unseen attack
4. Seize any external blind/paralyze/stun

- Plays like a **rogue**, not a brute — if no advantage, spends turn setting up advantage for next round
- *Cure wounds* on self if moderately wounded (183–105 hp) and gets a breather
- Objective is treasure — grabs what it wants and leaves

---

## Storm Giant Quintessent
- Legendary enemy with lair actions; a living storm pursuing immortality

### Lair Actions
- **Fog/murk cloud:** blinds everyone (truesight does NOT penetrate fog)
- **Wind blast/water jet:** pushes 15 ft; best vs 4+ opponents; does NOT clear fog
- **Thunderclap:** deafens 20-ft radius — combined with fog = blind AND deaf

### Combat
- Flies/hovers 15–20 ft up; swoops to attack; indifferent to opportunity attacks
- 15-ft reach + 600-ft ranged attack → targets magic weapon wielders, Dex-save casters, flyers

### Legendary Actions
- **Gust (1):** push dangerous melee fighters 20 ft away
- **Thunderbolt (2):** nuclear option vs anyone dealing 69+ hp/turn; use BEFORE that enemy's turn
- **One With the Storm (3):** emergency relocation/flee; may use immediately to avoid combat entirely

### Retreat
- Seriously wounded (≤92 hp): acknowledges danger
- ≤46 hp: abandons lair. Fears death — that's why it became a Quintessent.

> [!tip] DM Tips
> Mouth/Everlasting/Dreadnought: minimal decisions. Dreamwalker: surreal roleplay. Smiling One: think rogue — always set up advantage. Quintessent: fog first, fly-by attacks, Thunderbolt the biggest threat.
