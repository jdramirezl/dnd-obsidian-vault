# Vampire Tactics

> [!summary]
> Vampires are apex predators — shrewd, patient, and obsessed with self-preservation. They never attack recklessly. Vampire spawn are nocturnal ambush hunters driven by the compulsion to feed.

---

## Vampire Spawn

**Profile:** Exceptional physical stats, high Stealth, resistant to nonmagical physical + necrotic damage, regeneration (suppressed by sunlight/running water). Strictly nocturnal (Sunlight Hypersensitivity).

### Behavior
- Apex predators — pick off the young, weak, isolated, oblivious
- Won't attack groups larger than their own number
- Found near medium-to-large settlements (need abundant prey)

### Combat Sequence
1. **Ambush** — stalk or lie in wait, attack with surprise
2. **Claw (grapple intent)** → if grapple succeeds → **Bite**
3. If grapple fails vs non-proficient target (no Athletics/Acrobatics) → try grapple again
4. If grapple fails vs proficient target → switch to Claw/Claw for damage
5. If situational advantage appears (flanking) → one more grapple attempt
6. **Drag grappled victim** toward hiding at 15 ft/round; Spider Climb at 7 ft/round up walls
7. If attacked while feeding → Claw attacker + Bite victim (Multiattack), keep dragging
8. Victim unconscious → satiated, Disengage and return to resting place
9. **Flee at ≤32 hp** — Disengage, flee via most direct route (rooftops via Spider Climb)

> [!warning] Exception
> Commands from a master vampire override the spawn's self-preservation instinct.

## Vampire (Full)

**Additional features:** Shapechanger, Legendary Resistance (3/day), Misty Escape, Charm, Children of the Night (1/day), three legendary actions (Move, Unarmed Strike, Bite), double-range darkvision.

### Lair Security (Layered)
1. Physical security (walls, locks)
2. Mortal guards
3. **Children of the Night** triggered on yellow alert — 2d4 bat/rat swarms or 3d6 wolves (interference only, buys time)
4. Vampire spawn
5. The vampire itself

### Pre-Combat: The Parley
- Greets PCs at a time and place of its choosing
- Sizes up the party — what they want, whether they can be bought off
- Identifies the **lowest-Wisdom PC** (elves get +4 padding for Fey Ancestry)
- **Stealth-Charms** during conversation — disguised as a social roll
- May charm multiple PCs if undetected
- Evaluates each charmed PC as: agent, spawn thrall, or food

### Fight-or-Flight Decision

| Situation | Response |
|---|---|
| PCs are Easy encounter for vampire | Go for the kill, turn PCs into spawn |
| PCs are Hard+ encounter, not wounded | Abandon lair, flee to backup resting place |
| In-between | Stalk, divide, pick off isolated PCs |

### Combat Tactics
- **Round 1 (if vampire acts first):** Dodge action, let PCs come to it
- Uses **Unarmed Strike** legendary action vs first two melee attackers
- If a **third** melee attacker engages → legendary Move away (no opportunity attacks), climb wall if needed
- After Round 1 assessment → flee, retreat, or kill

**Going for the kill:**
- Targets PC with **lowest Athletics/Acrobatics** (not charmed PCs)
- Grapple → Bite sequence (same as spawn)
- When attacked while grappling: **strikes attacker twice** (not bite-then-strike like spawn)
- Legendary actions: Bite (drain grappled victim faster) + Unarmed Strike (punish attackers)
- Does NOT drag victims away — trying to finish the fight

**Tactical retreat (not fleeing):**
- Shapechange → bat, fly out of sight
- Immediately shift to mist form, circle back stealthily
- Whisper commands to charmed PCs: "sneak away, find a hiding place, wait"
- Reduce party size, then attack when odds improve

**Full flee:**
- Release charmed PCs (bonus action) — *or keep them charmed for interference*
- Shapechange → mist (action), exit

### Key Vulnerabilities to Exploit (as DM)
- **Radiant damage / holy water** suppresses Regeneration — vampire will legendary-Move away from that PC, or prioritize grappling + draining them
- At **≤57 hp** while taking radiant/holy water damage → reassess; if odds are bad, Shapechange to mist and flee lair
- If odds still OK → may switch to **Dodge** as action, fight only via legendary actions

> [!tip] DM Reminder
> A vampire always has **multiple resting places**. Establishing a new one can be the hook that lets PCs track it down.
