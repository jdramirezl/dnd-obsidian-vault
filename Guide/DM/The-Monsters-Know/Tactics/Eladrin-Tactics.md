# Eladrin Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/eladrin-tactics/) | **Date**: September 30, 2019
> **Book**: Mordenkainen's Tome of Foes

---

## Shared Traits (All CR 10)

- Resistance to nonmagical physical damage. Darkvision. Superior natural armor.
- **Magic Resistance**, Fey Step, proficiency with longswords and longbows.
- Fey Step lets them dart around the battlefield — appearing close or far as most inconvenient for targets.
- Types morph by season/mood on long rest — no mid-encounter changes.
- Wield longsword two-handed (no shield) and longbow. Stowing one to draw the other takes time.

## Spring Eladrin

- **Spellcaster first, skirmisher second**. Shuts opponents down rather than slaying them.
- **Joyful Presence (60 ft)**: Charms creatures. Enthrall concentrates attention on the eladrin, letting allies surround or slip away.
- **Remaining spells** target foes who resist charms: confusion (4 clustered targets), Tasha's hideous laughter (vs Extra Attack martial), suggestion, Otto's irresistible dance.
- **vs melee attacker**: Fey Step away (no OA) → move 30 ft → Multiattack with Longbow.
- **vs ranged attacker**: Move close → Fey Step into melee → Multiattack with Longsword.
- **Always thinking a move ahead** — temporizes a turn to swap weapons if needed.
- **Goal-oriented**: Driving PCs away? Preventing them from leaving? Steering into a trap? Only attacks those who won't be dissuaded. Prefers Persuasion/Deception.
- **Retreat at ≤50 hp**: Gives up, Dodges while retreating.

## Summer Eladrin

- **Aggressive shock attacker**. Wrath personified. Main objective: drive others off via Fearsome Presence.
- **Round 1**: Movement + Fey Step to charge nearest opponent within 60 ft of all others → Fearsome Presence triggers → Multiattack with Longsword (two-handed, ~20 damage/hit).
- **Frightened foes**: Disadvantage on attacks vs it. Takes OA when they move away.
- **Subsequent rounds**: Targets foes who overcame Fearsome Presence or were unaffected. Fey Step to disengage only if leaving a frightened opponent.
- **Can't reach target**: Stows sword, draws bow while Fey Step recharges. Back to sword if enemy reverses direction.
- **>80 ft away at start**: Uses Intimidation to order PCs off. Combat only if they approach anyway.
- **Parry**: Only vs nonmagical weapons when no foe with a magic weapon might need Parrying instead.
- **Never flees** — ruled by temper, no attachment to the material plane.

## Autumn Eladrin

- **Spellcaster, disinclined to fight**. Chaotic neutral but good-natured and helpful.
- **Pre-combat**: Uses Insight to discern PCs' needs and "get to yes." Approaches within 60 ft for Enchanting Presence.
- **Most spells are boons**: cure wounds, lesser/greater restoration, heal, raise dead.
- **Sleep (1st level)**: Practically useless vs high-level PCs. Only as gentle nonlethal coup de grâce.
- **Calm emotions**: Primary combat spell — turns hostile foes indifferent if they resist Enchanting Presence.
- **If attacked in melee**: Attacks with Longsword (nonlethal KO if reduces to 0 hp) → Fey Steps away → moves to safe distance.
- **Intransigent foe resists charms**: Maintains 60–150 ft range, potshots with Longbow until downed or dissuaded.
- **Foster Peace**: Can't protect itself (charmed creatures can't attack the charmer). Protects other creatures — allies or third parties.

## Winter Eladrin

- **Governed by melancholy**. Fights with profound sadness. Doesn't move unless forced — moves opponents instead.
- **Weapon attacks**: Hardly worth using. Frigid Rebuke (reaction) is better than OAs.
- **Sorrowful Presence**: DC significantly lower than other eladrin. Higher-level PCs likely resist.
- **Drastic measures**: Cone of cold and ice storm (each 1/day).
  - **Ice storm**: When ≥4 foes clustered in 20-ft circle (no charmed targets). Fey Step away first, center storm where it used to be (immune to cold damage but not bludgeoning).
  - **Cone of cold**: When ALL opponents insist on attacking. Fey Step to optimal position first.
- **Frigid Rebuke target selection**: Secretary problem — lets n/e opponents swing unmolested, then Rebukes whichever subsequent one seems less tough. After round 1, accurately judges lowest Con mod.
- **Gust of wind**: Pushes back greatest number of enemies. Ties broken by lowest Strength mods.
- **Fog cloud**: Only for covering retreat (not simultaneous with gust of wind — both require concentration).
- **Retreat at ≤50 hp** or after spending both ice storm and cone of cold — too depressing to continue.

## DM Tips

> [!tip] Running These Monsters
> Each eladrin type has a completely different combat personality. Spring thinks ahead, summer charges in rage, autumn tries to help, winter stands still in sadness. The weapon-swap timing matters — plan which weapon each eladrin needs before the round starts. Summer eladrin never flee; winter eladrin retreat when sad enough.
