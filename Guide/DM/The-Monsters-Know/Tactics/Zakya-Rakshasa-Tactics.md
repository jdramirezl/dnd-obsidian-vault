# Zakya Rakshasa Tactics

## Tactical Summary

CR 5 fiend. "Bloodthirsty footsoldiers of the rakshasa horde." Higher STR + CON than standard rakshasa, lower in everything else. Optimized for combat, not deception. Rakshasas reimagined as a ravaging host rather than subtle schemers.

- Better armor, magical weapon attacks, Martial Prowess (knock back/prone/disarm), improved Multiattack
- Only *detect thoughts* + *disguise self* remain from standard spells, plus *shield* (1/day)
- Limited Magic Immunity: spells ≤1st level only (vs ≤6th for standard)
- 30 ft speed (vs 40 ft). Physical damage resistance (downgraded from immunity)
- Still vulnerable to piercing from magic weapons wielded by good creatures
- Athletics + Perception instead of Deception + Insight

> [!warning] Encounter Scaling
> In numbers, strictly for high-level PCs. Mid-level PCs will struggle with just 2-3.

## Combat Behavior

### Approach

- Strike at **night** (darkvision advantage)
- INT + WIS high enough to plan attacks and ensure favorable conditions
- Hurl **javelins** while charging → switch to **longswords** in melee

### Shield (1/day)

Reaction: turns a hit against AC 18-23 into a miss, plus all subsequent attacks until start of next turn. Don't cast until the first hit actually lands.

### Martial Prowess (one per hit)

**Default: Knock prone.** First or second of three sword attacks lands → follow-ups get advantage. Always useful.

**Push 10 ft:** Situational. Environmental hazard behind target (flames, pit), or to disengage without opportunity attacks.

**Disarm:** Opponent has a magic weapon or critical object. Complication: sword + shield = no free hand. Must drop own sword (free action) → pick up prize (free interaction). May enter battle shieldless (AC 16) to keep a hand free — also allows two-handed longsword for slightly more damage. If opponent's weapon isn't better: kick it out of reach instead.

### Priority: Disarm Piercing Weapons

Good-aligned opponents with piercing magic weapons are the biggest threat (vulnerability). Prioritize disarming, especially obviously good-aligned targets (clerics of good gods, righteous paladins). *Detect thoughts* can locate hidden rogues/rangers (search for thoughts within 30 ft).

## Retreat Conditions

- Lawful evil: disciplined, obey orders. Demon overlord commanders may never order retreat
- Free to exercise own judgment: retreat as a group when forced from offense to defense and defense is failing — strategic judgment, not individual HP
- In Eberron, "demonic masters" are Demon Overlords (Sul Khatesh is LE, Rak Tulkesh is NE), not standard chaotic demons

> [!tip] DM Tip
> No-nonsense brutes, not schemers. Javelin volley on approach, longsword Multiattack in melee, knock prone as default. The *shield* spell is their one trick — save it for the hit that matters. Run them as disciplined soldiers in squads.
