# Githzerai Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/githzerai-tactics/) | **Date**: October 14, 2016
> **Related Bestiary**: [[Gith]]

---

## Tactical Summary

- Psychic super-monks — ascetic defenders from another plane. Disciplined, self-defensive, not aggressive.
- Paradoxically built as brutes despite a skirmisher ability profile: all abilities favor toe-to-toe melee.
- Unarmed Strike deals devastating damage: 1d8+2 bludgeoning + 2d8 psychic per hit, with Multiattack for two strikes.
- Shield (3/day, reaction) boosts AC from 14 to 19 — but efficacy drops sharply against multiple attackers.

## Combat Behavior

### Githzerai Monk
- Toe-to-toe melee only. No skirmisher features (no Nimble Escape, no bonus Disengage).
- Psychic Defense: Wisdom modifier added to AC → AC 14 base.
- Shield (reaction, 3/day): AC jumps to 19. Only effective against a single attacker — avoid being double-teamed.
- Jump/Feather Fall: Largely useless in combat. Jump costs an action and is constrained by movement speed.
- Retreats by Dashing in the 4th round or at ≤15 hp.

### Githzerai Zerth
- Advanced monk with higher Con, more Unarmed Strike damage, plus phantasmal killer and plane shift (1/day each).
- **Phantasmal killer**: Weak in practice — DC 14 Wis save, any success ends it. Only worth attempting against targets with Wis save modifier of −3 or worse.
- **Plane shift**: The real power. One failed Cha save = permanently banished. Target PCs with Cha modifier ≤0. Or keep in reserve for group escape.
- AC high enough to handle two attackers, but not three.
- At ≤33 hp, signals retreat and Readies plane shift triggered by last ally joining hands. Fights as rearguard until allies are clear.

## Key Decision Points

- Monk: being double-teamed? → Shield loses value. Reposition to face one opponent.
- Monk: 4th round or ≤15 hp? → Dash away.
- Zerth: target with very low Wisdom? → Consider phantasmal killer (only if Wis save ≈ −3).
- Zerth: target with low Charisma? → Plane shift them permanently.
- Zerth: fight going badly? → Signal retreat, Ready plane shift for group escape.

## Retreat Conditions

- **Monk**: Dashes away in the 4th round or at ≤15 hp.
- **Zerth**: At ≤33 hp, signals retreat and Readies plane shift for group escape. Rearguard until allies clear.

## DM Tips

> [!tip] Running This Monster
> Don't underestimate the monk's damage — two Unarmed Strikes average 27 damage per round, brutal for CR 2. The zerth's plane shift is campaign-altering; use as escape hatch rather than offensively unless you want to permanently remove a PC. Burn through Shield's three uses and the monk becomes very fragile.
