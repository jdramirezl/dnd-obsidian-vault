# Roper Tactics

## Tactical Summary

Dungeon predator/scavenger — camouflages as stalagmite or stalactite. Exceptional STR and CON, below-average DEX. Ambush attacker with 50 ft tendril reach compensating for 10 ft speed. AC 20. False Appearance = perfect disguise while stationary. High WIS (16) for target selection despite bestial INT (7).

## Ambush Setup

- **False Appearance**: stationary roper is undetectable — passive Perception and Searching won't reveal it
- Stealth (double proficiency) only matters if moving
- Waits for 4+ targets within **20–25 ft** (Reel only pulls 25 ft)
- Eye closed, tendrils retracted → **always surprises**

## Ceiling vs Floor

- **Ceiling strongly preferred** (stalactite) unless ceiling 50+ ft up
- Dangling prey can't easily fight back; breaking free = fall damage + prone
- Floor only if ceiling too high

## Attack Sequence: Tendril ×4 → Reel → Bite

- Strictly ordered: all tendrils first, then Reel, then Bite
- Tendrils do no damage — grapple + restrain on hit
- Targets least-armored first (ignores DEX mods), works up
- Bite attacks first successfully grappled target
- 6 tendrils total; 4 attacks per round

## Opportunity Attacks

- 50 ft tendril reach → fleeing PCs provoke at edge of reach

## Retreat (≤65 hp)

- Too slow to Dash/Disengage (10 ft speed) — uses tricky maneuver:
- Holds grappled enemies in place (speed 0, restrained), takes Dodge (free enemies) or Dash (all grappled), crawls away
- Lets tendrils play out to 50 ft, releases each enemy one by one
- Climbs walls during retreat; ceiling roper can simply **drop prey**

> [!tip] DM Tips
> - Pair with piercers (larval ropers) for a "mother bird" combo
> - Roper over water can dangle prey to drown them
> - Roper above a waterfall with tendrils disguised as vines = classic trap
> - Digests everything except platinum, gemstones, magic items — no bones to warn PCs
