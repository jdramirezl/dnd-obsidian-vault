# Fey Tactics: Darklings, Quicklings, and Redcaps

## Darkling

### Tactical Summary

Fey rogues. High DEX, above-average CON, low STR. 120 ft darkvision + 30 ft blindsight, light-sensitive. Dim light ideal.

### Combat Behavior

- Extra damage when attacking with advantage (partial Sneak Attack). Best: strike in darkness vs targets without darkvision
- Dagger range >20 ft loses advantage — prefer melee backstabs
- If spotted before attacking: Dash away, don't fight
- Dash if 1 enemy in reach; Disengage if 2+
- **Death Flash:** Multiple darklings exploit it — flash is instantaneous, blinded enemies give advantage to blindsight users

### Retreat Conditions

Without elder: flee at ≤5 hp.

## Darkling Elder

- Multiattack (2 shortswords), better sneak attack, Darkness feature (1/encounter)
- **Darkness:** Shuts down darkvision; darkling allies function via blindsight within 30 ft
- **Death Burn:** Also deals radiant damage. Elders fight to the death

## Quickling

### Tactical Summary

Malicious pranksters, not killers. Won't fight unless cornered. Blurred Movement = disadvantage on attacks against them.

### Combat Behavior

- If cornered: Multiattack with dagger → move through any gap at 4× speed. OAs are meaningless
- Once free: Dash. Nothing catches them. Use Chase rules (DMG pp. 252–55)

## Redcap

### Tactical Summary

Murder machines. Exceptional STR/CON. Iron Boots = no stealth. Magically created killers.

### Combat Behavior

- **Round 1 — Ironbound Pursuit:** Close 50 ft, kick (DC 14 DEX save, 3d10+4, prone)
- **Round 2+ — Multiattack:** 3 Wicked Sickle attacks (~26 avg). Pursue fleeing enemies relentlessly
- **Outsize Strength:** Medium for grappling — can grapple Large creatures. One redcap grapples, others slice
- Grapple only when enemy tries to retreat; otherwise just Multiattack

### Retreat Conditions

- Seriously wounded (≤18 hp) while opponent has 40%+ hp → flee
- Moderately wounded (≤31 hp) while opponent has 70%+ hp → flee

> [!tip] DM Tip
> Darklings = stealth encounters in dim light. Quicklings = chase encounters, not fights. Redcaps = relentless slashers — pair for grapple-and-slice combos. Know your Chase rules.
