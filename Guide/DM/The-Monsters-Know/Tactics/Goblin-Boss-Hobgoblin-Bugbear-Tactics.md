# Goblin Boss, Hobgoblin and Bugbear Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/goblin-boss-hobgoblin-bugbear-tactics/) | **Date**: August 19, 2016
> **Related Bestiary**: [[Bugbears]], [[Hobgoblins]], [[Goblins]]

---

## Tactical Summary

- Three advanced goblinoids with radically different combat personalities.
- **Goblin Boss**: Cowardly leader. Fights from the front initially, then darts in and out. Uses Redirect Attack to sacrifice minions.
- **Hobgoblin**: Disciplined soldier. Tight formation, night attacks, Martial Advantage for bonus damage, orderly retreats.
- **Bugbear**: Ambush brute. Hides, springs out at 30 ft, smashes with Surprise Attack nova, fights until badly hurt then panics.

## Combat Behavior

### Goblin Boss
- Begins on the front line with Multiattack (two scimitar swings, second nerfed).
- After taking any hit → Disengage (bonus) and retreat 15 ft behind the front line.
- Subsequent turns: dart into a gap → Attack (action) → Disengage (bonus) → dart back.
- **Redirect Attack** (reaction): Avoids a hit by deflecting it onto an adjacent goblin minion.
- Flees if reduced to ≤8 hp or if goblin minions are wiped out.

### Hobgoblin
- Natural soldiers. Attack at night (darkvision advantage). Require 2-to-1 numerical advantage in daylight.
- Move in tight teams of 4–6. Martial Advantage grants bonus damage when an ally is within 5 ft of the target.
- With goblin troops: stay behind the front line, shoot longbows, let goblins engage in melee. Martial Advantage still applies if goblins are in melee with the target.
- Multiple groups use flanking: one engages directly, one shoots from distance, extras move to flanks. Prepared before battle.
- **Orderly retreat**: When 2+ hobgoblins in a group are seriously injured (≤4 hp) or killed, the most injured Disengage and retreat at full speed. Others follow over 1–2 rounds. Archers cover the retreat.
- Hobgoblins' intense loathing of elves may cause suboptimal decisions — attacking elves first even when tactically wrong.

### Hobgoblin Captain
- Activates **Leadership** just before melee begins: 1d4 buff to all allies' attacks and saves within 30 ft for 10 rounds.
- Fights as an ordinary hobgoblin otherwise. Attached to the main melee group.
- Throws javelins at fleeing enemies rather than breaking ranks to chase.

### Hobgoblin Warlord
- **Parry** (reaction): +3 AC. Use when attack roll is 20–22.
- **Shield Bash/longsword/longsword** sequence: Use Shield Bash first only if the opponent has a negative Str modifier OR allies can also attack the prone target before it stands. Otherwise, three longsword attacks.

### Bugbear
- Hides (Stealth +6) until prey comes within 30 ft, then springs out with Surprise Attack (extra 2d6).
- Indiscriminate target selection — attacks whoever is closest. To the bugbear, they all look weak.
- Morningstar over javelin (more damage, prefers melee). Javelin only if it can't close.
- At ≤10 hp: confused, flees via Dash, eats opportunity attacks.

### Bugbear Chief
- Multiattack + Heart of Hruggek (advantage on saves vs. conditions).
- Won't blow stealth cover for Intimidation — ambush advantage is too valuable.
- May Intimidate only if surprised by PCs, in a stalemate, or outmatched.

## Retreat Conditions

- **Goblin Boss**: ≤8 hp or minions wiped out → flees.
- **Hobgoblins**: Orderly retreat when 2+ in a group are at ≤4 hp or dead.
- **Bugbear**: ≤10 hp → panicked Dash, eating opportunity attacks.

## DM Tips

> [!tip] Running This Monster
> Hobgoblins are the star here — their orderly retreat mechanic is one of the most sophisticated in the MM. Run it step by step: injured Disengage first, others follow next round, archers cover. The goblin boss's Redirect Attack is hilariously villainous — describe the goblin minion's betrayed expression. Bugbears are simple: hide, smash, panic.
