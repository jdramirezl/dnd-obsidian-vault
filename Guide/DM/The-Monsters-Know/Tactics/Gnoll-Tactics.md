# Gnoll Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/gnoll-tactics/) | **Date**: August 18, 2016
> **Related Bestiary**: [[Gnolls]]

---

## Tactical Summary

- **Pure melee shock troops**: High Strength, decent Dex, low Intelligence. Gnolls charge in and attack with spears — no ranged phase, no subtlety.
- **Rampage is the defining feature**: After reducing a foe to 0 hp in melee, a gnoll can move half speed and make a bonus bite attack. Chain-killing is the goal.
- **Instinct-driven, not tactical**: Gnolls are "Rrrraaaahhhh, stab stab stab" — one of the few monsters where that's actually correct behavior.
- **Nighttime raiders**: Darkvision gives advantage against PCs without it. They strike vulnerable targets during nighttime patrols.
- **Pack Lord's Incite Rampage** is part of Multiattack (not a separate action) — it grants another gnoll or giant hyena an immediate reaction attack.

## Combat Behavior

**Opening**: Gnolls spot a vulnerable target (preferably at night) and charge at full movement speed. No parley, no ranged softening.

**Core loop**: Attack (action) with spear. If the attack drops a foe to 0 hp → Rampage: move up to 15 ft toward another enemy + bonus bite attack. Repeat until nothing moves.

**Pack Lord**: Two attacks per Multiattack + Incite Rampage (part of the same action, not a separate one). Uses its glaive at 10 ft reach. Incite Rampage grants one nearby gnoll or giant hyena an immediate reaction attack — but this consumes that creature's reaction (no opportunity attack that round).

**Fang of Yeenoghu**: The smart one (Int 10). Triple Multiattack (claw/claw/bite). Actively maneuvers to target high-damage, low-HP PCs. Can "read" the party and prioritize fragile targets to maximize Rampage chains. Classified as a **fiend**, not humanoid — detectable by Divine Sense and Primeval Awareness, affected by *protection from evil/good*.

## Key Decision Points

- **Target selection (Fang of Yeenoghu)**: Prioritize low-HP, high-damage PCs to maximize Rampage triggers. The Fang zeros in on weakness.
- **Rampage triggered?** → Move up to 15 ft toward the nearest enemy and bite. Always use this — it's free damage.
- **Pack Lord: which ally to Incite?** → The gnoll or giant hyena best positioned to land a hit on an adjacent enemy. Remember it costs that creature's reaction.
- **Longbow listed in stat block?** → Ignore it. Gnolls' features are entirely melee-oriented, and they're not smart enough to craft or barter for bows.

## Retreat Conditions

- **8 hp or fewer**: Gnoll turns tail and Dashes away. Self-preservation overrides aggression — they have no concept of honor or valor.
- **Will eat opportunity attacks** while fleeing — they're not smart enough to Disengage.
- **No morale system**: Each gnoll decides individually based on its own HP. They don't coordinate retreats.

## DM Tips

> [!tip] Running This Monster
> Gnolls are the simplest monster to run — charge, stab, Rampage. The real danger comes from the Fang of Yeenoghu targeting your party's glass cannons. If you're a tenderhearted DM who wants to protect fragile PCs, don't use a Fang — it *must* follow its nature and hunt the weakest.
