# Treant Tactics

> [!info] Source
> [The Monsters Know — Treant Tactics](https://www.themonstersknow.com/treant-tactics/)

**Bestiary:** [[Treant]]

---

## Overview

Treants are chaotic good forest guardians (essentially ents). Good usually means friendly, but they're displeased by evil and by civilization encroaching on their turf.

## Ability Profile

- **Extraordinary Strength and Constitution**, poor Dexterity — straight-up brutes
- Resistant to **bludgeoning and piercing** (even magical), but NOT slashing
- **Vulnerable to fire** — angers them rather than scares them
- High Wisdom — prefer talking/warnings before fighting; can size up opponents

## Combat Tactics

### Engagement Philosophy
- Primary goal is **deterrence** — drive trespassers out
- If they can't drive them out → **attack to subdue** (nonlethal melee) → carry unconscious foes to forest edge and toss them
- Against low-level PCs → may simply **grapple** (pick them up) and carry them out; Huge creatures can carry Medium/Small PCs without slowing

### Attacks
- **Rock throwing** — while walking toward enemies; switch to melee once in range
- **Multiattack: two Slam attacks** per turn
- Stick to **nonlethal melee** unless opponents are truly evil or setting fires

### Fire Response
- Fire **angers** them and draws ire
- **41+ fire damage in one turn** → scared, backs off (heedless of opportunity attacks), hurls rocks from 30–60 ft at fire-dealer
- Fire eliminates qualms about killing — ranged attacks are always lethal

### Animate Trees (1/day)
- Two nearby trees become quasi-treants (woody drones, single slam each, no Multiattack)
- Only if **genuinely threatened** — easy/medium encounter, or surprised and takes 41+ damage before acting

### Calling for Aid
- If opponents too difficult alone → booming call; additional treants arrive rounds 3–5

### Retreat
- **Never retreat** once they've decided opponents need to die
- Fight to the death or accept surrender; won't be taken prisoner
