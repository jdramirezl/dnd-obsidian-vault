# Frost Salamander Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/frost-salamander-tactics/) | **Date**: September 3, 2019

---

## Tactical Summary

- **Huge elemental brute** of ice (water + air). Burrows, climbs, has tremorsense — lies in wait underground.
- **Hunting method**: Digs lairs in snow/ice, crouches nearby. Waits for prey to shelter in lair, strikes after nightfall.
- **Freezing Breath**: Cone between young and adult white dragon. Recharges on 6 — OR instantly on fire damage (Burning Fury).
- **Vulnerable to fire** but fire recharges breath weapon. Trap for PCs.
- **Multiattack**: Four Claws (10-ft reach) + one Bite (15-ft reach). Doesn't divide Claws among targets.

## Combat Behavior

**Freezing Breath timing**: Effectively single-use without Burning Fury. Waits until cone covers ALL opponents (or 6). In its lair, they're sitting ducks.

**When breath available AND targets positioned**: Always uses Freezing Breath.

**Otherwise**: Multiattack. Claws at 10-ft reach — doesn't close to 5 ft unless enemies come to it.

**Opportunity attack trick**: Waits until target leaves 15-ft Bite reach (not 10-ft Claw reach) for ~75% more OA damage.

**Target selection**: Mostly indiscriminate. Favors smaller, weaker-looking, or more isolated targets.

## Retreat Conditions

- **Moderately wounded (≤117 hp)**: Retreats, Dodging.
- **If pursued**: Freezing Breath and Multiattack while backing away at full movement.
- **If burrowing/climbing to retreat**: Dodges only (must face direction of travel).

## DM Tips

> [!tip] Running This Monster
> The lair-as-bait is the encounter setup: PCs find shelter from a blizzard, settle in, then tremorsense detects them. The Burning Fury / fire vulnerability tension is the tactical puzzle — let PCs discover it organically. Mark the Bite 15-ft vs Claw 10-ft reach difference on your battle mat for OAs.
