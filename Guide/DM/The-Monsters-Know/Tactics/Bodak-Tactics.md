# Bodak Tactics

> [!summary]
> The bodak is a CR 6 undead horror whose passive features — Death Gaze and Aura of Annihilation — deal constant damage on *opponents' turns*. Its approach is "come at me, bro": it holes up in a dark lair, forces enemies through a single approach, and lets its aura and gaze grind them down while it picks them off with Withering Gaze.

---

## Stat Block Profile
- **Type:** Undead, Medium
- **Uniformly high physical stats; low INT (6), above-average WIS/CHA**
- Immune: lightning, poison; charmed, frightened
- Resistant: cold, fire, necrotic; nonmagical physical damage
- **120 ft darkvision**, proficient in Perception + Stealth
- **Sunlight Hypersensitivity** — strictly nocturnal/subterranean

---

## Key Features

### Death Gaze (Passive)
- Triggers at the **start of each opponent's turn** within 30 ft
- DC 13 CON save or 3d10 psychic damage
- Fail by 5+ → drop to 0 hp (unless immune to frightened)
- Avoided by averting eyes → disadvantage on attacks against the bodak

### Aura of Annihilation (Passive, Toggle)
- 30 ft radius, always on (bonus action to toggle — no reason to turn off)
- 5 necrotic damage to any non-fiend/non-undead that **ends its turn** in range

### Withering Gaze (Action)
- Ranged, DC 13 CON save → 4d10 necrotic on fail, half on success
- Far superior to its feeble unarmed attack (1d4+2 + 2d8 necrotic)

---

## Combat Sequence

### Lair Setup
1. Dark location with clear sightlines to all approaches
2. Positions against a wall — no one can get behind it
3. Approaches are **narrow** — single-file only

### In Combat
1. Death Gaze + Aura grind enemies passively every round
2. **Withering Gaze** on its turn against priority targets
3. Unarmed attack only when it **can't see** targets (darkness spell, invisibility)

### Target Priority
1. **Clerics/Paladins** — can turn/destroy undead
2. **Berserker barbarians** — Mindless Rage = immune to frightened (negates Death Gaze instakill)
3. Anyone with **magic weapons**
4. Anyone dealing **acid, thunder, force, psychic, or radiant** damage
5. Otherwise opportunistic

---

## Retreat
- **Will not flee** unless turned — returns immediately when turning ends
- Outside its lair: **Dashes back** to lair (strategic retreat, not fleeing)
- Repositions on open ground to maximize sightlines, minimize flanking
- **Never** stays under open sky near dawn — always returns to lair before sunrise

---

## DM Encounter Tips

> [!tip] Alternative Stealth Strategy
> A bodak can stalk prey within 30 ft without being seen. Aura of Annihilation doesn't require sight. It can deal passive damage while hidden, then Hide again instead of attacking — systematically wearing down the party without revealing itself.

- Death Gaze + Aura = damage on *every creature's turn*, not just the bodak's
- Averting eyes avoids Death Gaze but imposes disadvantage — a brutal dilemma
- Clever PCs may wait at the lair entrance near dawn to catch it returning
