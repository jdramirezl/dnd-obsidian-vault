# Nagpa Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/nagpa-tactics/) | **Date**: April 16, 2019
> **Book**: Mordenkainen's Tome of Foes

---

## Overview

- Only **13 nagpas exist** — cursed wizards turned vulture-people, scavenging lost civilizations for arcane lore.
- **Long-range spellcaster** contour: extraordinary Int/Cha, exceptional Wis, very high Dex. Melee is a last resort.
- Proficient in all mental saves; Dex/Con saves are unremarkable → **vulnerable to damaging evocation/transmutation/necromancy**.
- Truesight + Perception proficiency → nearly impossible to hide from. But lacks Stealth proficiency — can't ambush.
- Proficient in Deception; uses *charm person*, *suggestion*, *dominate person* to manipulate proxies.

## Key Features

### Corruption (Bonus Action)
- Imposes charmed condition on one creature until start of nagpa's next turn.
- Prevents opportunity attacks from that target → free disengage from one melee opponent.
- Best targets: barbarians, druids, rangers, wizards (low Cha). Avoid bards, paladins, sorcerers, warlocks, elves, gnomes.

### Paralysis (Bonus Action, Recharge 6)
- Potentially paralyzes **every creature within 30 ft** for up to 1 minute. DC 20.
- Likely only fires once per encounter. **Save it** until 6+ foes (or all foes if fewer) are within 30 ft.

## The Paralysis Combo

- **Paralysis + Wall of Fire** is the optimal combination.
- Paralyzed targets auto-fail Dex saves, can't move out of the wall, and take repeated damage.
- Expected damage per paralyzed target in *wall of fire*: ~60 hp (vs ~19 per non-paralyzed target). Net gain of ~82 damage against just 2 targets.
- *Fireball* and *prismatic spray* are actually **worse** with Paralysis than without (fewer targets hit due to AoE mismatch).

## Opening Moves

1. **Ideal**: Maneuver foes into position → Paralysis (bonus action) → *Wall of Fire* (action).
2. **If positioning isn't right**: *Feeblemind* against the most threatening caster (Int save — bad for everyone except wizards), OR *prismatic spray* against 5–6 foes, OR *circle of death* against 12+ foes.
3. **Corruption** (bonus action) every round it can't use Paralysis — neutralize one threat.

## Spell Priority

| Spell | When to Use |
|---|---|
| *Wall of fire* | Immediately after Paralysis — the big combo |
| *Feeblemind* | Against a single dangerous non-wizard caster |
| *Prismatic spray* | Against 5–6 opponents (never fewer) |
| *Circle of death* | Against 12+ opponents |
| *Disintegrate* | Against a paralyzed target, or to counter *wall of force* |
| *Hold person* (4th level) | 3 targets with lowest Wis — bait for Paralysis positioning |
| *Counterspell* | Auto vs 3rd-level Dex/Con save spells; upcast vs 4th-level |
| *Etherealness* | **Escape hatch** — but can't cast if it already used both 7th and 8th slots |

## Retreat

- Shrewd and prudent. Casts *etherealness* and retreats when **moderately wounded (≤130 hp)**.
- If *etherealness* is blocked: tries *fly* or Paralysis/*wall of fire*.
- If all else fails: fights until **seriously wounded (≤74 hp)**, then flees with *witch bolt*, *fireball*, *fire bolt*, *suggestion*, and Corruption to cover retreat.
