# Golgari NPC Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/golgari-npc-tactics/) | **Date**: April 9, 2026

---

## Tactical Summary

- The Golgari Swarm: eusocial insectoid kraul warriors led by death priests, plus elven shamans. Communitarian, fanatical, weird.
- **Kraul warriors** move as one (Hive Mind within 30 ft = immune to charmed/frightened), use Pack Tactics, and have Spider Climb.
- **Kraul death priests** lead from the front, feed on nearby deaths, and combine melee + spellcasting via Multiattack.
- **Golgari shamans** are back-line spellslingers who create devastating plant growth + spike growth kill zones.
- "Golgari waste nothing" — every tactic maximizes efficiency.

## Combat Behavior

### Kraul Warrior
- Never stray more than 30 ft from nearest ally (Hive Mind). Pack Tactics = at least half the group engages in melee.
- Wield spears two-handed (no shield). Six limbs allow carrying extra spears for throwing.
- Ground assault: hurl two volleys of spears from 50 ft and 20 ft, then melee free-for-all.
- Spider Climb: walls and towers are no obstacle. Ceilings provide advantage if applicable.
- Prefer night/underground (60 ft darkvision). Direct, open assaults — no stealth.
- Individual kraul never retreat. A unit retreats when reduced to 40% strength, Disengaging then Dashing.
- Scouts keep 60+ ft distance, withdraw when spotted. Aggressive recon: approach to 60 ft, hurl spears, hightail it.
- Shock attacks: half close to melee (Pack Tactics for the rear group's thrown spears), rear group Dashes away after 2 rounds, front group fights to the death.

### Kraul Death Priest
- Leads from the front — no more than 15 ft behind the line. Feed on Death requires being within 30 ft of casualties.
- Multiattack: one melee attack + one spell per turn (except animate dead which takes a minute).
- Quarterstaff always two-handed. Six limbs mean no need to drop staff to cast.
- **Blight**: Priority spell against squishy back-line spellcasters within 30 ft.
- **Vampiric touch**: Only when reduced to ≤47 hp. Maintain until topped up, then drop it (maintaining means no Multiattack).
- Default melee spell: *poison spray* (cantrip, at will, Con save — no disadvantage in melee under 5E14).
- Under 5E24: *ray of enfeeblement* (Con save, not a ranged attack) useful against skirmishers/marksmen with finesse/ranged weapons.
- Never retreats. At ≤26 hp, kraul warriors gang up on the foe engaging the priest, then close ranks around them.
- Will signal willingness to parley if the battle turns — "The new life has been well fed this day!"

### Golgari Shaman
- Elven back-line caster. Very high Wisdom/Charisma, high Dex. Fungal Rot melee attack > Quarterstaff in every way.
- Feed on Death is a reaction (1/turn, bolsters only the shaman). No need to be near the front.
- **Devastating combo**: Round 1 → *plant growth* (4x movement cost). Round 2 → *spike growth* (2d4 per 5 ft traversed). Overlap both in a 40 ft diameter circle.
- Kraul warriors fan out 5 ft from the circle's edge for the last 2d4 as enemies exit.
- **Thorn whip** from 10–20 ft outside the circle: 2d6 piercing + drags target 10 ft through spikes (4d4 more).
- *Blight* against rogues/monks who escape the circle. *Giant insect* if spike growth is dispelled.
- *Dispel magic* to strip enemy buffs (priority: *haste*). *Cure wounds* on wounded leaders of CR 2+.
- Never retreats. Kraul warriors won't close ranks around the shaman (not part of hive mind).

## Key Decision Points

- Kraul unit at 40% strength? → Disengage and Dash away.
- Death priest at ≤47 hp? → Cast *vampiric touch*, maintain until topped up.
- Death priest at ≤26 hp? → Warriors close ranks around the priest.
- Shaman: round 1? → *Plant growth*. Round 2? → *Spike growth* (same area).
- Enemy stuck in the circle? → *Thorn whip* to drag them through spikes.
- Enemy escaped the circle? → *Blight* if squishy, melee Fungal Rot if they reach you.

## Retreat Conditions

- **Kraul warriors**: Unit retreats at 40% strength. Individuals never retreat.
- **Kraul death priest**: Never retreats. Will parley if battle turns (before losing more than half their forces).
- **Golgari shaman**: Never retreats. Dies where they stand.

## DM Tips

> [!tip] Running This Monster
> The plant growth + spike growth combo is the centerpiece. Set it up in rounds 1–2, then use thorn whip to drag PCs through the thorns. Kraul warriors in the hundreds are canon — if you're using a death priest, this should be a Deadly encounter. The Golgari are weird negotiators: they may demand fresh corpses or fungal mycelium rights as concessions.
