# Orc Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/orc-tactics/) | **Date**: August 16, 2016
> **Related Bestiary**: [[Orcs]]

---

## Tactical Summary

- **Brute-force chargers**: Orcs use Aggressive (bonus action) to close 30–60 ft, then Attack (action) with greataxes. No subtlety.
- **Brief parley before violence**: Orcs have Intimidation +2 — expect demands and threats, but a smooth talker can stave off combat (Persuasion DC 15–20 with disadvantage).
- **Honor-bound fighters**: Orcs are more willing to fight to the death than most creatures. Seeing allies slain makes survivors fight harder, not flee.
- **Variants add real complexity**: Orogs bring tactical intelligence and nighttime ambushes; War Chiefs buff large groups with Battle Cry; Eyes of Gruumsh add spellcasting and action economy.
- **First contact always at 30–60 ft** — the sweet spot for Aggressive to close and attack in one turn.

## Combat Behavior

**Opening**: Aggressive (bonus action) to charge + full movement → Attack (action) with greataxe. First contact is always at 30–60 ft.

**Sustained combat**: Attack (action) every round. If a frontline PC falls, the orc moves to the next target. If a squishy PC is 30–60 ft behind the fallen one, Aggressive (bonus action) again to reach them — terrifying for archers and casters.

**Outnumbered in melee**: An orc fighting 2+ foes repositions to face only one, risking opportunity attacks rather than giving up its Attack action (Dodge or Disengage feel un-orc-like).

**Orog**: Fights like an orc but smarter — Disengages before repositioning. Groups with orogs prefer nighttime attacks to exploit darkvision vs. blinded PCs.

**War Chief + Battle Cry**: With 10+ orcs, uses Battle Cry (action) on the charge round (grants all allies advantage on attacks for one turn). With 5–9 orcs, charges first, then Battle Cry on round 2 (still gets one bonus action attack).

**Eye of Gruumsh**: Charges with the group. Round 2: *spiritual weapon* (bonus action) + Attack (action). Round 3: *bless* (action) on self + up to 2 leaders + *spiritual weapon* attack (bonus action). Later rounds: Attack + *spiritual weapon*. Uses *command* ("Flee!") to provoke opportunity attacks when 4+ orcs surround a target.

## Key Decision Points

- **30–60 ft from PCs?** → Aggressive + charge immediately.
- **PC tries Intimidation?** → DC 20 opposed by Wisdom. Success + orc fails = rattled (advantage on next social check). Failure = instant attack.
- **Fighting 2+ enemies?** → Reposition (risking OA) to fight only one. Never Dodge or Disengage — that's not orc-like.
- **War Chief: how many orcs?** → 10+ = Battle Cry on charge round. 5–9 = charge first, Battle Cry round 2. <5 = just attack.
- **Eye of Gruumsh: use *command*?** → Only when target is within reach of 4+ orcs or 2+ including a leader.

## Retreat Conditions

- **6 hp or fewer**: Disengage (action) + retreat (move) — unless allies have been slain, in which case fight to the death.
- **Allies retreating successfully**: Makes remaining orcs more willing to retreat themselves.
- **Retreat ≠ surrender**: Surviving orcs watch for the PCs and attack again if the party appears weakened.
- **Orogs**: Same "death before dishonor" attitude despite higher Intelligence — Wisdom is still average.

## DM Tips

> [!tip] Running This Monster
> Orcs are straightforward to run — charge and hack. The real depth comes from the pre-combat parley (let PCs try social skills, but make it tense) and from variant orcs. An Eye of Gruumsh with *spiritual weapon* + *bless* running simultaneously transforms the action economy of an orc warband. Don't forget that retreated orcs come back for revenge.
