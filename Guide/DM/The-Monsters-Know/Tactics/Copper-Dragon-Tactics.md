# Copper Dragon Tactics

> [!summary]
> Copper dragons are tricksters, jokers, and bluffers who love to see who can put the more outrageous story over on the other. They use Deception to misdirect enemies, hit them with Slowing Breath, and flee. Acid Breath is reserved as a last resort against clearly evil foes.

---

## Metallic Dragon Shared Traits
- Same brute profile as chromatic dragons: high STR + CON, proficient in all "big three" saves + CHA
- Blindsight, darkvision, flying + one alternative movement mode
- **Two breath weapons:** one damaging, one nonlethal
- **Social skill proficiencies** in addition to Perception + Stealth
- Adult/ancient: legendary actions, Legendary Resistance, Frightful Presence
- Adult/ancient: only **2 lair actions** (vs chromatic's 3)
- **Good-aligned** — encounters rarely begin with combat

## Copper-Specific Traits
- **Climb speed** — alternative movement
- **Acid Breath** (damaging, line) + **Slowing Breath** (nonlethal, cone — slows targets)
- **Social skills:** bluff and brag, loves to see who can tell the more outrageous story (Deception-focused)
- **Personality:** Clowns and comedians. Young copper dragons are pranksters and tricksters
- Ancient copper dragons can **Change Shape**

---

## By Age

### Wyrmling
- Friendly — may approach adventurers and try talking in Draconic
- If attacked: **Slowing Breath once** to impede attackers, then fly/climb away at max speed
- Only uses Acid Breath if already damaged
- Only Bites if cornered

### Young
- Clown and comedian — initiates social interaction, loves bluffing and bragging
- If encounter turns hostile: uses **Deception to misdirect** PCs or trick them into dropping their guard
- Then: **Slowing Breath** → flee
- Uses Acid Breath only if already hurt; melee only if cornered

### Adult (Legendary)
- Would rather talk than fight, but **will start a fight** if PCs seem to have evil intent
- Opens with nonlethal means: "cloud" lair action + Slowing Breath
- Uses **Multiattack (Frightful Presence + Claw/Claw/Bite) before Acid Breath**
- Acid Breath is a last-ditch trump card

#### Lair Actions (Pick 1 per round, never same twice)
| Action | Type | Best Against |
|---|---|---|
| **Mud** | Cloud-ish action (difficult terrain/obscurement) | Melee opponents trying to close distance |
| **Spike Growth** | Push action (deals damage + inhibits movement) | Anyone moving through the area |

##### Fizban's New Lair Actions
| Action | Type | Best Against |
|---|---|---|
| **Laughing Gas** | Debilitator (conventional) | 4+ targets — incapacitates through uncontrollable laughter |
| **Torpid Energy** | Debilitator + movement restrictor (single target) | DEX-dependent attackers at any range — particularly nasty against rogues and archers |

#### Legendary Actions (3/round)
| Action | Cost | Use When |
|---|---|---|
| **Detect** | 1 | Any stealthy movement within line of sight |
| **Tail Attack** | 1 | PC behind the dragon |
| **Wing Attack** | 2 | 3+ PCs within 10 ft — knocks prone + bonus fly to reposition |

### Ancient
- Same tactics as adult; gains **Change Shape**
- Prefers to go about in disguise outside its lair
- If threatened in disguise: talks/tricks its way out, even surrenders to avoid revealing true nature
- Once eyes are off it: reverts or assumes different form to escape

## Metallic Dragon Combat Personality

> [!important] Trickster First, Fighter Last
> Copper dragons use Deception and misdirection before resorting to any kind of force. Even their nonlethal Slowing Breath is preceded by attempts to trick enemies into dropping their guard.

> [!important] Retreat Threshold
> Unless defending their lair, adult/ancient metallic dragons **retreat at ≤70% hp** — Disengage (action) → fly/climb away at max speed.

---

## Quick Reference: Combat Flow
1. **Pre-combat:** Social interaction — bluffing, bragging, jokes, seeing who can tell the bigger whopper
2. **Hostile intent detected:** Deception to misdirect → Mud (lair action) → Slowing Breath
3. **If PCs persist:** Frightful Presence + Claw/Claw/Bite Multiattack
4. **Last resort (clearly evil PCs):** Acid Breath
5. **≤70% hp:** Disengage → fly/climb away at max speed
6. **Outside lair in disguise:** Tricks way out → Change Shape to escape when unwatched

> [!tip] DM Note
> Copper dragon encounters should feel like a comedy routine that turns serious. The dragon is testing the PCs with jokes and tall tales. Torpid Energy (Fizban's) is particularly nasty against DEX-based characters — use it to shut down the party's rogue or archer.
