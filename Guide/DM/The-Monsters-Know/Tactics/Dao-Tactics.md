# Dao Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/dao-tactics/) | **Date**: November 1, 2016

---

## Tactical Summary

- **Earth genie brute**: Straight-up brutes lacking the cleverness of other genies. Above-average Int/Wis/Cha by humanoid standards, but tactics are simple.
- **Extraordinarily high Constitution**: Proficient in Int, Wis, and Cha saves. Susceptible to Dex-save spells.
- **Sure-Footed**: Advantage on saves against being knocked prone.
- **Maul preferred over unarmed**: Greater damage + can knock targets prone.
- **Abundance of movement options**: Fly, burrow, *passwall*, *gaseous form*, *invisibility*, *plane shift*.

## Combat Behavior

The sophistication of dao tactics doesn't extend beyond:

1. **Summon an earth elemental** (*conjure elemental*) to help out.
2. **If a spellcaster lobs a Dex-save spell**, wall them off with *wall of stone*.
3. **Pick a nearby target and whack it with the maul**.

***Phantasmal killer***: Essentially useless — requires two consecutive failed Wis saves. Only ~50/50 against targets with Wis save modifier of −1. Most dao conclude it doesn't work often enough to bother.

**Other earth spells** (*passwall*, *move earth*, *stone shape*): Utility only, no direct combat application.

## Retreat Conditions

- **Seriously injured (≤74 hp)**: Exits using one of many movement options — fly, burrow, *passwall*, *gaseous form*, *invisibility*, or *plane shift*.
- ***Plane shift* as banishment**: Might attempt to banish a pesky enemy to another plane out of rage or spite, but lacks the imagination to accurately guess if a target has low enough Charisma. May try it anyway and become more enraged if it fails.

## DM Tips

> [!tip] Running This Monster
> Dao are the least interesting genies — included for completeness. Their tactics are simple: summon elemental, wall off casters, maul everything else. The real flavor comes from their abundance of escape options when wounded. If running a Plane of Earth campaign, lean into *wall of stone* and *passwall* for environmental control rather than direct combat tricks.
