# Lich Tactics

> [!summary]
> The lich is a genius-level spellcaster that chose undeath. It's monomaniacal, arrogant, and fears nothing — until proven wrong. It never leaves its lair if it can help it, and every encounter on its terms should be frustrating and end with its escape. To truly destroy one, go after the phylactery.

---

## Core Traits
- All ability scores exceptional, INT highest
- Saves: CON, INT, WIS bonuses; immune to charm, fright, paralysis, poison, exhaustion
- Resistant to cold, lightning, necrotic; immune to poison + nonmagical physical
- Truesight 120 ft, passive Perception 19, proficient Perception + Insight
- **Legendary Resistance (3/day)** — uses every time it fails a save (failures are rare)
- **Turn Resistance** — advantage on WIS saves vs Turn Undead (+9 base)
- **Rejuvenation** — destruction is temporary unless phylactery is destroyed

## Personality as Tactics
- Potential threat? Destroy you before you grow strong
- Useful? Use you until you're not. You may be more useful dead
- No use? Kill you if it advances a goal. Otherwise, irrelevant
- Like you? No. A lich doesn't like anybody

## Spell Priority by Level

### Nuclear Options (1 use each)
| Spell | Target Priority |
|---|---|
| **Power Word Kill** (9th) | Highest-level wizard → warlock/draconic sorc → cleric/druid. Must be ≤100 hp. Refuses to use on wild magic sorcerers out of disdain |
| **Dominate Monster** (8th) | Strong fighter with average/low WIS — turn against allies. Never try on raging barbarian |
| **Power Word Stun** (7th) | Glass cannon with ≤150 hp. CON save to resist, so avoid high-CON melee fighters |

### Situational High-Level
| Spell | When to Use |
|---|---|
| **Finger of Death** (7th) | Finish off a wounded target (avg 61 dmg on fail, 30 on success). Corpse becomes zombie if killed outright |
| **Plane Shift** (7th) | Banish low-CHA fighter/barbarian/ranger to another plane |
| **Disintegrate** (6th) | Clumsy targets (low DEX), inanimate obstacles, force fields. Nothing on a successful save |
| **Globe of Invulnerability** (6th) | Shut down persistent low-level casters. Requires concentration (incompatible with cloudkill) |

### Workhorse Spells
| Spell | Role |
|---|---|
| **Cloudkill** (5th) | Likely **opening move** — cast at ground zero (lich is immune to poison). Observe PC reactions to gauge capabilities |
| **Fireball** (3rd) | Best combo: paralyze targets with Paralyzing Touch + power word stun, then fireball the group. Better vs high-CON/low-DEX parties |
| **Blight** (4th) | High-priority target within 30 ft, not in melee reach |
| **Counterspell** (3rd) | Slap away enemy spells (redundant if globe is up) |
| **Dispel Magic** (3rd) | Remove low-level PC buffs. Won't boost above 3rd (50/50 odds not good enough) |
| **Mirror Image** (2nd) | Only if PCs deal ≥41 hp in a single round — swallows pride |
| **Shield** (1st, reaction) | Automatic when hit roll is 17–21 or vs magic missile |
| **Ray of Frost** (cantrip) | 4d8 cold + speed reduction. Best legendary-action cantrip |

### Reserved Slots (Always)
- **1 × 2nd-level** for *Invisibility* (if reduced to ≤54 hp, considering retreat)
- **1 × 4th-level** for *Dimension Door* (escape hatch if invisibility isn't enough)

## Combat Flow

### Opening
- **Arrogance** — won't open with high-level spells unless PCs have already proven dangerous
- Likely opens with **Cloudkill** at its own position (immune to poison, forces PCs through gas)
- Observes reactions to identify PC strengths/weaknesses

### If PCs Act First
| PC Action | Lich Response (Legendary Actions) |
|---|---|
| 1 ranged attack from ≤60 ft | Ray of Frost (1 LA) |
| 1 melee attack | Frightening Gaze (2 LA) — melee fighters likely have more CON than WIS |
| 2 melee attacks | Wait for own turn → Blight |
| 3+ melee attackers | Cloudkill on own turn → Disrupt Life LA if 3+ melee or 4+ foes within 20 ft |

### Decision Tree: High-Level Slots
- **High-WIS/low-CON caster causing trouble?** → Power Word Stun
- **Low-WIS/high-CON brute causing trouble?** → Dominate Monster (send it after the caster)
- **High-HP/low-CHA martial?** → Plane Shift to another plane
- **Low-CON glass cannon already wounded?** → Finger of Death
- **Persistent low-level casters?** → Globe of Invulnerability
- **Force barrier or clumsy brute?** → Disintegrate

### Default Turn (No Urgent Threat)
1. Fireball if ≥2 paralyzed targets and ≥4 total in blast
2. Blight if priority target within 30 ft
3. Dispel Magic to strip PC buffs
4. Mirror Image if took ≥41 hp in one round
5. Otherwise: move toward **highest-INT PC** (−2 for half-casters, −4 for non-casters/wild magic sorcs) and Paralyzing Touch

### Legendary Actions (End of PC Turns)
1. **Disrupt Life** — if 3+ melee attackers or 4+ foes within 20 ft
2. **Frightening Gaze** — low-WIS PC ends turn within 10 ft (skip if already failed vs that PC)
3. **Paralyzing Touch** — PC ends turn in melee reach
4. **Ray of Frost** — if remaining PC turns ≤ remaining LAs

### Lair Actions (Initiative 20)
- **After casting a 6th–8th level spell** → "Roll d8, regain spell slot"
- **In melee with any PC** → Grounding tether (PC takes half of lich's damage)
- **Not in melee** → Necrotic apparitions (15d6 necrotic)
- Never same lair action two rounds in a row

---

## Retreat Conditions
- **≤54 hp** → consider Invisibility + reassess
- If PCs can detect invisible → Dimension Door out
- Every encounter on the lich's terms should end with its escape

> [!important] The Phylactery Endgame
> The only way to truly destroy a lich is to threaten its phylactery. When PCs get near it, the lich unleashes everything — it **cannot flee** knowing they'll destroy it. This is the fight where it goes all-in.
