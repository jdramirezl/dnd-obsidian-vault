# Sword Wraith Tactics

> **Source:** *The Monsters Know What They're Doing* — Keith Ammann
> **Bestiary:** Mordenkainen's Tome of Foes
> **Creatures:** Sword Wraith Warrior (CR 3), Sword Wraith Commander (CR 8)

---

## Tactical Summary

Sword wraiths are glory-obsessed undead warriors who died ignominiously in battle and refuse to stop seeking the valorous victory they never achieved. They're melee-focused brutes driven by compulsion — Martial Fury is always on, they charge the most formidable foe to prove their prowess, and they fight to destruction. The commander adds tactical coordination and the ability to summon reinforcements.

## Combat Behavior

### Both Types

- **Melee brutes:** Exceptional STR and CON. Longsword is the primary weapon
- **Longbow:** Used only in narrow circumstances — potshots at fleeing enemies they can't catch, when commanded by a commander, or if they were archers in life
- **Martial Fury (Bonus Action):** Extra attack at the cost of granting advantage on incoming attacks. Warriors use this **every round** — their compulsion overwhelms combat sense. They're resistant to nonmagical physical damage so the tradeoff is acceptable, but they can't adjust if facing magic weapons
- **Resistant** to nonmagical BPS damage
- **Not mindless:** Can be talked to, flattered, and offended. They haunt the locations of their deaths and want adulation for the valorous deeds they believed they could perform

### Sword Wraith Warrior

- Animal-level INT, below-average WIS — no independent tactical judgment
- Martial Fury is always active, reflexively and instinctually
- Engages the nearest attacker; prefers the most formidable-looking foe

### Sword Wraith Commander

- Humanoid-typical INT, above-average WIS — better situational awareness
- **Martial Fury:** Smart enough to know it should restrain itself (e.g., when facing 3+ attacks per round), but **unable to** because it's undead and compulsion-driven. Always on
- **Call to Honor (1/day):** Summons 1d4+1 sword wraith warriors. Can only be used after taking damage. Predetermined the number before the encounter based on desired difficulty — don't roll at the table
- Keep summoned warriors within 30 ft of the commander for **Turning Defiance** benefit
- Move summoned warriors as a tightly knit team
- **Target selection:** Better at identifying strategically important targets — support casters with *bless*, Abjuration wizards with Arcane Ward, etc. Rather than leaving the front line, directs a warrior to eliminate the asset (rush or Longbow depending on distance and obstacles)

## Key Decision Points

| Situation | Decision |
|---|---|
| Combat begins | Charge the nearest, most formidable-looking enemy |
| Every round | Martial Fury is always active (both types) |
| Commander takes damage | Call to Honor immediately — summon warriors |
| Support caster identified | Commander directs a warrior to eliminate them (melee rush or Longbow) |
| Enemy fleeing out of melee range | Switch to Longbow for potshots |
| Summoned warriors arrive | Keep within 30 ft of commander; move as a team |

## Retreat Conditions

- **Never retreat.** All sword wraiths fight until destroyed — this is the core expression of their death-before-dishonor compulsion. There is no HP threshold, no morale break, no tactical withdrawal. They are undead and they are obsessed.

> [!tip] DM Tip
> Sword wraiths don't have to be hostile on sight. They're closer to ghosts — they can be engaged in conversation, flattered about their martial prowess, or provoked by insults to their honor. A social encounter that goes wrong becomes a combat encounter fast. For the commander's Call to Honor, pre-set the number of summoned warriors rather than rolling — it gives you control over encounter difficulty and avoids a lucky roll turning a medium fight into a deadly one.
