# Drow Tactics, Part 1

> [Source](https://www.themonstersknow.com/drow-tactics-part-1/)
> Bestiary: [[Elves-Drow]]

---

## Tactical Summary

- Covers base **drow** (guard/scout) and **drow elite warrior**
- All drow: 120 ft darkvision, Sunlight Sensitivity, Stealth proficiency — ambush fighters operating at night/underground
- Innate spells: *dancing lights* (at will), *darkness* and *faerie fire* (1/day each)
- Poisoned hand crossbow bolts (DC 13 CON; fail by 5+ = unconscious) — drow manufacture their own poison

## Combat Behavior

- **Base drow**: patrol in darkness using Stealth; detect enemies at 120 ft; fan out and Hide; one drow casts *faerie fire* to illuminate targets; rest attack with surprise using poisoned crossbows
- **Ideal lighting**: dim light for drow (darkvision works best); *dancing lights* sheds dim light; *faerie fire* gives advantage on illuminated targets while drow remain unseen in darkness
- *Darkness*: drop on enemy spellcasters who are far from non-caster PCs (blinds even darkvision)
- *Dancing lights*: chase a hiding PC with dim light to prevent disadvantage on Perception checks
- Focus fire on dwarves (resistant to poison = irritating; also territorial competitors)
- **Elite warrior**: higher stats, Multiattack (melee only), Parry, poison damage on shortsword (4d6+4 per hit), all "big three" saves
  - Positions between crossbowmen and charging PCs to force OAs
  - Engages the biggest threat in melee; Parries attack rolls of 18–20
  - Dodges when outnumbered until allies focus fire on the biggest threat or it's moderately wounded (≤49 hp), then Attacks
  - In all-elite units: crossbow surprise round → warriors who poisoned targets rush in with shortsword Multiattack; *levitate* for ambush from above (but not the *faerie fire* caster — both need concentration)

## Key Decision Points

- One drow casts *faerie fire*; the rest attack — don't waste multiple actions on it
- *Darkness* is situational: only on isolated spellcasters far from melee PCs
- Elite warriors serve as rearguard for regular drow retreat
- Drow help upward in hierarchy (demonstrates loyalty) — unless not helping proves decisive for advancement

## Retreat Conditions

- **Base drow**: most fight to the death; some Dash away at ≤6 hp; none ever surrender
- **Elite warriors**: Disengage and retreat at ≤28 hp if no regular drow allies remain; the last elite warrior always fights to the death
- Drow are absolutely unwilling to be taken prisoner

> [!tip] The ideal drow ambush: enemies in dim light (*faerie fire*), drow in total darkness. Drow have advantage (faerie fire) while enemies have disadvantage (can't see attackers). Use guerrilla tactics against stronger parties — harass, disrupt rests, exhaust them, then spring the real ambush.
