# Gallows Speaker Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/gallows-speaker-tactics/) | **Date**: January 17, 2023

---

## Tactical Summary

- **Undead hazard, not a monster** in the functional sense. Haunts execution sites, echoing last words and foretelling doom.
- **Compulsion is all talk**: Yammers endlessly but never wanders from its gallows. Suffering Echoes is a side effect, not an intentional attack.
- **Fights only when attacked**. Behavior is wholly mechanistic due to undead compulsion.
- **Defenses**: Dex-primary, resistance to nonmagical physical damage, Incorporeal Movement, 40-ft fly speed.

## Combat Behavior

**Suffering Echoes**: Reflexive when a sentient creature comes close. No recharge — used repeatedly. Far more damage than Foretelling Touch.

**Foretelling Touch** (melee spell attack): Psychic damage + 1d4 penalty on next attack/save. Used only:
1. Against an opponent who just landed a melee hit (retaliation).
2. Against an adjacent target who just *succeeded* on Suffering Echoes save (making next save harder).

**Movement pattern**:
- Does NOT proactively stay out of melee reach.
- Withdraws into air only *after* being hit and retaliating with Foretelling Touch.
- Does not avoid the ensuing opportunity attack (ironically wastes the 1d4 penalty).
- Pulls back only 30 ft (not full 40) — wants Suffering Echoes range next turn.
- **Leashed to gallows**: Never moves more than ~50 ft from it.

## Retreat Conditions

- **Never flees**. Can't leave the gallows. Once attacked, no productive conversation possible.

## DM Tips

> [!tip] Running This Monster
> Best as a hazard guarding something valuable, not a combat encounter. Party has no reason to fight unless they need what's underneath. Seed clues — old "Wanted" posters, dialogue fragments — to reward investigation. The 50-ft leash centered on a battle mat keeps it contained.
