# Sword Wraith Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/sword-wraith-tactics/) | **Date**: July 23, 2020

---

## Overview

Warriors obsessed with glory, slain ignominiously, refusing to stop seeking that glorious victory despite being dead. Evil-aligned but **not necessarily hostile** — they can be talked to, flattered, and offended. They haunt the locations of their demise and want adulation for the valorous deeds they were sure they could perform.

## Shared Traits

- Both types are melee-focused brutes (exceptional STR/CON). Resistant to nonmagical physical damage.
- **Longbow**: Used only narrowly — potshots at fleeing enemies they can't catch, if commanded by a commander, or if they were archers in life. Greatest glory comes from face-to-face combat.

## Martial Fury

- Bonus action: extra attack at the cost of advantage on incoming attacks.
- **Sword Wraith Warrior** (animal INT, below-average WIS): Uses Martial Fury **all the time**, reflexively. Glory-obsession overwhelms combat sense. Can't adjust even against magic weapons.
- **Sword Wraith Commander** (humanoid INT, above-average WIS): Smart enough to know when to restrain itself but **unable to** — it's undead compulsion. Martial Fury is the mechanical expression of the sword wraith's core drive.

## Sword Wraith Commander: Call to Honor (1/day)

- Summons sword wraith warriors. Must be damaged first.
- **Best timing**: As soon as it takes damage (narratively satisfying + mechanically sound).
- Use a **predetermined number** within the range — don't roll. Keep warriors within 30 ft for Turning Defiance. Move as a tight team.

## Target Selection

- **Easily baited**: Engage the nearest attacker. All else equal, attack the most formidable foe to prove prowess.
- Commanders have slightly better strategic sense — can identify support casters (bless, Arcane Ward) as priority targets. Rather than leave the front line, they **direct a warrior** to eliminate the asset (rush or switch to Longbow depending on distance/obstacles).

## Retreat

- **Never**. All sword wraiths fight until destroyed — death-before-dishonor compulsion.
