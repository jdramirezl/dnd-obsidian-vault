# Gibbering Mouther Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/gibbering-mouther-tactics/) | **Date**: November 29, 2016
> **Related Bestiary**: [[Gibbering-Mouther]]

---

## Tactical Summary

- Atypical contour: average Strength, very high Constitution, low Dexterity. A lurking ambush predator.
- Real strength is the bite: 5d6 piercing + can knock prone. Compensates for poor AC and 10 ft speed.
- Aberrant Ground + Gibbering are defensive — keep rescuers away while the mouther devours its prey.
- Ambushes from liquid: hides in mud, muck, quicksand, or dark water. Surfaces when prey passes within 15 ft.

## Combat Behavior

**Opening**: Emerges from concealment in water/mud. Uses Blinding Spittle (if available) on the easiest target, then bites.

**Core loop**: Multiattack — Blinding Spittle first (if recharged), then Bite. Wants to blind or knock prone a victim, then chomp with advantage.

**Aberrant Ground**: Passively softens terrain in 10 ft radius. Failed Str save = speed 0; success = half speed.

**Gibbering**: Automatic within 20 ft. Failed Wis save → confusion-like effect. Wastes rescuers' actions 25–40% of the time.

**Blinding Spittle**: 15 ft range, 5 ft radius. Failed Dex save → blinded = disadvantage on attacks, advantage for mouther.

## Key Decision Points

- Blinding Spittle available? → Use first in Multiattack, then bite.
- Target already blinded or prone? → Bite with advantage. Stay on that target.
- Hit by ranged attack from >60 ft with no current victim? → Dash away from the source.

## Retreat Conditions

- Fights to the death by default — too slow to Dash (10 ft), AC too low for Dodge, not smart enough to Disengage.
- Exception: if hit by ranged attacks from beyond 60 ft, has no victim, and isn't in melee → Dashes opposite direction.

## DM Tips

> [!tip] Running This Monster
> The mouther is a terrain monster. Place it in water, mud, or quicksand where it can ambush. In open ground it's nearly useless — too slow, too visible. The horror comes from it erupting beneath your feet in a sewer or swamp.
