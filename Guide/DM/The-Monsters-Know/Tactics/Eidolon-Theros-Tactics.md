# Eidolon Tactics (Theros)

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/eidolon-tactics-theros/) | **Date**: August 27, 2025
> **Book**: Mythic Odysseys of Theros

---

## Theros Eidolons — Lore

- In Theros, when someone escapes the Underworld, their identity is ripped away. The body becomes one of the Returned; the lost identity becomes an eidolon. Personality without continuity.
- Dangerous eidolons are unhealthy personalities — those who gave in to fears and harmed others.

## Flitterstep Eidolon

- **Exceptional Dex**, low Str, middle-of-the-road mental abilities. Incorporeal hit-and-run attacker. Perception proficiency, Stealth expertise — often strikes from ambush.
- **Defenses**: AC 14 (all Dex) + Blurred Form (disadvantage on all attacks against it) + Evasion (half/no damage on Dex saves) + resistance to nonmagical physical damage + advantage vs turning.
- **Most vulnerable to**: Magic weapons and non-evocation spells. Targets these opponents first, especially spellcasters.
- **Attack pattern**: Flitterstep (bonus action) before first attack for advantage → attack high-priority target (both attacks on same target while focusing down) → zip away without worrying about OAs (Blurred Form). Always finish turn ≥20 ft from nearest opponent.
- When no longer worried about any opponent: divides attacks.
- **Retreat at ≤17 hp**: Subconsciously fears permanent destruction.

## Ghostblade Eidolon

- **Former warriors**. Lower Dex, higher Str/Int/Cha than flitterstep. Charges into melee.
- Shares Blurred Form and Turn Resistance but **lacks Evasion** — problem with ALL spellcasters. Tries to take them out first.
- **Compulsion**: When confronted by a magic weapon wielder, MUST accept the challenge. Can't help it.
- **In melee**: Stays engaged until foe is subdued. If targeted by spellcaster while fighting nonmagical-weapon wielder → breaks off to pursue caster. If melee opponent intervenes while fighting caster → divides Ghostblade attacks between both, stays put.
- **Never retreats. Fights until destroyed** (fleeing would be shameful).

## DM Tips

> [!tip] Running Theros Eidolons
> Flitterstep eidolons are frustrating to fight — Blurred Form + Evasion + hit-and-run makes them slippery. Force players to use magic weapons or targeted spells. Ghostblade eidolons can be baited with magic weapons — wave one to draw it away from squishier party members.
