# Star Spawn Tactics, Part 2

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/star-spawn-tactics-part-2/) | **Date**: June 21, 2018

---

## Psychic Mirror Addendum (Hulk)

- Psychic Mirror = "Psychic Amplifier" — for every *x* psychic damage the hulk would take, every creature within 10 ft takes *x*.
- Mind flayer Mind Blast example: Hulk fails INT save (80% chance) → full damage broadcast to all PCs within 10 ft, *even if they made their own saves*. Downside: hulk is stunned.
- Two hulks within 10 ft: Crawford ruling — same feature doesn't stack, but additional hulks extend the broadcast range.
- A seer can lob Psychic Orb at a hulk surrounded by enemies → hulk rebroadcasts damage, takes none itself.

## Star Spawn Seer

- Ability contour: INT/WIS peak, CON third. Support spellcaster / quasi-brute using mental attacks. DEX/WIS save proficiency (CON is a weakness). Immune to cold and nonmagical physical damage.

### Out-of-Phase Movement

- Like Incorporeal Movement but damages creatures it passes through. Small incentive to phase through opponents offensively.

### Bend Space (Reaction)

- Not just an escape hatch — use defensively *and* offensively simultaneously:
  - Bait an attack while surrounded → swap with a **hulk** (which then uses Reaping Arms).
  - Swap with a **mangler** to deliver Flurry of Claws to an otherwise unreachable target.

### Collapse Distance (Recharge 6)

- Likely once per encounter. Best against a target with ≥2 allies within 10 ft. Teleport the target somewhere dangerous (under a mangler, over a chasm).
- Less effective vs high-WIS targets (clerics, druids, rangers) — but star spawn grues' Aura of Madness (disadvantage on saves) can make it nearly irresistible.

### Attacks

- **Psychic Orb** (ranged): Use against front-line fighters, paladins, barbarians — hang back.
- **Comet Staff** (melee, two-handed): Close with marksmen and spellslingers. Can Bend Space to swap with a mangler to reach them.
- Lob Psychic Orb at a hulk surrounded by enemies for free AoE via Psychic Mirror.

### Retreat

- At ≤61 hp → Dash away using Out-of-Phase Movement. Most direct route, unobstructed by enemies/walls/floors (not ceiling — no fly speed).

## Star Spawn Larva Mage

- Legendary boss. Immune to paralysis/restraint (must be stunned to stop). Resistant to cold, nonmagical physical. Extraordinary CON, exceptional INT/STR → "psychic brute."
- Wants to be within 20 ft of ≥4 enemies.

### Actions

- **Plague of Worms** (Recharge 6): Big gun. Use while standing within 10 ft of as many opponents as possible. Ideal: surprise → drop into party center → Plague. This is the opening play if opponents are near each other.
- **Dominate Monster** (3/day): Better mind controller than a mind flayer (high DC + 3 uses). Use to round up scattered opponents for a Plague of Worms. Also: make PCs attack each other.
- **Slam** (10 ft reach): Only in self-defense. Otherwise *eldritch blast* (3 targets, 120 ft) deals 3× more damage.
- ***Eldritch blast***: Fallback against uncooperative distant opponents.

### Feed on Weakness

- 10 temp hp every time a creature within 20 ft fails a save. Slam's poison save triggers this. Synergizes with hulks forcing CON/DEX saves and grues imposing disadvantage.

### Legendary Actions (all cost 2)

- **Slam**: Only when needing self-defense + temp hp simultaneously.
- **Feed**: Good when opponents are restrained by Plague of Worms and Slam isn't needed.
- **Cantrip** (*eldritch blast*): Fallback if neither Feed nor Slam is beneficial.

### Endgame

- **Does not retreat**. At ≤32 hp: positions within 60 ft of all enemies → casts ***circle of death*** centered on itself. 2/3 chance of surviving its own spell. Each opponent takes ~21 necrotic.
- **Return to Worms**: If reduced to 0 hp, becomes a swarm of insects. Dashes away across walls/ceiling. Re-forms the next day.
