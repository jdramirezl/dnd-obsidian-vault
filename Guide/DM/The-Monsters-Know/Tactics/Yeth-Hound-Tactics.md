# Yeth Hound Tactics

## Tactical Summary

Evil fey nocturnal predator. Exceptional Str, very high Dex and Con — brute + shock attacker. Immune to nonmagical/nonsilvered physical damage, charmed, exhaustion, frightened. 40 ft. flying speed. Keen Hearing and Smell. Sunlight Banishment sends it to the ethereal plane — hunts only at night, most dangerous just past twilight.

## Hunting Ground

Ideal terrain: **difficult terrain full of natural hazards** — slopes, ditches, cliffs, roots, ravines, sinkholes. Flying speed means terrain doesn't inhibit the hound. Baleful Baying forces frightened victims to flee "by the most direct route, even if hazards lie that way."

## The Chase (Use DMG Ch. 8 Chase Rules)

1. **Detect prey** — Keen Hearing/Smell: ~350 ft. average for normal noise, smell potentially miles away
2. **Baleful Baying** (300 ft. range) — first indication of the hound; determines which PCs it chases (those who fail the save)
3. **Match pace** at ~10 ft. distance — does NOT close yet; waits for exhaustion to build
4. Yeth hound **never gets exhausted** (immune); PCs can only Dash a limited number of times before Con checks
5. **Ideal pounce moment**: target has **2-4 levels of exhaustion**
   - 2 levels: speed halved → hound can bay at 25 ft. and close next turn
   - 3 levels: disadvantage on saves → Baleful Baying more likely to stick
   - 4 levels: HP halved → doom more certain
   - 5 levels: can't run → attack immediately
6. **Final Baleful Baying** within 10-25 ft. → Bite on next turn (frightened target = more than double expected damage)

## Target Selection

- Ignores targets **not frightened** of it (can smell fear)
- If plucky PC makes save and stands ground → runs past them, chases someone else
- If hit with **magic or silvered weapon** → immediately abandons that target, switches direction
- Will attack an unfrightened target only if they have 4-5 exhaustion levels, or 2+ exhaustion and all other targets are immune

## Retreat

Seriously wounded (≤ 20 hp) by **nonphysical damage** → Dashes away. No other retreat trigger.

## Pack Encounters

Usually commanded by archfey or evil creature. A pack (4+) is Deadly for mid-level parties; PCs need level 9+ to handle equal numbers, level 11+ without significant risk.
