# Kuo-toa Tactics

> **Source:** [The Monsters Know — Kuo-toa Tactics](https://www.themonstersknow.com/kuo-toa-tactics/)
> **Bestiary:** [[Kuo-toa]]

---

## Tactical Summary

Kuo-toa are subterranean, semi-aquatic humanoids with Sunlight Sensitivity and 120 ft darkvision. High Strength but average Dex/Con. They need numerical superiority and fight to restrain and drown opponents. They prefer melee (Sticky Shield only works in melee) and always attack in pairs or better.

## Combat Behavior

### Regular Kuo-toa
- **Only attack with ≥2:1 numerical advantage.** Flee (Dash) when outnumbered.
- **Net + Stab combo:** One kuo-toa nets (restrains target), ally stabs with spear. Then flank the target.
- **Drowning tactic (if water available):** Net → grapple → drag into water to drown. Sequence matters: net first (restrain), then grapple, then drown. Target can escape grapple OR net per action, but not both.
- **Sticky Shield:** Reaction to disable an opponent's melee weapon. Only works in melee — another reason they prefer close combat.
- Flee (Dash) when **≤7 HP**. Swim away if possible.

### Kuo-toa Whip (cleric)
- Fights alongside regular kuo-toa. Pincer staff grapples on a melee hit (much higher success than skill contest).
- **If ally acts first:** Ally nets, then whip Multiattacks (bite + pincer staff). If grapple succeeds, drowns target next turn.
- **If whip acts first:** Readies Attack action triggered by ally's net. Gets pincer staff attack as reaction (no Multiattack).
- **Spells:** *Bane* if nets are mostly failing (3 nearest targets). *Shield of faith* if one PC has eluded nets and is attacking the whip. *Sacred flame* vs enemy spellcasters (not ranged attackers — they have high Dex).
- Flees (Dash) at **≤26 HP**. Doesn't help allies disengage.

### Kuo-toa Archpriest
- Stands outside the net-and-stab tactics. Very high Str/Con, decent Dex, electric scepter.
- **Turn 1:** *Spiritual weapon* (bonus action, no concentration, bonus attack every round for 10 rounds).
- **Turn 1 also (if standing water + ceiling ≤20 ft):** *Control water* (Flood) — fills room, enemies can't breathe. If PCs already dragged into water, Whirlpool option instead.
- **No water available:** *Spirit guardians* instead (requires concentration, mutually exclusive with *control water*).
- **Mass cure wounds:** When ≥6 allies are moderately wounded (kuo-toa ≤12 HP, whips ≤45 HP).
- **Default melee:** Multiattack with scepter (bludgeoning + lightning) when no advantageous spell to cast.
- Flees (Dash) at **≤38 HP**. Won't stand ground over treasure or holy site. Swims away if possible.

## Key Decision Points

1. **Net first, always.** The entire kuo-toa combat doctrine revolves around restraining targets before attacking.
2. **Archpriest's concentration:** *Control water* (Flood) is the default if water is present. *Spirit guardians* only when there's no water.
3. **Drowning sequence:** Net → grapple → drag underwater. The whip's pincer staff makes grappling much more reliable than regular kuo-toa attempts.

## Retreat Conditions

- **Regular kuo-toa:** Dash away at **≤7 HP** or when outnumbered. Swim if possible.
- **Kuo-toa whip:** Dash away at **≤26 HP**. Doesn't cover allies' retreat.
- **Kuo-toa archpriest:** Dash away at **≤38 HP**. Won't die for treasure or a holy site.

> [!tip] DM Tip
> Kuo-toa habitations should exist on a continuum from Venice to Atlantis — always have water at hand. The archpriest's *control water* (Flood) in a room with a ≤20 ft ceiling is nearly an automatic win since PCs can't breathe underwater. *Spiritual weapon* is always the first cast — free bonus attack every round with no concentration.
