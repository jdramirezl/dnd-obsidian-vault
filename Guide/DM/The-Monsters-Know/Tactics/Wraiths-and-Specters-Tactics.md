# Wraiths and Specters Tactics

> [!warning] Source URL returned 404 — this article may have been consolidated or removed from the blog. Tactics reconstructed from the book *The Monsters Know What They're Doing*.

## Specter

Incorporeal undead with high DEX and low everything else. Flies 50 ft, moves through solid objects. Sunlight Sensitivity. Life Drain is its only attack — reduces target's max HP on hit (CON save to halve).

- Ambush attacker — phases through walls/floors to strike, retreats back through solid matter
- Targets isolated or sleeping creatures
- No self-preservation instinct — fights until destroyed
- Resistant to acid, cold, fire, lightning, thunder; resistant to nonmagical physical damage
- Immune to necrotic, poison; immune to charmed, exhaustion, grappled, paralyzed, petrified, poisoned, prone, restrained, unconscious

## Wraith

More powerful incorporeal undead. Exceptional DEX, high CHA. Flies 60 ft, moves through objects. Sunlight Sensitivity.

### Key Features

- **Life Drain**: Necrotic melee reducing max HP (CON save to halve). Target killed rises as a specter under wraith's control
- **Create Specter**: Raise a humanoid dead ≤1 minute as a specter (action) — how wraiths build armies
- Same resistances/immunities as specters

### Combat Behavior

- More tactically aware than specters — high enough INT and WIS to choose targets wisely
- Prioritizes low-CON targets to kill quickly and raise as specters mid-combat
- Uses incorporeal movement to phase through walls, strike, and retreat
- Creates specters from freshly killed enemies or nearby corpses
- Wraith + specter swarm = action economy snowball — the real danger
- No retreat behavior — fights to destruction

### DM Notes

- Encounters escalate fast if PCs can't deal radiant damage
- A wraith in a dungeon with recent corpses can start with specter reinforcements already in play
- Pair with darkness and tight corridors to maximize incorporeal advantage
