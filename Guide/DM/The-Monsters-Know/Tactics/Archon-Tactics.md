# Archon Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/archon-tactics/)

---

## Tactical Summary
- Lawful good celestial guardians from *Morte's Planar Bestiary* (Planescape): lantern, hound, and warden
- Like angels, archons **subdue rather than kill** (except vs. fiends/undead)—only melee can knock out without killing
- Lantern archons snipe at range, swarm 3-to-1 for melee subdual; hound archons are bold brutes disguised as dogs; warden archons are immovable portal guardians
- Aura of Menace is free but low DC—nice when it works, don't count on it
- All fight to the death out of duty

## Combat Behavior
- **Lantern Archon**: Radiant Strike at range. For subdual, calls backup to outnumber 3-to-1 before closing. Teleport (substituted in Multiattack) to reposition 60 ft. May bait OAs to burn enemy reactions for allies. Incorporeal Movement to pursue through locked doors
- **Hound Archon**: Disguised as dog (Change Shape, bonus action to revert). Bite first (prone chance) → Shining Blade with advantage if prone. Teleport to close 80–160 ft gaps. Draws attacks to protect others
- **Warden Archon**: Stations 11.25 ft from portal center—nothing slips past. 30 ft truesight defeats invisibility. Claw auto-grapples; can only grapple one at a time. Tracker's Bite on additional intruders. If overwhelmed, shuts down the portal entirely. Targets healers first (clerics, druids, bards), then paladins

## Key Decision Points
- Lantern: stay at range to kill, close to melee to subdue—only close when outnumbering 3-to-1
- Hound: always Bite first for prone chance, then Shining Blade. Drop disguise when combat starts
- Warden: if blitzed by multiple creatures, shut down the portal instead of attacking

## Retreat Conditions
- None. All three types fight to the death.

> [!tip] Running This Monster
> Archons make excellent "you're not supposed to fight this" encounters—they're reasonable, they'll talk, and they genuinely don't want to hurt the party. Make the players feel like the aggressors.
