# Karrnathi Undead Soldier Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/karrnathi-undead-soldier-tactics/) | **Date**: November 20, 2020

---

## Tactical Summary

- **Necromancer-raised soldiers** from the kingdom of Karrnath (Eberron). Bulk of Karrnath's army. Many sealed away after the war — not all.
- **Very high Str/Con, respectable Dex/Int/Wis**: Brute melee fighters that close distance fast, but with genuine tactical flexibility.
- **Not dumb undead**: Clever and perceptive. Compulsion is to *triumph* by whatever means. Exercise sound independent judgment — will build a bridge over a trench, not stop or march into it.
- **Immune to charm, frighten, poison**. Darkvision 60 ft.

## Combat Behavior

**Approach — Longbow barrage**: Multiattack with Longbow while closing. Three shots per turn.

**Night march tactic**: Divide into two equal groups. Group A Dashes forward while Group B keeps shooting. Next round, Group B shoots while Group A Dashes to catch up. No letup in the barrage. The 60–150 ft darkvision gap is covered in two rounds this way.

**Beyond 150 ft in darkness**: Already have disadvantage from long range — darkness doesn't make it worse. Keep shooting.

**Melee — Longsword (two-handed)**: AC doesn't include a shield. Switch to two-handed longsword at close range.

## Target Priority

1. **Anyone dealing radiant damage** — the only damage type that prevents them from getting back up. Radiant = prime target.
2. **Identifiable clerics and paladins** — targeted by default (known radiant sources).
3. **Area-effect spellcasters** using save-based spells — especially *fireball*, *chain lightning*. High priority for elimination.
4. **Charm/frighten/poison effects are irrelevant** — don't concern them.

## Parry Reaction

Uses Parry when an attacker rolls 17–19 to hit. **Exception**: If being double-teamed and the other attacker is a cleric or paladin, holds reaction for that attacker's potential hit instead.

## Force Tactics

- **With numerical superiority**: Portion breaks through or outflanks the enemy line to reach backline targets.
- **If the line is solid**: 15–20% hang back behind the front, switch to bows, and pincushion threatening backline casters.
- **Swarm clerics/paladins** on the front line when numbers allow.
- **Ideal force ratio**: ≥2:1 against foes. Minimum deployment: 4 soldiers. Preferred: 6–8, especially against large parties.
- **Never attack alone**.

## Retreat Conditions

- **No self-preservation instinct**: Never retreat on their own.
- **Living commander orders retreat**: Disengage (vs. multiple melee opponents) or Dodge (vs. single opponent) while withdrawing.

## Encounter Design

- Best used against high-level PCs due to the 2:1 ratio preference.
- A handful works against level 7+ parties with only a couple front-line warriors.

## DM Tips

> [!tip] Running This Monster
> These are professional soldiers, not shambling zombies. Run them with military discipline — coordinated fire-and-maneuver, flanking attempts, priority targeting of healers. The leapfrog Dash/shoot approach at night is their signature move. They're relentless and unresponsive to parley — pure killing machines under orders.
