# Grell Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/grell-tactics/) | **Date**: November 28, 2016
> **Book**: Monster Manual

---

## Overview

- **Hovering brain with stinging tentacles**. Far Realm aberration — pairs well with mind flayers and intellect devourers.
- Above-average physical stats, Str-biased → **hit-and-run attacker**.
- Flies (hover), high Stealth proficiency. Immune to lightning. Blind but has blindsight.
- Intelligence = average humanoid. Self-preservation intact.

## Modus Operandi: Grab and Go

1. **Hovers in the air**, waiting patiently and still for a victim to pass below.
2. **Tentacle attack first** — successful hit grapples + restrains target, and forces a Con save vs paralyzing poison.
3. **Bite with advantage** (target is restrained).
4. **Floats away** with grappled victim. Movement halved while grappling → first move is **straight up**, out of melee reach.

> [!tip] Running the Grell
> The grell doesn't hang around. Once it has a victim, it leaves to eat in peace. This is a snatch-and-retreat predator, not a stand-and-fight monster.

## Target Selection

- Smart enough to plan and strategize, but may misjudge targets (e.g., grabbing a stout halfling, not realizing they're tough).
- **Colony grell** know dwarves make poor prey (poison resistance). Lone/feral grell may not know this.
- Merely being outnumbered usually isn't enough to deter it from attacking common humanoids.

## Negotiation

- Speaks its own language. Room for negotiation if PCs can communicate.
- Classifies most beings as "edible" — not inclined to negotiate with food.
- If party proves they're "great eaters" (capable of killing a grell), prospects improve.

## Retreat

- Flees when **seriously injured (≤22 hp)**.
- Smart enough to **Disengage** before floating away.
- May gabble a petulant concession in its own language — clue that it's capable of speech.
