# Dragon Tactics: Black Dragons

> [!info] Source
> Black dragon tactics are covered in the broader "Dragon Tactics, Part 1" article on chromatic dragons. This note extracts the black-dragon-specific content and the general chromatic dragon framework that applies to all ages.

## General Chromatic Dragon Framework

All dragons share: flight (2× land speed), one alternative movement (black = swim), high STR/CON (brute profile), proficiency on DEX/CON/WIS/CHA saves, Perception and Stealth proficiency, blindsight, darkvision. Breath weapon recharges on 5–6 (average: once every 3 turns).

## Black Dragon Personality

- **Cruel and brutal** — targets weakest enemies first
- Snarling bullies as young dragons
- Will accept a sufficiently attractive bargain but will **never surrender** or agree to limits on independence

## By Age

### Wyrmling
- Black wyrmlings lack the CON to take on all comers — may use Stealth to avoid combat
- Ambush from water (swim speed)
- Breath weapon first → bite on subsequent turns
- If ganged up on: breath weapon (backing up to catch all melee attackers), then reposition
- **Flees** (Dodge + fly) at ≤40% max hp

### Young
- Gains Claw/Claw/Bite Multiattack
- More willing to stand and fight than wyrmling
- Engages up to 2 melee opponents; at 3+, backs off
- Same breath weapon → reposition → Multiattack loop

### Adult (Legendary Creature)
- Gains **lair actions**, **legendary actions**, **Legendary Resistance**, **Frightful Presence**

#### Lair Actions (Black Dragon)
| Action | Best Against |
|---|---|
| **Grasping tide** (knock prone) | Melee opponents |
| **Swarming insects** (damage) | Anyone in range — good softener |
| **Darkness sphere** (blind) | Everyone, especially spellcasters; dragon has blindsight |

- Can't use same lair action two turns in a row
- Darkness sphere is particularly good if 3+ PCs are within 30 ft of each other

#### Legendary Actions
| Action | Cost | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| **Detect** | 1 | Free Perception check — use when no PC is in Tail/Wing range; triggers on any stealthy movement |
| **Tail Attack** | 1 | Freebie vs. any PC adjacent or up to 15 ft behind; don't use twice if 3+ melee attackers might trigger Wing Attack |
| **Wing Attack** | 2 | All PCs within 10 ft — use at 3+ nearby PCs; knocks prone + bonus fly movement to reposition |

- Wing Attack after serious injury (≤40% hp): knock attackers prone (disadvantage on OAs) → fly away

#### Breath Weapon Positioning
- Always repositions before breathing to catch maximum targets — doesn't care about opportunity attacks
- Black dragon breath is a **60 ft line** (not cone) — harder to catch multiple PCs; worth it at 2+, no-brainer at 3+

#### On Its Turn
1. Breath weapon if available (reposition first to maximize targets)
2. If no breath: Multiattack (Frightful Presence + Claw/Claw/Bite) — Frightful Presence only matters on first use

#### Legendary Resistance
- Uses it every time it fails a save — no conservation

### Retreat Behavior (Black Dragon Specific)
- Black dragons target weakest enemies first
- If opponents have managed to inflict real damage → **something's gone horribly wrong** → skedaddle
- May accept an attractive bargain from PCs but never surrenders or accepts limits on independence
- Seriously wounded (≤40% hp): Wing Attack (knock prone) → bonus fly movement → escape

### Ancient
- Same as adult but larger, higher save DCs, extended ranges — no new features

> [!tip] DM Tips
> - Black dragons are ambush predators — start encounters from water, at night, in swamps
> - The 60 ft line breath weapon is harder to optimize than a cone — set up terrain that funnels PCs
> - Darkness sphere lair action + blindsight is devastating — use it to shut down casters
> - Black dragons flee faster than other chromatics — if the PCs are winning, the dragon is already gone
> - A black dragon that escapes will hold a grudge and come back with a plan
> - Use the variant innate spellcasting (MM sidebar) for extra tactical depth — *darkness* or *insect plague* fit perfectly
