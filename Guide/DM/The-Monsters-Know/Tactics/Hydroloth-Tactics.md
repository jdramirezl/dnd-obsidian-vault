# Hydroloth Tactics

## Tactical Summary

CR 9 yugoloth. Toadlike denizens of the River Styx. Amphibious but vastly prefer water — that's where all advantages lie. Unusual ability contour: extraordinary DEX (1st), INT (2nd), very high CON (3rd). Fast melee assault (no ranged weapon attacks). WIS only 10 — indiscriminate target selection, slow to realize when outmatched.

Immune to acid/poison. Resistant to cold, lightning, physical from nonmagical weapons. Vulnerable to fire (but water submersion grants resistance, canceling it). Magic Resistance, blindsight + darkvision, immune to Styx memory-wipe and mind-reading.

## Multiattack

Choice between two melee attacks (Bite/Bite optimal) or one melee attack + one spell. Spell must exceed the opportunity cost of a second Bite.

## Spell Analysis

### At Will

- **Darkness** — blindsight means no penalty; advantage on attacks vs blinded targets, disadvantage on their attacks. Better on land than underwater (already has Watery Advantage). Cast first in Multiattack, then Bite
- **Detect magic** — assume already active as routine recon before combat
- **Dispel magic** — no concentration! Auto-ends spells ≤3rd level. Priority: *water breathing*. Also *haste, enlarge/reduce, invisibility, protection from evil and good, magic weapon*
- **Invisibility** (concentration) — use when space is open (targets walk out of *darkness*), posture is defensive, or effect doesn't need to last

### 1/Day

- **Control water** (concentration) — ship-killer. **Part Water** under a ship = falls into trench, topples, floods. **Flood** turns shore into hydroloth turf. Can't use with *darkness* simultaneously
- **Fear** (concentration) — targets drop held items. In water, magic weapons *sink*. Against 2+ magic-weapon wielders in AoE, excellent. Hydroloth's loot stash should contain nice magic weapons from past victims
- **Crown of madness** (concentration) — 120 ft range for indirect melee damage. Niche
- **Water walk** — cast on allies to prevent Styx memory erasure. Might sell this service to PCs

### Steal Memory (1/day)

4d6 psychic + potentially wipes all proficiencies, spells, communication. DC 16 INT save. Use against whoever has the poorest INT save, period. No strategic targeting. If no good target (mod > +1), skip it. Not an attack or spell — doesn't break *invisibility*.

## Combat by Mission

### Memory Theft (Espionage)

Approach within 60 ft under *invisibility* → Steal Memory (doesn't break invisibility) → leave. Target has a splitting headache and no explanation. This is a quest hook, not a combat encounter.

### Ship Attack

1. Approach under *invisibility*, position underneath
2. *Detect magic* sweep for magic weapons and buffs
3. Back to *invisibility*, move 40 ft away
4. *Control water* — Part Water opens trench (ship falls, topples, floods)
5. In-water: Watery Advantage, 40 ft swim vs enemies' 15 ft
6. Default: Bite/Bite most isolated enemy. *Fear* if magic weapons found. *Dispel magic* if troublesome buff. *Darkness* if 3+ enemies clustered

### Shore Raid

Lead with *control water* (Flood). Can't use other concentration spells simultaneously. If concentration broken: another Flood if available, else *darkness* or *invisibility*. Keep back to open water to bait opponents in.

## Retreat Conditions

- **Off home plane:** Never flees — essence re-forms
- **Home turf, seriously wounded (≤54 hp):** Teleport 60 ft away, swim at top speed

> [!tip] DM Tip
> Three distinct MOs for three story needs. Memory theft is a mystery hook. Ship attacks are devastating set-pieces (Part Water is jaw-dropping). Shore raids are attrition. Their loot stash should contain magic weapons from *fear* victims over the years.
