# Dragon Tactics, Part 2 — Metallic Dragons

> [!summary]
> Metallic dragons are the good counterparts to chromatic dragons. Statistically near-identical, they differ in four key ways: social skill proficiencies, Change Shape (some), only two lair actions, and a second nonlethal breath weapon. Encounters are fundamentally social — combat is a last resort.

---

## How Metallic Dragons Differ from Chromatic

| Feature | Chromatic | Metallic |
|---|---|---|
| Social skills | None (except green) | All have social proficiencies |
| Breath weapons | 1 (damaging) | **2** (1 damaging + 1 nonlethal) |
| Lair actions | 3 | **2** |
| Change Shape | Never | Ancient brass/copper; adult+ bronze/gold/silver |
| Alignment | Evil | Good |
| Combat initiation | Dragon attacks PCs | **Rarely** — PCs usually start it |

## Metallic Dragon Personalities

| Dragon | Social Style | Young Personality |
|---|---|---|
| **Brass** | Conversation, compliments, bargaining | Prattling blabbermouth |
| **Bronze** | Reads PCs' intentions before engaging | Shy observer |
| **Copper** | Bluffs and brags, outrageous stories | Clown and comedian |
| **Gold** | Conversation and bargaining; detects insincerity | Aloof and stuck-up |
| **Silver** | Exchanges knowledge | Sweetheart |

---

## Universal Metallic Tactics by Age

### Wyrmling
- Friendly and gentle, if aloof
- Brass/copper/silver: may walk right up and try talking (Draconic)
- Bronze/gold: hide and observe, won't flee unless PCs are belligerent
- **If attacked:** nonlethal breath weapon once → flee
- Damaging breath only if already hurt; Bite only if cornered

### Young
- Brass/copper/silver initiate social interaction; bronze/gold generally don't
- **If encounter turns hostile:**
  - Bronze/gold: fly away immediately
  - Copper: Deception to misdirect → Slowing Breath → flee
  - Brass: talk first → Sleep Breath → flee
  - Silver: may not realize danger → Paralyzing Breath → flee
- **Reposition before breath weapons** to catch max targets in cone AoE

### Adult (Legendary)
- Would rather talk, but **will initiate combat** against PCs with evil intent
- **Nonlethal first:** "cloud" lair action + nondamaging breath weapon
- **Multiattack before damaging breath** — Frightful Presence may deter opponents
- Damaging breath is a **last-ditch trump card**
- **Legendary Resistance:** use every failed save

### Ancient
- Same as adult with bigger numbers; no new features

## Lair Action Categories

| Category | Examples | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Cloud actions** | Brass Cloud of Sand, Bronze/Silver Fog Cloud, Copper Mud | Obscure vision, nonlethal opener |
| **Push actions** | Brass Strong Wind, Bronze Thunderclap, Copper Spike Growth, Silver Cold Wind | Damage and/or inhibit movement |
| **Gold (unique)** | Glimpse of the Future, Banishment | Advantage on everything / remove one enemy |

---

## Retreat and Escape

> [!important] Retreat at ≤70% hp
> Unless defending their lair, adult/ancient metallic dragons Disengage → fly away at max speed, or use alternative movement if attackers can't follow.

## Change Shape Tactics
- Take human form among humans, elven form among elves, beast form in wilderness
- If threatened in disguise: **talk their way out**, even surrender
- Once eyes are off them: revert to dragon form or assume a different escape form
