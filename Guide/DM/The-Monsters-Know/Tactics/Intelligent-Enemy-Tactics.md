# Intelligent Enemy Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/intelligent-enemy-tactics/) | **Date**: December 30, 2019

---

## Tactical Summary

- **Meta-article** on running creatures and NPCs with high or superhuman Intelligence. Not a specific monster entry — a DM philosophy guide.
- **Core concept — "quickness of apprehension"**: Highly intelligent creatures rapidly recognize the importance of what they see and hear. They read the room instantly.
- **Int 19+ = superhuman**: Can glean precise information about opponents at a glance — treat as if reading the character sheet. Anything the DM knows, the creature knows, as long as a plausible information vector exists.
- **Strategy vs. tactics distinction**: Strategy = overall plan exploiting comparative advantages/disadvantages. Tactics = specific techniques to execute the strategy.

## Combat Behavior

**Reading opponents**: A highly intelligent creature can distinguish a fighter from a paladin, a wizard from a sorcerer, a Life cleric from a Light cleric. It gauges Strength from weapon strikes, Dexterity from dodges, Constitution from hits taken, mental stats from callouts. It notes magical weapons and their properties.

**Exploiting weaknesses**: Aware of its own vulnerabilities and actively compensates — through allies that counter its counters, traps, protective magic, alarms, escape routes, or defensible terrain.

**Tilting the field**: Intelligent enemies don't fight on featureless plains. They construct environments favorable to themselves and unfavorable to foes.

**Learning across encounters**: If the same opponent is fought twice, the creature remembers everything — who was dangerous, what worked, what didn't. A spellcaster villain will study new spells specifically targeting those PCs.

**Contingency plans**: Smart creatures anticipate failure modes. The DM can use knowledge of players' go-to strategies and assume the villain predicted them. If the stat block doesn't offer many options, the terrain, home base, or inventory does.

## Group Intelligence

**Smart commanders deploy minions strategically**: Assign specific minions to counter specific PCs based on matchups (e.g., grell vs. archer to restrain and ground them, mob of commoners vs. paladin who won't fight civilians).

**Smart teams synergize**: Each member knows allies' capabilities and looks for combo opportunities. Example: yuan-ti nightmare speaker casts *hold person* → malisons attack paralyzed targets with advantage → abomination uses *fear* to drive enemies toward a concealed medusa.

## Information Gathering

- Agents in other locations sending reports
- Scrying or communing with supernatural entities
- Spies embedded in organizations, dominated critters watching terrain
- Psychic telepathy, staying mobile under stealth/disguise
- Racing to acquire powerful esoteric knowledge before rivals

## DM Tips

> [!tip] Running Intelligent Enemies
> The key insight: a very intelligent creature has the benefit of knowing more of what you, the DM, know — especially what you know and your players don't. Use your meta-knowledge of the campaign as the villain's actual intelligence. Pause to think when needed — that's simulating the creature's mental agility, not cheating.
