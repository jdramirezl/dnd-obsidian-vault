# Giant Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/giant-tactics/) | **Date**: March 28, 2017
> **Related Bestiary**: [[Giants]]

---

## Tactical Summary

- All giants have extraordinary Strength/Constitution, low Dexterity — brutes by default, but each race has distinct personality and tactics.
- Rock throwing is universal but secondary — Multiattack applies only to melee. Rocks fill the gap when targets are out of reach.
- Each type maps to a personality: hill = animal, stone = artist, frost = orc, fire = hobgoblin, cloud = trickster, storm = noble.

## Combat Behavior by Type

### Hill Giant
- Stupid, mean, impulsive. Charges immediately.
- Throws rocks while closing if target is >50 ft away — even at long range with disadvantage.
- Switches to melee Multiattack once within 50 ft.

### Stone Giant
- Introverted, artistic. Prefers to drive trespassers off rather than kill.
- Uses Stone Camouflage to hide, throws first rock from hiding (advantage).
- Does not close distance — stays at range. Rocks are deterrents.
- Rock attacks can knock targets prone; once down, considers the job done and moves to next target.

### Frost Giant
- Aggressive brutes by ideology. Throw rocks only until within 50 ft, then charge.
- May target the physically strongest opponent as a dominance gesture.
- Fight to the death. Retreat is cowardice.

### Fire Giant
- Militaristic and disciplined. Close distance methodically.
- At 60 ft → hurl rock. Next turn → advance 20 ft, hurl another. Third turn → charge into melee with greatsword Multiattack.
- Savvy target selection: zero in on glass cannons. Use terrain.

### Cloud Giant
- Shrewd and curious. Willing to parley — Insight proficiency reads interlocutors.
- Misty step (bonus action) is the key spell: teleport behind a glass cannon, escape being surrounded, or get in an archer's face.
- May grapple at 5 ft then keep hitting with reach. Other spells are situational.

### Storm Giant
- Generally good-aligned; won't fight unless forced.
- Lightning Strike is the go-to: prefers 2+ targets but still outdamages two greatsword hits against one.
- May pre-cast control weather to set up rain + wind: disadvantage on ranged attacks and Perception.

## Retreat Conditions

- **Hill**: Dash away at ≤42 hp (animal instinct).
- **Stone**: Tactical withdrawal at ≤88 hp (throwing rocks); flee with Dodge at ≤50 hp.
- **Frost**: Never. Fight to the death.
- **Fire**: Disengage at ≤64 hp (melee) or Dodge while retreating (ranged).
- **Cloud**: Fly, gaseous form, or fog cloud + walk away.
- **Storm**: Surrender and negotiate at ≤92 hp.

## DM Tips

> [!tip] Running This Monster
> The ordning matters. Each giant type has a distinct combat personality — don't run them all as "big thing hits hard." Stone giants are hidden snipers who knock you down and move on. Storm giants should almost never be hostile — if they're fighting, something has gone very wrong.
