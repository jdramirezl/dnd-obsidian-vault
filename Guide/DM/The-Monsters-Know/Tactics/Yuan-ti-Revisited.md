# Yuan-ti Revisited (Volo's Guide)

## Unusual Abilities (Customization Options)

- **Acid Slime** (bonus action): 1d10 acid to grappled foe or melee attacker. Free action economy — use turn 1.
- **Chameleon Skin**: passive advantage on Stealth. No decision needed.
- **Shapechanger** (pureblood): same disadvantages as malison/abomination. Skip it.
- **Shed Skin** (bonus action): auto-escape grapple/restrain. Use whenever applicable.
- **Bite** (action option): 1d4 piercing + 1d6 poison — substitute for one of two scimitar attacks.
- **Polymorph into Snake**: temporary, gives target extra HP. Only useful to shut down spellcasters/item users. **Terrifying combo with *divine word***: polymorph into low-HP snake → *divine word* kills instantly.
- **Snake Antipathy**: drive trespassers from sacred places or stall pursuit.
- **Sticks to Snakes**: costs 1 action, creates ally for 10 rounds. Always worth it.

## Yuan-ti Anathema (CR 12)

Super-abomination boss for high-level parties. Extraordinary Str, Con, and mental stats. 30 ft. blindsight, 6 heads (advantage on Perception + saves vs. blinded/charmed/deafened/frightened/stunned/unconscious).

### Spellcasting Priority
- **Round 1-2**: Concentration spell (*darkness* if alone, *entangle* if 4+ enemies, *haste* on lieutenant, or *suggestion* on low-Wis non-elf)
- **Round 3**: ***Divine word*** — coup de grace. Waiting ensures at least one PC is below 20 hp.
- *Fear* and *polymorph* are redundant to other features — don't waste slots.
- Pick plan A and plan B **before combat** (only one concentration spell at a time).

### Retreat
Ageless/immortal — has reason to stay alive despite fanaticism. Seriously wounded (≤ 75 hp) → Disengage + retreat (high speed, climb, swim).

## Yuan-ti Broodguards (CR 2)

Dumb, instinct-driven berserkers. Product of magical transformation — **no survival instinct**. Fight to the death. Use Reckless all the time. Completely indiscriminate targeting.

## Yuan-ti Warlock Variants

All are malison variants with warlock spellcasting (3rd-level slots, only 2 slots). Use spells round 1, then close to melee. 2/day damage boost (Mind Fangs / Death Fangs / Poison's Disciple) — use immediately rounds 1 and 2.

### Mind Whisperer (Type 1)
- Sseth's Blessing: 9 temp hp on killing a foe (tops up, doesn't stack) → **favors wounded targets**
- **Round 1**: *hypnotic pattern* (with allies) — highly effective, thematic
- Then close to melee; save remaining slot for *fly* or *expeditious retreat*

### Nightmare Speaker (Type 3)
- Invoke Nightmare: requires concentration — not great in combat
- Best spells: *hex* (single combat), *hold person* (2 foes at 3rd level), *hunger of Hadar* (4+ targets), *arms of Hadar* (walking bomb — charge in, 10 expected damage each)
- Type 3 = Constrict + scimitar combo in melee

### Pit Master (Type 2)
- **Meershaulk's Slumber** (round 1): burst incapacitation, drops non-elven spellcasters. Use it or lose it.
- Round 2: close to melee. *Vampiric touch* at ≤ 61 hp; *misty step* if surrounded; *counterspell* only vs. *fireball*/*lightning bolt*

### All Variants Retreat
Seriously wounded (≤ 35 hp) → Disengage (with allies) or Dash (alone).
