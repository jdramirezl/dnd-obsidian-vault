# Drow Tactics: House Captains

> [Source](https://www.themonstersknow.com/drow-tactics-house-captains/)
> Bestiary: [[Elves-Drow]]

---

## Tactical Summary

- CR 9 finesse fighter; unusual Multiattack (2 melee + crossbow OR whip); high WIS, decent CHA
- Leader of ambush attackers; Battle Command (passive aura) enhances allied action economy
- Proficient in all "big three" saves — less afraid of enemy spellcasters
- Leaves innate drow spells to underlings; Multiattack is better value

## Combat Behavior

- **Positioning**: front and center; Battle Command is passive area effect centered on captain — allies to sides and behind
- **Multiattack**: 2 melee attacks + crossbow (champion) or whip (tyrant)
- **Whip**: hitting an *ally* gives it advantage on attacks until end of its next turn
  - Best on allies about to make OAs (advantage on OA + next turn's attacks)
  - Even better on allies with Multiattack (advantage on all rolls)
- **Battle Command** (bonus action): Hide (ranged ally in position), melee attack (melee ally), or Dodge (ally under focused fire)
- **Parry** (reaction): reduces melee damage taken

## Key Decision Points

- Won't attack unless odds are very good — encounters should always be Deadly
- Whip vs crossbow based on personality: champion (crossbow) or tyrant (whip)
- Anticipate which foes might move without Disengaging; whip the drow fighting that foe

## Retreat Conditions

- May order retreat if ≥50% of its side is seriously wounded
- The captain itself never retreats on its own injuries — zealot with military training

> [!tip] Time the whip to set up opportunity attacks: whip an ally → ally gets advantage on OA when enemy flees → then advantage on all attacks on its own turn.
