# Beholder Tactics

## Tactical Summary

The beholder is a paranoid, territorial genius that fights exclusively from its lair. It positions itself like a security camera — hovering in a high corner with full sightlines, at least 10 ft. off the ground to stay out of melee reach. It attacks immediately on sight with no negotiation. Eye Rays (120 ft. range) are its primary weapon; it uses them intelligently, matching each ray to the target least likely to resist it.

## Combat Behavior

### Opening
- Attacks immediately when intruders appear — no parley
- Uses Eye Ray legendary actions against each PC who acts before it in initiative
- On its turn, fires 3 random Eye Rays, choosing optimal targets for each

### Eye Ray Targeting (INT 17)
- Matches rays to perceived weak saves:
  - **STR rays** → wizards, sorcerers (low Strength)
  - **DEX rays** → heavy-armor fighters, slow targets
  - **CON rays** → rogues, warlocks (lower Constitution)
  - **WIS rays** → fighters, rogues (non-elves for Sleep/Charm)
- Refines targeting each round: if a ray fails, avoids that save category against that PC; if it succeeds, uses sibling rays on the same target

### Antimagic Cone
- **Not used at range** — it suppresses the beholder's own Eye Rays
- **Used when surrounded** — isolates 1–2 melee opponents in the ~53° cone while firing Eye Rays in other directions
- Prioritizes shutting down spellcasters, but only if it can still target non-casters with rays

### Lair Actions (Initiative 20)
1. **Slippery slime** — preferred when PCs are charging in
2. **Grasping appendages** — preferred when PCs are near walls
3. **Random wall eye** — default / fallback option

### Bite Attack
- Used only as a last resort: target must be in melee range AND resistant to 2+ ray types
- Or if the only remaining threat is a melee combatant who shrugs off rays

## Key Decision Points

| Situation | Response |
|---|---|
| PCs at range (>10 ft.) | Eye Rays only, stay hovering high |
| PCs closing to melee | Consider Antimagic Cone to isolate casters |
| Guarding a passage (under *geas*) | Descends to block — now vulnerable to melee |
| Darkness / Invisibility on PCs | May gamble with Antimagic Cone to suppress concealment and bait PCs into dropping concentration |

## Retreat Conditions

- **In lair:** Fights to the death — always
- **Summoned as sentinel:** Fights to the death
- **Outside lair (foraging):** Flees after taking modest damage (≤126 hp). Disengages, ascends out of reach, then Dashes home

## Death Tyrant Variant
- Negative Energy Cone is **always on** (doesn't suppress its own rays)
- Orients cone to catch the most opponents, including unconscious ones (raises them as zombies on death)
- INT 19 — can effectively "read" PC stat blocks and target weakest saves precisely
- **Never flees**, regardless of circumstances

> [!tip] DM Tips
> - Pre-roll Eye Rays for each round to keep combat flowing
> - Track which rays have failed against which PCs — the beholder remembers
> - Use vertical space: 30+ ft. ceilings make the beholder nearly untouchable by melee
> - Darkness and Fog Cloud hard-counter Eye Rays — the beholder knows this and may use Antimagic Cone as a bluff to get PCs to drop concentration
> - Ranged attackers beyond 60 ft. are the beholder's true weakness — it has no magic resistance against arrows
