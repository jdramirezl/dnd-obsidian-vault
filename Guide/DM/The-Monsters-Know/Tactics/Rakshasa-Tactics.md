# Rakshasa Tactics

## Tactical Summary

CR 13 fiend. DEX + CON highest physical scores, but CHA exceeds all — prefers magic over claws. Expert in Deception + Insight: born con artist. Limited Magic Immunity (immune to spells ≤6th level). Immune to nonmagical physical damage. Vulnerable to piercing from magic weapons wielded by good creatures.

## Spell Analysis

### At Will (no combat use)

- *Detect thoughts, disguise self, mage hand, minor illusion* — social/infiltration tools
- *Disguise self* useful in urban chases (vanish in a crowd)

### 3/Day

- *Charm person, suggestion* — poor in combat (advantage on saves vs hostile casters)
- *Major image* — distraction (fake ally), but dispelled when "hit"
- **Invisibility** — key combat spell. Limited Magic Immunity means *see invisibility* and *true seeing* won't work against it

### 1/Day

- *Dominate person* — same combat drawback as *charm person*, but devastating pre-combat
- *True seeing* — primarily defensive
- *Fly* — risky if opponents have magic ranged weapons (ranged = piercing = vulnerability)
- **Plane shift** — escape hatch, used without hesitation

## Combat Behavior

### Pre-Combat (preferred)

- Keeps presence and nature concealed. Hunts and feeds without open fighting
- If discovered: uses wiles to make PCs look like the villains, turning the community against them
- Lays groundwork for escape while scheming
- Surrounds itself with willing or charmed allies

### If Cornered (all schemes failed)

- **Skirmisher:** Keeps 35-40 ft from opponents. Waits for one to overextend, rushes in for dual-claw Multiattack
- If ally runs up to help target, or target has Extra Attack: Disengage and retreat
- **When engaged by multiple opponents:** Uses *invisibility* to vanish, repositions near a weaker/isolated opponent. Can do this 3 times — make highest and best use
- If no room to retreat: Dodge while circling to find space
- Targets prey-like opponents first (young, weak, isolated, oblivious)
- Claw curse takes effect after combat — no in-fight benefit

### Fly Considerations

- Only use if no opponent has a magic ranged weapon
- If safe: fly up, swoop to attack, fly back out of melee reach
- If magic ranged weapons present: *fly* guarantees opponents use the very weapon that does double damage

## Retreat Conditions

- Moderately wounded (≤77 hp): *plane shift* out immediately
- Struck by a weapon it's vulnerable to: flee
- On foot if duplicity unknown (*disguise self* in crowds/castles)
- *Plane shift* if opponents are too clever for disguise
- Can't be permanently killed except in the Nine Hells — but intensely dislikes being "killed" on the material plane

> [!tip] DM Tip
> Rakshasas are not combat monsters — they're social predators. The fight happens only when everything else has failed. Build the encounter around deception, community manipulation, and the PCs' struggle to expose the rakshasa before it escapes. Piercing magic weapons wielded by good creatures are the silver bullet.
