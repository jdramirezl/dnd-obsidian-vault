# Sorrowsworn Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/sorrowsworn-tactics/) | **Date**: July 2, 2018

---

## Overview

Sorrowsworn are Shadowfell manifestations of mortal negative emotions. They **literally feed off** those emotions. All have 60 ft darkvision and resistance to physical damage from *any* weapon type (not just nonmagical) while out of bright light.

---

## The Wretched

- Extremely low stats across the board except average DEX. Lamprey-mouth horrors.
- **Always outnumber PCs** at least 2:1, preferably 3:1. Best used at levels 4–6 despite low CR.
- **Wretched Pack Tactics**: Advantage with an adjacent ally, but **disadvantage** without one — solo attacks are nearly pointless unless latched on.
- **Bite**: 1d10 damage; on hit, latches on for automatic 1d10 necrotic each turn.
- **Swarm behavior**: 3+ rush a single target. As many as possible latch on. A latched-on target becomes a magnet for other Wretched. A Wretched pulled off re-attacks if allies are still latched. Detaches if all allies on that target are killed.
- **Retreat**: Not based on HP — flee when they no longer outnumber targets 2:1.

## The Lost

- Multi-armed brutes (high STR/CON, decent DEX). Athletics proficiency for grappling. Appropriate solo for mid-level parties; multiple for level 11+.
- **Embrace** (preferred action): Damage ≈ Multiattack + grapple + ongoing psychic damage on target's turn. Taking damage while grappling punishes the target with *more* psychic damage.
- Can grapple only one creature but can Multiattack others while holding a victim. First goal: Embrace a target, then Multiattack defenders.
- **Target selection**: Whoever is most distressed by the party's disorientation.
- **Never flees**. Optionally holds on past unconsciousness until the target is *dead*.

## The Lonely

- Boss for mid-level, nuisance for high-level. Targets emotional isolation — PCs who feel alienated.
- Disadvantage on attacks if not within 30 ft of ≥2 other creatures. No Stealth proficiency → prefers **full darkness**.
- **Rigid Multiattack**: one Harpoon Arm (piercing + grapple) → one Sorrowful Embrace (reel in + psychic). **Psychic Leech** continues draining grappled targets passively.
- Once it has its target(s), **walks off** with them (Dash if unattacked, Dodge if attacked). Speed halved while carrying prey.
- Never lets go voluntarily. If one escapes, mechanistically re-grapples. Drops prey and retreats (Dodging) only when seriously wounded (≤44 hp).

## The Hungry

- Ferocious brute for high-level parties only. Appears when PCs are failing Survival rolls in the Shadowfell.
- Lunges at the most famished party member. **Multiattack**: bite (piercing + necrotic) + claws (damage + grapple + **restrain**). Restrained target → bite with advantage next turn.
- Keeps eating until target is **dead** (not just unconscious). If freed, chases that target. Moves to next victim after finishing one.
- No Intelligence to deviate from script. Retreats via Dash when seriously wounded (≤90 hp).

## The Angry

- Most dangerous sorrowsworn. Only one with enough INT to adapt. Brute that charges directly in.
- **Rising Anger**: Disadvantage on attacks in any round it hasn't taken damage. *Wants* to be hit — baits attacks from those most likely to connect.
- **Multiattack**: Two hook-hand attacks, every round. Switches targets only if another enemy hits more often or with "angrier" attacks (Rage, smites, Colossus Slayer, War domain, high-level evocations).
- Fights as long as enemies fight. If enemies go one full round without attacking it: roars and taunts. **Two full rounds** without attacks: huffs and stalks off.
