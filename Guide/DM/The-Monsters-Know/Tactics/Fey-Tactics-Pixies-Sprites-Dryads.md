# Fey Tactics: Pixies, Sprites, and Dryads

## Pixie

See also: [[Pixie]]

### Tactical Summary

STR 2, CON 8 — never alone, always in overwhelming numbers. They don't fight. Combat only happens because PCs are being jerks.

### Combat Behavior

- First: Superior Invisibility to vanish
- If PCs persist, swarm unleashes spells: *sleep* → non-elven casters; *dispel magic* → counter all PC spells; *confusion*/*phantasmal force*/*polymorph* → fighters; *entangle* → everyone else; *druidcraft*/*dancing lights* → disorientation
- Each spell 1/day per pixie, but dozens means every spell fires every round
- Don't be fair to players picking on pixies

## Sprite

See also: [[Sprite]]

### Tactical Summary

Fighters of fairyland. Longsword = 1 hp (melee, when grabbed). Primary: shortbow with knockout poison. Overwhelming numbers.

### Combat Behavior

- **Ideal range: 40 ft.** If PC approaches closer: Invisibility → reposition 40 ft away → shoot next round
- Volume of fire: make PCs roll a dozen poison saves
- One hit kills a sprite — retreat is about swarm losses, not individual HP

### Retreat Conditions

Swarm flees at 60% casualties.

## Dryad

See also: [[Dryad]]

### Tactical Summary

Unlikely to initiate combat unless PCs destroy their woods or interfere with a charmed companion. Mental > physical abilities. Nearly impossible to corner in a forest.

### Combat Behavior

- Ready *barkskin* as reaction to hostilities; if surprised, first action
- Melee: *shillelagh* (bonus) + club, only if cornered
- **Escape:** Tree Stride (innate, not an action!) through trees within attackers' reach (no OAs) → *entangle* from 60 ft → flee
- Delegates fighting to sylvan allies. Fey Charm for lone trespassers, *druidcraft* for small groups, heavy hitters for real threats

### Retreat Conditions

Withdraws at ≤8 hp, even when angry.

> [!tip] DM Tip
> Pixies and sprites are punishment encounters for murder-hobo behavior. Dryads are social encounters first. The *entangle* + sprite volley combo is a potential TPK — use it to teach respect for the forest, not to wipe the party.
