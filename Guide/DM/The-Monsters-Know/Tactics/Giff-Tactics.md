# Giff Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/giff-tactics/) | **Date**: June 27, 2023

---

## Tactical Summary

- Military hippo-folk with muskets, grenades, and a code of honor. Disciplined colonial marines.
- Shipmates sustain musket fire while advancing, then switch to melee.
- Shock troopers have Siege Monster — focus fire on fortifications to destroy walls before closing.
- Warlords lead from the front with exceptional Charisma and Intimidation expertise. Win the fight in the enemy's mind first.

## Combat Behavior by Type

### Giff Shipmate
- Musket fire while advancing. Multiattack includes musket shots.
- Switch to melee (mace + Headfirst Charge) once in range.
- Lob Fragmentation Grenades over walls to hit defenders behind cover.

### Giff Shock Trooper
- Siege Monster doubles damage against objects/structures.
- Focus musket fire on walls. Don't advance closer than 40 ft until the wall is breached.
- Once a hole is blown, advance with Headfirst Charge + melee.
- Thunder Bombs target creatures only (not objects as written) — anti-personnel, not anti-structure.

### Giff Warlord
- Leads from the front. Double-Barreled Musket + powerful melee + legendary actions.
- Intimidation expertise is the key feature — project irresistible force to coerce compliance before resistance forms.
- Proficient in Insight — will offer concessions for an easy victory without compromising the objective.
- Rallying Cry ends frightened condition on allies.

## Key Decision Points

- Fortified position? → Shock troopers focus fire on walls. Shipmates provide covering fire.
- Warlord present? → Attempt Intimidation contest before combat. DM tells the player the threat is real.
- Open field? → Sustained musket volleys while closing, then melee with Headfirst Charge.

## Retreat Conditions

- Disciplined soldiers — orderly retreats when outmatched, not panicked routs.
- Warlords negotiate terms if the objective can't be achieved.

## DM Tips

> [!tip] Running This Monster
> The warlord's Intimidation is the most interesting tool. When the warlord wins an Intimidation vs. Insight contest, tell the player the threat is real — not that the PC believes it, but that it *is* true. Let them decide how their PC reacts. The giff aren't rude about it — they're projecting inevitability.
