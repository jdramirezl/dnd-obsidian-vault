# Undead Tactics: Skeletons, Zombies and Shadows

> [!info] Source
> [The Monsters Know — Undead Tactics: Skeletons, Zombies, Shadows](https://www.themonstersknow.com/undead-tactics-skeletons-zombies-shadows/)

**Bestiary:** [[Skeletons]], [[Zombies]], [[Shadow]]

---

## Core Concept: Undead Compulsions

Undead aren't evolved creatures — undeath is a curse creating **compulsions**. "Survival" is meaningless to them. How badly injured they are has nothing to do with whether they flee. Tactics are driven by their particular compulsion, not self-preservation.

---

## Skeletons

Compulsion: **obedience to controller's orders** (usually killing interlopers). When idle, revert to habitual behaviors from former life.

### Tactics
- Fight based on **how they fought in life** (former archer maintains 40–80 ft range; former duelist may Dodge or Disengage)
- Most are former guards/shlubs → simple direct melee: engage, swing sword, repeat
- **Literal interpretation** of orders — a skeleton guarding a room breaks off combat with anyone who leaves; may forgo opportunity attacks against someone obviously leaving
- Will retrieve dropped weapons, open doors, avoid hazards, approach by the **easiest** path
- Vulnerable to bludgeoning but not reflective enough to avoid a PC with a mace
- **Fight until destroyed** regardless of damage or opposition

---

## Zombies

Compulsion: **obedience** (same as skeletons but even more mindless). When idle, just stand around.

### Tactics
- Retain **no vestige** of former selves
- Approach by the **most direct** path — even through environmental hazards
- Won't pick up dropped weapons, won't unlatch doors (might break through), won't think
- **Pursue targets** until dead or controller orders them to stop
- **Undead Fortitude** — frighteningly relentless; have them fall prone when "killed" then immediately get back up for dramatic effect
- D&D zombies don't eat victims or create new zombies — but don't tell your players that

---

## Shadows

Compulsion: **siphon vitality from living beings**, especially the pure of heart. Far more dangerous than commonly used.

### Key Features
- Avoid light, especially sunlight (disadvantage on everything)
- **Amorphous** — pass through crack under a door
- **Shadow Stealth** — Hide as bonus action in dim light or darkness
- High Stealth proficiency; resistance to most damage types including normal weapons
- Immune to most debilitating conditions
- Sole weakness: **radiant damage** — clerics/paladins are both juiciest victim AND best equipped to defeat it

### Tactics
1. **Lie in wait** for a victim
2. Use movement + bonus action to approach with Stealth, then **Hide**
3. If Hide succeeds → **Attack same round with advantage** using Strength Drain (action)
4. If Hide fails → keep moving, try again next round
5. DM can say "You saw a shadow move past you" — players may not realize it's a creature at first

### After First Successful Attack
- Can no longer Hide until it moves out of view (unless PCs are in total darkness without darkvision)
- **Compulsion kicks in:** keeps draining the same PC even though slipping away for another surprise attack might be more effective

### What Makes a Shadow Think Twice
- **Radiant damage**, magical weapon strike, or **Turn Undead**
- If hit by one of these → **Disengage** (action), move out of view, **Hide** (bonus action), wait for victim to come within range again

### Strength Drain — Why It's Terrifying
- Average 9 hp necrotic damage per hit
- Reduces target's Strength by **1d4 each hit** (gone until short/long rest)
- Affects attack/damage rolls, weapon/armor use
- **If Strength reaches 0 → the PC dies on the spot** (not unconscious — *dead*)
