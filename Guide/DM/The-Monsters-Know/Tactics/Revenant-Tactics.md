# Revenant Tactics

## Tactical Summary

Undead avenger — driven by compulsion to destroy the one who wronged it. Exceptional STR and CON, above-average DEX and INT, high WIS. Regenerates 10 hp/round unless fire or radiant damage taken. Effectively unkillable — soul returns in a new body 24 hours after destruction. Only *wish* or *true resurrection* can stop it permanently. Darkvision → attacks at night.

## Immunities and Resistances

- Immune to: charmed, exhaustion, frightened, paralyzed, poisoned, stunned
- Resistant to: necrotic, psychic
- Proficient saves: STR, CON, WIS, CHA
- **No** resistance to physical damage — PCs can hurt it, they just can't stop it

## Target Selection

- **Only attacks the one who wronged it** — ignores all others
- Vengeful Tracker: knows exact distance and direction, even across planes
- If target is an NPC the PCs protect: revenant never attacks PCs, relies on allies for interference

## Attack Sequence

### Opening: Vengeful Glare

- Requires line of sight + within 30 ft
- Paralyzes and frightens on failed WIS save
- Paralysis ends when target takes damage; fear persists

### Follow-up: Multiattack

1. **Grapple** (advantage if paralyzed — grapple does no damage so paralysis holds)
2. **Pummel** (advantage if still paralyzed)
3. Subsequent turns: pummel × 2 while grappled

### Adaptation

- Vengeful Glare fails twice → abandons it
- Grapple fails twice → switches to straight pummeling
- If tactics work but wear off → re-uses after a round of normal attacks

## Darkness Advantage

- Target can't see revenant in darkness → blinded → disadvantage on attacks regardless of frightened condition

## Allies

- Against parties level 5+: enlists allies to occupy companions
- Suitable: religious fanatics, former faction members, intelligent undead (ghasts, specters, wights)
- Allies scatter if revenant's body is destroyed

## Retreat

- **Never** — regeneration + soul return means nothing permanently stops it

> [!tip] DM Tips
> - The revenant is a *narrative* threat more than a combat one — it keeps coming back
> - A revenant enlisting the party for its own revenge is a great quest hook
> - *Invisibility* on target stops Vengeful Glare and gives disadvantage on attacks, but Vengeful Tracker still pinpoints location
> - If the revenant kills its quarry, it pummels until dead-dead (3 failed death saves)
