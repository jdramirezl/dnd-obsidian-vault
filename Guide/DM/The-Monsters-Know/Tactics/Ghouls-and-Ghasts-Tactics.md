# Ghouls & Ghasts Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/undead-tactics-ghouls-ghasts/) | **Date**: August 23, 2016
> **Related Bestiary**: [[Ghouls]]

---

## Tactical Summary

- **Claw to paralyze, then bite to devour**: Claws are the attack of first resort (paralysis chance on DC 10 Con save). Once paralyzed, switch to bite — every hit is a crit (4d6+2 damage).
- **Ghouls ignore elves** for paralysis purposes (immune to ghoul claws), but **ghasts can paralyze elves**.
- **Hunger overrides tactics**: Once a ghoul paralyzes a victim, it starts eating immediately and won't stop voluntarily. It will drag prey away rather than fight defenders.
- **Ghasts are the pack leaders**: They direct ghouls to gang up on strong PCs (2–3 per target) while the ghast goes after elves or the strongest PC.
- **Turning Defiance**: Ghouls within 30 ft of a ghast get advantage on saves against Turn Undead. Keep the pack clustered.

## Combat Behavior

**Ghoul opening**: No stealth — they move directly toward the nearest non-elf PC and attack with claws. Repeat claw attacks until paralysis succeeds.

**Paralysis achieved**: Switch to bite attacks with advantage (all hits are crits = 4d6+2 per bite). The victim gets a new save each turn, so the ghoul eats as fast as possible.

**If attacked while eating**: The ghoul tries to drag the paralyzed victim away using full movement (up to 195 lb carrying capacity; speed drops to 5 ft if victim exceeds this). It moves its full distance, then stops to bite again — it cannot resist the compulsion to keep eating. This creates a gruesome tug-of-war cycle.

**Ghast behavior**: Directs ghouls tactically. If ghasts+ghouls outnumber PCs, ghast targets an elf while ghouls gang up on strong PCs (2–3 per target). If outnumbered, ghast takes the strongest PC (leveraging higher Strength, AC, and Stench) while ghouls target weaker PCs for easier kills.

**Ghast Stench**: PCs within melee range may be poisoned (disadvantage on attacks), providing some protection against opportunity attacks when dragging prey away.

**Ranged attacks on a feeding ghoul**: Ghoul ignores them and keeps eating unless reduced to 8 hp or fewer. A ghast hit by ranged attacks more than once (but not seriously wounded, i.e., above 14 hp) gets annoyed and Dashes toward the ranged attacker.

## Key Decision Points

- **Target an elf? (Ghoul)**: No — elves are immune to ghoul paralysis. Skip them entirely.
- **Target an elf? (Ghast)**: Yes — ghast claws paralyze elves too. Ghasts specifically seek out elves.
- **Paralysis succeeded?** → Switch from claws to bite immediately. Every round counts before the victim saves.
- **Victim recovers mid-feast?** → Go back to claw attacks to re-paralyze.
- **Victim reduced to 0 hp?** → Turn on the nearest PC and start clawing again.
- **Ghouls clustering near ghast?** → Yes, stay within 30 ft for Turning Defiance advantage — except when dragging prey away.

## Retreat Conditions

- **Ghoul at 8 hp or fewer**: Dashes away, abandoning its meal.
- **Ghast at 14 hp or fewer**: Dashes away.
- **No morale break from numbers**: Ghouls are compulsion-driven and not daunted by being outnumbered. They approach prey directly regardless of odds.
- **Goal achieved**: Once a ghoul has a paralyzed victim at 0 hp and no other threats, it has no reason to keep fighting — it drags the corpse away to feed.

## DM Tips

> [!tip] Running This Monster
> The horror of ghouls isn't their damage — it's the paralysis-to-crit-bite pipeline and the image of a ghoul dragging a helpless PC away while eating them alive. Describe it graphically. The tactical counter is readied actions and focusing fire on ghouls before they paralyze anyone. Always keep ghouls within 30 ft of a ghast for Turning Defiance.
