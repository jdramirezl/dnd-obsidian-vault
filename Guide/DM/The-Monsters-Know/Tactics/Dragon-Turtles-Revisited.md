# Dragon Turtles Revisited

> [Source](https://www.themonstersknow.com/dragon-turtles-revisited/)
> Bestiary: [[Dragon-Turtle]]

---

## Tactical Summary

- Covers wyrmling, young, and ancient dragon turtle variants from *Fizban's Treasury of Dragons*
- **Wyrmlings**: simple ambush predators — attack to eat, not extort; conceal by staying submerged
- **Young**: territorial and boastful; beginning to grasp treasure and dominance
- **Ancient**: legendary mythic creature on par with greatwyrms; fast brute melee combatant that loves close quarters

## Combat Behavior

- **Wyrmlings**: open with Steam Breath vs 2+ targets; Bite the most wounded; flee at ≤63 hp
- **Young**: stay underwater until Steam Breath ready vs 3+ targets, then dive; first Multiattack targets vessel hull — if hull doesn't lose ≥50% hp, switch to passengers; target whoever took most Steam Breath damage, then shift to whoever hurts them most
- **Ancient**: dramatic leap from water + Steam Breath on deck, then submerge; Tail/Claw/Claw Multiattack (Tail knocks prone → legendary follow-ups with advantage); always prefer Steam Breath over Multiattack when available; Boiling Aura (3 LA) as early as possible; Armor of Storms mythic action for 40 temp hp + retaliatory lightning

## Key Decision Points

- **Young**: don't submerge between attacks unless taking damage from ≥2 sources
- **Ancient**: if PCs deal ≥123 damage in round 1, it pauses — won't risk its life; parley possible but difficult (Draconic/Aquan only); always choose Tail over Claw for legendary Attack action (more damage, reach, proning)
- Ancient effectively has 759 hp + repeated Armor of Storms temp hp

## Retreat Conditions

- **Wyrmlings**: flee at ≤63 hp, submerge and swim away
- **Young**: don't retreat until seriously wounded (≤71 hp)
- **Ancient**: if it commits after round-1 assessment, fights to the bitter end

> [!tip] Underwater combat rules (PHB ch. 9) make ranged weapons auto-miss at long range. Dragon turtles exploit this — you don't see them coming until it's too late. Ancient dragon turtles also get lair actions and regional effects in the Draconomicon section of Fizban's.
