# Jiangshi Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/jiangshi-tactics/) | **Date**: February 9, 2023

---

## Tactical Summary

- **"Hopping vampire"** — reanimated corpse from Chinese folklore. Pronounced *chyahng-shr* (both syllables level high tone). Name means "stiff corpse."
- **20-ft movement speed**: Can't chase anyone down. Not proficient in Stealth or Perception — not an ambush attacker. Must find sleeping/immobile victims or approach in disguise.
- **Exceptional Str/Con, miserable Dex**: Brute melee fighter by necessity.
- **High Wis, very high Int**: Despite the goofy movement, these are smart and self-restrained.
- **Compulsion is punitive**: Seeks revenge against those who wronged it in life, especially those who cut corners on its funeral.

## Combat Behavior — The Killer Combo

The Multiattack is 3× Slam + 1× Consume Energy. The goal is a precise sequence to create a wight:

1. **Slam #1**: Reduce target to 0 hp (unconsciousness)
2. **Slam #2**: Auto-crit against unconscious target within 5 ft → 2 death save failures
3. **Consume Energy**: Imposes a 3rd death save failure → target dies → rises as wight

**Critical detail**: Consume Energy creates a wight only when it *kills* the target, not just reduces to 0 hp. Consume Energy is *not an attack* — no auto-crit, just one death save failure regardless of damage.

## HP Threshold Management

The jiangshi must avoid accidentally KOing the target too early. It holds back attacks when the target's hp is in a danger zone:

| Certainty Level | Safe to Slam | Safe to Consume Energy |
|---|---|---|
| 98% | ≥20 hp | ≥28 hp |
| 95% | ≥19 hp | ≥27 hp |
| 90% | ≥18 hp | ≥25 hp |
| ~67% | ≥15 hp | ≥21 hp |

- If target hp is too low for Slam, the jiangshi **holds back a round** rather than risk a premature KO.
- Consume Energy has 30-ft range — can be redirected to a different target if the primary is too low.

## Opportunity Attacks

Yes — always takes them. If a conscious opponent flees without Disengaging and gets KO'd by the opportunistic Slam, the jiangshi gets a full turn against an unconscious foe with 0 death save failures: Slam (2 failures) → Consume Energy (death).

## Consume Energy Power-Up

Even without creating a wight, Consume Energy grants: doubled speed (40 ft), flight, and hover. Strong incentive to use it on bystanders for fuel even if the revenge target isn't ready.

## Change Shape

- Costs an action to revert — a full turn lost. So **don't revert**. Attack in disguise.
- Stats and actions remain the same in any form.
- Caveat: still has 20-ft movement speed in disguise. DM's call on whether rigor mortis shows.

## Weaknesses

- **Mirrors and holy symbols**: Never ends turn within 40 ft of someone brandishing a holy symbol (unless they're obviously not a cleric).
- **AC 16**: Unexceptional despite great saves. The sword doesn't care about your modifier breakdown.
- **Immune to charm/frighten**: Bards and Enchanter/Illusionist wizards are juicy targets, especially once airborne.

## Target Selection

- **Top priority**: The hated individual it came for. Once first attack lands, locked on completely.
- **General preference**: Lower AC and Constitution targets. Avoids well-armored melee combatants.
- After killing its revenge target and creating a wight, the new minion handles tanks while the jiangshi picks off the vulnerable — or simply leaves.

## Retreat Conditions

- **Seriously wounded (≤47 hp)**: Disengages and departs.
- **After revenge is complete**: Disengages and departs.

## DM Tips

> [!tip] Running This Monster
> The jiangshi is a precision killer, not a mindless brute. Track target hp carefully — the whole combo depends on not accidentally knocking someone out too early. The Change Shape approach is ideal: show up as a guest or servant, then start swinging without ever dropping the disguise. Seed the encounter with the jiangshi's backstory — who wronged it, and how.
