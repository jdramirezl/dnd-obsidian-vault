# Carrion Stalker and Strigoi Tactics

> [!summary]
> Two Ravenloft monstrosities. The carrion stalker is a Tiny burrowing hazard that latches onto victims and bursts larvae. The strigoi is an overgrown vampiric stirge — a fast-flying ambush predator that summons stirge swarms.

---

## Carrion Stalker

### Profile
- **Type:** Monstrosity, Tiny — INT 2, pure instinct
- Stealth proficiency, burrowing movement, tremorsense
- Multiattack: 3 Tentacles (unattached) or 2 Tentacles + Larval Burst (attached)

### Combat Sequence
1. Detects prey via **tremorsense** → bursts from substrate
2. **Tentacle** attacks until one hits → auto-attaches to target
3. While attached: **advantage** on attacks against that target
4. **Larval Burst** (1/day) when at least 1 other living creature within 10 ft (preferably 2+)
5. After Larval Burst → **scurries away**

### Retreat
- Pulled off + **moderately wounded (≤24 hp)** → flees into darkness
- Unhurt and pulled off → tries to latch onto nearest creature again

---

## Strigoi

### Profile
- **Type:** Monstrosity, Large — higher INT/WIS, can choose targets and adjust
- Very high STR + CON, proficient in Perception and Stealth, fast flyer

### Combat Sequence
1. Perches hidden → strikes chosen target within 40 ft without warning
2. Keeps attacking **same initial target** even if attacked by others
3. Summoned stirges act like normal stirges
4. If moderately wounded (≤36 hp) and hasn't summoned → **Ravenous Children** (up to 6 stirges)

### Retreat
- **Seriously wounded (≤20 hp)** → Disengages if in melee, flies away, then Dashes

---

## DM Encounter Tips

> [!tip] Encounter Difficulty
> Strigoi encounters should be **Deadly** by default. If the strigoi alone doesn't make it Deadly, assume it already summoned stirges before combat. It would avoid an Easy or Medium fight entirely — but it might *talk*.

- Carrion stalker is best used as an environmental hazard layered into another encounter
