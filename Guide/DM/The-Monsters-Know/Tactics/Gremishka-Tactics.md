# Gremishka Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/gremishka-tactics/) | **Date**: February 13, 2023
> **Book**: Van Richten's Guide to Ravenloft

---

## Overview

- **CR 1/8 Tiny monstrosity** — magical accident byproduct with animus against mages.
- Only above-average stat is Dex → **shock attacker**. Bite and run.
- Int above humanoid average. Understands Common (doesn't speak). Nocturnal (30 ft darkvision).
- Knows escape routes, may eavesdrop on conversations.

## Individual Gremishka

- **Bite → Disengage → flee** is the standard loop. Disengage preferred over Dash or parting Bite.
- Won't seek a fight. Encounter is about **finding and eliminating its escape routes** until cornered.
- Low AC means Dodge doesn't help much.

## Magic Allergy (Reaction)

When a spell is cast within 30 ft, roll d6:
- **1–2**: Reflects energy — small force damage to all nearby.
- **3–4**: Absorbs energy — heals a small amount.
- **5–6**: **Explodes into a Swarm of Gremishkas** (CR 2).

> [!warning] Level Warning
> Don't send level 1 PCs against even a single gremishka — it can become a CR 2 swarm. Level 2 minimum.

## Swarm of Gremishkas

- **Opportunistically mean**. Higher Wisdom → better target selection and threat assessment.
- Can move through Tiny spaces → chooses when to engage.
- Zeroes in on the **most magical** party member (wizards > warlocks/sorcerers > everyone else).
- Resistant to bludgeoning/piercing/slashing. Limited Spell Immunity + Spell Redirection make it very tanky.

### Swarm Weaknesses

- ***Magic missile***: Hits automatically, bypasses defenses (likely an oversight but RAW).
- **Fire**: Effective — swarm runs when hit by fire.
- **Acid**: Also works.

### Swarm Retreat

- Runs when hit by fire.
- Runs when hit by *magic missile* AND hp ≤12. Rushes the caster if hp ≥13.
- Runs when seriously wounded (≤9 hp).

> [!tip] Player Mood Check
> If players are laughing at the chase, continue cat-and-mouse with the swarm. If groaning, have the swarm stay in the open and act hyperaggressively instead.
