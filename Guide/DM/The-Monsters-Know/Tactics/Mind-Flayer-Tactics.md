# Mind Flayer Tactics

## Tactical Summary

The mind flayer is a scheming psionic commander with genius-level Intelligence (19) but mediocre physical stats. It never fights fair — it hides behind waves of enthralled minions, levitates out of melee reach, and uses Mind Blast to stun groups before moving in to extract brains. It values its survival highly but won't abandon an important scheme without a fight.

## Combat Behavior

### Positioning
- Stays 60 ft. behind its minion front line
- *Levitates* ~10 ft. off the ground so melee attackers can't reach it
- Minions (troglodytes, grimlocks, quaggoths, ogres, grells, enthralled commoners) form a defensive screen

### Action Priority
1. **Mind Blast** (60 ft. cone, recharge) — always the first choice when available; stuns targets on failed INT save
2. **Mind-control a PC** — second choice; targets the party's ranged attacker (archers are the mind flayer's true vulnerability since it resists magic but not arrows)
3. **Tentacle attack** — only against already-stunned or incapacitated targets; grapples and sets up Extract Brain
4. **Extract Brain** — instant kill against incapacitated + grappled targets; the mind flayer moves in only after the threat is neutralized

### Why Ranged Attackers Are Priority Targets
- Mind flayers have Magic Resistance but no arrow resistance
- Middling AC makes them vulnerable to martial ranged attacks beyond 60 ft.
- INT 19 means the mind flayer identifies this weakness and acts on it — it will mind-control the archer, not the barbarian

### Minion Tactics
- PCs must fight through waves of thralls before reaching the mind flayer
- Minions absorb opportunity attacks and block corridors
- Good minion pairings: troglodytes (stench), grimlocks (blindsight in dark lairs), grells (tentacle/brain theme synergy)

## Key Decision Points

| Situation | Response |
|---|---|
| Mind Blast available | Use it — always |
| Mind Blast recharging | Mind-control a PC (preferably the ranged attacker) or let minions handle it |
| PC breaks through minion line | Mind Blast if available; otherwise tentacle attack only if PC is already stunned |
| Cornered in melee | Mind Blast over tentacles, even at close range |
| Multiple lairs / schemes | Willing to cut losses on a less-important lair |

## Retreat Conditions

- **Single lair / critical scheme:** Holds out until seriously injured (≤28 hp), then casts *plane shift* to escape
- **Secondary lair / expendable scheme:** Retreats earlier at moderate injury (≤49 hp) via *plane shift*, leaving minions behind
- *Plane shift* is the escape — it doesn't run, it simply vanishes

## Homebrew Enhancement: Better Thrall-Making

The RAW *dominate monster* (1/day, concentration, 1 hour) is underwhelming for a psionic overlord. Consider replacing it with:
- **Aboleth's Enslave** — indefinite duration, no concentration, 3/day; target re-saves only if 1+ mile away
- **Modified *geas*** — 30 days, no repeat saves, psychic damage for disobedience
- **Vampire's Charm** — 24 hours, no concentration, but doesn't solve long-term control

All should use DC 15 INT save to match Mind Blast.

> [!tip] DM Tips
> - The mind flayer should never be the first enemy the party fights — make them earn the encounter through minion gauntlets
> - Levitate + 60 ft. Mind Blast range means melee PCs may never touch it without creative play
> - If a PC gets stunned and grappled by tentacles, the party has exactly one round to intervene before Extract Brain
> - For a scarier encounter, pair with an intellect devourer lurking inside a minion — surprise brain theft mid-combat
> - The mind flayer targets the archer/ranger specifically because INT 19 lets it identify its own vulnerability
