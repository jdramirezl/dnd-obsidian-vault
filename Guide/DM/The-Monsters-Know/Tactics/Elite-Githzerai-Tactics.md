# Elite Githzerai Tactics

> [!summary]
> Mordenkainen's elite githzerai: the enlightened (shock attacker with haste and Temporal Strike) and the anarch (legendary controller with flight and reverse gravity). Both highly intelligent — won't start fights they can't win, disengage after round 3 if not clearly winning.

---

## Githzerai Enlightened

### Opening
- **Cast *haste* first** — always, ideally before combat
- *Haste* > *blur* > *expeditious retreat* (all concentration; haste strictly superior)
- *Teleport* with up to 8 monks for surprise assault (more precise than *plane shift*)

### Temporal Strike (Recharge 6)
- Takes one enemy out for one round + ~65 avg damage (up to 116)
- **Wait one round** to observe before using
- Best targets: high-level fighters with triple/quadruple Attack, shock attackers (rogue, monk, barbarian)
- Avoid: spellcasters (overkill — likely kills outright, wasting the disable; better Wis saves), paladins (good Wis)

### Sustained Combat
- *Haste* + Multiattack for bludgeoning + psychic barrages
- *Jump* + *haste*: down enemy with one strike → *jump* toward next target
- *See invisibility*: only if invisible enemy is high-priority AND *haste* is active

### If Haste Drops
- Out one round; allies close ranks to protect
- Spread-out enemies / fewer attacks → *expeditious retreat*
- Close enemies / more attacks → *blur*

### Retreat
- After round 3: if losing or attrition → *teleport* or *plane shift* out with allies
- **Encounter design:** must be Deadly — enlightened won't start a lesser fight

## Githzerai Anarch

### Concentration Choices
- *Globe of invulnerability*: vs magical assault — generally favored
- *Wall of force*: vs physical assault; flexible shape
- **Change Gravity** (legendary action): conflicts with both above

### Change Gravity (3 Legendary Actions)
- 50-ft radius, 100-ft range, any orientation — equivalent to *reverse gravity*
- Worth it only when damage exceeds 3 strikes (~72 hp): 2 enemies × 100 ft, 3 × 70 ft, 4 × 50 ft, etc.
- Anarch can hover — immune to its own gravity reversal
- Can leave enemies "falling" upward indefinitely, then drop the spell

### Other Legendary Actions
- **Strike (1):** free retaliation vs engaged melee enemy
- **Teleport (1):** 30-ft repositioning without opportunity attacks

### Combat Style
- Hovers out of melee reach; AC 20 — may not care about opportunity attacks
- In a corridor: Change Gravity sends enemies "falling" 100 ft for 10d6, then 100 ft to cover before doing it again

### Retreat
- Moderately wounded (≤100 hp): retreats unless strong reason to fight
- Seriously wounded (≤57 hp): retreats regardless
- Uses *plane shift* to evacuate (has *teleportation circle*, not *teleport* — no bamf assault)

> [!tip] DM Tips
> Enlightened: *haste* round 0, teleport assault round 1, Temporal Strike round 2 on biggest martial threat, reassess round 3. Anarch: hover + Change Gravity is devastating in enclosed spaces; *globe* is default concentration. Both leave when losing.
