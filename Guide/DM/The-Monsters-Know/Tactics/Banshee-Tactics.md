# Banshee Tactics

## Tactical Summary

Incorporeal undead elf — fast (40 ft. fly), devastating openers, then hit-and-run strafing. Leads with Wail to drop targets outright, follows with Horrifying Visage to scatter survivors, then strafes with Corrupting Touch from the air. Cannot flee its haunt.

## Resistances & Immunities

- **Resistant:** acid, fire, lightning, thunder, nonmagical physical
- **Immune:** cold, necrotic, poison + most conditions
- **Red alert:** Magic weapons

## Combat Sequence

### Round 1: Wail (1/day, 30 ft. radius)
- Fly into the **center** of the enemy group first
- Failed save = drop to 0 hp; success = 3d6 psychic
- Must come before Horrifying Visage — frightened creatures can't approach, locking them out of Wail range

### Round 2: Horrifying Visage (60 ft.)
- Fly 10–15 ft. up out of melee reach, then use Visage
- One-and-done: no benefit from retrying

### Round 3+: Corrupting Touch (hit-and-run)
- Fly down → touch → fly back up; ignores OAs (resistant to nonmagical physical)
- **Position on the outside** of the fray — frightened allies can't move closer to help the victim

### Target Priority
1. Magic bow wielders (greatest threat)
2. Spellcasters and ranged attackers
3. Attractive PCs (vanity-driven obsessive envy)

## Roleplay

- Senses living creatures **5 miles** away — always knows you're coming
- Bound to its haunt; cannot flee
- Offer **objects of great beauty** to negotiate; can barter for information

## Retreat Conditions

- **Cannot flee** — cursed to remain
- ≤23 hp: becomes erratic — attacks randomly, hurls objects, tries to seize jewelry
- Especially panicked vs. magic melee weapons

> [!tip] DM Tips
> - Wail is a potential TPK button — position carefully for party level
> - Can pass through walls/floors as difficult terrain; disappear between attacks
> - Parley is viable: beauty-obsessed banshees make great information brokers
