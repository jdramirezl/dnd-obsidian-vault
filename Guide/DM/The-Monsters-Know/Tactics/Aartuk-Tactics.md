# Aartuk Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/aartuk-tactics/)

---

## Tactical Summary
- Intelligent plant creatures built for melee; sticky Tongue (bonus action, recharge 6, DC 11) drags foes into Branch-whomping range
- Tongue has a low DC and rarely recharges—aartuks rely on numbers to guarantee at least one grab
- Priests support by pulling enemies into allies' reach and casting *tongues* for parley; elders use *detect magic* to call priority targets
- Groups with a priest or elder only engage in Deadly encounters
- Radiant Pellet is a weak fallback ranged attack used only when melee is impossible

## Combat Behavior
- **Opening**: Every warrior opens with Tongue targeting the least-dexterous-looking foe, then Multiattack (2x Branch) if the grab succeeds
- **Group coordination**: Priests pull enemies toward allied warriors. Elders cast *detect magic* round 1 to ID magic-item wielders, then direct focused Radiant Pellet fire on that target
- **Parley**: Priests use *tongues* to negotiate if the fight looks unfavorable. Elders cast *calm emotions* to soften enemies first

## Key Decision Points
- If no warrior is in melee and fewer than 12 aartuks remain, reengagement odds are too low—withdraw
- Warriors always prefer melee; Radiant Pellet only when target is beyond 10 ft and Tongue hasn't recharged
- A priest will halt combat and negotiate if the encounter isn't clearly Deadly in the aartuks' favor

## Retreat Conditions
- Withdraw when no aartuk is in melee AND fewer than 12 warriors remain
- Withdraw when ≥50% seriously wounded (≤15 hp) and ≥50% of enemies are not
- Method: Disengage → Spider Climb out of reach → Dash

> [!tip] Running This Monster
> Run at least 4–6 warriors so the Tongue spam creates real tension, and include a priest to give the encounter a parley escape valve if things go sideways.
