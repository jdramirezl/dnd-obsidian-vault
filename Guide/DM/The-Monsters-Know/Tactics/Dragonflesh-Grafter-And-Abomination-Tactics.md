# Dragonflesh Grafter and Abomination Tactics

> [Source](https://www.themonstersknow.com/dragonflesh-grafter-and-abomination-tactics/)

---

## Tactical Summary

- **Grafter**: humanoid fused with dragon parts; brute (high STR/CON); straightforward melee + breath weapon
- **Abomination**: fully transformed; can fly, has ranged acid spit, Cloying Miasma aura; low INT but above-average WIS
- Both are monstrosities obsessed with treasure, territory, and food — deranged by clashing humanoid and draconic urges

## Combat Behavior

- **Grafter**: Multiattack (weapon + Claw) at 10 ft reach; Acid Retch (30 ft cone, recharge) whenever available vs ≥3 enemies; can't fly or avoid OAs, so must use Acid Retch from current position
- **Abomination** priority:
  1. Acid Belch (30 ft cone, recharge) vs ≥3 grouped enemies
  2. Melee Multiattack (Claw 10 ft / Tail 15 ft) vs targets in reach
  3. Acidic Spit (ranged) vs out-of-reach targets — last resort
- Abomination stays airborne between turns; descends only if forced (knocked prone); Cloying Miasma punishes melee closers

## Key Decision Points

- Abomination enraged by illusions or debuffs targeting INT/CHA; lashes out at whoever deals ≥20 damage first
- May hesitate if encounter wouldn't be Deadly for the party — decent gut-level threat sense
- Both are territorial and paranoid; humanoid remnant craves social connection but can't maintain it

## Retreat Conditions

- **Grafter**: flees at ≤20 hp, Dashing (accepts OAs); lower-WIS variant might fight to death
- **Abomination**: flees at ≤26 hp, Dashing while flying

> [!tip] The abomination can no longer speak but still understands language. It may pathetically attempt to communicate, growing frustrated until it attacks. Use this for dramatic roleplay before combat.
