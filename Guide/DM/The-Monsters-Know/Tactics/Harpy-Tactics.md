# Harpy Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/harpy-tactics/) | **Date**: November 17, 2016
> **Related Bestiary**: [[Harpy]]

---

## Tactical Summary

- Instinct-driven predator (INT 7) with balanced physical profile and slightly higher DEX. No ranged attack — relies on Luring Song to bring prey into melee.
- Congregates around natural hazards; harpies that roost near hazards survive better. Never attacks unless it has a clear advantage.

## Luring Song

- **Range**: 300 ft — prey hears the song long before seeing the harpy.
- **DC 11 WIS save**: Elves have advantage (Fey Ancestry). Success = immunity.
- Failure: charmed + incapacitated + must move toward harpy by most direct route.
- Repeat save at end of each turn; any success = immunity.
- With +1 WIS save: 55% success. With +4: 70%. With Fey Ancestry: ~80–91%.
- **Must cash in on the effect immediately** — even one more round is too long.

## Combat Sequence

1. Waits hidden, 20 ft above a natural hazard, until prey wanders within 25 ft of the hazard.
2. Uses Luring Song to entice victim toward the hazard. Victim gets another save at the edge if the hazard causes damage.
3. **If victim fails**: Fly down and Multiattack, then fly back up out of reach.
4. **If victim succeeds** (cunning harpy): Attempt to **shove** opponent into the hazard (in lieu of club attack), then claw attack, then fly back up.
5. **Round 2**: Flies down and Multiattacks again (club + claws) against the same target.
6. **Round 3**: If target isn't down, recognizes this isn't easy prey → **Dashes away** at double flying speed.
- Never prolongs a fight beyond two rounds of melee.

## Group Tactics

- Multiple harpies are far more effective: immunity from one harpy's song doesn't protect against another's. A creature must save against ALL harpies to be free.
- While charmed, the victim is incapacitated (no reactions) → harpies can swoop in, Multiattack, and fly back out without provoking opportunity attacks.
