# Water Weird Tactics

> [!warning] Source URL returned 404 — this article may have been consolidated or removed from the blog. Tactics reconstructed from the book *The Monsters Know What They're Doing*.

## Tactical Summary

Elemental guardian bound to a specific water-filled location (pool, fountain, etc.). Invisible while fully immersed in water. Serpentine shape only visible when it emerges to attack. Attacks any creature other than its summoner and those declared off-limits.

## Key Features

- **Invisible in water**: Cannot be seen until it attacks or leaves the water
- **Water-bound**: Dies if removed from water; incapacitated if water freezes
- Resistant to fire; immune to poison
- Resistant to nonmagical physical damage
- Immune to exhaustion, grappled, paralyzed, poisoned, prone, restrained, unconscious

## Combat Behavior

- **Constrict**: Only attack. Grapples and restrains on hit, then pulls target into the water to drown
- Waits invisibly until a creature comes within reach, then strikes with surprise
- Once grappling, holds target underwater — victim begins suffocating
- Can only grapple one creature at a time (restrained condition)
- If grapple is broken, retreats into water to become invisible again and waits for next opportunity

## Tactical Simplicity

Water weirds are simple guardians with animal-level INT. They don't retreat, don't strategize, and don't coordinate. They grab, pull, drown. The danger is entirely environmental — the water itself is the weapon.

## DM Notes

- Place in locations where PCs must interact with the water (drinking, crossing, retrieving something submerged)
- *Purify food and drink* removes its evil alignment (per MM flavor text)
- Pair with other aquatic hazards or guardians for a more complex encounter
- Cold damage is the real counter — freezing the water incapacitates it
