# NPC Tactics: Swashbucklers and Master Thieves

> [!info] Source
> [The Monsters Know — NPC Tactics: Swashbucklers and Master Thieves](https://www.themonstersknow.com/npc-tactics-swashbucklers-master-thieves/)

## Swashbucklers

Exceptional Dex, expert Acrobatics/Athletics/Persuasion, triple Multiattack. Suave Defense boosts AC; Lightfooted grants Dash or Disengage as bonus (no Hide).

**Core challenge**: Strike at melee then avoid getting hit. Must get >30 ft away to prevent retaliation.

### Dash vs. Disengage
- vs. 1 opponent with Extra Attack/Multiattack → Dash (risk 1 OA to avoid 2–3 attacks)
- vs. 2+ opponents → compare OA count vs. total attacks from equally-fast pursuers. More opponents → Disengage. Equal or fewer → Dash.
- Climbing out of reach (10 ft up vs. 35 ft away) → Disengage more viable

### Shock Attacker Rhythm
Finesse weapons use Dex → read as shock attacker: **Charge → Multiattack → Multiattack → Retreat → repeat**

**Moderately wounded (≤46 hp)** → look for escape, take it next opportunity. Running patter throughout — taunting if stronger, dickering if weaker. Always maximize terrain.

## Master Thieves

A thief, NOT an assassin. Level 7 rogue equivalent. Wants clean getaway — fights only to remove interference or if cornered.

**Strike first from hiding at melee range**: 3 shortsword attacks + Sneak Attack = 7d6+4 ≈ 28 hp. Melee preferred (3 attacks vs. 1 crossbow shot).

**After first strike**: Usually exits immediately. Uncanny Dodge halves OA damage → can Dash safely. Can Dash as action AND bonus → 90 ft. Or Disengage + Dash → 60 ft, zero OAs.

**Seriously wounded (≤33 hp) while pursued** → stop, surrender, cut a deal. Nothing worth dying for.
