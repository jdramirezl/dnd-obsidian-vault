# Bodytaker Plant Tactics

> [!summary]
> The bodytaker plant is an *Invasion of the Body Snatchers* monster — a huge, slow brute that grapples victims with vines, stuffs them into an entrapping pod, and transforms them into podling simulacra. It should **never be encountered alone**; its exceptional CHA represents commanding swarms of podling offspring as interference.

---

## Stat Block Profile
- **Type:** Plant, Huge
- **Exceptional STR + CON** — pure brute
- **High INT + WIS + CHA** — smart target selection, commands podlings
- Very slow (land/climb/swim); no ranged attacks
- **Vulnerable to poison**
- Vine Lash reach: 20 ft
- Must be salted/herbicided to prevent regrowth after "death"

---

## Key Features

### Vine Lash (Multiattack ×3)
- 20 ft reach; on hit: grapples the target (escape DC ~15)

### Entrapping Pod
- Requires target already grappled by Vine Lash
- Failed CON save (DC ~16) → enveloped, transformation begins
- Can only envelop **one creature at a time**

### Podling Link
- Telepathic communication with all podling offspring

---

## Combat Sequence

### Target Selection
1. **Poison damage dealers** — top priority once identified (vulnerable)
2. **Druids and rangers** — presumed extremely dangerous
3. **Ranged attackers beyond 20 ft** — mob with podlings
4. For Entrapping Pod: lowest combined **AC + STR + DEX + CON mods**
5. Don't waste the pod on martial types who can fight through grapple

### In Combat
1. Podlings mob ranged attackers and drag weak targets toward the plant
2. Vine Lash ×3 on nearest threats; grapple the squishiest target
3. Entrapping Pod on the best candidate once grappled

### Podlings
- High STR/CON, slow (20 ft), low damage (Slam: avg 5)
- Pile on: grapple, shove prone, drag toward parent plant
- Can strangle (suffocation rules, PHB ch. 8)
- Under command: fight to the death; without: flee when seriously wounded (≤10 hp)

---

## Retreat
- **Cannot retreat** — too slow; fights to the death by necessity
- Podlings without direct command flee when things go wrong

---

## DM Encounter Tips

> [!tip] Never Alone
> Throw *many* podlings at the party. The plant stays back while podlings do the dragging. Climbing ability + 20 ft reach = can hang on walls/ledges to reduce melee vulnerability.

- "Killing" the plant isn't enough — PCs must salt the earth or use herbicide
- High mental stats are for organizing podling replacements of townsfolk in optimal order
- Consider lair design: abandoned mines, sewers, limited escape routes
