# Gnolls Revisited

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/gnolls-revisited/) | **Date**: February 1, 2017
> **Related Bestiary**: [[Gnolls]]

---

## Tactical Summary

- Volo's Guide reinforces gnolls as savage, instinct-driven killing machines — hyenas transformed by Yeenoghu.
- Four new variants: gnoll hunter (stealthy sniper), gnoll flesh gnawer (back-line charger), gnoll witherling (undead reaction attacker), flind (magic flail leader).
- Ranged attacks are deterrents only — gnolls use bows to prevent prey from fleeing, not as an opening barrage.
- Best used as random encounters unless building a Yeenoghu-focused campaign.

## New Variant Behavior

### Gnoll Hunter
- High Dex → shock attacker. Stealth proficiency.
- Longbow hit slows target's speed by 10 ft.
- Attack from hiding → close to melee to finish the slowed target.

### Gnoll Flesh Gnawer
- High Dex + Sudden Rush: charge 90 ft as an action, ignoring opportunity attacks.
- Sprints past the front line to reach back-rank targets (spellcasters, archers).
- Not discriminating, but unarmored targets in the back are obvious prey.

### Gnoll Witherling
- Undead. Gets an extra reaction attack whenever an ally within sight is killed.
- Immune to poison and exhaustion. Otherwise unremarkable.
- Expendable — their deaths fuel other witherlings' reaction attacks.

### Flind
- Magic flail with three powers (one of each per Multiattack — no choice).
- Aura of Blood Thirst: gnoll allies within 10 ft get a bonus bite attack.
- Stays surrounded by allies to maximize the aura. Does not run off alone.

## Retreat Conditions

- Same as base gnolls: individual gnolls Dash away at ≤8 hp. No coordinated retreat.
- Flinds are slightly smarter but still demon-driven — unlikely to retreat while allies remain.

## DM Tips

> [!tip] Running This Monster
> Gnolls are dull by design — savage killing machines with no interest in parley. The flesh gnawer is the most interesting: a 90 ft charge past your front line to eat the wizard is terrifying at low levels. Use gnolls for horror and attrition, not clever encounters.
