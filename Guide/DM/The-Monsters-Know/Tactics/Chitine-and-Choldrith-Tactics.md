# Chitine and Choldrith Tactics

> [!summary]
> Chitines are hairy, multi-armed Lolth servitors — uncomplicated ambush attackers that lurk on ceilings in webbed darkness and dart between targets. Choldriths are their spider-elf cousins — back-rank battlefield clerics that Web targets, cast *hold person* and *spiritual weapon*, and "direct" chitines by imposing conditions that trigger their opportunism.

---

## Chitine (CR 1/2)

### Profile
- Multi-armed bipeds, Web Sense + Web Walker, climbing speed 30 ft
- INT 10, WIS 10 — not choosy about targets, not disciplined enough to Disengage
- DEX is primary offense + defense; relies heavily on Multiattack (3 Daggers)
- Double proficiency in Athletics (useful for grappling — capturing prisoners for drow masters)

### Behavior
- Fight in **webs + pitch darkness** for maximum comparative advantage
- Lurk on **ceilings** in the dark, drop onto prey
- Target by size, age, isolation, inability to see in dark, or debilitating conditions (e.g., restrained by webs)
- Dart between opponents — provoke OAs but minimize by climbing walls/ceilings to bypass enemies

### Combat Sequence
1. Ambush from ceiling in darkness
2. **Multiattack** (3 Daggers) against most vulnerable-looking target
3. Ditch current target to attack a more vulnerable one (restrained, blinded, etc.)
4. Climb walls/ceilings to bypass enemies they don't want to engage

### Retreat
- **No retreat** — lack flexibility and free will; fight until dead
- Exception: seriously wounded chitine retreats to **ceiling**, waits for a foe to drop guard, pounces when hands are full

### Grappling
- Double Athletics proficiency = excellent grappler
- Can't substitute grapple for Dagger in Multiattack — must forgo entire Multiattack to grapple
- Useful when serving drow masters who want **prisoners**

## Choldrith (CR 3)

### Profile
- Spider-elf with significantly higher WIS — good target selection, can adjust plans
- All chitine features minus Multiattack, plus: **Web** action, spellcasting
- Back-rank battlefield cleric, not a melee fighter
- Preferred distance: **~30 ft** (just close enough for Web without disadvantage)
- Speaks Undercommon — will monologue about Lolth's supremacy if PCs understand

### Spells
- **Hold person:** paralyze a foe → allies attack with advantage + auto-crits. Cast on front-line melee threats, shock attackers rushing the choldrith, or nuisance backline casters
- **Spiritual weapon:** bonus action, cast round 1 alongside Web
- **Bane:** used against groups where no single enemy stands out
- **Healing word / Sanctuary:** only on revered superiors, never on weaker allies (zealotry)
- **Shield of faith:** most likely cast on *itself* when blitzed — drops concentration on hold person/bane to save its own skin
- **Cantrips (thaumaturgy, resistance):** only when Web is on cooldown and no reason for hold person/bane

### Combat Sequence
1. **Web** first → restrain targets so chitine allies Multiattack with advantage
2. **Spiritual weapon** as bonus action (round 1)
3. Subsequent rounds: **Web** when available; spells when Web is on cooldown
4. **Hold person** on biggest individual threat; **Bane** against undifferentiated groups
5. Rarely uses Dagger — only when charged with no hold person slot, or throwing at a debilitated enemy 10–20 ft away

### Retreat
- **Zealots — always fight to the death** unless commanded otherwise

## DM Encounter Tips

> [!tip] Choldrith as Director
> Choldriths "direct" chitines by imposing conditions (restrained, paralyzed) that activate chitine opportunism. The choldrith doesn't give orders — it creates openings.

- Chitines + choldriths + darkness + webs = nightmare encounter for parties without darkvision
- Chitines are often minions of drow arachnomancers, giant spiders, or ettercaps
- Choldriths' "parleying" is just monologuing about Lolth
