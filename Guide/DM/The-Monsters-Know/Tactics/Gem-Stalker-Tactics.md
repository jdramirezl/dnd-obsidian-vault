# Gem Stalker Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/gem-stalker-tactics/) | **Date**: September 30, 2022

---

## Tactical Summary

- **CR 5 monstrosity** created by gem dragons from liquidated aberration remains. Shock attacker / brute hybrid.
- **Ability contour**: Highest Str, high Dex/Int/Con. Slight shock attacker bias. Proficiency in Perception and Stealth. Spider Climb.
- **Savage Multiattack**: Four Claw attacks (avg 10 damage each).
- **Crystal Dart** (bonus action, ranged spell attack): Used *before* closing — disadvantage on all ranged attacks in close combat.
- **Protective Link** (reaction): Only within 30 ft of creature it's guarding (usually its creator gem dragon).

## Combat Behavior

**Standard sequence**: Crystal Dart (bonus) → Move toward target (40 ft) → Claw ×4 (action).

**Crystal Dart riders** (by creator dragon type):
- **Teleportation**: Best — substitutes for movement, allows second move after mauling.
- **Temp HP**: Incentivizes fishing for darts even when marginal.
- **−1d4 attack penalty**: Weakens melee counterattacks.
- **Push 10 ft + prone**: Low DC but 40-ft speed closes gap easily.
- **Poisoned**: Low DC but strong effect.

**After round 2**: Takes cue from target. Target withdraws → lets them go. Target keeps fighting → keeps mauling. Does NOT chase retreating foes (baseline).

**Exception — hunting aberrations**: No quarter. Pursues with Crystal Dart → close → Multiattack until dead. Carries corpse home.

## Lair Defense Mode

- Stays within 30 ft of gem dragon for Protective Link.
- Focuses on enemies within 35 ft of dragon.
- Sapphire stalkers target lowest Str; topaz target lowest Con.
- Doesn't chase ranged attackers — Protective Link neutralizes them.

## Retreat Conditions

- **Created, not evolved**: Weak self-preservation. Doesn't retreat, but allows opponents to retreat.

## DM Tips

> [!tip] Running This Monster
> Crystal Dart before closing is the key rhythm — clashes with melee if used in close combat. On ceilings via Spider Climb, no one looks up. Use sparingly alongside a gem dragon or encounter difficulty spirals.
