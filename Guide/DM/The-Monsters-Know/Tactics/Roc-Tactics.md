# Roc Tactics

## Tactical Summary

Gargantuan avian predator — 200 ft wingspan (Boeing 747 scale). Unaligned, bestial INT (3). Extraordinary STR and CON, average DEX. Hunts big, slow-moving game (elk, buffalo, giants). Average humanoid isn't large enough to bother with — a roc would have to be starving to carry off adventurers. More likely targeting the party's horses.

## Defenses

- Enormous HP pool (248 max)
- Proficient in all big three saves (DEX, CON, WIS) + CHA
- Fearless — only driven off when reduced to **≤99 hp**

## Attack Pattern: Talon/Beak Multiattack

- **Talon** hit → grapples and restrains → advantage on follow-up **Bite**
- Avg 50 damage/round if both hit
- Tries to **finish prey first**, then fly to nest — real birds of prey don't carry prey alive

## Behavior While Holding Prey

- Continues attacking **only** the grappled creature until dead
- If attacked by others: pecks back with beak without releasing prey
- If prey ≤40% max HP: flies away with it, ignoring opportunity attacks
- Base flying speed 120 ft — Dash makes it uncatchable (short of *wind walk*)

## Retreat

- Only when reduced to **≤99 hp**
- Common folk's fear far exceeds the actual threat to humanoids

> [!tip] DM Tips
> - A roc diving at a caravan is probably after the horses, not the people
> - Grab-and-drop (fly 120 ft up, release for 12d6 fall damage) is iconic from mythology — popular homebrew addition
> - Rocs nest on mountain peaks — home turf adds vertical danger
> - INT 3 = smart cat/dog level — won't fall for the same trap twice
