# Behir Tactics

## Tactical Summary

Huge cavern predator — fast, tough, stealthy, built to grab-and-swallow. Ambushes from darkness, constricts a target, bites with advantage, swallows next turn, then flees with its meal. Lightning Breath is defensive, not an opener. Fights to eat, not to kill.

## Ambush Setup

- Lairs in inaccessible places: deep pits, high cliff caves, narrow twisting tunnels
- 12 legs = scrambles with ease; 50 ft. climb speed
- Conceals in darkness (darkvision) or among rocks at twilight
- Strikes from hiding = advantage (unseen attacker)

## Attack Sequence

### Round 1: Constrict → Bite
- **Constrict first** — grapples + restrains on hit (Large or smaller)
- **Bite second** — with advantage (restrained); combined ~56 damage

### Round 2: Swallow
- If still grappled: **Swallow** with advantage (Medium or smaller only)
- On success: full movement to **flee** where allies can't follow
- Counter: *enlarge* on the grappled target prevents Swallow

## Lightning Breath (Recharge 5–6)

- 20 ft. line, ~50 damage; usually hits only 1 target
- **Not an opener** — doesn't help the behir eat
- **Used defensively** only when moderately wounded (≤117 hp)
- Repositions to hit 2+ targets first
- When available and wounded: Lightning Breath; on cooldown: Multiattack

## Retreat Conditions

- ≤67 hp: abandons the hunt
- After swallowing: flees immediately regardless of HP
- 1 attacker in reach → Dash away; 2+ → Dodge until clear, then Dash

> [!tip] DM Tips
> - Tunnels are the behir's best friend: 20 ft. breath in a narrow corridor hits the whole marching order
> - Constrict → Swallow → flee removes a PC in 2 rounds; plan for the swallowed player
> - Speaks Draconic, INT 7 — parley technically possible but extremely limited
> - The behir grabs the front-liner and scrambles through twisting tunnels the party can't follow
