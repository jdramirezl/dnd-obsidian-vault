# Goblin Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/goblin-tactics/) | **Date**: August 13, 2016
> **Related Bestiary**: [[Goblins]]

---

## Tactical Summary

- **Ambush snipers**: Goblins fight from hiding at ~40 ft range using shortbows, exploiting Nimble Escape to Attack → Move → Hide every round.
- **Darkness is their ally**: They attack under cover of darkness to gain advantage against PCs without darkvision and impose disadvantage on Perception checks.
- **Never end a turn exposed**: The core loop is Attack (action), reposition (move), Hide (bonus action). Ending a turn hidden is the #1 priority.
- **Melee only in desperation**: Scimitars come out only in darkness, with numbers, and ideally flanking — never in the open.
- **Traps and alarms protect their lairs**: Crude but effective — falling rocks, pits, snares, and the occasional hunter's trap at key locations.

## Combat Behavior

**Opening**: Goblins ambush from hiding at 40–80 ft range with shortbows. They attack with advantage from concealment.

**Core Loop**: Attack (action) → Move to new cover (move) → Hide (bonus action). They reposition behind trees, rocks, or stalagmites (three-quarters cover = +5 AC even if Hide fails). They try to goad PCs into splitting up.

**If a PC closes to melee**: Disengage (bonus action) → Dash (action) to 40+ ft if the PC is dangerous, OR move 30 ft to cover → Hide (action) to set up an advantage shot next turn.

**Melee engagement**: Only in darkness + numbers + close quarters. Attack (action) → Disengage (bonus action) → retreat (move) to lure PCs into overextending into ambush caverns.

**Won't waste arrows** on targets behind three-quarters cover — they reposition to flank instead.

## Key Decision Points

- **Target behind cover?** → Reposition alongside or behind the target before shooting.
- **PC closes distance?** → Disengage (bonus) + Dash (action) if serious threat; move + Hide (action) if goblin thinks it can finish the PC.
- **Defeated a foe?** → If no other target visible, the greedy goblin loots the body (exploitable weakness).
- **Commanded by hobgoblin/bugbear?** → Will fight melee reluctantly, without coordination, and desert at first opportunity.

## Retreat Conditions

- **1–2 hp**: Flees immediately, no questions asked. Surrenders if captured, grovels, then escapes when attention wanders.
- **3–4 hp**: Stalks the PC who wounded it — retreats to safe distance, re-hides, then resumes sniping from 40 ft.
- **Ambush fails**: If they can't maintain the hide-and-shoot cycle, they scatter into darkness.

## DM Tips

> [!tip] Running This Monster
> The turn sequence matters: Attack → Move → Hide, not Move → Hide → Attack. Goblins must end their turn hidden or they're dead. Use their 30 ft movement to arc around PCs and break line of sight before hiding — the optimal engagement range is 40 ft from targets.
