# Flail Snail Tactics

## Tactical Summary

Large, tough, slow elemental. Technically a brute, but really it's prey, not a predator. INT 3 — no advanced tactics, just simple instinctive rules. Has tremorsense (60 ft), Antimagic Shell (passive, chance to bounce spells), Scintillating Shell (1/combat), Shell Defense (+4 AC), and Flail Tentacles.

## Combat Behavior

Follows a fixed instinctive sequence:

1. **Tremorsense alert:** Goes to red alert when attacked OR when a Medium+ creature enters 60 ft. Doesn't come out until area is clear
2. **Turn 1 — Shell Defense:** Activate (+4 AC). Move directly away from attackers, ignoring OAs (too dumb to know they exist). Shell Defense arguably doesn't prevent attacking (RAW: +4 AC vs total cover when tentacles are gone)
3. **Turn 2 — Multiattack:** Flail Tentacles against a random enemy within 10 ft that has dealt damage to it
4. **Turn 3 — Scintillating Shell (1/combat):** If foes are still hurting it and at least one is within 30 ft. Stuns on failed save
5. **Turn 4 — Multiattack:** Target only stunned opponents
6. **Turn 5+ — Multiattack:** No target preference, resume moving away
7. **Never drops Shell Defense** while any opponent is within 60 ft or dealing damage from farther

## Key Decision Points

- Scintillating Shell: save for when tentacles and Shell Defense aren't enough, but never use with no enemy within 30 ft
- All tentacle attacks must target the same creature (Multiattack restriction)

## Retreat Conditions

The flail snail has no effective means of running away (too slow). It relies entirely on defense mechanisms. It doesn't retreat — it endures or dies. Death Shriek on death may attract other predators/scavengers.

> [!tip] DM Tip
> The flail snail is a guilt-trip encounter. It's peaceful until provoked, and its Death Shriek attracts worse things. Pair with an owlbear or other predator that arrives 1d4 rounds after the shriek. Make your players feel bad about attacking it.
