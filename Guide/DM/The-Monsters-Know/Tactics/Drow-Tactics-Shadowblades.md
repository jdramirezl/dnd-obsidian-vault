# Drow Tactics: Shadowblades

> [Source](https://www.themonstersknow.com/drow-tactics-shadowblades/)
> Bestiary: [[Elves-Drow]]

---

## Tactical Summary

- CR 11 spy/assassin; extraordinary DEX; shock attacker that strikes from hiding to kill fast
- Shadow Step (bonus action): teleport 60 ft between dim/dark locations + advantage on first melee attack
- Proficient in all "big three" saves + Fey Ancestry — little to fear from enemy spellcasters
- Shadow Sword creates patches of magical darkness; second hit near existing darkness deals bonus necrotic (once/turn, from *previous turn's* darkness only)

## Combat Behavior

- **With allies**: allies engage directly while shadowblade snipes from darkness for 1–2 rounds; then Shadow Steps into the fray for devastating Multiattack
- **Solo**: jumps in immediately — no time for games; must accomplish mission within 3 rounds or before seriously wounded
- **Shadow Step sequence**: bonus action teleport (dim/dark → dim/dark) → melee Multiattack with advantage on first hit
- **Shadow Sword Multiattack**: ~55 expected damage turn 1, ~72 on subsequent turns (with bonus necrotic from prior-turn darkness cubes)
- **Hand crossbow**: generally a waste of an action compared to Multiattack; only useful for sniping from total darkness before closing
- An ally casting *darkness* creates a teleport pad for the shadowblade to Shadow Step into melee range
- Light sources are the shadowblade's enemy — hooded lanterns (30 ft bright) and *daylight* spell eliminate Shadow Step positions

## Key Decision Points

- Shadow Step advantage doesn't require being unseen — can teleport to dim/dark spot up to 30 ft from target, run up, and still get advantage
- If target illuminates surroundings, shadowblade needs a confederate to cast *darkness* to create a teleport pad
- Don't bother with innate spells — all require concentration, and Multiattack is far more valuable
- Skip variant Summon Demon (50% failure) — script the shadow demon into the encounter if desired

## Retreat Conditions

- **With allies**: retreats if mission fails or seriously wounded (≤60 hp), whichever comes first within 3 rounds
- **Solo**: same 3-round or ≤60 hp threshold; takes the L and skedaddles

> [!tip] The shadowblade's magical darkness from Shadow Sword doesn't block darkvision (unlike the *darkness* spell). Both the shadowblade and darkvision-equipped PCs can see through it. The darkness cubes serve as teleport pads for Shadow Step and bonus damage setup.
