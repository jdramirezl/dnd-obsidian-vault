# NPC Tactics: Bandits and Thugs

> **Book**: Monster Manual (NPC stat blocks)

## Bandit (CR 1/8)

- Low-level humanoid with scimitar and light crossbow. Leather armor, AC 12.
- **Cowardly in small numbers**. Bandits attack only when they outnumber their targets significantly (3:1 or better).
- Prefer **ambush** — attack from hiding along roads, in narrow passes, or from tree cover.
- Open with crossbow volleys, close to melee only if targets seem weakened.
- **Flee** when the fight turns — losing half their number or their leader triggers a rout.

## Bandit Captain (CR 2)

- The leader. Better armor, multiattack (dagger + scimitar), and a Parry reaction (+2 AC).
- Stays in the **thick of melee** to use Parry effectively. Leads from the front.
- Directs bandits to focus fire on the most dangerous-looking target.
- Will attempt to **negotiate or demand surrender** before fighting if the odds aren't clearly in their favor.
- Retreats if seriously wounded — bandits scatter without their captain.

## Thug (CR 1/2)

- Brute enforcer. Mace, heavy crossbow, Pack Tactics (advantage when ally is within 5 ft of target).
- **Pack Tactics** is the defining feature — thugs always fight in pairs or groups.
- More willing to stand and fight than bandits. Intimidation proficiency → may try to scare targets into compliance.
- Multiattack (2 mace strikes) makes them dangerous in melee.
- Retreat when **seriously wounded** or when their employer's cause is clearly lost.
