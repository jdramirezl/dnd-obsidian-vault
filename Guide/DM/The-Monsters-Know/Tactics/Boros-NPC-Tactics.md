# Boros NPC Tactics

> [!summary]
> The Boros Legion: zealous warriors devoted to justice over law. Soldiers fight in close ranks with Formation Tactics and won't retreat easily. Frontline medics heal from the front line. Reckoners are arcane brutes who open with *guiding bolt* then close to *shocking grasp*. Firefists are divine heavy-hitters who lead with *flame strike* and *blinding smite*.

---

## Boros Soldier (CR 1/2)
- Identical stats to Azorius soldier; shield + one-handed sword
- **Formation Tactics** — close ranks for advantage
- Don't always attack to subdue — against Rakdos, Dimir, Golgari: lethal force
- Fight on despite losses; may fight to the death depending on threat
- Small patrols may include **mastiffs** (enable Formation Tactics at distance)

---

## Frontline Medic (CR 1)
- Stronger, tougher, better armored than soldiers; less damage output
- **One per six soldiers** in a unit; fights on the front line, not behind it

### Spell Use
- **2nd-level slots:** Hold for *lesser restoration* if enemies can blind/deafen/paralyze/poison; otherwise pre-cast *aid* on unit before combat (+5 hp each)
- **1st-level slots:** *Cure wounds* on soldiers at half HP (or ≤6 hp under 5E24)
- *Sanctuary* on **hostages/bystanders**, not troops (troops need to attack)
- *Spare the dying* for NPCs/PCs making death saves
- *Resistance* for special noncombatants

---

## Reckoner (CR 4)
- Very high STR, high CON + INT; proficient Perception, expertise Arcana
- **First Strike** — moves up in initiative order
- Front-line brute with wizard spells; AC exceptionally high for CR

### Combat Flow
1. First Strike → advance; open with **guiding bolt** (boosted to 2nd level)
2. Once in melee: switch to **shocking grasp** (2d8, advantage vs. metal armor)
3. **Lightning bolt** if 2+ opponents line up
4. **Thunderwave** to clear 3+ brawlers blocking path to a priority target
5. **Shield** (reaction) while Lightning Backlash is on cooldown

### Lightning Backlash (Reaction, Recharge 5–6)
- Reserve for: ranged attacks, or melee hits dealing enough damage that halving > a sword swing

### Retreat
- Seriously wounded (≤20 hp): pauses to **Intimidate** — warns of coming wrath
- If foes don't back down, keeps fighting until it falls
- Pre-combat: cast **blur** if there's time (don't recast if concentration breaks)

---

## Firefist (CR 7)
- Divine spellcaster with greatsword; prefers magic first
- Company-level threat response; sent when 3+ reckoners aren't enough

### Spell Priority (Descending Damage)
1. **Flame strike** — 28 avg damage to 2+ foes (fire + radiant)
2. **Scorching ray** (4th level) — 35 total across 5 targets
3. **Blinding smite** (bonus action) — +13 radiant on next hit, potential blind; enables advantage on 2nd Multiattack swing
4. **Multiattack** (2× Greatsword) — 20 avg damage total
5. **Guiding bolt** — only when in melee with disadvantage on ranged, or target resists fire

### Other Spells
- **Banishment** — removes inconvenient enemies (permanent for extraplanar)
- **Wall of fire** — cuts off escape (no ring trick; firefist isn't fire-immune)
- **Revivify** — for important NPCs/PCs; pair with medic's *sanctuary*
- **Healing word** (bonus action) — only when *guiding bolt* would have disadvantage
- *Crusader's mantle* — only with 6+ allies within 30 ft
- *Shield of faith* — only if not planning *banishment* or *wall of fire*

### Guided Attack (Reaction, 1/encounter)
- +10 to one attack roll (own or ally within 30 ft)
- Save for an **important** roll — ranger's *lightning arrow*, paladin's smite, etc.

### Retreat
- Fights to the death; pauses to monologue when seriously wounded (≤46 hp)

## DM Encounter Tips

> [!tip] Boros Escalation
> Neighborhood threats: soldier patrols. District threats: add reckoners. Major threats: firefist arrives. The Boros escalate proportionally.

- Boros prioritize "good" over "lawful" — they'll break rules to stop real evil
- Soldiers fight on where Azorius would withdraw: "The line must be drawn *here!*"
- Reckoner's Intimidation skill is key to its personality — it's *scary*
