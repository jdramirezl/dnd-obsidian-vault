# Demon Tactics: Type 2 and 3

## Chasme (CR 6)

Demonic horse-sized mosquito. High STR/DEX — built for high-damage strikes. 60 ft. fly speed.

### Combat Behavior
1. Fly into midst of foes, hold position within **30 ft. of as many PCs as possible**
2. **Drone** affects everyone nearby — either works immediately or not at all; once all PCs have been exposed, no need to maintain position
3. Attacks one target and **focuses on it** until destroyed (conscious targets before unconscious)
4. Stays within reach and takes its licks — flying in/out would provoke opportunity attacks, and chasmes lack physical damage resistance
5. Indifferent to target selection (no preference for weak/strong)
6. Magic Resistance + DEX/WIS save proficiency — not impressed by spellcasters

## Hezrou (CR 8)

Foul-smelling brute. Dumb, tough, melee-only. Stench overwhelms nearby enemies.

**"Rrraaaahhhhh, claw claw bite."** Operating instructions fit in a fortune cookie. No complexity whatsoever.

## Glabrezu (CR 9)

### Tactical Summary
A boss enemy. INT 19, WIS 17 — can read PC stats accurately. 120 ft. truesight + telepathy. Goal is to **tempt mortals to destruction**, not fight. Opens with parley, not combat.

### Social Encounter (Default)
1. Detects approaching creatures via truesight/telepathy; opens negotiations before PCs know what they're talking to
2. Makes grandiose, ambiguous promises tailored to each PC's motivations
3. If PCs doubt: raises stakes with beautiful **cursed magic items** (curse triggers after delay — save every 10 days vs. DC = number of tendays owned)
4. If PCs refuse: dismisses them and attacks

### Combat Behavior
1. **First action:** Cast *darkness* (at will) — 15 ft. radius sphere. Overcomes darkvision and light spells ≤2nd level. PCs are blinded (disadvantage on attacks, glabrezu has advantage). PCs need *daylight* or higher to counter
2. **Multiattack:** 2 Pincer attacks (10 ft. reach) + 2 Fist attacks, OR 2 Pincers + 1 spell
3. **Default tactic:** Seize weakest opponent with pincer (grapple), seize a second, pull both adjacent, pummel with fists

### Spell Usage

| Spell | When |
|---|---|
| *Darkness* (at will, concentration) | Always first — shuts down non-magical PCs |
| *Confusion* (1/day) | 2+ clustered ungrappled opponents within 90 ft. 60% chance to deny action, 20% to redirect. Replaces *darkness* (both concentration) only if *darkness* alone isn't enough |
| *Power word stun* (1/day) | Single irritating ungrappled opponent within 60 ft. with ≤150 hp and CON save ≤+1. Won't use without ≥2:1 odds |
| *Dispel magic* (at will) | Shut down sustained buff spells — especially *daylight* cast at 3rd level |

### Retreat: Never. Demons don't fear death.

## Yochlol (CR 10)

Most likely summoned by a drow priestess of Lolth — the priestess is the boss, the yochlol synergizes.

### Combat Behavior
1. Appears in demon form (10 ft. reach on Slam — superior to spider form's 5 ft.)
2. Cast *dominate person* on the PC with highest **STR-to-WIS ratio**
3. Once that PC saves: cast *web* on unrestrained PCs, favoring melee fighters
4. Otherwise: two-Bite Multiattack
5. **Mist Form:** Limited use — incapacitates a single character, but DC 14 CON save is too easy. Only useful if allies can follow up on the incapacitated target, or to rescue allies from a dangerous PC

> [!tip] DM Tips
> - The glabrezu is a boss — build your adventure around it
> - Its social encounter is more dangerous than its combat; tailor temptations to each PC's backstory
> - *Darkness* + grapple combo is devastating — PCs without *daylight* are in serious trouble
> - Cursed items with delayed triggers (save every 10 days) create long-term campaign consequences
> - Hezrous are simple but their Stench aura makes them surprisingly effective area denial
