# Eye Monger Tactics

## Tactical Summary

Large durable brute (extraordinary STR/CON, terrible DEX/INT/CHA). Only 20 ft fly speed and no ranged attacks — relies entirely on False Appearance ambush (looks like a 12-ft asteroid). Can only swallow one creature at a time. Essentially a hazard, not a predator.

## Combat Behavior

- **Round 1 (Surprise):** False Appearance grants advantage on initiative. Bite → attempt to swallow on failed DEX save
- **Post-Swallow:** No reason to stay. Antimagic Gullet suppresses magic inside; victim is blinded and restrained. Stomach acid deals ~35 dmg/round vs Bite's ~12
- **Target Selection:** Prefers AC ≤16 targets (+9 attack = ~67% hit), but too dumb to do anything except bite whoever's closest
- **The Trap:** Swallowing a PC is strategically bad — forces the party to stay and fight, and the eye monger can't escape at 20 ft speed

## Key Decision Points

- Miss the ambush or target saves vs swallow → encounter is over, everything outruns it
- One meal limit: ~3 rounds minimum before digestion frees space
- Flying straight up works planetside; in Wildspace, PCs may keep pace via thought-movement

## Retreat Conditions

Fights to the death by circumstance — 20 ft speed means opponents won't let it leave. If it hasn't swallowed anyone and combat goes poorly, it drifts away, but anything can outpace it.

> [!tip] DM Tip
> Place among actual rocks where False Appearance matters. Consider homebrew speed boost (30–40 ft) or a paralysis cone to make it a real threat. Best as an environmental hazard in a debris field, not a standalone fight.
