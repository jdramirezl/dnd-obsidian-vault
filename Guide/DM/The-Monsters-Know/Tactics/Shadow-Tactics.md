# Shadow Tactics

> **Source**: [Original Article](https://www.themonstersknow.com/undead-tactics-skeletons-zombies-shadows/) | **Related**: [[Shadow]]

---

## Tactical Summary
- Undead driven by compulsion to siphon vitality from living beings, especially the pure of heart
- Avoids light, particularly direct sunlight (disadvantage on everything); running into open sun is the only sure escape
- Amorphous (passes through 1-inch cracks), Shadow Stealth (Hide as bonus action in dim light/darkness)
- Resistant to most damage including normal weapons; immune to most debilitating conditions
- Sole weakness: radiant damage — the good cleric/paladin is both juiciest victim and best counter

## Combat Behavior
- **Ambush**: Lies in wait → movement + bonus action to approach with Stealth → Hide
- **If Hide succeeds**: Attacks same round with advantage using Strength Drain (action)
- **If Hide fails**: Keeps moving, tries again next round
- **DM flavor**: "You saw a shadow move past you" — players may not realize it's a creature at first
- **Compulsion**: Once latched onto a PC, keeps draining that PC even though slipping away for another surprise attack might be more effective

## Retreat Triggers
- Radiant damage, magical weapon strike, or Turn Undead → Disengage (action), move out of view, Hide (bonus action), wait for victim to return within striking distance
- Does NOT flee from non-magical/non-radiant threats

## Strength Drain — Why It's Terrifying
- Avg 9 hp necrotic damage per hit
- Reduces target's Strength by 1d4 *each hit* — affects attack/damage rolls, weapon/armor use
- Lost Strength restored only after short or long rest
- **If Strength reaches 0: the PC dies outright** — not unconscious, *dead*

## DM Notes
- Pair with environments that limit bright light sources
- The cleric/paladin dilemma: shadow targets the pure-hearted, but they're also the counter
- Deserves more use in horror campaigns, subterranean dungeons, and creepy settings
