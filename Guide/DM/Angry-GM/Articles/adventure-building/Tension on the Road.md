# Tension on the Road to Elturel

> The Tension Pool is a generic mechanical tool that adds random complications and growing tension based on the players' actions. This article covers the streamlined rules and how to adapt the pool for wilderness travel.

## The Tension Pool (Current Version)

### Core Concept

A visible, growing pool of dice representing the growing chance something terrible will happen. The visibility is critical — it gives players dread that mirrors their characters. Use a glass bowl so players can see and hear the dice.

**You need:** Six d6s and a visible space in the middle of the table.

### Rules

- Pool starts **empty**, max **six dice**
- Adding the 6th die → immediately **Clear the Pool**
- Any die showing **1** when rolled = a **Complication** (always makes things worse, never beneficial)
- Rolling an empty pool → roll 1d6 alone (don't add it)

### Five Actions

| Action | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Add a Die** | Drop a d6 in pool. 6th die → Clear. |
| **Roll the Pool** | Roll all dice, note 1s, put back. |
| **Add and Roll** | Add a d6, then roll (unless 6th triggers Clear). |
| **Roll a Full Pool** | Roll 6d6 ignoring current pool. Don't change pool. |
| **Clear the Pool** | Roll all 6 dice, note 1s. Pool is now empty. |

### When to Use

- **Add** → cautious, time-consuming action
- **Roll** → reckless or dangerous action
- **Add and Roll** → both time-consuming AND reckless
- **Roll Full Pool** → action fills the whole pool (e.g., short rest in dungeon)

### Timekeeping

Each die = 10 minutes. Pool clears every hour. Spells < 10 min expire on die add. Spells ≥ 1 hour expire on pool clear.

## Wilderness Travel Adaptation

### The Day in Six Segments

| Period | Time |
|--------|------|
| Dawn | 4 AM – 8 AM |
| Morning | 8 AM – 12 PM |
| Afternoon | 12 PM – 4 PM |
| Evening | 4 PM – 8 PM |
| Night | 8 PM – 12 AM |
| Predawn | 12 AM – 4 AM |

### Normal Travel Day

- Travel **3 periods**, rest **2 periods** (long rest), 1 period idle
- **Roll a Full Pool** (6d6) once per day → ~66% chance of complication
- Distance per period: Fast 10mi, Normal 8mi, Slow 6mi

### Reckless Actions During Travel

Roll dice equal to periods elapsed: Dawn=1d6, Morning=2d6, Afternoon=3d6, Evening=4d6, Night=5d6, Predawn=6d6. Riskier later in the day — working as intended.

### Forced March

Beyond 3 travel periods: CON save DC 10/15/20 for 4th/5th/6th period. Slow pace = advantage, fast pace = disadvantage.

### Safe vs. Risky

**Safe:** Traveling by day, sleeping by night with watch rotation.

**Risky:** Traveling/resting in Unsafe Territory, traveling in Twilight/Darkness, camping without watch, camping in unsafe territory without hidden camp (Survival check).

## Seasonal Variation (Optional)

Three period types: **Daylight** (safe), **Twilight** (risky), **Dark** (risky + disadvantage).

| Season | Daylight | Twilight | Dark |
|--------|----------|----------|------|
| Spring/Fall | Dawn, Morning, Afternoon | Evening | Night, Predawn |
| Summer | Dawn–Evening | Night | Predawn |
| Winter | Morning, Afternoon | Dawn, Evening | Night, Predawn |

Party can safely push to 4 travel periods in summer. In winter, a full 12-hour day risks a Complication.
