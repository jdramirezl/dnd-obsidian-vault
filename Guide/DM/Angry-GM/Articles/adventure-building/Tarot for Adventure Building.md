# Tarot for Adventure Building Dummies

> Continuation of [Tarot for GMing Dummies](Tarot%20for%20GMing%20Dummies.md). Tarot makes a bad in-game mechanic, but because it's a storytelling tool at its core, it's a great way to build adventures.

## Caveats

- This is for **prep**, not at-the-table use
- Only good for **Big Prep** — outlining an adventure or planning a dungeon
- **Don't abandon intuition and judgment.** Let the cards guide you, but don't let them override your sensibilities

## RPG-Specific Tarot Associations

- **Major Arcana** → campaign-level events or major plot developments
- **Minor Arcana** → events/situations limited to the adventure itself
- **Court Cards** → NPCs whenever possible
- **Aces** → intangible, mystic, or divine forces
- Break any of these rules when your gut says to

## Spread 1: The Story-Gamer Spread

Based on the Celtic Cross. Pull ten cards, lay them Celtic Cross-style, reveal one at a time:

| Position | Name | Interpretation |
|----------|------|----------------|
| 1 | **The Party Situation** | Party's state at the adventure's start — the "Once Upon a Time" card |
| 2 | **The Major Conflict** | The big problem to solve (or the Apparent/Inciting Conflict for mystery) |
| 3 | **The Setting and the Tone** | Mood, atmosphere, what's going on in the world |
| 4 | **The Heart of the Conflict** | Why the conflict is happening — backstory, villain's motive, ancient event |
| 5 | **Past Consequences** | Something carried from past adventures — a consequence, rival, friend |
| 6 | **The Current Goal** | What the party is pursuing when the adventure starts |
| 7 | **What's at Stake for the Party** | What they have to win or lose |
| 8 | **What's at Stake for the World** | What others have to gain or lose |
| 9 | **Motivations** | How the adventure plays into the party's motivations |
| 10 | **The Effect on the Campaign** | Lasting impact. Minor Arcana = future lead. Court Card = NPC. Major Arcana = significant advancement |

## Spread 2: The Intuitive Dungeon-Builder Spread

### Setup

1. Decide room count (odd numbers best: 5, 7, 9, or 11)
2. Pull that many cards face-down
3. Get tokens for connections (doors/passages)
4. Lay first card — mark as **Entrance**
5. Add 1–2 connections, lay cards at their ends
6. Keep adding connections then cards; mark the last card as the **Goal**
7. Discard open connections

### Reading

1. **Reveal Entrance first** — defines the dungeon and sets the tone
2. **Reveal Goal second** — explains why the party is exploring
3. **Reveal remaining cards** as if moving through the dungeon from Entrance
4. **Interpret intuitively** — what does the card suggest for that room?

## Spread 3: The Table-Loving Dungeon-Builder Spread

For random dungeon generation without intuitive interpretation.

### Setup

Same as Intuitive Spread, plus pull half as many extra cards (rounded up). Place first extra under the Goal, rest under other rooms (not Entrance, max 2 per room).

### Reading Rules

- **One card** = a challenge
- **Two cards** = top is challenge, bottom is reward

### Suit Meanings

| Suit | As Challenge | As Reward |
|------|-------------|-----------|
| **Pentacles** | Combat | Treasure |
| **Wands** | Obstacles and traps | Boons, bonuses, shortcuts |
| **Swords** | Mysteries, investigations, hidden things | Information, knowledge, revelations |
| **Cups** | NPC encounters (nonhostile initially) | Friendship, contacts, alliances, favors |

- **Card value** = difficulty or value
- **Court Cards** involve NPCs regardless of suit
- **Major Arcana** = big challenges, rewards, or plot developments
