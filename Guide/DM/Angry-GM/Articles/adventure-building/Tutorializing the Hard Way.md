# Tutorializing the Hard Way

## Summary

When you want players to use a tactic they never think of — retreating, called shots, or any underused option — telling them doesn't work. Subtle hints don't stick. The only reliable method is Hard Tutorializing: a three-lesson structure borrowed from video game design that demonstrates the tool, reinforces it, and cements it. It requires briefly taking away player agency in one small encounter, but permanently expands their tactical options.

## Core Philosophy

As GM, you are the proxy game designer. Your job includes making sure the game is challenging but fair and equipping players with tools they need to win. If there's a strategy players never think of, it's your duty to make sure they know it exists. Once given the tool, it's on them to decide when to use it.

Why telling doesn't work: explaining rules doesn't stick, subtle reminders get ignored, NPC demonstrations get overlooked. Players only truly learn by doing.

## The Three-Lesson Hard Tutorial

### Lesson One: "Press Circle NOW!"

The heaviest-handed lesson. You temporarily take the controller.

**Setup:**
- Situation **extremely difficult to win** without the tactic
- **Easy to win** by employing the tactic
- **No good reason** to use any other strategy

**Execution:**
1. Start encounter normally
2. Before emergency, tell players narratively but unambiguously the normal approach won't work
3. Present the tactic as something the **character** knows
4. Don't give the player a chance to say no — go straight to rolling dice
5. Make sure it works this time — fudge if necessary

**Example (Retreat):** Adamantine guardian shrugs off all damage, slow, can't fit down hallway. Empty dead-end room. Tell players their characters realize this isn't winnable, explain retreat, resolve escape.

**Example (Called Shots):** Griffon in windy caves, ranged attacks penalized, massive flyby damage. Only winning move: target its already-injured wing.

**Keys:** Use absurd stats. Hide rolls and fudge. Present as character knowledge. Make a hidden knowledge roll to sell it.

### Lesson Two: "Looks Like Circle Works Here Too!"

Less heavy-handed. The tactic is the **best** answer but not the **only** answer.

**Setup:**
- Tactic is clearly the right move but situation isn't impossible without it — just punishing
- Tactic is part of the victory, not the whole thing

**Execution:**
1. Start encounter, give players one quick chance to remember on their own
2. If they don't think of it immediately, remind them

**Example (Retreat):** Dead-end room with visible key in alcove. Two stone guardians appear. Not as tough as the adamantine guardian, but grab the key and run is clearly best.

**Example (Called Shots):** Giant scorpion with venomous tail. Easy to cut off — if someone can flank or distract to reach it.

### Lesson Three: "Remember You Can Press Circle"

Almost subtle. The tactic is merely **helpful**, not required.

**Setup:** Normal encounter where the tactic is one good option among several.

**Execution:**
1. Run encounter normally
2. Watch if players use the tactic on their own
3. If they don't when appropriate, give a gentle reminder

> "Remember, you can always try to retreat from a fight."

## Practical Considerations

### Lesson Spacing
- **Minimum:** One non-tutorial encounter between lessons 1-2; two between lessons 2-3
- **Ideal (weekly, 4-6 encounters/session):** Lessons 1-2 in one session, lesson 3 next session
- **Maximum spread:** Over 4 sessions. Beyond that, lessons don't penetrate.

### Theming the Tutorial
Turn the tutorial into an adventure theme to avoid BS detection. An entire adventure about plundering a temple with invulnerable guardians (retreat theme). Risk: players may assume the tactic was adventure-specific.

### The Final Exam Boss Fight
A boss where the tactic is used multiple times to win. Retreat theme: juggernaut chasing players room to room while they disable runes. Called shots: tentacled monster with a glowing weak point.

### Post-Tutorial Maintenance
- **Let players overuse it** for a while — cements the lesson. Do NOT sabotage.
- Ensure useful situations every other session for a while
- Diminish reminders over time
- If players forget later, Hard Tutorial again
