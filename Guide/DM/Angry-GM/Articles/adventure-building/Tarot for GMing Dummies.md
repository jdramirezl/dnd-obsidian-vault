# Tarot for GMing Dummies

> Every GM should consider learning to read the Tarot — not as an in-game mechanic, but because there are a lot of crossover skills between Tarot reading and GMing.

## Part I: Understanding Tarot

### What Is Tarot?

A tradition of **cartomancy** — using 78 illustrated cards to gain insight into situations, mental states, and forces. Two subdecks:

- **Major Arcana** (22 cards) — named cards depicting archetypal life experiences
- **Minor Arcana** (56 cards) — four suits of 14 cards each

### How Tarot Cards Mean Things

Tarot speaks through **symbolism** to the unconscious mind:

- Most of what you think and do is influenced by unconscious brain processes (see Kahneman's *Thinking Fast and Slow*)
- Your unconscious is excellent at reading subjective and social situations — like RPGs
- Tarot's imagery deals with **archetypal human life experiences** — Jungian archetypes, Campbellian monomyth

### The Major Arcana: The Fool's Journey

22 cards telling the symbolic story of life — new beginnings, discovering personality, mastering impulses, dealing with change, finding meaning. Called the Fool's Journey because the Fool (card 0) represents the start.

### The Minor Arcana

| Suit | Domain | Element |
|------|--------|---------|
| **Cups** | Emotions and relationships | Water |
| **Pentacles** | Material and family matters | Earth |
| **Swords** | Intellect, reason, ideas (often conflict) | Air |
| **Wands** | Motivation, passion, creativity, action | Fire |

- **Numbered cards (Ace–Ten):** Events from budding potential (Ace) through completion (Ten)
- **Court Cards:** Page (energetic youth), Knight (brash young adult), Queen (wise nurturer), King (authoritative inspirer)

### Number Patterns

- Fives = struggle, loss, hardship
- Sixes = effort, work, persistence
- Twos = decisions, dualities
- Pages/Queens = inner qualities; Knights/Kings = outward-directed

### Learning: Intuitive Reading

**The best way to learn Tarot is to start reading Tarot.** Study the cards closely.

1. Look at the card — describe what's happening
2. Notice every detail — it's a hidden picture game
3. Trust your intuition — don't memorize fixed meanings
4. Move past difficult cards — they may reveal themselves later
5. Read the little book *after* readings, not during — it's another viewpoint, not an answer key
6. Notice patterns across suits and numbers

## Part II: Why Tarot Makes You a Better GM

### Trusting Your Gut

Most GMing is snap decisions. A quick intuitive decision is always better than a "correct" one. Tarot trains you to feel your way through every reading because every reading is subjective and context-sensitive. Stop overthinking, overplanning, overanalyzing.

### Flexing Your Creative Muscle

Creativity is forming associations and connections. Tarot trains your brain to make quick associations with **archetypal ideas that resonate with human beings**. The wiring carries over: swords → conflict → cunning → intellect → air elementals → magic swords → wizards.

### Recontextualization

Running a game means building a story from the inside as it unfolds. **Recontextualization** is reinterpreting past events to fit new information — what makes surprise revelations engaging, what lets GMs make meaningless things meaningful later without advance planning.

A Tarot reading is one long exercise in recontextualization — every revealed card requires reinterpreting every other card in the Spread.

> Tarot is about pulling a story out of disparate, jumbled images that are gradually revealed, incorporating the input from the Questioner. Sound familiar?
