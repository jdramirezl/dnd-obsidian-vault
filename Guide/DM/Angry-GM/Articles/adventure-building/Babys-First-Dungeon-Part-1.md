# Let's Build Baby's First Dungeon (Part 1 of 2)

**Source:** [The Angry GM](https://theangrygm.com/babys-first-dungeon-1/)
**Date:** November 3, 2021
**Series:** Practical Adventure Building

---

## Core Thesis

You can build a perfectly fun dungeon — a **Quick-and-Dirty Dungeon (QDD)** — in a couple of hours without extensive backstory, complex plots, or obsessive game balance. The key is to start with the *stuff inside the dungeon* (monsters, treasure, traps, terrain), not the story around it. Themes and narrative emerge organically from the process.

## The Process (Steps 1–3)

### Step 1: List What You Already Know

Write down any constraints or facts already established:
- Party level (keep it loose — "low-level" not "exactly 1st level")
- Anything already told to players about the dungeon
- Setting constraints (near civilization, etc.)

Don't start with story. Don't start with backstory, villain motivations, or goals. Those emerge later.

### Step 2: List Cool Stuff to Fill the Dungeon With

Gather your building pieces in this order:

#### Monsters First
- Browse the Monster Manual or DMG's CR index.
- Limit to slightly above party level and no more than 4 levels below.
- Read from biggest threats to smallest — you'll usually land on a climax creature first, then find friends for it.
- Jot down anything that looks fun. You won't use them all.

**Example list:**
- Spined Devil (CR 2), Imp (CR 1), Animated Armor (CR 1), Thug (CR 1/2), Flying Sword (CR 1/4), Blood Hawk (CR 1/8), Cultist (CR 1/8), Noble (CR 1/8)

Themes emerge naturally: "Spined devil → devils → cultists → animated objects → wizard's lair → Noble McBloodhawk the evil falconer leading a cult."

#### Treasure Second
- Assume one hoard per adventure (per DMG).
- Roll randomly, then edit what you don't like.
- Break the hoard up and scatter it around the dungeon.

**Example hoard:** 1,300 cp, 1,300 sp, 80 gp, 4×25gp art objects, brooch of shielding, +1 weapon, arcane spell scroll (swapped from boots of the winterlands — didn't fit the theme).

The treasure solidified the theme: lots of magic items + animated objects = abandoned wizard's lair. The cult showed up because the wizard had a pet devil.

#### Traps/Hazards/Terrain Third
- Animating armor and swords (basically traps)
- Spell turrets (lightning, fire, magic missiles)
- Invisible force walls (bonk into them, discover by breaking your nose)
- Invisible teleporter walls (Pac-Man tunnels — send you somewhere else)

### Step 3: Smash Game Elements into Encounters

Turn your pile of stuff into **encounter outlines** — not fully designed encounters, just plot points. Think about **progressions** (setups and payoffs, tutorials and twists).

#### Key Encounter Decisions:

1. **Spined devil boss fight** — too tough to combine with anything. Solo encounter. If PCs kill Noble McBloodhawk first and take his silver weapon, they have an edge.

2. **Noble McBloodhawk + blood hawk + cultist mooks** — noble on a balcony sending the hawk down. If the fight goes bad, noble flees to cower behind the devil. These two encounters form a **one-two climax punch**.

3. **Cultists in the mirror maze** — invisible walls + combat with enemies who know where the walls are.

4. **Flying swords guarding treasure (×2+)** — paired swords over art objects come alive when touched. Then: a single non-flying +1 sword over another object that *doesn't* attack. Players will be paranoid. Comedy gold.

5. **Plain cultist fight** — needed so players meet cultists on their own before the maze and the noble encounters.

6. **Invisible wall/teleporter puzzles** — introduce the mechanic safely before the maze fight.

7. **Animated armor** — one-off encounter guarding a treasure room. No setup needed; fits the wizard theme.

8. **Spell turret trap** — catches PCs by surprise. Maybe telegraph with a scorched cultist corpse nearby.

9. **Imp in a cage** — disguised as a rat, offers information for freedom. Can warn about the spined devil. Hidden consequences for dealing with devils later in the campaign.

#### Organized by Progression:

**Satan's Little Helpers:**
- Cult guards → Invisible wall/teleporter challenges → Cultists in mirror maze → Cultists with Noble McBloodhawk → Spined devil

**Magical Defenses:**
- Flying swords guarding treasure → Non-flying sword NOT guarding treasure → Spell turret trap guarding treasure → Animated armor guarding treasure

**Other:**
- Imp in a cage

## Key Principles

- **The dungeon isn't the adventure — the stuff inside is.** The map and backstory exist to serve the encounters, not the other way around.
- **Don't start with story.** Start with fun game elements. Story emerges.
- **Don't overthink game balance.** Keep one eyeball on it, but don't laser-focus.
- **Progressions turn a kitchen-sink hodgepodge into a real adventure.** Setups and payoffs, tutorials and twists.
- **Have fun with it.** "I love spined devils! Animated armor is so cool! Evil falconer!" is a valid design process.
- **You won't use everything.** That's fine. Whatever comes out is good enough.
