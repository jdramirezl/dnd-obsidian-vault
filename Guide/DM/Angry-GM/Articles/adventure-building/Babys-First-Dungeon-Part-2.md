# Let's Build Baby's First Dungeon (Part 2 of 2)

**Source:** [The Angry GM](https://theangrygm.com/babys-first-dungeon-2/)
**Date:** November 17, 2021
**Series:** Practical Adventure Building

---

## Core Thesis

Continuing from Part 1: once you have your encounters and progressions, you **sketch the map around the encounters** (not the other way around), then design encounter details, and finally polish. The whole process takes about two hours from a blank page.

## The Process (Steps 4–6)

### Step 4: Sketch the Map Until You're Happy

**Do NOT draw the map first and fill rooms later.** Place the big set-piece encounters on the map first, then build the map around them.

#### Mapping Process:
1. **Place set-piece encounters first** — figure out where they are, how big they are, and in what order they come. Earlier encounters in progressions must come first.
2. **Sketch, erase, resketch, repeat** — use pencil on graph paper (or mapping software). Multiple passes.
3. **Size encounters generously** — encounters need room. Minimum ~6×6 squares for most encounters. Constrain space deliberately only to affect gameplay (e.g., smaller room for the flying spined devil to limit its advantages).
4. **Consider the structure's logic** — if it's a building (villa, temple, castle), it needs to look like one. Caves are more forgiving.

#### The Example Villa Layout:
- **Courtyard (entrance):** Initial cultist encounter.
- **West wing:** Satan's Little Helpers progression (cultists → maze → noble → devil).
- **East wing:** Magical Defenses progression (flying swords → traps → animated armor → imp/treasure).

### The Painful Truth: Killing Your Darlings

The invisible wall teleporter mirror maze puzzle had to be cut. Not enough physical space or room in the adventure for a low-level QDD.

**This happens every time you create something.** You will always:
- Generate more ideas than you need
- Have to throw some out
- Not want to throw them out
- Have to do it anyway

Doing the *wrong* thing (forcing everything in) produces a bloated, contrived piece of crap. Doing the *right* thing (jettisoning what doesn't fit) keeps the adventure focused.

> "Ideas are cheap. You can afford to throw them away. There's no such thing as good ideas or bad ideas. The good — or the bad — is in the assembly, polish, and execution."

### Step 5: Encounter It Up

Design the mechanical details for each encounter. For a QDD, this means **numbers and terrain**, not fancy narrative design.

#### The Courtyard (3 cultists)
- Give cultists throwing daggers for ranged options (they'll fight in 3 encounters — need variety).
- Terrain: foyer with blind spots, low hedge wall, underbrush (difficult terrain), two trees (cover).
- Don't do encounter math. Three cultists. Gut feeling. Probably fine.

#### The Maze/Ballroom (acolyte + 2 cultist bodyguards)
- Since teleporter puzzles were cut, escalate with magic instead: an **acolyte** (MM 342) as a cult priest.
- Swap spells: *bless* → *bane*, *sanctuary* → *shield of faith*, *cure wounds* → *healing word* (weaker but ranged/bonus action).
- Terrain: ballroom with curtains around walls and stage.

#### Inner Courtyard (Noble + blood hawk + cultists)
- Blood hawk has *pack tactics* (fun surprise).
- Noble hides at far end while hawk dives the squishies.
- PCs should be able to bypass cultists and take down the noble before he runs (claiming his silver rapier for use against the devil).
- Terrain: colonnade around outside provides cover from divebombing hawk.

#### Spined Devil (solarium)
- Solarium with ceiling (limits flying) and scattered furniture (impedes ranged attacks).
- **Loaded solution** — multiple ways this can play out:
  - If noble fled here: tense standoff. Devil says "I don't work for you yet."
  - If noble is dead: devil opens negotiations rather than attacking.
  - If devil demolishes party: holds a hostage, demands they bring the Macguffin.
  - **The Macguffin:** A silver box in the library containing a rotted heart. The devil needs it to return to the Nine Hells. The wizard trapped it by withholding the heart after their deal was done.

#### East Wing Galleries (flying swords, trap, animated armor)
- **Southern galleries:** Paired flying swords over treasure on raised daises. Trigger radius = the dais. Players learn the pattern.
- **Northern gallery 1:** Single magical +1 longsword. Doesn't attack. Players will be terrified of it.
- **Northern gallery 2:** Copper mace on a dais. Looks like it'll animate. Actually a **lightning trap** (DC 11 Dex save, 1d10 lightning damage).
- **Library:** Imp in silver cage + spell scroll + silver Macguffin box. Imp offers information for freedom (vault location, devil weaknesses). Further answers cost "one drop of blood."
- **Vault:** Hidden behind secret bookshelf door (DC 15 Perception to notice, DC 15 Investigation to find mechanism). Contains animated armor (attacks without password) + brooch of shielding + cash.

### Step 6: Spit Shine and Polish

Go back over the whole dungeon and finish details. **Should take minutes to an hour, not more.**

#### Polish Items:
1. **Backstory** (one paragraph): Wizard made a deal with a fiend — spined devil on loan to handle a rival. Wizard had to cut out the rival's heart. Wizard kept the heart in a silver box to trap the devil. Wizard got assassinated. Years later, cultists show up. Noble tries to buy the devil's service but negotiations stall because the devil can't promise anything until someone gets the heart from the box.

2. **Map fixes:** Barricade the dining hall door (prevents bypassing the ballroom encounter). Close passages between southern and northern galleries (ensures players learn the flying sword pattern first).

3. **Lightning trap mechanics:** DC 11 Dex save, 5 (1d10) lightning damage. DC 15 Perception to spot runes, DC 15 Arcana to identify. *Detect magic* reveals evocation aura.

4. **Secret door:** DC 15 Perception to notice, DC 15 Investigation to find the book mechanism. Advantage if already aware of the door.

5. **Healing potions:** Scatter a few — one in the library, one on the acolyte.

6. **Custom treasure** (replace boring DMG table results):
   - Irregular wooden chalice of reddish-black wood stained with blood (10 gp)
   - Fist-sized crystal egg inscribed with whorls and spirals (35 gp)
   - Jade-handled ivory ritual knife (85 gp)
   - Ruby-like gemstone actually made of colored glass (2 gp)
   - Silver wire birdcage (50 gp) — contains the imp
   - Silver-plated bone coffer with simple clasp (125 gp) — contains the rotted heart

## Key Principles

- **Map serves encounters, not the other way around.** Draw the map around your encounters.
- **Kill your darlings.** Cut ideas that don't fit, no matter how cool they are.
- **Loaded solutions emerge from spitballing.** Think through how encounters play out and multiple outcomes appear naturally.
- **Don't overpolish.** If it takes more than an hour, you're doing too much.
- **Rule of thumb for dungeon composition:** ~1/3 encounters, ~1/3 obstacles and puzzles, ~1/3 empty space. (Treat as a guideline, not a formula.)
- **Total time from blank page to table-ready: ~2 hours.**
