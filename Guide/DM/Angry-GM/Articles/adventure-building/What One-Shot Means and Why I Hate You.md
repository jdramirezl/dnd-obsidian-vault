# What "One-Shot" Means and Why I Hate You

## Summary

A rant clarifying the term "one-shot" and raging against unproductive terminology fights. A one-shot is a **continuity-constrained** adventure — one with no connection to past or future games. It is NOT defined by fitting into a single session (that's a time constraint). The article also sets up a future discussion about how True Scenario Designers build complete single-session adventures.

## Definitions

### One-Shot = Continuity-Constrained

A one-shot adventure is an adventure with **no ongoing continuity**. When it's over, the characters go away, the world goes away, no one looks back. No connection to past or future games. Even if it spans multiple sessions, if the continuity ends when the adventure ends, it's a one-shot.

### Time-Constrained = Single-Session

A time-constrained adventure fits into a single session — it will never get more hours than scheduled. Convention games, event games, many pick-up games, some introductory games.

Most time-constrained adventures are also continuity-constrained. But the defining feature of a one-shot is the continuity limit, not the time limit.

### Terminology

- **One-shot** = continuity-constrained adventure
- **Single-session one-shot** = continuity-constrained AND time-constrained
- **Single-session adventure** = implies continuity constraint
- **One-off** = a time-constrained adventure within an ongoing campaign (e.g., a flashback session)

### The Three-Session Rule

Anything past three sessions is an actual game with its own continuity. A "four-session one-shot" is just a short campaign. There's no such thing as a time-constrained adventure longer than two sessions that needs special terminology.

## The Real Point: Stop Fighting About Terminology

The core message transcends RPGs:

**Once you understand what someone is asking about, stop quibbling over definitions and help them.**

In any discussion about something useful and productive, the second you understand the idea being discussed, all further debate about terminology is over. Continuing to argue terms after understanding is established makes you an "egotistical, unproductive, obstructionist asshole more concerned with proving yourself trivially right than being usefully helpful."

### When Terminology Matters

- When **teaching** — the teacher/advice-giver bears the burden of clarity
- When **establishing shared vocabulary** for ongoing discussion
- When **analyzing ideas** — terms should illuminate the underlying concepts

### When Terminology Doesn't Matter

- When someone has asked a question and you've clarified their meaning — now answer the question
- When the communication is already clear in context — the language did its job, move on

## Context: True Scenario Designery

This article sets the stage for a future piece on how True Scenario Designers build complete single-session adventures. The key design questions for constrained adventures come from identifying the constraints:

- **Continuity-constrained:** No past or future context. Everything must be self-contained.
- **Time-constrained:** Must fit within a fixed play window. Requires different structural choices.

Both demand special treatment. Time-constrained adventures have all the needs of continuity-constrained ones plus additional requirements around pacing and scope.
