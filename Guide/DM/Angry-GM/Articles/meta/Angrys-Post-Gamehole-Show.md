# Angry's Post Gamehole Show

## Summary

Published November 14, 2018. A convention recap of Angry's first visit to Gamehole Con in Madison, Wisconsin. After a minor medical issue delayed his regular articles, he shares his experiences from the weekend convention. He covers:

- **Gamehole Con overview:** A mid-sized convention at the Alliant Energy Center — not huge, but enough to fill a weekend. Good value if you don't have to fly. Notable for attracting industry names (Tim Kask, Jeff Easley, Mike Mearls, Matt Mercer, Monte Cook).
- **Friday night board games:** Played classic/older board games in the open gaming hall, including a memorable session of Fireball Island.
- **True Dungeon:** A life-sized dungeon crawl experience where you physically walk through rooms, solve puzzles, and use token-based combat. Angry and Tiny GM tried it for the first time and enjoyed it despite some pacing issues.
- **Pathfinder 2E Playtest:** Played a demo session and shares initial impressions of the system's changes from Pathfinder 1E.
- **General convention culture:** Observations about navigating the venue, the food truck situation in Wisconsin November, and trying to stay incognito as a known figure in the RPG community.

> [!tip] Context
> A personal convention recap — no actionable GM advice. Interesting for Angry's first impressions of Pathfinder 2E playtest and True Dungeon, and for a sense of the mid-tier convention scene.
