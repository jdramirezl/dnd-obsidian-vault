# Angry's Question of the Week — Martial Archetypes

**Source:** [The Angry GM](https://theangrygm.com/angrys-question-of-the-week-martial-archetypes/)
**Date:** September 18, 2018
**Category:** Meta

---

## Summary

The inaugural "Angry's Question of the Week" — a recurring community brainstorming exercise where Angry poses a question and readers answer in the comments under strict rules. This first installment asks readers to describe non-magical heroic fantasy character archetypes in five words or less. The article is mostly about establishing the format and rules for the series, with the actual question serving as the first test case.

---

## Key Points

### The Question of the Week Format

- Angry poses a question; readers answer in the comments — no discussion, no opinions on other answers, just answers.
- **No repeat answers** — you must read every existing answer before posting, which forces later answers to be more creative.
- **Length limits** — each question specifies a word or format limit (this week: five words or less).
- **One answer per person, one answer per comment.**
- All ideas posted become public domain — anyone can use them, including Angry for future articles or homebrew content.
- Rules are enforced strictly; rule-breaking comments are deleted, repeat offenders get banned.

### Why This Exists

- Angry's readership is clever and creative — the comment section is worth reading because trolls and morons have been weeded out over time.
- People keep asking Angry for ideas and sharing their homebrew — this format matches idea-makers with idea-needers.
- Angry also mines the answers for his own content and game design work.

### This Week's Question

> In five words or less, describe a heroic or adventurous fantasy character archetype who does not draw on any magic at all. No spells, no supernatural abilities, no blessings of the gods, nothing that couldn't exist in historical Earth or in a non-magic setting.

- Prompted by a previous article about D&D's problem with low-magic settings where almost every class has magical abilities.
- Generated 260+ answers ranging from "Knight errant" to "Improvised Weapons, Like Jackie Chan" to "Batman."
