# Losing the Plot Part 3 - Five Steps to Find It Again

**Source:** [The Angry GM](https://theangrygm.com/what-do-you-do-when-you-lose-the-plot-part-iii/)
**Date:** March 28, 2025
**Series:** Losing the Plot (Part 3 of 3)

---

## Clarifications

- "Losing the plot" is NOT about in-game plot. You can lose the plot running a completely open-ended hexcrawl. You can lose it in any long-term endeavor.
- It's about losing the connection between your goals (campaign vision) and your day-to-day actions (what happens each session).
- The behavior patterns (just getting by, flailing, firefighting, acting on whims) are usually things you do when you *subconsciously* sense you're off track.

## The Five-Step Recovery Process

### Step 1: Don't Just Do Something — Sit There

**Cancel your next two game sessions.** Seriously.

**Why:** Your brain has two systems:
- **System One:** Quick, snap decisions. Impulse, emotion, habit. Runs the game table.
- **System Two:** Long-term planning, strategic thinking. Information, logic, rational action. Runs your prep and campaign management.

When stressed, your brain assumes the world is burning and locks you into System One — even during prep time. If your game is a source of stress, even your planning becomes quick, impulsive, reactive.

**The fix:** Remove the pressure so System Two can engage. Go on hiatus — long enough to breathe, short enough the campaign doesn't die.

**Beware procrastination:** The minute pressure lifts, you'll procrastinate. Keep your scheduled game nights but use them for campaign repair instead of play. Block out time. **Write it down.**

**Think visibly:** If you're just thinking/meditating/ruminating, you're not doing anything. Write your thinking down — longhand on paper is scientifically better than typing, but anything physical beats pure thought.

### Step 2: Revisit and Revision Your Vision

Two sub-steps:

**Revisiting:** Dig out your written Campaign Vision and reread it. Twice. If you never wrote one (you should have), reconstruct it now — describe the campaign you *originally* wanted to run, not the one you're running now.

**Revisioning:** Give it a frank, honest review. Remove what's no longer valid. Adjust what you've discovered isn't possible or desirable. But be careful — stress makes you see everything negatively (Mental Filtering). Don't tear apart brilliant ideas just because you're frustrated.

Angry's revisions:
- ✅ Overall plot and structure still good
- ✏️ "One session between adventures" rule was unrealistic — players like world interaction, keep it but don't be rigid
- ❌ Six-month deadline no longer applies (external factors gone)
- 😤 AD&D 2E was a mistake — but stuck with it

### Step 3: How Is Today Going?

Describe the game you're *actually* running right now. Be blunt and clinical. No defending choices, no excuses, no analysis. Just facts.

> "I am running an inconsistent campaign... Most challenges come from random encounters as they travel... There is very little structure... it has taken the form of a continuous sequence of events..."

### Step 4: Find and Fix All the Problems

**Put your Vision and your Current State side by side. Spot the differences.** List everything you're not doing that you should be, and everything you are doing that you shouldn't.

Then for each difference, **state one small thing you can do differently** that might bring the game closer to the Vision.

**Key principles:**
- "Trying harder" never works. You need to actually change behavior.
- Solutions don't have to be perfect. You're lucky if a quarter of them fix anything.
- Small immediate improvements preserve your mental state while longer-term fixes take hold.

**Examples:**
- Problem: Canceling sessions due to poor prep → Solution: Schedule prep time on the calendar as an obligation
- Problem: Interludes filling entire sessions → Solution: Commit to narratively summarizing one scene per session instead of playing it out
- Problem: Not running structured adventures → Solution: Actually prep and run the next dungeon

### Step 5: Never Let This Happen Again (Until It Does)

**Make checking your Campaign Vision a regular habit.**
- Reread it every few sessions
- Ask: "Am I still running the game I described?"
- If not, identify deviations and fix one thing immediately
- Ideally, reread it before every prep session or game session

**Learn to catch yourself acting on impulse.** Before you add something on a whim mid-session, ask: "Is this aligned with my Campaign Vision? If not, why is it worth doing?" You don't have to stop improvising — just challenge yourself to justify it.

**Trust your gut.** Once goal-driven action becomes your habit, your instincts get good at spotting things that don't align. If something feels wrong, pull out the Vision and check.

## Summary

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Cancel sessions. Remove pressure. Let System Two engage. |
| 2 | Reread and lightly revise your Campaign Vision. |
| 3 | Clinically describe the game you're actually running. |
| 4 | Compare the two. List differences. One small fix per difference. |
| 5 | Regularly revisit the Vision. Catch drift early. |
