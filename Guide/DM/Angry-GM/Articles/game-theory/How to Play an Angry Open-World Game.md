# How to Play an Angry Open-World Game

**Source:** [The Angry GM — Angry Open World](https://theangrygm.com/angry-open-world/)
**Date:** April 28, 2021
**Series:** Angry's Open World Game (AOWG)

---

## Context

Angry designed an open-world D&D 3.5 campaign: zero-to-hero, no overarching plot, minimal GM prep, player-driven but GM-facilitated, organic discovery (no quest boards). Six sessions in, the game stalled because he forgot to tell his players how to play it. He wrote this instruction manual to fix it.

**The lesson:** RPGs are good at teaching rules but terrible at teaching players *how to play*.

---

## The 5 Player Principles

### 1. Know Your Needs, Know Your Wants, Know Your Motivations

**Needs** (survival first): safety, shelter, food, water → money in town, supplies in wilderness. Also: injury/disease treatment, equipment maintenance and upgrades, training resources for leveling up (space, sparring partners, libraries, trainers for new abilities).

**Motivations:** Wealth, glory, power, helping people, exploration. Guide decisions when survival is handled.

**Resources:** Anything useful — tools, contacts, political influence, magic items, information. Always stockpile.

### 2. Be Curious, Be Attentive, Make Note or Take Notes

The world is full of **opportunities**, **threats**, and **mysteries**:
- **Opportunities:** Job postings, NPCs with useful services, overheard rumors, subtle conversational hints
- **Threats:** Not always combat — can deprive resources, block motivations, carry costs if ignored
- **Mysteries:** Might be any of the above — investigate to find out

**Getting the Lay of the Land** — A time-consuming action: wander the local area, GM describes features. Skill checks may reveal non-obvious features. In wilderness, vantage points/spyglasses/familiars improve results.

### 3. Find a Need — Or Want — And Fill It

**Priority order:** 1) Immediate survival, 2) Near-term needs (equipment, training), 3) Wants and motivations.

Set up base camps before dungeon delving. Don't assume you must do things a certain way. Party splits for minor tasks, works together for major pursuits. Compromise is necessary.

### 4. Seek and Ye Shall Find

When your list of known opportunities doesn't meet your needs: be specific about what you're looking for and where/how you're searching. Ask the GM directly if stuck.

**Gathering Information** — A time-consuming action: canvas a locale for specific info or general rumors. Specificity improves results. May require bribes or skill checks.

### 5. Choose Carefully, But Not Too Carefully

You will never find or pursue all opportunities. No perfect choices exist. Every choice has costs, risks, and consequences. The game is about choices and what happens — not completion or optimization.

## Design Goals (GM Side)

| Goal | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Zero-prep capable | Can show up unprepared and still run a session |
| Player-driven | Players choose goals; GM doesn't assign quests |
| Organic hooks | Hooks emerge from world interaction, not quest boards |
| Proper adventures | Pursued goals feel like structured D&D with pacing and climaxes |
| Consequence-rich | Choices and non-choices both affect the world |
| Never lost | Players always see visible signs pointing toward fun |

## Key Quotes

> "This isn't a game you can complete and it's not a puzzle in optimization. Treat it as a playground."

> "It's not the players' jobs to actually do the game design. It's not their job to make the game fun."
