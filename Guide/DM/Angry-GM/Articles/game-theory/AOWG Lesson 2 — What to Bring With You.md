# AOWG Lesson 2: What to Bring With You

**Source:** [The Angry GM — Angry Open World Lesson Two](https://theangrygm.com/angry-open-world-lesson-two/)
**Date:** June 2, 2021
**Series:** Angry's Open World Game (AOWG)

---

## Prerequisites

Only works if you follow the core AOWG principles from Lesson 1. The goal: run an amazing open-world game without really working at it. Think small. Treat the world as terra incognita — you're exploring it with the players.

**The time rule:** If you spend more time prepping than running, you messed up.

---

## Thing 1: Books of Stats

Bring your Monster Manual and DMG. Lean on pre-made content — heavily.

- Most monsters don't survive 3 rounds — spending an hour on a custom one has no margin
- A good GM builds a good game out of anything

**Browse regularly.** Between sessions, leaf through monster books and magic item lists. Casual exposure builds improvisation ability. This is how you get "encyclopedic knowledge."

**Shortlist.** Skim with a purpose: 10 beasties for temperate grasslands, magic items for ancient ruins, traps for barrows. Shortlisting turns vague memory into ready-to-use options.

**Keep it accessible.** One core MM + one core DMG + a small folder of selected extras. Know what you've got and where it is.

---

## Thing 2: Crap Maps

You need maps, but they don't need to be nice. Working maps are utilitarian tools no one else sees.

- Hand-drawn blobs on hex paper, 10 minutes with Sharpie markers
- Leave blank space to add discoveries during play
- Must be easy to edit at the table
- Pretty player-facing maps are optional extras
- You don't need a battle map for every fight — most combats run fine without visual aids

### The Overworld Map (Most Important Map)

Shows the area players are currently exploring. Tracks where they are, where they're going, how they get there.

**What it needs:**
- A **dot in the middle** — the players' home base / hub town
- **Blobby geographical regions** with names (forests, hills, wastelands)
- A **scale** — hex paper with 6-mile hexes works great
- **Mostly blank space** — easy to add new dots and features

**Living document:** Keep updating it. Barrow Hill wasn't on the map at start — it was a name on a list. When players researched burial sites, it got placed and plonked onto both maps.

---

## Thing 3: Slapdash Lists of Things That Exist

Almost everything you bring is just a list. When you think of them as lists (not "notes"), you keep them brief.

**What a list is:** Items that exist somewhere in your world. You might not know much about them or where they are. Each is ready to drop into the game when needed. Detail varies from a single word to a sentence with stats.

**What a list is NOT:** Random name generators, personality trait tables, or adventure seed piles.

### The Regional List of Things to Find (2nd Most Important)

One list per region, broken into five sub-lists:

| Sub-list | What it is | Examples |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| **Points of Interest** | Destinations worth a map dot | Cities, dungeons, ruins, sacred groves |
| **Encounters** | Transient things stumbled over while traveling | Wandering creatures, hazard/lair references |
| **Travelers** | Specific NPCs wandering the region | `Plumcloak, Human Explorer`; `god disguised as old man` |
| **Lairs** | Small monster homes, one-and-done | Wolf dens, bandit camps, goblin villages |
| **Hazards** | Natural/supernatural obstacles | Quicksand, sinkholes, flash floods, fairy rings |

Thin on details, space to add more, mostly shortlisted from the MM + evocative names. Quick to create (~10 min).

### The Presumed Player Itinerary (Most Important List)

Made before every session. Not used during the game — guides between-session prep. How you present a world full of opportunity. *Covered in Lesson 3.*

## The Prep Philosophy

1. Prep what you absolutely need — only until good enough
2. Prep whatever's fun with remaining time
3. Stop prepping and go do something more fulfilling

Better uses of "prep" time: browse RPG books, read, brainstorm, daydream. All improve GMing more than over-preparing.
