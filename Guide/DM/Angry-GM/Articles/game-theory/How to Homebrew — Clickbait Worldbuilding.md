# How to Homebrew: Clickbait Worldbuilding

**Source:** [The Angry GM — How to Homebrew](https://theangrygm.com/how-to-homebrew/)
**Date:** March 30, 2020
**Series:** Start Your Own Campaign

---

## Core Thesis

Start small and build. Upfront worldbuilding is almost entirely unnecessary — and actively harmful. The best settings grow organically from play, not from pre-game design documents.

---

## The Worldbuilding Trap

Two ways to get caught:
1. Trying to build a complex world on par with a published setting before running a game
2. Getting more interested in building the world than in running games for players

**Why it's a trap:**
- Worldbuilding that doesn't grow out of game events is a waste of time
- The more you decide beforehand, the less you react, pivot, or follow whims
- Over-planned worlds feel artificial and contrived — real worlds are messy with gaps
- Backstory (world or character) constrains you in an inherently organic, reactive medium

**Key insight:** When you do it right, writing a game becomes an act of curiosity and running a game becomes an act of discovery. Unanswered questions about your world create genuine excitement.

---

## The 10-Fact System

No more than **5 facts for players** and **5 facts for yourself** before the game starts. Facts are sentences, not paragraphs.

### Player-Facing Facts (5)

1. **What the campaign is about and what the goal is** — Don't bury the lede. Be vague only to avoid spoilers.
2. **How the game starts** — Where the characters are and what they're doing at session one.
3. **Everything they need to make characters** — Races, classes, backgrounds, deity lists. Deliver in person if possible.
4. **Two neat, unique, interesting things about the world** — Make the world feel special. If you can't think of something, make it up and figure out what it means later.

### GM-Only Facts (5)

5. **What the campaign is really about** — Campaign secrets, hidden truths.
6–8. **Three wildcard facts** — Key NPCs, plot points, a timeline, a god list. Sentences or short lists only.

### Bonus Rules
- Always remove at least one race and one class from core options
- Never let players make characters before they know the premise and starting situation
- Negotiate during character creation — invent on the spot but also be willing to say no

---

## After the 10 Facts

Everything else comes from writing and running games. Track it all in a **campaign bible**.

---

## Key Quotes

> "When you do it right, writing a game becomes an act of curiosity and running a game becomes an act of discovery."

> "The most important thing to take from this whole article is that worldbuilding that doesn't grow out of the events of the game is a waste of time."

> "The best settings are the ones that grew organically. They weren't built as worlds in which to tell stories."

---

## Practical Takeaways

- [ ] Write no more than 10 facts before starting a homebrew campaign
- [ ] Resist the urge to detail anything the players can't interact with yet
- [ ] Keep a campaign bible and update it after every session
- [ ] Let gaps and unanswered questions exist — they make the world feel real
