# Systematic InterACTION!

**Source:** [The Angry GM – Systematic InterACTION!](https://theangrygm.com/systematic-interaction/)
**Date:** January 21, 2016
**Series:** How to F#%&ing GM

---

## Summary

A formalized system for running social interaction encounters, built from Angry's intuitive GM habits. Uses scored OBJECTIONS and INCENTIVES to track NPC willingness, with optional layers for leverage, offense, deflections, and external forces. The core is simple — "make up two numbers and compare them" — but the extensions make it rich.

---

## Core Concepts

### InterACTION! Encounters Don't Exist
- There are no "social encounters" — there are encounters the players *choose* to resolve through talking.
- Always be ready for players to switch approaches (combat, stealth, etc.) mid-scene.

### Every Encounter Needs Four Things
1. **Dramatic Question** — the goal stated as a question ("Will the king send troops?").
2. **Source of Conflict** — the reason the NPC won't cooperate (the OBJECTION).
3. **Decision Points** — choices the PCs must make.
4. **System** (optional) — a structure for pacing and scorekeeping.

## The OBJECTION! / INCENTIVE! System

### OBJECTIONS (Negative Scores)
- The reason the NPC won't cooperate. Score each from **-1** (minor) to **-5** (major).
- Example: King won't send troops because (a) orcs aren't a threat [-2] and (b) offended by Dongor [-2].

### INCENTIVES (Positive Scores)
- Reasons the NPC *would* help. Can be pre-existing or created by PCs during the encounter.
- Example: NPC wants to do the right thing [+2].

### Resolution
- When total INCENTIVES ≥ total OBJECTIONS → NPC cooperates.
- When OBJECTIONS exceed INCENTIVES by 5+ → NPC shuts down. Encounter fails.

### Scorekeeping During Play
- Successful PC action addressing an OBJECTION → reduce that OBJECTION (usually by 1).
- Successful PC action creating/boosting an INCENTIVE → increase that INCENTIVE.
- Failed actions usually leave scores alone, but can increase OBJECTIONS or decrease INCENTIVES.
- Amazing arguments or huge offers can shift scores by more than 1.

### Example Walkthrough
```
Starting state:
  Orcs aren't a threat: -2
  Offended by Dongor: -2

PC argues orcs are massing → succeeds →
  Orcs aren't a threat: -1
  Offended by Dongor: -2

PC appeals to personal glory → succeeds →
  Orcs aren't a threat: 0
  Offended by Dongor: -2
  Personal glory: +1

Need: INCENTIVES ≥ OBJECTIONS to win
```

## GM Responses: Open vs. Closed

- **Open response** — references a specific OBJECTION or INCENTIVE, gives PCs something to work with. ("But how can I be sure my family will be safe?")
- **Closed response** — general refusal, gives PCs nothing. ("I don't believe you.") Used after failed actions.

## Failure

- When the encounter fails, **be heavy-handed**: "You keep trying for several minutes but make no further progress. You have to give up. What do you do now?"
- Don't let players loop endlessly. Narrate the scene closed.
- Consequences: NPC begs them to leave, calls guards, has a breakdown, attacks, etc.

## Optional Extensions

### Leverage and Faux Pas
- **Leverage:** situational advantage (elf talking to racist elf NPC → bonus).
- **Faux Pas:** situational disadvantage (dwarf talking to racist elf → penalty).
- Can be pre-planned or improvised on the fly.

### Reception (Flattery Rule)
- Binary status: NPC is **Receptive** or not.
- Earned through proactive gestures (gifts, respecting traditions, research).
- Receptive → all actions get a bonus.

### Offense (Screw-Job Rule)
- **Minor Offense:** all actions take a penalty. Recoverable via apology.
- **Major Offense:** NPC shuts down completely. No score changes possible until recovered. Usually ends the encounter if not fixed immediately.
- Can be pre-planned ("questioning the king's strength → Major Offense").

### Blocks, Deflections, and Deceptions (Defense Mechanism Rule)
- **Block:** NPC raises an irrelevant objection ("Why should I trust you?"). Addressing it wastes time but doesn't advance scores.
- **Deflection:** fake OBJECTION/INCENTIVE the NPC keeps bringing back. A red herring.
- **Deception:** NPC lies. A failure state — king promises troops but won't send them.

### External Forces (Jafar Rule)
- An outside party actively works against the PCs during the conversation (hostile advisor, mind-controlling vampire).
- The external force increases OBJECTIONS or decreases INCENTIVES on its own turns.
- PCs can counter, discredit, or remove the external force.

### Insight / Sense Motive
- **Passive defense:** saving throw against blocks, deflections, deceptions. Reveals the truth.
- **Saving throw against Major Offense:** warns PCs before they say something catastrophic.
- **Active use:** idle chitchat + Insight roll reveals hidden OBJECTIONS or INCENTIVES.
- Do NOT let players just say "I roll Insight." They must describe what they're doing.

---

## Key Takeaways

- The system is intentionally loose — follow your gut, adapt on the fly.
- Each PC statement must be a genuinely new argument, not a rephrasing (GM still adjudicates "can it succeed?").
- Conversations have infinite decision points — unlike lockpicking, the same OBJECTION can be hit multiple times with different approaches.
- The extensions (leverage, offense, externalities) are the real depth — the base tracker is just scaffolding.
