# King Angry and the Holy Grail

**Source:** [The Angry GM](https://theangrygm.com/king-angry-and-the-holy-grail/)
**Date:** September 26, 2018
**Series:** Crafting a Crafting System

---

## The Quest for a Crafting System

The "Holy Grail" of tabletop RPGs: a crafting system that actually works. Many have tried, all have produced unsatisfying results. This article lays the groundwork for *why* crafting fails and what a good system would need to look like.

## Why Crafting Always Goes Wrong in D&D

Crafting is part of a pile of **"afterthought rules"** — things designers put off to figure out later (multiclassing, magic items, everything that isn't character generation or combat).

The deeper problem: **crafting needs to hang off a core part of gameplay that doesn't exist.** D&D has a carefully balanced progression for class abilities, but the system for customizing characters through equipment is nearly nonexistent:

- No real magic item progression (beyond vague guidelines)
- No progression for mundane equipment
- Beyond raw numbers and minor keywords, nothing distinguishes one weapon from another
- Spells already provide a carefully crafted progression of non-numerical abilities — a flying carpet *is* a mass fly spell, so it should be a 7th-level item craftable by a 7th-level artisan

D&D only has **one progression:** gain XP → acquire class levels → get new powers. All other progressions have been removed in the name of simplicity. Equipment and skills *should* be to fighters and rogues what spells are to wizards — the axis of customization and new abilities.

## Why Craft? Intrinsic Rewards

### Creative Expression
Race/background/class/build = multiple-choice questions with limited options. Custom equipment = open-ended. With 15 weapon types × 2 modifier slots × 20 modifiers, you get thousands of unique variations. Only *your* dwarven fighter artisan made *this* particular weapon.

### Discovery
Mixing and matching abilities, finding synergies between equipment and class features, exploring the mechanical space — this is a form of discovery many players crave.

### Fantasy Fulfillment
Some players simply want the experience of playing a craftsman or self-sufficient adventurer.

## Why Craft? Extrinsic Rewards

Custom equipment lets players:
- Select their own abilities outside class restrictions
- Prepare for upcoming challenges
- Cover class/race weaknesses or enhance strengths
- Make meaningful choices about their character's capabilities

**Creative expression + discovery + preparation for challenges ≈ the core engagement of an RPG.**

## It's Not Really About Crafting

What people actually want is a **player-controlled equipment customization system** — equipment choices that matter, that lie outside character archetype, and that the player actually gets to make.

Once you have a customizable equipment system, laying crafting over it is easy. Crafting is just an alternative to spending gold on equipment — cutting out the middleman.

## Equipment as a Second Progression

Equipment progression works exactly like leveling:

| Leveling | Equipment |
|----------|-----------|
| Overcome challenges → earn XP | Overcome challenges → earn gold |
| Trade XP for new level | Trade gold for new/upgraded equipment |
| New level = new abilities | New equipment = new abilities |

Equipment is basically **a spell list not tied to a specific class, gated behind gold instead of XP.**

Tracking wealth-by-level is no harder than tracking XP: "If one goblin has 10 GP, how much gold do four goblins have?"

## What's Actually Missing

The real missing piece isn't crafting — it's **a better, more robust equipment system.** You need that foundation before you can build crafting on top. A craftsman is only as good as his materials.
