# Tension About the Tension Pool

**Source:** [The Angry GM](https://theangrygm.com/tension-about-the-tension-pool/)
**Date:** September 23, 2021
**Series:** Hacking Rules

---

## Overview

A follow-up to the "Definitive, Final, Comprehensive" Tension Pool rules. Addresses community feedback, clarifies confusion, and explains the design philosophy behind the mechanic's evolution.

---

## Origin Story

Started in late 2016 as the "Time Pool," invented on deadline to solve a problem: D&D doesn't make players care about efficient play. Without consequences for wasting time, players just brute-force search everything.

**The invention:** Combined two ideas:
1. Old-school D&D timekeeping (exploration turns)
2. Visible dice pool mechanics (like the Doom Pool from *Marvel Heroic Roleplaying*)

**The logic:** Make wasting time scary with a visible indicator. It became the most frequently used mechanic Angry has invented. Every change since has been to maximize **versatility** and **elegance**.

---

## Core Rules Recap

### Tension Pool (Core Mechanic)
- **Time-Consuming Action** → add a Tension Die
- **Reckless Action** → roll all current Tension Dice
- **Six dice in the pool** → clear by rolling, then set aside
- **Any 1 on a roll** → Complication arises
- **Zero dice when Reckless** → roll one die but don't add it
- Don't track actual time. Evaluate each action as Normal, Reckless, Time-Consuming, or Both
- The pool runs forever across scenes and days

### Time Pool (Variant)
- Each die represents a specific time increment
- Add dice when that time passes, regardless of character actions
- Allows time tracking but requires changing temporal resolution at different scales

---

## Don't Change the Core Mechanics

The Tension Pool is a **player-facing behavioral mechanic**. It must be simple, intuitive, work the same every time, and not distract from gameplay. Don't mess with it.

**Do** customize Complications freely — that's adventure design, not mechanics.

---

## It's Not Just Random Encounters

Yes, it's built on old-school random encounter rules. But the **visible dice pool is the key**.

### Why Visibility Matters
- Tells players every random encounter is their own fault
- Makes time feel less arbitrary — less like GM fiat
- Changes player behavior — players think hard about when to search, when to risk

> If you moved the Tension Pool behind the screen but did everything the same, it would be mechanically identical. And it wouldn't work.

### Don't Use It Strictly as a Monster Generator
~Two Complications per three hours average. If every one is a monster, you'll overwhelm the party. Include harmless encounters (rats, bat swarms, spooky but non-threatening things).

---

## Using It at the Table

### When to Add/Roll
All fiddling happens during **Action Adjudication** — the same step where you decide ability scores, DCs, etc. Adding dice and rolling is part of **Action Resolution**.

### Complications Don't Change Outcomes
If the check succeeds, the action succeeds. A Complication can be a *consequence* but can't change the *outcome*. Hurt yourself battering a door, but the door still opens.

### Rolling Behind the Screen
Angry hides the roll. Benefits: keeps players on edge, they assume every bad thing comes from the pool (amplifies behavioral effect), avoids "waiting for the other shoe to drop."

### Reckless vs. Risky
Every action requiring a die roll is risky. **Reckless** means ignoring imminent danger or obvious consequences. Camping in the wilderness isn't Reckless. Camping in orc-sign territory with skulls on pikes? Reckless.

### Player Warnings
The pool fills the same role as verbal warnings:
- "That's going to take a long time" → "I'm adding a Tension Die"
- "That could have bad consequences" → "I have to roll the Tension Pool"

---

## On Active Perception Checks

Active Perception checks don't happen unprompted. Passive Perception handles awareness when not searching. A Wisdom (Perception) check means **deliberately searching** — physically interacting with the environment. Anyone watching would see the search.

**For the Tension Pool:** Actively searching = Time-Consuming. Add a die.

---

## VTTs

The pool **doesn't work unless everyone can see it** filling from zero to six. It can't be closed or hidden. Without visibility, it's just an overly complicated wandering monster system.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Power comes from visibility and simplicity — don't change core mechanics
- Customize Complications freely
- Reckless ≠ Risky
- Complications are consequences, not outcome-changers
- Visibility is non-negotiable
