# Through a Glass Darkly: IC, OOC, and the Myth of Player/Character Separation

**Source:** [The Angry GM](https://theangrygm.com/through-a-glass-darkly-ic-ooc-and-the-myth-of-playercharacter-seperation/)
**Date:** May 31, 2017
**Category:** How to F$&%ing GM

---

## Summary

The hard line between "player" and "character" is a myth that causes more problems than it solves. Trying to enforce strict IC/OOC separation leads to endless arguments about metagaming, information sharing, telepathic communication, and backpedaling on statements. The solution is "The Murky Mirror" — players and characters are imperfect reflections of each other, always synchronized in what they're doing even if the exact words differ.

## The Problem

The player/character separation creates a cascade of table problems:

- **Metagaming arguments:** Fights over whether players can act on information their characters "shouldn't" know
- **Information sharing fights:** One player identifies a monster's weakness, the whole party acts on it, GM flips out because the info wasn't "shared" yet
- **"That was out of character" backpedaling:** Players mouth off about an NPC who is right there, then claim it wasn't IC when the NPC reacts
- **Combat committee meetings:** Every turn becomes a group strategy discussion, or the GM forbids all communication because "you're not telepaths"
- **Internal monologue oversharing:** Players narrating thoughts, then getting upset when others react to actions rather than stated motivations

None of this is fun. It's all players fighting with each other or with the GM.

## The Murky Mirror

**Players and characters are reflections of each other in a murky mirror.** Not perfect reflections, but synchronous.

### Core Rules

1. **If the players are talking, so are the characters.** They're saying the same things in different words.
2. **Don't say anything out loud you don't want overheard.** If a player communicates something, their character is sharing it.
3. **If characters can't communicate, neither can players.** Force decisions in combat: "You only have a few seconds to act, everyone shut up, what do you do?"
4. **Tactical discussion is a double-edged sword.** If players say "protect Gertrude, she's vulnerable," enemies who understand Common will target Gertrude.
5. **Knowledge checks get worked into flavor text.** Assume the knowledgeable character shares info naturally.
6. **A lot of stuff happens off-screen.** Assume information sharing happens without playing it out.
7. **Jokes and banter between players reflect jokes and banter between characters.** Just different jokes.
8. **The game world doesn't pause for player discussion.** Argue too long at an intersection and something comes to investigate.

### Tone Enforcement

The Murky Mirror naturally enforces tone. If the world reacts realistically to silly behavior, players stop the silly behavior. If you roll with the crazy stuff, the tone is comedic.

## Inner Voices and Second Thoughts

The GM must act as a safety net:

- **Warn before disaster.** If a player casually says something that would enrage the king, jump in: "Do you really want the king to hear you say that?"
- **Correct wrong information.** If players spout bad info about the world, correct them.
- **Don't be a gotcha GM.** The point is to make things easier and more fun, not to pounce on mistakes.

> "Those who sacrifice the FUN of a GAME for REALISM deserve NEITHER."

## You Can't Compartmentalize

- You're not being a character — you're making choices for a character through the lens of your own perception
- Two players playing identical characters will still arrive at different choices
- You play the game because YOU want to make the decisions through your character
- It is impossible for you to ignore information in your head

## Practical Applications

| Situation | Murky Mirror Approach |
|---|---|
| Player says something dumb about an NPC | NPC reacts (but warn first if severe) |
| Players argue about which way to go | Something hears them and investigates |
| One player identifies a monster | Assume the info gets shared naturally |
| Players discuss combat tactics | Enemies who understand the language react |
| Player claims "that was out of character" | Too late — your character said something equivalent |
| Anachronisms and modern phrases | Characters are saying the equivalent in their own words |
