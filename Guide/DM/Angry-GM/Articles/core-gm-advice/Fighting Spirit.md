# We've Got Fighting Spirit, How About You?

**Source:** [The Angry GM](https://theangrygm.com/fighting-spirit/)
**Date:** March 30, 2016

---

## Summary

Angry analyzes why D&D combat devolves into a damage race with no defensive tactics, then proposes a homebrew system called **Fighting Spirit** — a buffer above hit points that creates an emergency "Dispirited" state where characters are weakened but still conscious and able to act.

## The Problem

### Why Players Let Characters Die
- On average, it takes ~3 hits to drop a character, yet players treat every battle as a race to death
- Players don't care about damage taken — only about dealing MORE damage than the enemy
- The "retreat and rest" paradox: if the GM prevents resting, they're just forcing the party to die slowly

### Total Existence Failure
D&D uses a "Total Existence Failure" HP system — you suffer zero ill effects from losing HP until you lose your LAST hit point, then you instantly go from fully functional to unconscious/dying. This creates problems:

- **No warning sign** that an emergency is happening — no one knows when to call for retreat
- **No one agrees** on when things have gone bad enough to change tactics
- **Every fight becomes a fight to the death** because defensive options always come at the expense of offense, and in a damage race, any non-damage action means you lose

### The Dying-Before-Death Safety Net Makes It Worse
The unconscious/dying state at 0 HP was meant to be a safety net, but it:
- Removes the player from the game entirely (no agency)
- Makes 0 HP LESS scary than true Total Existence Failure
- Requires abundant healing to function, making "undo damage" the only defensive strategy
- Creates the "always bring a healer" problem

### Death Spiral Is Not the Answer
Some games penalize you as you take damage (Savage Worlds). Problem: once you start losing, you lose faster and faster. D&D characters take too much damage for this to work well.

## The Solution: Fighting Spirit (D&D 5E)

### Hit Points Become Fighting Spirit (FS)
Everything that was HP — for PCs only — becomes **Fighting Spirit**. FS represents tenacity, defense, pain resistance, energy, parrying ability, training, etc. Just a name change.

### Characters Also Get Hit Points
At 1st level, characters get HP equal to their starting FS. A Fighter begins with 10 + Con mod FS AND 10 + Con mod HP. HP represent actual life-threatening injuries.

### Hit Dice Become Spirit Dice
Work exactly the same but recover Fighting Spirit only (not HP).

### Damage
- If you have ANY FS: damage goes to FS (you mostly defended yourself)
- If you have NO FS: damage goes to HP (you took a serious injury)
- **Damage cannot spill over** from FS to HP. If you have 3 FS and take 7 damage, you go to 0 FS and spillover is ignored. (This is critical to the system.)

### Healing
- Any magical/mundane healing applies to EITHER FS or HP, but not both
- Spirit Dice only recover FS
- Both FS and HP fully recover on a Long Rest (though HP might recover more slowly)

### The Dispirited Condition (0 Fighting Spirit)

> **Dispirited**
> - Disadvantage on attack rolls
> - Saving throws against the creature's spells/attacks/abilities have advantage
> - Exhaustion level increases by one while dispirited
> - Removed when the creature has at least 1 Fighting Spirit

**Offensively crippled, defensively intact.** Can still use healing spells, Disengage, Dodge, defensive abilities, and tactical options. Creates an emergency state between "fine" and "dying" where the character has agency.

### Progression: Healthy → Dispirited → Dying → Dead

### At Higher Levels
- Gain FS each level (same as normal HP progression)
- Gain HP increases at levels 4, 8, 12, 16, 19 (equal to one level's FS gain)
- Result: HP ≈ 1/3 of FS after ~3rd level
- At 0 FS, worst case = next hit is deadly; usually can survive 1-2 more hits

### Design Goals
- Emergency state that demands defense/healing/retreat without removing player agency
- Binary states (not a death spiral): Healthy → Dispirited → Dying → Dead
- Minimal new rules, doesn't break existing mechanics
- Makes logical sense (FS = the narrative BS parts of HP; HP = actual injuries)
- Increases value of defensive abilities and retreat tactics
- Players can handle their own emergencies before forcing the medic to break off

### Note
This was published as an untested experiment. Angry acknowledged it might not completely solve the healer-dependency problem but predicted it couldn't make things worse.
