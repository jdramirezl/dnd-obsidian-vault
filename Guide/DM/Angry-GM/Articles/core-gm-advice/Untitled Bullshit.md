# Untitled Bullshit

**Source:** [The Angry GM](https://theangrygm.com/untitled-bullshit/)
**Date:** March 30, 2025

---

## Summary

A deeply personal "Random Bullshit Feature" where Angry reflects on his identity as a Game Master, his love of games, and his growing sense that the RPG industry is drifting away from the kind of gaming he cares about. Part memoir, part manifesto.

## The Arena Adventure

Angry recounts one of his best sessions ever — "That Arena Adventure" — set in a magical gladiatorial arena (likely Eberron). A group of wizards built a fantasy MMA arena with a sphere of force, floating Overseer platform, and strict safety measures. A champion's widow hired the PCs to investigate three suspicious "accidental" deaths.

The PCs joined the arena, fought up the ranks, and discovered a shape-shifted pregnant succubus working with a cult to harvest champions' heart's blood for a demon super-soldier baby. She'd ethereal jaunt under the arena, possess victims mid-fight, and make them hesitate fatally.

The climax split the party: one PC was scheduled to fight the champ on murder night, the succubus possessed him, then possessed an Overseer to bring down the sphere of force, while the rest of the party fought demons below trying to reach her body. Multiple simultaneous crises, improvisation required, nearly failed. Still talked about 20 years later.

**Key lesson:** This kind of experience — carefully planned but with uncertain outcomes, where the players can win or lose by their own choices — is why Angry does this.

## For the Love of the Game

Angry traces his lifelong love of games: Atari, Nintendo, family board games, hex-and-chit wargames, inventing games for siblings and friends before ever finding D&D. Pool fighting games based on TMNT, spy games with flashlights, scavenger-hunt LARPs hidden around the neighborhood. He absorbed game design intuitively as a kid by imitating his favorite experiences.

Finding Mentzer's Basic D&D was the hook — it was essentially a three-part lesson in building games for your friends.

## I Choose Joy

**Joy** (defined correctly): a wonder that such things can be. Not happiness — you can feel joy alongside sadness, dread, or melancholy. As a GM, Angry wants to create joy — moments players will carry with them. The site is an extension: helping other GMs give their players joy.

> "I create joy, asshat. So fuck off."

## The Continental Drift of Roleplayingea

Angry perceives the RPG world splitting into two drifting continents:

- **East Roleplayingea:** Performance/cinematic play where the GM is a necessary evil, there only to give players a stage for their dramas. D&D and its imitators are heading here.
- **West Roleplayingea:** Old-school play where the GM is a hands-off referee building a world for players to explore without meddlesome quests. The OSR/indie scene.

And then there's Angry, in the middle:

> "Some people, you put a sword in their hand, they'll use it to plunder ancient ruins just because they can. But if you put a sword in my hand, I'm going to find a dragon to slay and a princess to save. I want quests. I want challenges to win. I want mighty monsters to slay. I want puzzles to solve. I want mysteries to unravel."

He wants to be Luke Skywalker, Indiana Jones, Aragorn, Link, Samus Aran, the Doom Slayer — not Conan the Murderhobo. He believes most gamers want this too.

**The difference between a game players can win and one they can't lose:** The grand art of game design is making something a player can win AND can lose, where the only difference is the player's choices and skills. That's agency.

## The Island Metaphor

Angry sits on an island with a bonfire, sensing the sea growing around him. Maybe he'll die alone screaming at a volleyball about Chrono Trigger. Maybe there are lots of boats out there that will see his fire. Maybe the island is bigger than he thinks. Maybe it's all just the depression talking.

**Core message:** There's a place between performance play and old-school sandbox for GMs who build carefully crafted adventures with real challenges, real stakes, and real joy — and Angry's keeping the fire lit for them.
