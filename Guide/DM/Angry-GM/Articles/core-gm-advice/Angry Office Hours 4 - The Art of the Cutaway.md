# Professor Angry's Office Hours: The Art of the Cutaway

**Source:** [The Angry GM](https://theangrygm.com/angry-office-hours-4-cutaway/)
**Date:** September 29, 2023
**Series:** True Game Mastery

---

## Overview

An exploration of the Art of the Cutaway — the GM technique of shifting the game's focus between characters, groups, scenes, and situations. Builds on the Action Queue and Action Clock concepts, answering a question about managing simultaneous social and non-social actions.

---

## The Core Insight: Everything Is a Cutaway

A Cutaway is the GM saying "And now, we're going to follow this action over here for a bit." You can only resolve one action at a time. Every time you Describe the Outcome and Invite the Next Principal Character to Act, you're already doing a Cutaway — pointing the camera somewhere.

> After every action, you can Cut Anywhere in Time or Space.

It doesn't matter if the next action is in the same room, a different scene, a different continent, or a different timeline.

---

## Levels of Cutaway Mastery

1. **Smooth-brained:** Strictly adhere to "Don't split the party." Games suffer.
2. **Little-brained:** Let parties split, but run each scene to completion before cutting.
3. **Big-brained:** Interweave multiple scenes, cutting back and forth as tasks play out.
4. **Galaxy-brained (Master of a Thousand Cuts):** Realize *everything is a Cutaway* and you can Cut anywhere, anytime.

---

## Practical Applications

### Split Party Scenes

Instead of playing an entire Shindig Scene then the entire Burglary Scene, interweave them action by action.

### Mixed Action Types

When some characters are in social conflict while others do non-social things (e.g., two distract an NPC while two search his office), Cut between them action by action — same as any split.

### Mid-Action Cuts

You can Cut in the *middle* of an action — this is what building an Action Queue is about:

> "Adam, declare your action! Okay, got it… but before I tell you how it turns out, let's cut to Beth and see what Beryllia is doing in the meanwhile."

### Complex Task Interweaving

Complex tasks (opening a chest, having a conversation) are strings of actions that can be broken up and interleaved with other characters' actions.

---

## On Spotlight Time

- Every player deserves roughly equal *opportunity* to participate, not exactly equal minutes.
- It's not a problem until a player says "I feel sidelined."
- **Every player must be able to wait quietly and attentively for at least 15 minutes.**
- Don't use Cutaways to obsessively equalize minutes. That's doing it wrong.

---

## The Antidote to "Don't Split the Party"

Games with split parties run by a Cutaway master are more dynamic, engaging, tense, and interesting. The old adage has left players afraid to:
- Split in town to tackle multiple tasks
- Scout ahead or blaze trails
- Come in two different doors to hit enemies from both sides

**Push your players to split up more.** Give them too much to do and not enough time.

---

## Practical Takeaways

1. **Everything is a Cutaway.** You're already doing it every time you move to the next character.
2. **You can Cut anywhere, anytime** — same room, different room, mid-action.
3. **Don't fear split parties.** Master the Cutaway and splits become a feature.
4. **Interweave scenes** rather than running them sequentially.
5. **Say "Meanwhile…"** — that's literally all it takes.
