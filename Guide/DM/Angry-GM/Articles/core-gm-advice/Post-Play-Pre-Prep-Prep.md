# Post-Play Pre-Prep Prep

**Source:** [The Angry GM](https://theangrygm.com/post-play-pre-prep-prep/)
**Date:** June 19, 2025
**Series:** Up Your GMing Level

---

## Fix Your Game Prep First

Before the main trick, Angry lays out the only game prep advice you'll ever need:

### Make It a Habit
- Schedule time to prep for every session and **stick to it**
- Figure out how much time you need (2 hours for a mid-adventure session, 4 hours for a new adventure's first session, 4–8 hours to write an adventure)
- Know whether you work better in long chunks or short shifts

### Follow a System
1. **Read** (don't skim) the summary of the adventure, then the section you're running next
2. **Note** any rules/mechanics to review, details to organize, resources needed (maps, tokens, stat blocks, NPC lists, flowcharts, timelines)
3. **Review** rules and flag them with sticky tabs or quick notes
4. **Build** your notes in chronological order — NPC lists, flowcharts, timelines
5. **Gather** bare minimum resources first (maps, stat blocks, tokens) before prettying anything up
6. **Enhance** with remaining time — prettify the biggest, most dramatic scenes first

Use every minute of your allotted time. Minimum first, enhancements after.

## The Actual Trick: Post-Game Pee Break

Block out **30 minutes** sometime between 12 and 24 hours after each game session. Three steps, ~10 minutes each:

### Step 1: Pee Out a Recap (~10 min)

Write a quick bulleted list of the session's major plot beats on an index card. Then add:
- Important details players learned
- Resources they gained access to
- Plans they made but haven't implemented yet
- Where the session ended and where the next one starts

Keep it to phrases and sentence fragments. If it doesn't fit on an index card, you overdid it.

### Step 2: Pee on Your World (~10 min)

Jot down any important details you established during the session and add them to your **campaign bible** — your compiled list of world details (nations, factions, locations, characters, events).

This applies whether you're running homebrew or published adventures. As soon as you start running anything, you establish your own details that need tracking.

### Step 3: Pee to Remember (~10 min)

This is the core trick. Most games are too static — they don't show players the impact of their small, incidental choices.

**The process:**
1. Take two index cards/sticky notes — label one **Now**, the other **Later**
2. Think through the last session and pick **two things** you want the game to *remember* — small, personal player choices or incidental events
3. The **Now** card goes into your prep notes for the next session — figure out one small change or element to add as a result
4. The **Later** card goes into your campaign bible — make a more permanent change to the world

**Examples of things to remember:**
- A character donating treasure to a temple
- Letting a captured goblin go free
- Prisoners they didn't save in time
- A day spent tending to the sick
- An item left behind

**Examples of consequences:**
- The temple priest makes repairs or displays a donated item
- The freed goblin returns to help later
- A grieving father takes to drinking
- A new shop opens after bandits are cleared out
- A child brings a character a honeycake as thanks

**Key principles:**
- It doesn't matter what you do with these details
- Smaller and more personal = better
- Connect consequences naturally through gameplay, not exposition
- If players make the connection → they feel their actions matter
- If they don't → they just think you run a dynamic, living world
- This is a **no-lose proposition**
