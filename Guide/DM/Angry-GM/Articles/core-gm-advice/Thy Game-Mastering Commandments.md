# Thy Game-Mastering Commandments

**Source:** [The Angry GM](https://theangrygm.com/thy-game-mastering-commandments/)
**Date:** January 20, 2014
**Category:** How to F$&%ing GM

---

## Summary

Every GM should write a personal Game Mastering Credo — a statement of core beliefs, principles, and ideals about how they run games. It's a numbered list of personal rules you strive to always follow. The Credo serves three purposes: self-examination (through writing it), self-improvement (through reviewing it), and communication (through sharing it with players).

## What Is a GM Credo?

A personal statement of your core GMing beliefs. Think of it as your Commandments:

- "Thou shalt always warn thy players of risks their characters take in ignorance."
- "Never shalt thou fudge dice."

These are PERSONAL rules — the things you personally think define you as a GM.

## Why It's Useful

### Writing = Self-Examination
The act of writing forces you to examine what you do and why. You'll naturally question things: "Do I really believe that? Where did I pick that habit up?"

### Reviewing = Self-Improvement
Review before and after sessions every few weeks. When you notice a broken rule:
- **One-time lapse:** Forgive it, move on.
- **Frequent violation:** Ask whether you still believe in the rule. If not, update the Credo. If yes, figure out what you're doing wrong.

### Sharing = Communication
The Credo tells players what they can expect and what lines you won't cross. If a player can't live with those rules, better to know up front.

## Rules for Writing a Good Credo

1. **A list of rules on a single sheet of paper.** If it takes more than a short statement, it's too complicated.
2. **System neutral.** Nothing specific to one game system.
3. **Rules for you, not your players.** Never address players directly. Think about what YOU can do to enable desired behavior.

## Angry's 23 Rules

1. **I Am a Dungeon Master** — Whatever game I'm running, I am a Dungeon Master.
2. **I Own the Game** — I get everyone together, make it happen, keep it flowing, and am responsible for it. Whatever happens is on me.
3. **Everyone Who Agrees to Abide by My Rules is Welcome at My Table** — My table will always be safe and comfortable for anyone who follows my credo.
4. **Any Offense Deserves an Apology** — If someone claims offense, they deserve a sincere apology first. Then the offense can be discussed.
5. **I Am an "At Will" Employer** — Players are welcome to leave at any time. No grudges. Always welcome to return.
6. **I Can't Run a Game Without Trust** — I trust my players. I assume all offenses are unintentional. If I can't trust a player, I can't run for them.
7. **I Will Always Be Fair and Consistent** — Rules and world applied consistently. "It is magic" is never sufficient reason alone.
8. **The Rules Are a Tool and They Belong to Me** — Rules resolve actions. They should never obstacle free will. A player shall never be disadvantaged for not knowing rules.
9. **The World is My Character** — I create and control the world. Nothing becomes part of it without my say so.
10. **I Am Not on the Players' Sides, But I Am Not Their Enemy Either** — Fair challenges. No unearned victories. No unavoidable defeats. No fudging dice.
11. **I Will Not Take a Character's Freedom Away Unless the Player Agrees** — Characters are free to act within the agreed-upon game. Mind control used sparingly.
12. **I Take My Game Seriously and I Will Let My Players Do The Same** — I can veto things that ruin suspension of disbelief. Players can ask me to remove things that upset them.
13. **Adventurers Lead Exciting Lives** — Never completely safe. New problems always arise. All victories and defeats are temporary.
14. **Nothing Can Be Solved Through Inaction** — If players refuse to act, things get worse.
15. **The Players and The Characters Are Reflections Through a Clouded Mirror** — Not direct reflections, but reflections at heart. If players are standing around talking, so are characters.
16. **I Will Not Create Challenges That Can Be Broken By Metagaming** — If player knowledge ruins a challenge, I designed a bad challenge.
17. **If There is Any Ambiguity At All, A Player Can Veto a Character Death** — Secret veto if survival is ambiguous. No veto if death is certain within the rules.
18. **Players Should Know All The Options Their Characters Have** — I will tell players about options unambiguously.
19. **I Will Never Allow a Character to Ignorantly Take a Substantial Risk** — I will inform the player of risks the character would be aware of.
20. **I Will Never Allow a Character to Ignorantly Attempt the Impossible** — I won't let players waste resources on impossible actions the character would know are impossible.
21. **I Will Never Require A Player to "Count Squares"** — No penalties for player ignorance of rules or tactical minutiae. I assume the safest, most reasonable course.
22. **I Will Always Allow the Characters a Break** — Players can always create a situation to talk things out, though it may cost time or resources.
23. **I Will Strive Never to Require Players to Ask What Their Characters Know** — I give relevant information the moment it becomes relevant.

## Process

1. **Write** your Credo — examine what you do and why
2. Let it sit for a day or two, then reread and finalize
3. Make a **player version** — expand each rule with a few sentences of explanation
4. **Review** before and after sessions every few weeks
5. **Update** as your beliefs evolve — but don't change it lightly
