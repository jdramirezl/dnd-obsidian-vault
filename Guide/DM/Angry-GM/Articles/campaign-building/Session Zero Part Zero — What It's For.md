# Session Zero Part Zero: What It's For

**Source:** [The Angry GM – The Session Zero Blitz (Part Zero)](https://theangrygm.com/session-zero-part-zero/)
**Date:** August 28, 2017
**Series:** Campaign Building / Session Zero (Part 1 of 3)

---

## What Session Zero Actually Is

A pre-campaign meeting where the GM gathers information needed to develop a **campaign premise** the players will want to say yes to. It fits AFTER deciding to start a campaign but BEFORE having a premise.

> Session Zero is an information-gathering tool **you** use. You listen to the players. Then **you** build the game. It is NOT a collaborative committee exercise.

## The Right Mindset

- Everything you bring is optional — your ideas, your players, even the rule system
- Write down your ideas beforehand and decide how important each one is
- Decide how important this specific group of players is to you
- If your magnum opus doesn't work with these players: compromise, dump the ideas, or dump the players — **you** choose
- If you don't walk in accepting everything is optional, don't bother

## The Eight Decisions

Session Zero helps you make (or deliberately defer) these. Most are decided during/after Session Zero based on listening — not discussed as a group checklist.

### Decision 1: Scheduling ⚠️ (Cannot defer)

How often are you running games? Must be explicitly discussed.

When frequency drops below ~3 times/month:
- Players forget the story between sessions
- Picking up mid-adventure becomes difficult
- Game time becomes extremely valuable — every minute must feel productive
- Banter, exposition, world-building take a back seat
- Likely limited to **adventure-of-the-week / plate of meatballs** campaigns

### Decision 2: Commitment and Reliability ⚠️ (Cannot defer)

Even with frequent scheduling, unreliable attendance creates the same problems plus missed story chunks.

- Ask everyone to be honest about actual attendance
- Attendance problems are only problems if you don't know they're coming
- Build the game around real commitment, not ideal commitment

### Decision 3: Lifespan (Optional)

Options: specific time span, experience level cap (e.g., end at level 10), specific goal as endpoint, or endless/open-ended.

> Not setting an ending is fine, but it should be a **deliberate choice**, not a default.

### Decision 4: Rules System (Usually pre-decided)

- Choose the system designed for the game you want to run
- Choose a system you're intimately familiar with
- These two factors are often in tension — balance them
- **Maintain veto power** — it matters more to the GM than to players
- If learning a new system: run something short first (3 months)

### Decision 5: Genre and Tone (Usually implicit)

- Genre usually implied by system + setting + premise
- Tone should be chosen more deliberately
- Have a benchmark in your head for enforcement during play

### Decision 6: Setting (Variable)

| Type | Description | Work Required |
|------|-------------|---------------|
| **Published** | Waterdeep, Forgotten Realms, etc. | Low |
| **Non-setting** | Generic world from core rules; invent a starting town | Low |
| **Homebrew** | Your own world | High |

The real point: provide enough information for players to generate characters that fit.

### Decision 7: Options and Restrictions

Two key factors: **Complexity** (every option you allow is one you must understand) and **Tone** (every option affects the world's genre feel).

> **Always opt-in, never opt-out.** Choose specific supplements that work with your game. Don't say "everything except X" — no RPG was meant to run with all mods installed.

### Decision 8: Shape and Glue

How adventures fit together (shape) and what keeps the party together (glue) are central to the premise. These are the culmination of the Session Zero process, affected by every other decision.

## The Secret Thing

In addition to the eight decisions, you also need to **secretly evaluate your players** to understand what kind of game they actually want to play — covered in Part Preface (Player Engagements / MDA).
