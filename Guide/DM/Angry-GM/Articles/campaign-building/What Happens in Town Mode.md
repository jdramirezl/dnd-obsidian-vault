# What Happens in Town Mode

**Source:** [The Angry GM](https://theangrygm.com/what-happens-in-town-mode/)
**Date:** July 13, 2022
**Series:** Town Mode (Lesson 2)
**Tags:** #angry-gm #town-mode #campaign-structure #pacing #campaign-building

---

## Core Thesis

Town Mode is NOT a waste of time. For every 2–3 sessions of adventure, spend at least half a session (ideally a full session) in Town Mode. The tangible outcome is just updated character sheets, but the *real* benefits are structural.

## The Four Pillars Town Mode Enhances

### 1. Empowerment
Players and characters get more effective over time:
- **Avatar Strength** — levels, gear, abilities (character sheet stuff)
- **Player Skill** — understanding abilities, understanding the world, teamwork, better plans

Town Mode ties mechanical development to narrative development and the wider game experience.

### 2. Gamefeel
The satisfying *gameplay* experience (as opposed to narrative experience). Town Mode enhances this by making between-adventure activities part of the actual game rather than homework.

### 3. Pacing
Not about passage of time — it's about how speed and tension affect the play experience. Always-tense games wear players out. Always-slow games bore them. **Town Mode is the brake section on the roller coaster** — it keeps the train on track and prevents G-force death.

### 4. Worldsense
The illusion that the RPG isn't just a game but a story in a fully developed world. Without Worldsense, a TTRPG is just an abstract pile of challenges. Worldsense is where emotional engagement comes from.

## The Nine Things Town Mode Delivers

### 1. Development
Character growth — both mechanical (levels, equipment) and narrative (personality, behavior). **Stop automatic level-ups.** Never let characters gain levels mid-adventure.

### 2. Information
Chances to learn about the world. Includes both useful intel AND "merely interesting" details that build Worldsense. Useless information proves a world exists beyond the adventure.

### 3. Opportunities
Adventure hooks, quests, sidequests. Best when players feel they *discovered* them and *can refuse*. Enhances Gamefeel and Worldsense.

### 4. Resources
Bankable useful things for later: contacts, access to locations/services, favors owed. Require strategic thinking to use — remember they exist, recognize when useful, tap appropriately.

### 5. Interaction
Interacting with NPCs and the world *outside* the current adventure context. Reveals a world beyond the game.

### 6. Agency
Players choose what to pursue without the constraints of an active adventure. **Never tell players how long they have in Town Mode.** Don't hand out "Downtime Hours." That provides fake Agency at the expense of real Agency and Worldsense.

### 7. Safety
Town Mode should feel reasonably safe almost all the time. Lets players relax, experiment, make mistakes, spend resources on things without immediate guaranteed payoff. GMs who never let up on danger are wrecking their games.

### 8. Respite
The game really IS kind of on pause. Adventure Mode is exhausting. Town Mode lets players recover energy *together, at the table, in the world* — which the void between sessions can't do.

### 9. Context
Passive exposure to the world beyond the game. Flavor text showing the lives of people their actions affect. Vital to Worldsense. Can't get this from a map and a list of downtime actions.

## When to Skip Town Mode

1. **You don't game often/long enough** — less than one 4-hour session equivalent per month
2. **Short campaign** — six sessions or fewer (try to fit it in around nine sessions)
3. **Your campaign already provides all these benefits** — expert-level assessment only

**"My players don't like Town Mode" is NOT a reason to skip it** — it's a reason to minimize it (half a session). Town Mode does things players are too dumb to know they need.
