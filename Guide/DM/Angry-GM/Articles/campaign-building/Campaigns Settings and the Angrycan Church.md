# Campaigns, Settings, and the Angrycan Church

**Source:** [The Angry GM](https://theangrygm.com/campaigns-settings-and-the-angrycan-church/)
**Date:** March 15, 2017
**Series:** Start Your Own Campaign

---

## Core Definitions

The RPG sphere lacks rigorous, shared definitions for key terms. This article establishes foundational terminology for campaign and world-building discussions.

## Campaign

A **campaign** is any ongoing game with any sort of continuity at all — a series of game sessions and adventures with continuity of players, characters, and story.

- **Continuity** exists when you can describe a sequence of narrative events forming a connection between game elements. If you can tell a story about how you got from there to here, there's continuity.
- A campaign is NOT synonymous with a setting. The Forgotten Realms is a SETTING, not a campaign.
- Campaigns can exist without overarching stories — many are "accidental campaigns" where the same players keep showing up with the same characters for unrelated adventures.
- The definition is intentionally bare-bones: mostly the same players, mostly the same characters, mostly the same world.

## Adventure Path

An **adventure path** is a sequence of adventures united by an overarching story — a series of adventures whose motivations are all defined by a single, common resolution.

Key distinctions from campaigns:
- A campaign has **continuity** (a continuous sequence of events). An adventure path has **story structure** (beginning, middle, ending, incitement, motivation, climax, resolution).
- The adventure path's **goal** becomes the **motivation** for all (or most) adventures inside it. Example: everything in Lord of the Rings was driven by the goal to defeat Sauron.
- Adventure paths can borrow narrative elements from their component adventures — they don't need their own separate inciting incident, climax, etc.
- Once you understand how to build one adventure path, you can string together as many as you want. The adventure path is the **largest meaningful structure** in RPG design.

## Setting

A **setting** is the fictional world in which the campaign takes place. Critical insight:

- **Setting design is NOT campaign design.** They are separate disciplines with different issues.
- Settings relate to campaigns the same way any narrative element relates to any other — no more, no less.
- You can't run adventures without a setting (there's always a world), but you CAN run adventures without a campaign.
- You can design settings without campaigns, campaigns without settings, characters without adventures, and adventures without characters.

## Practical Takeaway

When building games, keep these three concepts cleanly separated in your mind. Campaign-building and world-building are different skills that inform each other but are not inextricably tied together.
