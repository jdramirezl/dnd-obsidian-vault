# Mapping a Region — Simple Homebrew Campaign

**Source:** [The Angry GM — Mapping a Region](https://theangrygm.com/simple-campaign-region/)
**Date:** May 4, 2022
**Series:** Let's Start a Simple Homebrew Campaign

---

## Overview

How to create the Starter Region map. Covers the two types of regional maps (GM's and Players'), terrain systems, Points of Interest, hex scale, and a step-by-step design process.

---

## A Tale of Two Maps

If your game involves wilderness travel, you need a Regional Map. It must exist as **two separate maps.**

### The GM's Regional Map
- A **game mechanic**, not a pretty picture
- Hex-based tilemap — precise, mechanical, used for adjudicating travel
- Every hex marked with a terrain symbol
- Used at the table for plotting movement and resolving actions

### The Players' Regional Map
- A **visual aid** — as pretty as you can make it
- Vague and imprecise by design (the geographical equivalent of rolling a d20)
- Represents the characters' knowledge of the world
- Used for making plans and choices

> The GM's Regional Map is the world's stat block. The Players' Regional Map is the flavor text.

**You cannot use one map with the grid toggled on/off.** They must be separate.

---

## Map Layers

### The Terrain Layer (Background)

Every hex gets a terrain symbol. Terrain determines visibility, movement speed, navigation difficulty, hazards, monsters, and general challenge level.

#### Terrain Types (10)

| Terrain | Description |
|---|---|
| **Arctic** | Tundra, glacier, icefields, cold deserts |
| **Desert** | Sandy deserts, rocky deserts, salt flats |
| **Forest** | Temperate, rainforest, jungle, boreal, montane |
| **Grassland** | Plains, steppe, savannah, moorland, farmland |
| **Hill** | Rocky hills, mesas, canyons |
| **Mountain** | Difficult or impossible to navigate |
| **Swamp** | Marshes, bogs, salt marshes, forested swampland |
| **River/Lake** | Freshwater bodies |
| **Ocean** | Impassable without special tools |
| **Road** | Runs through other terrain, eases navigation |

**Name every landform.** Every contiguous mass of terrain needs an in-world name for supplemental notes, encounter tables, and special rules.

### The POI Layer (Foreground)

Points of Interest — castles, cities, towns, dungeons, ruins, shrines, caves, etc.

- **Mostly blank at campaign start.** Add POIs only when needed.
- Starting map: Starter Town, first adventure site, maybe 1–2 other dots.

---

## Hexes and Scale

- **Use hex maps.** Best polygon for overland grids.
- **Scale: 6 miles per hex** (3-mile radius = line of sight in open terrain)
- One hex = 2–3 hours of travel

### Rules of Thumb

| Situation | Estimate |
|---|---|
| Normal party | 4 hexes/day |
| Slow party | 3 hexes/day |
| Terrain-slowed | Subtract 1–2 hexes |
| Site within 2 hexes | One-day adventure |
| Every 2 extra hexes | +1 day |
| Large site | +1 extra day |

## How to Draw a Map

1. **Sketch** — Lightly sketch terrain (lines for coasts/mountains/rivers, blobs for forests/hills/swamps)
2. **Hex** — Draw hex map from sketch = GM's Regional Map
3. **Art** — Use hex map as guide for fuzzy Players' Regional Map

**Tools:** Hex paper or [Autarch templates](https://www3.autarch.co/?wpdmpro=region-hex-maps) for analog. [Worldographer](https://worldographer.com/) for GM's map, [Inkarnate](https://inkarnate.com/) or [Wonderdraft](https://www.wonderdraft.net/) for Players' map.

**Map Size:** 24 × 16 hexes (144 × 96 miles). Fits on 8½ × 11 paper.

---

## How to Design a Map (5 Steps)

1. **List Your Truths** — Review adventure/town notes. List what must appear on the map.
2. **Locate Your Town** — Dot in the middle-ish area (within 2 inches of center).
3. **Locate First Adventure Site** — Dot near Town (~1 inch ≈ 10–12 miles / 2 hexes).
4. **Draw a Cool Map Around the Dots** — Sketch terrain features, include all known truths.
5. **Name Everything** — Every river, sea, mountain range, forest, and terrain area.

---

## Design Tips

1. **Start with 1–2 borders** — Mountain ranges or ocean shores along map edges.
2. **Add a major river** — Curvy line across the middle. Rivers start near mountains, end at oceans, meander more downstream.
3. **Pick a predominant terrain** — Grassland (easiest/default), forest, or desert. Avoid mostly-swamp/hills/mountains.
4. **3–5 other terrain areas** — Sketch blobby shapes. Repeats fine.
5. **Keep 2/3 to 3/4 open** — Don't clutter. Most should be predominant terrain.
6. **Add 1–2 other settlements** — Outside the middle, one size bigger or smaller than Starter Town.
7. **Don't sweat the science** — Ignore wind patterns, climate, erosion, plate tectonics.
8. **Don't abandon common sense** — Rivers don't split, water flows downhill, deserts/swamps need buffers, mountains have foothills.
9. **Relax** — Lowest-stakes part of running a campaign. Enjoy it.
