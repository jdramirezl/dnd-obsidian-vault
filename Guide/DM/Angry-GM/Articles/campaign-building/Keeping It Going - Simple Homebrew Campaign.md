# Keeping It Going — Simple Homebrew Campaign

**Source:** [The Angry GM — Keeping It Going](https://theangrygm.com/simple-campaign-keeping-it-going/)
**Date:** June 1, 2022
**Series:** Let's Start a Simple Homebrew Campaign (Final Entry)

---

## Overview

The final article in the Simple Homebrew Campaign series. Covers how to maintain a campaign session after session through interlocking cycles: the Session Circle, the Adventure Spiral, and the Wheel of Prep.

---

## The Session Circle

Every session should follow a consistent structure. This is a metastructure imposed by the real world — sessions don't exist in the game world.

### Session Structure

1. **Bang in the Session** — Signal the start with a consistent ritual.
2. **Discuss Group Business** — Scheduling, rules clarifications, conflict resolutions, homework checks. If nothing, say *something* about the game or group anyway.
3. **Recap the Story So Far** — The GM does this, not the players. A proper recap covers:
   - Current goals, motivations, and relevant information
   - Major plot-relevant events from prior sessions
   - Brief summary of the previous session (include emotional highs/lows even if not plot-relevant)
   - Where the characters are now and what they planned to do next
   - Ask players to confirm accuracy
4. **Set the Scene and Start Gaming** — Describe the opening scene and invite action.
5. **Wind Down with Plenty of Time Left**
   - **Never end late.** Not even one second. Ending early is fine.
   - Stop gameplay 15–30 minutes before scheduled end time.
   - Never end mid-scene. End after a scene wraps or just before one starts.
   - Never interrupt players while planning.
   - **Always end when the party Returns to Town** so the next session starts in Town. Drop road encounters if needed.
   - You must know the party's next move before they leave.
6. **Grade the Players** — Recap victories and defeats out loud. Award XP every session. Even without XP, recap wins and losses.
7. **Preview the Next Session's Start** — Tell the party where/how the next session begins and their plan.
8. **Write the Recap** — After the session (ideally next day, never immediately), write the recap for next session.

## The Adventure Spiral

The campaign's narrative structure is a spiral, not a flat loop. Each cycle is a little different.

### The Core Gameplay Loop

1. Accept a quest in Town
2. Complete the quest at the adventure site
3. Return to Town — rewards, loot, restock, upgrade, level up, interact
4. Find a new adventure

This syncs narrative structure (inciting incident → rising action → climax → denouement) with gameplay structure (goal → minor conflicts → major conflict → resolution → downtime).

### Session Types

- **Adventure Sessions** — 2–3 sessions of adventure content
- **Town Sessions** — ½ to 1 session of downtime, business, adventure setup
- Town Sessions must start in Town. Adventure Sessions can't Return to Town mid-session.

### Moving On

After 2–3 adventures from one Town, push the party to a new Town:
- One-session travel adventure (escort a caravan, deliver an item)
- NPC friends move on, shopkeepers can't afford treasures, trainers recommend masters elsewhere
- Expect stacked Town sessions as they settle into the new location
- Requires Building a New Town and possibly Mapping a New Region

## The Wheel of Prep

You're always prepping. The level changes based on where you are in the cycle.

| Level | When | What |
|---|---|---|
| **World Prep** | Party about to Move On | Build New Towns, Map New Regions, Move On Hooks. Before Return to Town. |
| **Adventure Prep** | Party close to finishing adventure | Premises, Summaries, Motivations, Hooks, Act Structures. Before Return to Town. |
| **Session Prep** | Between every session | Encounters, NPCs, dungeon maps, moment-to-moment gameplay. Always required. |
| **Tweak Prep** | Already have content for next session | Review recap, check prep vs. party direction, incorporate choices, add ideas cautiously. |

### The Importance of Town

Town keeps all cycles in sync. Always start Town Sessions in Town, never return mid-session.

### Tweak Prep Danger Rule

> Past the midway point of an adventure's second session (or a single Town session): **introduce nothing new.** No new plot threads, sidequests, or characters. Use what you planned. If out of content, wrap up.

## Getting Good

- GMing is an art learned through practice, not study
- Commit to at least **12 sessions** running your Simple Homebrew Campaign as laid out
- No quitting, no experimenting, no seeking other advice during that stretch
- The only difference between novice and expert is years of practice
