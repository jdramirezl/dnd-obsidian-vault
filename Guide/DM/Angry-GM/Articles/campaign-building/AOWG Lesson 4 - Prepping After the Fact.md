# AOWG Lesson 4: Prepping After the Fact

## Key Advice

The best time to prep is **after** the session, not before. The game world doesn't exist until it appears at the table — everything else is just plans and hopes. Minimize prep time ruthlessly; prep is a necessary evil, not the fun part.

## Summary

The final lesson in the Angry Open-World Game series lays out a concrete 5-step post-session prep process designed to keep an open-world campaign running with minimal effort. The core philosophy: nothing is real until it happens at the table, so you prep based on what *did* happen, not what you *hope* will happen. The article provides a detailed walkthrough of each step — from writing terse recaps to maintaining a campaign bible to building the next session's Player Do List — and ends with a brutal system for limiting prep scope by forcing yourself to pick only the most essential item and then halving the time you think you need.

## Actionable Steps

1. **Write a Point-by-Point Recap** — Immediately after the session (within 24 hours), write a terse, bulleted timeline of what happened. Include location, time, events, outcomes, and information learned. No prose, no drama.
2. **Update the Campaign Bible** — Highlight NPCs, places, things, and ideas from the recap. Check each against your bible (notebook, wiki, OneNote — whatever). Add new entries; update existing ones with newly revealed info.
3. **Write the Player Do List** — List everything the party is currently doing or intends to do next. Base it on what players told you at session's end plus your best guesses. If the list is thin, you have a problem to fix.
4. **Expand the List / Write a Session Script** — Assess whether the Player Do List fills a session. It won't. Add follow-on goals, side encounters, runarounds, or smoke-on-the-horizon elements to flesh it out.
5. **Prep Only What You Absolutely Need** — From the expanded list, identify resources you need (maps, encounter keys, NPC details). Then ask: "If I could only prep ONE thing, what would it be?" Prep that — in half the time you think it needs. Repeat only if time remains.

> [!tip] The Prep Limiter
> Set hard boundaries on prep. Pick only 1 thing from your "need" list. Give yourself half the time you estimated. If you run out of time, wing it. GMs who don't limit prep will eventually spend 32 hours prepping a 4-hour session. The game world will survive your improvisation — it always does.
