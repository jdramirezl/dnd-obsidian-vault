# Resistance is Futile — Assimilating Your NPCs Into Factions

**Source:** [The Angry GM](https://theangrygm.com/resistance-is-futile/)
**Date:** December 9, 2016
**Series:** Give Non Players Character

---

## Overview

Factions are groups of NPCs banding together for common goals. They don't need complex stat blocks or reputation systems — a Faction is essentially a single NPC with multiple bodies. Treat them like NPCs: give them a goal, a game purpose, and a Face (first point of contact). Everything else grows organically from screen time.

## Why Factions Exist

### In-Game Reasons
- Humans are tribal — banding together is a basic part of the human experience
- Collectives accomplish things individuals can't
- Membership always has a cost/benefit trade-off (dues, rules, codes of conduct in exchange for support, resources, political power)

### Game Reasons (Same as NPCs, Plus More)
- **Story building tool:** If PCs murder a guild member, the guild provides instant collective opposition. If PCs befriend a sage, the sage's fraternal order provides access to more knowledge
- **Shorthand for players:** A faction uniform or badge instantly communicates an NPC's motivations and goals without the GM re-establishing them every time — "ditto"

## Fictional Faction Types

All factions reduce to four base types:

| Type | Purpose | Examples |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Professional Guild** | Protect business interests, provide licensing, political clout, quality assurance | Blacksmith Guild, Grocer's Guild |
| **Company** | Pool resources/skills for a specific goal or project | Mercenary company, ship crew, church building project |
| **Social Club** | Recreation, social interaction, exclusive venue | Gentleman's club, tavern regulars |
| **Educational Organization** | Training, education, academic pursuit | Arcane college, mercenary academy, monastery |

- **Criminal Guilds** are a subset of Professional Guilds (provide protection from law, fencing, alibis instead of political clout)
- **Religious Organizations** can be any of the four types depending on their actual purpose (community church = Social Club, crusading order = Company, monastery = Educational)

## Game-Purpose Faction Types

| Type | What It Does | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Resource** | Provides information or resources to PCs | Alchemists Guild, Healing Church, Smugglers Guild |
| **Patron** | Provides quests and motivations | Adventurers Guild, knightly order, church hierarchy |
| **Background** | Makes the world real and interactable; serves as NPC shorthand | Any guild whose emblem an NPC wears |
| **Antagonist** | Provides opposition | Evil cult, rival mercenary company |

## Building a Faction

A Faction needs only two things to appear in your game:

1. **A goal** — why does it exist? What does the collective want?
2. **A game purpose** — resource, patron, background, or antagonist?

That's it for the first appearance. As the Faction gets more screen time, add:
- **Backstory** — history of the organization
- **Relationships** with other Factions
- **Members** — individual NPCs with their own goals, hopes, fears

## The Face: The Most Important Member

Every Faction needs a **Face** — the first NPC the players interact with from that Faction.

### Rules for the Face
- The Face must be an **embodiment** of the Faction — utterly loyal, sharing its goals completely
- Players will define the entire Faction by the first member they meet
- The Face and the Faction must be unified — the Face IS the Faction to the players
- The Face doesn't have to be the leader — can be any rank. Just must wear the uniform and carry the banner with pride

### Can the Face Turn Traitor?
You **can** but **shouldn't** without careful preparation:
- The Face exists to garner sympathy for the Faction. Face and Faction are the same thing in the players' minds
- If the Face betrays the Faction, players must suddenly separate two things that were inseparable — unpredictable results
- **If you do it:** Have another NPC already established with a relationship to the players ready to step up as the new Face
- The new Face either reaffirms loyalty to the Faction (Face was wrong) or is antagonistic enough to show the Faction turned against the Face (Faction was wrong)

## Can Players Join Factions?

It doesn't change much mechanically:
- Members get resources or jobs from the Faction → same as non-members interacting with it
- Resources are never free — either pay directly or pay through membership
- Patrons offer jobs whether you're an employee or independent contractor
- The pay and the job are the same either way

## Faction Relationships

- Factions have relationships with the party (positive/negative) — no complex reputation system needed
- Just note the relationship and make decisions based on it, same as players do with NPCs
- After PCs interact with a Faction member, decide whether that interaction carries over to the whole Faction
- A Faction is a **vector** by which consequences spread from NPC to NPC

## Key Takeaway

Don't overcomplicate Factions. They are NPCs with extra bodies. Goal + game purpose + a Face = done. Everything else grows from play.
