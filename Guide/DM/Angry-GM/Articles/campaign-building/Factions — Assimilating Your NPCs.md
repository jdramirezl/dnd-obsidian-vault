# Factions — Assimilating Your NPCs

**Source:** [The Angry GM – Resistance is Futile: Assimilating Your NPCs (Into Factions)](https://theangrygm.com/resistance-is-futile/)
**Date:** December 9, 2016
**Series:** Campaign Building / NPCs and Factions

---

## Core Insight: A Faction Is an NPC

A Faction is essentially a single NPC with multiple bodies — a hive mind. It has a goal, a personality, relationships, and a backstory, just like any NPC. You don't need complicated stats, reputation systems, or special rules. Play Factions the same way you play NPCs.

## Why Factions Exist

### In-Game Reasons

Humans are tribal. They band together for evolutionary advantage and to accomplish things impossible for individuals. Membership always has a cost-benefit trade-off: dues, rules, or service in exchange for support, resources, or political power.

### Game-Purpose Reasons

Factions serve the same purposes as NPCs but with extras:

1. **Story-building tool** — Factions create teams of NPCs. Murder a guild member? The whole guild responds. Befriend a sage? Access the whole fraternal order.
2. **Shorthand for players** — A faction uniform or badge instantly communicates an NPC's motivations and goals without the GM re-establishing them every time.

## Fictional Faction Types

All factions reduce to four base types:

| Type | Purpose | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Professional Guild** | Protect business interests, provide licensing, political clout, quality assurance | Blacksmith Guild, Grocer's Guild |
| **Company** | Pool resources/skills for a specific goal or project | Mercenary company, ship crew, church building project |
| **Social Club** | Recreation and social interaction in an exclusive venue | Gentleman's club, tavern regulars, hobby group |
| **Educational Organization** | Training, education, academic pursuit | Arcane college, mercenary academy, monastery |

**Criminal Guilds** = subset of Professional Guilds (provide alibis, safe houses, black market access).
**Religious Organizations** = can be any of the four types depending on their actual function (community church = Social Club; crusading order = Company; monastery = Educational).

## Mechanical Faction Types (Game Purpose)

| Type | Function |
|---|---|
| **Resource Faction** | Provides information, goods, or services to the PCs (Alchemists Guild, Healing Church) |
| **Patron Faction** | Provides quests and motivations (employer, quest-giver organization) |
| **Background Faction** | Makes the world real and interactable; serves as NPC shorthand |
| **Antagonist Faction** | Provides opposition — campaign villain, rival, or obstacle |

## Can Players Join Factions?

**It doesn't matter much mechanically.** Whether PCs are members or outsiders, they still get resources from Resource Factions and jobs from Patron Factions. The pay and the job are the same whether they're employees or independent contractors.

## What's in a Faction

Build Factions exactly like NPCs — only as much detail as screen time demands:

1. **Goal** (required) — Why does it exist? What does the collective want?
2. **Game purpose** (required) — Resource, Patron, Background, or Antagonist?
3. **Backstory** — Add as needed when the faction gets more screen time
4. **Relationships with other Factions** — Rivalries, alliances, tensions
5. **Members** — Individual NPCs with their own goals, hopes, fears

A faction appearing once needs only a goal and game purpose. The more screen time it gets, the more details you layer on.

## The Face: The Most Important Member

Every Faction needs a **Face** — the first NPC the players interact with from that organization. The Face is critical:

- **Must be a pure expression of the Faction** — utterly loyal, shares its goals completely
- **Defines the Faction for the players** — whatever the Face says and does, that IS the Faction in the players' minds
- **Does NOT have to be the leader** — can be any rank, as long as they wear the uniform with pride
- **Must exist even if players already know about the Faction** — the first in-person contact must be the Face

### Why the Face Must Align with the Faction

Players define the Faction by their first contact. The Face exists to garner sympathy (or antipathy) for the Faction. If the Face and Faction diverge, you short-circuit emotional engagement — players can't separate the two because they were the same thing in their minds.

### Can the Face Turn Traitor?

You **can** but **shouldn't** without preparation:

- A Faction must ALWAYS have a Face
- If the current Face will betray the Faction, another NPC must already exist with an established relationship with the players, ready to step up as the new Face
- The new Face either reaffirms loyalty to the Faction (players turn against the traitor) or is antagonistic (players realize the Faction turned against the Face, not vice versa)

## Factional Relationships

- Factions have collective relationships with the party (positive or negative)
- **No complex reputation systems needed** — just note positive/negative relationships and make decisions based on them
- Social interactions with faction members should consider the NPC's relationship with their own Faction (loyal member of a faction that likes the PCs = bonus; action that would get the NPC in trouble with the faction = obstacle)
- After interacting with a member, decide whether the interaction affects the party's relationship with the whole Faction (roughing up a nobody vs. insulting the leader)

**A Faction is a vector by which consequences spread from NPC to NPC.**

## Quick-Build Checklist

- [ ] What is the Faction's goal?
- [ ] What is its game purpose? (Resource / Patron / Background / Antagonist)
- [ ] Who is the Face? (First NPC the players will meet)
- [ ] Does the Face purely represent the Faction's goals and values?
- [ ] What fictional type is it? (Guild / Company / Club / Educational)
