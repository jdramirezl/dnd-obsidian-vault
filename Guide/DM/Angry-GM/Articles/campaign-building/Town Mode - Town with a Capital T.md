## Core Concept: town vs Town

- **town** (lowercase) = a dot on the map, a middle-sized settlement in the fantasy world
- **Town** (capital T) = a *concept*, a mode of play — when heroes are between adventures (buying supplies, doing research, training, hunting adventure hooks), they are "in Town" regardless of whether they're in a village, city, or outpost

When the next adventure starts, heroes *leave* Town. When it's done, they *return* to Town.

## Modes of Play

RPG campaigns comprise several distinct *kinds* of gameplay. Each feels like its own game within the bigger game:
- **Combat Mode** — sequential turn order, precise timekeeping (rounds/seconds)
- **Wilderness Mode** — timekeeping in hours/days, resource management matters
- **Dungeon Mode** — room-to-room exploration, light tracking, searching
- **Town Mode** — between-adventure activities, vague timekeeping, broad actions

### Hard vs Soft Modes
- **Hard modes** = rules are different, certain things forbidden, strict transitions (like M:tG phases)
- **Soft modes** = easy transitions, malleable, suggest rather than restrict (like Starcraft phases)
- **TTRPGs should always use soft modes** — everything's possible everywhere, anytime

### Explicit vs Implicit Modes
- **Explicit** = mechanics clearly signal what mode you're in (Combat Mode with initiative rolls and battle maps)
- **Implicit** = players feel the difference but mechanics don't announce it
- **Town Mode works best as soft and implicit**

## The Heisenberg UncertainTown

Modern RPGs have infected players with a specific kind of stupid: playing *everything* as moment-to-moment, near-instantaneous actions.

**Town Mode actions are NOT punctual** (not existing as a point in time/space):
- Hunting for adventure leads = talking to dozens of people over several hours across multiple locations
- Resolved with a *single die roll* and a narrative summary
- Characters interact with *locales and neighborhoods*, not specific people at specific times

You can always drop into Encounter/Combat Mode when needed (thugs in an alley, important social encounter), then pop back to Town Mode seamlessly.
