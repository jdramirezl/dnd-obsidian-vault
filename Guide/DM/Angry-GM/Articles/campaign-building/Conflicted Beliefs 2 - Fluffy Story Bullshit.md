# Conflicted Beliefs: Fluffy Story Bullshit

**Source:** [The Angry GM](https://theangrygm.com/conflicted-beliefs-2/)
**Date:** August 1, 2018
**Series:** Start Your Own Campaign / World-Building

---

## Follow-Up Clarifications

### On Overlapping Ideals
- Some overlap between paired ideals is fine and even desirable. The ideals only overlap if you look at definitions in isolation — they're defined by their specific conflict endpoints.
- "Free will" (opposing determinism) and "freedom" (opposing security) aren't the same because each is pinned to a specific conflict.
- **Always write down what conflicts mean and how endpoints relate** — don't just list words.

### On Mismatched Pairings
- Some pairings will match well, some won't. That's fine. Unusual pairings force more creative god-building.
- You can use whatever system you want to pair ideals — the process matters more than the specific arrangement.

### On D&D Polytheism
- D&D is based on **classical polytheism**, not modern religions. There's one religion — all gods exist and everyone knows it.
- You pray to whichever god handles what you need. A cleric of the death god still calls on the weather god to end a storm.
- The five major gods should be elevated above all other elements. Lesser gods, angels, devils can be added as subordinates.

## Worked Example: Building a Pantheon

Starting from the five conflict pairs (Justice/Idealism, Freedom/Progress, Pragmatism/Free Will, Compassion/Tradition, Determinism/Safety), here's how a mythology emerges through free-writing:

### The Family Structure
- **Father God (Barad)** — Justice + Idealism → King of the Heavens, god of Light, rules, laws, order. Patron of rulers, lawmakers, philosophers. Favored by dwarves. Domains: Light. Paladins of Devotion and Vengeance.
- **Mother Goddess (Hesta)** — Compassion + Tradition → Queen of the World, goddess of Life, home, hearth, community. Patron of healers, midwives, farmers. Favored by halflings. Domain: Life.
- **War God / Son (Joran)** — Freedom + Progress → Noble, independent, curious explorer-warrior. Patron of soldiers, explorers, generals. Favored by humans (centaurs as honored). Domain: War. Paladins of Devotion.
- **Nature Goddess / Daughter (Gaera)** — Pragmatism + Free Will → Wild, untamed hunter goddess. Patron of hunters, survivalists. Respected by wood elves. Domain: Nature. Paladins of the Ancients.
- **The Seer (Nelaam)** — Determinism + Safety → Mysterious moon-dweller, reader of omens, keeper of the dead. Patron of seers, oracles, lorekeepers. Dislikes arcane magic. Respected by high elves. Domain: Knowledge.

### Lesser Gods
- **The Trickster (Maleen)** — Nelaam's sister, imprisoned among the stars, freed by Joran. Married to Joran. Witch goddess, source of arcane magic. Sows discord but isn't purely evil. Domain: Trickery.
- **The Stormbringer (Vrad)** — A Primordial who fell in love with Gaera. Temperamental, secretive. Domain: Tempest.

### The Creation Myth
1. The **Wanderer** (absent Creator) created the cosmos and moved on, leaving a plan written in the stars.
2. **Barad** was born to rule the Heavens; **Hesta** sprang from the world itself.
3. **Nelaam** was left to interpret the Wanderer's plan from the moon.
4. **Primordials** overran the young world. Nelaam manipulated Barad and Hesta together.
5. Their children **Joran** and **Gaera** were born. Joran freed **Maleen** from her star-prison.
6. Joran convinced Barad to send the Celestial Host against the Primordials. The war was won.
7. Gaera fell in love with the Primordial **Vrad** and protected him. Hesta turned against Barad and Joran.
8. The gods dispersed — Barad to the Eastern Mountains, Hesta to the Northern Headwaters, Gaera to the Western Forest, Joran roaming the world, Nelaam watching from the moon.

## Key Design Lessons

- Start with symbolic dichotomies (Father/Mother, Heaven/Earth) to give structure, then let the mythology grow organically.
- **Assign D&D domains and oaths** to gods early — they provide constraints that make the mythology feel grounded in gameplay.
- Pick five symbolic things (elements, colors, terrains, creatures) and assign them to your five ideal-pairs as a starting scaffold.
- The mythology doesn't need to be complete — just enough to start a campaign and grow from there.
