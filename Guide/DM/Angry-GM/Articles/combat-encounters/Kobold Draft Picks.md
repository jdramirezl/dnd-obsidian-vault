# Kobold Draft Picks

**Source:** [The Angry GM – Kobold Draft Picks](https://theangrygm.com/kobold-draft-picks/)
**Date:** July 9, 2020
**Series:** Practical Adventure Building

---

## Core Concept

Before building combat encounters, create a **roster** — a shortlist of thematically connected, mechanically interesting monsters you can mix and match. Building rosters around factions ensures encounters feel cohesive and strategically varied rather than like a random kitchen-sink dungeon.

---

## The Roster-Building Process

1. **Determine the faction(s)** in your adventure — each faction gets its own roster
2. **Pick a thematic identity** — color, element, culture, fighting style
3. **Decide on roster size** — aim for 5–9 generic monsters for ~12 encounters; unique bosses and setpieces are built separately when needed
4. **Design a racial/factional strategy** — a unifying tactical theme expressed through shared traits
5. **Create variations** that contribute to, change, or subvert that theme
6. **Differentiate organization levels** — some monsters appear in gangs (2:1 vs PCs), others in party-sized numbers

---

## Encounter Budget Guidelines

For a scene that fills 1–2 sessions:
- **1 climax encounter**
- **2 major encounters**
- **2–4 minor encounters**
- Total: ~6 encounters (close to the DMG's adventuring day)
- ~3 hours of focused play, or 1–2 sessions with exploration and complications

This is a guideline, not an exact science. Fast parties burn through it in 4 hours; slow parties stretch it to 3 sessions. The structure **feels** well-paced regardless of actual duration.

---

## Factional Thinking

Build rosters around the forces/factions in your adventure:
- **Single faction adventure** (kobolds + drakes) → one roster
- **Multi-faction adventure** (demons vs. devils) → separate rosters per faction
- **Kitchen sink dungeon** (random vermin) → just a list of monsters, less strategic cohesion needed

Monsters within a faction should **fit together thematically** and **work together mechanically** in interesting ways.

## Racial Strategy Design (Example: Black Talon Kobolds)

### Unifying Theme
Kobolds are clever, cunning, and dangerous in numbers. The core trait is **Pack Tactics**, but redesigned for more strategic depth:

> **Pack Tactics (Revised).** Kobolds gain advantage on attacks when there are more of the kobold's allies adjacent to the target than the target's own allies.

This creates a **numbers game** with multiple player counterstrategies:
1. Avoid getting mobbed
2. Clump up so kobolds can't outnumber any single engagement
3. Use forced movement (thunderwave, shove) to break kobold numerical advantage

### Elemental Identity
Black Talon tribe = **acid** affinity + acid resistance. Chosen for splash damage synergy with the pack tactics strategy.

---

## The Five Roster Entries

### 1. Black Talon Kobold Warrior
- **Role:** Standard melee combatant
- **Organization:** Gang (6–10 per major encounter, 2:1 vs PCs)
- **Stats:** Low-to-average threat; lower HP, lower accuracy (relies on Pack Tactics for advantage), average AC and damage
- **Gear:** Proper weapons and armor (industrious miners, not skins-and-spears)
- **Design note:** Single attack (no multiattack) to avoid amplifying Pack Tactics too much in early rounds

### 2. Black Talon Kobold Slinger
- **Role:** Ranged battlefield controller with staff-slings
- **Organization:** Gang
- **Abilities:**
  - **Acid bombs** — splash damage to adjacent creatures; punishes PC clumping (kobolds resist acid, so friendly fire is minimal)
  - **Glue pots** — pin PCs down, prevent them from joining engagements or reaching the Slinger
- **Tactical synergy:** Stays at range behind obstacles; forces melee PCs to isolate themselves to deal with them, making them vulnerable to Warrior mobs

### 3. Black Talon Kobold Drakemaster
- **Role:** Hardy melee controller with mancatcher (catchpole)
- **Organization:** Party (similar numbers to PCs)
- **Abilities:**
  - **Mancatcher** — snare and drag PCs out of engagements, isolating them
  - **Hooked goad** — stab snared PCs with off-hand weapon
  - **Drake sauce** — pots of goo that attract drakes to a target and give them a frenzy damage boost (players can potentially loot these and turn drakes against kobolds)
- **Tougher stats** than Warriors — higher HP, promoted to party-level organization

### 4. Leaping Drake
- **Role:** Mobile melee skirmisher
- **Organization:** Gang
- **Abilities:**
  - **Pounce** — scythe-like rear talons usable only after moving (must leap at foe)
  - **Disengage/Dash as bonus action** — ping-pongs around the battlefield
- **Tactical synergy:** Can disengage from targets and join kobold engagements to tip the numbers for Pack Tactics; highly mobile harassment
- **Stats:** Lower AC than kobolds but more HP (hardy but unarmored)

### 5. Acidspitter Drake
- **Role:** Pure ranged attacker
- **Organization:** Party (smaller numbers, more powerful)
- **Abilities:**
  - **Acid spit** — ranged acid attack with splash damage
- **Tactical synergy:** Punishes PC clumping (like Slingers but as a dedicated damage dealer rather than controller)
- **Stats:** Lower AC, more HP (drake statline)

---

## Design Principles Demonstrated

- **Avoid multiattack on mob creatures** — it amplifies advantage effects (Pack Tactics) too much
- **Give variations distinct tactical roles** — melee, ranged/controller, melee/controller, mobile skirmisher, ranged damage
- **Create counterplay** — players should be able to figure out and counter the faction's strategy
- **Build synergies between monster types** — Slingers punish clumping, Warriors punish isolation, Drakemasters break formations, Leaping Drakes tip numbers
- **Differentiate organization levels** — gangs (2:1) for weaker monsters, party-sized groups for tougher ones
- **Save unique/boss monsters** for when you build those specific encounters later

---

## Key Takeaways

- Build a **roster** before designing individual encounters — it's your palette of building blocks
- Design around a **factional tactical theme** with variations that create strategic depth
- Aim for **5–9 generic monsters** per faction for a multi-session adventure arc
- Each variation should have a **distinct role** that synergizes with the others
- Players should be able to **learn and counter** the faction's strategy over multiple encounters
- The battlefield itself provides additional variety on top of the roster mix-and-match
