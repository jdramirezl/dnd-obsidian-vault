# Oh No, More Bosses: Oozes, Slimes, and a Duplicating Wizard

## Summary

This article extends the Paragon system to creatures that can split apart and recombine — oozes, swarms, and duplicating spellcasters. It introduces several new Paragon tools: **Paragon Division** (reactive splitting), **Paragon Replication** (voluntary spawning), **Paragon Absorption** (recombining), **Paragon Stabilization** (redistributing HP across pools), **Paragon Actions** (once-per-round special actions on initiative count 20), and **Paragon Expansion** (size scales with number of active pools). It also introduces the Spawn/Unmake mechanic for creatures that create different entities from their HP pools (e.g., a wizard creating phantom duplicates).

## Key Advice

### New Paragon Mechanics

#### Paragon Stabilization
- Distributes all current HP evenly across all pools above zero.
- Cannot resurrect pools already at zero.
- Creates a tactical nightmare: the creature is harder to kill through attrition because focused fire gets "smoothed out."
- Math-heavy but worth doing once per round for the depth it adds.

#### Paragon Replication (Voluntary Split)
- Creature transfers one or more HP pools to a new copy of itself.
- New copy has identical stats but fewer pools (and thus fewer turns).
- Original loses the transferred pools entirely.
- New creature is worth zero XP when defeated.

#### Paragon Absorption (Recombine)
- Creature absorbs an adjacent copy, destroying it.
- Gains the absorbed creature's HP pools (including any at zero).
- May immediately use Paragon Stabilization after absorbing.

#### Paragon Division (Reactive Split)
- Triggered as a **Reaction** when a condition occurs (e.g., slashing or lightning damage).
- Creature splits into two; HP pools divided as evenly as possible.
- Each new creature is worth half the original's XP.
- May use Paragon Stabilization before dividing.

#### Paragon Actions
- Occur on initiative count 20 (losing ties), once per round.
- Modeled on Lair Actions but travel with the creature.
- Menu of options: Stabilization, Replication, Absorption.

#### Paragon Expansion
- Size scales with active HP pools: 6+ = Huge, 4+ = Large, 2+ = Medium, 1 = Small.
- Enables size-dependent abilities (grapple range, space coverage, etc.).

### Spawn / Unmake (Duplicating Creatures)

For creatures that create *different* entities from their HP pools:

- **Spawn**: Reduce one HP pool to zero → create a new creature with HP equal to what was lost. Cannot reduce last pool.
- **Unmake**: Destroy a spawned creature → heal HP equal to its current HP. Can distribute healing across pools, even reviving zero pools.

### Example Creatures

#### Umbral Pudding (CR ~3–4 encounter)
- 8 HP pools, immune to slashing and lightning (but those trigger Division).
- Grapples creatures in its space; grapple scales with size.
- Stabilization makes attrition difficult — you almost WANT it to split so it can't stabilize.
- But splitting means 8 separate targets running around.
- Works best when the party fights it multiple times and learns its behavior.
- 400 XP (50 × 8), ×2.5 multiplier = 1,000 XP difficulty. Good for level 3–4.

#### Berserker Wasp Swarm (CR ~5 encounter)
- 6 HP pools, attacks every creature in its space.
- At Huge size (6 pools), covers 9 squares with 8 turns per round.
- Spreading out prevents Stabilization but leaves weak party members isolated.
- Staying together lets the swarm recombine and stabilize.
- 1,200 XP (200 × 6), ×2 multiplier = 2,400 XP difficulty. Good for level 5.

#### Nongahnim (Duplicating Wizard, CR ~10 encounter)
- Uses Spawn/Unmake to create Phantom copies of itself.
- Teleportation tied to spawning — shuffles positions so players can't track the real one (shell game).
- Each Phantom gets full spell slots (this is correct balance — see below).
- Can shuffle turns between real and phantoms.

### The Spellcasting Flaw in Paragon Monsters

- A Paragon spellcaster is mechanically multiple creatures sharing one body.
- If they share one set of spell slots, they burn through slots at 2–3× the normal rate (multiple turns per round).
- Each HP pool should effectively have its own spell slots to maintain correct CR output.
- Giving each Phantom full slots solves this — they're separate creatures with separate resources.

## Key Takeaways

1. Paragon Stabilization (HP redistribution) is the key innovation — it makes focused fire less effective and adds tactical depth.
2. Splitting/recombining creates a genuine tactical dilemma: big creature is scarier but can stabilize; small pieces are weaker but numerous.
3. Paragon Actions (initiative count 20, once/round) provide a clean action economy for gimmick abilities.
4. Size scaling with pool count makes the split/recombine dynamic visually and mechanically meaningful.
5. Paragon spellcasters need separate spell slot pools per HP pool to maintain correct damage output.
6. The Spawn/Unmake mechanic enables creatures that create different entities (phantoms, skeletons, etc.) from their life force.
