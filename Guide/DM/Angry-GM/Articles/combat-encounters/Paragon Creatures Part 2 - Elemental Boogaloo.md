# Son of the D&D Boss Fight Part 2: Elemental Boogaloo

**Source:** [The Angry GM](https://theangrygm.com/elemental-boogaloo/)
**Date:** April 15, 2015

---

This article extends the Paragon Creature system from Part 1 by introducing **transforming boss monsters** — creatures that change form mid-fight, forcing players to adapt their tactics. It also revises the core Paragon Traits and introduces three elemental archon boss monsters.

## Revised Paragon Traits

Three core traits now exist in a dedicated "Paragon Traits" section of the stat block:

- **Paragon Fortitude.** Multiple hit point pools tracked separately. All damage/healing applies to one pool only. When a pool hits zero, all ongoing conditions/effects end. A zeroed pool can't be healed until after a long rest. Creature dies only when ALL pools reach zero.
- **Paragon Exhaustion.** One turn per round for each pool above zero. Advantage on initiative for the first turn. Additional turns inserted after PC turns. Gets one reaction between each of its turns. Loses turns as pools are depleted.
- **Paragon Fury.** One additional turn per round for each pool that HAS been reduced to zero. Starts with one turn and gains more as it weakens. Advantage on initiative for the first turn. Additional turns inserted after PC turns.

## Transforming Boss Monsters

The core idea: cram two (or more) different stat blocks into one creature. When the first hit point pool drops to zero, the creature transforms — changing equipment, tactics, abilities, or even its entire nature.

**Why transformations work:**
- Break up the fight and force players to change tactics
- Create an "oh f---" moment just as players settle into a rhythm
- Combined with Paragon action economy, each phase feels mechanically distinct

### The Paragon Transformation Trait

> **Paragon Transformation.** The creature has multiple forms, each corresponding to one of the creature's hit point pools. The creature begins the battle in one form. When that form's hit point pool has been reduced to zero, it assumes its next form immediately. Statistics, traits, or actions keyed to a specific form are only used when the creature is in that form.

### Building a Transforming Boss

1. **Start with as many base monsters as you want forms.** Each form needs its own stat block as a starting point.
2. **Keep hit points and challenge rating the same across forms.** Different HP/CR is possible but makes balancing the action economy much harder.
3. **Name each hit point pool** to match its corresponding form.
4. **Key unique stats, traits, and actions** to specific form names.
5. **Focus on a small number of very obvious changes** — players notice trait/action changes more than stat changes.

### Example: Guardian Armor

- **Aggressive Form:** Animated armor with a maul. Lumbering, heavy hits, one turn.
- **Defensive Form:** Draws a side sword and kite shield. Faster, more defensive, multiattack. One turn (uses Paragon Fury — gains the second turn in this form).

Inspired by Dark Souls black knights and Zelda: Twilight Princess darknuts.

## Elemental Archons

Archons are small elementals crammed into magical armor and given weapons — bound elemental infantry (inspired by 4E's creative monster redesigns).

**The transformation concept:** In armored form, the archon fights like a normal armored soldier (elemental resistances suppressed by the binding). When the armor is destroyed, the elemental breaks free — completely different fight. The freed elemental is enraged and attacks anything, friend or foe.

### Design Challenges

Elementals have resistance to nonmagical weapons, which effectively doubles their HP for defensive CR calculation. This required heavy rebalancing — AC and damage outputs vary between forms to keep the challenge rating consistent. Makes the creatures swingy but functional.

### Three Archons

| Archon | Role | Armored Form | Elemental Form |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Avalanche** | Tank | Heavy melee, high AC | Earth elemental, resistant, crushing |
| **Storm** | Artillery | Ranged attacks, easy to pin down | Air elemental, flight + range (very hard combo to deal with) |
| **Blazing** | Damage-over-time | Scimitar attacks | Fire form, enters spaces, sets PCs on fire, chips away at 2–3 PCs at once |

### The Blazing Archon's Hidden Damage

The Blazing Archon looks weak on paper but deals ~34 damage/round from multiple sources:
- Fire Form trait: 10 damage to melee attackers
- Entering a PC's space: 5 damage + sets them on fire (5 more next turn)
- Three touch attacks: 12 damage
- Potential fire spread: 5 more

This is why it gets 4 scimitar attacks but only 3 touches or 1 fire breath — the passive damage is already enormous.

## Practical Takeaways

- **Transforming bosses are simple to build** once you understand the Paragon framework — just key different stats/abilities to different hit point pools.
- **Both forms should have the same CR and HP** for easy balance.
- **Focus on obvious, dramatic changes** between forms (equipment, movement, attack style) rather than subtle stat shifts.
- **The "uncontrolled fury" mechanic** (freed elemental attacks everyone) rewards players who encounter archons at multiple levels — at low levels it's a boss fight, at higher levels it becomes a tactical tool.
- **Consider ALL damage sources** when calculating offensive CR — passive auras, fire-on-contact, and lingering effects add up fast.
