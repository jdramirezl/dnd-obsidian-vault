# Strategy, Tactics, and Teamwork for Dummies Teaching Dummies (Part I)

**Source:** [The Angry GM](https://theangrygm.com/strategy-tactics-and-teamwork-1/)
**Date:** July 27, 2022

---

Part 1 of a three-part series on teaching players Strategic Planning, Tactical Decision Making, and Teamwork (ST&T). This installment defines the core concepts: Goals, Plans, Strategies, and Tactics. Parts 2 and 3 cover Teamwork and Coaching.

## Important Caveats

- **Most modern RPGs don't require ST&T.** Systems are designed so each player can spam their best ability, ignore everyone else, and still win. Games like Blades in the Dark simulate strategic teamwork without requiring players to actually do it.
- **This isn't about creative stunts.** Swinging from chandeliers and attacking weak points is cinematic action, not strategy. The best tactical choices are often mundane.
- **If your players don't need ST&T to win, they won't use it.** You have to make their lives hard enough that they have to. That's not for every table.

## Goals

Goals are things you want to accomplish. They come in several flavors:

### Short-Term vs. Long-Term

- **Short-term goals** are simple and quick to achieve.
- **Long-term goals** are big, complicated, and require sustained investment.
- Smart people break long-term goals into a series of short-term goals — a checklist.

### Goals Nest and Interconnect

Every short-term goal is usually part of a long-term goal. Goals also interconnect across different long-term objectives. Understanding this nesting is critical for ST&T.

### Goals of Doing vs. Goals of Being

- **Goals of Doing:** Accomplish a specific end. "End the bandit threat."
- **Goals of Being:** A vision of yourself to fulfill. "Be a good paladin who upholds justice."

Most players can only articulate their Goal of Doing — and often misstate it. A paladin's goal isn't "kill the bandits," it's "end the bandit threat and bring them to justice." This misunderstanding is where most ST&T failures begin.

**Every character has multiple simultaneous goals:** the mission goal, their moral/identity goals, and survival/advancement goals.

## Plans

A plan is a list of intended future actions. Simple as that. But plans get complicated fast because you have multiple goals running simultaneously and they interconnect.

## Strategies

A Strategy is a Plan devised to accomplish a goal — usually a long-term one — **based on assumptions and predictions about the future.** It's not just a to-do list; it incorporates what you think is likely to happen.

Key distinction: assumptions and predictions are NOT blind guesses. They're conclusions drawn from available information.

### Why Strategize At All?

Even though reality never plays out as predicted, Strategic Plans are valuable because:

1. **Complex goals can't be accomplished in the moment.** They require setting priorities and allocating resources in advance.
2. **Strategizing forces you to analyze goals, prioritize, and gather resources.** This makes you far more equipped to handle whatever actually happens.
3. **You make better Tactical Decisions** because you're in tune with your goals, priorities, and available resources. Every tactical choice is a cost-benefit analysis, and Strategy keeps you calibrated on costs and benefits.

### Overstrategizing

Players love taking this too far. Problems with overplanning:

- **Guesses aren't assumptions.** Preparing for a non-existent breath weapon wastes resources and creates vulnerabilities.
- **Every plan element has a cost** — even mental overhead. Complex plans mean someone forgets a key element at a crucial moment.
- **More contingencies ≠ more success.** This is provably false.
- **Analysis paralysis.** Players shoot down good ideas because they can imagine a flaw in every plan, leading to hours of non-planning followed by going in with no plan and dying.

## Tactics

Tactics are the in-the-moment actions you take based on what's actually happening. Strategies are Plans based on predictions. Tactics are Choices based on the here-and-now.

Tactical Decisions are about **seizing opportunities** or **mitigating disasters** — weighing costs and benefits in real time.

Sometimes the best tactical decision is to retreat and devise a new strategy.

## Strategy vs. Tactics in TTRPGs

- **Teams create Strategies** — the party plans together before the action.
- **Players implement Tactics** — each player makes their own character's moment-to-moment decisions during combat.

## Practical Takeaways

- **Goals are everything.** If your players can't enumerate their goals — mission goals, identity goals, survival goals — they can't strategize.
- **Strategy = Plan + Predictions.** Not a to-do list, not a wild guess. Based on available information.
- **Tactics = Real-time choices.** Seize opportunities, mitigate disasters, know when to retreat and replan.
- **Overplanning kills more parties than underplanning.** A good-enough plan executed now beats a perfect plan that never materializes.
- **You can't coach what you don't understand.** GMs need to grasp these distinctions before they can teach players.
