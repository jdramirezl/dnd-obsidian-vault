## Key Advice

Building encounters is simple: decide how many "positions" the encounter has (3–6), then fill them with monsters using a straightforward slot system based on organizational size (mob, gang, group, pair, solo). Stop obsessing over precise balance — D&D assumes every fight starts with a near-full-health party, and the range between "trivial" and "fun" is enormous. Just eyeball it and experiment.

## Summary

Angry demonstrates his encounter-building system by populating a hobgoblin lair dungeon, showing how the position/slot math works in practice. The article is equal parts practical example and passionate plea to stop over-engineering encounter balance.

**Calm down about balance:**
- D&D designers are on record: encounters are balanced assuming full HP and mostly full resources. Limited resources (spells, rages, smites) are "gravy" on top.
- Published WotC modules don't even follow their own balance math. Precision is a myth.
- The range between "trivial cakewalk" and "frustrating TPK" is very wide. Most fights land in the fun zone.
- Don't try to stop players from resting. Let resting be an interesting choice with natural consequences (dungeon restocks), not a punishment.
- Player agency trumps narrative pacing. Your climactic encounter will get blown apart sometimes. Laugh and move on.

**The Dungeon Roster:**
- Before building encounters, create a shortlist of monsters (the roster). Use half to a third as many monster types as you have encounters.
- 6 encounters → 2–3 monster types. 12 encounters → 4–6 monster types.
- Pick monsters of the right tier with varied organizational sizes for encounter variety.
- Example roster: Hobgoblin Infantryman (Group), Hobgoblin Archer (Group), Hobgoblin Sergeant (Group), Goblin Skirmisher (Gang, re-tiered), Giant Rat (Mob), Ogre Berserker (Pair).

**The Position/Slot System:**
- Every encounter (Force) has a number of open positions:
  - Complication/Restock: 2–3 positions
  - Minor/Major Encounter: 3–5 positions
  - Climactic Encounter: 4–6 positions
- Position count has nothing to do with party size. Design for a tier of play (3–5 PCs).

**How monsters fill positions:**
- **Group** (standard): 1 monster = 1 position
- **Gang** (weaker): 2 monsters = 1 position
- **Mob** (weakest, simple): 3–4 monsters = 1 position
- **Pair** (stronger): 1 monster = 2 positions
- **Solo** (boss): 1 monster = 4 positions (or all positions)

**Mixing and matching examples:**
- 4 Hobgoblin Infantrymen = simple 4-position encounter.
- 3 Infantrymen + 2 Archers = 5-position encounter showing shield wall + ranged support.
- 3×(2 Goblin Skirmishers) = 3-position encounter, 6 goblins total.
- 3 Infantrymen + 2 Goblins = 4-position mixed encounter.
- 2 Goblins + 12 Giant Rats = 4-position kennel encounter.
- 3 Infantrymen + 1 Ogre Berserker = 5-position encounter (ogre fills 2 slots, one left blank).
- Half-positions work: 1 Goblin + 2 Rats fills one position. Mix freely.

**The Formation keyword (bonus content):**
- A formation = a contiguous group of adjacent allies. No special rules by itself, but abilities can reference it (e.g., hobgoblin shield wall requires 3+ in formation).

**Mega Man approach to monster introduction:**
- Introduce base monsters first (plain hobgoblin soldiers), then layer in new types gradually.
- Let players learn monster abilities before mixing them into complex encounters.
- Some monsters are obvious enough to skip introduction; some are unique one-off challenges.
- Train players against recurring abilities, then remix those abilities in a unique boss.

## Actionable Steps

1. **Build a roster first** — pick 2–6 monster types of the right tier with varied organizational sizes.
2. **Decide position count** — 2–3 for complications, 3–5 for normal encounters, 4–6 for climactic fights.
3. **Fill positions using the slot math** — Group=1, Gang=½, Mob=¼, Pair=2, Solo=4.
4. **Mix freely** — half-positions are fine. Leave positions empty to vary difficulty. Experiment.
5. **Introduce monsters gradually** — simple encounters with one type first, then mix in new types as the dungeon progresses.
6. **Stop sweating balance** — the party will nova your climactic encounter sometimes. That's the game. Laugh and design the next one.
