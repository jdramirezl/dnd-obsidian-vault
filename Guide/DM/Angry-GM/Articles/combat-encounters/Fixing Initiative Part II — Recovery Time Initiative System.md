## Key Advice

Instead of Speed Factor Initiative (action determines turn order *before* you act), flip it: the action you just took determines when you act *next round* via a Recovery Die. This creates meaningful turn-order decisions, improves pacing with rerolled initiative every round, and is easy to track with physical dice on the table. Three stackable variants let you adopt as much or as little change as you want.

## Summary

Part II delivers the actual system in three stackable variants: a tracking method, the core Recovery Time system, and advanced rules for maximum mechanical impact.

### Variant 1: Turn Order on the Table
- After rolling initiative, set an extra d20 (or d10 for the full system) to your score and place it by your miniature or in front of you.
- After acting, roll your Recovery Die and hold it aside until the next round starts.
- GM uses sticky notes for monster groups with their tracker die on top.
- This alone makes rerolling initiative every round practical at a physical table.

### Variant 2: Angry's Recovery Time Initiative System
**Core concept:** Rounds have 10 phases (1–10). Your Recovery Die result = your phase next round.

**Initiative Die (first round, by creature size):**
- Small or smaller: d4
- Medium: d6
- Large: d8
- Huge+: d10

**Recovery Die (after acting, determines next round's phase):**
- **Attack action:** Recovery Die = base damage die of the weapon (e.g., longsword 1d8 → d8 Recovery; dagger 1d4 → d4 Recovery; greatsword 2d6 → d10 Recovery).
- **Cast a Spell:** d8 normally; d6 for cantrips; d10 for upcasting with a higher-level slot.
- **Dash/Disengage/Dodge/Help/Hide/Search/Use Object:** use your size-based Initiative Die.
- **Ready action:** based on the readied action when triggered. If the readied action never triggers, you automatically go in Phase 1 next round (deliberate use allowed).
- **Bonus action used:** increase Recovery Die by one size.
- **Free object interaction:** increase Recovery Die by one size.
- **No ability modifiers on initiative.** Straight die rolls only. Highly randomized turn order = more dynamic combat.

**Ties:** GM decides order within a phase.

### Variant 3: Full-On Recovery Time Initiative (Advanced Rules)

**Durations and timing:**
- 1 minute = 3 rounds (not 10). Durations tick down at end of each round, including the round the effect starts. This makes timing buffs/debuffs matter.

**Recovering condition:**
- From the start of a round until the start of your phase, you are Recovering.
- While Recovering: no reactions (including opportunity attacks).
- Rogues get Sneak Attack against Recovering targets without needing advantage or nearby allies.

**Knockback:**
- If hit by an attack/spell whose base damage die is *larger* than your size-based Initiative Die, bump your Turn Order Tracker up by 1 (act later).
- If already at Phase 10, you are Delayed: lose your turn, roll your base Initiative Die for next round.
- No effect if you've already acted this round.
- Heavy weapons can stunlock large opponents.

**Checked Attacks (light attack / heavy attack):**
- On a melee attack, you can voluntarily reduce your base damage die (minimum d4) to roll a better Recovery Die.
- Must declare before rolling the attack. Damage modifiers and bonus dice (Sneak Attack, etc.) are unaffected.
- If you have multiple attacks, the highest base damage die determines your Recovery Die — so you must Check all attacks to benefit.
- Example: Halberd (1d10+5) checked to 1d6+5 → rolls d6 Recovery instead of d10.

## Actionable Steps

1. **Start with Variant 1** — just track initiative with dice on the table and reroll every round. Zero rule changes, immediate pacing improvement.
2. **Add Variant 2** — introduce Recovery Dice. The GM just tells players which die to roll after their turn. Minimal player-side complexity.
3. **Layer in Variant 3 rules one at a time** — Recovering condition first (biggest tactical impact), then Knockback, then Checked Attacks.
4. **Use round breaks** — the natural pause between rounds (setting out Turn Order Trackers) is the perfect moment for a "combat wide shot" summary.
5. **Ignore Dexterity for initiative** — the system is designed around naked die rolls with no modifiers.
6. **For existing initiative modifiers** (Improved Initiative, *haste*, *slow*), model them as moving one die size up or down the ladder.
