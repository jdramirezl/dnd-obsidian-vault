## Key Advice

Resting is not a button the party presses — it's an automatic consequence of sitting around long enough. Understanding the evolution from Natural Healing to Rest-as-Action reveals that the rest mechanic is really just a pacing knob that controls the structure of the adventuring day. You can twist that knob to change how many encounters fit between rests, and thus reshape the entire feel of your campaign.

## Summary

Angry traces the history of rest mechanics from AD&D through 5E, answers two good questions and one stupid one, and shows how to modify resting to change the game's structure.

**Evolution of rest mechanics:**
- **AD&D:** No "resting" mechanic. Natural Healing: 1 HP/day of complete rest. You either adventured or recovered — not both.
- **AD&D 2E:** Added detail (hiking = 1 HP/day, bed rest = 3 HP/day), but still recovery vs. adventure as a binary choice.
- **3E:** Subtle shift — 8 hours of sleep = 1 HP/level recovered + spell preparation. Recovery became part of the daily adventure routine, not a separate activity. Also introduced many X-per-day abilities beyond spells.
- **4E:** Completed the shift. Extended Rest (6 hours) = full HP + all resources. Short Rest (5 minutes) = spend Healing Surges + recover encounter powers. Made balance much easier since every encounter assumed near-full resources.
- **5E:** Kept Rest-as-Action. Long Rest (8 hours) = full HP + half Hit Dice + all daily resources. Short Rest (1 hour) = spend Hit Dice + recover short-rest abilities.

**Why is a Short Rest 1 hour?**
- So parties can't take one after every fight. The intended structure is: 2 encounters → Short Rest → 2 encounters → Short Rest → 2 encounters → Long Rest (6 encounters/day, 2 Short Rests).
- Angry suspects a Short Rest after every encounter wouldn't actually break the game, since Hit Dice are the real limiter.

**The stupid question (interrupted Long Rest → retroactive Short Rest):**
- Resting is NOT a declared action. If you sit around for an hour, you automatically get Short Rest benefits. Period. There's no "Rest Button" to press.
- A party 4 hours into a Long Rest has already completed multiple Short Rests' worth of time. The benefits manifest automatically.
- The fiction of choosing to rest vs. not rest makes no in-world sense — what is the character doing differently?

**Modifying rests to change game structure:**

*Fewer encounters per day (1–2 instead of 6):*
- Make Short Rest = 24 hours of downtime. Long Rest = 2 consecutive days of rest.
- This maintains the intended resource attrition curve but spreads it across days instead of hours.
- Optionally divorce spell/ability recovery (still 8 hours of sleep) from HP recovery (24-hour Short Rest) to keep utility magic functional.

*Short Rest after every encounter (4E style):*
- Change Short Rest to 5 minutes. Probably fine — Hit Dice are still the limiter.
- A few abilities (Bard's Song of Rest, Druid shapeshifting HP tricks) might be slightly more useful but not game-breaking.

*Eliminate Short Rests entirely:*
- Convert all short-rest abilities to "once per encounter."
- Increase all PC HP by 50% to compensate for lost Hit Dice healing (math: half your Hit Dice per day ≈ half your max HP in expected healing).
- Adjust HP-threshold abilities (Disintegrate, Sleep) when used against PCs.

**Key design principle:** Whatever you do, rest mechanics must be based on in-fiction actions the characters take, not arbitrary GM fiat. If the party sits for 8 hours, they get Long Rest benefits. No "story-based" rest gates.

## Actionable Steps

1. **Stop treating rests as buttons** — if the party has been sitting around for an hour, they've taken a Short Rest whether they "declared" it or not.
2. **To slow the pace (fewer encounters/day):** Short Rest = 24 hours, Long Rest = 2 days. Keep spell recovery at 8 hours if desired.
3. **To speed the pace (more encounters/day):** Short Rest = 5 minutes (after every fight). Monitor Warlock and Battlemaster power levels.
4. **To eliminate Short Rests:** Give PCs 150% HP, convert short-rest abilities to once-per-encounter.
5. **Don't punish resting** — let the party rest when they want. Make it an interesting *choice* with natural consequences (dungeon restocks, time passes) rather than a wrong answer.
6. **Understand the intended structure** — 6 encounters, 2 Short Rests, 1 Long Rest per adventuring day. Modify from this baseline deliberately.
