# The Lore of the Angryverse: Undead

## Why This Matters

This article is an example of a **Campaign Bible** entry — cohesive lore that doubles as monster-design guidelines. It's not just fluff; it informs mechanical design, answers edge-case rulings on the fly, and gives players discoverable world-knowledge to exploit.

> "Because I know what undead ARE and how they work, it's a lot easier for me to create a new undead creature… or deal with weird questions like 'what happens if I use this obscure spell that affects the soul on a vampire?'"

The Angryverse isn't a published setting — it's a **cosmological framework** that stays consistent across campaigns. Geography and history change; how the universe works does not.

## Cosmology: Life, Death, and Souls

### Beasts and Anima

- The natural world exists at the boundary between divine and primordial realms — a world of balance.
- The world is suffused with **natural magic** (what arcane casters manipulate). From this magic arise **anima** — spirits of pure will, instinct, drive, and base emotion.
- Anima are fluid and interconnected, like water in a sea. Strong spirits can exist separately (the wolf spirit, the spirit of the Red Hills) but eventually rejoin the whole.
- Gods ordered the world with **natural cycles** (day/night, seasons, evolution) because anima couldn't tolerate rigid, unchanging divine order. This is when beasts and plants appeared.
- When a beast dies: body returns to the world, animus rejoins the greater anima, carrying instincts and memories back.

### Mortals and Souls

- Gods created mortals by imbuing living things with **divine sparks** — pieces of their own essence. This grants reason, choice, and the capacity for good and evil.
- A mortal = **material body** + **animus** (will to live) + **soul** (reason, choice, higher ideals).
- Some mortals were made by imbuing existing creatures (metallic dragons from chromatic dragons, worgs from wolves, gnolls from hyenas). Others were shaped directly in the gods' image (humanoid races).
- On death: body consumed by the world, animus rejoins anima, **soul departs** to the divine realms.

### The Plane of Long Shadows

- Souls are weighed down by negative emotions, animal instincts, and guilty memories accumulated during life.
- Instead of returning directly to the divine realms, souls "fall through the veil" into the **Plane of Long Shadows** — a dark, twisted reflection of the natural world.
- Souls journey across this plane toward the **Gates of Judgment**, shedding negative baggage as they travel.
- That shed dark energy coagulates into **shades** — miasma of negative emotions that torment other passing souls and constantly seek entry into the natural world.

## What Undead Are

> An undead is a dead mortal — or rarely, a beast — whose body or spirit or both cannot return to the natural world because a **dark shade** is sustaining them in place of a soul. Or, in the worst cases, holding the soul hostage.

**Necromancy is always evil.** It offends the divine order, offends the natural world, and allows shades (composed of hate, suffering, gluttony, guilt) into the living world.

### Three Types of Undead

| Type | What It Is | Examples |
|------|-----------|----------|
| **Corporeal** | Dead body (± animus) sustained by negative energy | Zombie, Ghoul, Ghast, Mummy, Revenant, Wight |
| **Spectral** | Animus corrupted and sustained without a body | Ghost, Banshee, Haunt, Poltergeist, Shadow, Wraith |
| **True Undead** | Body + animus + **trapped soul**, sustained by dark energy | Lich, Vampire, Death Knight, Dracolich |

- Corporeal and spectral undead **have no soul**. They may appear intelligent but are just corrupted instincts and emotions. A ghost "avenging" its murder is just anger sustained by a hateful shade — helping it get revenge disperses the anchoring emotions.
- **True undead** retain their soul (trapped in an object for liches, trapped in the body for vampires). The soul gradually rots, twists, and corrupts. They can reason and remember, but are consumed by dark emotions beneath a mask of sanity. Most dangerous because they can pass as mortal.

### How Undead Arise

- **Spontaneously**: When a mortal dies, the departing soul thins the veil. A shade can seep through into the vacated body (corporeal) or catch a strongly-willed animus before it disperses (spectral).
- **Mass death sites**: Places where many died — especially in strong negative emotion — keep the veil thin for years. Remains are connected to the thinness.
- **Necromancy**: Wizards/priests purposely open the veil and let shades into remains. Can even use beast remains (beasts don't thin the veil on their own, but can house shades).
- **True undead**: Only through powerful magic — liches anchor their own souls; greater vampires result from curses, dark pacts, or profane magic; lesser vampires are spawned by greater ones.

### Caring for the Dead

Funeral rites serve multiple purposes:
1. **Quell** powerful emotions that attract shades
2. **Ward** against shade intrusion
3. **Heal** the thinness in the veil
4. **Allow** body and animus to return to the world safely
5. **Contain** any undead that do arise (sarcophagi, sealed tombs)
6. **Maintain the conduit** between remains and the traveling soul — prayers and memories bolster the soul on its journey through the Plane of Long Shadows

> "We remember the dead so their souls do not get lost."

## Monster Design Rules for Undead

### Universal Undead Traits

- **Type**: Undead. **Tag**: Corporeal, Spectral, or True Undead.
- **Alignment**: Always evil. Most are NE or CE. LE is rare except for true undead.
- **Ability Scores**: Good Constitution (necromantic energy slows decay, negates biological needs). Corporeal/spectral have low Int and often low Wis (no soul = instinct-driven). True undead have good mental scores from lifetimes of experience.
- **Saving Throws**: All undead are proficient in **Wisdom saves** (willful, resistant to turning). Powerful spectral undead may add Charisma saves; powerful corporeal may add Constitution saves.

### Vulnerabilities, Resistances, Immunities

| | All Undead | Corporeal | Spectral |
|---|-----------|-----------|----------|
| **Vulnerable** | Radiant | Fire (most) | Force |
| **Immune** | Necrotic, Poison, Poisoned, Exhaustion | — | Bludgeoning/Piercing/Slashing (nonmagical); Grappled, Paralyzed, Petrified, Prone, Restrained |

### Required Traits

**All undead:**
> ***Sustained by Necrotic Energy.*** When the creature takes necrotic damage, it is not only immune to the damage, it regains HP equal to the damage it would have suffered.

**Spectral undead** also get:
- Fly speed (no walking speed), hover
> ***Incorporeal Movement.*** Can move through creatures and objects as difficult terrain. Takes 1d10 force damage if ending turn inside an object.

**All undead** should have sunlight sensitivity if not outright harmed by it:
> ***Sunlight Sensitivity.*** Disadvantage on attack rolls and Wisdom (Perception) checks relying on sight while in sunlight.

### Defensive Philosophy

- **Low AC, high HP.** Undead should feel hard to destroy because they absorb damage without feeling it. Players should hit often but wonder how much more it takes.
- Regeneration and reformation traits are encouraged, especially for spectral and true undead.
- Ghosts should keep coming back unless the anchoring emotion is resolved.
- True undead (vampires, liches) should have **complex destruction requirements** that drive entire adventure arcs.

### Senses

- Darkvision (standard)
- **Blindsight 30–60 ft** — all undead can sense the living

### Languages

- Most corporeal undead cannot speak (may understand languages from life).
- Spectral and true undead speak languages from life.
