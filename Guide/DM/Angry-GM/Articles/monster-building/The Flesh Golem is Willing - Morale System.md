# The Flesh Golem is Willing, But the Spirit Failed its Morale Saving Throw

**Source:** [The Angry GM](https://theangrygm.com/flesh-is-willing-spirit-failed/)
**Date:** August 7, 2019
**Series:** Hacking Rules

---

## Why Morale?

Morale adds **depth** to the sort of game where you want more than just "race the enemy to zero hit points." It provides:

- **Multiple ways fights can end** — flee, surrender, rout, not just death
- **New player strategies** — take out the leader, intimidate, show of force
- **Scalable combat** — some fights are shorter than others (wolves flee after first hit, goblins break at half HP, boss fights go to the wire)
- **Moral dilemmas** — what do you do with surrendered enemies?
- **Monster differentiation** — goblins are cowards, hobgoblins are disciplined, gnolls are unrelenting, and you can SHOW it mechanically
- **Shorter fights** — the five-round fight ends in three, leaving more time for more encounters
- **Player tactics** — players can surrender or flee too, reducing TPKs

---

## The Core Rule

> When a creature takes enough damage to make it go "oh shit, I'm really in trouble," it makes a **Wisdom saving throw (DC 12)**. If the save fails, its morale breaks.

That's it. One roll. One check per creature per fight.

---

## The "Oh Shit" Threshold

Three levels, chosen per creature/encounter:

### Bloodied (Default)
Creature has lost **half its hit points**. Visible to the world — announced at the table. This is the standard trigger for most monsters.

### About to Die (Boss/Climactic Fights)
Creature takes a hit that leaves it with enough HP that **two more similar hits will kill it** (~20% HP). Used for powerful solo monsters, boss fights, leader-type enemies, or plot-relevant battles.

### Wounded (Minor Fights)
Creature takes **any damage at all**. Used for nuisance fights, wandering monsters, mooks. Wild animals that don't risk serious injury.

**Stat block notation:**
> HP: 150 (30) — about to die threshold
> HP: 25 (12) — bloodied threshold
> HP: 10 (9) — wounded threshold

---

## Morale Save DC

- **DC 12** — standard
- **DC 7** — brave, disciplined, or stubborn creatures
- **DC 17** — cowardly creatures

Simple: DC 12 ± 5 for Easy/Medium/Hard.

---

## Why Wisdom?

Wisdom is the saving throw used for ALL fear and panic effects in D&D 5E:
- Frightful Presence of dragons
- Horrifying Visage of ghosts
- Spells: Antipathy, Command, Fear, Compulsion, Crown of Madness, Dominate Person
- Cleric turning/abjuring

Natural creatures and unintelligent creatures have low Charisma but decent Wisdom. Bears, badgers, boars, wolves — fearless creatures with good Wisdom. Basing morale on Wisdom means the creatures that should be hard to scare off actually are.

(If you prefer Charisma, it works fine — just bump DCs up for natural predators.)

## Morale Break Keywords

When morale breaks, the creature's behavior is determined by a keyword:

### Panic
Drop weapons, flee heedless of opportunity attacks. If cornered → surrender.

### Rout
Withdraw and move away, but keep weapons. Don't care about allies. If cornered → surrender.

### Retreat
Withdraw with allies in good order, call for retreat. If cut off from escape → resume fighting.

### Surrender
Make it clear they don't want to fight. Rely on enemies to let them live. Reserved for lawful creatures who expect honorable treatment.

### Rage
Keep fighting but no longer recognize allies. Don't benefit from ally-dependent abilities. If no target available, attack closest creature (friend or foe). Gone berserk.

### Betray
Turn on their own allies, ignoring PCs. For opportunistic mercenaries, or enslaved/abused creatures (summoned elementals, demons, golems) who turn on their masters.

**Stat block notation:**
> **Morale:** Wisdom save DC 17 or Panic
> **Morale:** Wisdom save DC 12 or Retreat
> **Morale:** Wisdom save DC 7 or Rage

## Adding Depth

### Mechanical Hooks on Keywords
- Gnolls could gain bonus damage or advantage when enraged
- A routed dragon might explode in elemental fury to cover escape
- A panicked creature with telepathy might cause psychic backlash
- An enslaved ogre whose morale breaks might beat its goblin master to death

### Player Interaction
- **Intimidate action:** Player's Intimidate check becomes the DC for the Wisdom save. Advantage if monster is uninjured/outnumbering; Disadvantage if below threshold or allies fleeing.
- **Weaken Wisdom saves** with spells/abilities to push morale breaks
- **Don't** try to break the will of gnolls (Rage) or mind flayers (psychic scream)

### NPC Actions
- Leaders can use Intimidate/Persuasion to "unbreak" allies' morale (new save vs. leader's skill check)
- Panicked creatures can spread broken morale — force adjacent allies to make Morale saves

### Additional Triggers
- Many animals check morale if they take **fire damage**
- Creatures with vulnerabilities suffer **Disadvantage** on Morale saves when damaged by their vulnerability
- Leaders grant **Advantage** on allies' Morale saves

### Creatures That Never Check Morale
- Mindless undead
- Uncontrolled constructs
- Creatures with **Fearlessness** (magical effect)

---

## Effective HP Impact

Morale reduces effective monster HP to roughly **72.5%** of stated HP on average. This is intentional — fights end earlier, players get through more encounters per day, and action varies in scope and scale.

---

## Narrating Morale

Describe the morale check as part of the hit narration:

> "You landed a solid blow. The goblin is forced backward, ribs cracking. It looks panicked, frantically looking for an escape route. It's about to flee."

> "You landed a solid blow. The hobgoblin drops back, armor dented. For a moment it hesitates. But then its resolve firms. Its eyes narrow and it steps back up, ready to answer your attack."

Players learn to recognize these narrations and can safely ignore creatures whose morale has broken.
