# The Volcano Dragon: Some July Fourth Fireworks!

## Overview

A custom-built **3-stage Paragon Monster** volcano dragon for D&D 5E, designed as a campaign-ending boss fight. Built from scratch rather than converting an existing red dragon, because Paragon Monster rules work best with original designs.

- **Target**: 4–5 PCs of Level 14
- **Difficulty**: Moderate to Hard (remember to double XP for difficulty calculation — it counts as three monsters)
- **Design origin**: Inspired by a reader question about finishing a campaign with a dragon fight

## Key Design Principles

This monster demonstrates Angry's Paragon Monster system in practice:

1. **Three stages** — the dragon transforms/evolves as each HP pool is depleted, keeping the fight dynamic
2. **Built from scratch** — converting existing monsters to Paragon format produces inferior results compared to purpose-built designs
3. **Multi-phase boss feel** — each stage should play differently, forcing players to adapt tactics

## Known Errata

- The Blazing Form's Lava Belch damage was originally listed as 16d8 (56) — this was the calculation value assuming two targets. Correct damage is **8d6 (28)**.

The original PDF was hosted on Google Drive but has since been taken down and the stat block data was lost. The article remains as a reference for the design philosophy and Paragon Monster approach.

## Takeaways for Monster Building

- **Paragon Monsters** are Angry's answer to solo boss fights in 5E — splitting a single powerful creature into multiple "phases" with separate HP pools and escalating abilities
- **Build bosses from scratch** when possible rather than retrofitting existing stat blocks
- **Campaign-ending fights** deserve custom monsters tailored to the party's level and composition
- Each phase should introduce **new mechanics** that change how players approach the fight
