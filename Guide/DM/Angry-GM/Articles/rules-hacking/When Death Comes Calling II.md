# When Death Comes Calling II

**Source:** [The Angry GM — True Mechanical Managery: When Death Comes Calling II](https://theangrygm.com/true-mechanical-managery-when-death-comes-calling-ii/)
**Date:** November 26, 2024
**Series:** True Campaign Managery — True Mechanical Managery module

---

## Strategy vs. Plan

- Character Death doesn't lend itself to rigid Policies or Procedures — too many unknowns. Every death is unique.
- True Campaign Managers have a **Character Death Strategy**: know your goals, understand the situation, make the best decisions when death happens.
- "Best decision possible" ≠ "perfectly accomplish every goal every time."

### You Can't Have It All

- Sometimes all you can do is recognize it's going to suck and power through.
- Part of leadership is knowing when the ideal solution is beyond you and not wasting resources fighting it.

### Bending and Breaking the Game

- Every GM eventually faces a death that forces them to decide **how far they'll bend the game** to recover.
- No universally right or wrong answer. Options range from "player sits out a month" to "retcon resurrection rules" to "play as a ghost."
- If you erase consequences because they're too hard, you might as well have fudged the dice to prevent the death.
- It's on **you** to decide how much you're willing to retcon or rewrite.

### Punishment Is a State of Mind

- Costs, consequences, setbacks, and losses are NOT punishments — they're just negative outcomes.
- What makes something a punishment: it's **designed to teach a lesson** or **arrest a behavior**.
- A player sitting out because their character died = logical result of the game, not punishment.
- **Stop using the word "punishment" entirely.**

## Handling Each Problem

### Problem 1: Death Is Upsetting
- **Goal:** Keep the game moving. **Tools:** Suggest breaks, call session early if needed, use conflict resolution.
- **Don't prime:** No written policy about taking breaks when upset — that creates a self-fulfilling prophecy.

### Problem 2: Death Wrecks the Current Encounter
- Remind players firmly they'll deal with the death after the fight. Don't let them leave the corpse behind.
- Try to finish the current encounter before breaking the session.

### Problems 3+: The "What Next?" Discussion

**Die Quietly Policy:** Unless death is visually obvious, only the downed player knows their status. Death Saves are silent and secret. No one knows until someone interacts with the body.
- Deaths often aren't discovered until the crisis is over.
- Downed characters are treated as emergencies.

**The What Next discussion is inherently metagame.** That's fine. GM and players work out:
- What survivors do next (continue or retreat?)
- How to get the eliminated player back permanently (resurrection or new character?)

**Think through your priorities in advance:**
- How long can a player sit out?
- Can survivors finish the current adventure first?
- How much agency do survivors have vs. the eliminated player?

## Angry's Personal Strategy (As Example)

1. **One full session sitting out is acceptable.** Maybe two for reliable weekly games.
2. **Doesn't bend the game.** No convenient chance meetings. No random prisoners. Death costs the party, not just the player.
3. **Doesn't override survivor agency.** Vetoes only if choices would sideline the eliminated player too long.
4. **Sidebar discussions:** Privately asks the eliminated player what they want first. Then advocates gently during the survivors' discussion without overriding their choices.

## Core Takeaway

No instruction manual exists. Identify your goals, set priorities, then work out solutions with your best judgment — firm as needed, flexible as required. Think it through **before** you need it.
