# Silver and Gold: How You Should Handle Treasure (Part 2)

**Source:** [The Angry GM](https://theangrygm.com/how-you-should-handle-treasure-2/)
**Date:** January 26, 2022

---

This is the second half of the treasure rules — how to actually get treasure into your game. How to decide how much of what kind of treasure to drop in your adventures, and how to present it to your players.

## Caveats

- These rules require Part 1's treasure classification system (class, rarity, quality).
- References Angry's tier-based advancement system (level ranges noted in tables).
- Values roughly match DMG treasure tables. Close enough — treasure doesn't impact the game that much.

**Key Assumptions:**
1. Traditionally-structured game with periodic quests/adventures/objectives
2. Heroes complete one quest every 1–2 sessions
3. Heroes complete ~6 quests per three-level tier (Apprentice tier = levels 1–2, ~3 adventures)
4. If none of that works: give one Treasure Trove every 1–2 sessions

## Totally Random Treasure Sucks

You've got to place treasure deliberately. It's got to make sense in the world. Goblin raiders should have stolen merchant goods, not ruby-studded platinum crowns. Ancient pyramids should have ancient treasures, not turnips. Whatever the rules or dice say, override and do what makes sense.

## Presenting Treasure

Each treasure item has two parts:
1. **Narrative description** — what it actually is in the world
2. **Mechanical classification** — class, rarity, quality

Record format: `Bloodstone [Uncommon Gemstone]` or `Sack of Undersized Turnips [Inferior Common Trade Good]`

The descriptive part tells you and the players how the item interacts with the world. A sack of turnips can feed famine-stricken villagers. A brass horn engraved with hunting scenes can be gifted to a hunting-obsessed lord. Don't skimp on descriptions — but don't over-detail either. Be vague. Use patterns, motifs, themes.

## Building Treasure Troves

A Treasure Trove is a list of all treasure the heroes *might* find in an adventure. It includes a pile of cash and a list of treasure items. Total value is based on tier of play.

**General guidelines:** ~1/3 coins, ~2/3 items by value. About 6 individual items. ~1 in 6 items should be modified quality.

### Method 1: Deliberate Trove Building

Start with total Trove value, divide between coins and items.

**Trove Value by Tier:**

| Tier | Levels | Total Value | Normal Coins | Normal Items | Fewer Coins Items |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Apprentice | 1–2 | 350 gp | 100 gp | 250 gp | 50/300 |
| Journeyman | 3–5 | 1,500 gp | 500 gp | 1,000 gp | 300/1,200 |
| Adventurer | 6–8 | 2,500 gp | 700 gp | 1,800 gp | 500/2,000 |
| Veteran | 9–11 | 5,000 gp | 1,500 gp | 3,500 gp | 1,000/4,000 |
| Champion | 12–14 | 25,000 gp | 7,000 gp | 18,000 gp | 5,000/20,000 |
| Heroic | 15–17 | 50,000 gp | 15,000 gp | 35,000 gp | 10,000/40,000 |
| Legendary | 18+ | 100,000 gp | 35,000 gp | 65,000 gp | 15,000/85,000 |

**Item Values (to "buy" items for your trove):**

| Rarity | Trade Good | Gemstone | Art Object |
|---|---|---|---|
| Common | 10 gp | 50 gp | 100 gp |
| Uncommon | 25 gp | 100 gp | 500 gp |
| Rare | 50 gp | 500 gp | 1,000 gp |
| Very Rare | 250 gp | 1,000 gp | 5,000 gp |
| Legendary | 500 gp | 5,000 gp | 10,000 gp |

**Systematic approach:** Spend half the item budget on one big item. Then half the remainder on the next. Repeat until budget is low, then fill with small items.

### Method 2: Fistfuls of Dice

Roll d6s to generate troves quickly (~10 minutes).

**Trove Dice and Coin Multiplier by Tier:**

| Tier | Levels | Trove Dice | Coin Multiplier |
|---|---|---|---|
| Apprentice | 1–2 | 2d6 | × 25 gp |
| Journeyman | 3–5 | 3d6 | × 50 gp |
| Adventurer | 6–8 | 3d6 | × 100 gp |
| Veteran | 9–11 | 4d6 | × 250 gp |
| Champion | 12–14 | 4d6 | × 1,000 gp |
| Heroic | 15–17 | 5d6 | × 2,500 gp |
| Legendary | 18+ | 5d6 | × 5,000 gp |

**Process:**
1. Allocate Trove Dice between coins and items
2. Roll coin dice × Coin Multiplier = total coin value
3. Roll item dice = number of individual items
4. Roll all item dice: any 1s = unusual quality → reroll those (1–3 = inferior, 4–6 = superior)
5. Choose a treasure profile and roll item dice to determine class:

**Item Class by Profile (d6):**

| d6 | Balanced | More Trade | More Art | No Trade | No Art |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Trade Good | Trade Good | Trade Good | Gemstone | Trade Good |
| 2 | Trade Good | Trade Good | Gemstone | Gemstone | Trade Good |
| 3 | Gemstone | Trade Good | Gemstone | Gemstone | Trade Good |
| 4 | Gemstone | Gemstone | Art Object | Art Object | Gemstone |
| 5 | Art Object | Gemstone | Art Object | Art Object | Gemstone |
| 6 | Art Object | Art Object | Art Object | Art Object | Gemstone |

6. Roll each pile's dice for rarity:

**Item Rarity by Tier (d6):**

| d6 | Apprentice | Journeyman | Adventurer | Veteran | Champion | Heroic | Legendary |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Common | Common | Common | Common | Uncommon | Rare | Rare |
| 2 | Common | Common | Common | Uncommon | Rare | Rare | Very Rare |
| 3 | Common | Uncommon | Uncommon | Rare | Rare | Very Rare | Very Rare |
| 4 | Common | Uncommon | Uncommon | Rare | Very Rare | Very Rare | Legendary |
| 5 | Common | Uncommon | Rare | Rare | Very Rare | Very Rare | Legendary |
| 6 | Uncommon | Rare | Rare | Very Rare | Very Rare | Legendary | Legendary |

## Stocking the Adventure

A Treasure Trove represents all treasure the heroes *might* find. Where they find it is up to you — locked chests, dead adventurers' backpacks, ancient vaults, dragon hoards, bandit camps, NPC payments.

### Rules of Thumb

1. **At least half** the Trove's value should come from completing the adventure's objective (dragon's hoard, patron's payment, etc.)
2. **Half the remainder** should lie along the path to the finish line (archaeological finds, minion loot, dead adventurer corpses)
3. **Whatever's left** goes down optional paths, behind secret doors, guarding optional mini-bosses, or as side quest rewards
4. **At least two-thirds** of on-the-way and optional treasure should require work (defeating encounters, traps, puzzles, secret doors, risks, resource expenditure). No more than one-third unguarded.

These are rules of thumb — don't break out a calculator. It's about feels, not thinks.

## Pocket Change

Don't micromanage individual mook treasure. Just roll when players go scrounging:

- **Poor mooks:** 1d6 cp
- **Average mooks:** 2d6 cp + 1d6 sp
- **Well-off cannon fodder:** 3d6 cp + 2d6 sp + 1d6 gp

Give major NPCs (goblin shaman, orc underchief, named bandit lieutenant) actual treasure from the Trove.
