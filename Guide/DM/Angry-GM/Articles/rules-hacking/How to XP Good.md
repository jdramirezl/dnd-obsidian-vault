# How to XP Good

**Source:** [The Angry GM – How to XP Good](https://theangrygm.com/how-to-xp-good/)
**Date:** November 27, 2017
**Series:** Hacking Rules / XP & Advancement

---

## Why Character Advancement Matters

Character advancement is tied to multiple layers of the game experience:

- **Flow & Progress:** Players need a sense of growth over time. Advancement lets the scope and scale of the game expand, creating a feeling of moving toward something rather than going in circles.
- **Psychological Reward:** Gaining levels feels good. It's quantifiable, measurable, and permanent — unlike fleeting story victories.
- **Agency:** A point-based system creates a direct, observable through-line from player actions → progress → goals. XP creates a sense of agency in the "G" part of RPG the same way story goals create agency in the "RP" part.
- **Behavior Driving:** XP lets the designer or GM incentivize specific styles of play. Award more XP for non-lethal solutions? Players negotiate. Award more for combat? Players fight everything. Break the through-line and you lose this lever entirely.

## Why Point-Based XP Beats "Milestone" Leveling

GMs who throw out XP and just tell players when to level up are cutting corners on one of the most psychologically important parts of the game.

- **Players prefer XP.** GMs hate doing the math; players want to see the numbers. Many GMs who switched to milestone did so out of laziness, not design insight.
- **Milestone kills agency.** Without a visible, quantifiable connection between actions and advancement, players feel like their growth is arbitrary — at the GM's whim.
- **"XP only rewards combat" is wrong.** DMG 260–261 explicitly explains how to reward XP for non-combat encounters and story milestones. You don't need to throw out XP to reward things other than killing.

## The Angry XP System

### Core Principle
XP is success-based and goal-based. Players earn XP by accomplishing things — not for "good RP" or "clever ideas."

### Encounter XP (The Basic Unit)
Forget individual monster XP. Rate each encounter (combat, negotiation, trap, obstacle) as **Easy**, **Medium**, or **Hard**:

| Outcome | XP Award |
|---|---|
| Success | Full XP for encounter difficulty at party level |
| Barely scraped by (heavy losses/damage) | Half XP |
| Failure (retreat, capture, TPK) | No XP |

Dole out XP during the game, immediately after each encounter. Appoint a player to keep a running tally.

### Milestones (Story Accomplishments)

| Milestone Type | Description | XP Value |
|---|---|---|
| **Major Milestone** | Completing the adventure's primary goal | Medium encounter equivalent |
| **Minor Milestone** | A required intermediate step that takes more than one encounter to achieve | Easy encounter equivalent |
| **Optional Milestone** | A sidequest or bonus objective not required to complete the adventure | Half an easy encounter equivalent |

### Tweaks for Different Campaign Styles
- **Story-heavy:** Bump major milestones to hard encounter XP, minor to medium.
- **Sidequest-heavy:** Make optional milestones worth easy encounter XP.
- **Loot-focused:** Assign XP values to treasure hauls.
- **Exploration/Hex-crawl:** Assign XP per hex cleared.
- **Remove scoring pressure:** Drop the half-XP penalty for scraping by.

## Ideal Rate of Advancement

- **Don't level every session.** Players need an adjustment period to learn new abilities, work out rules, and see how they fit the group dynamic. One session to shake out new toys, one session to be comfortable with them.
- **Sweet spot:** One level every 2–3 sessions (for weekly/biweekly games).
- **The DMG's built-in rate is intentional.** Some levels go slower than others, roughly aligned with levels that include big class changes.
- **Players are more forgiving of slow advancement if they can see XP gains.** The GMs who get in trouble are the ones who only hand out XP at the end of multi-session adventures.

## Session Planning Framework

In a 4–5 hour session, expect 5–8 encounters:

- 2–3 Easy encounters
- 2–3 Medium encounters
- 1 Hard encounter
- 1 Major milestone
- 1–2 Minor milestones

Hand out XP after every encounter or at end of session. Only allow level-ups between sessions. Following this framework roughly matches the DMG's intended "adventuring day" progression.
