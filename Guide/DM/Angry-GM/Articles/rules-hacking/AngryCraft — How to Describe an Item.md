# AngryCraft — How to Describe an Item

**Source:** [The Angry GM – The Great Magic Item Analysis: How to Describe an Item](https://theangrygm.com/how-to-describe-an-item/)
**Date:** June 19, 2020
**Series:** Crafting a Crafting System / AngryCraft

---

## Context

This article continues the AngryCraft series. With prices assigned (see [[AngryCraft — How to Price an Item]]), the next step is defining the **magical qualities** (traits/descriptors) that materials can have, so every DMG magic item can be described by a formula of typed, raritied, quality-tagged materials.

## The Goal: 20 Qualities or Fewer

Materials have a **type** (metal, mineral, herb, fluid, essence, etc.), a **rarity**, and up to **three magical qualities**. Qualities must:

- Cover every magical effect in the DMG's ~800 items
- Be intuitive and memorable (no more than 20)
- Draw on existing D&D terms wherever possible
- Be applied consistently so players can reason about what they can craft from what they find

## The Formula Format (Hypothetical Example)

> **Frost Brand Greatsword**
> - 8 Very Rare Materials (6 Very Rare Metals)
> - 4 Cold Materials
> - Cannot Use: Common, Uncommon, Wood, Plant, Bone, Hide, Fire

Players satisfy all conditions from their material inventory. Overlap is allowed — a Very Rare Cold Metal counts toward both the rarity and quality requirements.

## Method: Descriptive, Not Prescriptive

1. **Initial pass** — Read every DMG item; assign gut-feeling descriptors (damage types, schools of magic, fantasy flavor words like "inert," "chaos," "mercurial").
2. **Compile the raw descriptor list** — Ended up with far more than 20 words, including creature types, hermetic elements, schools of magic, ability scores, etc.
3. **Index-card clustering** — Write each descriptor on a card, physically rearrange into ~20 groups based on mystical correspondences (alchemy/astrology-style "as above, so below" logic).
4. **Second pass** — Re-assign the 20 final qualities to every item with fresh eyes, renaming and shuffling correspondences as patterns emerge.

## Key Design Decisions

- **Schools of magic** are recorded as an intermediate step but are NOT used as final qualities directly — they're inconsistent (some describe flavor, some describe mechanics). However, some schools (transmutation, conjuration) do inform the final groupings.
- **Three-quality limit per formula** — No item requires more than three distinct qualities. In-world explanation: too many magical fields interfere with each other.
- **Chaos/Wild Magic as wildcard** — Items needing 4+ quality types use Chaotic materials, which can substitute for any quality. This also makes Chaotic materials more valuable and explains cursed items (too much chaos = unpredictable results).
- **Resistance items use same-element materials** — Fire resistance comes from fire materials (not cold). D&D has no built-in elemental opposition system, so using opposites would be arbitrary and unreinforced by anything else in the game.
- **+1/+2/+3 items need no special qualities** — Just materials of the proper rarity.
- **Theurgic materials = magical batteries** — Required for any item with charges that recharge. Also powers constructs and golems.

## The 20 Material Qualities

| Quality | Associated With |
|---|---|
| **Acidic** | Acid damage, corrosion, rusting |
| **Chaotic (Wild Magic)** | Randomness, wildcard substitute for other qualities |
| **Cold** | Cold damage, ice, cold resistance |
| **Dimensional** | Teleportation, portals, summoning, pocket dimensions |
| **Fiendish** | Fiends, lower planes, evil effects, warlock fiend pact |
| **Fiery** | Fire damage, fire resistance, fire element |
| **Forceful** | Force damage, force fields, abjuration wards, Strength |
| **Inert (Antimagic)** | Magic resistance, antimagic, earth element, giants, resilience |
| **Lightning** | Lightning damage, lightning resistance |
| **Mercurial** | Transmutation, shapeshifting, water element, Dexterity |
| **Necrotic** | Necromancy, necrotic damage, undead |
| **Phantasmal (Fey)** | Illusion, senses, music, Feywild, Wisdom |
| **Poisonous** | Poison damage, poison condition, neutralizing poison |
| **Psychic** | Psychic damage, enchantment, mind reading, Charisma |
| **Radiant** | Radiant damage, light, celestials, divine blessings, good |
| **Theurgic (Metamagic)** | Magic rules, spell slots, charges, divination, Intelligence |
| **Thundering** | Thunder damage, thunder resistance, explosive sound |
| **Vital (Living)** | Healing, regeneration, disease treatment, Constitution |
| **Void** | Darkness, destruction, madness, aberrations, Great Old One pact |
| **Zephyrous (Windy)** | Wind, air element, flight, breathing, hovering |

## Why This Matters for GMs

Even without the full AngryCraft system, the 20-quality list is a useful mental model for:
- Deciding what materials a monster might yield
- Improvising what a pile of loot could be used to craft
- Giving players intuitive hooks ("we have a lot of cold metal — we can probably make frost weapons")
- Keeping homebrew item creation consistent
