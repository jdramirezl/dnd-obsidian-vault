# Why Race Isn't Broken — Separating Biology from Culture

**Source:** [The Angry GM — Why Race Isn't Broken in Pathfinder and How to Fix It](https://theangrygm.com/why-race-isnt-broken/)
**Date:** August 26, 2015
**Series:** Hacking Rules

---

## The Problem

D&D and Pathfinder do not differentiate between **inborn racial traits** (biological — a matter of species) and **learned cultural traits** (a matter of upbringing and society). Weapon training, tool proficiency, enemy-specific combat bonuses, and language knowledge are clearly cultural — but they're baked into race as if every member of a species is born knowing them.

**Why it matters now more than before:**
- Early D&D: races were insular and isolated (Tolkien model). Race = culture. No conflict.
- D&D 3E onward: the world became cosmopolitan. Mixed populations, cross-cultural backgrounds. But racial features stayed the same.
- D&D 5E: added Backgrounds (representing formative education/upbringing) — the perfect place for cultural traits. But racial abilities still didn't change.
- Result: An elf orphan raised in a human city automatically knows longsword and longbow. A human soldier gets no automatic martial training from their background.

**The deeper issue:** Over 40 years, the *role* of race in D&D was completely rewritten (from archetype to cosmopolitan option), but the actual racial details stayed mostly the same. Lazy design.

## The Solution: Race + Culture

Using Pathfinder's *Advanced Race Guide* point-buy system for racial features:

### Step 1: Strip Races to Biology Only

Go through each race, separate biological traits from cultural ones, balance the point values. Each core race ends up with ~5 points of biological features.

**Example — Biological Dwarf:**
- +2 Con, +2 Wis, -2 Cha
- Medium, Slow and Steady (20 ft, unmodified by armor/encumbrance)
- Darkvision 60 ft
- Hardy (+2 saves vs. poison, spells, spell-like abilities)
- Stability (+4 CMD vs. bull rush/trip while on ground)

**Example — Biological Elf:**
- +2 Dex, +2 Int, -2 Con
- Medium, Normal Speed
- Low-Light Vision
- Elven Immunities (immune to magic sleep, +2 vs. enchantment)
- Keen Senses (+2 Perception)

**Example — Biological Human:**
- +2 to One Ability Score
- Medium, Normal Speed
- Bonus Feat

### Step 2: Build Cultures (~5 points each)

Cultures contain the removed cultural traits. Mix and match Race + Culture.

**Dwarven Mountain Clanhold:**
- Defensive Training (+4 dodge AC vs. giants)
- Greed (+2 Appraise for precious metals/gems)
- Hatred (+1 attack vs. orcs and goblinoids)
- Stonecunning (+2 Perception for unusual stonework)
- Weapon Familiarity (battleaxes, heavy picks, warhammers, "dwarven" weapons)

*Dwarf + Dwarven Clanhold = standard core dwarf.*

**High Elven Enclave:**
- Elven Magic (+2 caster level checks to overcome SR, +2 Spellcraft to identify magic items)
- Weapon Familiarity (longbows, longswords, rapiers, shortbows, "elven" weapons)

*Elf + High Elven Enclave = standard core elf.*

### Step 3: Get Creative — Build New Cultures

**Warrior Tribe** (for half-orcs, humans, or wood elves):
- Intimidating (+2 Intimidate)
- Survivalist (+2 Survival)
- Weapon Familiarity (two traditional weapons or one weapon + one racial weapon group)

**Cosmopolitan Metropolis** (for humans, half-elves):
- Multitalented (two favored classes)
- Skilled (+1 skill rank per level)

**Farming Village** (for halflings, humans, dwarves):
- Hard Working (+4 vs. fatigue, exhaustion, forced marches, starvation, extreme temperatures)
- Stubborn (+2 Will vs. charm/compulsion enchantments; extra save 1 round later on failure)

**Wandering Caravanserai** (for halfling gypsies, low elves):
- Glib (+1 Bluff/Diplomacy, extra language per Linguistics rank)
- Urban Survivalist (+2 Appraise, +2 Perception for hidden objects/spoiled food/potion identification)
- Undaunted Spirits (+2 vs. fear/despair; reroll natural 1 once per day)

**Mountaintop Monastery:**
- Mountain Dweller (+2 Acrobatics on ledges, immune to altitude sickness, keep Dex bonus while climbing)
- Serene Mind (+2 Will saves)
- Useful Skills (one Craft/Profession as class skill with +2 bonus)

**God-Favored City-State:**
- Blessed Health (+2 vs. disease, ingested poisons, nausea/sickness)
- Favored Foe (+1 attack vs. specific creature types — enemies of the patron god)
- Historied (+2 Knowledge: Religion)

## The Power of the System

- **Any race + any culture** = unique, world-consistent characters.
- "My dwarf is from an exiled clan that wanders the world" = Dwarf + Wandering Caravanserai.
- A half-elf raised by high elves is mechanically very similar to other elves (like Elrond).
- GMs can build pages of cultures to flesh out their world. Players mix and match.
- Each culture tells a story about a place and its people through mechanics.

## Applicability to 5E

- 5E already has Backgrounds — the natural home for cultural traits.
- Strip racial features to biology, bulk up Backgrounds with the cultural stuff.
- The system practically writes itself, though 5E lacks a point-buy framework for racial features.
