# Tweaking the Core of D&D 5E

**Source:** [The Angry GM — Tweaking the Core of D&D 5E](https://theangrygm.com/tweaking-the-core-of-dd-5e/)
**Date:** March 31, 2017
**Series:** Hacking New Modes of Play

---

## Goal

Gently tweak D&D 5E's core action resolution to create a robust toolkit for building subsystems and new modes of play. Not rewriting — organizing and clarifying the toolbox.

## The Restated Core Mechanic

1. Player declares an action: a **desired outcome** + an **approach**.
2. GM determines: Can it succeed? Can it fail? Is there risk/cost for failure?
   - Can't succeed → automatic failure (or warn the player).
   - Can't fail → automatic success.
   - Can fail but no risk/cost (can retry forever) → automatic success.
   - Otherwise → roll dice.
3. GM picks the governing **Ability** (based on approach), sets **DC**, determines **Proficiency**, applies **Modifiers**.
4. Roll 1d20 + Ability Mod + Proficiency (if relevant) + bonuses/penalties.
5. Success = accomplish outcome or make progress. Failure = don't accomplish it, may suffer costs.
6. Regardless of success/failure, GM applies **consequences based on the approach**.

## Key Concepts: Approach, Outcome, Cost, Consequence

- **Outcome:** What the player wants (open the door).
- **Approach:** How they try (pick the lock vs. smash it). Determines mechanics used.
- **Consequences:** Results of the approach regardless of success/failure. Smashing is LOUD whether you succeed or not.
- **Costs of failure:** What you risk by attempting. No cost = no roll needed.
- **Time is not a cost** unless there's a timer. Missing an attack in combat IS a cost (rounds are limited).

## Divorce Skills from Abilities

- Skills are NOT subcategories of specific abilities. Divorce them completely.
- GM picks Ability based on approach, then determines if any Proficiency is relevant.
- Allows creative application: persuade with Intelligence (reason), Athletics with Constitution (endurance).
- Players describe actions — they don't ask to "make a check."

## The Reaction Rule (New)

> When a character has a moment to respond to something unexpected, the GM describes what they're aware of. The player describes their reaction. The GM determines if it could work, then asks for a Save or Check. Any Reaction ability can be used.

**Trap example:** Alice steps on a pressure plate. "You feel a tile give way. What do you do?" Alice tumbles forward → Dex Save. Bob raises his shield → won't help against a pit; he falls.

**Stealth example:** Carol sneaks behind a goblin about to turn. "The goblin freezes. What do you do?" Carol can hide (Disadvantage) or throw a dagger (Advantage, goblin unaware).

Gives players **choice** in defensive situations. Useful for traps, stealth, future modules.

## Passive Proficiencies as Knowledge (New)

> All Proficiencies represent active uses AND background knowledge. When a player with a Proficiency encounters something relevant, the GM gives them information automatically. Passive Skill = **8 + Ability Mod + Proficiency Bonus** (±5 for Adv/Dis).

**Gated flavor text:**
- *Skeletal creature in tattered linens, glowing eyes.*
  - [Religion 20] It's a lich — powerful undead spellcaster...
  - [Religion] It doesn't appear to be a normal skeleton.

**Don't roll for passive things.** Set knowledge DCs based on the fact that no one will ever roll.

## Opposed Rolls (New)

> DC = **8 + target's Ability Mod + target's Proficiency** (±5 for Adv/Dis).

Notation: Roll Dexterity (Stealth) vs. the scout's Wisdom (Perception).

**Base 8 instead of 10:** Favors the person taking action. Makes untrained characters more viable.

## Working Together (Revised)

> Highest modifier leads. Leader gets **+2 for 1–2 helpers** or **+5 for 3+ helpers**. Helpers must be capable of attempting the task alone.

Replaces standard Aid Another. Scales with group size. Leaves room for Advantage/Disadvantage from other sources.

## Group Checks (Revised)

> - **Any succeeds → all succeed:** Highest base modifier rolls.
> - **Any fails → all fail:** Lowest base modifier rolls.

Shifts focus to teamwork: "How do we get Elaine through this?" Party focuses buffs on one character. One roll, faster resolution, more tension.

## Summary Table

| Tool | Effect |
|------|--------|
| Approach/Outcome/Cost/Consequence | Hooks for subsystems beyond pass/fail |
| Reaction Rule | Player choice in defensive situations |
| Passive Proficiencies | Automatic knowledge, no rolls |
| Opposed Rolls (DC 8 base) | Streamlined opposition, favors action |
| Working Together (+2/+5) | Scalable teamwork |
| Group Checks (best/worst) | One roll, focused teamwork, faster play |
