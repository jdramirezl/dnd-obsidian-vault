# When Death Comes Calling I

**Source:** [The Angry GM — True Mechanical Managery: When Death Comes Calling I](https://theangrygm.com/true-mechanical-managery-when-death-comes-calling-i/)
**Date:** October 29, 2024
**Series:** True Campaign Managery — True Mechanical Managery module

---

## What "Character Death" Actually Means

Character Death = any game mechanic that **involuntarily and permanently** removes a PC from play, leaving the player unable to participate. Includes:

- Running out of HP / failing Death Saves
- Petrification, soul destruction, severing the Astral Cord
- Maiming so severe the character can't continue adventuring
- GM takeover (turned into abomination, raised as undead, crossing a moral event horizon)

**Gray areas:**
- Noble sacrifice = still Character Death (character is destroyed, even if chosen).
- Retirement due to permanent conditions = NOT Character Death (player had the option to continue).
- Reversibility (resurrection) doesn't mean it's not permanent — there's a Limbo of uncertainty.
- Temporary conditions (sleep, paralysis) = NOT Character Death — the character is coming back shortly.

## Three Attitudes You Must Fix

### 1. Stop Saying "Punishment"

- Death is NOT a punishment for bad play or stupid choices. It's an outcome — the end of a long series of choices, outcomes, and circumstances.
- "Death should only result from player decisions, not random dice" is just a subtle restatement of "death is punishment for poor choices."

### 2. Feelings Are Real and Normal

- Players feel grief, loss, frustration, guilt, anger, despair when characters die. **This is healthy.** It means they care.
- The GM also feels feelings — grief, annoyance, anger, guilt, self-doubt, anxiety, defensiveness, shame. All normal.
- Dismissing these feelings ("it's just a game") is callous and cruel.
- True Campaign Managers must navigate these emotions so the game can continue.

### 3. Feelings Aren't Everything

- "Games should never make anyone feel bad" is stupid and impossible.
- Anything you're invested in can hurt. Shielding players from all bad feelings creates **brittle players** and a worse game.
- Players who discover they can bully you with tantrums will make you miserable until you quit.

**The balance:** Accept death as natural. Treat players with compassion. Don't shy away, but don't be a dick.

## The Cascade of Problems When a Character Dies

### Problem 1: Emotional Shock
- Hits the table like a bomb. Emotional tenor changes instantly. Every player and the GM may react differently.

### Problem 2: The Game World Doesn't Stop
- Characters rarely die during calm scenes. There's usually an active crisis still happening.

### Problem 3: A Player Is Sidelined
- No character = can't play. They're just sitting there with no voice in the game world.

### Problem 4: "What Do We Do Now?"
- All plans on hold. Party lost resources, skills, possibly a key role. Can they continue? Should they retreat? What about the body? Is resurrection possible/affordable?

### Problem 5: The Meta-Issue — Who Gets a Say?
- The eliminated player has the most at stake but technically has no voice (no character = no agency). The GM has a huge stake but shouldn't dictate choices. Resurrection costs create uncomfortable social dynamics.

### Problem 6: Resurrection Logistics
- Availability, cost, who can cast it, NPC access, time limits on the corpse, what the eliminated player does during all of this.

### Problem 7: New Character Creation
- Level, gear, backstory, how they join — everything from campaign start must be answered again, probably differently.

### Problem 8: Incorporating the New Character
- How does the new character actually enter the ongoing game?

## Bottom Line

Character Death is a massive disruption touching emotions, logistics, agency, pacing, and continuity. You need a plan before it happens.
