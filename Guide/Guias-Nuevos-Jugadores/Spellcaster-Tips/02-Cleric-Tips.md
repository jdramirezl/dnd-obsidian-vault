# ⛪ Cleric — New Player Tips (Levels 1–5)

> **Role**: Healer, tank, blaster, support (depends on domain)
> **Casting Stat**: Wisdom | **System**: Prepared caster (full cleric spell list daily)
> **Spell Slots**: Full caster progression, recover on long rest
> **Focus**: Holy symbol (can be on shield)

---

## 1. Creative Uses for Class Features

### Channel Divinity: Turn Undead (2nd Level)
- **AoE with no attack roll**: Every undead within 30 feet must save — no "to hit" needed. Against a horde of zombies, this is better than Fireball
- **No concentration**: Turn Undead doesn't require concentration, so you can maintain *Spirit Guardians* while turning undead
- **Destroy Undead at 5th level**: Undead CR 1/2 or lower are *instantly destroyed* on a failed save — no damage roll, just gone. Wipes out zombie hordes
- **Recharges on short rest**: Unlike spell slots, Channel Divinity comes back on a short rest. Use it aggressively

### Channel Divinity: Preserve Life (Life Domain, 2nd Level)
- **Burst AoE healing**: 5 × cleric level HP divided among allies within 30 feet. At level 5, that's 25 HP distributed however you want
- **No spell slot cost**: This is Channel Divinity, not a spell. Use it to save slots for offense
- **Can't overheal past half HP**: But that's fine — it's designed for emergency stabilization, not topping off

### Divine Domain Spells
- **Always prepared, don't count against your limit**: This is huge. Life Domain gives you *Bless* and *Cure Wounds* for free — prepare other spells instead
- **Non-cleric spells become cleric spells**: Some domains give you spells not on the cleric list (Trickery gets *Mirror Image*, Tempest gets *Shatter*). These use your Wisdom

### Heavy Armor (Life, Nature, Tempest, War Domains)
- **AC 18 at level 1**: Chain mail (16) + shield (2) = 18 AC. You're tankier than most fighters at level 1
- **Stand in front**: With high AC and healing, you can be the party's frontline. Don't hide in the back

## 2. Spell Selection Tips (Levels 1–5)

### Always Prepare These
| Spell | Level | Why |
|-------|-------|-----|
| *Bless* | 1st | +1d4 to attacks AND saves for 3 allies. Best buff in the game at this level. Concentration |
| *Healing Word* | 1st | Bonus action, 60-foot range. Pick up downed allies without wasting your action |
| *Guiding Bolt* | 1st | 4d6 radiant + next attack has advantage. Huge damage for a 1st-level spell |
| *Spiritual Weapon* | 2nd | Bonus action attack every turn, no concentration. Your best sustained damage |
| *Spirit Guardians* | 3rd | 3d8 damage to every enemy near you, every turn, for 10 minutes. The cleric's best spell, period |

### Avoid These
| Spell | Why |
|-------|-----|
| *Cure Wounds* (as your only heal) | It's an action and touch range. Always take *Healing Word* too |
| *Light* | Take it as a cantrip if you need it, don't waste a prepared spell slot |
| *Purify Food and Drink* | Extremely situational. Prepare it only when you know you'll need it |

## 3. Common Cleric Mistakes

1. **Only healing**: Clerics are NOT just healers. *Spirit Guardians* + *Spiritual Weapon* deals more damage than most martial characters. Heal only when someone drops to 0
2. **Forgetting you can change spells daily**: You prepare from the ENTIRE cleric spell list every long rest. Heading into an undead dungeon? Swap in *Protection from Evil and Good*. This is your biggest advantage over bards and sorcerers
3. **Not using your holy symbol on your shield**: Your holy symbol IS your focus. Emblazon it on your shield — now you can cast with a weapon in one hand and shield in the other
4. **Casting Cure Wounds in combat**: In combat, *Healing Word* (bonus action) is almost always better. Use your action for *Spiritual Weapon*, *Guiding Bolt*, or a cantrip
5. **Forgetting concentration limits**: You can only concentrate on ONE spell. *Bless* OR *Spirit Guardians*, not both. Plan which one you'll use before combat

## 4. Combat Tactics Cheat Sheet (Levels 1–5)

### Level 1–2: Holy Warrior
**Round 1**: *Bless* (3 allies) or *Guiding Bolt* (big single target) → move into position
**Round 2+**: Cantrip (*Sacred Flame* or *Toll the Dead*) or weapon attack
**Emergency**: *Healing Word* (bonus action) + cantrip (action)

### Level 3–4: The Damage Engine Starts
**Round 1**: *Spiritual Weapon* (bonus action, no concentration) → *Guiding Bolt* or weapon attack (action)
**Round 2+**: Bonus action: *Spiritual Weapon* attack → Action: cantrip or weapon attack
**This combo lasts 10 rounds with no additional slots spent**

### Level 5: God Mode
**Round 1**: *Spirit Guardians* (action, concentration) → move into enemies
**Round 2**: *Spiritual Weapon* (bonus action) → Dodge action (keep concentration safe) or cantrip
**Round 3+**: Enemies take 3d8 each turn from Spirit Guardians + you attack with Spiritual Weapon as bonus action
**This is the strongest sustained damage any caster can do at level 5**

### Golden Rule
> ***Spirit Guardians* + *Spiritual Weapon* = your best combat combo.** Spirit Guardians damages every enemy near you automatically. Spiritual Weapon gives you a bonus action attack. Your action can be Dodge (to protect concentration), a cantrip, or a weapon swing.

## 5. Spell Slot Management

- **Levels 1–2**: 2–3 slots. *Bless* in the first fight, save a slot for *Healing Word*. Use cantrips (*Toll the Dead* = 1d12 vs damaged targets) for damage
- **Level 3–4**: 4–6 slots. *Spiritual Weapon* is your best investment — 1 slot for 10 rounds of bonus action attacks. Don't cast it in fights that will end in 2 rounds
- **Level 5**: 7 slots including 3rd-level. *Spirit Guardians* in big fights, *Spiritual Weapon* in medium fights. Save 1st-level slots for *Healing Word*
- **Channel Divinity recharges on short rest**: Use it every fight if relevant. It's free
- **Domain spells are free**: They don't count against your prepared limit. That's 2 extra spells at every tier
- **Ritual cast when possible**: *Detect Magic*, *Detect Poison and Disease* — if you have it prepared and have 10 minutes, don't spend a slot
