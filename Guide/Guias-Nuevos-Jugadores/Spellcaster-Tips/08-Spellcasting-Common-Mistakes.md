# ⚠️ Common Spellcasting Mistakes — Rules Everyone Gets Wrong

These are the most frequently misunderstood spellcasting rules in D&D 5e. Print this page and keep it at the table.

---

## 1. Concentration

**The Rule**: You can only concentrate on ONE spell at a time. Casting a new concentration spell ends the previous one immediately.

### Mistakes People Make
- ❌ Concentrating on *Bless* AND *Spirit Guardians* at the same time
- ❌ Forgetting to make concentration saves when taking damage
- ❌ Not realizing that being incapacitated or killed ends concentration

### How Concentration Saves Work
- **Trigger**: You take damage while concentrating on a spell
- **Save**: Constitution saving throw
- **DC**: 10 or half the damage taken, whichever is HIGHER
- **Fail**: The spell ends immediately
- **Multiple hits**: Each hit requires a separate save (e.g., 3 arrows = 3 saves)

### Tips
- Mark concentration spells with a (C) on your character sheet
- Use a physical token on the table to remind you when you're concentrating
- The War Caster feat gives advantage on concentration saves — excellent for frontline casters

## 2. Bonus Action Spells

**The Rule**: If you cast a spell as a bonus action, the only other spell you can cast that turn is a CANTRIP with a casting time of 1 action.

### Mistakes People Make
- ❌ Casting *Healing Word* (bonus action) and *Guiding Bolt* (action) in the same turn — **illegal**
- ❌ Casting *Spiritual Weapon* (bonus action) and *Cure Wounds* (action) in the same turn — **illegal**
- ✅ Casting *Healing Word* (bonus action) and *Sacred Flame* (cantrip, action) — **legal**
- ✅ Casting *Misty Step* (bonus action) and *Fire Bolt* (cantrip, action) — **legal**

### The Exception
- Casting a spell as a reaction (like *Counterspell* or *Shield*) is NOT affected by this rule. You can cast *Healing Word* (bonus action), a cantrip (action), AND *Shield* (reaction) all in the same round

## 3. Ritual Casting

**The Rule**: Ritual spells can be cast without expending a spell slot, but it takes 10 extra minutes.

### Who Can Ritual Cast (and How)
| Class | Requirement |
|-------|-------------|
| **Wizard** | Spell must be in your spellbook (does NOT need to be prepared) |
| **Cleric/Druid** | Spell must be prepared |
| **Bard** | Spell must be known |
| **Warlock** | Only with Book of Ancient Secrets invocation (Pact of the Tome) |
| **Sorcerer** | **CANNOT ritual cast at all** |

### Mistakes People Make
- ❌ Sorcerers trying to ritual cast (they can't)
- ❌ Clerics ritual casting a spell they didn't prepare today
- ❌ Wizards thinking they need to prepare a ritual spell (they don't — just need it in the spellbook)
- ❌ Forgetting that ritual casting takes 10 extra minutes (not instant)

## 4. Material Components

**The Rule**: Spells with material (M) components require you to have the material OR a spellcasting focus. BUT if the material has a gold cost or is consumed, you MUST have the specific material.

### Examples
- *Shield*: "V, S" — no material needed, just verbal and somatic
- *Chromatic Orb*: "V, S, M (a diamond worth at least 50 gp)" — you MUST have the diamond. A focus doesn't replace it
- *Revivify*: "V, S, M (diamonds worth 300 gp, which the spell consumes)" — you need the diamonds AND they're destroyed when you cast

### Mistakes People Make
- ❌ Using a focus to replace a component with a gold cost
- ❌ Forgetting that consumed components are gone after casting
- ❌ Not tracking expensive material components (diamonds for *Revivify*, etc.)

## 5. Spell Attacks vs. Saving Throws

**The Rule**: Spells either require an attack roll (you roll to hit) OR force a saving throw (the target rolls to resist). Never both.

### Mistakes People Make
- ❌ Rolling to hit AND asking for a saving throw on the same spell
- ❌ Adding your spellcasting modifier to damage on spells that don't say to (most spells don't — *Eldritch Blast* with Agonizing Blast is an exception)
- ❌ Forgetting that spell attack rolls can critically hit (natural 20 = double damage dice)

## 6. Upcasting

**The Rule**: You can cast a spell using a higher-level slot than required. Some spells gain extra effects when upcast; others don't.

### Mistakes People Make
- ❌ Assuming all spells get better when upcast (many don't — check the spell description for "At Higher Levels")
- ❌ Forgetting that warlock slots are always at max level, so spells are always upcast
- ✅ *Cure Wounds* at 2nd level: 2d8 + WIS (gains 1d8 per level)
- ❌ *Shield* at 2nd level: still +5 AC (no upcast benefit)

## 7. Counterspell

**The Rule**: As a reaction, you attempt to interrupt a creature casting a spell. If the spell is the same level or lower than your *Counterspell* slot, it automatically fails. If higher, you make an ability check (DC 10 + spell level).

### Mistakes People Make
- ❌ Counterspelling a spell you can't see being cast (you must see the creature casting)
- ❌ Forgetting the ability check for higher-level spells
- ❌ Not knowing that *Counterspell* can itself be counterspelled (unless cast with Subtle Spell)
- ❌ Bards forgetting that Jack of All Trades applies to the Counterspell ability check

---

## 8. Prepared vs. Known Spells

### Prepared Casters (Cleric, Druid, Wizard)
- Choose which spells to prepare each long rest
- Can cast any prepared spell using any available slot
- Casting a spell does NOT remove it from your prepared list
- **Wizard special**: Prepares from spellbook, not the full wizard list

### Known Casters (Bard, Sorcerer, Warlock)
- Know a fixed number of spells (see class table)
- Can swap ONE spell per level-up
- Cannot change spells on a long rest

### Mistakes People Make
- ❌ Thinking casting a prepared spell "uses it up" (it doesn't — you use a slot, not the spell)
- ❌ Bards/sorcerers trying to change spells on a long rest (only prepared casters can)
- ❌ Wizards thinking they prepare from the full wizard list (only from your spellbook)

## 9. Opportunity Attacks and Spells

**The Rule**: Casting a ranged spell within 5 feet of a hostile creature gives you disadvantage on the attack roll. Moving away from an enemy provokes an opportunity attack (but forced movement like *Repelling Blast* does NOT).

### Mistakes People Make
- ❌ Forgetting disadvantage on ranged spell attacks in melee
- ❌ Thinking that casting a spell provokes an opportunity attack (it doesn't, unless you use the War Caster feat to cast a spell AS your opportunity attack)
- ❌ Thinking forced movement (push, pull, teleport) provokes opportunity attacks (it doesn't)

## 10. "Targets" vs. "Area of Effect"

**The Rule**: Some features (like Twinned Spell) require a spell that "targets only one creature." AoE spells like *Fireball* target a point, not creatures — even if only one creature is in the area.

### Mistakes People Make
- ❌ Trying to Twin *Fireball* (it targets a point, not a creature)
- ❌ Trying to Twin *Ice Knife* (it targets one creature but has an AoE explosion)
- ✅ Twinning *Haste* (targets one creature) — legal
- ✅ Twinning *Hold Person* at base level (targets one creature) — legal
