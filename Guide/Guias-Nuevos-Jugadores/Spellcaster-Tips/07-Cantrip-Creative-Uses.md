# 🕯️ Cantrip Creative Uses — Beyond Combat

Cantrips cost nothing. Use them constantly. Here are non-obvious uses for the most common cantrips.

---

## Prestidigitation (Bard, Sorcerer, Warlock, Wizard)

The Swiss army knife of cantrips. 6 effects, all free:

1. **Warm or chill food/drink**: Serve hot meals in the wilderness. Chill drinks in a tavern. Instant hospitality = better NPC reactions
2. **Clean anything instantly**: Covered in blood after a fight? Clean yourself before entering town. Clean a crime scene. Clean a disgusting dungeon room to find hidden details
3. **Create a sensory effect**: A puff of wind, a shower of sparks, a musical note. Use as a **distraction** — guards look toward the sound while you sneak past
4. **Light or snuff a candle/torch**: Light campfires without tinderboxes. Snuff out enemy torches from 10 feet away to create darkness
5. **Create a trinket or illusory image**: Forge a fake coin, a noble's signet ring impression, or a "wanted poster" to bluff your way past guards. Lasts until end of your next turn
6. **Flavor or color objects**: Make poison taste like wine. Make a plain cloak look like a guard's uniform (for 1 hour). Mark objects invisibly so you can track them

---

## Thaumaturgy (Cleric)

The cleric's intimidation toolkit:

1. **Boom your voice 3× louder**: Instant authority. Address a crowd, intimidate a prisoner, or call for help across a battlefield
2. **Slam doors open/shut**: Dramatic entrances. Lock a door shut (briefly) to slow pursuers. Open a door from 30 feet away to check for traps
3. **Cause tremors**: Convince superstitious NPCs that the gods are angry. Shake items off shelves as a distraction
4. **Change eye appearance**: Glowing red eyes during an Intimidation check = advantage (at DM discretion). Roleplay your divine connection
5. **Cause flames to flicker/change color**: Signal allies with colored flames. Make a campfire flare up to scare animals. Create an ominous atmosphere for negotiations

---

## Druidcraft (Druid)

Nature's utility cantrip:

1. **Predict weather**: Know if a storm is coming in the next 24 hours. Plan travel, warn the party, or use it to justify taking shelter (and a short rest)
2. **Instantly bloom a flower**: Bribe a nature spirit, impress a druid NPC, or create a trail of flowers to mark your path
3. **Light or snuff small flames**: Same as Prestidigitation — light campfires, snuff torches
4. **Create a tiny sensory effect**: A bird call to signal allies. The sound of a predator to scare off animals. A gust of wind to scatter papers on a desk
5. **Identify plants**: Not explicitly stated, but many DMs allow Druidcraft to help identify whether a plant is edible, poisonous, or magical

---

## Minor Illusion (Bard, Sorcerer, Warlock, Wizard)

The most abusable cantrip in the game:

1. **Create a box to hide in**: A 5-foot cube illusion of a crate, barrel, or rock. Crouch inside it. Enemies must use an action to investigate (INT check vs. your spell DC)
2. **Fake sounds**: A guard's voice calling "all clear." A monster's roar to scare NPCs. A baby crying to lure someone into a room
3. **Fake a wall**: Block a corridor with an illusory wall. Enemies who don't investigate walk into it and realize it's fake — but that costs them time
4. **Create a distraction**: An illusory gold coin on the ground. A fake person standing in a doorway. A sound of footsteps running the other direction
5. **Fake evidence**: An illusory document, a fake wanted poster, a "magical rune" on a door to scare enemies away. Lasts 1 minute — long enough for most bluffs

---

## Mage Hand (Bard, Sorcerer, Warlock, Wizard)

A 30-foot invisible assistant:

1. **Trigger traps from safety**: Use the hand to open chests, pull levers, or step on pressure plates from 30 feet away
2. **Pickpocket**: The hand is invisible (Arcane Trickster version) or at least not obviously connected to you. Slip a key off a guard's belt
3. **Pass items secretly**: Hand a note or potion to an ally across a room without anyone noticing
4. **Manipulate the environment**: Pull a rug out from under someone. Open a window latch from outside. Turn a doorknob. Knock items off a shelf as a distraction
5. **Retrieve items from dangerous places**: Grab a key from a pool of acid. Pick up a glowing object that might be cursed. Reach into a narrow crack

## Sacred Flame (Cleric)

More than just damage:

1. **Ignores cover**: The target must succeed on a DEX save — the flame descends from above. Enemies behind half or three-quarters cover get no AC benefit
2. **Radiant damage**: Very few creatures resist radiant. Excellent against undead and fiends
3. **Light source**: The flame briefly illuminates the area — useful for spotting hidden enemies in darkness

---

## Vicious Mockery (Bard)

The bard's signature:

1. **Disadvantage on next attack**: The damage (1d4) is terrible, but the disadvantage on the target's next attack roll is incredible. Use it on the enemy about to attack your low-HP ally
2. **Roleplay gold**: You must verbally mock the target. Creative insults make the table laugh and the DM might give inspiration
3. **No attack roll**: It's a WIS save — works against high-AC enemies that are hard to hit with weapon attacks

---

## Eldritch Blast (Warlock)

Not just damage — with invocations:

1. **Repelling Blast as crowd control**: Push enemies into hazards, off ledges, away from downed allies, or out of melee range of your squishy caster
2. **Multiple beams = multiple pushes**: At level 5, 2 beams = push one enemy 20 feet, or push two enemies 10 feet each. At level 11, 3 beams
3. **Forced movement doesn't provoke opportunity attacks**: Pushing an enemy with Repelling Blast is safe — they don't get a free swing at your allies as they move

---

## Guidance (Cleric, Druid)

The most forgotten powerful cantrip:

1. **+1d4 to any ability check**: Picking a lock? Guidance. Persuading a guard? Guidance. Climbing a wall? Guidance. Investigating a room? Guidance
2. **Cast it before EVERY ability check**: It's free, it's a cantrip, and +1d4 (avg 2.5) is significant at low levels. The only cost is concentration (1 minute)
3. **Remind your DM**: Many groups forget to use Guidance. Make it a habit: "Before I roll, I cast Guidance on myself/the rogue"

---

## Toll the Dead (Cleric, Warlock, Wizard)

The damage cantrip people underestimate:

1. **1d12 against damaged targets**: If the target has taken ANY damage, it's 1d12 instead of 1d8. That's the highest cantrip damage die in the game
2. **WIS save, not attack roll**: Works against high-AC enemies. Pairs well with *Sacred Flame* (DEX save) — use whichever save the enemy is weaker against
3. **Necrotic damage**: Rarely resisted. Good against most creature types
