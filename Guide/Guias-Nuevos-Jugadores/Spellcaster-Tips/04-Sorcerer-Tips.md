# 🔥 Sorcerer — New Player Tips (Levels 1–5)

> **Role**: Blaster, controller, face (CHA-based)
> **Casting Stat**: Charisma | **System**: Spells Known (swap 1/level-up)
> **Spell Slots**: Full caster progression, recover on long rest
> **Focus**: Arcane focus (crystal, orb, rod, staff, wand)
> **Unique Resource**: Sorcery Points (fuel Metamagic)

---

## 1. Creative Uses for Class Features

### Metamagic (3rd Level)
- **Twinned Haste**: At level 5, spend 3 Sorcery Points to *Haste* two allies simultaneously. Two fighters with double attacks is encounter-ending
- **Twinned Healing Word**: In emergencies, pick up TWO downed allies with one bonus action spell (costs 1 SP for a 1st-level spell)
- **Subtle Spell + Counterspell**: Counterspell normally has verbal/somatic components, so it can itself be counterspelled. Subtle Spell removes those components — your Counterspell is uncounterable
- **Subtle Spell in social encounters**: Cast *Suggestion* or *Charm Person* without anyone seeing you cast. No verbal or somatic components = no one knows magic happened
- **Quickened Spell + cantrip**: Cast *Fireball* as a bonus action (2 SP), then *Fire Bolt* as your action. Two damage sources in one turn
- **Heightened Spell on save-or-suck**: Spend 3 SP to give the boss disadvantage on their save against *Hold Person* or *Banishment*. Expensive but devastating when it lands

### Font of Magic (2nd Level)
- **Morning conversion**: Before adventuring, convert your 1st-level slots into Sorcery Points. You probably won't cast many 1st-level spells at level 5 — those points fuel Metamagic
- **Emergency slot creation**: Out of 3rd-level slots? Spend 5 SP to create one. It's expensive but can save the party
- **Never convert high-level slots**: The SP-to-slot ratio is terrible. A 3rd-level slot gives 3 SP, but creating a 3rd-level slot costs 5 SP. Only convert slots you won't cast

### Draconic Bloodline (1st Level)
- **Free Mage Armor**: Draconic Resilience gives AC 13 + DEX. That's equal to *Mage Armor* without spending a spell known or a slot. Take a different spell instead
- **Extra HP**: +1 HP per level effectively makes your d6 a d8. You're as tough as a warlock or bard
- **Choose Fire ancestry**: Red/Gold Dragon gives fire damage type. Fire has the most spell support (*Burning Hands*, *Scorching Ray*, *Fireball*). At level 6, Elemental Affinity adds CHA to fire damage

## 2. Spell Selection Tips (Levels 1–5)

### Always Take These
| Spell | Level | Why |
|-------|-------|-----|
| *Shield* | 1st | +5 AC as a reaction. Saves your life repeatedly. Non-negotiable |
| *Mage Armor* | 1st | AC 13 + DEX. **Skip if Draconic Bloodline** (you already have this) |
| *Misty Step* | 2nd | Bonus action teleport 30 feet. Escape grapples, get out of melee, cross gaps |
| *Web* | 2nd | Restrained in a 20-foot cube. Excellent control, and restrained gives advantage on attacks |
| *Fireball* | 3rd | 8d6 in a 20-foot radius. The gold standard of AoE damage. Twinned doesn't work (AoE), but Empowered does |

### Avoid These
| Spell | Why |
|-------|-----|
| *True Strike* | Wastes your action for advantage on one attack. Terrible |
| *Witch Bolt* | 1d12 initial, then 1d12/turn — but requires concentration AND your action each turn. *Scorching Ray* does more damage immediately |
| *Chromatic Orb* | Requires a 50 gp diamond component. At level 1, you probably can't afford it. *Magic Missile* is more reliable |

### Spell Known Budget Warning
> Sorcerers know the **fewest spells of any full caster** (max 15). Every spell choice matters. Prioritize versatile spells that work in many situations over niche picks.

## 3. Common Sorcerer Mistakes

1. **Burning all Sorcery Points on extra spell slots**: SP are for Metamagic first, emergency slots second. Creating a 1st-level slot costs 2 SP — that's a Twinned cantrip or a Subtle Spell you're giving up
2. **Forgetting sorcerers can't ritual cast**: Every spell costs a slot. If you need *Detect Magic* regularly, consider taking it and accepting the slot cost, or let the wizard/bard handle it
3. **Taking too many damage spells**: You only know ~6 spells at level 5. If 4 of them are damage, you have no utility. Take 2 damage spells, 2 control spells, and 2 defensive/utility spells
4. **Not using Metamagic every combat**: You have Sorcery Points for a reason. If you're not using Metamagic, you're just a worse wizard. Twinned and Subtle should be used almost every fight
5. **Forgetting concentration limits**: *Web* and *Hold Person* both require concentration. You can't use both. Pick one per fight

## 4. Combat Tactics Cheat Sheet (Levels 1–5)

### Level 1–2: Glass Cannon
**Round 1**: *Sleep* (auto-incapacitates low-HP enemies, no save) or *Burning Hands* (AoE cone)
**Round 2+**: Cantrips (*Fire Bolt* = 1d10, best damage cantrip) → *Shield* as reaction if attacked
**Positioning**: Stay behind the frontline. You have d6 HP and no armor (unless Draconic)

### Level 3–4: Metamagic Online
**Round 1**: *Web* (restrained, advantage for allies) or Twinned *Chromatic Orb*/*Scorching Ray*
**Round 2+**: Cantrip or Twinned cantrip (1 SP) → *Shield* as reaction
**Subtle Spell**: Use in social encounters for *Suggestion* or *Charm Person* without detection

### Level 5: The Power Spike
**Round 1**: *Fireball* (8d6 AoE) + Empowered Spell (reroll bad damage dice, 1 SP)
**Alternative**: Twinned *Haste* on two martial allies (3 SP) — this wins fights
**Quickened combo**: Quickened *Scorching Ray* (bonus action, 2 SP) → *Fire Bolt* cantrip (action)
**Control**: *Hypnotic Pattern* or Heightened *Hold Person* (3 SP for disadvantage on save)

### Golden Rule
> **You are a sorcerer, not a wizard.** You know fewer spells but can twist them with Metamagic. A Twinned *Haste* or Subtle *Counterspell* is worth more than having 10 extra spells in a book.

## 5. Spell Slot Management

- **Levels 1–2**: 2–3 slots. You're fragile — save one slot for *Shield*. Use cantrips (*Fire Bolt*) as your main damage
- **Level 3–4**: 4–6 slots + 3–4 Sorcery Points. Convert one 1st-level slot to SP in the morning if you don't expect to need it. That's fuel for Twinned Spell
- **Level 5**: 7 slots + 5 SP. Big fights: *Fireball* or Twinned *Haste*. Medium fights: Twinned cantrips (1 SP each). Always keep 1 slot for *Shield*
- **Sorcery Point economy**: At level 5, you have 5 SP. Twinned *Haste* costs 3, leaving 2 for a Subtle Spell or Quickened cantrip. Plan your SP budget before the adventuring day
- **Convert leftover 1st-level slots**: At level 5, your 1st-level slots are mostly for *Shield*. If you have extras, convert them to SP (1 SP each) for more Metamagic fuel
- **No short rest recovery**: Unlike warlocks and some other classes, sorcerers get nothing back on a short rest (until Sorcerous Restoration at level 20 in PHB, or level 5 in 2024 rules). Pace yourself
