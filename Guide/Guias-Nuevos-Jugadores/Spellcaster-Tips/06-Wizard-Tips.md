# 📖 Wizard — New Player Tips (Levels 1–5)

> **Role**: Controller, blaster, utility, ritual caster
> **Casting Stat**: Intelligence | **System**: Spellbook (prepare daily from your book)
> **Spell Slots**: Full caster progression, recover on long rest + Arcane Recovery
> **Focus**: Arcane focus (crystal, orb, rod, staff, wand)

---

## 1. Creative Uses for Class Features

### Spellbook & Ritual Casting
- **Ritual cast without preparing**: Wizards can ritual cast ANY spell in their spellbook, even if it's not prepared. This is unique — clerics and druids must have rituals prepared. Copy every ritual spell you find
- **Copy spells from scrolls and other spellbooks**: Every wizard spell you find can be added to your book (2 hours + 50 gp per spell level). Your spellbook grows far beyond the 2 free spells per level
- **Backup your spellbook**: Copying your own spells costs 1 hour + 10 gp per level. Losing your spellbook is catastrophic — keep a backup in a safe place
- **Trade spells with other wizards**: If another wizard is in the party or you meet an NPC wizard, you can copy from each other's books. Both benefit

### Arcane Recovery (1st Level)
- **Use it every day**: Once per day on a short rest, recover spell slots with combined level ≤ half your wizard level (rounded up). At level 5, that's 3 levels — a 3rd-level slot or a 2nd + 1st
- **Recover your best slot**: At level 5, recover a 3rd-level slot to get a second *Fireball* or *Counterspell*. Don't waste it on 1st-level slots unless you really need *Shield*
- **Time it right**: Use Arcane Recovery after the first big fight of the day, not at the end. You want those slots for the rest of the adventuring day

### School of Evocation (2nd Level)
- **Sculpt Spells**: When you cast an evocation spell (like *Fireball*), choose a number of creatures equal to 1 + spell level. They automatically succeed on their save AND take no damage. You can *Fireball* your own melee allies safely
- **This changes Fireball from "careful positioning" to "nuke everything"**: At level 5, Sculpt Spells protects up to 4 creatures. Drop *Fireball* on the enemy cluster even if your fighter is in the middle

### School of Divination (2nd Level)
- **Portent**: Roll 2d20 after each long rest. You can replace ANY attack roll, saving throw, or ability check (yours or someone else's) with one of these rolls. Rolled a 3? Force the boss to fail their save against *Hold Person*. Rolled an 18? Give it to the rogue for a critical Sneak Attack
- **This is arguably the strongest 2nd-level subclass feature in the game**: Two guaranteed outcomes per day. Use the low rolls on enemy saves, high rolls on ally attacks

## 2. Spell Selection Tips (Levels 1–5)

### Always Prepare These
| Spell | Level | Why |
|-------|-------|-----|
| *Shield* | 1st | +5 AC as a reaction until your next turn. You have d6 HP — this keeps you alive |
| *Mage Armor* | 1st | AC 13 + DEX. Cast once per day (8 hours). Non-negotiable unless you have another AC source |
| *Misty Step* | 2nd | Bonus action teleport 30 feet. Escape grapples, reposition, cross gaps. No verbal component needed |
| *Web* | 2nd | 20-foot cube of restrained enemies. Excellent control, lasts 1 hour, and restrained gives advantage on attacks |
| *Fireball* | 3rd | 8d6 fire in a 20-foot radius. The benchmark AoE damage spell. Learn it at level 5, prepare it always |

### Also Excellent (Keep in Spellbook)
| Spell | Level | Why |
|-------|-------|-----|
| *Find Familiar* | 1st (ritual) | Owl familiar: flyby Help action for advantage, scouting, delivering touch spells. Ritual = no slot cost |
| *Detect Magic* | 1st (ritual) | Ritual cast for free. Always have it in your book |
| *Counterspell* | 3rd | Shut down enemy casters. Reaction, no save. Essential |
| *Hypnotic Pattern* | 3rd | AoE incapacitate. Alternative to *Fireball* when you want to disable instead of damage |

### Avoid These
| Spell | Why |
|-------|-----|
| *True Strike* | Wastes your action. Always terrible |
| *Burning Hands* | 3d6 in a 15-foot cone. *Fireball* at level 5 does 8d6 in a 20-foot sphere. Just wait |
| *Magic Missile* (as a prepared spell) | Reliable but low damage. Keep it in your spellbook for emergencies, but don't prepare it daily |

## 3. Common Wizard Mistakes

1. **Not copying found spells**: Every scroll and spellbook you find is an opportunity. The wizard's power scales with spellbook size. Spend the gold — it's the best investment in the game
2. **Preparing only damage spells**: Wizards have the best spell list in the game. If you prepare 6 damage spells and 0 utility/control, you're wasting your class's strength. Mix damage, control, and utility
3. **Forgetting ritual casting from the spellbook**: You don't need to prepare *Detect Magic*, *Find Familiar*, *Comprehend Languages*, etc. Just have them in your book and ritual cast (10 extra minutes, no slot)
4. **Standing in the front**: You have d6 HP and no armor (without *Mage Armor*). Stay behind the frontline. Use 120-foot range spells. If enemies reach you, *Misty Step* or *Shield*
5. **Not using Arcane Recovery**: It's free spell slots once per day. Use it every single day. At level 5, recovering a 3rd-level slot is like getting a free *Fireball*

## 4. Combat Tactics Cheat Sheet (Levels 1–5)

### Level 1–2: Careful Positioning
**Before combat**: Cast *Mage Armor* (lasts 8 hours) and *Find Familiar* (ritual, owl recommended)
**Round 1**: *Sleep* (auto-incapacitates, no save, amazing at level 1) or *Thunderwave* (if surrounded)
**Round 2+**: Cantrips (*Fire Bolt* = 1d10, or *Toll the Dead* = 1d12 vs damaged targets)
**Reaction**: *Shield* (+5 AC) when attacked. This is your lifeline
**Familiar**: Owl uses flyby Help action → you attack with advantage

### Level 3–4: Control Wizard
**Round 1**: *Web* (restrained, 20-foot cube) or *Levitate* (remove one melee enemy from the fight entirely)
**Round 2+**: Cantrip on restrained/disadvantaged targets → familiar Help action
**Arcane Recovery**: After first fight, recover a 2nd-level slot on short rest

### Level 5: Artillery Mode
**Round 1**: *Fireball* (8d6 AoE) — Evocation wizards: Sculpt Spells to protect allies in the blast
**Alternative**: *Hypnotic Pattern* (AoE incapacitate) for disabling instead of damaging
**Round 2+**: *Counterspell* enemy casters (reaction) → cantrip or *Scorching Ray* (action)
**Divination wizard**: Use low Portent roll to force boss to fail a save. Use high roll on ally's attack

### Golden Rule
> **Control the battlefield first, deal damage second.** A *Web* that restrains 4 enemies is worth more than a *Fireball* that damages 4 enemies. Restrained enemies give advantage to your entire party for multiple rounds.

## 5. Spell Slot Management

- **Levels 1–2**: 2–3 slots. *Mage Armor* before the day starts (lasts 8 hours). *Sleep* in the first fight. Save 1 slot for *Shield*. Cantrips are your bread and butter
- **Level 3–4**: 4–6 slots. *Mage Armor* (1 slot for the day), *Web* in big fights, save 1–2 for *Shield*. Arcane Recovery: get back a 2nd-level slot on short rest
- **Level 5**: 7 slots + Arcane Recovery (3 levels). *Mage Armor* (1 slot), *Fireball*/*Hypnotic Pattern* in big fights (3rd-level slots), *Web* in medium fights (2nd-level), *Shield* for defense (1st-level). Recover a 3rd-level slot on short rest
- **Ritual cast everything you can**: *Find Familiar*, *Detect Magic*, *Comprehend Languages*, *Identify* — never spend a slot on these unless you're in combat
- **Arcane Recovery priority**: Always recover your highest available slot. A 3rd-level slot is worth more than three 1st-level slots
- **Find Familiar is permanent**: Cast it once (ritual, no slot), and your familiar persists until killed. If it dies, re-summon during a short rest. The owl's flyby Help action is free advantage every round
