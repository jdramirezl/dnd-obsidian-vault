# 👁️ Warlock — New Player Tips (Levels 1–5)

> **Role**: Sustained damage, utility, short-rest powerhouse
> **Casting Stat**: Charisma | **System**: Pact Magic (all slots same level, short rest recovery)
> **Spell Slots**: 1–2 slots that recharge on short rest (NOT full caster progression)
> **Focus**: Arcane focus
> **Unique**: Eldritch Invocations (at-will abilities)

---

## 1. Creative Uses for Class Features

### Eldritch Blast + Invocations
- **Repelling Blast + cliff/hazard**: Each beam pushes 10 feet. At level 5 you have 2 beams = 20 feet of push. Shove enemies off cliffs, into *Spike Growth*, into campfires, or off bridges
- **Repelling Blast + prone ally**: Push an enemy away from a downed party member so they can't make melee attacks (which auto-crit on unconscious targets within 5 feet)
- **Agonizing Blast makes EB your "weapon"**: With CHA 16, *Eldritch Blast* deals 1d10+3 per beam. At level 5, that's 2d10+6 (avg 17) — better than most martial weapons, at 120-foot range, for free
- **Eldritch Spear + Agonizing Blast**: 300-foot range with full damage. Snipe from positions enemies can't reach

### Pact Boons (3rd Level)
- **Pact of the Chain — Invisible scout**: An imp can turn invisible at will and has 120-foot darkvision. Send it ahead to scout every room. With Voice of the Chain Master, you see through its eyes from any distance on the same plane
- **Pact of the Chain — Help action**: Your familiar can use the Help action to give you (or an ally) advantage on an attack roll. An invisible imp giving Help can't be targeted by most enemies
- **Pact of the Tome — Cantrip theft**: Choose 3 cantrips from ANY class. Take *Guidance* (cleric, +1d4 to ability checks), *Shillelagh* (druid, CHA-based melee), or *Spare the Dying* (cleric, stabilize at range)
- **Pact of the Tome + Book of Ancient Secrets**: You become the party's ritual caster. Copy *Find Familiar*, *Detect Magic*, *Identify*, *Comprehend Languages* — all castable without spell slots
- **Pact of the Blade — Summon any weapon**: Need a ranged weapon? Summon a javelin. Need reach? Summon a halberd. The weapon is always magical for overcoming resistance

### Dark One's Blessing (Fiend, 1st Level)
- **Temp HP on every kill**: CHA mod + warlock level. At level 5 with CHA 16, that's 8 temp HP per kill. In fights with multiple enemies, you're constantly refreshing a buffer
- **Finish off weakened enemies**: Prioritize killing blows on low-HP enemies to trigger the temp HP. Let allies soften targets, then you finish them off with *Eldritch Blast*

### Mask of Many Faces (Invocation)
- **At-will Disguise Self**: Become anyone, anywhere, unlimited times. Perfect for infiltration, social encounters, and avoiding consequences
- **No concentration**: Unlike many illusion spells, *Disguise Self* doesn't require concentration

## 2. Spell Selection Tips (Levels 1–5)

### Always Take These
| Spell | Level | Why |
|-------|-------|-----|
| *Hex* | 1st | +1d6 necrotic to every hit. With 2 *Eldritch Blast* beams at level 5, that's +2d6 per round. Lasts 1 hour, transfers on kill. No-brainer |
| *Armor of Agathys* | 1st | 5 temp HP + 5 cold damage to melee attackers per slot level. At 3rd-level slot: 15 temp HP + 15 cold damage. Scales amazingly because all warlock slots are max level |
| *Misty Step* | 2nd | Bonus action teleport. Escape danger, reposition, cross gaps |
| *Counterspell* | 3rd | Shut down enemy casters. Essential at level 5+ |
| *Hypnotic Pattern* | 3rd | AoE incapacitate. Ends encounters. Cast it, then *Eldritch Blast* the survivors |

### Avoid These
| Spell | Why |
|-------|-----|
| *Witch Bolt* | Requires concentration (competes with *Hex*), uses your action each turn, and only does 1d12/turn. *Eldritch Blast* + *Hex* does more damage for free |
| *Charm Person* | Too situational for your tiny spell list. Use *Mask of Many Faces* for social manipulation instead |
| *Comprehend Languages* | Take *Eyes of the Rune Keeper* invocation instead (read all writing, at will, no slot) |

### Warlock Spell Budget Warning
> You know very few spells (6 at level 5) and have only 2 slots. Every spell must justify its existence. If an invocation can do the same thing at will, take the invocation instead.

## 3. Common Warlock Mistakes

1. **Not taking short rests**: Your slots recharge on short rest. If the party takes 0 short rests, you effectively have 2 spell slots per day. Push for short rests — you need them more than anyone
2. **Forgetting Eldritch Blast is your main action**: Between spell slots, *Eldritch Blast* + Agonizing Blast is your "weapon." It's not a fallback — it's your primary damage source. Spells are for special occasions
3. **Not taking Agonizing Blast**: Without it, *Eldritch Blast* deals 1d10 (avg 5.5). With it at CHA 16, it deals 1d10+3 (avg 8.5). That's a 55% damage increase for free. Take it at level 2
4. **Casting Hex every fight**: *Hex* costs a spell slot and you only have 2. In easy fights, just *Eldritch Blast* without *Hex*. Save slots for hard fights or utility spells
5. **Forgetting all slots are the same level**: At level 5, both your slots are 3rd-level. *Armor of Agathys* at 3rd level gives 15 temp HP. *Hex* at 3rd level lasts 8 hours (no need to recast between fights). This is a feature, not a bug

## 4. Combat Tactics Cheat Sheet (Levels 1–5)

### Level 1–2: Blast and Hex
**Round 1**: *Hex* (bonus action, concentration) → *Eldritch Blast* (action, +1d6 from Hex)
**Round 2+**: *Eldritch Blast* (action) → move *Hex* if target dies (bonus action)
**Easy fights**: Skip *Hex*, just *Eldritch Blast*. Save the slot

### Level 3–4: Pact Boon Online
**Chain**: Send imp to scout → Help action for advantage → *Eldritch Blast* with advantage + *Hex*
**Tome**: Ritual cast utility spells between fights → combat same as above
**Blade**: Melee attack (2 with Thirsting Blade at 5th) → *Hex* for +1d6 per hit

### Level 5: Double Beam
**Round 1**: *Hex* (bonus action) → *Eldritch Blast* (2 beams, each +1d6 Hex + CHA from Agonizing Blast)
**Damage per round**: 2×(1d10 + 3 + 1d6) = avg 23 damage, at will, 120-foot range
**Big fight opener**: *Hypnotic Pattern* (incapacitate a group) → *Eldritch Blast* survivors
**Repelling Blast**: Push enemies into hazards or away from downed allies (10 ft per beam = 20 ft total)

### Golden Rule
> ***Eldritch Blast* is your sword. Spell slots are your grenades.** Use grenades for special occasions. Swing your sword every turn.

## 5. Spell Slot Management

- **You have 2 slots. That's it (until level 11).** Treat every slot like gold
- **Short rest = full recharge**: After every fight, push for a short rest. Two short rests per day means 6 effective spell slots instead of 2
- **Hex lasts 1 hour (3rd-level slot: 8 hours)**: At level 5, cast *Hex* once and it can last all day if you maintain concentration. That's one slot for an entire adventuring day of +1d6 per hit
- **Armor of Agathys scales with slot level**: At level 5, it's 15 temp HP + 15 cold damage per hit. Cast it before a fight you know will be melee-heavy
- **Invocations are free**: *Mask of Many Faces*, *Misty Visions*, *Devil's Sight* — these cost nothing. Use them constantly. They're your real utility toolkit
- **Save one slot for emergencies**: If you have 2 slots, spend 1 on *Hex* or *Armor of Agathys*, keep 1 for *Counterspell*, *Misty Step*, or a clutch *Hypnotic Pattern*
