# 🎵 Bard — New Player Tips (Levels 1–5)

> **Role**: Support caster, skill expert, party face, off-healer
> **Casting Stat**: Charisma | **System**: Spells Known (swap 1/level-up)
> **Spell Slots**: Full caster progression, recover on long rest
> **Focus**: Musical instrument

---

## 1. Creative Uses for Class Features

### Bardic Inspiration (1st Level)
- **Save a friend's saving throw**: Inspiration works on ability checks, attack rolls, AND saving throws — give it to the fighter about to get hit by a dragon's breath weapon
- **Boost a grapple check**: Grappling is a contested ability check — your d6 can tip the scales for the party's wrestler
- **Feed the Paladin**: Give Inspiration to a Paladin before they attack — if they hit, they can choose to Divine Smite. Your die helps guarantee the hit that triggers massive damage
- **Rogue insurance**: Give it to the Rogue before their turn — if their attack misses, they lose Sneak Attack damage for the whole round. Your die prevents that
- **Don't hoard it**: At 5th level, Font of Inspiration recharges uses on short rest. Use them every combat

### Jack of All Trades (2nd Level)
- **Better initiative**: Initiative is a Dexterity *check* — Jack of All Trades adds +1 at levels 2–8
- **Better Counterspell**: The ability check to counter a higher-level spell benefits from Jack of All Trades — bards are secretly the best counterspellers in the game
- **Improvised tool use**: Trying to pick a lock without thieves' tools proficiency? You still add +1
- **Dispel Magic checks**: Same as Counterspell — your half-proficiency applies

### Song of Rest (2nd Level)
- **Free party healing**: Costs nothing — no action, no slot, no resource. Just perform during a short rest
- **Scales with party size**: Every ally who spends Hit Dice gets the bonus. In a 4-person party, that's 4×1d6 = ~14 extra HP per short rest
- **Remind your DM**: Many groups forget this feature exists. Speak up every short rest

### College of Lore (3rd Level)
- **Cutting Words on damage rolls**: Most people use it on attack rolls, but subtracting from a dragon's damage roll can save a downed ally from death saves
- **Cutting Words is reactive**: Unlike Bardic Inspiration (proactive, bonus action), Cutting Words uses your reaction — you can do both in the same round
- **6 skill proficiencies at level 3**: More than any other class. You are the skill monkey

## 2. Spell Selection Tips (Levels 1–5)

### Always Prepare These
| Spell | Level | Why |
|-------|-------|-----|
| *Healing Word* | 1st | **Bonus action** ranged healing — pick up downed allies while still casting a cantrip |
| *Faerie Fire* | 1st | Advantage on attacks for the whole party vs. affected creatures. No concentration-free alternative exists at this level |
| *Dissonant Whispers* | 1st | Forces movement = provokes opportunity attacks from your melee allies. Devastating action economy |
| *Heat Metal* | 2nd | Auto-damage every turn, no save, disadvantage on attacks. Destroys armored enemies |
| *Hypnotic Pattern* | 3rd | AoE incapacitate — can end encounters. One of the best control spells in the game |

### Avoid These
| Spell | Why |
|-------|-----|
| *True Strike* | Wastes your action to get advantage on ONE attack next turn. Just attack twice instead |
| *Friends* | The target knows you charmed them when it ends. Almost always backfires |
| *Heroism* | Temp HP that doesn't stack, requires concentration. *Bless* (via Magical Secrets) is better in every way |

## 3. Common Bard Mistakes

1. **Forgetting Bardic Inspiration is a bonus action**: You can cast a spell AND give Inspiration in the same turn. Do both
2. **Hoarding Inspiration dice**: Especially after level 5 when they recharge on short rest — use them every fight
3. **Not swapping spells on level-up**: Bards can replace ONE known spell each level. Drop spells that aren't working
4. **Casting Cure Wounds instead of Healing Word**: Cure Wounds is an action and touch range. Healing Word is a bonus action at 60 feet. Always take Healing Word
5. **Forgetting ritual casting**: Bards can ritual-cast known spells with the ritual tag (Detect Magic, Speak with Animals) without spending slots

## 4. Combat Tactics Cheat Sheet (Levels 1–5)

### Level 1–2: The Support Engine
**Round 1**: Cast *Faerie Fire* or *Dissonant Whispers* → Bonus action: give Bardic Inspiration to the biggest damage dealer
**Subsequent rounds**: Cantrip (Vicious Mockery for disadvantage on enemy attacks) → Bonus action: Inspiration or Healing Word if someone drops

### Level 3–4: Controller Online
**Round 1**: *Dissonant Whispers* or *Heat Metal* on armored enemy → Bonus action: Inspiration
**If outnumbered**: *Shatter* for AoE damage
**Emergency**: *Healing Word* (bonus action) + cantrip (action)

### Level 5: Full Power
**Round 1**: *Hypnotic Pattern* to disable a group → Bonus action: Inspiration
**Single target**: *Heat Metal* (no save, auto-damage each turn)
**Cleanup**: Cantrips + Inspiration every round
**Downed ally**: *Healing Word* at 2nd level for better healing + still act on your turn

### Golden Rule
> **Never spend your action on healing unless someone will die.** Use *Healing Word* (bonus action) and keep your action for spells or cantrips.

## 5. Spell Slot Management

- **Levels 1–2**: You have 2–3 slots. Use ONE on your best spell in combat, save one for *Healing Word* emergencies. Lean on *Vicious Mockery* (cantrip) for consistent value
- **Level 3–4**: 4–6 slots. You can afford to open with a leveled spell most combats. Still save 1 slot for emergency healing
- **Level 5**: 7 slots including 3rd-level. Open big fights with *Hypnotic Pattern*, use 1st-level slots for *Healing Word* and *Dissonant Whispers*
- **Song of Rest is free healing**: Push for short rests to stretch your party's HP without spending slots
- **Ritual cast when possible**: *Detect Magic*, *Speak with Animals* — never spend a slot on these outside combat
