# ⚔️ Combat & Exploration Quick Reference

> **Everything new players need at the table — actions, common mistakes, tactics, and exploration rules in one place.**

---

## 1. 🎯 Combat Actions Cheat Sheet

### Your Turn Budget

| Resource | How Many | Resets |
|----------|----------|--------|
| **Action** | 1 per turn | Every turn |
| **Bonus Action** | 1 per turn (only if a feature grants one) | Every turn |
| **Movement** | Up to your speed | Every turn |
| **Free Object Interaction** | 1 per turn | Every turn |
| **Reaction** | 1 per round | Start of your next turn |

### Standard Actions

| Action | What It Does | When to Use It |
|--------|-------------|----------------|
| **⚔️ Attack** | Make one melee or ranged attack (more with Extra Attack) | Default action for martial characters; your bread and butter |
| **🔮 Cast a Spell** | Cast a spell with a casting time of 1 action | When a spell is more impactful than a weapon attack |
| **🏃 Dash** | Gain extra movement equal to your speed (double total) | Closing distance, fleeing, or reaching an objective |
| **🛡️ Disengage** | Your movement doesn't provoke opportunity attacks this turn | Escaping melee without getting hit; retreating safely |
| **🔄 Dodge** | Attacks against you have disadvantage; Dex saves with advantage | When you're taking heavy fire and can't do anything better |
| **🤝 Help** | Give an ally advantage on their next ability check or attack roll | Setting up a rogue's Sneak Attack; helping with skill checks |
| **🫥 Hide** | Make a Dexterity (Stealth) check to become hidden | Rogues wanting Sneak Attack; archers breaking line of sight |
| **⏳ Ready** | Prepare an action with a trigger; uses your reaction when triggered | Waiting for an enemy to appear; coordinating with allies |
| **🔍 Search** | Make a Perception or Investigation check | Looking for hidden enemies, traps, or secret doors mid-combat |
| **🎒 Use an Object** | Use an item that requires an action (or interact with a 2nd object) | Drinking a potion, activating a magic item, using caltrops |

> [!tip] Reminder
> **Improvised actions** are always an option! Describe what you want to do — the DM will call for a roll. Kicking sand in someone's eyes, swinging from a chandelier, flipping a table for cover — all fair game.

### Bonus Actions (Only If You Have One)

You do NOT automatically get a bonus action. You only have one if a class feature, spell, or item grants it.

| Source | Bonus Action | Notes |
|--------|-------------|-------|
| **Two-Weapon Fighting** | Attack with off-hand light weapon | Don't add ability modifier to damage (unless negative) |
| **Rogue: Cunning Action** | Dash, Disengage, or Hide | Available every turn from level 2 |
| **Monk: Martial Arts** | Unarmed strike | After taking the Attack action |
| **Monk: Step of the Wind** | Dash or Disengage (costs 1 ki) | Also doubles jump distance |
| **Monk: Patient Defense** | Dodge (costs 1 ki) | Full Dodge as a bonus action |
| **Barbarian: Rage** | Enter rage | Advantage on Str checks/saves, bonus rage damage, resistance to B/P/S |
| **Bonus Action Spells** | [[Healing Word]], [[Misty Step]], [[Spiritual Weapon]], [[Shield of Faith]], etc. | ⚠️ If you cast a BA spell, you can only cast a cantrip with your action |
| **Paladin: Smite Spells** | Various smite spells | Cast as BA, apply on next weapon hit |
| **Ranger: Hunter's Mark** | Mark a target for extra 1d6 damage | Concentration; move to new target as BA when current dies |

### Reactions (One Per Round)

| Reaction | Trigger | Effect |
|----------|---------|--------|
| **Opportunity Attack** | Hostile creature you can see leaves your reach | One melee attack against them |
| **Readied Action** | Your chosen trigger occurs | Take the action you prepared |
| **[[Shield]] spell** | You are hit by an attack or targeted by magic missile | +5 AC until start of your next turn |
| **[[Counterspell]]** | A creature within 60 ft casts a spell | Attempt to negate the spell |
| **Paladin: [[Shield of Faith]]** | — | (Cast as bonus action, not reaction — but worth noting) |
| **Fighter: Riposte** (Battle Master) | Creature misses you with melee attack | Make a weapon attack + superiority die damage |
| **Monk: Deflect Missiles** | Hit by a ranged weapon attack | Reduce damage by 1d10 + Dex mod + monk level |

## 2. ❌ Common Combat Mistakes

### Opportunity Attacks

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Right |
|---------|---------|
| "I get an OA when the enemy moves near me" | OAs trigger when a creature **leaves** your reach, not enters it |
| "I use my OA to cast a spell" | OAs are specifically **one melee attack** — no spells (unless War Caster feat) |
| "The enemy Dashed away so I get an OA" | Dash doesn't prevent OAs — only **Disengage** does |
| "I was shoved so I get an OA" | Forced movement (shove, Thunderwave, etc.) does **NOT** provoke OAs |
| "I get multiple OAs per round" | You get **one reaction per round** — one OA maximum |

### Bonus Action Timing

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Right |
|---------|---------|
| "I always have a bonus action" | You only have one if a **feature specifically grants it** |
| "I cast Healing Word and Fireball" | If you cast a BA spell, your action spell must be a **cantrip** (casting time 1 action) |
| "I'll save my bonus action for later" | Bonus actions can only be used **on your turn** |
| "I use my bonus action to drink a potion" | Drinking a potion is the **Use an Object action** (your action, not bonus action) |

### Two-Weapon Fighting

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Right |
|---------|---------|
| "I attack with both weapons as my action" | Main hand = Attack action; off-hand = **bonus action** (separate) |
| "I add my Str/Dex to off-hand damage" | Off-hand damage has **no ability modifier** (unless negative or you have the Fighting Style) |
| "I dual-wield longswords" | Both weapons must have the **light** property (unless you have Dual Wielder feat) |
| "I get Extra Attack with both weapons" | Extra Attack applies to the Attack action only; off-hand is still **one bonus action attack** |

### Concentration Checks

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Right |
|---------|---------|
| "I'm concentrating on Bless and Spirit Guardians" | You can only concentrate on **one spell at a time** — casting a new one drops the old |
| "I took damage but my spell is fine" | Taking damage requires a **Con save** (DC = 10 or half damage, whichever is higher) |
| "I failed the save but keep the spell" | Failed concentration save = **spell ends immediately** |
| "Concentration is optional" | If a spell says "Concentration" in its duration, you **must** concentrate or it ends |

### Other Common Mistakes

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Right |
|---------|---------|
| "I move 30 ft, attack, then move 30 ft more" | You can split movement, but total can't exceed your **speed** (usually 30 ft) |
| "I attack the prone enemy with my bow" | Ranged attacks against prone targets have **disadvantage** (melee has advantage) |
| "Natural 20 on a skill check — auto success!" | Nat 20 auto-hit only applies to **attack rolls**, not ability checks or saves (RAW) |
| "I stabilize my friend as a bonus action" | Stabilizing (Medicine DC 10) costs your **action** |
| "I pick up my sword and draw my shield" | You get **one free object interaction** per turn — the second requires your action |

## 3. 🧠 Tactical Tips by Situation

### ⚔️ Outnumbered (More Enemies Than Allies)

- **Bottleneck**: Funnel enemies through doorways or narrow passages — only 1–2 can attack at a time
- **Dodge action**: Tanks in front using Dodge force disadvantage on all incoming attacks
- **AoE spells**: [[Fireball]], [[Thunderwave]], [[Spirit Guardians]] — hit as many as possible
- **Focus fire**: Kill one enemy at a time to reduce incoming damage per round
- **Disengage and reposition**: Don't let them surround you; keep your backs to walls
- **Help action**: Give your heavy hitter advantage to drop enemies faster

### 🐉 Fighting a Single Boss

- **Spread out**: Avoid clustering (AoE breath weapons, legendary actions)
- **Grapple + Shove Prone**: Melee attackers get advantage on all attacks against a prone grappled target
- **Help action**: Give advantage to your biggest damage dealer
- **Focus on saves**: Target the boss's weak saving throws (Int/Cha/Wis for brutes, Str/Con for casters)
- **Ready actions**: If the boss moves a lot, Ready your attack for when it comes into range
- **Concentration breakers**: If the boss is concentrating on a spell, hit it to force Con saves

### 🏚️ Tight Corridors / Dungeons

- **Marching order matters**: Tank in front, squishy casters in back
- **Reach weapons**: Polearms (10 ft reach) let you attack from the second rank
- **Avoid AoE on allies**: Careful with Fireball in a 5-ft-wide hallway — you'll hit your friends
- **Chokepoints are your friend**: One character with Shield + high AC can hold a doorway
- **Prone is devastating**: Enemies can't easily retreat if prone in a corridor
- **Light sources**: Someone needs a torch or Light cantrip — Darkvision still imposes disadvantage on Perception

### 🌾 Open Field

- **Cover is scarce**: Create it with spells ([[Wall of Fire]], [[Wall of Stone]], [[Fog Cloud]])
- **Ranged advantage**: Archers and spellcasters shine — stay at max range
- **Mounted combat**: Horses double your effective movement
- **Dash to close distance**: Melee fighters may need to Dash on round 1
- **Prone for ranged defense**: Drop prone to impose disadvantage on ranged attacks against you (but melee gets advantage, so only if enemies are far away)
- **Flanking**: Spread out to avoid AoE, but coordinate to surround key targets

### 🌊 Underwater Combat

| Rule | Effect |
|------|--------|
| Melee without swim speed | **Disadvantage** (except dagger, javelin, shortsword, spear, trident) |
| Ranged beyond normal range | **Auto-miss** |
| Ranged within normal range | **Disadvantage** (except crossbow, net, javelin, spear, trident, dart) |
| Fire damage | **Resistance** (half damage) for fully immersed creatures |

**Tips**:
- Bring thrusting weapons (spear, trident, shortsword) — they work normally
- Crossbows are the only ranged weapon without penalty at normal range
- Fire spells are half as effective — use cold, lightning, or thunder instead
- Get a swimming speed (spell, magic item, or race) to remove melee disadvantage

### 🌑 Fighting in Darkness

| Condition | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| **Dim light** (lightly obscured) | Disadvantage on Perception checks relying on sight |
| **Darkness** (heavily obscured) | Effectively **Blinded** — auto-fail sight-based checks |
| **Blinded attacker** | Disadvantage on attack rolls |
| **Unseen target** | Advantage on attacks against creatures that can't see you |

**Tips**:
- **Darkvision ≠ normal vision**: Darkness becomes dim light = still disadvantage on Perception
- **Light cantrip**: Free, no concentration — put it on a rock and throw it
- Cast **[[Faerie Fire]]**: Outlines invisible/hidden creatures, grants advantage on attacks against them
- **Devil's Sight** (Warlock invocation): See normally in magical and nonmagical darkness to 120 ft
- **Darkness + Devil's Sight combo**: You can see, enemies can't — advantage on all your attacks, disadvantage on theirs

## 4. 🤼 Grapple & Shove Guide

### Grappling Step by Step

```
1. Take the Attack action
2. Replace ONE of your attacks with a grapple attempt
3. Requirements:
   - Target is no more than one size larger than you
   - Target is within your reach
   - You have at least one free hand
4. Roll: YOUR Strength (Athletics) vs. TARGET's Strength (Athletics) or Dexterity (Acrobatics) — target chooses
5. Auto-success if target is Incapacitated
6. On success: Target gains the Grappled condition (speed = 0)
7. You can release at any time (no action required)
```

**Grappled Condition**: Target's speed becomes 0. That's it — they can still attack, cast spells, and take actions normally. They just can't move.

**Escaping**: Target uses its **action** to make Athletics or Acrobatics vs. your Athletics. Success = grapple ends.

**Dragging**: You can move and drag the grappled creature, but your **speed is halved** (normal speed if they're 2+ sizes smaller).

### Shoving Step by Step

```
1. Take the Attack action
2. Replace ONE of your attacks with a shove attempt
3. Requirements:
   - Target is no more than one size larger than you
   - Target is within your reach
   - (No free hand required)
4. Roll: YOUR Strength (Athletics) vs. TARGET's Athletics or Acrobatics — target chooses
5. Auto-success if target is Incapacitated
6. On success, choose ONE:
   a. Knock the target Prone
   b. Push the target 5 feet away from you
```

### The Grapple + Shove Prone Combo

This is one of the strongest martial tactics in the game:

| Step | Action | Result |
|------|--------|--------|
| 1 | **Grapple** (replaces 1st attack) | Target's speed = 0 |
| 2 | **Shove Prone** (replaces 2nd attack) | Target is prone |
| **Result** | Target is **grappled + prone** | Can't stand up (needs movement, but speed = 0) |

**Why it's devastating**:
- All melee attacks against the target have **advantage** (prone)
- All ranged attacks against the target have **disadvantage** (prone) — so coordinate with your party
- Target **cannot stand up** because standing costs half speed, and grappled = speed 0
- Target must use its action to **escape the grapple first**, then use movement to stand — that's its whole turn wasted

**Requirements**: You need **Extra Attack** (level 5 Fighter, Paladin, Ranger, etc.) to grapple + shove in one turn.

### When Is Grappling Worth It?

| ✅ Good Situations | ❌ Bad Situations |
|-------------------|------------------|
| Enemy is trying to flee | Enemy has multiple attacks (they can still hit you) |
| Dragging enemy into hazards (fire, cliff, spike pit) | Enemy is 2+ sizes larger than you |
| Locking down a caster (they can't move to escape AoE) | You need both hands for your weapon (greatsword) |
| Combo with Shove Prone for advantage | Enemy has high Athletics/Acrobatics |
| Holding an enemy in a zone spell ([[Spirit Guardians]], [[Moonbeam]]) | You're a Dex-based character with low Str |

## 5. 🧱 Cover & Positioning Tips

### Cover Quick Reference

| Cover Type | AC Bonus | Dex Save Bonus | Examples |
|------------|----------|----------------|----------|
| **Half Cover** | +2 | +2 | Low wall, furniture, narrow tree, **another creature** |
| **Three-Quarters Cover** | +5 | +5 | Portcullis, arrow slit, thick tree trunk |
| **Total Cover** | Untargetable | Untargetable | Fully behind a wall (AoE can still reach you) |

### Key Rules

- **Multiple sources don't stack** — only the best cover applies
- **Direction matters** — cover only works against attacks from the opposite side
- **Creatures provide half cover** — allies AND enemies between you and the attacker give +2 AC
- **Total cover blocks targeting** — but area-of-effect spells (Fireball) can still hit you

### How to USE Cover

- **Ranged characters**: Stand behind a low wall or ally for +2 AC while shooting
- **Peek-a-boo tactic**: Move out from total cover, attack, move back behind total cover (if you have enough movement)
- **Use allies as cover**: Position a tanky ally between you and the enemy archer — free +2 AC
- **Arrow slits / murder holes**: +5 AC is massive — fight from fortified positions whenever possible
- **Prone behind cover**: Drop prone behind half cover for additional ranged defense (disadvantage on ranged attacks against prone targets)

### How to DENY Cover to Enemies

- **Flank**: Move to a position where no obstacle is between you and the target
- **Get above them**: Elevated positions often negate ground-level cover
- **Destroy cover**: Smash furniture, burn wooden walls, Shatter stone barriers
- **AoE spells**: Fireball, Shatter, and other AoE ignore cover (they just need to include the target in the area)
- **Forced movement**: Shove, Thunderwave, or Eldritch Blast (Repelling Blast) to push enemies out from behind cover
- **Close the distance**: Move into melee — cover is mostly a ranged concern

## 6. 🗺️ Exploration Essentials

### Marching Order

Set this BEFORE entering a dungeon. It determines who triggers traps and who fights first.

| Position | Who Goes Here | Why |
|----------|--------------|-----|
| **Front** | High AC / high HP (Fighter, Paladin, Barbarian) | Triggers traps, engages enemies first |
| **Middle** | Support casters, skill characters (Bard, Cleric) | Protected from front and rear attacks |
| **Back** | Ranged / perceptive characters (Ranger, Rogue) | Watches for followers, covers retreat |

**Tips**:
- Put your **highest passive Perception** character in front (spot traps) AND back (spot followers)
- In narrow corridors, you may only fit **single file** — front rank is alone
- **Rogue in front** can be smart: high Perception, can check for traps, Cunning Action to Dash away

### Handling Traps

| Step | Action | Check |
|------|--------|-------|
| 1. **Detect** | Passive Perception or active Search action | Wisdom (Perception) or Intelligence (Investigation) |
| 2. **Identify** | Examine the trap mechanism | Intelligence (Investigation) |
| 3. **Disarm** | Use thieves' tools or creative solution | Dexterity check (with thieves' tools proficiency) |
| 4. **Avoid** | Go around, trigger from distance, or tank it | Depends on trap type |

**Pro tips**:
- **10-foot pole**: Prod the ground ahead of you — triggers pressure plates from safety
- **Mage Hand**: Trigger traps from 30 feet away (no damage to you)
- **Find Traps spell**: Tells you traps exist nearby but NOT where exactly — limited usefulness
- **Passive Perception**: Always active — DM compares it to trap DC automatically
- **Don't rush**: Slow pace in dungeons lets you search more carefully

### Searching Rooms Efficiently

1. **Announce what you're searching**: "I search the desk" is better than "I search the room"
2. **Split tasks**: One person searches, one watches the door, one checks for magic (Detect Magic)
3. **Investigation vs. Perception**:
   - **Perception** (Wisdom): Spotting something hidden (secret door, hidden lever)
   - **Investigation** (Intelligence): Deducing information (how a mechanism works, what a clue means)
4. **Take 10 minutes**: Thorough room searches typically take ~10 minutes of in-game time
5. **Check the ceiling**: Players always forget to look up — DMs love hiding things there

### Travel Pace

| Pace | Per Hour | Per Day (8 hrs) | Effect |
|------|----------|-----------------|--------|
| **Fast** | 4 miles | 30 miles | **−5 passive Perception** (miss hidden threats!) |
| **Normal** | 3 miles | 24 miles | No effect |
| **Slow** | 2 miles | 18 miles | **Can use Stealth** |

**Forced March**: After 8 hours of travel, each additional hour requires a **Con save** (DC 10 + 1 per extra hour). Failure = 1 level of **Exhaustion**.

**Difficult Terrain**: Halves all travel distances (dense forest, swamp, mountains, rubble).

### Travel Activities (Choose One — Sacrifices Passive Perception)

| Activity | Check | Purpose |
|----------|-------|---------|
| **Navigate** | Wisdom (Survival) | Prevent getting lost |
| **Draw a Map** | None | Record progress for backtracking |
| **Track** | Wisdom (Survival) | Follow creature tracks |
| **Forage** | Wisdom (Survival) | Find food and water |
| **Watch for Danger** | Passive Perception | **Default** — only this contributes to spotting threats |

> [!warning] Important
> Characters doing Navigate, Map, Track, or Forage do **NOT** contribute their passive Perception to noticing threats. Only characters actively watching for danger can spot ambushes.

## 7. 💤 Rest & Recovery Quick Reference

### Short Rest vs. Long Rest

| Feature | Short Rest | Long Rest |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| **Duration** | 1 hour minimum | 8 hours minimum (6 sleep + 2 light activity) |
| **HP Recovery** | Spend Hit Dice (roll + Con mod each) | **All HP restored** |
| **Hit Dice Recovery** | None | Regain **half your total Hit Dice** (min 1) |
| **Spell Slots** | ❌ Not restored (except Warlock pact slots) | ✅ All spell slots restored |
| **Class Features** | Some reset (Channel Divinity, Second Wind, Action Surge) | Most reset |
| **Limit** | No limit per day | **One per 24 hours** |
| **Interruption** | — | 1+ hour of strenuous activity = restart |
| **Requirement** | — | Must have at least 1 HP to benefit |

### Hit Dice Healing Math

**Your Hit Dice** = your class's hit die × your level

| Class | Hit Die |
|-------|---------|
| Sorcerer, Wizard | d6 |
| Bard, Cleric, Druid, Monk, Rogue, Warlock | d8 |
| Fighter, Paladin, Ranger | d10 |
| Barbarian | d12 |

**Per Hit Die spent**: Roll the die + Constitution modifier = HP regained (minimum 0)

**Example**: Level 5 Fighter (d10), Constitution 16 (+3)
- Has 5 Hit Dice total (5d10)
- Each short rest: spend up to 5 Hit Dice
- Each die heals: 1d10 + 3 (average 8.5 HP per die)
- Spending all 5: average ~42 HP recovered

### How Many Short Rests Per Day?

The game assumes **2 short rests per adventuring day** (the "standard adventuring day" from the DMG):

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| Morning | 2–3 encounters |
| **Short Rest** | 1 hour |
| Afternoon | 2–3 encounters |
| **Short Rest** | 1 hour |
| Evening | 1–2 encounters |
| **Long Rest** | 8 hours |

**Short-rest classes** (Fighter, Warlock, Monk) need these rests to keep up with long-rest classes (Wizard, Cleric). If your DM only runs 1–2 encounters per long rest, short-rest classes feel weaker.

### Hit Dice Economy

| Event | Hit Dice Effect |
|-------|----------------|
| Short rest | **Spend** Hit Dice to heal |
| Long rest | **Regain** half your total Hit Dice (rounded down, min 1) |

**Example over 3 days** (Level 6 Fighter, 6 Hit Dice total):

| Day | Start | Spent | Regained (Long Rest) | End |
|-----|-------|-------|---------------------|-----|
| 1 | 6 | 4 | +3 | 5 |
| 2 | 5 | 3 | +3 | 5 |
| 3 | 5 | 5 | +3 | 3 |

> [!tip] Tip
> Don't spend ALL your Hit Dice in one day — you only get half back on a long rest. If you burn through all of them, it takes multiple days to fully recover. Budget 50–70% per day.

### What Resets on Each Rest?

| Feature | Short Rest | Long Rest |
|---------|:---------:|:---------:|
| Hit Points (via Hit Dice) | ✅ | ✅ (all) |
| Hit Dice | ❌ | ✅ (half) |
| Spell Slots | ❌ | ✅ |
| Warlock Pact Slots | ✅ | ✅ |
| Fighter: Second Wind | ✅ | ✅ |
| Fighter: Action Surge | ✅ | ✅ |
| Cleric: Channel Divinity | ✅ | ✅ |
| Paladin: Channel Divinity | ✅ | ✅ |
| Monk: Ki Points | ✅ | ✅ |
| Barbarian: Rage | ❌ | ✅ |
| Bard: Bardic Inspiration | ❌ | ✅ |
| Druid: Wild Shape | ✅ | ✅ |
| Wizard: Arcane Recovery | ✅ (1/day) | — |
| Sorcerer: Sorcery Points | ❌ | ✅ |
| Death Save Successes/Failures | Reset on any healing | Reset on any healing |
