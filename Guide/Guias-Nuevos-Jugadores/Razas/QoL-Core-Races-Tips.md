# 🎲 Core Race Deep Dive: Tips for New Players

---

## 🪓 Dwarf

### 1. Creative Uses for Racial Abilities

- **Dwarven Resilience as a social tool.** Advantage on poison saves + poison resistance means your dwarf can win drinking contests against almost anyone. Use this to extract information from NPCs at taverns or prove your worth to a clan.
- **Stonecunning is an investigation superpower.** Double proficiency on History checks about stonework origins. In any dungeon or ruin, ask: "Was this wall built by dwarves or humans?" — Stonecunning answers that.
- **Heavy armor doesn't slow you down.** A human in full plate moves 20 ft. You still move 25 ft. Best heavy-armor users in the game.
- **Tool Proficiency is free money.** Brewer's supplies brew ale during downtime. Smith's tools repair gear. Mason's tools assess structural integrity. They generate income and solve puzzles.
- **Dwarven Combat Training on non-martial classes.** A dwarf Cleric or Wizard gets battleaxe and warhammer proficiency for free. Your dwarf cleric should use a warhammer (d8/d10 versatile), not a mace (d6).

### 2. Roleplay Behavior Tips

- **When angry:** Go *quiet*. Clench jaw, grip weapon, remember the slight for decades. Grudges are sacred.
- **When happy:** Celebrate with *craft* — buy a round, sing a clan song, start carving something.
- **When greeting strangers:** Size people up by their hands. Calloused = respect. Soft = suspicion. Ask what you do before asking your name.
- **When scared:** Plant feet, grip axe, mutter a prayer to Moradin. Fear = stubbornness — refuse to retreat.
- **Mannerisms:** Stroke beard when thinking. Tap stone walls (checking quality). Complain about elven wine. Swear by ancestors: "By Durin's beard!"

### 3. Homebrew Minor Items

| Item | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Clan Signet Stone** | Palm-sized carved stone with clan rune. Rub when homesick, hold during oaths. |
| **Beard Oil of the Deep** | Mineral-scented oil from underground fungi and gemstone dust. Daily grooming ritual. |
| **Ancestor's Tally Rod** | Notched iron rod marking significant deeds — promises kept, foes slain, crafts completed. |
| **Stonebrew Flask** | Stone flask that keeps ale at perfect underground temperature. Sharing = deep trust. |
| **Forge-Prayer Beads** | Metal beads stamped with runes of Moradin. Click before battle, like a rosary. |

### 4. Common New Player Mistakes

- Forgetting heavy armor doesn't reduce your speed
- Never using Stonecunning (every dungeon has stone — ask about it!)
- Ignoring Tool Proficiency (they have specific uses, not just flavor)
- Choosing weapons poorly (use warhammer d8/d10, not mace d6)
- Playing dwarves as one-note grumpy (they're lawful *good* — loyal, generous, honorable)

### 5. Subrace Comparison

| | Hill Dwarf | Mountain Dwarf |
|---|---|---|
| **Stats** | Con +2, Wis +1 | Con +2, Str +2 |
| **Special** | +1 HP per level | Light & medium armor |
| **Best For** | Cleric, Druid, Ranger | Fighter, Paladin, *Wizard* |

> 💡 **Sleeper pick:** Mountain Dwarf Wizard — medium armor + Con +2 for concentration saves. Chain shirt + fireballs.

## 🧝 Elf

### 1. Creative Uses for Racial Abilities

- **Trance = you're the night watch.** 4 hours for a long rest. Take first watch AND still get a full rest while the party sleeps 8 hours.
- **Fey Ancestry blocks more than you think.** Advantage vs charm protects against *charm person*, *hypnotic pattern*, vampire charm, succubus charm. Sleep immunity shuts down the *sleep* spell entirely.
- **Keen Senses is a free skill.** Perception is the most-rolled skill in D&D. Getting it free means you spend class picks elsewhere.
- **Darkvision for scouting.** No torch = enemies don't see you coming. Pair with stealth.
- **Elf Weapon Training on caster classes.** An elf Wizard with longbow proficiency can contribute ranged damage when out of spell slots.

### 2. Roleplay Behavior Tips

- **When angry:** Go *cold*. Speak more formally, more precisely, with devastating politeness. "How... quaint."
- **When happy:** Hum, notice beauty in small things, share a memory from 300 years ago casually.
- **When scared:** Hyper-alert and still. Ears twitch. Stop blinking. Assess every exit. Like a cat about to bolt.
- **When greeting strangers:** Observe before speaking. Study someone for an uncomfortable amount of time. Slight bow, formal titles until trust is established.
- **Mannerisms:** Touch trees as they pass. Tilt head when listening. Eat slowly. Refer to events from centuries ago as recent. Zone out in trance-like reflection.

### 3. Homebrew Minor Items

| Item | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Reverie Incense** | Dried forest herbs burned during trance. Different blends evoke different forests. |
| **Moonthread Bookmark** | Thin silver bookmark for poetry collections. Often centuries old. |
| **Memory Bead Bracelet** | Glass beads with pressed flowers/sand from meaningful places. One per century. |
| **Starmap Cloth** | Dark silk embroidered with constellations from homeland. Navigation + comfort. |
| **Songwood Whistle** | Wooden whistle from home forest. Unique tone. Signal or play during quiet moments. |

### 4. Common New Player Mistakes

- Forgetting you can't be put to sleep by magic (*sleep* spell = immune)
- Not using Trance strategically (4 hours free during 8-hour rest — keep watch, study, craft)
- Wasting a skill pick on Perception (you already have it from Keen Senses)
- Ignoring weapon proficiencies on caster classes (longbow on a Wizard!)
- Playing elves as emotionless (they're chaotic good — passionate, artistic, haughty not cold)

### 5. Subrace Comparison

| | High Elf | Wood Elf | Drow |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Stats** | Dex +2, Int +1 | Dex +2, Wis +1 | Dex +2, Cha +1 |
| **Special** | Free cantrip + language | 35 ft speed + Mask of Wild | 120 ft darkvision + innate spells |
| **Best For** | Wizard, Bladesinger | Ranger, Druid, Monk | Warlock, underground campaigns |

> ⚠️ **Drow warning:** Sunlight Sensitivity = disadvantage on attacks and Perception in direct sunlight. If your campaign is mostly outdoors during the day, this hurts.

## 🍀 Halfling

### 1. Creative Uses for Racial Abilities

- **Lucky works on death saves.** Rolling a 1 = two failures. Lucky lets you reroll. This literally saves your life.
- **Halfling Nimbleness is a battlefield taxi.** Move through any Medium+ creature's space. Slip through enemy lines without Disengage.
- **Brave stacks with other fear resistance.** Dragons' Frightful Presence, *fear* spells, banshee wails — all much less threatening.
- **Naturally Stealthy (Lightfoot) works with enemies too.** Duck behind an ogre to hide, pop out and attack with advantage. Rogues: free Sneak Attack.
- **Stout Resilience = poison-immune scout.** Walk through poison gas traps, drink suspicious potions, shrug off poisoned weapons.

### 2. Roleplay Behavior Tips

- **When angry:** Don't get angry — get *disappointed*. "Well, that's not very neighborly." Truly furious = cook aggressively.
- **When happy:** Celebrate with food, stories, hugs. Tell a rambling story about a cousin. Joy is communal and loud.
- **When scared:** Grab nearest friend's hand. Crack nervous jokes. Look for hiding spots. Fear is honest but Brave means they push through.
- **When greeting strangers:** Offer food. Always. "Have you eaten?" Refusing a halfling's food is a mild insult.
- **Mannerisms:** Walk barefoot. Hum while working. Carry snacks always. Stand on chairs for eye level. Pat taller people on the elbow.

### 3. Homebrew Minor Items

| Item | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Grandmother's Recipe Book** | Food-stained journal of family recipes. Different handwriting from different ancestors. |
| **Hearthstone Pendant** | Smooth river stone from home village on a cord. Always warm from body heat. |
| **Lucky Copper** | Specific copper coin carried since childhood. Flipped before big decisions. |
| **Traveler's Spice Pouch** | Dried herbs from home. A pinch in every meal because "foreign food needs help." |
| **Foot-Warming Salve** | Herbal salve for bare feet. Earthy, comforting scent like fresh-turned soil. |

### 4. Common New Player Mistakes

- Forgetting Lucky works on death saves (reroll that 1!)
- Not using Nimbleness in combat (run through enemy lines to reach the caster)
- Thinking Small size is only a disadvantage (fit through small spaces, ride mastiffs as mounts!)
- Ignoring Naturally Stealthy (hide behind ANY Medium+ creature — allies AND enemies)
- Playing halflings as comic relief only (they display "remarkable ferocity" when friends are threatened)

### 5. Subrace Comparison

| | Lightfoot | Stout |
|---|---|---|
| **Stats** | Dex +2, Cha +1 | Dex +2, Con +1 |
| **Special** | Hide behind larger creatures | Poison advantage + resistance |
| **Best For** | Rogue, Bard, face characters | Ranger, Fighter, frontline |

> 💡 **Overlooked combo:** Stout Halfling Monk — Dex +2, Con +1, Lucky on saves, Brave for fear, poison resistance, Nimbleness to weave through enemies.

## 👤 Human

### 1. Creative Uses for Racial Abilities

- **+1 to ALL stats = no dump stats.** Every saving throw is slightly better. You're never *bad* at anything.
- **Extra language is an intelligence tool.** Pick Goblin for Lost Mines of Phandelver. Pick strategically for your campaign.
- **Variant Human's level-1 feat breaks the game open.** Start with Polearm Master, Sentinel, Sharpshooter, War Caster, or Lucky. Your build comes online immediately.
- **Variant's free skill fills party gaps.** No one has Medicine? Take it. No Arcana? Take it.
- **Standard Human is secretly great for MAD classes.** Paladin (Str/Cha/Con), Monk (Dex/Wis/Con), Ranger (Dex/Wis/Con) all benefit from +1 to everything.

### 2. Roleplay Behavior Tips

- **When angry:** Volatile and expressive. Shout, slam tables, make threats. Cool down fast — furious to apologetic in an hour.
- **When happy:** Celebrate immediately and loudly. Buy drinks, make speeches, toast to things.
- **When scared:** Look to each other. Form groups, make plans, build walls. Reach for a weapon and a friend.
- **When greeting strangers:** Adapt to whoever they're meeting — bow for elves, handshake for dwarves, smile for halflings. Cultural chameleons.
- **Mannerisms:** Reference short lifespan ("Life's too short for bad ale"). Make plans constantly. Name-drop institutions. Judge by ambition.

### 3. Homebrew Minor Items

| Item | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Founder's Journal** | Leather-bound journal of goals, plans, and legacy. Sketches of places, names of people helped. |
| **Heirloom Coin** | Coin from homeland with a local hero's face. Reminder of origins. |
| **Trade Tongue Phrasebook** | Useful phrases in six languages, badly pronounced but earnest. |
| **Ambition Token** | Carved wooden token of life goal (tiny crown, sword, book). Carved as a child, carried for life. |
| **Campfire Spice Kit** | Salt, pepper, herbs, hot sauce. Humans always try to make trail rations taste better. |

### 4. Common New Player Mistakes

- Always picking Variant without thinking (Standard is better for MAD classes and new players)
- Picking a bad feat as Variant (Keen Mind at level 1 = waste. Pick PAM, Sentinel, GWM, Lucky, etc.)
- Forgetting the extra language (ask your DM what's useful in the campaign)
- Thinking humans are boring (most versatile race — empires in a single lifetime)
- Not choosing Variant's free skill strategically (fill party gaps)

### 5. Standard vs Variant

| | Standard | Variant |
|---|---|---|
| **Stats** | +1 to ALL six | +1 to two of choice |
| **Special** | Extra language | Free skill + **feat at level 1** |
| **Best For** | MAD classes, new players | Specific builds, experienced players |

> ⚠️ Variant Human requires DM permission (optional feat rules). Always ask first.

## 📊 Quick Cross-Race Reference

| Situation | Best Race |
|---|---|
| "I want to never die" | Hill Dwarf (Con +2, +1 HP/level, poison resistance) |
| "I want to be the best Rogue" | Lightfoot Halfling (Dex +2, Lucky, Naturally Stealthy) |
| "I want to be the best Wizard" | High Elf (Int +1, free cantrip) or Mountain Dwarf (armor!) |
| "I want maximum flexibility" | Variant Human (feat at level 1) |
| "I want to be the party scout" | Wood Elf (35 ft, Mask of Wild, Darkvision, Perception) |
| "I'm brand new to D&D" | Standard Human (simple) or Hill Dwarf (tanky, forgiving) |
