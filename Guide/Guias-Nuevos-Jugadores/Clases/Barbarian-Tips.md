# ⚔️ Barbarian — New Player Tips

> Quick-reference guide for getting the most out of your Barbarian at levels 1–5.

---

## 1. Creative Uses for Class Features

### Rage — Beyond Just Hitting Harder
- **Rage gives advantage on Strength CHECKS, not just attacks.** Rage before kicking down a door, bending prison bars, grappling an enemy, or shoving someone off a cliff. The advantage on Athletics is massive.
- **Grapple + Shove combo while raging.** Grapple a creature (advantage from Rage), then shove it [[Prone|prone]]. Now it can't stand up because its speed is 0 from the grapple, and all melee attacks against it have advantage.
- **Use Rage resistance as a "healing" tool.** Taking half damage from physical attacks effectively doubles your HP pool. At level 1 with 14 CON, your 15 HP functions like 30 HP against swords and arrows.
- **Rage to auto-succeed on Strength saves.** Advantage on STR saves means you're much harder to push, restrain, or knock prone via spells like *Entangle* or *Thunderwave*.

### Reckless Attack — Calculated Recklessness
- **Use Reckless Attack to "taunt" enemies.** Enemies get advantage against you — but you have Rage resistance. This draws attacks away from your squishy allies. You WANT to be hit.
- **Pair with Great Weapon Master.** The -5/+10 trade becomes much more reliable with advantage on every attack.
- **Reckless Attack doubles your crit chance** (from 5% to ~9.75%). More crits = more Brutal Critical dice later.

### Danger Sense
- **Stack Danger Sense with Rage resistance.** Succeed on a DEX save for half damage → then halve THAT with Rage resistance = only 25% of the original damage from a *Fireball*.
- **Danger Sense works against traps.** Stepping on a pressure plate that triggers a DEX save? You have advantage.

### Unarmored Defense
- **You can use a shield with Unarmored Defense.** Many new players miss this. AC = 10 + DEX + CON + 2 (shield) is very competitive.

## 2. Common Mistakes & Misunderstandings

| ❌ Mistake | ✅ Correct Rule |
|-----------|----------------|
| Casting spells while Raging | You **cannot** cast spells or concentrate on them while Raging. Concentration drops immediately. |
| Using Rage with heavy armor | Rage benefits only work if you are **not wearing heavy armor**. Medium armor is fine. |
| Rage damage on ranged attacks | Rage bonus damage only applies to **melee weapon attacks using Strength**. Thrown javelins are ranged attacks — RAW no Rage damage. |
| Forgetting to maintain Rage | Rage ends if your turn ends and you **haven't attacked a hostile creature or taken damage** since your last turn. |
| Reckless Attack with DEX weapons | Reckless Attack only works with **melee weapon attacks using Strength**. Finesse weapons using DEX don't qualify. |
| Stacking Unarmored Defense formulas | Barbarian + Monk Unarmored Defense don't combine. Pick one. |
| Danger Sense helps against all DEX saves | Only works against effects **you can see**. Invisible enemies don't trigger it. |

## 3. Combat Tactics Cheat Sheet (Levels 1–5)

### Level 1 — The Basics
| Phase | What to Do |
|-------|-----------|
| **Bonus Action** | **Rage** (if this is a serious fight — you only have 2/day) |
| **Action** | Attack with your biggest weapon (greataxe or greatsword) |
| **Movement** | Get into melee with the most dangerous enemy |
| **Remember** | Rage damage (+2) on every melee STR hit. Resistance to B/P/S. |

### Level 2 — Reckless Attacker
| Phase | What to Do |
|-------|-----------|
| **First Attack** | Declare **Reckless Attack** → advantage on all melee STR attacks this turn |
| **Action** | Attack with advantage |
| **Remember** | Enemies get advantage on you too — but Rage resistance makes it worth it |

### Level 3 — Primal Path Online
| Subclass | Priority |
|----------|----------|
| **Berserker** | Frenzy for boss fights only (bonus action attack each turn, but exhaustion after) |
| **Totem (Bear)** | Rage = resistance to ALL damage except psychic. You are the wall. |
| **Totem (Wolf)** | Stay adjacent to enemies — your melee allies get advantage on attacks against them |

### Level 5 — Extra Attack Power Spike
| Phase | What to Do |
|-------|-----------|
| **Bonus Action** | Rage |
| **Action** | Attack TWICE (Extra Attack). Declare Reckless on the first. |
| **Damage** | 2 × (1d12 + STR + 2 Rage) with advantage on both |
| **Berserker** | Frenzy adds a THIRD attack as bonus action = 3 attacks/turn |

### General Tips
- **Don't hoard Rages at low levels.** You get them back on long rest.
- **Position aggressively.** You are the hardest character to kill.
- **Grapple high-value targets.** Rage + Athletics proficiency + advantage = almost impossible to break.

## 4. Roleplay Prompts

### What Motivates a Barbarian?
- **Protecting the tribe/family** — everything you do is for them
- **Proving your strength** — every battle is a test, every scar a trophy
- **Rage as trauma** — your fury comes from loss, injustice, or a curse you can't control
- **The call of the wild** — civilization feels wrong; you need open sky and honest danger
- **A debt of honor** — someone saved your people, and you swore to repay it

### How Does a Barbarian Train?
- Wrestling bears, swimming in frozen rivers, running through forests barefoot
- Meditation through physical exhaustion — push until the mind goes quiet
- Ritual combat with tribe members — scars are marks of respect
- Communing with animal spirits (Totem Warrior) through fasting and vision quests
- Berserkers practice controlled fury — learning to ride the edge of madness

### Daily Routine
- Wake before dawn, hunt or forage for breakfast
- Sharpen weapons obsessively — a dull blade is an insult
- Uncomfortable in inns — prefer sleeping outdoors, even in rain
- Tell stories of great battles, carve totems, tend to trophies

### Personality Hooks
- "I don't understand why you need a *key* when the door is right there." (kicks it down)
- Deeply loyal but terrible at expressing emotions with words
- Fascinated by magic — it's like rage but from books?
- Keeps a collection of teeth/claws from defeated enemies
- Speaks in short, direct sentences but is surprisingly wise about nature

## 5. Multiclass Dip Tips

| Dip | Levels | What You Get | Why It's Good |
|-----|--------|-------------|---------------|
| **Fighter 1** | 1 level | Fighting Style (Defense/GWF), Second Wind, CON save proficiency | Defense (+1 AC) stacks with everything. Second Wind = bonus action healing. |
| **Fighter 2** | 2 levels | Action Surge | 4 Reckless attacks in one turn at Barb 5/Fighter 2. Devastating nova. |
| **Rogue 1** | 1 level | Expertise (Athletics for grappling), Sneak Attack (1d6 with finesse + Reckless) | Expertise in Athletics makes you the ultimate grappler. |
| **Druid 1** | 1 level | *Goodberry*, *Absorb Elements*, ritual casting | Out-of-combat healing and utility. Cast before Raging. |

### Best Combos
- **Barbarian 5 / Fighter 2**: Extra Attack + Action Surge = 4 Reckless attacks in one turn.
- **Barbarian X / Rogue 1**: Expertise in Athletics + Reckless enabling Sneak Attack.

> [!warning] Multiclass Requirements
> Barbarian requires **STR 13** to multiclass into or out of.
