# ⚔️ Ranger — New Player Tips

> Quick-reference guide for getting the most out of your Ranger at levels 1–5.

---

## 1. Creative Uses for Class Features

### Favored Enemy — Knowledge Is Power
- **Advantage on Intelligence checks to recall info about your favored enemy.** Ask your DM: "What do I know about this creature's weaknesses, habits, or lair?" You're the party's monster expert.
- **You learn a language spoken by your favored enemy.** Choose strategically — Goblin, Draconic, or Abyssal can let you eavesdrop on enemies or negotiate.
- **Coordinate with your DM.** If the campaign is about fighting undead, pick undead. This feature is only as good as its relevance to the campaign.

### Natural Explorer — Trivialize the Wilderness
- **Your group can't become lost in your favored terrain** (except by magic). You're the party's GPS.
- **You find twice as much food when foraging.** In survival-heavy campaigns, this keeps the party fed without spending gold.
- **You can move stealthily at normal pace when alone.** Scout ahead without slowing down. Report back to the party.
- **Difficult terrain doesn't slow your group.** In forest-heavy or swamp campaigns, this saves hours of travel time.
- **While tracking, you learn exact number, sizes, and how long ago creatures passed.** This is incredible intelligence — you know exactly what you're walking into.

### Spellcasting — Your Secret Weapon
- **Hunter's Mark is your bread and butter.** +1d6 damage on every hit against the marked target. With Extra Attack, that's +2d6/turn. Concentrate on it and keep swinging.
- **Goodberry heals 10 HP total (10 berries × 1 HP each) for a 1st-level slot.** Cast it at the end of the day with leftover slots — berries last 24 hours. Best out-of-combat healing per slot in the game.
- **Pass Without Trace (+10 to Stealth for the whole party)** is one of the most powerful 2nd-level spells in the game. Even the cleric in plate mail can sneak past guards.
- **Cure Wounds is your emergency heal.** Don't use it in combat unless someone is dying — *Goodberry* is more efficient for out-of-combat healing.
- **Spike Growth (2nd level)** turns a 20-ft radius into a damage zone: 2d4 piercing per 5 ft of movement. Combine with forced movement (shove, *Thorn Whip*) for massive damage.

### Hunter Subclass Features
- **Colossus Slayer triggers on ANY creature below max HP.** After the first hit of combat, every enemy qualifies. It's essentially +1d8 damage per turn for free.
- **Horde Breaker gives a free extra attack** against a different creature within 5 ft of your first target. Position to hit two enemies and you get 3 attacks at level 5 (2 Extra Attack + 1 Horde Breaker).

### Beast Master Tips
- **Your beast's HP = max of (normal HP, 4 × ranger level).** At level 5, that's 20 HP minimum. Pick a beast with good attacks, not HP.
- **Before level 5, commanding your beast to Attack costs YOUR action.** Use the beast for Help (advantage on your next attack) or Dodge instead until you get Extra Attack.

## 2. Common Mistakes & Misunderstandings

| ❌ Mistake | ✅ Correct Rule |
|-----------|----------------|
| Hunter's Mark damage on every attack | Hunter's Mark adds 1d6 **once per hit**, but only against the **marked target**. If you switch targets, you need a bonus action to move the mark. |
| Forgetting Hunter's Mark requires concentration | If you take damage, you must make a CON save to maintain it. Losing concentration = losing the mark AND the spell slot. |
| Natural Explorer works everywhere | It only works in your **chosen favored terrain**. Outside that terrain, you're a normal traveler. |
| Favored Enemy gives combat bonuses | In the 2014 PHB, Favored Enemy gives **no combat bonus** — only advantage on Survival (tracking) and Intelligence (recall) checks. The 2024 version is different. |
| Beast Master: beast attacks for free | Before level 5, commanding the beast to Attack **costs your action**. You don't attack that turn. At 5+, you get one weapon attack when the beast attacks. |
| Primeval Awareness reveals location | Primeval Awareness only tells you **yes/no** if certain creature types are within 1 mile. No location, no number, no direction. |
| Rangers can't change spells | Rangers can swap **one spell per level-up**. Plan ahead — you're stuck with your choices between levels. |

## 3. Combat Tactics Cheat Sheet (Levels 1–5)

### Level 1 — Weapon Specialist (No Spells Yet in 2014 PHB)
| Phase | What to Do |
|-------|-----------|
| **Action** | Attack with longbow (Archery style = +2 to hit) or dual-wield melee |
| **Bonus Action** | Off-hand attack if dual-wielding |
| **Remember** | You have d10 hit dice and medium armor. You're tougher than you think. |

### Level 2 — Spells + Fighting Style
| Phase | What to Do |
|-------|-----------|
| **Round 1 Bonus Action** | Cast **Hunter's Mark** on the biggest threat |
| **Action** | Attack the marked target (+1d6 per hit) |
| **When target dies** | Bonus action to move Hunter's Mark to a new target (no new slot) |
| **Fighting Style** | Archery (+2 ranged), TWF (ability mod on off-hand), Dueling (+2 one-handed), Defense (+1 AC) |

### Level 3 — Subclass Online
| Subclass | Priority |
|----------|----------|
| **Hunter (Colossus Slayer)** | +1d8/turn on any target below max HP. After round 1, this is always active. |
| **Hunter (Horde Breaker)** | Free extra attack vs. a different creature within 5 ft of your target. Great vs. groups. |
| **Beast Master** | Use beast for Help action (gives you advantage) until level 5. Don't sacrifice your own attacks. |

### Level 5 — Extra Attack + 2nd-Level Spells
| Phase | What to Do |
|-------|-----------|
| **Bonus Action** | Hunter's Mark (if not already active) |
| **Action** | Attack TWICE (Extra Attack). Each hit adds +1d6 (Hunter's Mark) |
| **Hunter** | Colossus Slayer adds +1d8 on one hit. Horde Breaker adds a 3rd attack vs. adjacent enemy. |
| **New Spells** | *Pass Without Trace* (+10 Stealth, whole party), *Spike Growth* (area denial) |

### General Tips
- **Archery + Hunter's Mark is your core loop.** +2 to hit, +1d6 per hit, from 150 ft away. Simple and devastating.
- **Protect your concentration.** If you lose Hunter's Mark, you lose your biggest damage boost AND a spell slot.
- **You're a half-caster with few slots.** Don't burn them all on combat spells — save one for *Goodberry* or *Pass Without Trace*.

## 4. Roleplay Prompts

### What Motivates a Ranger?
- **Guardian of the wild** — civilization encroaches, and someone must hold the line
- **Vengeance hunter** — a specific monster destroyed your home, and you won't rest until it's dead
- **Wanderer** — you don't belong anywhere, so you belong everywhere
- **Protector of the road** — you keep the trade routes safe so others can travel in peace
- **Student of nature** — every creature, every terrain, every ecosystem is a lesson

### How Does a Ranger Train?
- Tracking animals through the wilderness for days — learning patience and observation
- Archery practice at dawn: 50 arrows at increasing distances, in wind, in rain
- Studying animal behavior — how they hunt, how they flee, how they communicate
- Learning herbalism — which plants heal, which poison, which feed
- Silent movement drills — walking through dry leaves without a sound

### Daily Routine
- Wake with the sun, check the perimeter for tracks or signs of danger
- Forage for breakfast — identify edible plants, set snares for small game
- Scout ahead of the party — report terrain, threats, and resources
- Evening: tend to weapons, craft arrows, study the stars for navigation
- Sleep lightly, one eye open — the wilderness doesn't rest

### Personality Hooks
- More comfortable talking to animals than people
- Keeps a detailed journal of every creature encountered — sketches, behaviors, weaknesses
- Has a rivalry with a specific monster that keeps escaping
- Speaks softly but carries an encyclopedic knowledge of the natural world
- Uncomfortable in cities — too many smells, too much noise, nowhere to see the horizon

## 5. Multiclass Dip Tips

| Dip | Levels | What You Get | Why It's Good |
|-----|--------|-------------|---------------|
| **Fighter 1** | 1 level | Fighting Style (stack Archery + Defense), Second Wind, CON saves | Two Fighting Styles. CON saves help maintain Hunter's Mark concentration. |
| **Rogue 1–2** | 1–2 levels | Sneak Attack (1d6), Expertise, Cunning Action at 2 | Sneak Attack triggers easily (ally adjacent to target). Expertise in Stealth/Perception. |
| **Cleric 1 (Nature)** | 1 level | Heavy armor, *Bless*, *Healing Word*, a druid cantrip | Heavy armor + shield + Defense style = very tanky ranger. *Healing Word* as bonus action. |
| **Druid 1** | 1 level | More spell options, WIS synergy, cantrips | *Absorb Elements*, *Entangle*, *Shillelagh*. WIS-based like Ranger. |
| **Fighter 2** | 2 levels | Action Surge | 4 attacks in one turn, all with Hunter's Mark = 4d6 extra damage. |

### Best Combos
- **Ranger 5 / Fighter 2**: Extra Attack + Action Surge = 4 attacks with Hunter's Mark. Archery + Defense styles.
- **Ranger 5 / Rogue 3 (Scout)**: Expertise in Nature/Survival, Sneak Attack, Cunning Action. Ultimate wilderness operative.
- **Ranger X / Cleric 1 (Nature)**: Heavy armor ranger with *Bless* and *Healing Word*. Surprisingly tanky.

> [!warning] Multiclass Requirements
> Ranger requires **DEX 13 and WIS 13** to multiclass into or out of.
