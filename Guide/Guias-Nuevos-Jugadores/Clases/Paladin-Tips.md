# ⚔️ Paladin — New Player Tips

> Quick-reference guide for getting the most out of your Paladin at levels 1–5.

---

## 1. Creative Uses for Class Features

### Divine Smite — The Nova King
- **You choose to Smite AFTER you hit.** This is huge — you never waste a spell slot on a miss. Wait for a critical hit, then Smite for double dice (4d8 on a 1st-level crit, 6d8 vs undead/fiends).
- **Divine Smite is NOT a spell.** It can't be counterspelled, doesn't require concentration, and works in areas of *Silence*. It just costs a spell slot.
- **Smite on your last attack of the day for maximum drama.** Save your highest slot for the killing blow on the boss.
- **Don't Smite every hit.** You have very few spell slots (2 at level 2). Save Smites for crits, boss fights, or finishing blows. Use weapon attacks for regular enemies.

### Divine Sense — Supernatural Radar
- **Divine Sense detects through walls** (as long as there's no total cover). Use it to scan a room before entering — if you sense a fiend or undead, you know there's danger.
- **It detects consecrated/desecrated areas** (like the *Hallow* spell). Useful for finding hidden temples, cursed ground, or unholy altars.
- **Use it to verify if an NPC is secretly a fiend, celestial, or undead.** That friendly merchant might be a disguised devil. Divine Sense reveals their type (but not identity).

### Lay on Hands — Precision Healing
- **You can heal exactly 1 HP.** This is the most efficient way to pick up an unconscious ally — spend 1 HP from your pool to bring them back to consciousness. Don't waste a full *Cure Wounds*.
- **Lay on Hands cures diseases and poisons** (5 HP from pool each). No spell slot needed. You're a walking *Lesser Restoration* for poison/disease.
- **Split your pool across multiple allies.** Heal the Rogue for 5, the Wizard for 10, yourself for 5. It's flexible.

### Spellcasting — More Than Just Smite Fuel
- **Bless is one of the best spells in the game.** +1d4 to attacks AND saves for 3 allies. At low levels, this is often better than Smiting.
- **Shield of Faith (+2 AC, concentration)** on yourself or the party tank is excellent for long fights.
- **Command ("Grovel")** — the target falls prone, giving your melee allies advantage. No concentration, 1st-level slot.

### Oath Features
- **Devotion's Sacred Weapon adds CHA to attack rolls.** With 16 CHA, that's +3 to hit for a full minute. Use it on boss fights.
- **Vengeance's Vow of Enmity is a BONUS ACTION** for advantage on all attacks against one target for 1 minute. This is your "I'm killing this thing" button.

## 2. Common Mistakes & Misunderstandings

| ❌ Mistake | ✅ Correct Rule |
|-----------|----------------|
| Smiting on ranged attacks | Divine Smite requires a **melee weapon attack**. No Smiting with bows or thrown weapons. |
| Smiting costs an action | Smite has **no action cost** — it's part of the melee hit. You still get your full Attack action. |
| Using highest slot on every Smite | Smite damage caps at **5d8** (4th-level slot). Using a 5th-level slot gives the same damage as 4th. |
| Lay on Hands is an action | In the 2014 PHB, Lay on Hands is an **action**. In the 2024 PHB, it's a **bonus action**. Check which version your table uses. |
| Forgetting Oath spells are always prepared | Oath spells are **always prepared and don't count against your prepared spell limit**. They're free extra spells. |
| Channel Divinity recharges on long rest only | Channel Divinity recharges on **short OR long rest**. Use it every fight. |
| Thinking Paladins are just "Smite bots" | *Bless*, *Shield of Faith*, *Command*, Lay on Hands, Aura of Protection — Paladins are incredible support characters. |

## 3. Combat Tactics Cheat Sheet (Levels 1–5)

### Level 1 — Lay on Hands + Melee
| Phase | What to Do |
|-------|-----------|
| **Action** | Attack with your weapon (longsword + shield is classic) |
| **Bonus Action** | Nothing yet (or Lay on Hands if 2024 rules) |
| **Between fights** | Use Lay on Hands to top off allies. Spend 1 HP to revive downed allies. |

### Level 2 — Smite + Spells Online
| Phase | What to Do |
|-------|-----------|
| **Concentration** | Cast *Bless* on round 1 (yourself + 2 allies). Maintain it. |
| **Action** | Attack. On a **critical hit**, Smite with your highest slot. |
| **Spell Slots** | You only have 2. Use one for *Bless*, save one for a clutch Smite or *Cure Wounds*. |
| **Don't** | Smite on every hit. You'll run dry by fight 2. |

### Level 3 — Sacred Oath
| Oath | Priority |
|------|----------|
| **Devotion** | Sacred Weapon (+CHA to attacks) for boss fights. Turn the Unholy vs undead hordes. |
| **Vengeance** | Vow of Enmity (bonus action, advantage on attacks vs 1 target) = Smite crits more often. |
| **Ancients** | *Ensnaring Strike* + oath spells for battlefield control. |

### Level 5 — Extra Attack + 2nd-Level Spells
| Phase | What to Do |
|-------|-----------|
| **Action** | Attack TWICE (Extra Attack). Smite on crits. |
| **New spells** | *Find Steed* (permanent mount!), *Aid* (+5 max HP to 3 allies), *Magic Weapon* |
| **Nova turn** | Vow of Enmity → 2 attacks with advantage → Smite on both hits = massive burst |

### General Tips
- **You are a half-caster.** Spell slots are precious. *Bless* is usually better than 2 Smites.
- **Position near allies.** At level 6, Aura of Protection gives +CHA to all saves within 10 ft. Stay close.
- **You're the best at picking up downed allies.** 1 HP from Lay on Hands + they're back in the fight.

## 4. Roleplay Prompts

### What Motivates a Paladin?
- **Sacred duty** — you swore an oath, and breaking it would destroy everything you are
- **Redemption** — you were once wicked, and your oath is your path back to the light
- **Righteous fury** — evil exists, and you are the sword that cuts it down (Vengeance)
- **Compassion** — you protect the weak because no one protected you when you needed it
- **Faith tested** — you believe, but the world keeps challenging that belief

### How Does a Paladin Train?
- Morning prayers and meditation — your power comes from your oath, not just your muscles
- Weapon drills combined with reciting tenets — every swing reinforced by conviction
- Studying scripture, law, or philosophy depending on your oath
- Practicing healing touch on the sick and wounded — Lay on Hands is a sacred gift
- Fasting and vigils before important battles

### Daily Routine
- Dawn: prayer and meditation (preparing spells takes 1 min/spell level)
- Polish armor until it gleams — appearance reflects inner virtue
- Check on allies' wellbeing — physical and spiritual
- Evening: reflect on the day's actions against your oath tenets
- Sleep with weapon within reach and conscience clear

### Personality Hooks
- Struggles with the tension between mercy and justice
- Sees every conflict as a moral test, not just a tactical problem
- Radiates calm confidence that makes others feel safe
- Quotes their oath tenets in conversation (sometimes annoyingly)
- Deeply uncomfortable with moral ambiguity — needs clear right and wrong

## 5. Multiclass Dip Tips

| Dip | Levels | What You Get | Why It's Good |
|-----|--------|-------------|---------------|
| **Warlock 1–2 (Hexblade)** | 1–2 levels | CHA for weapon attacks, *Hex* (1d6/hit), short rest slots for Smiting | Use CHA for attacks AND spells AND aura. *Hex* + Smite = nuclear damage. Short rest Warlock slots = more Smites. |
| **Sorcerer 2** | 2 levels | Font of Magic, metamagic | Convert sorcery points to spell slots for more Smites. Quickened *Booming Blade* + Attack. |
| **Fighter 1** | 1 level | Fighting Style (stack with Paladin's), Second Wind, CON saves | Two Fighting Styles (Defense + Dueling). Second Wind for self-healing. |
| **Bard 1** | 1 level | Bardic Inspiration, more spell slots | CHA synergy. Extra spell slots = more Smites. Inspiration helps allies. |

### Best Combos
- **Paladin 6 / Warlock X (Hexblade)**: CHA for everything. Aura of Protection + short rest Smite slots. The most popular Paladin multiclass.
- **Paladin 6 / Sorcerer X**: "Sorcadin." Quickened spells + Smites + Aura. Incredible nova damage.
- **Paladin 2 / Fighter X**: Just 2 levels of Paladin gives Divine Smite + Lay on Hands on a Fighter chassis with more attacks and ASIs.

> [!warning] Multiclass Requirements
> Paladin requires **STR 13 and CHA 13** to multiclass into or out of.
