# ⚔️ Monk — New Player Tips

> Quick-reference guide for getting the most out of your Monk at levels 1–5.

---

## 1. Creative Uses for Class Features

### Martial Arts — Your Fists Are Weapons
- **You can use DEX for unarmed strikes AND monk weapons.** This means your AC stat (DEX) is also your attack stat. No MAD (Multiple Ability Dependency) conflict between offense and defense.
- **The bonus action unarmed strike is FREE — no ki cost.** At level 1 you get 2 attacks per turn (Attack + bonus action) without spending anything. Flurry of Blows upgrades this to 3.
- **Monk weapons include quarterstaffs.** Use a quarterstaff two-handed (1d8 via Versatile) for your Attack action, then punch with your bonus action. Best damage at levels 1–4.

### Ki — Versatile Resource
- **Step of the Wind doubles your jump distance AND gives Dash/Disengage.** Jump over a 20-foot chasm, leap onto a balcony, or vault over enemies. Monks are the best at vertical movement.
- **Patient Defense (Dodge as bonus action) is underrated.** When surrounded or low on HP, Dodge imposes disadvantage on ALL attacks against you. Combine with your high AC and speed to survive.
- **Step of the Wind + Dash = absurd movement.** Base 30 + 10 (Unarmored Movement) = 40 ft. Dash action = 80 ft. Step of the Wind Dash = 120 ft in one turn at level 2.
- **Ki recharges on SHORT rest.** Don't hoard it — spend it every fight and push for short rests.

### Deflect Missiles — Catch and Throw
- **If you reduce the damage to 0, you CATCH the projectile.** Then spend 1 ki to throw it back as a ranged attack (20/60 ft) using your Martial Arts die. You can kill an archer with their own arrow.
- **The reduction scales with level:** 1d10 + DEX + monk level. At level 5 with +4 DEX, that's 1d10 + 9 — you'll catch most arrows.

### Unarmored Movement
- **At 9th level you can run on walls and water.** But even at low levels, +10 ft speed lets you reach backline enemies that other melee characters can't.
- **You don't provoke opportunity attacks if you use Step of the Wind (Disengage).** Hit-and-run: attack, then Disengage and retreat to safety.

### Slow Fall
- **Use your reaction to reduce fall damage by 5 × monk level.** At level 4, that's 20 damage reduced — you can jump off a 20-foot wall and take zero damage. Use this for dramatic entrances.

## 2. Common Mistakes & Misunderstandings

| ❌ Mistake | ✅ Correct Rule |
|-----------|----------------|
| Stunning Strike is once per round | You can attempt Stunning Strike on **every melee hit** (each costs 1 ki). Multiple attempts per turn if you have ki. |
| Flurry of Blows replaces the free bonus action strike | Flurry of Blows **replaces** the Martial Arts bonus action strike. You get 2 unarmed strikes (Flurry) instead of 1 (Martial Arts). Not 3. |
| Wearing armor "just for the AC" | ANY armor disables Martial Arts, Unarmored Defense, AND Unarmored Movement. Never wear armor as a monk. |
| Using a shield | Shields disable all monk features that require "no armor or shield." |
| Monk weapons include longswords/martial weapons | Monk weapons are **shortswords + simple melee weapons without two-handed or heavy**. No longswords, no greatswords. |
| Deflect Missiles works on spell attacks | Deflect Missiles only works on **ranged weapon attacks**, not spell attacks like *Fire Bolt* or *Eldritch Blast*. |
| Ki recharges on long rest only | Ki recharges on **short OR long rest** (with 30 min meditation). Short rests are essential for monks. |

## 3. Combat Tactics Cheat Sheet (Levels 1–5)

### Level 1 — Two Attacks, No Resources
| Phase | What to Do |
|-------|-----------|
| **Action** | Attack with quarterstaff (1d8 versatile) or shortsword |
| **Bonus Action** | FREE unarmed strike (Martial Arts) |
| **Movement** | Use your speed to reach squishier targets in the back |

### Level 2 — Ki Unlocked
| Situation | Spend Ki On |
|-----------|------------|
| **Need more damage** | Flurry of Blows (1 ki → 2 bonus action strikes instead of 1) = 3 attacks total |
| **Need to survive** | Patient Defense (1 ki → Dodge as bonus action) |
| **Need to reposition** | Step of the Wind (1 ki → Dash or Disengage as bonus action + double jump) |
| **Default** | Free Martial Arts bonus strike (0 ki) — save ki for when it matters |

### Level 3 — Subclass + Deflect Missiles
| Subclass | Priority |
|----------|----------|
| **Open Hand** | Use Flurry of Blows → impose knock prone (DEX save), push 15 ft (STR save), or deny reactions. Knock prone THEN attack for advantage. |
| **Way of Shadow** | Spend 2 ki for *Pass Without Trace* (+10 Stealth for whole party). Best stealth spell in the game. |
| **Reaction** | Save your reaction for Deflect Missiles if enemies have ranged attacks. |

### Level 5 — Stunning Strike + Extra Attack
| Phase | What to Do |
|-------|-----------|
| **Action** | Attack TWICE (Extra Attack) |
| **On hit** | Attempt Stunning Strike (1 ki, CON save). If it fails, try again on hit 2. |
| **Bonus Action** | Flurry of Blows (1 ki) for 2 more attacks = 4 total. More chances to stun. |
| **If target is stunned** | All attacks have advantage, auto-fail STR/DEX saves, can't take reactions. Devastating. |

### General Tips
- **You are a skirmisher, not a tank.** Hit, stun, retreat. Don't stand in the middle of 5 enemies.
- **Prioritize stunning high-value targets.** Stun the enemy spellcaster, not the goblin.
- **Short rests are CRITICAL.** Your ki pool is small (= monk level). You need short rests to function.

## 4. Roleplay Prompts

### What Motivates a Monk?
- **Seeking perfection** — the body is a temple, and you are still building it
- **Atonement** — you did something terrible before the monastery took you in
- **Wandering teacher** — you left the monastery to learn what books can't teach
- **Duty to the order** — your master sent you on a mission only you can complete
- **Inner peace through outer conflict** — you fight to understand yourself

### How Does a Monk Train?
- Meditation at dawn — 30 minutes minimum (this is how you recover ki)
- Kata practice: flowing sequences of strikes, blocks, and movement
- Balance exercises: standing on one foot on a post, walking a tightrope
- Breathing exercises to channel ki — inhale power, exhale weakness
- Sparring blindfolded to sharpen other senses

### Daily Routine
- Wake before anyone else, meditate in silence
- Simple breakfast — monks eat simply and deliberately
- Practice forms and stretches while others break camp
- Walk barefoot when possible — feel the earth, stay grounded
- Evening: meditate again, reflect on the day's lessons

### Personality Hooks
- Speaks in proverbs and koans that are sometimes profound, sometimes nonsense
- Refuses to use weapons — fists are more honest
- Fascinated by other fighting styles — watches warriors spar with intense focus
- Eats very little but is particular about what they eat
- Calm in combat but surprisingly competitive about trivial things (races, arm wrestling)

## 5. Multiclass Dip Tips

| Dip | Levels | What You Get | Why It's Good |
|-----|--------|-------------|---------------|
| **Rogue 1** | 1 level | Sneak Attack (1d6 with shortsword), Expertise | Expertise in Stealth or Acrobatics. Sneak Attack triggers easily with Stunning Strike (advantage). |
| **Fighter 1** | 1 level | Fighting Style (Defense doesn't work — no armor), Second Wind, CON saves | Second Wind = bonus action healing. CON save proficiency helps maintain effects. |
| **Cleric 1 (War)** | 1 level | *Bless*, *Shield of Faith*, bonus action attacks | *Bless* adds 1d4 to your many attack rolls. War Priest bonus attacks stack with Martial Arts. |
| **Druid 1** | 1 level | *Goodberry*, *Absorb Elements*, WIS-based casting | WIS synergy — your ki DC and spell DC use the same stat. |
| **Ranger 2** | 2 levels | Fighting Style (Dueling for shortsword), *Hunter's Mark* (1d6/hit) | *Hunter's Mark* on 3–4 attacks/turn is 3d6–4d6 extra damage. Requires concentration. |

### Best Combos
- **Monk X / Rogue 1**: Expertise + Sneak Attack. Shadow Monk + Rogue is the ultimate ninja.
- **Monk 5 / Cleric 1 (War)**: *Bless* on yourself + 4 attacks/turn = 4 extra d4s to hit.
- **Monk X / Ranger 2**: *Hunter's Mark* on 4 attacks = 4d6 extra damage/turn.

> [!warning] Multiclass Requirements
> Monk requires **DEX 13 and WIS 13** to multiclass into or out of.
