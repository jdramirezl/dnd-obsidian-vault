# Yuan-ti

> **Type**: Monstrosity/Humanoid (yuan-ti) | **CR**: 1 / 3 / 7 | **Source**: Monster Manual p.307–311

Yuan-ti are devious serpent folk devoid of compassion. From remote temples in jungles, swamps, and deserts, the yuan-ti plot to supplant and dominate all other races and to make themselves gods.

## Sidebar: Serpent Gods

The yuan-ti revere a number of powerful entities as gods:

- **Dendar, the Night Serpent.** Dendar's followers say that one day she will grow so large from feasting on the fears and nightmares of the world that she will devour it whole. Yuan-ti that serve Dendar terrorize other creatures in any way they can, growing and nurturing the fears of humanoids to feed the Night Serpent.
- **Merrshaulk, Master of the Pit.** Merrshaulk is the long-slumbering chief deity of the yuan-ti. His priests are yuan-ti abominations that maintain traditions of living sacrifice and cause suffering in the god's name. With enough vile acts, the abominations believe Merrshaulk will reawaken and restore the yuan-ti to their rightful place.
- **Sseth, the Sibilant Death.** Sseth appeared to the yuan-ti of antiquity in the form of a winged yuan-ti claiming to be an avatar of Merrshaulk. Many of Merrshaulk's devout turned to the worship of Sseth. Some yuan-ti suspect Sseth is an usurper taking advantage of Merrshaulk's slumber — and might even have devoured Merrshaulk.

## Lore

**Forsaken Humanity.** The yuan-ti were once humans who thrived in the earliest days of civilization and worshiped serpents as totem animals. Their advanced philosophy taught the virtue of detachment from emotion and of clear, focused thought. Through foul sorcery, the yuan-ti bred with snakes, utterly sacrificing their humanity to become like the serpent gods in form, thought, and emotion.

**Serpent Kings of Fallen Empires.** The yuan-ti view their physical transformation as a transcendent moment, allowing them to shed their frail humanity like dead skin. Those that did not transform became slaves or food. The yuan-ti empires withered or were defeated, and the serpent folk were left in the ruins of their great capitals.

**Cold of Heart.** Humanoid emotions are foreign to most yuan-ti, which understand sentiment only as an exploitable weakness. Yuan-ti first influence other creatures with the promise of wealth and power. A yuan-ti that acts honorably does so only as part of a grander design.

**False Worship.** Yuan-ti life revolves around their temples, yet they don't love the gods they worship. Instead, they see worship as a means to attain power. A yuan-ti believes an individual who attains enough power can devour and replace one of the yuan-ti gods.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!quote] Tactical Deep Dive
> See [[Yuan-ti-Revisited]] for detailed combat tactics from *The Monsters Know What They're Doing*.


> [!tip] Magic Resistance on Every Single One
> ALL yuan-ti have advantage on saving throws against spells. This makes them uniquely resistant to caster-heavy parties. *Hold person*, *fireball*, *banishment* — all have reduced effectiveness. Plan encounters knowing that spell-based crowd control will fail more often than usual.

- **What they are:** A caste-based serpent society. Purebloods (CR 1) are near-human infiltrators. Malisons (CR 3) are half-serpent warriors in three body types. Abominations (CR 7) are monstrous serpent-bodied leaders. All have Magic Resistance and poison immunity.
- **When to use:** Jungle temples, political intrigue, infiltration plots, cult storylines. Purebloods make excellent hidden agents in cities. Malisons are temple guards. Abominations are the masterminds.
- **Key combat tactics:**
  - *Pureblood (CR 1):* Two scimitar attacks or shortbow with 2d6 poison. *[[Suggestion|Suggestion]]* 3/day is the real threat — social manipulation before combat. *Poison spray* cantrip for close range. Deception +6 makes them excellent spies.
  - *Malison (CR 3):* Type 1 (snake head) is the most versatile — melee or ranged with poison. Type 2 (snake arms) makes two poison bites per turn (avg 24 damage). Type 3 (serpent body) can constrict + [[Restrained|restrain]]. *Suggestion* 3/day. All are shapechangers.
  - *Abomination (CR 7):* Three melee attacks per turn (bite + constrict + scimitar) or two ranged. Bite deals 3d6 poison, longbow deals 3d6 poison at range. **Fear** 1/day, *suggestion* 3/day. Constrict [[Restrained|restrains]].
- **Notable abilities:** Magic Resistance on ALL yuan-ti is extremely strong — advantage on all spell saves. Poison immunity means they're immune to each other's venom. Shapechanging (malison/abomination) allows escape as snakes. *Suggestion* across all types enables social encounters to turn dangerous.
- **Conditions inflicted:** [[Grappled|grappled]]/[[Restrained|restrained]] (Constrict), [[Frightened|frightened]] (*fear*)
- **Suggested encounter pairings:** A pureblood spy ring in a city (social/investigation). Temple guard: 1 abomination + 2 malisons + 4 purebloods. Malison Type 2 pair as assassins. Abomination with swarms of poisonous snakes as a boss fight.
