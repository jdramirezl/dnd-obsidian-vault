# Yetis

![Yetis](https://www.aidedd.org/dnd/images/yeti.jpg)


> **Type**: Large/Huge monstrosity, chaotic evil | **CR**: 3 / 9 | **Source**: Monster Manual p.305–307

Hulking predators of alpine peaks, yetis stalk through blizzards in a ceaseless hunt for food. Their snow-white fur lets them move like ghosts against the frozen landscape, and their icy simian eyes can freeze prey in place.

## Yeti

> **Type**: Large monstrosity, chaotic evil | **CR**: 3 (700 XP) | **Source**: Monster Manual p.306

### Stat Block

**Armor Class** 12 (natural armor)
**Hit Points** 51 (6d10 + 18)
**Speed** 40 ft., climb 40 ft.

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| 18 (+4) | 13 (+1) | 16 (+3) | 8 (-1) | 12 (+1) | 7 (-2) |

**Skills** Perception +3, Stealth +3
**Damage Immunities** cold
**Senses** darkvision 60 ft., passive Perception 13
**Languages** Yeti
**Challenge** 3 (700 XP)

#### Traits

**Fear of Fire.** If the yeti takes fire damage, it has disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks until the end of its next turn.

**Keen Smell.** The yeti has advantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on smell.

**Snow Camouflage.** The yeti has advantage on Dexterity (Stealth) checks made to hide in snowy terrain.

#### Actions

**Multiattack.** The yeti can use its Chilling Gaze and makes two claw attacks.

**Claw.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +6 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 7 (1d6 + 4) slashing damage plus 3 (1d6) cold damage.

**Chilling Gaze.** The yeti targets one creature it can see within 30 feet of it. If the target can see the yeti, the target must succeed on a DC 13 Constitution saving throw against this magic or take 10 (3d6) cold damage and then be [[Paralyzed|paralyzed]] for 1 minute, unless it is immune to cold damage. The target can repeat the saving throw at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on itself on a success. If the target's saving throw is successful, or if the effect ends on it, the target is immune to the Chilling Gaze of all yetis (but not abominable yetis) for 1 hour.

## Lore

**Keen Hunters.** Folk of the high peaks travel in groups and go armed, knowing that yetis can smell living flesh from miles away. Yetis hunt in solitude or in small family groups. When creatures flee or engage a yeti in battle, other yetis might catch the scent of blood and close in. The territorial yetis fight one another for the spoils, and yetis slain in the fight are also eaten.

**Terrifying Howlers.** Before an avalanche, a blizzard, or a deadly frost, the yetis' howls sweep down the mountain slopes on the icy wind. Some believe the voices of loved ones killed in avalanches sound out in the wails of the yetis, crying warnings of ill omen.

**Brutal Rampagers.** When mountain herds are abundant, yetis stay clear of humanoid realms. Driven by hunger, they attack settlements in waves, breaking down gates and stockade walls. Devious mountain folk sometimes use yetis as unwitting weapons — laying slaughtered sheep to draw yetis into an enemy's camp.

**Abominable Yetis.** An abominable yeti is larger than a normal yeti, standing three times as tall as a human. It typically lives and hunts alone, though a pair might live together long enough to raise young. These towering yetis are highly territorial and savage, attacking and devouring any warm-blooded creatures they encounter.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!tip] Chilling Gaze → Paralysis → Auto-Crit
> The yeti's Chilling Gaze paralyzes on a failed DC 13 Con save. Melee attacks against paralyzed targets have advantage and auto-crit within 5 ft. A paralyzed PC takes 2× claw damage. Fear of Fire is the hard counter — any fire damage gives the yeti disadvantage on everything.

- **What they are:** Alpine predators with cold-based attacks and paralyzing gaze. Yeti (CR 3) is a solid mid-level threat; Abominable Yeti (CR 9) is a devastating boss-tier version with breath weapon.
- **When to use:** Mountain passes, frozen peaks, blizzard encounters, arctic wilderness. Yetis work as ambush predators in snowstorms where their Snow Camouflage shines.
- **Key combat tactics:**
  - *Yeti (CR 3):* Chilling Gaze + two claws per turn. Gaze [[Paralyzed|paralyzes]] on DC 13 Con — then claws auto-crit the [[Paralyzed|paralyzed]] target. Pack of 2–3 yetis can lock down a party fast. Stealth +3 with advantage in snow for ambushes.
  - *Abominable Yeti (CR 9):* Same gaze/claw combo but DC 18 and much higher damage. Cold Breath (Recharge 6) deals 45 (10d8) in a 30-ft cone — devastating opener. Gaze immunity from regular yetis does NOT protect against the abominable version.
- **Notable abilities:** Fear of Fire is a critical weakness — fire damage gives disadvantage on attacks and checks. Smart parties will exploit this. Cold immunity means cold-based casters are useless. Snow Camouflage + Keen Smell makes them nearly impossible to avoid in their terrain.
- **Conditions inflicted:** [[Paralyzed|paralyzed]] (Chilling Gaze)
- **Suggested encounter pairings:** A pack of 3–4 yetis during a blizzard (visibility-limited). An abominable yeti with 2 regular yetis as a mountain boss fight. Yetis drawn to a camp by a rival warlord's bait — social encounter turned combat.
