# Yugoloths

![Yugoloths](https://www.aidedd.org/dnd/images/arcanaloth.jpg)


> **Type**: Medium/Large fiend (yugoloth), neutral evil | **CR**: 5 / 9 / 12 / 13 | **Source**: Monster Manual p.311–315

Yugoloths are fickle fiends that inhabit the planes of Acheron, Gehenna, Hades, and Carceri. They act as mercenaries and are notorious for their shifting loyalties. They are the embodiments of avarice. Before serving under anyone's banner, a yugoloth asks the only question on its mind: *What's in it for me?*

## Variant: Yugoloth Summoning

Some yugoloths have an action option that allows them to summon other yugoloths.

**Summon Yugoloth (1/Day).** The yugoloth chooses what to summon and attempts a magical summoning.
- An **arcanaloth** has a 40 percent chance of summoning one arcanaloth.
- A **mezzoloth** has a 30 percent chance of summoning one mezzoloth.
- A **nycaloth** has a 50 percent chance of summoning 1d4 mezzoloths or one nycaloth.
- An **ultroloth** has a 50 percent chance of summoning 1d6 mezzoloths, 1d4 nycaloths, or one ultroloth.

A summoned yugoloth appears in an unoccupied space within 60 feet of its summoner, does as it pleases (unless its summoner is an ultroloth, in which case it acts as an ally of its summoner), and can't summon other yugoloths. The summoned yugoloth remains for 1 minute, until it or its summoner dies, or until its summoner takes a bonus action to dismiss it.

## Lore

**Spawn of Gehenna.** The first yugoloths were created by a sisterhood of night hags on Gehenna, commissioned by Asmodeus in the hope of creating an army of fiends not bound to the Nine Hells. The hags crafted four magic tomes — the Books of Keeping — recording the true names of every yugoloth they created, save one: the General of Gehenna. When the sisterhood dissolved, the Books were lost or stolen, and the yugoloths gained independence.

**Fiendish Mercenaries.** Summoned yugoloths demand much for their time and loyalty. Whatever promises a yugoloth makes are quickly broken when a better opportunity presents itself. Unlike demons, yugoloths can be reasoned with, but unlike devils, they are rarely true to their word. The high cost of maintaining a yugoloth army's loyalty typically exceeds what any warlord on the Material Plane can pay.

**Back to Gehenna.** When a yugoloth dies, it dissolves into a pool of ichor and reforms at full strength on Gehenna. Only on its native plane can a yugoloth be destroyed permanently. When permanently destroyed, its name vanishes from every Book of Keeping.

**The Books of Keeping.** Each Book still exists, drifting from plane to plane. A yugoloth summoned using its true name, as inscribed in the Books, is forced to serve its summoner obediently — but hates being controlled and will follow instructions to the letter while looking for opportunities to misinterpret them.

**The General of Gehenna.** Somewhere in the brimstone wastes of Gehenna roams an ultroloth so strong that none contests his power. Many yugoloths search for this great general in the hope of serving with him. His personal name is unknown, and even the Books of Keeping contain no mention of this powerful entity.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!warning] Arcanaloth (CR 12) / Ultroloth (CR 13) — Fiendish Commanders
> The arcanaloth is a 16th-level wizard with *counterspell*, *chain lightning*, and *finger of death*. The ultroloth has Hypnotic Gaze (DC 17 Wis, stun) plus *mass suggestion* and *fire storm*. Both reform on Gehenna when killed — they can't be permanently destroyed off their home plane.

> [!tip] They Reform on Gehenna
> All yugoloths killed outside Gehenna reform there eventually. This means killing one is a temporary solution — it will come back, and it will remember who killed it. Use this for recurring mercenary villains who hold grudges across campaigns.

- **What they are:** Fiendish mercenaries of the Lower Planes. Mezzoloths (CR 5) are insectoid foot soldiers. Nycaloths (CR 9) are gargoyle-like airborne shock troops. Arcanaloths (CR 12) are jackal-headed spellcaster diplomats. Ultroloths (CR 13) are featureless commanders with hypnotic gaze. All have Magic Resistance, magic weapons, teleport, and acid/poison immunity.
- **When to use:** Blood War campaigns, planar mercenary contracts, fiendish negotiations. Arcanaloths are perfect for contract-based social encounters. Ultroloths command yugoloth armies. The Books of Keeping are excellent quest hooks.
- **Key combat tactics:**
  - *Mezzoloth (CR 5):* Claw + trident per turn. **Cloudkill** 1/day is devastating in enclosed spaces. *[[Darkness|Darkness]]* 2/day with blindsight 60 ft lets it fight blind while enemies can't see. AC 18 is very high for CR 5.
  - *Nycaloth (CR 9):* Fly 60 ft for hit-and-run. Claw inflicts stacking fiendish wound (2d4 per hit, cumulative). Greataxe deals 2d12+5. Can teleport as part of Multiattack. *[[Invisibility|Invisibility]]* and **mirror image** at will for evasion.
  - *Arcanaloth (CR 12):* Full 16th-level wizard spellcasting — *[[Counterspell|counterspell]]*, *[[Fireball|fireball]]*, **banishment**, *[[Chain Lightning|chain lightning]]*, *[[Finger of Death|finger of death]]*. Plus innate *[[Magic Missile|magic missile]]* and *invisibility* at will. Truesight 120 ft. Speaks all languages. Poison claws in melee.
  - *Ultroloth (CR 13):* Hypnotic Gaze [[Stunned|stuns]] on DC 17 Wis + three longsword attacks per turn. Innate *[[Mass Suggestion|mass suggestion]]*, *[[Fire Storm|fire storm]]*, *[[Wall of Fire|wall of fire]]*. *[[Suggestion|Suggestion]]* and **detect thoughts** at will for social manipulation. Immune to [[Charmed|charmed]] and [[Frightened|frightened]].
- **Notable abilities:** All yugoloths reform on Gehenna when killed — they can't be permanently destroyed off-plane. Summoning variant can snowball encounters. Arcanaloths have *counterspell* — they shut down enemy casters. Nycaloth fiendish wounds stack and require an action + Medicine check to stop.
- **Conditions inflicted:** [[Stunned|stunned]] (Ultroloth Hypnotic Gaze), [[Poisoned|poisoned]] (Arcanaloth Claws)
- **Suggested encounter pairings:** Mezzoloth squad (4–6) as mercenary foot soldiers. Nycaloth pair as aerial assassins. Arcanaloth as a contract broker with mezzoloth bodyguards. Ultroloth commanding a mixed force of all types. An arcanaloth holding a Book of Keeping as a campaign-level quest.
