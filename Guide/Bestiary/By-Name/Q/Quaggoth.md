### Traits

**Wounded Fury.** While it has 10 hit points or fewer, the quaggoth has advantage on attack rolls. In addition, it deals an extra 7 (2d6) damage to any target it hits with a melee attack.

### Actions

**Multiattack.** The quaggoth makes two claw attacks.

**Claw.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +5 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 6 (1d6 + 3) slashing damage.

## Variant: Quaggoth Thonot

A quaggoth thonot is a normal quaggoth with a challenge rating of 3 (700 XP) and the following additional trait.

**Innate Spellcasting (Psionics).** The quaggoth's innate spellcasting ability is Wisdom (spell save DC 11). The quaggoth can innately cast the following spells, requiring no components:
- At will: *feather fall*, *mage hand* (the hand is invisible)
- 1/day each: *cure wounds*, *enlarge/reduce*, *heat metal*, *mirror image*

## Lore

Savage and territorial, quaggoths climb the chasms of the Underdark. They maul their foes in a frenzy, becoming even more murderous in the face of death.

**Quaggoth Origins.** Quaggoths were never an enlightened species, but they were not always the brutal Underdark denizens they are today. In a distant age, quaggoth tribes dwelled upon the surface as nocturnal arboreal hunters, possessing their own language and culture. When elves appeared in the mortal realm, they clashed with the quaggoths, eventually driving them to near extinction. Only by fleeing deep into the Underdark did the quaggoths survive.

**Servants of the Drow.** The ancient enmity between quaggoths and surface elves makes them easy converts to the dark elf cause. Wealthy drow houses have legions of quaggoths at their command. The drow cultivate the quaggoths' hatred of the elves by leading them on surface raids against known elven enclaves.

**Thonots.** Some quaggoths absorb psionic energy that suffuses certain parts of the Underdark. When a tribe discovers that one of its own has inherited such powers, they press it into the role of tribal shaman, or thonot. A thonot keeps a tribe's lore and ensures its superiority against enemies.

**Poison Immunity.** Generations of hunting venomous subterranean creatures and perpetual exposure to the molds and fungi that grow in the depths have forced quaggoths to adapt immunities to poisons of all kinds.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!quote] Tactical Deep Dive
> See [[Quaggoth-Tactics]] for detailed combat tactics from *The Monsters Know What They're Doing*.


> [!tip] Wounded Fury Makes Them Deadlier Dying
> Below 10 HP, a quaggoth gains advantage on attacks AND +2d6 damage per hit. Don't leave them at low HP — either finish them off or expect a desperate burst of 13 damage per claw with advantage. This punishes parties that spread damage instead of focusing fire.

> [!example] Quaggoth Thonot (CR 3)
> Adds psionic spellcasting: *heat metal* punishes armored PCs (no save to avoid ongoing damage if they keep holding the item), *enlarge/reduce* buffs a champion quaggoth, and *mirror image* makes the thonot hard to hit. The brains behind the brawn.

- **What it is:** A savage, ape-like Underdark humanoid. CR 2 with 45 HP, two claw attacks, and Wounded Fury — when below 10 HP, it gains advantage on attacks and +2d6 damage per hit. Poison immune. The Thonot variant (CR 3) adds psionic spellcasting.
- **When to use:** Underdark encounters, drow outposts, subterranean ambushes. Quaggoths serve as drow shock troops and expendable muscle. A pack of quaggoths led by a thonot makes a solid Underdark encounter for levels 3–5.
- **Key combat tactics:** Swarm in packs, climbing walls and ceilings (climb 30 ft.) to attack from unexpected angles. Wounded Fury makes them more dangerous as they take damage — don't leave them at low HP. Thonot uses **heat metal** to punish armored characters, *enlarge/reduce* to buff a champion, and **mirror image** for self-defense.
- **Notable abilities:** Wounded Fury is the defining feature — a quaggoth at 10 HP or less deals 6 + 7 (1d6+3 + 2d6) per claw with advantage. Poison immunity means they ignore many Underdark hazards. Darkvision 120 ft. for deep Underdark operations. Climb 30 ft. for three-dimensional combat.
- **Conditions inflicted:** None (base); Thonot's *heat metal* forces repeated Con saves or drop the item
- **Suggested encounter pairings:** A pack of 4–6 quaggoths with a thonot leader. Drow elite warriors commanding quaggoth shock troops. Quaggoths climbing out of a chasm to ambush the party on a narrow bridge. Pair with a drider or drow priestess for a drow-themed encounter.
