# Gnolls

![Gnolls](https://www.aidedd.org/dnd/images/gnoll.jpg)


> **Type**: Medium humanoid (gnoll) / fiend (gnoll) | **CR**: 1/2 / 2 / 4 | **Source**: Monster Manual p.162–164

Gnolls are feral humanoids that attack settlements along the frontiers and borderlands of civilization without warning, slaughtering their victims and devouring their flesh.

## Lore

**Demonic Origin.** The origin of the gnolls traces back to a time when the demon lord Yeenoghu found his way to the Material Plane and ran amok. Packs of ordinary hyenas followed in his wake, scavenging the demon lord's kills. Those hyenas were transformed into the first gnolls.

**Nomadic Destroyers.** Gnolls are dangerous because they strike at random. They emerge from the wilderness, plunder and slaughter, then move elsewhere. They attack like a plague of locusts.

**Gnoll Fang of Yeenoghu** — Sometimes the demon lord rewards his worshipers by allowing one of them to be possessed by a demonic spirit. A hyena that feasts on a fang's slain foe undergoes a horrible transformation, becoming a full-grown adult gnoll. The Fang of Yeenoghu is typed as a **fiend**, not a humanoid.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!quote] Tactical Deep Dive
> See [[Gnolls-Revisited]] for detailed combat tactics from *The Monsters Know What They're Doing*.


> [!tip] DM Tip
> Rampage gives a free bonus action bite whenever a gnoll drops a creature to 0 HP — in a horde, kills snowball as each downed target triggers another attack. Pack Lord's Incite Rampage lets an ally attack as a reaction. Fang of Yeenoghu creates new gnolls from humanoids it kills.

- **What they are:** Demonic hyena-humanoids in three tiers. Gnoll (CR 1/2, basic raider), Pack Lord (CR 2, leader with Incite Rampage), Fang of Yeenoghu (CR 4, fiend-typed champion). All share the Rampage trait.
- **When to use:** Frontier raids, wilderness ambushes, demon-cult storylines. Gnolls are perfect "horde" enemies for levels 1–5. The Fang of Yeenoghu ties them to Yeenoghu and the Abyss for deeper campaigns.
- **Key combat tactics:**
  - *Gnoll (CR 1/2):* Rampage gives a free bite + movement when they drop a target. In packs of 6+, they chain kills into more attacks. AC 15 with shield.
  - *Pack Lord (CR 2):* Incite Rampage (Recharge 5–6) grants a nearby gnoll a reaction attack. Glaive has 10-ft reach. Two attacks + Incite per turn.
  - *Fang of Yeenoghu (CR 4):* Three attacks per turn (bite + 2 claws). Bite adds DC 12 Con or 2d6 poison. Typed as fiend — immune to *[[Hold Person|hold person]]* and similar humanoid-targeting spells. Speaks Abyssal.
- **Notable abilities:** Rampage is the defining gnoll mechanic — snowballing bonus attacks when they kill. Fang of Yeenoghu's fiend typing is a common trap for players who prepare *hold person*.
- **Conditions inflicted:** None directly (poison damage from Fang bite, not [[Poisoned|poisoned]] condition)
- **Suggested encounter pairings:** Pack of 6 gnolls + 1 pack lord for a standard raid. Add a Fang of Yeenoghu + hyenas for a larger warband. Pair with a shoosuva (demon) or leucrotta for Yeenoghu-themed encounters.
