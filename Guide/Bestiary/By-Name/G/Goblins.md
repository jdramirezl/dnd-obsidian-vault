# Goblins

![Goblins](https://www.aidedd.org/dnd/images/goblin.jpg)


> **Type**: Small humanoid (goblinoid), neutral evil | **CR**: 1/4 / 1 | **Source**: Monster Manual p.165–166

Goblins are small, black-hearted, selfish humanoids that lair in caves, abandoned mines, despoiled dungeons, and other dismal settings. Individually weak, goblins gather in large — sometimes overwhelming — numbers.

**Redirect Attack.** When a creature the goblin can see targets it with an attack, the goblin chooses another goblin within 5 feet of it. The two goblins swap places, and the chosen goblin becomes the target instead.

## Lore

**Goblinoids.** Goblins belong to a family of creatures called goblinoids. Their larger cousins, hobgoblins and bugbears, like to bully goblins into submission.

**Leaders and Followers.** Goblins are ruled by the strongest or smartest among them. Goblin bosses are easily ousted, and many goblin tribes are taken over by hobgoblin warlords or bugbear chiefs.

**Challenging Lairs.** Goblins festoon their lairs with alarms designed to signal the arrival of intruders. Those lairs are riddled with narrow tunnels and bolt-holes that human-sized creatures can't navigate.

**Rat Keepers and Wolf Riders.** Goblins have an affinity for rats and wolves, raising them to serve as companions and mounts, respectively. When they hunt from the backs of wolves, goblins use hit-and-run attacks.

**Worshipers of Maglubiyet.** Maglubiyet the Mighty One, the Lord of Depths and Darkness, is the greater god of goblinoids. Goblins believe that when they die in battle, their spirits join the ranks of Maglubiyet's army on the plane of Acheron.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!quote] Tactical Deep Dive
> See [[Goblin-Tactics]] for detailed combat tactics from *The Monsters Know What They're Doing*.


> [!tip] DM Tip
> Nimble Escape (bonus action Disengage or Hide) is what makes goblins dangerous — they hit and run every turn. In their warrens, use narrow tunnels (squeeze rules for Medium PCs), traps, and wolf mounts. The Goblin Boss uses Redirect Attack to sacrifice minions.

- **What they are:** The quintessential low-level D&D enemy. Goblin (CR 1/4) is a sneaky skirmisher; Goblin Boss (CR 1) is a cowardly leader. Both have Nimble Escape (bonus action Disengage/Hide) making them slippery.
- **When to use:** Starter dungeons, forest ambushes, cave lairs. The iconic first encounter for new campaigns. Scale up with wolf mounts, traps, and narrow tunnels.
- **Key combat tactics:**
  - *Goblin (CR 1/4):* AC 15 is high for CR 1/4. Nimble Escape lets it hit-and-run: attack, then bonus action Disengage or Hide. Stealth +6 means it hides effectively. In groups of 6+, they overwhelm with numbers.
  - *Goblin Boss (CR 1):* AC 17 is very high for CR 1. Two scimitar attacks (second at disadvantage). Redirect Attack is the signature move — when targeted, it swaps places with an adjacent goblin, making that goblin take the hit instead. Cowardly and effective.
- **Notable abilities:** Nimble Escape is what makes goblins annoying rather than trivial. Redirect Attack on the boss means the party has to kill the minions first or isolate the boss. Stealth +6 enables effective ambushes.
- **Conditions inflicted:** None
- **Suggested encounter pairings:** 6 goblins + 1 boss in a cave with narrow escape tunnels. Goblin wolf riders (goblins mounted on wolves). A goblin lair with traps, alarms, and a bugbear or hobgoblin overlord. Pair with worgs for a more dangerous mounted encounter.
