# Grick

![Grick](https://www.aidedd.org/dnd/images/grick.jpg)


> **Type**: Monstrosity, neutral | **CR**: 2 / 7 | **Source**: Monster Manual p.173–174

The wormlike grick waits unseen, blending in with the rock of the caves and caverns it haunts. Only when prey comes near does it rear up, its four barbed tentacles unfurling to reveal its hungry, snapping beak.

## Lore

**Passive Predators.** Gricks rarely hunt. Instead, they drag their rubbery bodies to places where creatures regularly pass, lurking out of sight amid rocky rubble and debris, squeezing into burrows, holes, or crevices, climbing up to ledges, or coiling around stalactites to drop on unwary prey.

**Roving Ambushers.** Gricks remain in an area until the food supply dwindles. When prey is scarce in the Underdark, gricks venture aboveground to hunt in the wilderness. A grick pack is often led by a single well-fed, oversized alpha around which the others congregate.

**Spoils of Slaughter.** Over time, grick lairs accumulate the cast-off possessions of intelligent prey. Underdark explorers sometimes seal off the routes leading to and from a grick lair to starve them, then claim the wealth of the foul creatures' victims.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!quote] Tactical Deep Dive
> See [[Grick-Tactics]] for detailed combat tactics from *The Monsters Know What They're Doing*.


> [!tip] DM Tip
> Both gricks resist nonmagical B/P/S — low-level parties without magic weapons will struggle. Stone Camouflage gives advantage on Stealth in rocky terrain. The Alpha (CR 7) has 10-ft reach and much higher damage — use it leading a pack of 3–4 regular gricks.

- **What they are:** Wormlike ambush predators with barbed tentacles and a snapping beak. Grick (CR 2) is Medium; Grick Alpha (CR 7) is Large with 10-ft reach and much higher damage. Both resist nonmagical B/P/S.
- **When to use:** Underdark tunnels, cave systems, rocky wilderness. Classic ambush monsters that lurk on ceilings and in crevices. The alpha leading a pack of 3–4 gricks is a solid mid-level encounter.
- **Key combat tactics:**
  - *Grick (CR 2):* Stone Camouflage for ambush. Tentacles (2d6+2) → conditional beak (1d6+2) for up to 14 damage/round. Damage resistance to nonmagical weapons makes it tough for martial characters without magic weapons. Climb 30 ft for vertical ambushes.
  - *Grick Alpha (CR 7):* Three attacks per round: tail (2d6+4), tentacles (4d8+4), conditional beak (2d8+4). Potential 46 damage/round. AC 18 with 75 HP. 10-ft reach on all attacks. A serious threat.
- **Notable abilities:** Damage resistance to nonmagical B/P/S is the key defensive feature — parties without magic weapons deal half damage. Stone Camouflage + climb speed enables excellent ambush positioning.
- **Conditions inflicted:** None
- **Suggested encounter pairings:** A grick alpha with 3–4 regular gricks in a cavern. Gricks lurking near a treasure hoard (the remains of previous victims). In a vertical shaft where they climb the walls and attack from all sides.
