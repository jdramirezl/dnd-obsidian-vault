## Lore

**Devourers of Flesh.** Like maggots or carrion beetles, ghouls thrive in places rank with decay and death. A ghoul's undead flesh never rots, and this monster can persist in a crypt or tomb for untold ages without feeding.

**Abyssal Origins.** Ghouls trace their origins to the Abyss. Doresain, the first of their kind, was an elf worshiper of Orcus. As a reward for feasting on humanoid flesh, Orcus transformed Doresain into the first ghoul. Since then, elves have been immune to the ghouls' paralytic touch.

**Ghasts.** Orcus sometimes infuses a ghoul with a stronger dose of abyssal energy, making a ghast. Whereas ghouls are little more than savage beasts, a ghast is cunning and can inspire a pack of ghouls to follow its commands.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!tip] DM Tip
> Ghoul paralysis (DC 10 Con) is devastating in packs — 4–6 ghouls can paralyze-lock an entire party. Elves are immune to ghoul paralysis (but NOT ghast paralysis). A ghast's Turning Defiance protects nearby ghouls from Turn Undead.

- **What they are:** Classic low-level undead pack hunters. Ghoul (CR 1) paralyzes with its claws; Ghast (CR 2) is a tougher leader variant with Stench and Turning Defiance. Elves are immune to ghoul paralysis (but NOT ghast paralysis).
- **When to use:** Graveyards, crypts, battlefields, sewers — anywhere corpses accumulate. A ghast leading a pack of 4–6 ghouls is a classic encounter for levels 2–4.
- **Key combat tactics:**
  - *Ghoul:* Swarms in packs. Claws paralyze on DC 10 Con (not elves or undead). Once a target is [[Paralyzed|paralyzed]], all attacks against it have advantage and melee hits are auto-crits. Pack of ghouls can lock down a party fast.
  - *Ghast:* Stench [[Poisoned|poisons]] creatures within 5 ft (DC 10 Con). Turning Defiance gives itself and nearby ghouls advantage vs. Turn Undead — counters clerics. Claws paralyze even elves (unlike ghouls). Higher damage across the board.
- **Notable abilities:** Ghoul paralysis is the defining threat — DC 10 is low but with 4+ ghouls attacking, someone will fail. Ghast's Turning Defiance is specifically designed to counter the cleric's best anti-undead tool.
- **Conditions inflicted:** [[Paralyzed|paralyzed]] (Claws), [[Poisoned|poisoned]] (Ghast Stench)
- **Suggested encounter pairings:** A ghast with 4–6 ghouls in a crypt. Ghouls emerging from graves during a nighttime encounter. Pair with a wight or wraith as a more powerful undead commander.
