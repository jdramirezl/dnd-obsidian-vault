# Giants

![Giants](https://www.aidedd.org/dnd/images/hill-giant.jpg)


> **Type**: Huge giant | **CR**: 5 / 7 / 8 / 9 / 9 / 13 | **Source**: Monster Manual p.149–157

Ancient empires once cast long shadows over a world that quaked beneath the giants' feet. In those lost days, these towering figures were dragon slayers, dreamers, crafters, and kings, but their kind fell from glory long ago.

## The Ordning

Each of the main giant races are related by common elements of history, religion, and culture. Giants belong to a caste structure called the ordning. Based on social class and highly organized, the ordning assigns a social rank to each giant. At the highest level, the races are ranked: storm giants are the highest, followed by cloud giants, fire giants, frost giants, stone giants, hill giants, and finally giant kin such as fomorians, ettins, and ogres.

## Lore

**Cloud Giant** — Cloud giants live extravagant lives high above the world. They are muscular with light skin and hair of silver or blue. A cloud giant earns its place in the ordning by the treasure it accumulates. They are inveterate gamblers with a hunger for high risks and high rewards. Their patron god is Memnor the Trickster.

**Fire Giant** — Master crafters and organized warriors, fire giants dwell among volcanoes, lava floes, and rocky mountains. Their mastery of metalwork is legendary. From birth, a fire giant is taught to embrace a legacy of war. Their renowned fighting prowess comes from endless discipline and training.

**Frost Giant** — Gigantic reavers from the freezing lands beyond civilization. They respect only brute strength and skill in battle. Frost giants prize gems and jewelry large enough to be worn and noticed, but save treasures for trading with other giants more adept at crafting.

**Hill Giant** — Selfish, dimwitted brutes that hunt, forage, and raid in constant search of food. Their laziness and dullness would long ago have spelled their end if not for their formidable size and strength.

**Stone Giant** — Reclusive, quiet, and peaceful as long as they are left alone. Among stone giants, artistry ranks as the greatest virtue. They view the surface world as a realm of dreams where nothing is entirely true or real.

**Storm Giant** — Contemplative seers that live in places far removed from mortal civilization. They can foretell the rise and fall of kings and empires. A storm giant roused to anger is a force of utter destruction.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!quote] Tactical Deep Dive
> See [[Giant-Tactics]] for detailed combat tactics from *The Monsters Know What They're Doing*.


> [!warning] Deadly Encounter
> Storm Giant CR 13 (party level 10+), Cloud Giant CR 9 (party level 7+), Fire Giant CR 9 (party level 7+). All giants throw rocks at 60/240 ft. for massive damage. Storm Giants have *lightning bolt* at will and legendary actions.

> [!tip] DM Tip
> Rock throwing (60/240 ft.) is the opener — 4d10+Str mod damage before the party even closes to melee. In melee, giants deal 3d8+Str with reach weapons. Hill giants are dumb brutes; storm giants are genius tacticians. Match tactics to Intelligence.

- **What they are:** Six giant types forming a hierarchy (the ordning). Hill (CR 5) → Stone (CR 7) → Frost (CR 8) → Fire/Cloud (CR 9) → Storm (CR 13). All are Huge with rock-throwing and massive melee damage.
- **When to use:** Hill giants for mid-level brute encounters. Stone giants for Underdark or mountain exploration. Frost giants for arctic raids. Fire giants for volcanic fortresses. Cloud giants for sky castles and intrigue. Storm giants for epic-tier encounters or wise NPCs.
- **Key combat tactics:**
  - *Hill (CR 5):* Simple brute. Two greatclub attacks (3d8+5) or rock (3d10+5). No special abilities. Dangerous in groups.
  - *Stone (CR 7):* Rock Catching negates thrown projectiles. Rock attack knocks [[Prone|prone]] on DC 17 Str. 15-ft reach. Stone Camouflage for ambush.
  - *Frost (CR 8):* Two greataxe attacks (3d12+6). Cold immune. Straightforward damage dealer.
  - *Fire (CR 9):* AC 18 (plate). Two greatsword attacks (6d6+7 = avg 28 each). Fire immune. Highest sustained melee damage.
  - *Cloud (CR 9):* Innate spellcasting (*fly*, *misty step*, *telekinesis*). 200 HP. Most versatile giant.
  - *Storm (CR 13):* Lightning Strike is devastating — 12d8 in a 10-ft radius at 500-ft range. 230 HP, +14 to hit, 6d6+9 greatsword. Amphibious for underwater encounters.
- **Conditions inflicted:** [[Prone|prone]] (Stone Giant rock)
- **Suggested encounter pairings:** Hill giants with ogres and dire wolves. Frost giants with winter wolves. Fire giants with hell hounds and enslaved dwarves. Cloud giants with griffons and perytons. Storm giants with rocs or as quest-giving NPCs.
