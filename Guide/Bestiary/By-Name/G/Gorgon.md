# Gorgon

![Gorgon](https://www.aidedd.org/dnd/images/gorgon.jpg)


> **Type**: Large monstrosity, unaligned | **CR**: 5 (1,800 XP) | **Source**: Monster Manual p.171

## Stat Block

**Armor Class** 19 (natural armor)
**Hit Points** 114 (12d10 + 48)
**Speed** 40 ft.

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| 20 (+5) | 11 (+0) | 18 (+4) | 2 (-4) | 12 (+1) | 7 (-2) |

**Skills** Perception +4
**Condition Immunities** [[Petrified|petrified]]
**Senses** darkvision 60 ft., passive Perception 14
**Languages** —
**Challenge** 5 (1,800 XP)

### Traits

**Trampling Charge.** If the gorgon moves at least 20 feet straight toward a creature and then hits it with a gore attack on the same turn, that target must succeed on a DC 16 Strength saving throw or be knocked [[Prone|prone]]. If the target is [[Prone|prone]], the gorgon can make one attack with its hooves against it as a bonus action.

### Actions

**Gore.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +8 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 18 (2d12 + 5) piercing damage.

**Hooves.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +8 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 16 (2d10 + 5) bludgeoning damage.

**Petrifying Breath (Recharge 5–6).** The gorgon exhales petrifying gas in a 30-foot cone. Each creature in that area must succeed on a DC 13 Constitution saving throw. On a failed save, a target begins to turn to stone and is [[Restrained|restrained]]. The [[Restrained|restrained]] target must repeat the saving throw at the end of its next turn. On a success, the effect ends on the target. On a failure, the target is [[Petrified|petrified]] until freed by the *greater restoration* spell or other magic.

## Lore

Few creatures that encounter a gorgon live to tell about it. Its body is covered in iron plates, and its nostrils fume with green vapor.

**Horrific Structure.** A gorgon's iron plates range from steely black to gleaming silver, but this natural armor in no way hinders its movement or mobility. A sick or inactive gorgon gathers rust like fungus or mange.

**Monstrous Predator.** When a gorgon spots potential prey, it charges with a hideous clamor of metal on metal. Faced with multiple foes, the gorgon exhales its deadly vapor to overcome the creatures it touches by turning them to stone. When it grows hungry, it smashes its petrified prey to rubble and grinds the stone into a powder that provides nourishment.

> [!tip] DM Tip
> Petrifying Breath (30-ft cone, DC 13 Con) is a two-stage save: restrained → petrified. Trampling Charge (20 ft. + gore) knocks prone for bonus action hooves — total charge damage avg 34. AC 19 is brutal for CR 5 parties.

- **What it is:** An iron-plated bull that breathes petrifying gas. CR 5 with AC 19, 114 HP, and a two-stage petrification breath weapon. Not to be confused with a medusa (which D&D calls a "medusa," not a "gorgon").
- **When to use:** Wilderness encounters in rocky terrain, guarding mountain passes, or as a wandering predator. The petrification threat makes it a memorable mid-level encounter.
- **Key combat tactics:** Opens with Petrifying Breath (30-ft cone, DC 13 Con). Failed save = [[Restrained|restrained]], then [[Petrified|petrified]] on a second failure. Trampling Charge: 20-ft charge + gore = DC 16 Str or [[Prone|prone]], then bonus action hooves. Total charge damage: 2d12+5 + 2d10+5 = avg 34. AC 19 is brutal for level 5 parties.
- **Notable abilities:** Petrifying Breath is a two-save petrification — [[Restrained|restrained]] first, then [[Petrified|petrified]] on the next failed save. This gives the party one round to help (casting *lesser restoration* ends [[Restrained|restrained]]). AC 19 with 114 HP makes it a tank. Immune to its own petrification.
- **Conditions inflicted:** [[Prone|prone]] (Trampling Charge), [[Restrained|restrained]] (Petrifying Breath), [[Petrified|petrified]] (Petrifying Breath second failure)
- **Suggested encounter pairings:** Alone in a mountain pass. In a field littered with [[Petrified|petrified]] creatures as a warning. Pair with a basilisk for a double-petrification nightmare. Near a medusa's lair as an "outer guard."
