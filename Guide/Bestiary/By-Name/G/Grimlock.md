### Traits

**Blind Senses.** The grimlock can't use its blindsight while deafened and unable to smell.

**Keen Hearing and Smell.** The grimlock has advantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on hearing or smell.

**Stone Camouflage.** The grimlock has advantage on Dexterity (Stealth) checks made to hide in rocky terrain.

### Actions

**Spiked Bone Club.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +5 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 5 (1d4 + 3) bludgeoning damage plus 2 (1d4) piercing damage.

## Lore

The degenerate subterranean grimlocks were once human, but their worship of the mind flayers over generations of prowling the Underdark transformed them into blind, monstrous cannibals long ago.

**Debased Cultists.** The empire of the mind flayers once spread across many worlds, enslaving countless races. Among those were human cultures whose high priests the mind flayers subverted. Over time, the rituals of these enslaved humans created fervent cannibal cults that regarded the brain eating of the mind flayers as a holy sacrament.

**Blind Hunters.** When the rule of the mind flayers crumbled, their cults fled into the Underdark. Over generations in that lightless realm, the cultists learned to rely on their other senses. In time, their eyes withered away and eyelids sealed. A grimlock's ears prick up at the faintest footfall, and it can track by scent like a bloodhound. For a grimlock, sightlessness is a boon — it isn't fooled by visual illusions or misperceptions.

**Endless War.** Grimlocks still venerate the mind flayers, serving them whenever possible. They continue to return to the surface world to abduct captives for their illithid masters.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!tip] DM Tip
> Blindsight 30 ft. means darkness, invisibility, and visual illusions don't work against grimlocks. Deafen them to reduce blindsight to 10 ft. Use as mind flayer minions in Underdark encounters — they're the "cultist" stat block of the deep.

- **What it is:** Blind, degenerate humanoids that serve mind flayers. CR 1/4 with blindsight 30 ft, making them immune to visual illusions and darkness-based tactics. Essentially Underdark cultist fodder.
- **When to use:** Mind flayer lairs as minions and scouts. Underdark tunnels as ambushers. Surface raids to abduct captives. They're the "cultist" stat block of the Underdark.
- **Key combat tactics:** Stone Camouflage + Stealth +3 for ambush in rocky terrain. Blindsight 30 ft means darkness, invisibility, and visual illusions don't work against them. Spiked Bone Club deals 1d4+3 + 1d4 (avg 7). Str +3 makes them decent grapplers for CR 1/4. Deafen them to reduce blindsight to 10 ft; deafen AND remove smell to blind them entirely.
- **Notable abilities:** Blindsight is the key — it counters many low-level party strategies. Immune to [[Blinded|blinded]] condition. Keen Hearing and Smell for tracking. The counter is *silence* or deafening effects, which cripple their blindsight.
- **Conditions inflicted:** None
- **Suggested encounter pairings:** Groups of 6–8 as mind flayer minions. Mixed with intellect devourers as a scouting party. In dark tunnels where the party needs light but the grimlocks don't. Pair with a mind flayer for a classic Underdark encounter.
