# Gibbering Mouther

![Gibbering Mouther](https://www.aidedd.org/dnd/images/gibbering-mouther.jpg)


> **Type**: Medium aberration, neutral | **CR**: 2 (450 XP) | **Source**: Monster Manual p.157–158

## Stat Block

**Armor Class** 9
**Hit Points** 67 (9d8 + 27)
**Speed** 10 ft., swim 10 ft.

**Condition Immunities** [[Prone|prone]]
**Senses** darkvision 60 ft., passive Perception 10
**Languages** —
**Challenge** 2 (450 XP)

### Traits

**Aberrant Ground.** The ground in a 10-foot radius around the mouther is doughlike difficult terrain. Each creature that starts its turn in that area must succeed on a DC 10 Strength saving throw or have its speed reduced to 0 until the start of its next turn.

**Gibbering.** The mouther babbles incoherently while it can see any creature and isn't [[Incapacitated|incapacitated]]. Each creature that starts its turn within 20 feet of the mouther and can hear the gibbering must succeed on a DC 10 Wisdom saving throw. On a failure, the creature can't take reactions until the start of its next turn and rolls a d8 to determine what it does during its turn. On a 1 to 4, the creature does nothing. On a 5 or 6, the creature takes no action or bonus action and uses all its movement to move in a randomly determined direction. On a 7 or 8, the creature makes a melee attack against a randomly determined creature within its reach or does nothing if it can't make such an attack.

### Actions

**Multiattack.** The gibbering mouther makes one bite attack and, if it can, uses its Blinding Spittle.

**Bites.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +2 to hit, reach 5 ft., one creature. *Hit:* 17 (5d6) piercing damage. If the target is Medium or smaller, it must succeed on a DC 10 Strength saving throw or be knocked [[Prone|prone]]. If the target is killed by this damage, it is absorbed into the mouther.

**Blinding Spittle (Recharge 5–6).** The mouther spits a chemical glob at a point it can see within 15 feet of it. The glob explodes in a blinding flash of light on impact. Each creature within 5 feet of the flash must succeed on a DC 13 Dexterity saving throw or be [[Blinded|blinded]] until the end of the mouther's next turn.

## Lore

Of all the terrors created by foul sorcery, gibbering mouthers are among the most wicked and depraved. This creature is the composite eyes, mouths, and liquefied matter of its former victims. Driven to insanity by the destruction of their bodies and absorption into the mouther, those victims gibber incoherent madness.

**Amoeboid Form.** The gibbering mouther's body is an amorphous mass of mouths and eyes that propels itself by oozing forward. Though it moves slowly, it swims through water, mud, and quicksand with ease.

**All-Consuming.** A gibbering mouther leaves nothing of its prey behind. Even as the last of a victim's body is consumed, its eyes and mouth boil to the surface, ready to join the chorus of tormented gibbering.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!tip] DM Tip
> Gibbering (20 ft., DC 10 Wis) has a 50% chance of making targets do nothing on their turn. Aberrant Ground (10 ft.) can reduce speed to 0. In tight spaces, the mouther controls the entire battlefield through debuffs, not damage.

- **What it is:** An amorphous mass of mouths and eyes that drives creatures mad with its gibbering. CR 2 but extremely disruptive — it controls the battlefield through difficult terrain, madness, and blindness rather than raw damage.
- **When to use:** Underdark caverns, mad wizard's labs, areas of corrupted magic. The gibbering mouther is pure horror — great for unsettling encounters. Best in tight spaces where its aura effects overlap with the party.
- **Key combat tactics:** Aberrant Ground creates difficult terrain in a 10-ft radius and can reduce speed to 0 (DC 10 Str). Gibbering forces DC 10 Wis saves within 20 ft — failure means random behavior (50% chance of doing nothing). Blinding Spittle [[Blinded|blinds]] in a 5-ft AoE. Bites deal 5d6 piercing and knock [[Prone|prone]]. Absorbed victims are gone.
- **Notable abilities:** The combination of Aberrant Ground + Gibbering creates a zone of chaos. Creatures can't flee (speed 0) and can't act rationally (random behavior). AC 9 and 10-ft speed mean it's easy to hit but hard to escape from.
- **Conditions inflicted:** [[Blinded|blinded]] (Blinding Spittle), [[Prone|prone]] (Bites), speed reduction (Aberrant Ground), random behavior (Gibbering)
- **Suggested encounter pairings:** Alone in a narrow tunnel. In a flooded cavern where its swim speed matters. Near an aboleth's lair as a byproduct of aberrant magic. Multiple mouthers in a large chamber is a nightmare.
