### Actions

**Multiattack.** The grell makes two attacks: one with its tentacles and one with its beak.

**Tentacles.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +4 to hit, reach 10 ft., one creature. *Hit:* 7 (1d10 + 2) piercing damage, and the target must succeed on a DC 11 Constitution saving throw or be [[Poisoned|poisoned]] for 1 minute. The [[Poisoned|poisoned]] target is [[Paralyzed|paralyzed]], and it can repeat the saving throw at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on a success. The target is also [[Grappled|grappled]] (escape DC 15). If the target is Medium or smaller, it is also [[Restrained|restrained]] until this grapple ends. While grappling the target, the grell has advantage on attack rolls against it and can't use this attack against other targets. When the grell moves, any Medium or smaller target it is grappling moves with it.

**Beak.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +4 to hit, reach 5 ft., one creature. *Hit:* 7 (2d4 + 2) piercing damage.

## Lore

A grell resembles a bulbous floating brain with a wide, sharp beak. Its ten long tentacles are made of hundreds of ring-shaped muscles sheathed in tough fibrous hide. Sharp barbs line the tip of each tentacle and inject paralytic venom.

**Floating Ambushers.** A grell prefers to ambush lone creatures or stragglers, hovering silently near the ceiling of a passage or cavern until a suitable target passes below, whereupon it descends quickly and wraps its tentacles around its prey. It then floats away to its lair with the paralyzed creature in its clutches.

**Alien Devourers.** Grells are alien predators that group other creatures into three categories: edibles, inedibles, and Great Eaters (those rare creatures that might prey on a grell). A grell will sometimes allow adventurers to wage war on other monstrous inhabitants of its dungeon, waiting for the right time to strike.

> [!tip] DM Tip
> Tentacles grapple + restrain + paralyze (DC 11 Con) in one hit, then the grell flies away with the victim. Blindsight only — it has no eyes, so visual illusions and darkness are irrelevant. Target isolated party members from above.

- **What it is:** A floating brain-monster with paralytic tentacles. CR 3 ambush predator that grabs, paralyzes, and carries off victims. Blindsight-only — it has no eyes.
- **When to use:** Underdark caverns, dungeon ceilings, anywhere with high ceilings for ambush. The grell is the classic "something grabs you from above" monster. Best against isolated party members.
- **Key combat tactics:** Hovers near the ceiling (Stealth +6) and drops on a lone target. Tentacles hit for 1d10+2 + DC 11 Con or [[Paralyzed|paralyzed]] + [[Grappled|grappled]] + [[Restrained|restrained]] (if Medium or smaller). Then flies away with the victim while attacking with advantage. Beak deals 2d4+2 to the helpless target.
- **Notable abilities:** Tentacles inflict four conditions at once ([[Poisoned|poisoned]], [[Paralyzed|paralyzed]], [[Grappled|grappled]], [[Restrained|restrained]]). Lightning immunity is unexpected. Blindsight 60 ft means darkness and invisibility don't help against it. Hover means it doesn't fall when [[Incapacitated|incapacitated]].
- **Conditions inflicted:** [[Poisoned|poisoned]], [[Paralyzed|paralyzed]], [[Grappled|grappled]], [[Restrained|restrained]] (all from Tentacles)
- **Suggested encounter pairings:** Alone in a high-ceilinged cavern targeting the party's rear. A coven of 3 grells in a large chamber. Near a mind flayer colony as a scavenger. In a vertical shaft where it can drag victims upward.
