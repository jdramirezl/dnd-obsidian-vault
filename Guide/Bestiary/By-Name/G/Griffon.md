## Lore

Griffons are ferocious avian carnivores with the muscular bodies of lions and the head, forelegs, and wings of eagles. When they attack, griffons are as swift and deadly as eagles, even as they strike with a lion's might and grace.

**Horse Eaters.** Griffons hunt in small prides, flying high over plains and forests near their rocky cliff-side aeries. Herd animals and horses are the prey they crave above all others. A griffon ignores a horse's rider when possible, and a rider that abandons its mount can escape unharmed while the griffon targets its chosen prey.

**Sky Dwellers.** Griffons lair in high rocky clifftop aeries. Aggressive and territorial, griffons engage in brutal aerial combat to defend their aeries, tearing and shredding the wings of flying intruders.

**Trained Mounts.** A griffon raised from an egg can be trained to serve as a mount. However, such training is time consuming, expensive, and dangerous. Once trained, a griffon is a fierce and loyal steed. It bonds with one master for life, fighting to the death to protect that rider.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!tip] DM Tip
> Griffons target horses first — a mounted party will lose their mounts before the griffons attack riders. 80-ft fly speed makes them excellent dive-bombers. Can be raised from eggs as mounts — great player reward.

- **What it is:** The classic eagle-lion hybrid. CR 2 flying predator with solid damage output (avg 19/round) and 80-ft fly speed. Unaligned — can be a mount, a predator, or a guardian.
- **When to use:** Mountain encounters, cloud giant lairs (they keep griffons as hunting beasts), wilderness travel. As a potential mount for PCs (raised from an egg). Griffon aeries make great exploration encounters.
- **Key combat tactics:** Dives from above with 80-ft fly speed. Two attacks per round: beak (1d8+4) + claws (2d6+4) for avg 19 damage. Keen Sight + Perception +5 means it spots prey from great distances. Targets horses first — a party on horseback will lose their mounts.
- **Notable abilities:** Fly 80 ft is very fast. Keen Sight for spotting prey. No special resistances or immunities — straightforward but effective. Passive Perception 15 makes it hard to sneak past.
- **Conditions inflicted:** None
- **Suggested encounter pairings:** A pride of 3–4 griffons attacking the party's horses. Griffons serving as mounts for cloud giant patrols. A griffon aerie with eggs that could be trained as mounts (quest hook). Pair with perytons and wyverns in a cloud giant's menagerie.
