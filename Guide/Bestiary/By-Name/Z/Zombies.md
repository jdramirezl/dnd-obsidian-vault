**Bite.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +3 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 14 (4d6) piercing damage.

**Eye Ray.** The zombie uses a random magical eye ray, choosing a target that it can see within 60 feet of it.

1. **Paralyzing Ray.** The targeted creature must succeed on a DC 14 Constitution saving throw or be [[Paralyzed|paralyzed]] for 1 minute. The target can repeat the saving throw at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on itself on a success.
2. **Fear Ray.** The targeted creature must succeed on a DC 14 Wisdom saving throw or be [[Frightened|frightened]] for 1 minute. The target can repeat the saving throw at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on itself on a success.
3. **Enervation Ray.** The targeted creature must make a DC 14 Constitution saving throw, taking 36 (8d8) necrotic damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one.
4. **Disintegration Ray.** If the target is a creature, it must succeed on a DC 14 Dexterity saving throw or take 45 (10d8) force damage. If this damage reduces the creature to 0 hit points, its body becomes a pile of fine gray dust. If the target is a Large or smaller nonmagical object or creation of magical force, it is disintegrated without a saving throw. If the target is a Huge or larger nonmagical object or creation of magical force, this ray disintegrates a 10-foot cube of it.

## Lore

**Dark Servants.** Sinister necromantic magic infuses the remains of the dead, causing them to rise as zombies that do their creator's bidding without fear or hesitation. Most zombies are made from humanoid remains, though the flesh and bones of any formerly living creature can be imbued with a semblance of life. Once turned into a zombie, a creature can't be restored to life except by powerful magic, such as a *resurrection* spell.

**Hideous Forms.** Zombies appear as they did in life, showing the wounds that killed them. Dead warriors might rise from a battlefield, eviscerated and bloated after days in the sun. A zombie might wash ashore or rise from a marsh, swollen and reeking after weeks in the water.

**Mindless Soldiers.** Zombies take the most direct route to any foe, unable to comprehend obstacles, tactics, or dangerous terrain. A zombie might stumble into a fast-flowing river, step out of an open window, or shamble through roaring infernos without hesitation. A zombie can follow simple orders and distinguish friends from foes, but its ability to reason is limited to shambling in whatever direction it is pointed.

**Undead Nature.** A zombie doesn't require air, food, drink, or sleep.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!tip] Undead Fortitude — They Keep Getting Up
> On dropping to 0 HP, zombies make a Con save (DC 5 + damage taken) to stay at 1 HP instead. Only radiant damage or critical hits bypass this. A cleric with *sacred flame* or a paladin's Divine Smite are the hard counters. Without them, zombies can survive 2-3 "deaths" each.

- **What they are:** Classic mindless undead in three tiers. Zombie (CR 1/4) is the quintessential shambling corpse. Ogre Zombie (CR 2) is a brute-force upgrade. Beholder Zombie (CR 5) is a hovering horror with random eye rays. All share Undead Fortitude.
- **When to use:** Graveyards, necromancer lairs, battlefields, any undead-themed adventure. Zombies are the bread and butter of necromantic encounters at all tiers.
- **Key combat tactics:**
  - *Zombie (CR 1/4):* Slow (20 ft) and weak individually, but dangerous in hordes. Undead Fortitude with +3 Con means they keep getting back up — radiant damage or crits bypass this. Use in groups of 6–12 to overwhelm.
  - *Ogre Zombie (CR 2):* 85 HP with Undead Fortitude makes it extremely durable for CR 2. Morningstar hits for 13 avg damage. 30 ft speed (faster than regular zombies). A single ogre zombie can anchor a zombie horde.
  - *Beholder Zombie (CR 5):* Hovers (immune to ground hazards). Random eye ray each turn — Disintegration Ray deals 45 (10d8) force and can instant-kill at 0 HP. Paralyzing Ray locks down targets. Enervation Ray deals 36 (8d8) necrotic. Randomness makes it unpredictable and exciting.
- **Notable abilities:** Undead Fortitude is the defining zombie trait — Con save (DC 5 + damage taken) to drop to 1 HP instead of 0. Bypassed only by radiant damage or critical hits. Clerics and paladins are the hard counter. Beholder Zombie eye rays are rolled randomly (d4), so even the DM doesn't know what's coming.
- **Conditions inflicted:** [[Paralyzed|paralyzed]] (Beholder Paralyzing Ray), [[Frightened|frightened]] (Beholder Fear Ray)
- **Suggested encounter pairings:** Horde of 8–12 zombies with an ogre zombie leading the charge. Beholder zombie in a death tyrant's lair with regular zombies. Zombies rising from graves mid-combat as waves. A necromancer commanding mixed zombie types from behind the lines.
