# Pixie

![Pixie](https://www.aidedd.org/dnd/images/pixie.jpg)


> **Type**: Tiny fey, neutral good | **CR**: 1/4 (50 XP) | **Source**: Monster Manual p.253–254

## Stat Block

**Armor Class** 15
**Hit Points** 1 (1d4 - 1)
**Speed** 10 ft., fly 30 ft.

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| 2 (-4) | 20 (+5) | 8 (-1) | 10 (+0) | 14 (+2) | 15 (+2) |

**Skills** Perception +4, Stealth +7
**Senses** passive Perception 14
**Languages** Sylvan
**Challenge** 1/4 (50 XP)

### Traits

**Magic Resistance.** The pixie has advantage on saving throws against spells and other magical effects.

**Innate Spellcasting.** The pixie's innate spellcasting ability is Charisma (spell save DC 12). It can innately cast the following spells, requiring only its pixie dust as a component:
- At will: *druidcraft*
- 1/day each: *confusion*, *dancing lights*, *detect evil and good*, *detect thoughts*, *dispel magic*, *entangle*, *fly*, *phantasmal force*, *polymorph*, *sleep*

### Actions

**Superior Invisibility.** The pixie magically turns invisible until its concentration ends (as if concentrating on a spell). Any equipment the pixie wears or carries is invisible with it.

## Lore

Standing barely a foot tall, pixies resemble diminutive elves with gossamer wings like those of dragonflies or butterflies. Curious as cats and shy as deer, pixies go where they please.

**Magical Faerie Folk.** With their innate power of invisibility, pixies rarely appear unless they wish to be seen. In the Feywild and on the Material Plane, pixies etch patterns of frost on winter ponds and rouse the buds in springtime.

**Pixie Dust.** When pixies fly visibly, a shower of sparkling dust follows in their wake like the glittering tail of a shooting star. A mere sprinkle of pixie dust is said to be able to grant the power of flight, confuse a creature hopelessly, or send foes into a magical slumber.

**Tiny Tricksters.** Pixies are too shy to reveal themselves at first. They study visitors from afar to gauge their temperament or play harmless tricks on them to measure their reactions.

**Opposed to Violence.** Unlike their fey cousins, the sprites, pixies abhor weapons and would sooner flee than get into a physical altercation with any enemy.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!tip] Polymorph at CR 1/4
> A friendly pixie can cast *polymorph* once per day — turning a party member into a T-Rex or Giant Ape. This is campaign-altering power from a 1 HP creature. As a DM, decide in advance whether pixie allies will offer this, and set narrative conditions for earning their trust.

- **What it is:** A tiny fey with 1 HP but an absurdly powerful spell list for CR 1/4. **Polymorph**, *[[Fly|fly]]*, **confusion**, *[[Sleep|sleep]]*, *[[Dispel Magic|dispel magic]]* — all 1/day each. Superior Invisibility as an action (concentration). The ultimate glass cannon support creature.
- **When to use:** Feywild encounters, enchanted forests, fairy rings. Pixies are tricksters and allies, not combat threats. A group of pixies can dramatically alter an encounter by buffing the party or debuffing enemies.
- **Key combat tactics:** Pixies don't fight directly — 1 HP means any hit kills them. They turn [[Invisible|invisible]] (Superior Invisibility), then cast spells from hiding. *Polymorph* can turn an ally into a T-Rex or an enemy into a snail. *Confusion* and *sleep* disrupt groups. They flee if threatened.
- **Notable abilities:** *Polymorph* at CR 1/4 is the headline — a friendly pixie can transform a party member into a giant ape or T-Rex. Magic Resistance protects against counterspells. Superior Invisibility makes them nearly impossible to target. 1 HP means area damage is the only reliable counter.
- **Conditions inflicted:** Varies by spell — *confusion* (random behavior), *sleep* ([[Unconscious|unconscious]]), **entangle** ([[Restrained|restrained]])
- **Suggested encounter pairings:** A grove of pixies playing tricks on travelers — potential allies if treated kindly. Pixies fleeing from a hag or redcap who has captured their queen. A Feywild court with pixies as attendants to an archfey. Pair with sprites, satyrs, or a dryad for a full fey encounter.
