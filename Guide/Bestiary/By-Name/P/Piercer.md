# Piercer

> **Type**: Medium monstrosity, unaligned | **CR**: 1/2 (100 XP) | **Source**: Monster Manual p.253

## Stat Block

**Armor Class** 15 (natural armor)
**Hit Points** 22 (3d8 + 9)
**Speed** 5 ft., climb 5 ft.

|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|

**Skills** Stealth +5
**Senses** blindsight 30 ft., darkvision 60 ft., passive Perception 8
**Languages** —
**Challenge** 1/2 (100 XP)

### Traits

**False Appearance.** While the piercer remains motionless on the ceiling, it is indistinguishable from a normal stalactite.

**Spider Climb.** The piercer can climb difficult surfaces, including upside down on ceilings, without needing to make an ability check.

### Actions

**Drop.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +3 to hit, one creature directly underneath the piercer. *Hit:* 3 (1d6) piercing damage per 10 feet fallen, up to 21 (6d6). *Miss:* The piercer takes half the normal falling damage for the distance fallen.

## Lore

Clinging to the ceilings of caverns and large subterranean passages, piercers blend in perfectly with natural rock, dropping in silence to impale unsuspecting foes on the ground below.

A piercer is the larval form of a [[Roper|roper]], and the two creatures often attack in tandem. A rock-like shell encases a piercer's body, giving it the look and texture of a stalactite.

**Patient Hunters.** Piercers can see, but they can also respond to noise and heat, waiting for living creatures to pass beneath them, then falling to attack. A piercer that misses its chance to kill must make its slow way back to the ceiling. Piercers gather in colonies to maximize the effectiveness of their attacks, dropping simultaneously to increase the odds of striking prey.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!tip] Ceiling Height = Lethality
> A piercer's Drop attack deals 1d6 per 10 ft. fallen. From a 60 ft. ceiling, that's 6d6 (avg 21) — enough to one-shot a level 1–2 character. Always note ceiling height when placing piercers. From 20 ft., it's only 2d6 — much more survivable.

- **What it is:** A living stalactite — the larval form of a roper. CR 1/2 ambush predator with one shot: drop from the ceiling for up to 21 (6d6) piercing damage. If it misses, it's nearly helpless (5 ft. speed).
- **When to use:** Underdark caverns, natural caves, anywhere with high ceilings and stalactites. Best as a surprise hazard rather than a standalone encounter. Pair with [[Roper|ropers]] for a thematic parent-child ambush.
- **Key combat tactics:** Waits motionless on the ceiling (False Appearance = auto-surprise unless detected). Drops on a creature directly below for 1d6 per 10 ft. fallen. A 60 ft. ceiling means 6d6 damage — lethal for low-level characters. If it misses, it takes half falling damage and is essentially out of the fight at 5 ft. speed.
- **Notable abilities:** False Appearance makes detection the real challenge — passive Perception vs. Stealth +5. The Drop attack scales with ceiling height, making environment critical. Spider Climb lets it reposition between ambushes (very slowly).
- **Conditions inflicted:** None
- **Suggested encounter pairings:** A colony of 4–6 piercers on a cavern ceiling above a narrow path. Mixed with a [[Roper|roper]] (its adult form) for a layered ambush. Darkmantle colonies in the same cavern for a ceiling-themed gauntlet.
