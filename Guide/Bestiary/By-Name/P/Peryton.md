# Peryton

![Peryton](https://www.aidedd.org/dnd/images/peryton.jpg)


> **Type**: Medium monstrosity, chaotic evil | **CR**: 2 (450 XP) | **Source**: Monster Manual p.251–252

## Stat Block

**Armor Class** 13 (natural armor)
**Hit Points** 33 (6d8 + 6)
**Speed** 20 ft., fly 60 ft.

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| 16 (+3) | 12 (+1) | 13 (+1) | 9 (-1) | 12 (+1) | 10 (+0) |

**Skills** Perception +5
**Damage Resistances** bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing from nonmagical weapons
**Senses** passive Perception 15
**Languages** understands Common and Elvish but can't speak
**Challenge** 2 (450 XP)

### Traits

**Dive Attack.** If the peryton is flying and dives at least 30 feet straight toward a target and then hits it with a melee weapon attack, the attack deals an extra 9 (2d8) damage to the target.

**Flyby.** The peryton doesn't provoke an opportunity attack when it flies out of an enemy's reach.

**Keen Sight and Smell.** The peryton has advantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on sight or smell.

### Actions

**Multiattack.** The peryton makes one gore attack and one talon attack.

**Gore.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +5 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 7 (1d8 + 3) piercing damage.

**Talons.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +5 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 8 (2d4 + 3) piercing damage.

## Lore

Although this monstrous carnivore feeds on any creature, it prefers humanoids, especially elves, half-elves, and humans. When it kills a humanoid, a peryton rips out its prey's heart and takes it back to its nest to be devoured. The peryton blends the body and wings of a bird of prey with the head of a stag. Its strangest feature is its shadow, which appears humanoid rather than reflecting the creature's physical form.

**Unnatural Hunger.** A peryton's reproductive cycle depends on the heart of a freshly killed humanoid. When a peryton consumes a heart, its shadow changes for a brief time to reflect its true monstrous form. When attacking a humanoid, a peryton is single-minded and relentless, fighting until it or its prey dies.

**Bane of the Mountains.** Perytons roost atop mountain ridges and lair in high caves. Because normal weapons are less effective against perytons, the folk of the mountains know to avoid confrontations with these monsters at all costs.

> [!tip] Flyby + Dive Attack = Untouchable
> Dive 30+ ft. for bonus 2d8 damage, then fly away with no opportunity attacks (Flyby). Repeat every round. Nonmagical weapon resistance at CR 2 means low-level martial characters deal half damage. The party needs magic weapons or ranged spells to handle these effectively.

- **What it is:** A flying stag-headed bird of prey that hunts humanoids for their hearts. CR 2 but punches above its weight thanks to Flyby, Dive Attack, and nonmagical weapon resistance. Only 33 HP but very hard to pin down.
- **When to use:** Mountain passes, high cliffs, coastal bluffs. A flock of perytons terrorizing a mountain village is a classic quest hook. Their humanoid shadows make for great horror flavor.
- **Key combat tactics:** Dive Attack from 30+ ft. for an extra 2d8 damage, then Flyby out of reach with no opportunity attacks. Repeat every round. Focus one target relentlessly (lore-accurate). Resistance to nonmagical B/P/S makes them surprisingly durable against martial characters without magic weapons.
- **Notable abilities:** Flyby + Dive Attack is a devastating hit-and-run combo. Nonmagical weapon resistance at CR 2 is unusual and punishing for low-level parties. Understands Common and Elvish — can eavesdrop on plans.
- **Conditions inflicted:** None
- **Suggested encounter pairings:** A mated pair hunting along a mountain road. A flock of 3–4 attacking a caravan. Nesting near a griffon eyrie, competing for territory. Pair with harpies for a themed aerial ambush in a mountain pass.
