## Lore

The white winged horses known as pegasi soar through the skies, a vision of grace and majesty. When they touch down on solid ground, they linger only for a moment, drinking from mountain springs and pristine lakes.

**Noble Mounts.** Pegasi are highly prized as swift and reliable steeds, being faster and less temperamental than griffons, hippogriffs, and wyverns. However, these wild and shy creatures are as intelligent as humanoids, and so can't be traditionally broken and tamed. A pegasus must be persuaded to serve a good-aligned creature as a mount, but when it does so, it forges a lifelong bond with its new companion.

**Born of the Planes.** Pegasi trace their origins to the Olympian Glades of Arborea, where they soar through the skies of that plane and serve as faithful mounts to the Seldarine, the pantheon of elven gods.

**Pegasi Nests.** Pegasi mate for life, build their nests in hard-to-reach locations, and give birth to live young.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!tip] Best Mount in the Game
> 90 ft. fly speed, understands four languages, and forms a lifelong bond with a good-aligned rider. A pegasus is a reward, not an encounter. Give one to a paladin or ranger who earns it through roleplay — it changes the campaign's mobility dynamics permanently.

- **What it is:** A celestial winged horse — CR 2 with no special abilities beyond flight. 59 HP, AC 12, and a single Hooves attack for 11 damage. Its value is as a mount, not a combatant.
- **When to use:** Reward for a good-aligned party, divine messenger, wilderness encounter in mountain meadows or Feywild crossings. A pegasus appearing signals celestial favor or an urgent quest.
- **Key combat tactics:** Pegasi avoid combat. If forced to fight, they use their 90 ft. fly speed to stay mobile and kick with hooves. They flee from threats rather than stand and fight.
- **Notable abilities:** 90 ft. fly speed makes it one of the fastest mounts in the game. Understands four languages (Celestial, Common, Elvish, Sylvan) but can't speak. No traits or special features — pure simplicity.
- **Conditions inflicted:** None
- **Suggested encounter pairings:** A pegasus fleeing from perytons or griffons that the party can rescue. Sent by a deity to carry a wounded hero. A herd of pegasi in a mountain glade guarded by a unicorn or deva. Elven knights mounted on pegasi as allies or quest-givers.
