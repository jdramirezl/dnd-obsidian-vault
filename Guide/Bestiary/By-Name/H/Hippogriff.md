# Hippogriff

![Hippogriff](https://www.aidedd.org/dnd/images/hippogriff.jpg)


> **Type**: Large monstrosity, unaligned | **CR**: 1 (200 XP) | **Source**: Monster Manual p.185

## Lore

A beast whose magical origins are lost to history, a hippogriff possesses the wings and forelimbs of an eagle, the hindquarters of a horse, and a head that combines the features of both animals.

Reclusive and omnivorous, hippogriffs mate for life and seldom venture more than a few miles from their nest. When defending its mate or its young, a hippogriff fights to the death. Hippogriffs don't lay eggs but give birth to live young.

Dragons, griffons, and wyverns have a taste for hippogriff meat and frequently prey on these creatures.

**Flying Mounts.** A hippogriff raised in captivity can be trained to be a faithful companion and mount. Of all the creatures that can serve as flying mounts, hippogriffs are among the easiest to train and the most loyal once trained properly.

**Multiattack.** The hippogriff makes two attacks: one with its beak and one with its claws.

**Claws.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +5 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 10 (2d6 + 3) slashing damage.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!tip] DM Tip
> Easiest flying mount to train — great player reward. Multiattack at CR 1 (avg 18 damage/round) hits hard but only 19 HP makes them glass cannons. Fly 60 ft. for dive attacks or escape vehicles.

- **Flying mount candidate** — easiest to train of all flying mounts; great reward for players
- **Multiattack** at CR 1: Beak (1d10+3) + Claws (2d6+3) = avg 18 damage/round — hits hard but fragile at 19 HP
- **Fly 60 ft.** makes them effective dive-attackers or escape vehicles
- **Fights to the death** defending mate/young — otherwise flees when outmatched
- **Keen Sight** + Perception +5 = excellent aerial scouts
- **Encounter use:** Flock of 3–4 defending a mountain nest, trained mounts for an elven patrol, or wild ones preyed upon by a griffon or wyvern
