# Hell Hound

![Hell Hound](https://www.aidedd.org/dnd/images/hell-hound.jpg)


> **Type**: Medium fiend, lawful evil | **CR**: 3 (700 XP) | **Source**: Monster Manual p.183

## Lore

Monstrous, fire-breathing fiends that take the form of powerful dogs, hell hounds are found on the battlefields of Acheron and throughout the Lower Planes. On the Material Plane, hell hounds are most commonly seen in service to devils, fire giants, and other evil creatures that use them as guard animals and companions.

**Burning Hunger.** Hell hounds hunt in packs, feeding on any creature that appears edible. They avoid potentially dangerous foes in favor of targeting the weakest prey with their savage bite and fiery breath, demonstrating a relentless determination as they pursue that prey to the bitter end. When hell hounds feed, the flesh they consume stokes the infernal fires that burn within them. When a hell hound dies, that fire consumes the creature's remains in a billowing eruption of smoke and blazing embers, leaving nothing behind but scorched tufts of black fur.

**Evil to the Core.** Hell hounds are smarter than mundane beasts, and their lawful nature makes them good at following orders. However, a hell hound's evil disposition means that the creature can't be trained to be anything other than a ruthless killer. If a hell hound isn't allowed to indulge its malevolent hunger, it quickly abandons or turns against its master.

**Damage Immunities** fire
**Senses** darkvision 60 ft., passive Perception 15
**Languages** understands Infernal but can't speak it
**Challenge** 3 (700 XP)

### Traits

**Keen Hearing and Smell.** The hound has advantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on hearing or smell.

**Pack Tactics.** The hound has advantage on an attack roll against a creature if at least one of the hound's allies is within 5 feet of the creature and the ally isn't [[Incapacitated|incapacitated]].

### Actions

**Bite.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +5 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 7 (1d8 + 3) piercing damage plus 7 (2d6) fire damage.

**Fire Breath (Recharge 5–6).** The hound exhales fire in a 15-foot cone. Each creature in that area must make a DC 12 Dexterity saving throw, taking 21 (6d6) fire damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!tip] DM Tip
> Pack Tactics gives advantage on every attack when an ally is adjacent — always run hell hounds in groups of 2–3. Fire Breath (Recharge 5–6) from multiple hounds on clustered PCs is devastating. Keen Hearing and Smell means they always detect the party first.

- **Pack Tactics** = advantage on every attack when allies are adjacent — run them in groups of 2–3 minimum
- **Bite** deals 1d8+3 piercing + 2d6 fire (avg 14) — with advantage from Pack Tactics, that's very reliable damage
- **Fire Breath** (Recharge 5–6): 15 ft. cone, 6d6 fire (avg 21), DC 12 Dex — devastating when multiple hounds breathe on clustered PCs
- **Speed 50 ft.** — they outrun most PCs; fleeing is not an option
- **Fire immune** — don't bother with fire spells; they're fiends, not beasts
- **Keen Hearing and Smell** + Perception +5 (passive 15) — excellent guards, nearly impossible to sneak past
- **Encounter pairings:** 2–3 hell hounds guarding a devil's lair, pack of 4 hunting in the wilderness, or paired with a fire giant as war dogs
