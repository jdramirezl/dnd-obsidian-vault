# Kuo-toa

![Kuo-toa](https://www.aidedd.org/dnd/images/kuo-toa.jpg)


> **Type**: Humanoid (kuo-toa) | **CR**: 1/4 – 6 | **Source**: Monster Manual p.199–202

Kuo-toa are degenerate fishlike humanoids that once inhabited the shores and islands of the surface world. Long ago humans and their ilk drove the kuo-toa underground, where they dwell in madness and everlasting night. Kuo-toa can no longer abide daylight.

**Mad Slaves.** At the height of the illithid empire, the mind flayers captured kuo-toa by the thousands and forced them into bondage. The kuo-toa were simple creatures, never meant to endure the oppressive mental force the illithids unleashed against them. By the time the mind flayers abandoned them, the prolonged psychic subjugation endured by the kuo-toa had driven them mad. Their minds shattered beyond repair, the kuo-toa adopted a religious fervor, inventing gods to protect them against threats. Most notable of these threats are the drow, which have slain the kuo-toa on sight since the days when the two races first met.

**God Makers.** Kuo-toa worship gods of their own insane creation, but if enough kuo-toa believe that a god is real, the energy of their collective subconscious can cause that god to manifest as a physical entity. One of the most revered gods of the kuo-toa is Blibdoolpoolp the Sea Mother, who takes the form of a female human with a crayfish head, a crayfish's claws, and an articulated shell covering her shoulders. Kuo-toa that cross paths with an aboleth often find themselves worshiping it as a god, their madness blinding them to the fact that the aboleth is merely using them for its own nefarious ends.

**Theocratic Rulers.** Kuo-toa archpriests are surrounded by fanatical devotees of their faith. The archpriest of a kuo-toa domain demands that all its subjects worship a specific god. An archpriest's mad belief in its god is so fervent that it manifests the powers of a high cleric. The archpriest can also bestow spells to devout underlings called whips. The archpriest's decrees are enforced by monitors, devout kuo-toa that act as the archpriest's eyes and ears. Monitors are deadly hand-to-hand combatants, and lesser kuo-toa live in fear of them.

**Kuo-toa Gear.** Many weapons of the kuo-toa are designed to capture rather than kill. Nets are common, though some carry pincer staffs (also called mancatchers) designed to trap and immobilize foes. Kuo-toa warriors also treat their shields with a sticky goo that catches incoming weapons. In general, kuo-toa don't like the weight of armor on their slippery bodies and rely on their natural rubbery hides for protection.

## Kuo-toa

> **Type**: Medium humanoid (kuo-toa), neutral evil | **CR**: 1/4 (50 XP) | **Source**: Monster Manual p.200

### Stat Block

**Armor Class** 13 (natural armor, shield)
**Hit Points** 18 (4d8)
**Speed** 30 ft., swim 30 ft.

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| 13 (+1) | 10 (+0) | 11 (+0) | 11 (+0) | 10 (+0) | 8 (-1) |

**Skills** Perception +4
**Senses** darkvision 120 ft., passive Perception 14
**Languages** Undercommon
**Challenge** 1/4 (50 XP)

#### Traits

**Amphibious.** The kuo-toa can breathe air and water.

**Otherworldly Perception.** The kuo-toa can sense the presence of any creature within 30 feet of it that is invisible or on the Ethereal Plane. It can pinpoint such a creature that is moving.

**Slippery.** The kuo-toa has advantage on ability checks and saving throws made to escape a grapple.

**Sunlight Sensitivity.** While in sunlight, the kuo-toa has disadvantage on attack rolls, as well as on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on sight.

#### Actions

**Bite.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +3 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 3 (1d4 + 1) piercing damage.

**Spear.** *Melee or Ranged Weapon Attack:* +3 to hit, reach 5 ft. or range 20/60 ft., one target. *Hit:* 4 (1d6 + 1) piercing damage, or 5 (1d8 + 1) piercing damage if used with two hands to make a melee attack.

**Net.** *Ranged Weapon Attack:* +3 to hit, range 5/15 ft., one Large or smaller creature. *Hit:* The target is [[Restrained|restrained]]. A creature can use its action to make a DC 10 Strength check to free itself or another creature in a net, ending the effect on a success. Dealing 5 slashing damage to the net (AC 10) frees the target without harming it and destroys the net.

#### Reactions

**Sticky Shield.** When a creature misses the kuo-toa with a melee weapon attack, the kuo-toa uses its sticky shield to catch the weapon. The attacker must succeed on a DC 11 Strength saving throw, or the weapon becomes stuck to the kuo-toa's shield. If the weapon's wielder can't or won't let go of the weapon, the wielder is [[Grappled|grappled]] while the weapon is stuck. While stuck, the weapon can't be used. A creature can pull the weapon free by taking an action to make a DC 11 Strength check and succeeding.

## Variant: Kuo-toa Monitor

> A kuo-toa monitor has a challenge rating of 3 (700 XP). It has the same statistics as a kuo-toa whip except that it adds its Wisdom modifier to its Armor Class (AC 13), loses the Spellcaster trait, and replaces the whip's action options with the following action options.

**Multiattack.** The kuo-toa makes one bite attack and two unarmed strikes.

**Bite.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +6 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 4 (1d4 + 2) piercing damage.

**Unarmed Strike.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +6 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 5 (1d6 + 2) bludgeoning damage plus 3 (1d6) lightning damage, and the target can't take reactions until the end of the kuo-toa's next turn.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!quote] Tactical Deep Dive
> See [[Kuo-toa-Tactics]] for detailed combat tactics from *The Monsters Know What They're Doing*.


> [!tip] DM Tip
> Otherworldly Perception detects invisible and ethereal creatures within 30 ft. — stealth-based strategies fail. Sticky Shield disarms melee attackers (DC 11 Str). The Archpriest (CR 6) with *spirit guardians* + *spiritual weapon* + lightning scepter has devastating action economy. Kuo-toa can literally will gods into existence through collective belief — amazing campaign hook.

- **What they are:** Mad fish-folk of the Underdark with a theocratic society. CR 1/4 (base), CR 1 (Whip), CR 3 (Monitor variant), CR 6 (Archpriest). Their signature trait is Otherworldly Perception — they detect [[Invisible|invisible]] and ethereal creatures within 30 ft.
- **When to use:** Underdark encounters, underground lake/river settlements, aboleth-worshiping cults. The "god maker" lore is fantastic for campaigns — kuo-toa can literally will gods into existence through collective belief.
- **Key combat tactics:** Base kuo-toa use Nets to [[Restrained|restrain]] targets, then swarm with spears. Sticky Shield punishes melee attackers by disarming them (DC 11 Str). Whips grapple with Pincer Staff at 10 ft. reach and cast *bane*/*shield of faith*. Monitors are melee bruisers — lightning unarmed strikes that deny reactions. Archpriest is the real threat: *spirit guardians* + *spiritual weapon* + Scepter (1d6+3 + 4d6 lightning) is devastating action economy.
- **Notable abilities:** Otherworldly Perception (detects invisible/ethereal within 30 ft.) counters stealth-based parties. Sticky Shield is a unique disarm reaction. Archpriest has 10th-level cleric casting including *[[Hold Person|hold person]]*, *spirit guardians*, and *mass [[Cure Wounds|cure wounds]]*.
- **Conditions inflicted:** [[Restrained|restrained]] (Net), [[Grappled|grappled]] (Pincer Staff, Sticky Shield)
- **Suggested encounter pairings:** 6–8 base kuo-toa with 2 whips and a monitor as a patrol. An archpriest leading worship in a shrine with kuo-toa guards — the "god" they worship might actually manifest. Pair with an aboleth as the secret power behind the kuo-toa settlement.
