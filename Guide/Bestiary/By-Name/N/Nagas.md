# Nagas

![Nagas](https://www.aidedd.org/dnd/images/guardian-naga.jpg)


> **Type**: Large monstrosity/undead | **CR**: 4 / 8 / 10 | **Source**: Monster Manual p.233–235

Nagas are intelligent serpents that inhabit the ruins of the past, guarding their secrets with deadly magic. Whether combating evil or spreading it, a naga shapes the world and the lives of those around it.

## Bone Naga

> **Type**: Large undead, lawful evil | **CR**: 4 (1,100 XP) | **Source**: Monster Manual p.233

### Stat Block

**Armor Class** 15 (natural armor)
**Hit Points** 58 (9d10 + 9)
**Speed** 30 ft.

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| 15 (+2) | 16 (+3) | 12 (+1) | 15 (+2) | 15 (+2) | 16 (+3) |

**Damage Immunities** poison
**Condition Immunities** [[Charmed|charmed]], exhaustion, [[Paralyzed|paralyzed]], [[Poisoned|poisoned]]
**Senses** darkvision 60 ft., passive Perception 12
**Languages** Common plus one other language
**Challenge** 4 (1,100 XP)

#### Traits

**Spellcasting.** The naga is a 5th-level spellcaster (spell save DC 12, +4 to hit with spell attacks) that needs only verbal components to cast its spells.

If the naga was a **guardian naga** in life, its spellcasting ability is Wisdom, and it has the following cleric spells prepared:
- Cantrips (at will): *mending*, *sacred flame*, *thaumaturgy*
- 1st level (4 slots): *command*, *shield of faith*
- 2nd level (3 slots): *calm emotions*, *hold person*
- 3rd level (2 slots): *bestow curse*

If the naga was a **spirit naga** in life, its spellcasting ability is Intelligence, and it has the following wizard spells prepared:
- Cantrips (at will): *mage hand*, *minor illusion*, *ray of frost*
- 1st level (4 slots): *charm person*, *sleep*
- 2nd level (3 slots): *detect thoughts*, *hold person*
- 3rd level (2 slots): *lightning bolt*

#### Actions

**Bite.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +5 to hit, reach 10 ft., one creature. *Hit:* 10 (2d6 + 3) piercing damage plus 10 (3d6) poison damage.

## Lore

**Benevolent Dictators and Tyrants.** A naga rules its domain with absolute authority. Whether it rules with compassion or by terrorizing its subjects, the naga believes itself the master of all other creatures that inhabit its domain.

**Rivalry.** Nagas have a long-standing enmity with the yuan-ti, with each race seeing itself as the epitome of serpentine evolution. Though cooperation between them is rare, nagas and yuan-ti sometimes set aside their differences to work toward common objectives. However, yuan-ti always chafe under a naga's authority.

**Immortal Nature.** A naga doesn't require air, food, drink, or sleep.

**Bone Nagas.** In response to the long history of conflict between the yuan-ti and the nagas, yuan-ti created a necromantic ritual that could halt a naga's resurrection by transforming the living naga into a skeletal undead servitor. A bone naga retains only a few of the spells it knew in life.

**Guardian Nagas.** Wise and good, the beautiful guardian nagas protect sacred places and items of magical power from falling into evil hands. In their hidden redoubts, they research spells and hatch convoluted plots to thwart the evil designs of their enemies. A guardian naga doesn't seek out violence, warning off intruders rather than attacking.

**Spirit Nagas.** Spirit nagas live in gloom and spitefulness, constantly plotting vengeance against creatures that have wronged them — or that they believe have wronged them. Lairing in dismal caverns and ruins, they devote their time to developing new spells and enslaving the mortals with which they surround themselves.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!quote] Tactical Deep Dive
> See [[Naga-Tactics]] for detailed combat tactics from *The Monsters Know What They're Doing*.


> [!warning] Guardian Naga — CR 10 Immortal Serpent
> AC 18, 127 HP, 11th-level cleric casting, and Rejuvenation (returns in 1d6 days — only *wish* prevents it). Its Spit Poison forces DC 15 Con for 45 (10d8) poison damage at range. A guardian naga protecting a sacred site is a recurring challenge the party cannot permanently remove.

> [!tip] Rejuvenation Changes Everything
> Spirit and Guardian nagas return in 1d6 days after death — only *wish* stops this. Bone nagas do NOT have Rejuvenation. This means killing a spirit or guardian naga is a temporary solution at best. Build encounters around this: the naga is a recurring antagonist or an immortal guardian the party must negotiate with.

- **What they are:** Immortal serpentine spellcasters spanning good (Guardian, CR 10), evil (Spirit, CR 8), and undead (Bone, CR 4). All are verbal-only casters. Guardian and Spirit nagas have Rejuvenation — only **wish** stops them from returning in 1d6 days.
- **When to use:** Ancient temples, hidden ruins, underground lairs. Guardian nagas protect sacred sites; spirit nagas lurk in cursed caverns; bone nagas serve yuan-ti masters.
- **Key combat tactics:**
  - *Bone Naga (CR 4):* 5th-level caster with two possible spell lists depending on origin. Guardian-origin gets *[[Hold Person|hold person]]* and **bestow curse**; spirit-origin gets *[[Lightning Bolt|lightning bolt]]* and *[[Charm Person|charm person]]*. Bite deals solid poison damage. No Rejuvenation — stays dead.
  - *Spirit Naga (CR 8):* 10th-level wizard caster. Opens with *[[Dominate Person|dominate person]]* or **blight**. *Lightning bolt* for AoE. Bite forces DC 13 Con save for 31 (7d8) poison — devastating. Rejuvenation means it comes back unless the party uses *wish*.
  - *Guardian Naga (CR 10):* 11th-level cleric caster. *[[Flame Strike|Flame strike]]* and **geas** at 5th level, *[[True Seeing|true seeing]]* at 6th. Spit Poison is a ranged attack forcing DC 15 Con for 45 (10d8) poison. AC 18 and 127 HP make it very durable. Rejuvenation applies.
- **Notable abilities:** Rejuvenation (Spirit & Guardian) makes them effectively immortal. Bone Naga's dual spell list is unique — DM chooses based on origin. All nagas cast with verbal components only — no hands needed, can't be disarmed of a focus.
- **Conditions inflicted:** [[Charmed|charmed]] (charm person), [[Paralyzed|paralyzed]] (hold person), [[Poisoned|poisoned]] (poison damage/saves)
- **Suggested encounter pairings:** Bone naga with yuan-ti purebloods in a serpent temple. Spirit naga with charmed humanoid thralls. Guardian naga as a quest-giver or guardian of a MacGuffin the party needs.
