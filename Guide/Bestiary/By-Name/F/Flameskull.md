# Flameskull

![Flameskull](https://www.aidedd.org/dnd/images/flameskull.jpg)


> **Type**: Tiny undead, neutral evil | **CR**: 4 (1,100 XP) | **Source**: Monster Manual p.134–135

## Stat Block

**Armor Class** 13
**Hit Points** 40 (9d4 + 18)
**Speed** 0 ft., fly 40 ft.

|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|

**Damage Immunities** cold, fire, poison
**Condition Immunities** [[Charmed|charmed]], [[Frightened|frightened]], [[Paralyzed|paralyzed]], [[Poisoned|poisoned]]
**Senses** darkvision 60 ft., passive Perception 12
**Languages** Common
**Challenge** 4 (1,100 XP)

### Traits

**Illumination.** The flameskull sheds either dim light in a 15-foot radius, or bright light in a 15-foot radius and dim light for an additional 15 feet. It can switch between the options as an action.

**Magic Resistance.** The flameskull has advantage on saving throws against spells and other magical effects.

**Rejuvenation.** If the flameskull is destroyed, it regains all its hit points in 1 hour unless holy water is sprinkled on its remains or a *dispel magic* or *remove curse* spell is cast on them.

**Spellcasting.** The flameskull is a 5th-level spellcaster. Its spellcasting ability is Intelligence (spell save DC 13, +5 to hit with spell attacks). It requires no somatic or material components to cast its spells. The flameskull has the following wizard spells prepared:
- Cantrip (at will): *mage hand*
- 1st level (3 slots): *magic missile*, *shield*
- 2nd level (2 slots): *blur*, *flaming sphere*
- 3rd level (1 slot): *fireball*

### Actions

**Multiattack.** The flameskull uses Fire Ray twice.

**Fire Ray.** *Ranged Spell Attack:* +5 to hit, range 30 ft., one target. *Hit:* 10 (3d6) fire damage.

## Lore

Blazing green flames and mad, echoing laughter follow a disembodied skull as it patrols its demesne. Dark spellcasters fashion flameskulls from the remains of dead wizards.

**Legacy of Life.** A flameskull only dimly recalls its former life. Though it might speak in its old voice and recount key events from its past, it is but an echo of its former self. Its undead transformation grants it full access to the magic it wielded in life.

**Eternally Bound.** Intelligent and vigilant, a flameskull serves its creator by protecting a hidden treasure hoard, a secret chamber, or a specific individual. It interprets commands to the letter.

**Eldritch Rejuvenation.** A flameskull's shattered fragments reform unless splashed with holy water or subjected to *dispel magic* or *remove curse*. If it can no longer fulfill its intended purpose, the re-formed flameskull becomes autonomous.

**Undead Nature.** A flameskull doesn't require air, food, drink, or sleep.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!quote] Tactical Deep Dive
> See [[Flameskull-Tactics]] for detailed combat tactics from *The Monsters Know What They're Doing*.


> [!tip] DM Tip
> Rejuvenation means the flameskull reforms in 1 hour unless *dispel magic* or holy water is applied to its remains. It will keep coming back — tell the party or this becomes a frustrating loop. Opens with its one *fireball* slot, then kites with Fire Ray.

- **What it is:** A flying, spellcasting undead skull wreathed in green flame. CR 4 but punches above its weight with *[[Fireball|fireball]]* and Rejuvenation. A classic dungeon guardian.
- **When to use:** Wizard towers, ancient tombs, dungeon vaults. Iconic in modules like Lost Mine of Phandelver. Great as a recurring nuisance thanks to Rejuvenation.
- **Key combat tactics:** Opens with *fireball* (its only 3rd-level slot). Then Fire Ray twice per turn for 3d6 fire each. *Shield* as a reaction bumps AC to 18. *Blur* for sustained defense. Flies out of melee reach and kites.
- **Notable abilities:** Rejuvenation reforms it in 1 hour unless holy water, *[[Dispel Magic|dispel magic]]*, or *remove curse* is applied. Magic Resistance + *shield* makes it surprisingly durable. Immune to cold and fire.
- **Conditions inflicted:** None directly
- **Suggested encounter pairings:** Zombies or skeletons as fodder while the flameskull rains fire from above. Pair with animated armor or a flesh golem in a wizard's lab. Multiple flameskulls in a large chamber is devastating.
