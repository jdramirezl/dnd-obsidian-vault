# Fomorian

![Fomorian](https://www.aidedd.org/dnd/images/fomorian.jpg)


> **Type**: Huge giant, chaotic evil | **CR**: 8 (3,900 XP) | **Source**: Monster Manual p.136–137

## Stat Block

**Armor Class** 14 (natural armor)
**Hit Points** 149 (13d12 + 65)
**Speed** 30 ft.

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| 23 (+6) | 10 (+0) | 20 (+5) | 9 (-1) | 14 (+2) | 6 (-2) |

**Skills** Perception +8, Stealth +3
**Senses** darkvision 120 ft., passive Perception 18
**Languages** Giant, Undercommon
**Challenge** 8 (3,900 XP)

### Actions

**Multiattack.** The fomorian attacks twice with its greatclub or makes one greatclub attack and uses Evil Eye once.

**Greatclub.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +9 to hit, reach 15 ft., one target. *Hit:* 19 (3d8 + 6) bludgeoning damage.

**Evil Eye.** The fomorian magically forces a creature it can see within 60 feet of it to make a DC 14 Charisma saving throw. The creature takes 27 (6d8) psychic damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one.

**Curse of the Evil Eye (Recharges after a Short or Long Rest).** With a stare, the fomorian uses Evil Eye, but on a failed save, the creature is also cursed with magical deformities. While deformed, the creature has its speed halved and has disadvantage on ability checks, saving throws, and attacks based on Strength or Dexterity. The transformed creature can repeat the saving throw whenever it finishes a long rest, ending the effect on a success.

## Lore

The most hideous and wicked of all giantkind are the godless fomorians, whose deformed bodies reflect their vile demeanors.

**Fey Curse.** The elves remember when the fomorians were among the most handsome of races, possessed of brilliant minds and unrivaled magical ability. The fomorians sought to conquer the Feywild and enslave its inhabitants. When the fey united to defend their realm, the fomorians were subjected to a terrible curse — their bodies warped to reflect the evil in their hearts. Stripped of their grace, the wretched horrors fled from the light, delving deep beneath the world.

**Giants of the Underdark.** The fomorians dwell in eerily beautiful caverns in the Underdark, rarely venturing to the surface. Wickedness and depravity are the cornerstones of fomorian society, in which the strongest and cruelest giants rule.

**Curse of the Evil Eye.** Fomorians can pass their curse onto others using a power called the evil eye — a last vestige of the giants' once-remarkable spellcasting ability.

> [!tip] DM Tip
> Curse of the Evil Eye (DC 14 Cha) halves speed AND imposes disadvantage on Str/Dex attacks, checks, and saves — cripples martial characters. Evil Eye also deals 6d8 psychic as part of Multiattack. Target the party's strongest melee fighter first.

- **What it is:** A cursed, deformed Underdark giant with a devastating magical gaze. CR 8 with high HP (149), strong melee, and a nasty curse. Unlike other giants, fomorians can't throw rocks.
- **When to use:** Underdark encounters, Feywild-connected storylines, or as slavers in deep caverns. Their tragic backstory (cursed by the fey) makes them interesting villains.
- **Key combat tactics:** Opens with Curse of the Evil Eye on the party's strongest martial character (DC 14 Cha save). The curse halves speed and imposes disadvantage on Str/Dex attacks, checks, and saves. Then Multiattack: one greatclub (3d8+6) + one Evil Eye (6d8 psychic). 15-ft reach on the greatclub is excellent.
- **Notable abilities:** Curse of the Evil Eye recharges on short rest — once per fight. The curse persists until a long rest save succeeds. Evil Eye deals 6d8 psychic at 60-ft range with no attack roll. Passive Perception 18 makes it very hard to ambush.
- **Conditions inflicted:** Cursed (Curse of the Evil Eye — speed halved, disadvantage on Str/Dex)
- **Suggested encounter pairings:** Enslaved creatures (duergar, grimlocks, quaggoths) as fodder. Multiple fomorians in a cavern lair. Pair with a Feywild quest about the fey who cursed them.
