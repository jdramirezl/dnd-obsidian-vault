# Fungi

![Fungi](https://www.aidedd.org/dnd/images/shrieker.jpg)


> **Type**: Plant, unaligned | **CR**: 0 / 1/4 / 1/2 | **Source**: Monster Manual p.137–139

With its sky of jagged stone and perpetual night, the Underdark is home to all manner of fungi. Taking the place of plants in the subterranean realm, fungi are vital to the survival of many underground species, providing nourishment and shelter in the unforgiving darkness. Not needing sunlight or warmth to grow, fungi thrive in every corner and crevice of the Underdark.

## Gas Spore

> **Type**: Large plant, unaligned | **CR**: 1/2 (100 XP) | **Source**: Monster Manual p.138–139

### Stat Block

**Armor Class** 5
**Hit Points** 1 (1d10 - 4)
**Speed** 0 ft., fly 10 ft. (hover)

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| 5 (-3) | 1 (-5) | 3 (-4) | 1 (-5) | 1 (-5) | 1 (-5) |

**Damage Immunities** poison
**Condition Immunities** [[Blinded|blinded]], [[Deafened|deafened]], [[Frightened|frightened]], [[Paralyzed|paralyzed]], [[Poisoned|poisoned]], [[Prone|prone]]
**Senses** blindsight 30 ft. (blind beyond this radius), passive Perception 5
**Languages** —
**Challenge** 1/2 (100 XP)

#### Traits

**Death Burst.** The gas spore explodes when it drops to 0 hit points. Each creature within 20 feet of it must succeed on a DC 15 Constitution saving throw or take 10 (3d6) poison damage and become infected with a disease on a failed save. Creatures immune to the [[Poisoned|poisoned]] condition are immune to this disease. Spores invade an infected creature's system, killing the creature in a number of hours equal to 1d12 + the creature's Constitution score, unless the disease is removed. In half that time, the creature becomes [[Poisoned|poisoned]] for the rest of the duration. After the creature dies, it sprouts 2d4 Tiny gas spores that grow to full size in 7 days.

**Eerie Resemblance.** The gas spore resembles a beholder. A creature that can see the gas spore can discern its true nature with a successful DC 15 Intelligence (Nature) check.

#### Actions

**Touch.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +0 to hit, reach 5 ft., one creature. *Hit:* 1 poison damage, and the creature must succeed on a DC 10 Constitution saving throw or become infected with the disease described in the Death Burst trait.

## Lore

**Gas Spore** — The first gas spores are thought to have been spawned from dead beholders, whose moldering corpses fed a parasitic fungus with aberrant magic. A gas spore is a spherical, balloon-like fungus that resembles a beholder from a distance. Piercing the shell causes the creature to burst apart, releasing a cloud of deadly spores. A creature that inhales the spores and survives inherits one or more of the beholder's fragmented memories.

**Shrieker** — A shrieker is a human-sized mushroom that emits a piercing screech to drive off creatures that disturb it. Various intelligent races of the Underdark cultivate shriekers on the outskirts of their communities to discourage trespassers.

**Violet Fungus** — This purplish mushroom uses root-like feelers to creep across cavern floors. The four stalks protruding from its central mass lash out at prey, rotting flesh with the slightest touch.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!tip] DM Tip
> Gas Spores look exactly like beholders — use them to fake out the party. Shriekers are living alarms that alert nearby monsters. Violet Fungi guard passages with 10-ft reach Rotting Touch. All are best as environmental hazards, not standalone encounters.

- **What they are:** Three Underdark fungi — Gas Spore (beholder mimic, CR 1/2), Shrieker (living alarm, CR 0), Violet Fungus (melee attacker, CR 1/4). All are plant creatures with extremely low stats.
- **When to use:** Underdark exploration, fungal caverns, myconid territory. Shriekers are classic dungeon alarms. Gas spores are beholder-shaped traps. Violet fungi guard passages.
- **Key combat tactics:**
  - *Gas Spore:* Has 1 HP. The danger is Death Burst — DC 15 Con or take 3d6 poison AND contract a lethal disease. The disease kills in hours. Eerie Resemblance (DC 15 Nature) means parties may mistake it for a beholder and waste resources.
  - *Shrieker:* No attacks. Shrieks when anything comes within 30 ft, audible for 300 ft. Alerts every creature in the dungeon. Kill it fast or avoid it.
  - *Violet Fungus:* 1d4 Rotting Touch attacks per turn at 10-ft reach dealing 1d8 necrotic each. Surprisingly dangerous in groups.
- **Suggested encounter pairings:** Shriekers near a drow patrol route. Gas spores in a beholder's former lair. Violet fungi mixed with shriekers — the shriekers alert nearby monsters while the fungi attack. Myconids cultivating all three types.
