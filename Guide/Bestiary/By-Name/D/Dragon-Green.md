# Green Dragon

![Green Dragon](https://www.aidedd.org/dnd/images/green-dragon.jpg)


> **Source**: Monster Manual pp.94–97

## Lore

The most cunning and treacherous of true dragons, green dragons use misdirection and trickery to get the upper hand against their enemies. Nasty tempered and thoroughly evil, they take special pleasure in subverting and corrupting the good-hearted. In the ancient forests they roam, green dragons demonstrate an aggression that is often less about territory than it is about gaining power and wealth with as little effort as possible.

A green dragon is recognized by its curved jawline and the crest that begins near its eyes and continues down its spine, reaching full height just behind the skull. A green dragon has no external ears, but bears leathery spiked plates that run down the sides of its neck. A wyrmling green dragon's thin scales are a shade of green so dark as to appear nearly black. As a green dragon ages, its scales grow larger and lighter, turning shades of forest, emerald, and olive green.

**Capricious Hunters.** A green dragon hunts by patrolling its forest territory from the air and the ground. It eats any creature it can see, but its favorite prey is elves. Green dragons are consummate liars and masters of double talk. They favor intimidation of lesser creatures, but employ more subtle manipulations when dealing with other dragons.

**Manipulative Schemers.** A wily and subtle creature, a green dragon bends other creatures to its will by assessing and playing off their deepest desires. Among their own kind, they are loud, crass, and rude, especially when dealing with dragons of the same age and status.

**Conflict and Corruption.** Green dragons sometimes clash with other dragons over territory where forest crosses over into other terrain. They accept the servitude of goblinoids, ettercaps, ettins, kobolds, orcs, and yuan-ti. They also delight in corrupting and bending elves to their will.

**Living Treasures.** A green dragon's favored treasures are the sentient creatures it bends to its will, including popular heroes, well-known sages, and renowned bards. Among material treasures, a green dragon favors emeralds, wood carvings, musical instruments, and sculptures.

**A Green Dragon's Lair.** A perpetual fog hangs in the air in a legendary green dragon's wood, carrying an acrid whiff of the creature's poison breath. The moss-covered trees grow close together except where winding pathways trace their way like a maze into the heart of the forest. At the center, a green dragon chooses a cave in a sheer cliff or hillside for its lair, preferring an entrance hidden from prying eyes — behind waterfalls, or partly submerged caverns accessed through lakes or streams.

## Lair Actions

On initiative count 20 (losing initiative ties), the dragon takes a lair action to cause one of the following effects; the dragon can't use the same effect two rounds in a row:

- **Grasping Roots.** Grasping roots and vines erupt in a 20-foot radius centered on a point on the ground the dragon can see within 120 feet. That area becomes difficult terrain, and each creature there must succeed on a DC 15 Strength saving throw or be [[Restrained|restrained]] by the roots and vines. A creature can be freed if it or another creature takes an action to make a DC 15 Strength check and succeeds. The roots wilt away when the dragon uses this lair action again or dies.
- **Thorn Wall.** A wall of tangled brush bristling with thorns springs into existence on a solid surface within 120 feet. The wall is up to 60 feet long, 10 feet high, and 5 feet thick, and it blocks line of sight. When the wall appears, each creature in its area must make a DC 15 Dexterity saving throw. A creature that fails takes 18 (4d8) piercing damage and is pushed 5 feet out of the wall's space. Moving through the wall costs 4 feet of movement per 1 foot traveled, and a creature in the wall must make a DC 15 Dexterity saving throw each round, taking 18 (4d8) piercing damage on a failure, or half on a success. Each 10-foot section has AC 5, 15 HP, vulnerability to fire, resistance to bludgeoning and piercing, and immunity to psychic damage.
- **Charming Fog.** Magical fog billows around one creature the dragon can see within 120 feet. The creature must succeed on a DC 15 Wisdom saving throw or be [[Charmed|charmed]] by the dragon until initiative count 20 on the next round.

## Regional Effects

The region containing a legendary green dragon's lair is warped by the dragon's magic:

- Thickets form labyrinthine passages within 1 mile of the lair. The thickets act as 10-foot-high, 10-foot-thick walls that block line of sight. Moving through them costs 4 feet per 1 foot, and a creature must make a DC 15 Dexterity saving throw each round or take 3 (1d6) piercing damage from thorns. Each 10-foot-cube has AC 5, 30 HP, resistance to bludgeoning and piercing, vulnerability to fire, and immunity to psychic and thunder damage.
- Within 1 mile of its lair, the dragon leaves no physical evidence of its passage unless it wishes to. Tracking it there is impossible except by magical means. It ignores movement impediments and damage from non-magical, non-creature plants.
- Rodents and birds within 1 mile serve as the dragon's eyes and ears. Deer and other large game are strangely absent.

If the dragon dies, the rodents and birds lose their supernatural link. The thickets remain but within 1d10 days become mundane plants and normal difficult terrain, losing their thorns.

### Tactical Notes

- **Wyrmling (CR 2)**: Suitable for level 1–3 parties. Ambushes from dense undergrowth. Poison breath in a cone is harder to avoid than a line.
- **Young (CR 8)**: Suitable for level 4–7 parties. Uses forest canopy for cover. Stalks the party for days before attacking. Prefers to capture rather than kill — prisoners have value.
- **Adult (CR 15)**: Suitable for level 10–13 parties. Highly intelligent (INT 18). Uses Charming Fog lair action to turn party members against each other. Deception +8 and Persuasion +8 make it a dangerous social encounter too.
- **Ancient (CR 22)**: Suitable for level 16–19 parties. INT 20 and a full suite of social skills. The 90-ft. cone breath weapon covers enormous areas. Grasping Roots + Thorn Wall creates kill zones.
- **Lair Tactics**: The green dragon is the most tactically sophisticated chromatic dragon. It uses Grasping Roots to immobilize melee fighters, Thorn Wall to split the party, and Charming Fog to turn the party's strongest member against them. It fights from the maze of thickets in its forest, using its immunity to plant-based movement impediments to kite the party.
