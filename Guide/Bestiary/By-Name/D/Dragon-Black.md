# Black Dragon

![Black Dragon](https://www.aidedd.org/dnd/images/black-dragon.jpg)


> **Source**: Monster Manual pp.87–89

## Lore

The most evil-tempered and vile of the chromatic dragons, black dragons collect the wreckage and treasures of fallen peoples. These dragons loathe seeing the weak prosper and revel in the collapse of humanoid kingdoms. They make their homes in fetid swamps and crumbling ruins where kingdoms once stood.

With deep-socketed eyes and broad nasal openings, a black dragon's face resembles a skull. Its curving, segmented horns are bone-colored near the base and darken to dead black at the tips. As a black dragon ages, the flesh around its horns and cheekbones deteriorates as though eaten by acid, leaving thin layers of hide that enhance its skeletal appearance.

**Brutal and Cruel.** All chromatic dragons are evil, but black dragons stand apart for their sadistic nature. A black dragon lives to watch its prey beg for mercy, and will often offer the illusion of respite or escape before finishing off its enemies.

**Foes and Servants.** Black dragons hate and fear other dragons. Evil lizardfolk venerate and serve black dragons. Kobolds infest the lairs of many black dragons like vermin.

**Wealth of the Ancients.** Black dragons hoard the treasures and magic items of crumbled empires and conquered kingdoms to remind themselves of their greatness.

**A Black Dragon's Lair.** Black dragons dwell in swamps on the frayed edges of civilization. A black dragon's lair is a dismal cave, grotto, or ruin that is at least partially flooded, providing pools where the dragon rests, and where its victims can ferment. The lair is littered with the acid-pitted bones of previous victims and the fly-ridden carcasses of fresh kills. Centipedes, scorpions, and snakes infest the lair.

## Lair Actions

On initiative count 20 (losing initiative ties), the dragon takes a lair action to cause one of the following effects; the dragon can't use the same effect two rounds in a row:

- **Grasping Tide.** Pools of water that the dragon can see within 120 feet surge outward. Any creature on the ground within 20 feet of such a pool must succeed on a DC 15 Strength saving throw or be pulled up to 20 feet into the water and knocked [[Prone|prone]].
- **Insect Swarm.** A cloud of swarming insects fills a 20-foot-radius sphere centered on a point the dragon chooses within 120 feet. The cloud spreads around corners, is lightly obscured, and remains until dismissed, reused, or the dragon dies. Any creature in the cloud when it appears must make a DC 15 Constitution saving throw, taking 10 (3d6) piercing damage on a failed save, or half on a success. A creature that ends its turn in the cloud takes 10 (3d6) piercing damage.
- **Magical Darkness.** Magical darkness spreads from a point the dragon chooses within 60 feet, filling a 15-foot-radius sphere. Darkvision can't see through it, nonmagical light can't illuminate it. If overlapping with light from a spell of 2nd level or lower, that spell is dispelled.

## Regional Effects

The region containing a legendary black dragon's lair is warped by the dragon's magic:

- The land within 6 miles of the lair takes twice as long as normal to traverse, since the plants grow thick and twisted, and the swamps are thick with reeking mud.
- Water sources within 1 mile of the lair are supernaturally fouled. Enemies of the dragon that drink such water regurgitate it within minutes.
- Fog lightly obscures the land within 6 miles of the lair.

If the dragon dies, vegetation remains as it has grown, but other effects fade over 1d10 days.

### Tactical Notes

- **Wyrmling (CR 2)**: Suitable for level 1–3 parties. Simple bite + breath. Ambushes from murky water.
- **Young (CR 7)**: Suitable for level 4–6 parties. Gains Multiattack. Uses swamp terrain to limit movement, then strafes with acid breath.
- **Adult (CR 14)**: Suitable for level 9–12 parties. Frightful Presence shuts down low-Wisdom characters. Uses lair actions to pull enemies into water and obscure vision.
- **Ancient (CR 21)**: Suitable for level 15–18 parties. The 90-ft. line breath weapon is 10 ft. wide — harder to dodge in tight swamp corridors. Lair actions create a nightmarish battlefield.
- **Lair Tactics**: Fights in its flooded lair whenever possible. Uses Grasping Tide to pull melee fighters into deep water, Magical Darkness to blind ranged attackers, and Insect Swarm for attrition zones. Retreats underwater between attacks using swim speed and Amphibious.
