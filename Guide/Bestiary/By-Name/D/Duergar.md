# Duergar

![Duergar](https://www.aidedd.org/dnd/images/duergar.jpg)


> **Type**: Medium humanoid (dwarf), lawful evil | **CR**: 1 (200 XP) | **Source**: Monster Manual p.122–123

## Stat Block

**Armor Class** 16 (scale mail, shield)
**Hit Points** 26 (4d8 + 8)
**Speed** 25 ft.

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| 14 (+2) | 11 (+0) | 14 (+2) | 11 (+0) | 10 (+0) | 9 (−1) |

**Damage Resistances** poison
**Senses** darkvision 120 ft., passive Perception 10
**Languages** Dwarvish, Undercommon
**Challenge** 1 (200 XP)

### Traits

**Duergar Resilience.** The duergar has advantage on saving throws against poison, spells, and illusions, as well as to resist being [[Charmed|charmed]] or [[Paralyzed|paralyzed]].

**Sunlight Sensitivity.** While in sunlight, the duergar has disadvantage on attack rolls, as well as on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on sight.

### Actions

**Enlarge (Recharges after a Short or Long Rest).** For 1 minute, the duergar magically increases in size, along with anything it is wearing or carrying. While enlarged, the duergar is Large, doubles its damage dice on Strength-based weapon attacks (included in the attacks), and makes Strength checks and Strength saving throws with advantage. If the duergar lacks the room to become Large, it attains the maximum size possible in the space available.

**War Pick.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +4 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 6 (1d8 + 2) piercing damage, or 11 (2d8 + 2) piercing damage while enlarged.

**Javelin.** *Melee or Ranged Weapon Attack:* +4 to hit, reach 5 ft. or range 30/120 ft., one target. *Hit:* 5 (1d6 + 2) piercing damage, or 9 (2d6 + 2) piercing damage while enlarged.

**Invisibility (Recharges after a Short or Long Rest).** The duergar magically turns invisible until it attacks, casts a spell, or uses its Enlarge, or until its concentration is broken, up to 1 hour (as if concentrating on a spell). Any equipment the duergar wears or carries is invisible with it.

## Lore

The tyrannical duergar, also known as gray dwarves, dwell in fantastic cities deep in the Underdark. Using ancient dwarven knowledge and myriad slaves, they work tirelessly to expand their subterranean kingdoms.

**Slaves to Slavers.** The duergar were once dwarves, before their greed and endless delving brought them into contact with the mind flayers. Held in captivity for generations by the illithids, the dwarves eventually won their independence with the aid of the evil god Laduguer. Slavery had forever changed them, however, darkening their spirits to make the duergar as evil as the tyrants they had escaped.

**Born of Darkness.** A duergar can increase its size and strength for a short time, becoming a powerful ogre-sized warrior. If it faces a foe it can't fight, it can just as easily become invisible to slip away into the darkness.

**Infernal Master.** Asmodeus, Lord of the Nine Hells, has been known to impersonate duergar gods in order to cultivate the evil brimming in the hearts of the gray dwarves.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!tip] DM Tip
> Enlarge doubles weapon damage dice (war pick becomes 2d8+2) and Invisibility allows ambushes — both as actions. Sunlight Sensitivity gives disadvantage in bright light. Much more dangerous than typical CR 1 humanoids.

**What it is:** A CR 1 Underdark dwarf with **Enlarge** (doubles weapon damage) and **[[Invisibility|invisibility]]**. Much more dangerous than a typical CR 1 humanoid.

**When to use:** Underdark patrols, slave-raiding parties, duergar fortresses. Staple Underdark humanoid from tier 1 onward.

**Combat tactics:**
- Scouts use **Invisibility** to observe — the party may be watched long before combat
- Opens with **Enlarge** on round 1, then attacks with doubled damage dice (2d8+2 war pick)
- In groups, some Enlarge while others stay [[Invisible|invisible]] for a devastating ambush round

**Notable abilities:**
- **Duergar Resilience** — advantage vs. spells, illusions, [[Charmed|charmed]], [[Paralyzed|paralyzed]]; extremely hard to control
- **Sunlight Sensitivity** — massive weakness aboveground
- Both Enlarge and Invisibility recharge on short rest — always available in a dungeon

**Encounter pairings:**
- Patrol of 4–6 with invisible scouts and enlarged front-liners
- Slave-driving captured humanoids through Underdark tunnels
- Allied with a mind flayer as thralls or uneasy partners
