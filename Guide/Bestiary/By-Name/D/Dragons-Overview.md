# Dragons — Overview

> **Source**: Monster Manual pp.86–119

True dragons are winged reptiles of ancient lineage and fearsome power. They are known and feared for their predatory cunning and greed, with the oldest dragons accounted as some of the most powerful creatures in the world. Dragons are also magical creatures whose innate power fuels their dreaded breath weapons and other preternatural abilities.

Many creatures, including wyverns and dragon turtles, have draconic blood. However, true dragons fall into the two broad categories of **chromatic** and **metallic** dragons.

## Chromatic Dragons

The black, blue, green, red, and white dragons represent the evil side of dragonkind. Aggressive, gluttonous, and vain, chromatic dragons are dark sages and powerful tyrants feared by all creatures — including each other.

**Driven by Greed.** Chromatic dragons lust after treasure, and this greed colors their every scheme and plot. They believe that the world's wealth belongs to them by right, and a chromatic dragon seizes that wealth without regard for the humanoids and other creatures that have "stolen" it.

**Creatures of Ego.** Chromatic dragons are united by their sense of superiority, believing themselves the most powerful and worthy of all mortal creatures. Trying to humble a chromatic dragon is like trying to convince the wind to stop blowing.

**Dangerous Lairs.** A dragon's lair serves as the seat of its power and a vault for its treasure. Most chromatic dragon lairs are hidden in dangerous and remote locations to prevent all but the most audacious mortals from reaching them.

**Queen of Evil Dragons.** Tiamat the Dragon Queen is the chief deity of evil dragonkind. She dwells on Avernus, the first layer of the Nine Hells. Tiamat is a gigantic dragon whose five heads reflect the forms of the chromatic dragons that worship her — black, blue, green, red, and white. Her most hated enemy is Bahamut the Platinum Dragon.

## Metallic Dragons

The brass, bronze, copper, gold, and silver dragons are noble, good, and highly respected by the wise. Metallic dragons seek to preserve and protect, viewing themselves as one powerful race among the many creatures that have a place in the world.

**King of Good Dragons.** Bahamut, the Platinum Dragon, is the chief deity of metallic dragonkind. He dwells in the Seven Heavens of Mount Celestia but often wanders the Material Plane in the guise of a venerable human male.

---

## Dragon Hoards

All true dragons covet wealth, hoarding mounds of coins and gathering gems, jewels, and magic items. Dragons with large hoards are loath to leave them for long, venturing out of their lairs only to patrol or feed. A dragon's hoard is the stuff of legend.

---

## Variant: Dragons as Innate Spellcasters

A young or older dragon can innately cast a number of spells equal to its Charisma modifier. Each spell can be cast once per day, requiring no material components, and the spell's level can be no higher than one-third the dragon's challenge rating (rounded down). The dragon's bonus to hit with spell attacks is equal to its proficiency bonus + its Charisma bonus. The dragon's spell save DC equals 8 + its proficiency bonus + its Charisma modifier.

## Dracolich Template

> *See full details: [[Dracolich]]*

Only an ancient or adult true dragon can be transformed into a dracolich. The dragon consumes a toxic brew that slays it instantly; attendant spellcasters ensnare its spirit and transfer it to a special gemstone (phylactery). The dracolich's type changes to **undead**, it gains resistance to necrotic damage, immunity to poison and the [[Charmed|charmed]], [[Frightened|frightened]], [[Paralyzed|paralyzed]], and [[Poisoned|poisoned]] conditions, and gains **Magic Resistance**.

---

## Shadow Dragon Template

> *See full details: [[Shadow-Dragon]]*

Only a true dragon born in the Shadowfell or that remains there for several years can become a shadow dragon. The transformation grants resistance to necrotic damage, **Living Shadow** (resistance to all damage except force, psychic, or radiant while in dim light or darkness), **Shadow Stealth**, and **Sunlight Sensitivity**. Its breath weapon deals necrotic damage instead of its original type.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!tip] DM Tip
> Adult+ dragons get Legendary Resistance (3/day), Legendary Actions, Frightful Presence, and Lair Actions. Always fight dragons in their lair for the full experience. Breath weapon recharge (5–6) means it comes back ~33% of rounds — plan accordingly.

### Dragon Age vs Party Level Guide

| Age Category | CR Range | Party Level Tier | Encounter Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Wyrmling** | CR 1–4 | Low (1–4) | Solo boss for level 1–3 parties; easy encounter for level 4+ |
| **Young** | CR 6–10 | Mid (5–10) | Challenging boss fight; first "real" dragon encounter for most parties |
| **Adult** | CR 13–17 | High (11–16) | Deadly with lair actions; requires party strategy and resources |
| **Ancient** | CR 20–24 | Epic (17–20) | Campaign-defining threat; legendary actions + lair = TPK potential |

### Lair Action Reminder

- Lair actions trigger on **initiative count 20** (losing ties).
- Only **adult and ancient** dragons have lair actions.
- A dragon can use **one** lair action per round; can't use the same one two rounds in a row.
- Lair actions occur in the dragon's lair only — lure the dragon out to negate them.
- **Regional effects** extend miles from the lair and fade over 1d10 days if the dragon dies.

### Innate Spellcaster Variant — Quick Rules

- Available to **young or older** dragons.
- **Spells known** = dragon's Charisma modifier (no material components).
- **Max spell level** = dragon's CR ÷ 3 (rounded down).
- **Spell attack bonus** = proficiency bonus + Charisma modifier.
- **Spell save DC** = 8 + proficiency bonus + Charisma modifier.
- Each spell is cast **once per day**.

| Example | CR | Cha Mod | Max Spell Level | Spells Known |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Young Red | 10 | +4 | 3rd | 4 spells |
| Adult Blue | 16 | +4 | 5th | 4 spells |
| Ancient Gold | 24 | +7 | 8th | 7 spells |

> **Tip**: Choose spells that match the dragon's personality — *suggestion* for green, *wall of fire* for red, *detect thoughts* for gold.
