# Death Knight

![Death Knight](https://www.aidedd.org/dnd/images/death-knight.jpg)


> **Type**: Medium undead, chaotic evil | **CR**: 17 (18,000 XP) | **Source**: Monster Manual p.47

## Stat Block

**Armor Class** 20 (plate, shield)
**Hit Points** 180 (19d8 + 95)
**Speed** 30 ft.

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| 20 (+5) | 11 (+0) | 20 (+5) | 12 (+1) | 16 (+3) | 18 (+4) |

**Saving Throws** Dex +6, Wis +9, Cha +10
**Damage Immunities** necrotic, poison
**Condition Immunities** [[Exhaustion|exhaustion]], [[Frightened|frightened]], [[Poisoned|poisoned]]
**Senses** darkvision 120 ft., passive Perception 13
**Languages** Abyssal, Common
**Challenge** 17 (18,000 XP)

### Traits

**Magic Resistance.** The death knight has advantage on saving throws against spells and other magical effects.

**Marshal Undead.** Unless the death knight is [[Incapacitated|incapacitated]], it and undead creatures of its choice within 60 feet of it have advantage on saving throws against features that turn undead.

**Spellcasting.** The death knight is a 19th-level spellcaster. Its spellcasting ability is Charisma (spell save DC 18, +10 to hit with spell attacks). It has the following paladin spells prepared:

- *1st level (4 slots):* command, compelled duel, searing smite
- *2nd level (3 slots):* hold person, magic weapon
- *3rd level (3 slots):* dispel magic, elemental weapon
- *4th level (3 slots):* banishment, staggering smite
- *5th level (2 slots):* destructive wave (necrotic)

### Actions

**Multiattack.** The death knight makes three longsword attacks.

**Longsword.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +11 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 9 (1d8 + 5) slashing damage, or 10 (1d10 + 5) slashing damage if used with two hands, plus 18 (4d8) necrotic damage.

**Hellfire Orb (1/Day).** The death knight hurls a magical ball of fire that explodes at a point it can see within 120 feet of it. Each creature in a 20-foot-radius sphere centered on that point must make a DC 18 Dexterity saving throw. The sphere spreads around corners. A creature takes 35 (10d6) fire damage and 35 (10d6) necrotic damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one.

### Reactions

**Parry.** The death knight adds 6 to its AC against one melee attack that would hit it. To do so, the death knight must see the attacker and be wielding a melee weapon.

## Lore

When a paladin that falls from grace dies without seeking atonement, dark powers can transform the once-mortal knight into a hateful undead creature. A death knight is a skeletal warrior clad in fearsome plate armor. Beneath its helmet, one can see the knight's skull with malevolent pinpoints of light burning in its eye sockets.

**Eldritch Power.** The death knight retains the ability to cast divine spells. However, no death knight can use its magic to heal. A death knight also attracts and commands lesser undead, although death knights that serve powerful fiends might have fiendish followers instead. Death knights often use warhorse skeletons and nightmares as mounts.

**Immortal Until Redeemed.** A death knight can arise anew even after it has been destroyed. Only when it atones for a life of wickedness or finds redemption can it finally escape its undead purgatory and truly perish.

> [!info] Lord Soth
> Lord Soth began his fall from grace with an act of heroism, saving an elf named Isolde from an ogre. Soth and Isolde fell in love, but Soth was already married. He had a servant dispose of his wife and was charged with murder, but fled with Isolde. When his castle fell under siege, he prayed for guidance and was told that he must atone for his misdeeds by completing a quest, but growing fears about Isolde's fidelity caused him to abandon his quest. Because his mission was not accomplished, a great cataclysm swept the land. When Isolde gave birth to a son, Soth refused to believe that the child was his and slew them both. All were incinerated in a fire that swept through the castle, yet Soth would find no rest in death, becoming a death knight.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!quote] Tactical Deep Dive
> See [[Death-Knight-Tactics]] for detailed combat tactics from *The Monsters Know What They're Doing*.


> [!warning] Deadly Encounter
> CR 17 — recommended for parties of level 13–17. Hellfire Orb (8d6 fire + 8d6 necrotic, DC 18 Dex) is a TPK-level opener. Three melee attacks with +4d8 necrotic each round after.

> [!tip] DM Tip
> Open with Hellfire Orb on a clustered party, then wade into melee. Destructive Wave and Banishment from paladin spellcasting give it incredible versatility. Magic Resistance + Marshal Undead make it a nightmare with undead minions.

**What it is:** A CR 17 undead anti-paladin — one of the most dangerous single-monster encounters in the game. AC 20, 180 HP, three attacks per round dealing 4d8 necrotic on each hit, plus full paladin spellcasting.

**When to use:** As a major villain or boss encounter for high-level parties (13–17). Perfect for fallen-hero storylines, undead armies, or as a lieutenant to a lich or demon lord.

**Combat tactics:**
- Opens with **Hellfire Orb** — 10d6 fire + 10d6 necrotic in a 20-ft sphere is devastating (average 70 damage, DC 18 Dex)
- Follows with three longsword attacks per round, each dealing 1d8+5 slashing + 4d8 necrotic (average 27 per hit, 81 per round)
- Uses **Parry** reaction to boost AC to 26 against one melee attack
- Casts *[[Hold Person|hold person]]* to lock down martial characters, *banishment* to remove key threats, *destructive wave* for AoE
- **Marshal Undead** makes it nearly impossible to turn its undead allies

**Notable abilities:**
- Magic Resistance gives advantage on all spell saves — hard to control with magic
- Immune to necrotic and poison damage — many spells and abilities are useless
- *Destructive wave* (necrotic) is a 5th-level AoE: 5d6 thunder + 5d6 necrotic, DC 18 Con
- Immortal Until Redeemed — it comes back unless the story resolves its redemption arc

**Conditions inflicted:** [[Frightened|frightened]] (via spells), [[Paralyzed|paralyzed]] (hold person), [[Prone|prone]] (destructive wave)

**Encounter pairings:**
- Mounted on a nightmare or warhorse skeleton
- Leading a squad of wights and skeletons (Marshal Undead protects them from turning)
- As a recurring villain that returns after being "destroyed"
- Paired with a flameskull or wraith as lieutenants
