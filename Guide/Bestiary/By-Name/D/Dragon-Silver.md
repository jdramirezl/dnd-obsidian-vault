# Silver Dragon

![Silver Dragon](https://www.aidedd.org/dnd/images/silver-dragon.jpg)


> **Source**: Monster Manual pp.117–119

## Lore

The friendliest and most social of the metallic dragons, silver dragons cheerfully assist good creatures in need.

A silver dragon shimmers as if sculpted from pure metal, its face given a noble cast by its high eyes and sweeping beard-like chin spikes. A spiny frill rises high over its head, tracing down its neck to the tip of its tail. A silver wyrmling's scales are blue-gray with silver highlights. As the dragon approaches adulthood, its color gradually brightens until its individual scales are barely visible. As a silver dragon grows older, its pupils fade until its eyes resemble orbs of mercury.

**Dragons of Virtue.** Silver dragons believe that living a moral life involves doing good deeds and ensuring that one's actions cause no undeserved harm to other sentient beings. They don't take it upon themselves to root out evil, as gold and bronze dragons do, but they will gladly oppose creatures that dare to commit evil acts or harm the innocent.

**Friends of the Small Races.** Silver dragons enjoy the company of other silver dragons. Their only true friendships outside their own kin arise in the company of humanoids, and many silver dragons spend as much time in humanoid form as they do in draconic form. A silver dragon adopts a benign humanoid persona such as a kindly old sage or a young wanderer, and it often has mortal companions with whom it develops strong friendships. Silver dragons must step away from their humanoid lives on a regular basis, returning to their true forms to mate and rear offspring, or to tend to their hoards and personal affairs. Because many lose track of time while away, they sometimes return to find that their companions have grown old or died. Silver dragons often end up befriending several generations of humanoids within a single family as a result.

**Respect for Humanity.** Silver dragons befriend humanoids of all races, but shorter-lived races such as humans spark their curiosity in a way the longer-lived elves and dwarves don't. Humans have a drive and zest for life that silver dragons find fascinating.

**Hoarding History.** Silver dragons love to possess relics of humanoid history. This includes great piles of coins they covet, minted by current and fallen humanoid empires, as well as art objects and fine jewelry crafted by numerous races. Other treasures that make up their hoards can include intact ships, the remains of kings and queens, thrones, the crown jewels of ancient empires, inventions and contraptions, and monoliths carried from the ruins of fallen cities.

## Lair Actions

Silver dragons dwell among the clouds, making their lairs on secluded cold mountain peaks. Though many are comfortable in natural cavern complexes or abandoned mines, silver dragons covet the lost outposts of humanoid civilization. An abandoned mountaintop citadel or a remote tower raised by a long-dead wizard is the sort of lair that every silver dragon dreams of.

On initiative count 20 (losing initiative ties), the dragon takes a lair action to cause one of the following effects:

- The dragon creates fog as if it had cast the *fog cloud* spell. The fog lasts until initiative count 20 on the next round.
- A blizzard-like wind blows through the lair near the dragon. Each creature within 120 feet of the dragon must succeed on a DC 15 Constitution saving throw or take 5 (1d10) cold damage. Gases and vapors are dispersed by the wind, and unprotected flames are extinguished. Protected flames, such as lanterns, have a 50 percent chance of being extinguished.

## Regional Effects

The region containing a legendary silver dragon's lair is warped by the dragon's magic, which creates one or more of the following effects:

- Once per day, the dragon can alter the weather in a 6-mile radius centered on its lair. The dragon doesn't need to be outdoors; otherwise the effect is identical to the *control weather* spell.
- Within 1 mile of the lair, winds buoy non-evil creatures that fall due to no act of the dragon's or its allies. Such creatures descend at a rate of 60 feet per round and take no falling damage.
- Given days or longer to work, the dragon can make clouds and fog within its lair as solid as stone, forming structures and other objects as it wishes.

If the dragon dies, changed weather reverts to normal, as described in the spell, and the other effects fade in 1d10 days.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!quote] Tactical Deep Dive
> See [[Silver-Dragon-Tactics]] for detailed combat tactics from *The Monsters Know What They're Doing*.


> [!warning] Deadly Encounter
> Ancient Silver Dragon is CR 23 — recommended for parties of level 18+. Paralyzing Breath (Con save) is one of the most powerful breath weapons in the game — paralyzed targets auto-fail Str/Dex saves and attacks auto-crit within 5 ft.

> [!tip] DM Tip
> Paralyzing Breath is the priority — paralyzed PCs auto-fail Str/Dex saves, grant advantage on attacks, and take auto-crits in melee. Use it first, then follow up with melee Multiattack on paralyzed targets for devastating damage.

### Tactical Summary

Silver dragons are the second-highest CR metallic dragon (CR 23 Ancient) and uniquely dangerous thanks to Paralyzing Breath — one of the most powerful breath weapons in the game.

**Breath Weapon Priority:**
- **Cold Breath** (90-ft cone, DC 24 Con, 67 cold damage) — solid damage, CON save is harder for casters to resist.
- **Paralyzing Breath** (90-ft cone, DC 24 Con, [[Paralyzed|paralyzed]] for 1 min) — **the star ability**. [[Paralyzed|Paralyzed]] creatures auto-fail Str/Dex saves, attacks against them have advantage, and melee hits are automatic crits. A successful Paralyzing Breath followed by melee attacks from the dragon or its allies is devastating.
- **Combo**: Open with Paralyzing Breath to lock down the party, then use Multiattack (with auto-crits on [[Paralyzed|paralyzed]] targets) to deal massive damage. Switch to Cold Breath on subsequent recharges if the party has spread out.

**Change Shape (Adult+):**
- Polymorphs into any humanoid or beast of equal or lower CR. Retains HP, Hit Dice, Int/Wis/Cha, Legendary Resistance, and lair actions.
- Silver dragons are the most likely metallic dragon to be encountered in humanoid form — they actively seek humanoid companionship and maintain long-term disguises.
- A silver dragon NPC might be a recurring ally the party doesn't realize is a dragon until a dramatic reveal.

**Legendary Actions (3/round):**
- **Wing Attack** (2 actions) — 15-ft AoE knockdown + reposition. Essential for escaping melee and forcing the party to close distance again.
- **Tail Attack** (1 action) — reliable damage at 20-ft reach.
- **Detect** (1 action) — find [[Invisible|invisible]] or hidden creatures.

**Lair Tactics:**
- **Fog Cloud** provides heavy obscurement — the dragon has blindsight 60 ft. and can fight normally while the party is effectively [[Blinded|blinded]]. Ranged attackers and spellcasters requiring line of sight are shut down.
- **Blizzard Wind** (DC 15 Con, 5 cold damage + extinguishes flames) — minor damage but devastating for parties relying on torches or lanterns in the mountain lair. Also disperses gases, countering *cloudkill* and similar spells.

**Mountain Lair Advantage:** Silver dragon lairs on mountain peaks and in citadels create vertical combat spaces. The dragon can fly, the party likely can't. Wing Attack to reposition to high ground, then breathe down on clustered enemies.

**Key Weaknesses:**
- No fire resistance — fire damage is fully effective.
- DEX +0 (Ancient) / +0 (Adult) — low Dexterity makes them vulnerable to DEX-targeting effects, though Legendary Resistance compensates.
- Only 2 lair actions (most chromatic dragons get 3).

### Encounter Design

- **Wyrmling (CR 2)**: A silver wyrmling might be a rescued hatchling or a curious young dragon investigating humanoid settlements. Good for level 2–3 parties.
- **Young (CR 9)**: Could be living among humanoids already, perhaps as a scholar or wanderer. A reveal encounter works perfectly. Level 7–9 parties.
- **Adult (CR 16)**: A powerful ally or patron figure. Likely maintains a humanoid identity in a nearby city while keeping its true lair on a remote peak. Level 12–14 parties.
- **Ancient (CR 23)**: A legendary figure — possibly the secret protector of an entire kingdom. Its mountaintop citadel lair is a dungeon unto itself, with fog-filled corridors and wind-swept battlements. Level 17–20 parties.
- **Roleplaying Note**: Silver dragons are the most social metallic dragons. They make excellent long-term NPCs who genuinely care about the party. A silver dragon patron who reveals its true nature at a dramatic moment is a classic D&D story beat.
