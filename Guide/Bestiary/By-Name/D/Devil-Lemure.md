### Traits

**Devil's Sight.** Magical darkness doesn't impede the lemure's darkvision.

**Hellish Rejuvenation.** A lemure that dies in the Nine Hells comes back to life with all its hit points in 1d10 days unless it is killed by a good-aligned creature with a *bless* spell cast on that creature or its remains are sprinkled with holy water.

### Actions

**Fist.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +3 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 2 (1d4) bludgeoning damage.

## Lore

A lemure arises when a mortal soul is twisted by evil and banished to the Nine Hells for eternity. The lowest type of devil, lemures are repugnant, shapeless creatures doomed to suffer torment until they are promoted to a higher form of devil, most commonly an [[Devil-Imp|imp]].

A lemure resembles a molten mass of flesh with a vaguely humanoid head and torso. A permanent expression of anguish twists across its face, its feeble mouth babbling even though it can't speak.

See [[Devils-Overview]] for shared devil lore, the infernal hierarchy, and the Nine Hells.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!tip] DM Tip
> Hellish Rejuvenation means lemures reform in 1d10 days in the Nine Hells — endless waves. Permanent kill requires *bless* on the killer or holy water on the corpse. Use in swarms of 10+ for atmosphere.

- **Threat level:** Essentially zero — 2 avg damage, AC 7, 13 HP; cannon fodder
- **Hellish Rejuvenation:** In the Nine Hells, they come back in 1d10 days — endless waves of fodder
- **Permanent kill:** Requires *bless* on the killer OR holy water on the corpse — important quest detail
- **Condition immunities:** [[Charmed|charmed]], [[Frightened|frightened]], [[Poisoned|poisoned]] — mindless, can't be controlled or scared
- **Int 1:** Cannot be reasoned with, strategize, or follow complex orders; purely instinctual
- **Defenses:** Fire/poison immune, cold resistant — but AC 7 and 13 HP means anything kills them
- **Weakness:** Everything; 15 ft. speed means they can't catch anyone who walks away
- **Encounter use:** Atmosphere in Nine Hells scenes, tortured masses for [[Devil-Chain|chain devils]] to abuse, swarms of 10+ as speed bumps; great for showing the horror of damnation
