# Displacer Beast

![Displacer Beast](https://www.aidedd.org/dnd/images/displacer-beast.jpg)


> **Type**: Large monstrosity, lawful evil | **CR**: 3 (700 XP) | **Source**: Monster Manual p.81

## Stat Block

**Armor Class** 13 (natural armor)
**Hit Points** 85 (10d10 + 30)
**Speed** 40 ft.

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| 18 (+4) | 15 (+2) | 16 (+3) | 6 (−2) | 12 (+1) | 8 (−1) |

**Senses** darkvision 60 ft., passive Perception 11
**Languages** —
**Challenge** 3 (700 XP)

### Traits

**Avoidance.** If the displacer beast is subjected to an effect that allows it to make a saving throw to take only half damage, it instead takes no damage if it succeeds on the saving throw, and only half damage if it fails.

**Displacement.** The displacer beast projects a magical illusion that makes it appear to be standing near its actual location, causing attack rolls against it to have disadvantage. If it is hit by an attack, this trait is disrupted until the end of its next turn. This trait is also disrupted while the displacer beast is [[Incapacitated|incapacitated]] or has a speed of 0.

### Actions

**Multiattack.** The displacer beast makes two attacks with its tentacles.

**Tentacle.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +6 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. *Hit:* 7 (1d6 + 4) bludgeoning damage plus 3 (1d6) piercing damage.

## Lore

This monstrous predator takes its name from its ability to displace light so that it appears to be several feet away from its actual location. A displacer beast resembles a sleek great cat covered in blue-black fur. However, its otherworldly origins are clear in its six legs and the two tentacles sprouting from its shoulders, both ending in pads tipped with spiky protrusions. A displacer beast's eyes glow with an awful malevolence that persists even in death.

**Unseelie Origins.** Displacer beasts roamed the twilight lands of the Feywild for ages, until they were captured and trained by the Unseelie Court. The warriors of the court selectively bred the beasts to reinforce their ferocious and predatory nature, using them to hunt unicorns, pegasi, and other wondrous prey. However, it didn't take long for the displacer beasts to use their malevolent intelligence to escape their masters. Running and breeding freely in the Feywild, the displacer beasts soon came to the attention of the Seelie Court. With blink dog companions at their side, fey hunters drove these predators to the fringes of the Feywild, where many crossed over to the Material Plane. To this day, displacer beasts and blink dogs attack each other on sight.

**Love of the Kill.** Displacer beasts kill not just for food but also for sport. They target prey even when not hungry, often toying with their victims to entertain themselves until they are ready to eat. After killing its prey using its tentacles, a displacer beast drags the corpse to a quiet place where it can feed without distraction.

**Prized Guards and Pets.** Intelligent evil creatures favor displacer beasts as pets, but a displacer beast enters such an alliance only if it appears beneficial. A displacer beast might guard a vault or act as a bodyguard for a prominent individual.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!quote] Tactical Deep Dive

> [!tip] DM Tip
> Displacement forces disadvantage on ALL attacks against it until it's hit — then it resets at the start of its next turn. This makes the displacer beast much tougher than CR 3 suggests. AoE spells bypass Displacement entirely.

**What it is:** A CR 3 six-legged panther-like predator with displacement illusion and tentacle attacks. Iconic D&D monster — tougher than its CR suggests due to Displacement forcing disadvantage on all attacks.

**When to use:** Feywild encounters, dark forests, or as a pet/guardian for an evil NPC. Great for mid-level parties (3–5) who need a challenging single-monster fight.

**Combat tactics:**
- **Displacement** forces disadvantage on all attack rolls against it until it's hit — effectively raises its AC significantly
- **Avoidance** means AoE damage spells are halved even on a failed save, and negated on a success
- 10-foot tentacle reach lets it strike without provoking opportunity attacks from 5-ft melee fighters
- Hunts in packs — a pride of 2–3 displacer beasts is devastating with Displacement active on all of them
- Retreats and re-engages to reset Displacement after being hit

**Notable abilities:**
- Displacement is disrupted only until the end of its next turn — it resets automatically
- Avoidance + Displacement together make it extremely resilient for CR 3
- No special resistances or immunities — raw damage works, you just have to land the hit
- 40 ft. speed lets it chase down or flee from most PCs

**Encounter pairings:**
- A pride of 2–3 in a dark forest ambush
- As a guardian beast for a hag, drow, or fey villain
- Paired with blink dogs as rival predators in a Feywild hunt scenario
- Stalking a trade road, picking off stragglers from caravans
