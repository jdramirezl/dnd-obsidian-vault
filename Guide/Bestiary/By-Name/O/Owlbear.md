## Lore

An owlbear's screech echoes through dark valleys and benighted forests, piercing the quiet night to announce the death of its prey. Feathers cover the thick, shaggy coat of its bearlike body, and the limpid pupils of its great round eyes stare furiously from its owlish head.

**Deadly Ferocity.** The owlbear's reputation for ferocity, aggression, stubbornness, and sheer ill temper makes it one of the most feared predators of the wild. There is little, if anything, that a hungry owlbear fears. Even monsters that outmatch an owlbear in size and strength avoid tangling with it.

**Consummate Predators.** An owlbear emerges from its den around sunset and hunts into the darkest hours of the night. It makes its den in a cave or ruin littered with the bones of its prey. Owlbears hunt alone or in mated pairs.

**Savage Companions.** Although they are more intelligent than most animals, owlbears are difficult to tame. However, with enough time, food, and luck, an intelligent creature can train an owlbear to recognize it as a master. Elven communities encourage owlbears to den beneath their treetop villages. Hobgoblins favor owlbears as war beasts, and hill giants and frost giants sometimes keep owlbears as pets.

**Owlbear Origins.** Scholars have long debated the origins of the owlbear. The most common theory is that a demented wizard created the first specimen by crossing a giant owl with a bear. However, venerable elves claim to have known these creatures for thousands of years, and some fey insist that owlbears have always existed in the Feywild.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!quote] Tactical Deep Dive
> See [[Owlbear-Tactics]] for detailed combat tactics from *The Monsters Know What They're Doing*.


> [!tip] 24 Average Damage at CR 3
> Beak (10) + Claws (14) at +7 to hit is brutal for CR 3. An owlbear can down a level 3 PC in two rounds. It fights to the death and can't be reasoned with. Keen Sight and Smell means sneaking past one requires both visual and scent concealment.

- **What it is:** The iconic D&D hybrid predator — part owl, part bear, all fury. CR 3 with high Strength (+5) and solid two-attack damage output. A straightforward but dangerous beast encounter.
- **When to use:** Forests, hills, caves, ruins — any wilderness area. Classic random encounter for levels 2–4. Owlbear dens make great mini-dungeons. Also works as a tamed war beast for hobgoblins or giants.
- **Key combat tactics:** Charges in and attacks relentlessly — no special abilities, just raw damage. Beak (10) + Claws (14) = 24 average damage per round at +7 to hit. 40 ft. speed closes distance quickly. Keen Sight and Smell makes it nearly impossible to ambush or sneak past. Won't retreat — fights to the death.
- **Notable abilities:** Keen Sight and Smell gives advantage on Perception checks using sight or smell — excellent tracker and hard to surprise. +7 to hit is very high for CR 3. 20 Strength means it can grapple effectively if needed (though it has no grapple action). No languages — purely bestial.
- **Conditions inflicted:** None
- **Suggested encounter pairings:** Mated pair guarding a den with cubs (cubs as motivation not to kill). Hobgoblin war party with an owlbear war beast. Owlbear loose in a gladiatorial arena. Encountered while tracking prey through the forest at night. Nesting in ruins the party needs to explore.
