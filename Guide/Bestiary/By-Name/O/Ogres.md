# Ogres

![Ogres](https://www.aidedd.org/dnd/images/ogre.jpg)


> **Type**: Large giant | **CR**: 1 / 2 | **Source**: Monster Manual p.237–239

Ogres are as lazy of mind as they are strong of body. They live by raiding, scavenging, and killing for food and pleasure. The average adult specimen stands between 9 and 10 feet tall and weighs close to a thousand pounds.

## Lore

**Furious Tempers.** Ogres are notorious for their quick tempers, which flare at the smallest perceived offense. Insults and name-calling can rouse an ogre's wrath in an instant. When its rage is incited, an ogre lashes out in a frustrated tantrum until it runs out of objects or creatures to smash.

**Gruesome Gluttons.** Ogres eat almost anything, but they especially enjoy the taste of dwarves, halflings, and elves. When they can, they combine dinner with pleasure, chasing scurrying victims around before eating them raw.

**Greedy Collectors.** An ogre's eyes glitter with avarice when it sees the possessions of others. Smarter creatures can earn an ogre's trust by offering it gold or a weapon forged for a creature of its size.

**Legendary Stupidity.** Few ogres can count to ten, even with their fingers in front of them. Ogres believe what they are told and are easy to fool or confuse, but they break things they don't understand.

**Ogre Gangs.** Ogres sometimes band together in small, nomadic groups. They associate freely with goblinoids, orcs, and trolls, and practically worship giants. In the giants' ordning, ogres rank beneath the lowest giants in status.

**Half-Ogre (Ogrillon).** When an ogre mates with a human, hobgoblin, bugbear, or orc, the result is always a half-ogre. An adult half-ogre or ogrillon stands 8 feet tall and weighs 450 pounds on average.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!tip] Simple Stat Blocks, Dangerous in Numbers
> Ogres have zero special abilities — their threat is pure math. 13 damage per hit at +6 with 59 HP means two ogres can down a level 2 PC in one round. Use their legendary stupidity for roleplay: they're easy to trick, bribe with shiny objects, or misdirect.

- **What they are:** Classic brute monsters. Ogre (CR 2) is a straightforward melee smasher with high Strength and low everything else. Half-Ogre (CR 1) is a slightly weaker crossbreed variant.
- **When to use:** Wilderness encounters, bandit camps, goblinoid war bands, giant lairs. Ogres are the quintessential "big dumb brute" — perfect muscle-for-hire or random wilderness threat for levels 1–4.
- **Key combat tactics:**
  - *Ogre (CR 2):* One attack per round but it hits hard — 13 damage with greatclub. 40 ft. speed lets it close distance fast. Javelin at range. Low AC (11) and poor saves make it easy to control with spells. 59 HP is beefy for CR 2.
  - *Half-Ogre (CR 1):* Slightly less damage and HP. Battleaxe is versatile (12 or 14 damage). Better balanced stats than a full ogre but still not bright.
- **Notable abilities:** Neither has any special abilities — pure stat blocks. That's the point: ogres are simple, direct threats. Their danger comes from numbers and raw damage output.
- **Conditions inflicted:** None
- **Suggested encounter pairings:** Ogre with a band of goblins or orcs as muscle. Two ogres guarding a bridge or cave entrance. Half-ogres mixed into a hobgoblin warband. An ogre serving a hill giant chief. Ogre gang raiding a farmstead.
