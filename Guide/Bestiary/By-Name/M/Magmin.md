# Magmin

![Magmin](https://www.aidedd.org/dnd/images/magmin.jpg)


> **Type**: Small elemental, chaotic neutral | **CR**: 1/2 (100 XP) | **Source**: Monster Manual p.212

A grinning, mischievous magmin resembles a stumpy humanoid sculpted from a black shell of magma. Even when it isn't ablaze and radiating heat like a bonfire, small jets of flame erupt from its porous skin.

## Stat Block

**Armor Class** 14 (natural armor)
**Hit Points** 9 (2d6 + 2)
**Speed** 30 ft.

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| 7 (-2) | 15 (+2) | 12 (+1) | 8 (-1) | 11 (+0) | 10 (+0) |

**Damage Resistances** bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing from nonmagical weapons
**Damage Immunities** fire
**Senses** darkvision 60 ft., passive Perception 10
**Languages** Ignan
**Challenge** 1/2 (100 XP)

### Traits

**Death Burst.** When the magmin dies, it explodes in a burst of fire and magma. Each creature within 10 feet of it must make a DC 11 Dexterity saving throw, taking 7 (2d6) fire damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one. Flammable objects that aren't being worn or carried in that area are ignited.

**Ignited Illumination.** As a bonus action, the magmin can set itself ablaze or extinguish its flames. While ablaze, the magmin sheds bright light in a 10-foot radius and dim light for an additional 10 feet.

### Actions

**Touch.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +4 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 7 (2d6) fire damage. If the target is a creature or a flammable object, it ignites. Until a creature takes an action to douse the fire, the creature takes 3 (1d6) fire damage at the end of each of its turns.

## Lore

**Summoned Pyromaniacs.** Magmins are fire elemental spirits bound into physical forms by magic, and they appear in the Material Plane only when summoned. They view flammable objects as kindling for a grand conflagration, and only the magical control exerted by their summoners keeps them from setting everything they touch ablaze. A mob of magmins summoned inside a castle can reduce it to a burning shell within minutes.

**Fiery Destruction.** Although its flame is potent, the magmin's hard magma shell prevents it from instantly igniting everything it comes into contact with. However, like the fires inside them, magmins are capricious and unpredictable. If it has the opportunity while in service to its master, a magmin seeks out areas of great heat, such as forest fires or the bubbling magma of an active volcano.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!tip] Death Burst Chain Reactions
> Multiple magmins dying near each other can chain-react their Death Bursts (2d6 fire, 10 ft. radius each). In tight quarters, killing one can trigger a domino effect that wipes out the rest — and anything nearby. Position them in clusters for maximum chaos.

- **What it is:** A tiny fire elemental prankster — living magma that delights in burning things. CR 1/2 but dangerous in groups due to Death Burst chain reactions.
- **When to use:** Elemental-themed encounters, volcanic lairs, fire cult summoning rituals, or as minions for efreet and fire giants.
- **Key combat tactics:** Runs into melee and uses Touch to ignite targets (ongoing 1d6 fire per round until doused — costs the target an action). When killed, Death Burst deals 2d6 fire in a 10 ft. radius — multiple magmins dying near each other can chain-react. Ignited Illumination is a bonus action toggle for light.
- **Notable abilities:** Touch ignite forces targets to spend actions dousing flames instead of fighting. Death Burst punishes melee attackers and can ignite the environment.
- **Conditions inflicted:** None (but ongoing fire damage from Touch)
- **Suggested encounter pairings:** A swarm of 4–6 magmins in a volcanic cave. Summoned by a fire cult alongside an azer or fire elemental. Pair with a salamander as its minions.
