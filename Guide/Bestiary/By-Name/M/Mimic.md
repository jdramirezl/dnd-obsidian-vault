# Mimic

![Mimic](https://www.aidedd.org/dnd/images/mimic.jpg)


> **Type**: Medium monstrosity (shapechanger), neutral | **CR**: 2 (450 XP) | **Source**: Monster Manual p.220

Mimics are shapeshifting predators able to take on the form of inanimate objects to lure creatures to their doom. In dungeons, these cunning creatures most often take the form of doors and chests, having learned that such forms attract a steady stream of prey.

## Stat Block

**Armor Class** 12 (natural armor)
**Hit Points** 58 (9d8 + 18)

**Languages** —
**Challenge** 2 (450 XP)

### Traits

**Shapechanger.** The mimic can use its action to polymorph into an object or back into its true, amorphous form. Its statistics are the same in each form. Any equipment it is wearing or carrying isn't transformed. It reverts to its true form if it dies.

**Adhesive (Object Form Only).** The mimic adheres to anything that touches it. A Huge or smaller creature adhered to the mimic is also [[Grappled|grappled]] by it (escape DC 13). Ability checks made to escape this grapple have disadvantage.

**False Appearance (Object Form Only).** While the mimic remains motionless, it is indistinguishable from an ordinary object.

**Grappler.** The mimic has advantage on attack rolls against any creature [[Grappled|grappled]] by it.

### Actions

**Pseudopod.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +5 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 7 (1d8 + 3) bludgeoning damage. If the mimic is in object form, the target is subjected to its Adhesive trait.

**Bite.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +5 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 7 (1d8 + 3) piercing damage plus 4 (1d8) acid damage.

## Lore

**Imitative Predators.** Mimics can alter their outward texture to resemble wood, stone, and other basic materials, and they have evolved to assume the appearance of objects that other creatures are likely to come into contact with. A mimic in its altered form is nearly unrecognizable until potential prey blunders into its reach, whereupon the monster sprouts pseudopods and attacks. When it changes shape, a mimic excretes an adhesive that helps it seize prey and weapons that touch it.

**Cunning Hunters.** Mimics live and hunt alone, though they occasionally share their feeding grounds with other creatures. Although most mimics have only predatory intelligence, a rare few evolve greater cunning and the ability to carry on simple conversations in Common or Undercommon. Such mimics might allow safe passage through their domains or provide useful information in exchange for food.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!quote] Tactical Deep Dive
> See [[Mimic-Tactics]] for detailed combat tactics from *The Monsters Know What They're Doing*.


> [!tip] Adhesive Grapple Is Brutal
> Escape DC 13 with *disadvantage* on the check — most low-level characters need a 16+ to break free. The mimic then attacks with advantage via Grappler. Don't overuse mimics, but when you do, the first PC to touch the "treasure chest" is in serious trouble.

- **What it is:** The quintessential dungeon trap monster — a shapeshifter disguised as a treasure chest, door, or other object. CR 2 ambush predator. Every D&D player's paranoia made real.
- **When to use:** Dungeons, treasure rooms, abandoned buildings — anywhere adventurers might touch objects. Best used sparingly to maintain surprise, or frequently to breed paranoia.
- **Key combat tactics:** Waits in object form (indistinguishable from a real object). When touched, Adhesive auto-grapples (escape DC 13 with disadvantage). Then attacks with advantage (Grappler trait): Pseudopod (1d8+3) and Bite (1d8+3 + 1d8 acid). Speed 15 ft. means it can't chase — it's an ambush-only predator.
- **Notable abilities:** Adhesive grapple with disadvantage on escape checks is brutal. False Appearance is undetectable while motionless. Acid immunity means it can digest anything. Immune to [[Prone|prone]].
- **Conditions inflicted:** [[Grappled|grappled]] (Adhesive, escape DC 13 with disadvantage)
- **Suggested encounter pairings:** A lone mimic disguised as a treasure chest in a dungeon room. Multiple mimics in a "too good to be true" treasure hoard. A talking mimic that trades information for food.
