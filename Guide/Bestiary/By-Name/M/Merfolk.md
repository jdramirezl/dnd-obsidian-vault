# Merfolk

![Merfolk](https://www.aidedd.org/dnd/images/merfolk.jpg)


> **Type**: Medium humanoid (merfolk), neutral | **CR**: 1/8 (25 XP) | **Source**: Monster Manual p.218

Aquatic humanoids with the upper body of a human and the lower body of a fish, merfolk adorn their skin and scales with shell decorations. Merfolk tribes and kingdoms span the world, and their people are as varied in color, culture, and outlook as the human races of the surface.

## Stat Block

### Traits

**Amphibious.** The merfolk can breathe air and water.

### Actions

**Spear.** *Melee or Ranged Weapon Attack:* +2 to hit, reach 5 ft. or range 20/60 ft., one target. *Hit:* 3 (1d6) piercing damage, or 4 (1d8) piercing damage if used with two hands to make a melee attack.

## Lore

**Hunter-Gatherer Tribes.** Merfolk lack the materials and practical means to forge weapons beneath the waves, to write books and keep lore, or to shape stone to raise buildings and cities. As a result, most live in small hunter-gatherer tribes, each of which holds unique values and creeds. Only occasionally do merfolk unite under the rule of a single leader to face a common threat or to complete a conquest.

**Merfolk Settlements.** Merfolk build their settlements in vast undersea caverns, mazes of coral, the ruins of sunken cities, or structures they carve from the rocky seabed. They live in water shallow enough that the passage of time can be marked by the gleam and fade of sunlight through the water. In the reefs and trenches near their settlements, merfolk harvest coral and farm the seabed, shepherding schools of fish as land-based farmers tend sheep. Merfolk defend their communities with spears crafted from whatever materials they can salvage from shipwrecks, beaches, and dead undersea creatures.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!tip] NPCs, Not Enemies
> Merfolk are neutral and speak Common — they're far more useful as quest-givers, guides, or allies than combat encounters. With only 10 ft. land speed, they're helpless on shore. Use them to introduce underwater plot hooks or sahuagin conflicts.

- **What it is:** Basic aquatic humanoid — the "commoner of the sea." CR 1/8, non-hostile by default (neutral alignment). More useful as NPCs than combat encounters.
- **When to use:** Coastal and underwater adventures, shipwreck scenarios, underwater kingdom politics. As allies, guides, or quest-givers rather than enemies.
- **Key combat tactics:** Single Spear attack (+2, 1d6/1d8). Amphibious but only 10 ft. land speed — nearly helpless on land. Swim 40 ft. makes them fast in water.
- **Notable abilities:** Amphibious lets them operate on land and water. Speak Common, so they can communicate with the party.
- **Conditions inflicted:** None
- **Suggested encounter pairings:** A merfolk patrol asking for help against sahuagin or merrow. Merfolk village threatened by a sea hag or aboleth. Pair with a merfolk leader (use the priest or knight stat block with Amphibious).
