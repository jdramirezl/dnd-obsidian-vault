### Actions

**Multiattack.** The roc makes two attacks: one with its beak and one with its talons.

**Beak.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +13 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. *Hit:* 27 (4d8 + 9) piercing damage.

**Talons.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +13 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 23 (4d6 + 9) slashing damage, and the target is [[Grappled|grappled]] (escape DC 19). Until this grapple ends, the target is [[Restrained|restrained]], and the roc can't use its talons on another target.

## Lore

At first sight, a roc's silhouette looks much like any other bird of prey. As it descends, however, its unearthly size becomes terrifyingly clear. In flight, a roc's wingspan spreads two hundred feet or more. At rest, perched upon the mountain peaks that are its home, this monstrous bird rivals the oldest dragons in size.

**Sky Titans.** In the ancient days when giants battled dragons for control of the world, Annam, the father of the giant gods, created the rocs so that his worshipers might challenge the dragons' dominance of the air. When the war ended, the rocs were freed from giant domination and spread throughout the world. Cloud giants and storm giants sometimes tame these great birds, but rocs treat even giants as potential prey.

**Remote and Alone.** Rocs are solitary creatures that can live for centuries. They lair in nests made from trees, tents, broken ships, and the remains of caravans they carry off, placing these massive tangles in mountain clefts out of the reach of lesser creatures.

> [!warning] CR 11 — Grab and Drop
> Talons grapple (DC 19 escape) and restrain, then the roc flies straight up at 120 ft./round. A grappled PC who breaks free at altitude takes full falling damage. Even if the party "wins," the roc can simply fly away — 120 ft. fly speed outpaces everything.

> [!tip] The Altitude Kill
> A roc that grapples a PC and flies 200 ft. up creates a lethal dilemma: break free and take 20d6 fall damage, or stay grappled and get bitten with advantage. The party needs *feather fall*, *fly*, or ranged damage to solve this. Plan the encounter around altitude.

- **What it is:** A Gargantuan bird of prey — CR 11 with 248 HP, +13 to hit, and 50 average damage per round (27 beak + 23 talons). Talons grapple (DC 19 escape) and restrain, then the roc can fly away with its prey. 120 ft. fly speed is extraordinary.
- **When to use:** Mountain wilderness, open sky encounters, giant-themed campaigns. A roc swooping down to grab a horse, a wagon, or a party member is an unforgettable encounter. Works as a natural hazard or a set-piece battle.
- **Key combat tactics:** Dives from extreme altitude, grabs a target with talons (grapple DC 19, restrained), then flies straight up. The grappled creature takes fall damage if it escapes at altitude. Beak attack on the restrained target has advantage. 120 ft. fly speed means it can disengage to extreme range in one turn.
- **Notable abilities:** Talons grapple + restrain is the core threat — a restrained creature can't escape easily, and the roc can carry it to lethal heights. 120 ft. fly speed outpaces almost everything. 248 HP and AC 15 make it a durable aerial threat. Keen Sight for spotting prey from miles away.
- **Conditions inflicted:** [[Grappled|grappled]], [[Restrained|restrained]] (Talons, DC 19)
- **Suggested encounter pairings:** A roc attacking a caravan the party is guarding. Storm giants riding rocs as mounts. A roc nest containing treasure from devoured caravans — the party must raid it while the roc hunts. Pair with giant eagles or perytons for an aerial-themed encounter chain.
