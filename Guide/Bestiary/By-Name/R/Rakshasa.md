# Rakshasa

![Rakshasa](https://www.aidedd.org/dnd/images/rakshasa.jpg)


> **Type**: Medium fiend, lawful evil | **CR**: 13 (10,000 XP) | **Source**: Monster Manual p.258

## Stat Block

**Armor Class** 16 (natural armor)
**Hit Points** 110 (13d8 + 52)
**Speed** 40 ft.

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| 14 (+2) | 17 (+3) | 18 (+4) | 13 (+1) | 16 (+3) | 20 (+5) |

**Skills** Deception +10, Insight +8
**Damage Vulnerabilities** piercing from magic weapons wielded by good creatures
**Damage Immunities** bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing from nonmagical weapons
**Senses** darkvision 60 ft., passive Perception 13
**Languages** Common, Infernal
**Challenge** 13 (10,000 XP)

### Traits

**Limited Magic Immunity.** The rakshasa is immune to spells of 6th level or lower unless it wishes to be affected. It has advantage on saving throws against all other spells and magical effects.

**Innate Spellcasting.** The rakshasa's innate spellcasting ability is Charisma (spell save DC 18, +10 to hit with spell attacks). The rakshasa can innately cast the following spells, requiring no material components:
- At will: *detect thoughts*, *disguise self*, *mage hand*, *minor illusion*
- 3/day each: *charm person*, *detect magic*, *invisibility*, *major image*, *suggestion*
- 1/day each: *dominate person*, *fly*, *plane shift*, *true seeing*

### Actions

**Multiattack.** The rakshasa makes two claw attacks.

**Claw.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +7 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 9 (2d6 + 2) slashing damage, and the target is cursed if it is a creature. The magical curse takes effect whenever the target takes a short or long rest, filling the target's thoughts with horrible images and dreams. The cursed target gains no benefit from finishing a short or long rest. The curse lasts until it is lifted by a *remove curse* spell or similar magic.

## Lore

The rakshasa employs delicacy and misdirection in its pursuit of dominion over others. Few creatures ever see the fiend in its true form, for it can take on any guise it wants, although it prefers to masquerade as someone powerful or influential. A rakshasa's true form combines the features of a human and a tiger, with one noteworthy deformity: its palms are where the backs of the hands would be on a human.

**Evil Spirits in Mortal Flesh.** Rakshasas originated long ago in the Nine Hells, when powerful devils created a dark ritual to free their essence from their fiendish bodies in order to escape the Lower Planes. A rakshasa enters the Material Plane to feed its appetite for humanoid flesh and evil schemes.

**Evil Reborn.** For a rakshasa, death on the Material Plane means an agonizing and torturous return to the Nine Hells, where its essence remains trapped until its body reforms — a process that can take months or years. When the rakshasa is reborn, it has all the memories and knowledge of its former life, and it seeks retribution against the one who slew it. Rakshasas killed in the Nine Hells are forever destroyed.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!quote] Tactical Deep Dive
> See [[Rakshasa-Tactics]] for detailed combat tactics from *The Monsters Know What They're Doing*.


> [!warning] Limited Magic Immunity — CR 13
> The rakshasa is immune to spells of 6th level or lower and has advantage on saves against everything else. *Fireball*, *hold person*, *banishment* — all useless. Only 7th+ level spells work reliably. The specific counter is piercing damage from magic weapons wielded by good creatures (vulnerability).

> [!tip] The Claw Curse Prevents Rest
> Every claw hit curses the target — no benefit from short or long rests until *remove curse* is cast. In a campaign where the rakshasa strikes and retreats, this curse creates a ticking clock of exhaustion. The party can't just long rest and try again.

- **What it is:** A fiendish shapeshifter and master manipulator. CR 13 but its power is in its defenses: immune to spells of 6th level or lower, immune to nonmagical weapons, and vulnerable only to piercing from magic weapons wielded by good creatures. 110 HP is low for CR 13 — its survivability comes from Limited Magic Immunity.
- **When to use:** Political intrigue, long-term villain, infiltration plots. A rakshasa disguised as a noble or merchant is a campaign-defining antagonist. It manipulates from the shadows, using *[[Charm Person|charm person]]*, *[[Suggestion|suggestion]]*, and *[[Dominate Person|dominate person]]* to control key figures.
- **Key combat tactics:** Avoids direct combat — uses *[[Disguise Self|disguise self]]* and *[[Invisibility|invisibility]]* to stay hidden. If forced to fight, curses targets with its claws (no rest benefits until *[[Remove Curse|remove curse]]*). Uses *dominate person* to turn party members against each other. **Plane shift** is its escape button. Limited Magic Immunity means most casters are useless against it — only 7th+ level spells affect it.
- **Notable abilities:** Limited Magic Immunity is the headline — *[[Fireball|fireball]]*, *[[Hold Person|hold person]]*, **banishment** (all 6th or lower) simply don't work. The claw curse prevents rest, creating a ticking clock. **Detect thoughts** at will for reading minds during social encounters. Vulnerability to piercing from magic weapons wielded by good creatures is the specific counter.
- **Conditions inflicted:** Cursed (Claw — no benefit from short or long rests)
- **Suggested encounter pairings:** Disguised as a noble with [[Charmed|charmed]] guards and servants. A rakshasa that has infiltrated a temple or guild. Pair with dominated NPCs who fight the party on the rakshasa's behalf. A revenge plot where a previously slain rakshasa returns to hunt the party.
