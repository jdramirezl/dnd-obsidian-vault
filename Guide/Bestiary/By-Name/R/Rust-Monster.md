# Rust Monster

![Rust Monster](https://www.aidedd.org/dnd/images/rust-monster.jpg)


> **Type**: Medium monstrosity, unaligned | **CR**: 1/2 (100 XP) | **Source**: Monster Manual p.262–263

## Stat Block

**Armor Class** 14 (natural armor)
**Hit Points** 27 (5d8 + 5)
**Speed** 40 ft.

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| 13 (+1) | 12 (+1) | 13 (+1) | 2 (-4) | 13 (+1) | 6 (-2) |

**Senses** darkvision 60 ft., passive Perception 11
**Languages** —
**Challenge** 1/2 (100 XP)

### Traits

**Iron Scent.** The rust monster can pinpoint, by scent, the location of ferrous metal within 30 feet of it.

**Rust Metal.** Any nonmagical weapon made of metal that hits the rust monster corrodes. After dealing damage, the weapon takes a permanent and cumulative -1 penalty to damage rolls. If its penalty drops to -5, the weapon is destroyed. Nonmagical ammunition made of metal that hits the rust monster is destroyed after dealing damage.

### Actions

**Bite.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +3 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 5 (1d8 + 1) piercing damage.

**Antennae.** The rust monster corrodes a nonmagical ferrous metal object it can see within 5 feet of it. If the object isn't being worn or carried, the touch destroys a 1-foot cube of it. If the object is being worn or carried by a creature, the creature can make a DC 11 Dexterity saving throw to avoid the rust monster's touch.

If the object touched is either metal armor or a metal shield being worn or carried, it takes a permanent and cumulative -1 penalty to the AC it offers. Armor reduced to an AC of 10 or a shield that drops to a +0 bonus is destroyed. If the object touched is a held metal weapon, it rusts as described in the Rust Metal trait.

## Lore

Most dwarves would rather face a squad of orcs than confront a single rust monster. These strange, normally docile creatures corrode ferrous metals, then gobble up the rust they create. In doing so, they have ruined the armor, shields, and weapons of countless adventurers.

**Underground Scavengers.** Rust monsters roam subterranean passages in search of ferrous metals such as iron, steel, adamantine, and mithral to consume. They ignore creatures not carrying such metals, but can become aggressive toward those bearing steel weapons and armor. A rust monster can smell its food at a distance, immediately dashing toward the scent's source to corrode and consume the object.

**Subterranean Wanderers.** Rust monsters are rarely found in large numbers, preferring to hunt alone or in small groups. Their wanderings often bring them into contact with other Underdark denizens that find them harmless or unappetizing. If they are well treated and well fed, they can also become friendly companions or pets.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!quote] Tactical Deep Dive
> See [[Rust-Monster-Tactics]] for detailed combat tactics from *The Monsters Know What They're Doing*.


> [!tip] The Real Threat Is Equipment Loss
> A rust monster doesn't kill PCs — it destroys their gear. Every metal weapon that hits it degrades (-1 cumulative, destroyed at -5). Antennae corrode armor on touch (DC 11 Dex). Smart players use wooden weapons, unarmed strikes, or spells. Warn the fighter before their +1 sword is gone forever.

- **What it is:** The bane of fighters everywhere. CR 1/2 but its threat isn't damage — it's destroying equipment. Rust Metal corrodes any nonmagical metal weapon that hits it (-1 cumulative penalty, destroyed at -5). Antennae corrode armor, shields, and weapons on touch (DC 11 Dex to avoid). Iron Scent detects metal within 30 ft.
- **When to use:** Underdark tunnels, abandoned mines, anywhere the party's gear matters more than their HP. A rust monster encounter is a resource drain, not a combat challenge. The real question is: how much equipment are you willing to lose?
- **Key combat tactics:** The rust monster is docile unless it smells metal — it charges the most metal-laden character. Uses Antennae to corrode armor and shields (DC 11 Dex). Rust Metal passively degrades any metal weapon used against it. Smart parties use wooden weapons, unarmed strikes, or spells. The rust monster flees if no metal is available.
- **Notable abilities:** Rust Metal is the signature — hitting the rust monster with a metal weapon degrades it automatically. Antennae actively targets worn/carried metal (DC 11 Dex). Iron Scent pinpoints metal within 30 ft. — it will find hidden metal items. 40 ft. speed means it can chase down most characters.
- **Conditions inflicted:** None (but permanent equipment degradation is arguably worse)
- **Suggested encounter pairings:** A rust monster wandering into camp at night, attracted by the fighter's plate armor. A pack of 2–3 in a narrow mine tunnel with no room to maneuver. Pair with [[Piercer|piercers]] or [[Roper|ropers]] in an Underdark cavern. A drow patrol using rust monsters as living weapons against armored surface dwellers.
