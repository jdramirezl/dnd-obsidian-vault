### Actions

**Multiattack.** The wyvern makes two attacks: one with its bite and one with its stinger. While flying, it can use its claws in place of one other attack.

**Bite.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +7 to hit, reach 10 ft., one creature. *Hit:* 11 (2d6 + 4) piercing damage.

**Claws.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +7 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 13 (2d8 + 4) slashing damage.

**Stinger.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +7 to hit, reach 10 ft., one creature. *Hit:* 11 (2d6 + 4) piercing damage. The target must make a DC 15 Constitution saving throw, taking 24 (7d6) poison damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one.

## Lore

Travelers in the wild sometimes look to the skies to see the dark-winged shape of a wyvern carrying its prey. These cousins to the great dragons hunt the same tangled forests and caverns as their kin. Their appearance sends ripples of alarm through the borderlands of civilization.

A wyvern has two scaly legs, leathery wings, and a sinewy tail topped with its most potent weapon: a poison stinger. The poison in a wyvern's stinger can kill a creature in seconds. Extremely potent, wyvern poison burns through its victim's bloodstream, disintegrating veins and arteries on its way to the heart. As deadly as wyverns can be, however, hunters and adventurers often track them to claim the venom, which is used in alchemical compounds and to coat weapons.

**Aerial Hunters.** A wyvern doesn't fight on the ground unless it can't reach its prey by any other means, or if it has been fooled into a position from which aerial combat isn't an option. If forced into a confrontation on the ground, a wyvern crouches low, keeping its stinger poised above its head as it hisses and growls.

**Aggressive and Reckless.** A wyvern intent on its prey backs down only if it sustains serious injury, or if its prey eludes it long enough for another easier potential meal to wander along. Although they possess more cunning than ordinary beasts, wyverns lack the intelligence of their draconic cousins. Creatures that maintain their composure as a wyvern hunts them from the air can often elude or trick it. Wyverns follow a direct path to their prey, with no thought given to possible ambushes.

**Tamed Wyverns.** A wyvern can be tamed for use as a mount, but doing so presents a difficult and deadly challenge. Raising one as a hatchling offers the best results. However, a wyvern's violent temperament has cost the life of many a would-be master.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!quote] Tactical Deep Dive
> See [[Wyvern-Tactics]] for detailed combat tactics from *The Monsters Know What They're Doing*.


> [!tip] The Stinger Is the Kill Shot
> The tail stinger deals 11 piercing + 24 poison (DC 15 Con for half) — up to 35 damage in one hit. That can drop a level 5 PC outright. The wyvern always leads with the stinger. Fly 80 ft. means it dive-bombs, stings, and retreats. Grounding it (nets, *earthbind*) neutralizes its advantage.

- **What it is:** A CR 6 draconic predator with a devastating poison stinger. The stinger deals 11 piercing + 24 poison (DC 15 Con save for half) — up to 35 damage in one hit. Fly 80 ft. makes it an excellent aerial threat. 110 HP is beefy for CR 6.
- **When to use:** Wilderness encounters in mountains, forests, and borderlands. Wyverns are the "lesser dragons" — use them when a true dragon is too powerful. Great as mounts for orc warlords, hobgoblin commanders, or other villains.
- **Key combat tactics:** Dive-bombs from the air — fly 80 ft. means it can close distance fast. While flying, it gets bite + claws + stinger (three attacks). On the ground, only bite + stinger (two attacks). The stinger is the real threat: 35 max damage in one hit can drop a level 5–6 PC. Retreats if badly wounded — it's aggressive but not suicidal.
- **Notable abilities:** Stinger poison (DC 15 Con, 7d6 poison) is the encounter-defining ability. Fly 80 ft. is very fast — hard to escape. 10 ft. reach on bite and stinger means it can hit from outside most melee ranges. No intelligence (Int 5) means it can be tricked or lured.
- **Conditions inflicted:** None
- **Suggested encounter pairings:** A mated pair of wyverns defending a nest. A wyvern ridden by a hobgoblin warlord or orc war chief. Wyverns circling a mountain pass as a travel hazard. A wyvern hunting the same prey as the party — competition rather than combat.
