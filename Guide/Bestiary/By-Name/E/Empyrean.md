# Empyrean

![Empyrean](https://www.aidedd.org/dnd/images/empyrean.jpg)


> **Type**: Huge celestial (titan), chaotic good (75%) or neutral evil (25%) | **CR**: 23 (32,500 XP) | **Source**: Monster Manual p.130–131

## Stat Block

**Armor Class** 22 (natural armor)
**Hit Points** 313 (19d12 + 190)
**Speed** 50 ft., fly 50 ft., swim 50 ft.

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| 30 (+10) | 21 (+5) | 30 (+10) | 21 (+5) | 22 (+6) | 27 (+8) |

**Saving Throws** Str +17, Int +12, Wis +13, Cha +15
**Skills** Insight +13, Persuasion +15
**Damage Immunities** bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing from nonmagical weapons
**Senses** truesight 120 ft., passive Perception 16
**Languages** all
**Challenge** 23 (32,500 XP)

### Traits

**Innate Spellcasting.** The empyrean's innate spellcasting ability is Charisma (spell save DC 23, +15 to hit with spell attacks). It can innately cast the following spells, requiring no material components:
- At will: *greater restoration*, *pass without trace*, *water breathing*, *water walk*
- 1/day each: *commune*, *dispel evil and good*, *earthquake*, *fire storm*, *plane shift* (self only)

**Legendary Resistance (3/Day).** If the empyrean fails a saving throw, it can choose to succeed instead.

**Magic Resistance.** The empyrean has advantage on saving throws against spells and other magical effects.

**Magic Weapons.** The empyrean's weapon attacks are magical.

### Actions

**Maul.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +17 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. *Hit:* 31 (6d6 + 10) bludgeoning damage. If the target is a creature, it must succeed on a DC 15 Constitution saving throw or be [[Stunned|stunned]] until the end of the empyrean's next turn.

**Bolt.** *Ranged Spell Attack:* +15 to hit, range 600 ft., one target. *Hit:* 24 (7d6) damage of one of the following types (empyrean's choice): acid, cold, fire, force, lightning, radiant, or thunder.

### Legendary Actions

The empyrean can take 3 legendary actions, choosing from the options below. Only one legendary action option can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature's turn. The empyrean regains spent legendary actions at the start of its turn.

**Attack.** The empyrean makes one attack.

**Bolster.** The empyrean bolsters all nonhostile creatures within 120 feet of it until the end of its next turn. Bolstered creatures can't be [[Charmed|charmed]] or [[Frightened|frightened]], and they gain advantage on ability checks and saving throws until the end of the empyrean's next turn.

**Trembling Strike (Costs 2 Actions).** The empyrean strikes the ground with its maul, triggering an earth tremor. All other creatures on the ground within 60 feet of the empyrean must succeed on a DC 25 Strength saving throw or be knocked [[Prone|prone]].

## Lore

Empyreans are the celestial children of the gods of the Upper Planes. They are universally beautiful, statuesque, and self-assured.

**Manifest Emotion.** An empyrean can experience deity-like fits of serenity or rage. It can affect the environment around it by its mood. When unhappy, clouds might cry tears of salt water, wildflowers wilt, or dead fish wash ashore. When jubilant, sunlight follows it everywhere, small animals frolic in its footsteps, and birds fill the sky with song.

**Evil Empyreans.** A few empyreans have turned to evil after venturing to the Lower Planes and becoming corrupted, or as the result of being cursed by evil gods. An evil empyrean can't survive long on the Upper Planes and usually retreats to the Material Plane, where it can rule over a kingdom of mortals as an indomitable tyrant.

**Immortal Titans.** Empyreans don't age but can be slain. Because few empyreans can imagine their own demise, they fight fearlessly when drawn into battle, refusing to believe that the end is upon them even when standing at death's door. When an empyrean dies, its spirit returns to its home plane, where one of its parents resurrects it unless there is a good reason not to.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!quote] Tactical Deep Dive
> See [[Empyrean-Tactics]] for detailed combat tactics from *The Monsters Know What They're Doing*.


> [!warning] Deadly Encounter
> CR 23 — recommended for parties of level 18+. A demigod with Legendary Resistance (3/day), legendary actions, and innate spellcasting including *earthquake* and *plane shift*. Can be good or evil (25% chance evil).

> [!tip] DM Tip
> Bolts from the heavens deal 7d6 damage at 600 ft. range as a legendary action — the empyrean can bombard the party from extreme distance. Trembling Strike (bonus action) knocks prone in a 60-ft radius. An evil empyrean is a campaign-ending villain.

**What it is:** A CR 23 demigod — child of the gods themselves. One of the most powerful non-deity creatures in the game. Can be a benevolent ally or a tyrannical villain depending on alignment.

**When to use:** Tier 4 capstone encounters, divine quests, planar adventures. An evil empyrean makes an excellent campaign villain ruling a mortal kingdom. A good empyrean is a powerful quest-giver or ally.

**Combat tactics:**
- **Maul** at +17 deals 31 avg damage and forces DC 15 Con save or [[Stunned|stunned]] — devastating action denial
- **Bolt** at 600 ft. range with flexible damage type means it always has the right element; 24 avg damage
- **Legendary Action: Attack** means it can Maul (and stun) on other creatures' turns
- **Trembling Strike** (2 actions) — DC 25 Str save or [[Prone|prone]] in 60 ft.; nearly impossible to resist
- **Bolster** grants immunity to [[Charmed|charmed]]/[[Frightened|frightened]] + advantage on saves to all allies in 120 ft.
- Opens with *fire storm* or *earthquake* for massive AoE, then closes to melee

**Notable abilities:**
- **Legendary Resistance (3/Day)** — first three save-or-suck spells auto-fail
- **Magic Resistance** — advantage on all spell saves even after Legendary Resistance is spent
- **Immune to nonmagical weapons** — martials without magic weapons are useless
- *Greater restoration* at will — removes any condition on itself or allies every turn
- *Plane shift* (self only) — can escape any fight it's losing
- **Truesight 120 ft.** — invisibility, illusions, and shapechanging are useless

**Key weaknesses:**
- No condition immunities — [[Stunned|stunned]], [[Paralyzed|paralyzed]], etc. all work (after burning Legendary Resistance)
- DC 15 Con save on Maul stun is surprisingly low for CR 23
- No ranged multiattack — kiting at range forces it to use Bolt (lower damage)
- No lair actions — fights at the same power everywhere

**Encounter pairings:**
- Alone as a solo boss with legendary actions carrying the action economy
- With celestial servants (couatls, devas) if good-aligned
- Evil empyrean with mortal cultists and dominated champions
- As the final guardian of a divine artifact or planar gate
