# Behir

> **Type**: Huge monstrosity, neutral evil | **CR**: 11 (7,200 XP) | **Source**: Monster Manual p.25

## Stat Block

**Armor Class** 17 (natural armor)
**Hit Points** 168 (16d12 + 64)
**Speed** 50 ft., climb 40 ft.

### Actions

**Multiattack.** The behir makes two attacks: one with its bite and one to constrict.

**Bite.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +10 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. *Hit:* 22 (3d10 + 6) piercing damage.

**Constrict.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +10 to hit, reach 5 ft., one Large or smaller creature. *Hit:* 17 (2d10 + 6) bludgeoning damage plus 17 (2d10 + 6) slashing damage. The target is [[Grappled|grappled]] (escape DC 16) if the behir isn't already constricting a creature, and the target is [[Restrained|restrained]] until this grapple ends.

**Lightning Breath (Recharge 5–6).** The behir exhales a line of lightning that is 20 feet long and 5 feet wide. Each creature in that line must make a DC 16 Dexterity saving throw, taking 66 (12d10) lightning damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one.

**Swallow.** The behir makes one bite attack against a Medium or smaller target it is grappling. If the attack hits, the target is also swallowed, and the grapple ends. While swallowed, the target is [[Blinded|blinded]] and [[Restrained|restrained]], it has total cover against attacks and other effects outside the behir, and it takes 21 (6d6) acid damage at the start of each of the behir's turns. A behir can have only one creature swallowed at a time.

If the behir takes 30 damage or more on a single turn from the swallowed creature, the behir must succeed on a DC 14 Constitution saving throw at the end of that turn or regurgitate the creature, which falls [[Prone|prone]] in a space within 10 feet of the behir. If the behir dies, a swallowed creature is no longer [[Restrained|restrained]] by it and can escape from the corpse by using 15 feet of movement, exiting [[Prone|prone]].

## Lore

The serpentine behir crawls along floors and clambers up walls to reach its prey. Its lightning breath can incinerate most creatures, even as more powerful foes are constricted in its coils and eaten alive. A behir's monstrous form resembles a combination of centipede and crocodile. Its scaled hide ranges from ultramarine to deep blue in color, fading to pale blue on its underside.

**Cavern Predators.** Behirs lair in places inaccessible to other creatures, favoring locations where would-be intruders must make a harrowing climb to reach them. Deep pits, high caves in cliff walls, and caverns reached only by narrow, twisting tunnels are prime sites for a behir ambush. A behir's dozen legs allow it to scramble through its lair site with ease. When not climbing, it moves even faster by folding its legs beside its body and slithering like a snake. Behirs swallow their prey whole, after which they enter a period of dormancy while they digest. While dormant, a behir chooses a hiding place where intruders in its lair might overlook it.

**Foes of the Dragons.** In times long forgotten, giants and dragons engaged in seemingly endless war. Storm giants created the first behirs as weapons against the dragons, and behirs retain a natural hatred for dragonkind. A behir never makes its lair in an area it knows to be inhabited by a dragon. If a dragon attempts to establish a lair within a few dozen miles of a behir's lair, the behir is compelled to kill the dragon or drive it off. Only if the dragon proves too powerful to fight does a behir back down, seeking out a new lair site a great distance away.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!quote] Tactical Deep Dive
> See [[Behir-Tactics]] for detailed combat tactics from *The Monsters Know What They're Doing*.


> [!warning] Deadly Encounter
> CR 11 — recommended for parties of level 8–10. Lightning Breath deals 66 (12d10) damage and Swallow removes a PC from the fight entirely. Vertical terrain makes this fight much harder.

> [!tip] DM Tip
> Constrict → Swallow is the kill combo. Grapple a Medium PC, then Swallow next turn for 6d6 acid/round. The swallowed PC must deal 30+ damage in one turn to force regurgitation.

**What it is:** A CR 11 serpentine predator with lightning breath and a swallow attack. Think "blue dragon's rival" — created by storm giants specifically to fight dragons.

**When to use:** Mid-tier boss monster for underground or mountainous encounters. Excellent dragon substitute when you want something unexpected. Works as a territorial predator blocking a key passage.

**Combat tactics:**
- Opens with **Lightning Breath** if multiple targets are lined up — 66 (12d10) lightning damage is devastating
- Uses **Multiattack** (Bite + Constrict) to lock down the biggest melee threat. Constrict deals 34 average damage AND [[Grappled|grapples]]/[[Restrained|restrains]]
- Next turn: **Swallow** the [[Grappled|grappled]] target to remove them from the fight entirely. 21 (6d6) acid per turn inside
- 50 ft. speed + 40 ft. climb means it controls vertical terrain. It will retreat up walls to recharge Lightning Breath
- Stealth +7 means it can ambush effectively despite being Huge

**Notable abilities:**
- Lightning Breath recharges on 5–6, so it comes back frequently
- Swallow only works on Medium or smaller — Large PCs or wild-shaped druids are safe
- The swallowed creature can force regurgitation by dealing 30+ damage in one turn (DC 14 Con save)
- Lightning immune — don't pair with lightning-using allies, and *[[Lightning Bolt|lightning bolt]]* is useless against it

**Encounter pairings:**
- Solo boss in a vertical cavern with multiple ledges
- Guarding a mountain pass — the party must get past it or negotiate
- In conflict with a young dragon (the party walks into a territorial war)
- With kobold or troglodyte minions who worship it
