# Basilisk

![Basilisk](https://www.aidedd.org/dnd/images/basilisk.jpg)


> **Type**: Medium monstrosity, unaligned | **CR**: 3 (700 XP) | **Source**: Monster Manual p.24

## Stat Block

**Armor Class** 15 (natural armor)
**Hit Points** 52 (8d8 + 16)
**Speed** 20 ft.

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| 16 (+3) | 8 (−1) | 15 (+2) | 2 (−4) | 8 (−1) | 7 (−2) |

**Senses** darkvision 60 ft., passive Perception 9
**Languages** —
**Challenge** 3 (700 XP)

### Traits

**Petrifying Gaze.** If a creature starts its turn within 30 feet of the basilisk and the two of them can see each other, the basilisk can force the creature to make a DC 12 Constitution saving throw if the basilisk isn't incapacitated. On a failed save, the creature magically begins to turn to stone and is [[Restrained|restrained]]. It must repeat the saving throw at the end of its next turn. On a success, the effect ends. On a failure, the creature is [[Petrified|petrified]] until freed by the *greater restoration* spell or other magic.

A creature that isn't surprised can avert its eyes to avoid the saving throw at the start of its turn. If it does so, it can't see the basilisk until the start of its next turn, when it can avert its eyes again. If it looks at the basilisk in the meantime, it must immediately make the save.

If the basilisk sees its reflection within 30 feet of it in bright light, it mistakes itself for a rival and targets itself with its gaze.

### Actions

**Bite.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +5 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 10 (2d6 + 3) piercing damage plus 7 (2d6) poison damage.

## Lore

Travelers sometimes find objects that look like pieces of remarkably lifelike stone carvings of wildlife. Missing parts appear to have been bitten off. Seasoned explorers regard such relics as warnings, knowing that the basilisk that created them is likely to be nearby.

**Adaptable Predators.** Basilisks thrive in arid, temperate, or tropical climates. They lair in caves or other sheltered sites. Most often, basilisks are encountered underground. A basilisk born and raised in captivity can be domesticated and trained. Such a trained basilisk knows how to avoid meeting the eyes of those its master wishes to protect from its gaze, but it makes a daunting guardian beast. Because of this use, basilisk eggs are highly prized.

**Gaze of Stone.** Basilisks are ponderous for hunting creatures, but they needn't chase prey. Meeting a basilisk's supernatural gaze can be enough to affect a rapid transformation, transforming a victim into porous stone. Basilisks, with their strong jaws, are able to consume the stone. The stone returns to organic form in the basilisk's gullet.

Some alchemists are said to know how to process the basilisk's gullet and the fluids contained within. Properly handled, the gullet produces an oil that can return [[Petrified|petrified]] creatures to flesh and life. Unfortunately for such a victim, any parts lost in stone form remain absent if the creature revives. Revivification using the oil is impossible if a vital part of the petrified creature, such as its head, is detached.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!tip] DM Tip
> Players must choose each turn: look at the basilisk (risk petrification, DC 12 Con) or avert eyes (can't see it, attacks have disadvantage). A mirror in bright light turns its gaze against itself.

**What it is:** A slow, eight-legged reptile whose gaze turns creatures to stone. Classic dungeon hazard creature — unaligned, so it's a predator, not evil.

**When to use:** Caves, underground lairs, ruins with suspiciously lifelike statues. The [[Petrified|petrified]] statues are the environmental foreshadowing.

**Combat tactics:**
- The basilisk doesn't need to do anything clever — Petrifying Gaze triggers automatically at the start of any creature's turn within 30 ft. if they can see each other
- Players must choose: avert eyes (attack with disadvantage / can't target it) or risk DC 12 Con save → [[Restrained|restrained]] → [[Petrified|petrified]]
- Its bite is solid (2d6+3 piercing + 2d6 poison) but it only has 20 ft. speed, so kiting is viable
- Only 52 HP and AC 15 — it dies fast if the party commits to fighting blind

**Notable abilities:**
- **Petrifying Gaze** is a two-stage save: first fail = [[Restrained|restrained]], second fail = [[Petrified|petrified]]. This gives the party one round to react (kill it, break line of sight, use *greater restoration*)
- Mirror trick works RAW — showing it its own reflection in bright light petrifies itself
- Averting eyes means you can't see the basilisk, imposing disadvantage on attacks against it

**Encounter pairings:**
- Alone in a cave full of petrified creatures (environmental storytelling)
- With a medusa or other gaze creature for a "don't look" themed dungeon
- Guarded by a handler who is immune or knows how to avoid its gaze
- Multiple basilisks in a nest — egg retrieval quest
