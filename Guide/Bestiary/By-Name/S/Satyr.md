### Traits

**Magic Resistance.** The satyr has advantage on saving throws against spells and other magical effects.

### Actions

**Ram.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +3 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 6 (2d4 + 1) bludgeoning damage.

**Shortsword.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +5 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 6 (1d6 + 3) piercing damage.

**Shortbow.** *Ranged Weapon Attack:* +5 to hit, range 80/320 ft., one target. *Hit:* 6 (1d6 + 3) piercing damage.

### Variant: Satyr Pipes

A satyr might carry panpipes that it can play to create magical effects. Usually, only one satyr in a group carries such pipes. If a satyr has pipes, it gains the following additional action option.

**Panpipes.** The satyr plays its pipes and chooses one of the following magical effects: a charming melody, a frightening strain, or a gentle lullaby. Any creature within 60 feet of the satyr that can hear the pipes must succeed on a DC 13 Wisdom saving throw or be affected as described below. Other satyrs and creatures that can't be [[Charmed|charmed]] are unaffected. An affected creature can repeat the saving throw at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on itself on a success. If a creature's saving throw is successful or the effect ends for it, the creature is immune to these panpipes for the next 24 hours.

- **Charming Melody.** The creature is [[Charmed|charmed]] by the satyr for 1 minute. If the satyr or any of its companions harms the creature, the effect ends immediately.
- **Frightening Strain.** The creature is [[Frightened|frightened]] for 1 minute.
- **Gentle Lullaby.** The creature falls asleep and is [[Unconscious|unconscious]] for 1 minute. The effect ends if the creature takes damage or if someone takes an action to shake it awake.

## Lore

**Hedonistic Revelers.** Satyrs are raucous fey that frolic in wild forests, driven by curiosity and hedonism in equal measure. They resemble stout male humans with the furry lower bodies and cloven hooves of goats. Satyrs crave the strongest drink, the most fragrant spices, and the most dizzying dances. A satyr feels starved when it can't indulge itself, and it goes to great lengths to sate its desires. Inebriated on drink and pleasure, satyrs give no thought to the consequences of the hedonism they incite in others.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!tip] Panpipes Are the Real Threat
> The Pipes variant affects ALL creatures within 60 ft. (DC 13 Wis). Gentle Lullaby can knock out the entire party in one action. A single piper satyr behind a line of fey allies is a devastating encounter — the party must silence or reach the piper fast.

> [!example] Satyr Pipes Variant
> Only one satyr per group typically carries pipes. The piper gains three AoE options: Charming Melody (charmed), Frightening Strain (frightened), or Gentle Lullaby (unconscious). All 60 ft. range, DC 13 Wis, repeatable saves. Decide before the encounter which satyr has pipes.

- **What it is:** A fey reveler with Magic Resistance and decent combat for CR 1/2. The Pipes variant adds powerful AoE crowd-control (charm/frighten/sleep in 60 ft, DC 13 Wis).
- **When to use:** Fey forests, wild celebrations, trickster NPC encounters. A piper satyr is an excellent encounter centerpiece while other fey allies attack.
- **Key combat tactics:** Magic Resistance makes satyrs resilient against casters. Shortsword and shortbow both hit at +5 for 1d6+3. The Pipes variant is the real threat — Gentle Lullaby can knock out the whole party (DC 13 Wis, 60-ft range). Use Charming Melody to neutralize the fighter, Frightening Strain to scatter the rest.
- **Notable abilities:** Magic Resistance (advantage on all spell saves) is exceptional for CR 1/2. Panpipes affect all creatures within 60 ft — a single failed save removes a PC from combat.
- **Conditions inflicted:** [[Charmed|charmed]], [[Frightened|frightened]], [[Unconscious|unconscious]] (Panpipes variant)
- **Suggested encounter pairings:** A piper satyr with 3–4 regular satyrs. Satyrs alongside dryads or pixies for a fey court encounter. A satyr NPC luring adventurers into a wild revel.
