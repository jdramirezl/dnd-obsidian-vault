# Stirge

![Stirge](https://www.aidedd.org/dnd/images/stirge.jpg)


> **Type**: Tiny beast, unaligned | **CR**: 1/8 (25 XP) | **Source**: Monster Manual p.285

## Stat Block

**Armor Class** 14 (natural armor)
**Hit Points** 2 (1d4)
**Speed** 10 ft., fly 40 ft.

|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| 4 (-3) | 16 (+3) | 11 (+0) | 2 (-4) | 8 (-1) | 6 (-2) |

**Senses** darkvision 60 ft., passive Perception 9
**Languages** —
**Challenge** 1/8 (25 XP)

### Actions

**Blood Drain.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +5 to hit, reach 5 ft., one creature. *Hit:* 5 (1d4 + 3) piercing damage, and the stirge attaches to the target. While attached, the stirge doesn't attack. Instead, at the start of each of the stirge's turns, the target loses 5 (1d4 + 3) hit points due to blood loss.

The stirge can detach itself by spending 5 feet of its movement. It does so after it drains 10 hit points of blood from the target or the target dies. A creature, including the target, can use its action to detach the stirge.

## Lore

This horrid flying creature looks like a cross between a large bat and an oversized mosquito. Its legs end in sharp pincers, and its long, needle-like proboscis slashes the air as it seeks its next meal.

**Blood Drain.** Stirges feed on the blood of living creatures, attaching and draining them slowly. Although they pose little danger in small numbers, packs of stirges can be a formidable threat, reattaching as quickly as their weakening prey can pluck them off. A stirge attacks by landing on a victim, finding a vulnerable spot, and plunging its proboscis into the flesh while using its pincer legs to latch on. Once sated, it detaches and flies off to digest its meal.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!tip] Action Economy Tax
> Detaching a stirge costs an action — not a bonus action, not free. In a swarm of 8+, the party spends entire turns pulling stirges off instead of fighting. Meanwhile, each attached stirge drains 1d4+3 HP automatically. The real danger isn't damage — it's lost actions.

- **What it is:** A tiny blood-sucking beast. +5 to hit is absurdly high for CR 1/8. Attaches and drains 1d4+3 HP per turn automatically — no attack roll needed after the initial hit.
- **When to use:** Swarms in caves, ruins, swamps, or any dank environment. Classic low-level nuisance that becomes deadly in numbers. Great for draining party resources before a bigger fight.
- **Key combat tactics:** Flies in, attaches on a hit (+5 is very accurate), then drains automatically each turn. Only 2 HP so easy to kill — but detaching costs an action, creating an action economy tax. In a swarm of 6–10, the party spends turns pulling them off instead of fighting.
- **Notable abilities:** Blood Drain auto-damage (no save, no attack roll) after attachment. +5 to hit at CR 1/8 is the highest accuracy-to-CR ratio in the game. Detaches after draining 10 HP total.
- **Conditions inflicted:** None (but attachment is mechanically similar to [[Grappled|grappled]])
- **Suggested encounter pairings:** A swarm of 8–10 stirges in a cave. Stirges disturbed during exploration of ruins. Pair with a larger predator that uses the stirge-weakened party as prey.
