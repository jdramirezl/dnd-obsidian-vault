# Slaadi

![Slaadi](https://www.aidedd.org/dnd/images/red-slaad.jpg)


> **Type**: Tiny–Large aberration, chaotic neutral/evil | **CR**: 1/8 / 5 / 7 / 8 / 9 / 10 | **Source**: Monster Manual p.275–279

In the Ever-Changing Chaos of Limbo, bits of forest and meadow, ruined castles, and isolated islands drift through a tumult of fire, water, earth, and wind. The foremost inhabitants of this inhospitable plane are the toad-like slaadi.

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| 16 (+3) | 12 (+1) | 16 (+3) | 6 (-2) | 6 (-2) | 7 (-2) |

**Skills** Perception +1
**Damage Resistances** acid, cold, fire, lightning, thunder
**Senses** darkvision 60 ft., passive Perception 11
**Languages** Slaad, telepathy 60 ft.
**Challenge** 5 (1,800 XP)

#### Traits

**Magic Resistance.** The slaad has advantage on saving throws against spells and other magical effects.

**Regeneration.** The slaad regains 10 hit points at the start of its turn if it has at least 1 hit point.

#### Actions

**Multiattack.** The slaad makes three attacks: one with its bite and two with its claws.

**Bite.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +6 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 8 (2d4 + 3) piercing damage.

**Claw.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +6 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 7 (1d8 + 3) piercing damage. If the target is a humanoid, it must succeed on a DC 14 Constitution saving throw or be infected with a disease — a minuscule slaad egg.

A humanoid host can carry only one slaad egg to term at a time. Over three months, the egg moves to the chest cavity, gestates, and forms a slaad tadpole. In the 24-hour period before giving birth, the host starts to feel unwell, its speed is halved, and it has disadvantage on attack rolls, ability checks, and saving throws. At birth, the tadpole chews its way through vital organs and out of the host's chest in 1 round, killing the host in the process.

If the disease is cured before the tadpole's emergence, the unborn slaad is disintegrated.

## Variant: Slaad Control Gems

As a slaad emerges from the Spawning Stone, the stone magically implants a fragment of itself in the slaad's brain. This fragment takes the form of a magic gem roughly the size and shape of a human child's fist. The gem is the same color as the slaad. Another creature can use magic to draw forth a slaad's gem and use it to subjugate the slaad.

A slaad with a control gem in its brain has the following additional trait:

**Control Gem.** Implanted in the slaad's brain is a magic control gem. The slaad must obey whoever possesses the gem and is immune to being [[Charmed|charmed]] while so controlled.

Certain spells can be used to acquire the gem. If the slaad fails its saving throw against *imprisonment*, the spell can transfer the gem to the spellcaster's open hand, instead of imprisoning the slaad. A *wish* spell, if cast in the slaad's presence, can be worded to acquire the gem. A *greater restoration* spell cast on the slaad destroys the gem without harming the slaad.

Someone who is proficient in Wisdom (Medicine) can remove the gem from an [[Incapacitated|incapacitated]] slaad. Each try requires 1 minute of uninterrupted work and a successful DC 20 Wisdom (Medicine) check. Each failed attempt deals 22 (4d10) psychic damage to the slaad.

## Lore

**The Spawning Stone.** Long ago, Primus, overlord of the modrons, created a gigantic, geometrically complex stone imbued with the power of law and cast it adrift in Limbo. The chaotic energy absorbed by the stone spawned the horrors that came to be known as slaadi. The slaadi wiped out every last modron enclave in Limbo.

**Birth and Transformation.** Slaadi have horrific cycles of reproduction. Red slaadi implant eggs via their claws; blue slaadi infect with chaos phage. Each green slaad undergoes a lifelong cycle of transformation into the more powerful gray and death slaadi. With each transformation, the slaad retains its memories.

**Shapechangers.** Some slaadi can transform into the humanoid creatures from which they were originally spawned. These slaadi return to the Material Plane to sow discord in the guise of their former selves.

### Slaad Lifecycle

| Stage | Trigger |
|---|---|
| Red Slaad claw implants egg → Slaad Tadpole | Egg gestates 3 months, tadpole eats its way out |
| Tadpole → Blue Slaad (or Green if host was 3rd+ level caster) | 2d12 hours after emergence |
| Blue Slaad claw inflicts chaos phage → victim becomes Red Slaad (or Green if 3rd+ level caster) | When max HP reduced to 0 by disease |
| Green Slaad → Gray Slaad | Magical self-transformation (years/decades) |
| Gray Slaad eats death slaad corpse → Death Slaad | Instant transformation |

## DM Quick Reference

> [!warning] Death Slaad — CR 10 Apex Predator
> 170 HP, AC 18, every attack adds 2d6 necrotic, *cloudkill* + *fireball* for AoE, and *plane shift* to escape or banish a PC. Shapechanges to infiltrate, then ambushes. The death slaad commands lesser slaadi and is nearly impossible to pin down.

> [!tip] Chaos Phage and Egg Implantation Are Ticking Clocks
> Red slaad claws implant eggs (DC 14 Con) — the host births a tadpole in 3 months. Blue slaad claws inflict chaos phage (DC 15 Con) — max HP drops daily, and the victim transforms into a slaad at 0 HP. Only *wish* reverses chaos phage. Track these diseases between sessions.

- **What they are:** Chaotic toad-like aberrations from Limbo in a CR 1/8–10 progression. All share Magic Resistance, damage resistances (acid/cold/fire/lightning/thunder), and Regeneration (10 HP/turn, except tadpole). Green/Gray/Death are shapechangers with innate spellcasting.
- **When to use:** Planar incursions, Limbo-themed adventures, body horror campaigns (egg implantation/chaos phage), or disguised infiltrators (shapechangers). The lifecycle makes them excellent recurring threats.
- **Key combat tactics:**
  - *Tadpole (CR 1/8):* Nuisance. Magic Resistance is its only notable feature.
  - *Red (CR 5):* Three attacks, Regeneration 10. Claw implants eggs (DC 14 Con) — a ticking time bomb for the party. 93 HP with regen makes it durable.
  - *Blue (CR 7):* Stronger red. Claw inflicts chaos phage (DC 15 Con) — can't heal, max HP drops 3d6/day, transforms at 0. Only **wish** reverses it.
  - *Green (CR 8):* Shapechanger with *[[Fireball|fireball]]* (1/day), **fear** (2/day), *[[Invisibility|invisibility]]*. Hurl Flame for ranged. Infiltrates in humanoid form, then ambushes.
  - *Gray (CR 9):* AC 18, magical weapons, *fireball*/*[[Fly|fly]]*/*fear* (2/day each), **plane shift** escape. Greatsword + claws in melee.
  - *Death (CR 10):* The apex. 170 HP, AC 18, +9 to hit, every attack adds 2d6 necrotic. **Cloudkill** + *fireball* for AoE. *Plane shift* to escape or banish a PC.
- **Notable abilities:** Chaos phage (blue) is one of the most dangerous diseases in the game — only *wish* reverses the transformation. Red slaad egg implantation is a 3-month death sentence without *[[Lesser Restoration|lesser restoration]]* or similar. Control Gems allow subjugation of any slaad.
- **Conditions inflicted:** [[Frightened|frightened]] (*fear* — green/gray/death)
- **Suggested encounter pairings:** A death slaad commanding gray and green slaadi as an invasion force. A green slaad disguised as an NPC ally (reveal mid-campaign). Red and blue slaadi loose in a city, spreading eggs and chaos phage. A slaad control gem as a quest reward/MacGuffin.
