# Skeletons

![Skeletons](https://www.aidedd.org/dnd/images/skeleton.jpg)


> **Type**: Medium/Large undead, lawful evil | **CR**: 1/4 / 1/2 / 2 | **Source**: Monster Manual p.273–274

Skeletons arise when animated by dark magic. They heed the summons of spellcasters who call them from their stony tombs and ancient battlefields, or rise of their own accord in places saturated with death and loss.

## Lore

**Animated Dead.** Whatever sinister force awakens a skeleton infuses its bones with a dark vitality, adhering joint to joint and reassembling dismantled limbs. An animated skeleton retains no connection to its past, although resurrecting a skeleton restores it body and soul, banishing the hateful undead spirit that empowers it.

**Obedient Servants.** Skeletons raised by spell are bound to the will of their creator. They follow orders to the letter, never questioning the tasks their masters give them. Because of their literal interpretation of commands, skeletons adapt poorly to changing circumstances. A skeleton can fight with weapons and wear armor, can load and fire a catapult, scale a siege ladder, or form a shield wall.

**Habitual Behaviors.** Independent skeletons sometimes pantomime actions from their past lives. The skeleton of a miner might lift a pick and start chipping away at stone walls. The skeleton of a guard might strike up a post at a random doorway. Left alone in a ballroom, the skeletons of nobles might continue an eternally unfinished dance.

**Undead Nature.** A skeleton doesn't require air, food, drink, or sleep.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!tip] Bludgeoning Vulnerability Is the Key
> All skeletons take double damage from bludgeoning. A cleric with a mace or a monk's unarmed strikes are ideal counters. Remind players to switch weapons — a warhammer turns a CR 1/4 skeleton from a nuisance into a one-hit kill.

- **What they are:** Classic low-level undead in three forms: Skeleton (CR 1/4) is the basic ranged/melee minion, Minotaur Skeleton (CR 2) is a charging brute, Warhorse Skeleton (CR 1/2) is an undead mount. All share bludgeoning vulnerability, poison immunity, and darkvision.
- **When to use:** Crypts, tombs, necromancer lairs, battlefields — the quintessential dungeon fodder. Skeletons in large groups are a resource drain. Minotaur skeletons guard labyrinthine dungeons. Warhorse skeletons serve as mounts for skeleton cavalry.
- **Key combat tactics:**
  - *Skeleton (CR 1/4):* +4 to hit with shortsword or shortbow for 1d6+2. Dangerous in groups of 6+. Bludgeoning vulnerability means maces and warhammers are ideal counters.
  - *Minotaur Skeleton (CR 2):* Charge adds 2d8 damage and knocks [[Prone|prone]] (DC 14 Str). Greataxe hits for 2d12+4. 67 HP is substantial for CR 2. Use in corridors where it can charge.
  - *Warhorse Skeleton (CR 1/2):* 60 ft speed, +6 to hit for 2d6+4. Excellent as a mount for a skeleton rider or death knight.
- **Conditions inflicted:** [[Prone|prone]] (Minotaur Skeleton Charge)
- **Suggested encounter pairings:** 6–8 skeletons with shortbows in a tomb. A minotaur skeleton in a labyrinth. Skeleton cavalry (skeletons mounted on warhorse skeletons). Skeletons led by a necromancer or wight.
