**Languages** —
**Challenge** 1/2 (100 XP)

### Traits

**Amorphous.** The shadow can move through a space as narrow as 1 inch wide without squeezing.

**Shadow Stealth.** While in dim light or darkness, the shadow can take the Hide action as a bonus action.

**Sunlight Weakness.** While in sunlight, the shadow has disadvantage on attack rolls, ability checks, and saving throws.

### Actions

**Strength Drain.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +4 to hit, reach 5 ft., one creature. *Hit:* 9 (2d6 + 2) necrotic damage, and the target's Strength score is reduced by 1d4. The target dies if this reduces its Strength to 0. Otherwise, the reduction lasts until the target finishes a short or long rest.

If a non-evil humanoid dies from this attack, a new shadow rises from the corpse 1d4 hours later.

## Lore

**Dark Disposition.** Shadows are undead that resemble dark exaggerations of humanoid shadows. From the darkness, the shadow reaches out to feed on living creatures' vitality. They are especially drawn to creatures untainted by evil. A creature that lives a life of goodness and piety consigns its basest impulses and strongest temptations to the darkness where the shadows hunger.

**Shadow Spawn.** As a shadow drains its victim's strength and physical form, the victim's shadow darkens and begins to move of its own volition. In death, the creature's shadow breaks free, becoming a new undead shadow hungry for more life to consume.

**Undead Nature.** A shadow doesn't require air, food, drink, or sleep.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!quote] Tactical Deep Dive
> See [[Shadow-Tactics]] for detailed combat tactics from *The Monsters Know What They're Doing*.


> [!tip] Strength Drain Kills at Str 0, Not HP 0
> Shadows bypass HP entirely — each hit reduces Strength by 1d4. A wizard with 8 Str can die in 2 hits. Kills spawn new shadows, creating exponential danger. Radiant damage and sunlight are the counters. Track Str scores carefully during shadow encounters.

- **What it is:** A stealthy undead that drains Strength and spawns new shadows from its kills. Deceptively dangerous for CR 1/2 — a pack can TPK a low-level party through Strength drain alone.
- **When to use:** Dark dungeons, crypts, haunted ruins, or anywhere with dim light. Shadows are classic "the dungeon fights back" monsters. Excellent for horror encounters where the party's torches are their lifeline.
- **Key combat tactics:** Shadow Stealth lets it Hide as a bonus action in dim light — attack, then vanish. Strength Drain (2d6+2 necrotic + 1d4 Str reduction) kills at Str 0, not HP 0. Target low-Str casters and rogues. Amorphous lets it slip through walls and floors. Radiant vulnerability and Sunlight Weakness are its counters.
- **Notable abilities:** Strength Drain bypasses HP entirely — a wizard with 8 Str can die in 2 hits. Shadow Spawn creates exponential threats if kills go unchecked. Immune to 8 conditions and resistant to most damage types.
- **Conditions inflicted:** None directly, but Strength drain to 0 = death
- **Suggested encounter pairings:** 4–6 shadows in a lightless crypt. Shadows spawned by a wraith as minions. A shadow-infested tomb where every kill creates more shadows — a race against exponential danger.
