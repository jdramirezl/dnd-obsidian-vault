# Shambling Mound

![Shambling Mound](https://www.aidedd.org/dnd/images/shambling-mound.jpg)


> **Type**: Large plant, unaligned | **CR**: 5 (1,800 XP) | **Source**: Monster Manual p.271

## Stat Block

**Armor Class** 15 (natural armor)
**Hit Points** 136 (16d10 + 48)
**Speed** 20 ft., swim 20 ft.

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| 18 (+4) | 8 (-1) | 16 (+3) | 5 (-3) | 10 (+0) | 5 (-3) |

**Skills** Stealth +2
**Damage Resistances** cold, fire
**Damage Immunities** lightning
**Condition Immunities** [[Blinded|blinded]], [[Deafened|deafened]], exhaustion
**Senses** blindsight 60 ft. (blind beyond this radius), passive Perception 10
**Languages** —
**Challenge** 5 (1,800 XP)

### Traits

**Lightning Absorption.** Whenever the shambling mound is subjected to lightning damage, it takes no damage and regains a number of hit points equal to the lightning damage dealt.

### Actions

**Multiattack.** The shambling mound makes two slam attacks. If both attacks hit a Medium or smaller target, the target is [[Grappled|grappled]] (escape DC 14), and the shambling mound uses its Engulf on it.

**Slam.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +7 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 13 (2d8 + 4) bludgeoning damage.

**Engulf.** The shambling mound engulfs a Medium or smaller creature [[Grappled|grappled]] by it. The engulfed target is [[Blinded|blinded]], [[Restrained|restrained]], and unable to breathe, and it must succeed on a DC 14 Constitution saving throw at the start of each of the mound's turns or take 13 (2d8 + 4) bludgeoning damage. If the mound moves, the engulfed target moves with it. The mound can have only one creature engulfed at a time.

## Lore

**All-Consuming Devourers.** A shambling mound trudges ponderously through bleak swamps, dismal marshes, and rain forests, consuming any organic matter in its path. Only their rarity and plodding speed prevent them from overwhelming entire ecosystems.

**Unseen Hunters.** Composed of decaying leaves, vines, roots, and other natural swamp compost, shamblers can blend into their environs. They remain in place, sustaining themselves by absorbing nutrients from their surroundings as they wait for prey to come to them.

**Spawned by Lightning.** A shambling mound results from a phenomenon in which lightning or fey magic invigorates an otherwise ordinary swamp plant. As the plant is reborn, it chokes the life from plants and animals around it, mulching their corpses in a heap around its roots.

**A Resurgent Menace.** If a shambling mound faces defeat, the root-stem can feign death. If not subsequently killed, the root-stem beds down in the shambler's remains to slowly regrow its full body.

## DM Quick Reference

> [!quote] Tactical Deep Dive

> [!tip] Lightning Heals It — Warn Subtly
> Lightning Absorption means *lightning bolt*, *call lightning*, and *shocking grasp* all heal the shambling mound instead of damaging it. Describe sparks being absorbed into its mass before a player wastes a spell slot. Two slam hits on the same target auto-grapple into Engulf.

- **What it is:** A plant ambush predator that engulfs prey and is healed by lightning. 136 HP and resistances to cold/fire make it a durable CR 5 threat. Lightning Absorption is a nasty surprise for storm sorcerers.
- **When to use:** Swamps, marshes, rain forests, or Underdark fungal caverns. Perfect ambush monster — Stealth +2 with natural camouflage in swamp terrain. The "feign death" lore makes it a recurring threat.
- **Key combat tactics:** If both slam attacks hit the same Medium or smaller target, it's auto-[[Grappled|grappled]] and Engulfed in one turn. Engulf [[Blinded|blinds]], [[Restrained|restrains]], and suffocates — 2d8+4 damage per turn with no attack roll. Lightning heals it, so warn players subtly before they waste spell slots. 20 ft speed means kiting is effective.
- **Notable abilities:** Lightning Absorption turns a common damage type into healing. Engulf is devastating — [[Blinded|blinded]], [[Restrained|restrained]], suffocating, and 13 damage/turn. Only one creature at a time, but that creature is in serious trouble.
- **Conditions inflicted:** [[Grappled|grappled]] (Multiattack), [[Blinded|blinded]], [[Restrained|restrained]] (Engulf)
- **Suggested encounter pairings:** A shambling mound in a swamp with will-o'-wisps that lure prey to it. Pair with a druid who uses *call lightning* to heal it. Two shamblers blocking a narrow marsh path.
