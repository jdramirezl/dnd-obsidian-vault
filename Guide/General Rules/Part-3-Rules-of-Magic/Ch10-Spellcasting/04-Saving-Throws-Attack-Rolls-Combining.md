# Saving Throws, Attack Rolls, and Combining Magical Effects

## Original Text

### Saving Throws

Many spells specify that a target can make a saving throw to avoid some or all of a spell's effects. The spell specifies the ability that the target uses for the save and what happens on a success or failure.

The DC to resist one of your spells equals 8 + your spellcasting ability modifier + your proficiency bonus + any special modifiers.

### Attack Rolls

Some spells require the caster to make an attack roll to determine whether the spell effect hits the intended target. Your attack bonus with a spell attack equals your spellcasting ability modifier + your proficiency bonus.

Most spells that require attack rolls involve ranged attacks. Remember that you have disadvantage on a ranged attack roll if you are within 5 feet of a hostile creature that can see you and that isn't incapacitated (see chapter 9).

### Combining Magical Effects

The effects of different spells add together while the durations of those spells overlap. The effects of the same spell cast multiple times don't combine, however. Instead, the most potent effect—such as the highest bonus—from those castings applies while their durations overlap. Or the most recent effect applies if the castings are equally potent and their durations overlap.

For example, if two clerics cast bless on the same target, that character gains the spell's benefit only once; he or she doesn't get to roll two bonus dice.

---

## Condensed Version

### Saving Throws

- Target rolls a saving throw to resist some or all of a spell's effects
- Spell description specifies which **ability** is used and what happens on success/failure

**Spell Save DC** = 8 + spellcasting ability modifier + proficiency bonus + any special modifiers

- Example (Wizard, Int 16, Prof +2): DC = 8 + 3 + 2 = **13**
- The target must meet or beat this DC to succeed

### Attack Rolls

- Some spells require the caster to roll to hit

**Spell Attack Bonus** = spellcasting ability modifier + proficiency bonus

- Example (Wizard, Int 16, Prof +2): Attack bonus = 3 + 2 = **+5**
- Most spell attacks are **ranged attacks**
- **Disadvantage** on ranged spell attacks if a hostile creature is within 5 feet (and can see you, and isn't [[Incapacitated|incapacitated]])

### Saving Throws vs. Attack Rolls

| Feature | Saving Throw | Attack Roll |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| Who rolls | Target | Caster |
| Formula | DC = 8 + ability mod + prof | Bonus = ability mod + prof |
| Common for | AoE spells, debuffs | Single-target damage spells |

### Combining Magical Effects

> [!info] Rule Reminder
> **Same spell cast twice** on one target = only the strongest (or most recent) applies. **Different spells** stack normally. Two castings of *bless* on the same target = only one bonus die, not two.

- **Different spells**: Effects stack (add together) while durations overlap
- **Same spell, multiple castings**: Effects **don't** stack
  - Only the **most potent** effect applies
  - If equally potent, the **most recent** casting applies
- Example: Two clerics cast [[Bless|bless]] on the same target → only one bless applies (no double bonus dice)

**Key Concept:** Spells either force the target to make a saving throw (target rolls) or require the caster to make an attack roll (caster rolls) — never both. Same-spell effects don't stack; only the strongest or most recent applies.
