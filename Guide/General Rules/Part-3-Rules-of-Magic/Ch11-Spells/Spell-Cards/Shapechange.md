# Shapechange

*9th-level transmutation*

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Casting Time** | 1 action |
| **Range** | Self |
| **Components** | V, S, M (a jade circlet worth at least 1,500 gp, which you must place on your head before you cast the spell) |
| **Duration** | Concentration, up to 1 hour |

## Effect

You assume the form of a different creature for the duration. The new form can be of any creature with a challenge rating equal to your level or lower. The creature can't be a construct or an undead, and you must have seen the sort of creature at least once. You transform into an average example of that creature, one without any class levels or the Spellcasting trait.

Your game statistics are replaced by the statistics of the chosen creature, though you retain your alignment and Intelligence, Wisdom, and Charisma scores. You also retain all of your skill and saving throw proficiencies, in addition to gaining those of the creature. If the creature has the same proficiency as you and the bonus listed in its stat block is higher than yours, use the creature's bonus. You retain your class features, and can use them provided the new form is physically capable of doing so.

Your hit points and Hit Dice are replaced by those of the new form. When you revert to your normal form, you return to the number of hit points you had before you transformed. If you revert as a result of dropping to 0 hit points, any excess damage carries over to your normal form.

You can use your action to change into a new form during the spell's duration, following the same restrictions.
