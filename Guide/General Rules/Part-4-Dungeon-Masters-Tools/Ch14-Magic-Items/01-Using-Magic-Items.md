# Using a Magic Item

## Original Text

A magic item's description explains how the item works. Handling a magic item is enough to give a character a sense that something is extraordinary about the item. The *identify* spell is the fastest way to reveal an item's properties. Alternatively, a character can focus on one magic item during a short rest, while being in physical contact with the item. At the end of the rest, the character learns the item's properties, as well as how to use them. Potions are an exception; a little taste is enough to tell the taster what the potion does.

Some magic items require a creature to form a bond with them before their magical properties can be used. This bond is called attunement, and certain items have a prerequisite for it. If the prerequisite is a class, a creature must be a member of that class to attune to the item. If the prerequisite is to be a spellcaster, a creature qualifies if it can cast at least one spell using its traits or features, not using a magic item or the like.

Attuning to an item requires a creature to spend a short rest focused on only that item while being in physical contact with it (this can't be the same short rest used to learn the item's properties). This focus can take the form of weapon practice, meditation, or some other appropriate activity. If the short rest is interrupted, the attunement attempt fails. Otherwise, at the end of the short rest, the creature gains an intuitive understanding of how to activate any magical properties of the item, including any necessary command words.

An item can be attuned to only one creature at a time, and a creature can be attuned to no more than three magic items at a time. Any attempt to attune to a fourth item fails; the creature must end its attunement to an item first. Additionally, a creature can't attune to more than one copy of an item. For example, a creature can't attune to more than one ring of protection at a time.

A creature's attunement to an item ends if the creature no longer satisfies the prerequisites for attunement, if the item has been more than 100 feet away for at least 24 hours, if the creature dies, or if another creature attunes to the item. A creature can also voluntarily end attunement by spending another short rest focused on the item, unless the item is cursed.

## Condensed Version

### Identifying Magic Items

**Handling**: Gives a sense something is extraordinary
**identify Spell**: Fastest way to reveal properties
**Short Rest**: Focus on one item during a short rest while touching it — learn properties at end of rest
**Potions**: A small taste reveals what it does (no rest needed)

### Attunement

**What It Is**: A bond required by certain magic items before their properties can be used

**Prerequisites**:
- **Class Requirement**: Must be a member of that class
- **Spellcaster Requirement**: Must be able to cast at least one spell using traits or features (not from a magic item)

**How to Attune**:
- Spend a short rest focused on the item while in physical contact
- Cannot be the same short rest used to identify the item
- Focus can be weapon practice, meditation, or similar activity
- If the short rest is interrupted, attunement fails
- On success: gain intuitive understanding of activation and Command words

### Attunement Limits

- **One creature per item**: An item can only be attuned to one creature at a time
- **Three items per creature**: Maximum of 3 attuned items; a 4th attempt automatically fails
- **No duplicates**: Can't attune to more than one copy of the same item (e.g., two rings of protection)

### Ending Attunement

Attunement ends if:
- The creature no longer meets the prerequisites
- The item has been more than **100 feet away** for at least **24 hours**
- The creature **dies**
- **Another creature** attunes to the item
- The creature **voluntarily** spends a short rest to end it (unless the item is cursed)

**Key Concept**: Magic items can be identified via the *identify* spell or a short rest. Items requiring attunement need a separate short rest to bond with, and a creature can attune to a maximum of 3 items at a time.
