# Legendary Creatures

## Original Text

A legendary creature can do things that ordinary creatures can't. It can take special actions outside its turn, and it might exert magical influence for miles around.

If a creature assumes the form of a legendary creature, such as through a spell, it doesn't gain that form's legendary actions, lair actions, or regional effects.

### Legendary Actions

A legendary creature can take a certain number of special actions—called legendary actions—outside its turn. Only one legendary action option can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature's turn. A creature regains its spent legendary actions at the start of its turn. It can forgo using them, and it can't use them while incapacitated or otherwise unable to take actions. If surprised, it can't use them until after its first turn in the combat.

### A Legendary Creature's Lair

A legendary creature might have a section describing its lair and the special effects it can create while there, either by act of will or simply by being present. Not all legendary creatures have lairs. This section applies only to legendary creatures that spend a great deal of time in their lairs and are most likely to be encountered there.

### Lair Actions

If a legendary creature has lair actions, it can use them to harness the ambient magic in its lair. On initiative count 20 (losing all initiative ties), it can use one of its lair action options. It can't do so while incapacitated or otherwise unable to take actions. If surprised, it can't use one until after its first turn in the combat.

### Regional Effects

The mere presence of a legendary creature can have strange and wondrous effects on its environment, as noted in this section. Regional effects end abruptly or dissipate over time when the legendary creature dies.

### Grapple Rules for Monsters

Many monsters have special attacks that allow them to quickly grapple prey. When a monster hits with such an attack, it doesn't need to make an additional ability check to determine whether the grapple succeeds, unless the attack says otherwise.

A creature grappled by the monster can use its action to try to escape. To do so, it must succeed on a Strength (Athletics) or Dexterity (Acrobatics) check against the escape DC in the monster's stat block. If no escape DC is given, assume the DC is 10 + the monster's Strength (Athletics) modifier.

## Condensed Version

### Legendary Creatures

**What Makes Them Special**: Can act outside their turn and exert magical influence for miles
- **Shapechanging into** a legendary creature does **not** grant legendary actions, lair actions, or regional effects

### Legendary Actions

**How They Work**:
- Take a set number of **special actions outside the monster's turn**
- Use **only one** legendary action at a time
- Use **only at the end** of another creature's turn
- **Regain all** spent legendary actions at the **start of its turn**

**Restrictions**:
- Can choose to **forgo** using them
- **Can't use** while [[Incapacitated|incapacitated]] or unable to take actions
- **If surprised**: can't use until after its first turn in combat

### A Legendary Creature's Lair

- Some legendary creatures have a **lair** with special effects
- Effects created by **act of will** or simply by **being present**
- **Not all** legendary creatures have lairs
- Only applies to creatures that **spend significant time** in their lair

### Lair Actions

**Timing**: Initiative count **20** (losing all initiative ties)
- Use **one** lair action option per round
- Harnesses **ambient magic** in the lair
- **Can't use** while incapacitated or unable to take actions
- **If surprised**: can't use until after first turn in combat

### Regional Effects

- **Passive environmental effects** from the creature's mere presence
- Can extend for **miles** around the lair
- **End abruptly or dissipate** over time when the legendary creature **dies**

### Grapple Rules for Monsters (Sidebar)

**Monster Grapple Attacks**:
- When a monster **hits** with a grapple attack, the grapple **automatically succeeds** (no additional ability check needed, unless the attack says otherwise)

**Escaping a Monster's Grapple**:
- Use your **action** to attempt escape
- Succeed on **Strength (Athletics)** or **Dexterity (Acrobatics)** check
- DC = **escape DC in stat block**, or if none given: **10 + monster's Strength (Athletics) modifier**

**Key Concept**: Legendary creatures break the action economy by acting outside their turn (legendary actions) and controlling the battlefield through their lair (lair actions on initiative 20) and surrounding region. These features make them appropriate solo boss encounters against a full party.

*See Chapter 9 for grapple and combat rules*
