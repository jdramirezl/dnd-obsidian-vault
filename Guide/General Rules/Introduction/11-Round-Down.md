# Introduction - Round Down

## Original Text

There's one more general rule you need to know at the outset. Whenever you divide a number in the game, round down if you end up with a fraction, even if the fraction is one-half or greater.

---

## Condensed Version

**The Rounding Rule**: Whenever you divide a number in D&D, **always round down**, even if the result is 0.5 or greater.

**Examples**:
- 7 ÷ 2 = 3.5 → rounds to **3**
- 9 ÷ 2 = 4.5 → rounds to **4**
- 5 ÷ 2 = 2.5 → rounds to **2**

**When This Applies**:
- Calculating ability modifiers
- Determining half damage
- Splitting resources or effects
- Any division in the game

**Key Concept**: D&D uses consistent rounding (always down) to keep math simple and predictable. Never round up unless a rule specifically tells you to.
