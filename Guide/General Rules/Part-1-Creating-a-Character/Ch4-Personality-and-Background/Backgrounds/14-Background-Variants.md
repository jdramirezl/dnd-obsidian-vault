# Background Variants

Several backgrounds have variant forms that share the same proficiencies and characteristics but differ in theme or feature. Use the parent background's mechanics and swap in the variant's narrative and feature where noted.

---

## Spy (Criminal)

**Parent:** [[02-Criminal|Criminal]]

A spy is a Criminal variant focused on espionage rather than street crime. You are a trusted operative who gathers intelligence, infiltrates organizations, and reports to a handler. You might have been an agent for a crown, a guild, or a secret society. Use the Criminal background's proficiencies (Deception, Stealth; one gaming set, thieves' tools) and the **Criminal Contact** feature, but your contact is an intelligence handler rather than an underworld figure.

---

## Gladiator (Entertainer)

**Parent:** [[08-Entertainer|Entertainer]]

A Gladiator is an Entertainer variant whose performances are feats of combat rather than music or acting. You thrill crowds in arenas, pit fights, or staged duels. Use the Entertainer background's proficiencies (Acrobatics, Performance; disguise kit, one musical instrument—replace the instrument with an unusual weapon that doubles as your performance prop) and the **By Popular Demand** feature, flavored as arena fame rather than stage fame.

---

## Guild Merchant (Guild Artisan)

**Parent:** [[09-Guild-Artisan|Guild Artisan]]

A Guild Merchant is a Guild Artisan variant who trades goods rather than crafting them. You are a member of a merchant's guild, caravan master, or shopkeeper. Use the Guild Artisan background's proficiencies (Insight, Persuasion; one artisan's tools—replace with navigator's tools or an additional language) and the **Guild Membership** feature, with your guild focused on commerce rather than a specific craft.

---

## Knight (Noble)

**Parent:** [[04-Noble|Noble]]

A Knight is a Noble variant who has earned a title through service rather than birth. You have been granted a knighthood by a lord, a military order, or a religious institution. Use the Noble background's proficiencies (History, Persuasion; one gaming set; one language) and replace the **Position of Privilege** feature with **Retainers**: you have three retainers (commoners) loyal to you—a squire, a groom, and a servant—who perform mundane tasks but will not fight or follow you into danger.

---

## Pirate (Sailor)

**Parent:** [[12-Sailor|Sailor]]

A Pirate is a Sailor variant who spent their career in larceny on the high seas. You served under a dread pirate, indulged in plunder, and earned an unsavory reputation in many a port town. Use the Sailor background's proficiencies (Athletics, Perception; navigator's tools, vehicles (water)) and replace the **Ship's Passage** feature with **Bad Reputation**: in civilized settlements, you can get away with minor criminal offenses (refusing to pay for food, breaking down doors) since most people will not report your activity to the authorities.
