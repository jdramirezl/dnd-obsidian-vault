# 🛡️ Armor Progression Guide

> [!info] How Armor Class Works
> Your AC determines how hard you are to hit. An attacker must roll ≥ your AC on a d20 + modifiers. Every +1 AC reduces incoming hits by ~5%. See [[02-Armor-and-Shields|Armor and Shields]] for full rules.

---

## 📊 AC Breakpoints — What the Numbers Mean

| AC | Chance to Be Hit (vs +5 attack) | Tier |
|---|---|---|
| 10 | 80% | Unarmored — you're getting hit almost every time |
| 13 | 65% | Minimum viable — leather + DEX 14 |
| 15 | 55% | Decent — chain shirt + shield or scale mail + DEX 14 |
| 16 | 50% | Good — chain mail; coin-flip to be hit |
| 17 | 45% | Great — splint; half plate + DEX 14 + shield |
| 18 | 40% | Excellent — plate; the gold standard |
| 20 | 30% | Outstanding — plate + shield; hard to hit |
| 22+ | 20% or less | Legendary — magic armor + shield + spells |

> [!tip] The +2 Shield
> A shield adds +2 AC for just 10 gp and 6 lb. That's the single best AC-per-gold item in the game. If you have a free hand and proficiency, **always consider a shield**.

## 🏋️ Heavy Armor Path (STR-Based: Fighter, Paladin, Cleric)

**You ignore DEX for AC. Invest in STR instead.**

### Progression

| Level | Armor | AC | Cost | Str Req | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Chain mail | 16 | 75 gp | 13 | Starting armor for most heavy armor classes |
| 1 | Chain mail + Shield | 18 | 85 gp | 13 | Sword-and-board; excellent starting AC |
| 3–5 | Splint | 17 | 200 gp | 15 | First upgrade; +1 AC over chain mail |
| 3–5 | Splint + Shield | 19 | 210 gp | 15 | Outstanding early-game AC |
| 5–8 | Plate | 18 | 1,500 gp | 15 | The endgame mundane armor |
| 5–8 | Plate + Shield | 20 | 1,510 gp | 15 | AC 20 — the non-magical ceiling |
| 8+ | +1 Plate + Shield | 21 | DM loot | 15 | Magic armor territory |

### Key Notes
- **All heavy armor gives Stealth disadvantage** — accept it; you're a tank, not a scout
- **Str 15 required** for splint and plate, or your speed drops by 10 ft
- **Donning time:** 10 minutes to put on, 5 minutes to remove (halved with help)
- **Ring mail (AC 14, 30 gp)** is a trap — chain mail is only 45 gp more for +2 AC

> [!warning] Ring Mail Is a Trap
> Ring mail costs 30 gp for AC 14. Chain mail costs 75 gp for AC 16. That's +2 AC for 45 gp — always worth it. Never buy ring mail.

### When to Upgrade
- **Chain mail → Splint (200 gp):** As soon as you have STR 15 and the gold. Usually levels 3–5.
- **Splint → Plate (1,500 gp):** This is a major investment. Save up or hope for loot. Usually levels 5–8.
- **After Plate:** You're done with mundane armor. Look for magic armor from treasure.

## 🏃 Light Armor Path (DEX-Based: Rogue, Ranger, Bard, Monk)

**Your AC scales with DEX. Maximize DEX to 20.**

### Progression

| DEX | Leather (11+DEX) | Studded Leather (12+DEX) | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| 14 (+2) | 13 | 14 | 10 gp / 45 gp |
| 16 (+3) | 14 | 15 | — |
| 18 (+4) | 15 | 16 | — |
| 20 (+5) | 16 | 17 | — |

### Key Notes
- **Studded leather is the only light armor that matters** — 45 gp for AC 12 + DEX, no stealth penalty
- **Leather (10 gp)** is fine at level 1 if you can't afford studded; upgrade ASAP
- **Padded armor is a trap** — same AC as leather but with Stealth disadvantage
- **No shield** for most DEX builds (need free hand for bow or two-weapon fighting)
- **Max AC without magic:** 17 (studded leather + DEX 20)
- **With shield (Valor Bard, some Rangers):** 19 (studded leather + DEX 20 + shield)

### When to Upgrade
- **Leather → Studded Leather (45 gp):** Priority #1. Buy at level 1 if possible.
- **After Studded Leather:** You're done. Just increase DEX at ASI levels (4, 8, 12...).

## ⚖️ Medium Armor Path (DEX 14+: Cleric, Druid, Ranger, some Fighters)

**Cap DEX bonus at +2 (DEX 14–15). Invest other ASIs elsewhere.**

### Progression

| Level | Armor | AC (DEX 14) | Cost | Stealth | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Scale mail | 16 | 50 gp | Disadvantage | Cheap and effective; accept stealth penalty |
| 1 | Chain shirt | 15 | 50 gp | No penalty | If you need stealth |
| 1 | Hide | 14 | 10 gp | No penalty | Budget option; Druids start here |
| 3–5 | Breastplate | 16 | 400 gp | No penalty | Same AC as scale mail, no stealth penalty |
| 5–8 | Half plate | 17 | 750 gp | Disadvantage | Best medium armor AC; stealth penalty |
| — | Half plate + Shield | 19 | 760 gp | Disadvantage | Excellent AC for Clerics |

### Key Notes
- **DEX 14 is the sweet spot** — you get the full +2 bonus; going higher doesn't help AC
- **Breastplate (400 gp)** is the best medium armor for stealth — AC 16 with no penalty
- **Half plate (750 gp)** is the best medium armor for pure AC — AC 17 but stealth disadvantage
- **Scale mail = half plate lite** — AC 16 for 50 gp with stealth disadvantage; great at level 1
- **Druids:** Won't wear metal armor (flavor restriction). Hide (AC 14) → look for magical non-metal options

### Medium Armor Master Feat
- Removes stealth disadvantage from medium armor
- Increases DEX cap to +3 (need DEX 16+)
- Half plate + [[24-Medium-Armor-Master|Medium Armor Master]] + DEX 16 = AC 18 with no stealth penalty
- **Verdict:** Niche. Usually better to just wear heavy armor or invest the ASI in DEX/CON.

### When to Upgrade
- **Hide/Chain shirt → Scale mail (50 gp):** Immediately if stealth doesn't matter
- **Scale mail → Breastplate (400 gp):** When you need stealth + AC. Levels 3–5.
- **Breastplate → Half plate (750 gp):** Only if you don't care about stealth. Levels 5–8.

## 🧘 Unarmored Defense (Barbarian, Monk)

### Barbarian: 10 + DEX + CON

| DEX | CON 14 (+2) | CON 16 (+3) | CON 18 (+4) | CON 20 (+5) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 14 (+2) | 14 | 15 | 16 | 17 |
| 16 (+3) | 15 | 16 | 17 | 18 |
| 18 (+4) | 16 | 17 | 18 | 19 |
| 20 (+5) | 17 | 18 | 19 | 20 |

- **Competes with medium armor** — at DEX 14 + CON 16, you match chain shirt (AC 15)
- **Beats plate at DEX 20 + CON 20** — AC 20 unarmored, but that's two maxed stats
- **Practical advice:** Use medium armor (half plate + shield = AC 19) until your stats catch up
- **Shield works with Unarmored Defense** — Barbarian with shield + DEX 14 + CON 16 = AC 17

### Monk: 10 + DEX + WIS

| DEX | WIS 14 (+2) | WIS 16 (+3) | WIS 18 (+4) | WIS 20 (+5) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 16 (+3) | 15 | 16 | 17 | 18 |
| 18 (+4) | 16 | 17 | 18 | 19 |
| 20 (+5) | 17 | 18 | 19 | 20 |

- **No shields, no armor** — Monks need both DEX and WIS high
- **Starts weak** — AC 15 at DEX 16 + WIS 14 is below chain mail
- **Scales late** — needs two ASIs to catch up to armored characters
- **Practical advice:** Accept lower AC early; use Patient Defense (Dodge as bonus action) to compensate

## 📋 Quick Reference: Best Starting Armor by Class

| Class | Best Starting Armor | AC | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| Barbarian | No armor (Unarmored Defense) or Shield | 14–17 | 0–10 gp |
| Bard | Studded Leather | 14 | 45 gp |
| Cleric | Chain mail + Shield or Scale mail + Shield | 18 or 18 | 85 gp or 60 gp |
| Druid | Hide + Shield (no metal) | 16 | 20 gp |
| Fighter | Chain mail + Shield | 18 | 85 gp |
| Monk | None (Unarmored Defense) | 13–16 | 0 gp |
| Paladin | Chain mail + Shield | 18 | 85 gp |
| Ranger | Scale mail or Studded Leather | 16 or 14 | 50 gp or 45 gp |
| Rogue | Studded Leather | 14 | 45 gp |
| Sorcerer | None (Mage Armor = AC 13 + DEX) | 13–16 | 0 gp |
| Warlock | Studded Leather | 14 | 45 gp |
| Wizard | None (Mage Armor = AC 13 + DEX) | 13–16 | 0 gp |

> [!tip] Mage Armor
> Wizards and Sorcerers with the *Mage Armor* spell get AC 13 + DEX for 8 hours (no concentration). With DEX 16, that's AC 16 — better than chain mail. It costs a 1st-level spell slot, so cast it before combat.
