# ⚔️ Weapon Comparison Cheat Sheet

> [!info] Quick Reference
> All weapon stats from the [[03-Weapons|Weapons Table]]. This guide tells you **which weapon to pick and why** based on your class and build.

## 📊 Damage Tier List

### Two-Handed Weapons (Highest Damage)

| Weapon | Damage | Avg | Properties | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Greatsword | 2d6 slashing | 7 | Heavy | Highest average damage; best with [[12-Great-Weapon-Master\|GWM]] |
| Maul | 2d6 bludgeoning | 7 | Heavy | Same as greatsword but bludgeoning |
| Greataxe | 1d12 slashing | 6.5 | Heavy | Better crits (12 max vs 6+6); Barbarian Brutal Critical |
| Glaive/Halberd | 1d10 slashing | 5.5 | Heavy, reach | 10-ft reach; best with [[29-Polearm-Master\|Polearm Master]] |
| Pike | 1d10 piercing | 5.5 | Heavy, reach | Same as glaive but piercing; 18 lb. (heavy!) |

> [!tip] Greatsword vs Greataxe
> **Greatsword (2d6):** Average 7, more consistent damage, lower variance. Better for most characters.
> **Greataxe (1d12):** Average 6.5, but crits roll the full d12 again. Better for **Barbarians** with Brutal Critical (extra crit dice at levels 9/13/17) and **Half-Orcs** (Savage Attacks = extra crit die).

| Class | Best Weapon | Why |
|---|---|---|

## 🔑 Key Weapon Properties Explained

### Finesse — The DEX Warrior's Best Friend
Use STR **or** DEX for attack and damage. Critical for:
- **Rogues** — Sneak Attack requires finesse or ranged
- **DEX Fighters/Rangers** — use DEX for both AC (light armor) and damage
- **Best finesse weapons:** Rapier (1d8), Shortsword (1d6, light), Dagger (1d4, light, thrown)

### Versatile — Flexibility Without a Feat
Use one-handed (with shield) or two-handed (more damage):
- Longsword: 1d8 / 1d10
- Quarterstaff: 1d6 / 1d8
- Spear: 1d6 / 1d8
- **When to use:** You want the option to switch between shield+weapon and two-handed damage mid-combat

### Reach — Control the Battlefield
+5 ft to your attack range (10 ft total). Opportunity attacks at 10 ft too.
- **Glaive, Halberd, Pike:** 1d10, heavy, reach — the polearm trio
- **Whip:** 1d4, finesse, reach — only finesse reach weapon; niche but unique
- **With [[29-Polearm-Master\|Polearm Master]]:** OA when enemies enter your 10-ft reach = they never reach you

### Heavy — Small Race Warning
Small creatures (Halflings, Gnomes) have **disadvantage** on attacks with heavy weapons.
- **Affected:** Greatsword, Greataxe, Maul, Glaive, Halberd, Pike, Heavy Crossbow, Longbow
- **Small race alternatives:** Rapier (1d8 finesse), Longsword (1d8 versatile), Shortbow (1d6)

### Light — Two-Weapon Fighting
Both weapons must be light to dual-wield (without the [[07-Dual-Wielder|Dual Wielder]] feat).
- **Best combo:** Shortsword + Shortsword (1d6 + 1d6) or Scimitar + Scimitar
- **Bonus action attack** uses no ability modifier to damage (unless you have the Two-Weapon Fighting style)
- **Rogues:** Dual-wield for two chances to land Sneak Attack per turn

### Loading — The Crossbow Tax
Only 1 shot per action/bonus action/reaction, **regardless of Extra Attack**.
- **Problem:** A Fighter with Extra Attack can only fire a crossbow once per turn
- **Solution:** [[05-Crossbow-Expert\|Crossbow Expert]] feat ignores loading
- **Doesn't affect:** Longbow, Shortbow (they use Ammunition, not Loading)

## 📈 Damage Per Round (DPR) Comparisons

Assuming +3 ability modifier, level 5 (Extra Attack), 65% hit chance:

| Build | Weapon | Attacks | Avg DPR |
|---|---|---|---|
| Greatsword + GWM | 2d6+13 × 2 | 2 (40% hit) | ~16 |
| Greatsword (no GWM) | 2d6+3 × 2 | 2 (65% hit) | ~13.7 |
| Longsword + Shield | 1d8+3 × 2 | 2 (65% hit) | ~9.8 |
| Longbow + Sharpshooter | 1d8+13 × 2 | 2 (40% hit) | ~14 |
| Longbow + Archery + SS | 1d8+13 × 2 | 2 (50% hit) | ~17.5 |
| Hand Crossbow + CBE + SS | 1d6+13 × 3 | 3 (50% hit) | ~24.8 |
| Rapier (Rogue, SA 3d6) | 1d8+3+3d6 | 1 (65% hit) | ~13.3 |
| Polearm + PAM | 1d10+3 ×2 + 1d4+3 | 3 (65% hit) | ~16.6 |

> [!tip] Key Takeaway
> **Hand Crossbow + Crossbow Expert + Sharpshooter** is the highest sustained ranged DPR build in the game. **Greatsword + GWM** is the melee equivalent. Both trade accuracy for damage — use them when you have advantage (Reckless Attack, Faerie Fire, etc.).
