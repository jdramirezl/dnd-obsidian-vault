## Original Text

**Linguist**

You have studied languages and codes, gaining the following benefits:

- Increase your Intelligence score by 1, to a maximum of 20.
- You learn three languages of your choice.
- You can ably create written ciphers. Others can't decipher a code you create unless you teach them, they succeed on an Intelligence check (DC equal to your Intelligence score + your proficiency bonus), or they use magic to decipher it.

---

## Condensed Version

**Key Concept:** Learn three languages, create unbreakable ciphers, and gain +1 INT.

- **+1 Intelligence** (max 20)
- **Learn 3 languages** of your choice
- **Create written ciphers** — DC to crack = **Intelligence score + proficiency bonus** (or magic)

> [!tip] DM Note
> A niche utility feat best suited for intrigue or espionage campaigns. The cipher feature is unique and can enable secure party communication, dead drops, and spy networks. The DC scales well — an INT 20 character with +6 proficiency sets DC 26, which is nearly impossible without magic. Three languages is generous but rarely worth a feat on its own. Best as a half-feat to round odd INT on a Wizard or Artificer in a roleplay-heavy game.
