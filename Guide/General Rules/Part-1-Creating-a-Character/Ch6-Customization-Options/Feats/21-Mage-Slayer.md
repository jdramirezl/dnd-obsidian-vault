## Original Text

**Mage Slayer**

You have practiced techniques useful in melee combat against spellcasters, gaining the following benefits:

- When a creature within 5 feet of you casts a spell, you can use your reaction to make a melee weapon attack against that creature.
- When you damage a creature that is concentrating on a spell, that creature has disadvantage on the saving throw it makes to maintain its concentration.
- You have advantage on saving throws against spells cast by creatures within 5 feet of you.

---

## Condensed Version

**Key Concept:** Anti-caster melee specialist — reaction attack when adjacent enemies cast, impose disadvantage on concentration saves, and advantage on saves vs. adjacent spellcasters.

- **Reaction attack:** When a creature **within 5 ft** casts a spell, make a **melee weapon attack** as a reaction
- **Concentration breaker:** When you damage a concentrating creature, it has **disadvantage on the concentration save**
- **Spell resistance:** **Advantage on saving throws** against spells cast by creatures **within 5 ft** of you

> [!tip] DM Note
> Highly situational but devastating against enemy spellcasters. The reaction attack triggers *after* the spell is cast (not before), so it doesn't prevent the spell — but it can break concentration immediately. The disadvantage on concentration saves stacks brutally with multiple hits (e.g., Extra Attack). The 5-foot range limitation is significant — most casters try to stay at range. Best on mobile melee characters ([[01-Monk-Overview|Monks]], [[01-Rogue-Overview|Rogues]], [[01-Paladin-Overview|Paladins]]) who can close distance reliably. Consider how often your campaign features enemy casters before recommending.
