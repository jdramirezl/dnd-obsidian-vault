## Original Text

**Healer**

You are an able physician, allowing you to mend wounds quickly and get your allies back in the fight. You gain the following benefits:

- When you use a healer's kit to stabilize a dying creature, that creature also regains 1 hit point.
- As an action, you can spend one use of a healer's kit to tend to a creature and restore 1d6 + 4 hit points to it, plus additional hit points equal to the creature's maximum number of Hit Dice. The creature can't regain hit points from this feat again until it finishes a short or long rest.

---

## Condensed Version

**Key Concept:** Turn a cheap healer's kit into a powerful non-magical healing tool — stabilize to 1 HP and provide scaling burst healing.

- **Stabilize + 1 HP:** When you use a healer's kit to stabilize a dying creature, it also **regains 1 hit point** (gets them conscious)
- **Heal (action):** Spend one use of a healer's kit to restore **1d6 + 4 + creature's max Hit Dice** in HP. Once per creature per **short or long rest**

> [!tip] DM Note
> Exceptional for non-caster parties or as a supplement to limited healing slots. The stabilize-to-1-HP feature is essentially a free [[Healing Word|*healing word*]] without a spell slot. The scaling healing (via max Hit Dice) keeps it relevant at higher levels — a level 10 Fighter gets 1d6 + 14 HP. A healer's kit has 10 uses and costs 5 gp, making this incredibly cost-efficient. Pairs well with Thief Rogue's Fast Hands (use as a bonus action).
