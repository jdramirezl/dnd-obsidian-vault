## Original Text

**War Caster**

*Prerequisite: The ability to cast at least one spell*

You have practiced casting spells in the midst of combat, learning techniques that grant you the following benefits:

- You have advantage on Constitution saving throws that you make to maintain your concentration on a spell when you take damage.
- You can perform the somatic components of spells even when you have weapons or a shield in one or both hands.
- When a hostile creature's movement provokes an opportunity attack from you, you can use your reaction to cast a spell at the creature, rather than making a melee attack. The spell must have a casting time of 1 action and must target only that creature.

---

## Condensed Version

**Key Concept:** The essential feat for front-line casters — maintain concentration, cast with full hands, and use spells as opportunity attacks.

- **Prerequisite:** Ability to cast at least one spell
- **Advantage on concentration saves** when you take damage
- **Somatic components with hands full** — cast while holding weapons and/or a shield
- **Cast a spell as an opportunity attack** — must be 1 action casting time and target only that creature

> [!tip] DM Note
> One of the best feats for any concentration caster in melee. The OA spell can be devastating — *booming blade* is the classic choice (target takes extra damage if they keep moving). The somatic component benefit solves the common "sword and shield" casting problem for [[01-Cleric-Overview|clerics]] and [[01-Paladin-Overview|paladins]]. Competes with [[30-Resilient|Resilient]] (Constitution) at higher levels where the flat proficiency bonus may outperform advantage on CON saves.
