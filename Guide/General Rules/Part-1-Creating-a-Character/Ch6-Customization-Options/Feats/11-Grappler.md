## Original Text

**Grappler**

*Prerequisite: Strength 13 or higher*

You've developed the skills necessary to hold your own in close-quarters grappling. You gain the following benefits:

- You have advantage on attack rolls against a creature you are grappling.
- You can use your action to try to pin a creature grappled by you. To do so, make another grapple check. If you succeed, you and the creature are both restrained until the grapple ends.
- Creatures that are one size larger than you don't automatically succeed on checks to escape your grapple.

---

## Condensed Version

**Key Concept:** Enhances the grapple mechanic — advantage on attacks against grappled targets and the ability to pin (restrain) them at the cost of restraining yourself.

- **Prerequisite:** STR 13+
- **Advantage on attack rolls** against creatures you are grappling
- **Pin action:** Make a grapple check to [[Restrained|**restrain**]] both you and the grappled creature (until grapple ends)
- **Size override:** Creatures one size larger don't auto-succeed on escape checks

> [!tip] DM Note
> The pin feature restrains *both* combatants, giving enemies advantage on attacks against the grappler too — a significant downside. Generally considered one of the weaker feats. Shoving a grappled creature prone (no feat required) achieves a similar result (advantage on melee attacks) without the self-penalty. Best for tanky characters who can absorb the incoming hits.
