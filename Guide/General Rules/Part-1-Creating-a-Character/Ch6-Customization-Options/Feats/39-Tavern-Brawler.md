## Original Text

**Tavern Brawler**

Accustomed to rough-and-tumble fighting using whatever is at hand, you gain the following benefits:

- Increase your Strength or Constitution score by 1, to a maximum of 20.
- You are proficient with improvised weapons.
- Your unarmed strike uses a d4 for damage.
- When you hit a creature with an unarmed strike or an improvised weapon on your turn, you can use a bonus action to attempt to grapple the target.

---

## Condensed Version

**Key Concept:** A brawling half-feat — boost STR or CON, fight with anything, and grapple as a bonus action after hitting.

- **+1 Strength or Constitution** (max 20)
- **Proficient with improvised weapons** (chairs, bottles, etc.)
- **Unarmed strike deals 1d4** damage (up from the normal 1 + STR mod)
- **Bonus action grapple** after hitting with an unarmed strike or improvised weapon on your turn

> [!tip] DM Note
> A fun flavour feat that enables grapple-focused builds. The bonus action grapple is the real value — normally grappling replaces an attack, but here it's a bonus action on top of your attacks. Pairs well with the [[11-Grappler|Grappler]] feat or a [[01-Barbarian-Overview|barbarian]]'s Rage (advantage on STR checks). The d4 unarmed damage is largely irrelevant for monks, who already have Martial Arts dice.
