# ⚠️ Feat Traps — Feats That Look Good But Aren't

> [!info] Why This Matters
> Feats replace Ability Score Improvements. A bad feat means you gave up +2 to your main stat (or +1 to two stats) for something that barely helps. These feats aren't *useless* — they're just almost always outclassed by better options.

---

## 🔴 Avoid These (Almost Always Bad)

### [[32-Savage-Attacker|Savage Attacker]]
**What it does:** Once per turn, reroll melee weapon damage dice and keep the better result.

**Why it's a trap:**
- Average damage increase on a d8 weapon: **+0.58 damage per turn**
- On a greatsword (2d6): ~+1.2 damage per turn
- Compare to [[12-Great-Weapon-Master|Great Weapon Master]]: +10 damage per hit
- **Verdict:** The worst damage feat in the game. A +2 STR ASI gives +1 damage per hit on *every* attack, plus +1 to hit. Savage Attacker only affects one attack per turn.

### [[11-Grappler|Grappler]]
**What it does:** Advantage on attacks vs grappled targets. Pin action to restrain both you and the target.

**Why it's a trap:**
- **Shoving prone is free** and gives advantage on melee attacks vs the target (no feat needed)
- The pin **restrains you too** — enemies get advantage on attacks against you
- Grapple + shove prone (no feat) = advantage for you, disadvantage for them. Strictly better.
- **Verdict:** The feat makes grappling *worse* by encouraging the pin, which hurts you. Just grapple + shove prone instead.

### [[42-Weapon-Master|Weapon Master]]
**What it does:** +1 STR/DEX, proficiency with 4 weapons.

**Why it's a trap:**
- Most martial classes already have all weapon proficiencies
- A 1-level multiclass dip gives more proficiencies + other features
- Racial traits (Elf, Dwarf) often grant the specific weapons you'd want
- **Verdict:** Only relevant for a Wizard who desperately wants a longsword. Even then, a multiclass dip is better.

### [[04-Charger|Charger]]
**What it does:** Dash action → bonus action melee attack (+5 damage if you moved 10 ft straight).

**Why it's a trap:**
- **Requires using your action to Dash** — you give up your Attack action
- A Fighter with Extra Attack loses 2+ attacks to gain 1 attack with +5 damage
- Net damage loss at level 5+: you deal *less* damage than just attacking normally
- **Verdict:** Mathematically worse than just attacking for any class with Extra Attack. Even for single-attack classes, the positioning requirement makes it unreliable.

## 🟡 Situational (Not Bad, But Niche)

### [[09-Durable|Durable]]
**What it does:** +1 CON, minimum Hit Die healing = 2 × CON modifier.

**Why it's often a trap:**
- Only matters during short rests when rolling Hit Dice
- With CON 16 (+3), minimum healing per HD = 6. Average d8 HD = 4.5, so you gain ~1.5 per die
- If your party doesn't take short rests, this feat does literally nothing beyond +1 CON
- **Better alternative:** [[40-Tough|Tough]] gives +2 HP/level unconditionally

### [[17-Keen-Mind|Keen Mind]]
**What it does:** +1 INT, always know north/time, recall 1 month of details.

**Why it's often a trap:**
- The recall ability replaces... taking notes. Which you can do for free.
- Knowing north and time is rarely mechanically relevant
- +1 INT is the only mechanical benefit, and INT is the least useful ability score for most classes
- **Better alternative:** Just take notes at the table. Or take [[28-Observant|Observant]] for +5 passive Perception/Investigation.

### [[19-Linguist|Linguist]]
**What it does:** +1 INT, 3 languages, create written ciphers.

**Why it's often a trap:**
- Languages are rarely a bottleneck — Comprehend Languages is a 1st-level ritual spell
- Ciphers are extremely niche
- +1 INT is weak for most builds
- **Better alternative:** [[31-Ritual-Caster|Ritual Caster]] gives you Comprehend Languages + many other spells

### [[18-Lightly-Armored|Lightly Armored]] / [[26-Moderately-Armored|Moderately Armored]] / [[14-Heavily-Armored|Heavily Armored]]
**What they do:** Grant armor proficiency + half ASI.

**Why they're often traps:**
- A 1-level multiclass dip (Fighter, Cleric) gives the same proficiency + more features
- Lightly Armored is almost never needed — Mage Armor exists
- **Exception:** Moderately Armored on a Warlock (medium armor + shield = huge AC boost without multiclassing) and Heavily Armored on a Cleric are legitimate picks

### [[24-Medium-Armor-Master|Medium Armor Master]]
**What it does:** No stealth disadvantage in medium armor, +3 DEX cap (instead of +2).

**Why it's often a trap:**
- Requires DEX 16+ to benefit from the +3 cap
- If you have DEX 16+, you could just wear studded leather (AC 15) instead of half plate (AC 18 with this feat)
- Wait — half plate + this feat + DEX 16 = AC 18. Studded leather + DEX 20 = AC 17. So it's +1 AC for a feat AND requiring DEX 16.
- **Verdict:** Very narrow use case. Usually better to invest in heavy armor or just pump DEX.

### [[10-Elemental-Adept|Elemental Adept]]
**What it does:** Ignore resistance to one element, treat 1s as 2s on damage dice.

**Why it's often a trap:**
- Does NOT overcome immunity, only resistance
- The 1→2 reroll averages about +0.5 damage per die
- Only matters if you fight resistant enemies frequently
- **When it's good:** Fire Draconic Sorcerer or Evocation Wizard who casts Fireball every fight. Otherwise, just cast a different damage type.

## 📊 The "Just Take an ASI Instead" Test

Before taking any feat, ask: **"Would +2 to my main ability score be better?"**

A +2 to your primary stat gives:
- +1 to hit on every attack
- +1 damage on every hit
- +1 to save DCs (casters)
- +1 to related skill checks
- Possibly +1 AC (DEX builds)

If the feat doesn't clearly outperform that, take the ASI. The feats that pass this test are the ones in the [[QoL-05-Top-Feats-by-Role|Top 10 Feats by Role]] guide.

> [!tip] Rule of Thumb
> **Max your primary ability score to 20 first**, then take feats. The exceptions are feats so powerful they're worth taking at level 4: [[12-Great-Weapon-Master|GWM]], [[34-Sharpshooter|Sharpshooter]], [[29-Polearm-Master|PAM]], [[33-Sentinel|Sentinel]], [[41-War-Caster|War Caster]], [[20-Lucky|Lucky]], and [[05-Crossbow-Expert|Crossbow Expert]].
