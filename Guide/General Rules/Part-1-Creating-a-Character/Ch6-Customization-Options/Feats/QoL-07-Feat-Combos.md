# 🧬 Feat + Race/Class Combos — Powerful Synergies

> [!info] How to Read This Guide
> Each combo lists the feat(s), the race/class it works best with, and a concrete example of why it's strong. Combos are ranked by power level: 🔥 = meta-defining, ⚡ = very strong, ✨ = strong niche.

---

## 🔥 Meta-Defining Combos

### Polearm Master + Sentinel
**Feats:** [[29-Polearm-Master|Polearm Master]] + [[33-Sentinel|Sentinel]]
**Best On:** Fighter, Paladin, Barbarian (any STR melee with reach weapon)

**How it works:**
1. Wield a glaive or halberd (10-ft reach)
2. Polearm Master: enemies provoke OA when they **enter** your 10-ft reach
3. Sentinel: OA hit reduces their speed to **0**
4. Result: enemies stop 10 ft away and **can never reach you**

**Example:** A level 8 Fighter with a halberd. An ogre charges at you. At 10 ft away, you get an OA → hit → ogre's speed drops to 0. The ogre is stuck 10 ft away, can't attack you, and you attack it normally on your turn. Repeat forever.

**Math:** At level 8 with 20 STR and GWM, your OA deals 1d10+15 damage. You get this for free every round on top of your normal attacks.

### Great Weapon Master + Barbarian Reckless Attack
**Feat:** [[12-Great-Weapon-Master|Great Weapon Master]]
**Best On:** Barbarian (any subclass)

**How it works:**
1. GWM: −5 to hit / +10 damage
2. Reckless Attack: advantage on all STR melee attacks (enemies get advantage on you too)
3. Advantage offsets the −5 penalty, making the +10 damage nearly free
4. Rage damage stacks on top

**Example:** Level 5 Barbarian, 16 STR, greatsword, raging:
- Normal attack: +6 to hit, 2d6+5 damage (avg 12) × 2 = 24 DPR
- With GWM + Reckless: +1 to hit with advantage (~75% hit rate), 2d6+15 damage (avg 22) × 2 = **33 DPR**
- That's a **37% damage increase** with minimal accuracy loss thanks to advantage

### Sharpshooter + Crossbow Expert
**Feats:** [[34-Sharpshooter|Sharpshooter]] + [[05-Crossbow-Expert|Crossbow Expert]]
**Best On:** Fighter (Battle Master, Samurai), Ranger

**How it works:**
1. Hand crossbow + Crossbow Expert = 3 attacks/turn at level 5 (2 from Extra Attack + 1 bonus action)
2. Sharpshooter: −5/+10 on each attack
3. Archery fighting style: +2 to hit offsets the −5 penalty
4. Ignore cover and long-range disadvantage

**Example:** Level 5 Fighter, 18 DEX, Archery style, hand crossbow:
- 3 attacks at +5 (+4 DEX +3 prof +2 Archery −5 SS +1 from 18 DEX)
- Each hit: 1d6+14 damage (avg 17.5)
- At 50% hit rate: 3 × 17.5 × 0.5 = **26.3 DPR** — highest sustained ranged damage in the game

### Lucky + Halfling (Halfling Luck)
**Feat:** [[20-Lucky|Lucky]]
**Best On:** Halfling (any class)

**How it works:**
1. Halfling Luck: reroll natural 1s on attack rolls, ability checks, and saving throws
2. Lucky feat: 3 luck points/day to roll an extra d20 and choose which to use
3. Combined: you almost never critically fail anything
4. Lucky can also force attackers to use your d20 roll — pick the lower one

**Example:** You roll a natural 1 on a death saving throw. Halfling Luck lets you reroll. You get a 3 — still failing. Spend a luck point, roll a 17. You choose the 17. You went from dead to stable in one roll. Lucky + Halfling Luck = the luckiest character possible.

## ⚡ Very Strong Combos

### War Caster + Any Concentration Caster
**Feat:** [[41-War-Caster|War Caster]]
**Best On:** Cleric, Paladin, Druid, Bladesinger Wizard, Hexblade Warlock

**How it works:**
1. Advantage on concentration saves (CON saves when you take damage)
2. Cast with hands full — sword + shield Clerics and Paladins can cast freely
3. Cast a spell as an OA — Booming Blade is the classic (extra 2d8 if they keep moving)

**Example:** A Cleric with Spirit Guardians (3d8 radiant/round to nearby enemies) takes 15 damage. CON save DC = max(10, 15/2) = 10. With War Caster advantage, you have a ~91% chance to maintain concentration vs a ~65% chance without. Spirit Guardians staying up for one extra round = 3d8 damage to multiple enemies = massive value.

### Polearm Master + Great Weapon Master
**Feats:** [[29-Polearm-Master|Polearm Master]] + [[12-Great-Weapon-Master|Great Weapon Master]]
**Best On:** Fighter, Paladin, Barbarian

**How it works:**
1. PAM gives a bonus action d4 attack with the butt end
2. GWM's +10 damage applies to the bonus action attack too
3. PAM's OA on entering reach also gets +10 damage
4. Three attacks per round at level 5, all with +10 damage potential

**Example:** Level 8 Fighter with halberd, 20 STR:
- Attack 1: 1d10+15 (GWM)
- Attack 2: 1d10+15 (GWM)
- Bonus action: 1d4+15 (PAM + GWM)
- Potential OA: 1d10+15 (PAM trigger + GWM)
- That's up to **4 attacks per round**, each dealing 16–25 damage

### Sentinel + Polearm Master + Paladin
**Feats:** [[33-Sentinel|Sentinel]] + [[29-Polearm-Master|Polearm Master]]
**Best On:** Paladin (any Oath)

**How it works:**
1. PAM + Sentinel lockdown combo (enemies can't reach you)
2. Every OA hit is a chance to **Divine Smite** — 2d8+ radiant damage
3. Paladins have the burst damage to make every OA devastating
4. Aura of Protection (level 6) makes you and nearby allies harder to disable

**Example:** An enemy triggers your PAM opportunity attack. You hit with your halberd (1d10+5) and smite (2d8 radiant) = avg 19 damage. Their speed drops to 0. They can't reach your party's Wizard standing behind you. You just dealt 19 damage AND protected an ally, using only your reaction.

---

### Mobile + Monk
**Feat:** [[25-Mobile|Mobile]]
**Best On:** Monk (any tradition)

**How it works:**
1. Mobile: +10 ft speed, no OA from creatures you attack
2. Monk already has bonus speed (+10 at level 2, scaling up)
3. Combined: 50+ ft speed at level 2, 60+ at level 6
4. No need to spend ki on Step of the Wind (Disengage) — save ki for Flurry of Blows
5. Attack → move away → no OA. Free hit-and-run every turn.

**Example:** Level 6 Monk with Mobile: 45 ft base speed (30 + 15 Monk + 10 Mobile) = 45 ft. Dash as bonus action = 90 ft in one turn. Attack two enemies in different locations, neither can OA you. You're untouchable.

### Resilient (CON) + War Caster (Stacking)
**Feats:** [[30-Resilient|Resilient]] (CON) + [[41-War-Caster|War Caster]]
**Best On:** Any concentration caster who takes damage frequently (Cleric, Druid, Paladin)

**How it works:**
1. War Caster: advantage on CON concentration saves
2. Resilient (CON): add proficiency bonus to CON saves
3. Combined at level 12 (prof +4): advantage on CON saves with +4 proficiency + CON modifier
4. DC 10 concentration save with +7 bonus and advantage = **~98% success rate**

**When to take both:** If your character concept demands being in melee while concentrating (Spirit Guardians Cleric, Druid Wild Shape with concentration spells). Take War Caster at level 4, Resilient at level 12.

---

### Shield Master + STR Fighter/Paladin
**Feat:** [[35-Shield-Master|Shield Master]]
**Best On:** STR-based Fighter, Paladin

**How it works:**
1. Bonus action shove after Attack action → knock prone
2. Prone enemies: your melee attacks have advantage, their attacks have disadvantage
3. Allies in melee also get advantage
4. +2 to DEX saves from shield, plus Evasion-like reaction

**Example:** Level 5 Fighter: Attack 1 → hit. Bonus action shove → enemy falls prone. Attack 2 → advantage (because prone). All melee allies also get advantage until the enemy stands up on their turn. You just gave your whole frontline advantage for free.

## ✨ Strong Niche Combos

### Alert + Assassin Rogue
**Feat:** [[01-Alert|Alert]]
**Best On:** Assassin Rogue

**Why:** Assassinate feature gives advantage + auto-crit on surprised creatures, and advantage on creatures that haven't acted yet. +5 initiative from Alert means you almost always go first = advantage on your first attack = Sneak Attack with auto-crit potential. At level 5: 1d8 (rapier) + 3d6 (Sneak Attack) = all doubled on a crit = avg 35 damage in round 1.

---

### Crossbow Expert + Warlock (Hexblade)
**Feat:** [[05-Crossbow-Expert|Crossbow Expert]]
**Best On:** Hexblade Warlock

**Why:** Hexblade's Hex Warrior lets you use CHA for weapon attacks. Hand crossbow + CBE = bonus action attack. At level 5 with Thirsting Blade: 3 attacks/turn with CHA, all benefiting from Hexblade's Curse (+prof to damage) and Hex (1d6/hit). Single-attribute dependency (CHA) for attacks, spells, and social skills.

---

### Magic Initiate (Druid) + Non-Caster
**Feat:** [[22-Magic-Initiate|Magic Initiate]] (Druid list)
**Best On:** Any non-caster (Fighter, Barbarian, Rogue)

**Why:** Shillelagh (cantrip) makes your quarterstaff use WIS for attacks — great for WIS-based builds. Goodberry (1st-level) = 10 berries that each heal 1 HP, usable to stabilize dying allies. Druidcraft for flavor. Gives a martial character emergency healing and a WIS-based attack option.

---

### Inspiring Leader + Bard/Sorcerer/Warlock
**Feat:** [[16-Inspiring-Leader|Inspiring Leader]]
**Best On:** Any CHA-based class

**Why:** At level 10 with 20 CHA: temp HP = 15 per creature × 6 creatures = **90 temp HP** for the party before every fight, refreshing on short rest. That's equivalent to a 5th-level Aura of Vitality in total healing, but it costs no spell slots and no concentration. The best "free" party buff in the game.

---

### Healer + Thief Rogue
**Feat:** [[13-Healer|Healer]]
**Best On:** Thief Rogue

**Why:** Thief's Fast Hands lets you use objects as a **bonus action**. Healer's Kit use is a "Use an Object" action. Combined: bonus action heal for 1d6+4+creature's max HD. At level 5, that's 1d6+4+5 = avg 12.5 HP as a bonus action, no spell slots, no magic. You're a combat medic who still gets Sneak Attack on your action.
