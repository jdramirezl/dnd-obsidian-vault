# Fighter - Creating a Fighter

## Original Text

As you build your fighter, think about two related elements of your character's background: Where did you get your combat training, and what set you apart from the mundane warriors around you? Were you particularly ruthless? Did you get extra help from a mentor, perhaps because of your exceptional dedication? What drove you to this training in the first place? A threat to your homeland, a thirst for revenge, or a need to prove yourself might all have been factors.

You might have enjoyed formal training in a noble's army or in a local militia. Perhaps you trained in a war academy, learning strategy, tactics, and military history. Or you might be self-taught—unpolished but well tested. Did you take up the sword as a way to escape the limits of life on a farm, or are you following a proud family tradition? Where did you acquire your weapons and armor? They might have been military issue or family heirlooms, or perhaps you scrimped and saved for years to buy them. Your armaments are now among your most important possessions—the only things that stand between you and death's embrace.

## Condensed Version

**Creating a Fighter**: Consider your combat background and what makes you exceptional.

### Key Background Questions

**Training Origin**: Where did you learn to fight?
- Formal training in a noble's army or local militia
- War academy (strategy, tactics, military history)
- Self-taught — unpolished but well tested

**What Set You Apart**: Why are you more than a common soldier?
- Particularly ruthless nature
- Extra help from a mentor
- Exceptional dedication to training

**Motivation**: What drove you to train?
- A threat to your homeland
- A thirst for revenge
- A need to prove yourself

### Lifestyle and Equipment

**Background Path**:
- Escaping the limits of farm life
- Following a proud family tradition
- Seeking fortune and glory

**[[03-Weapons|weapons]] and [[02-Armor-and-Shields|armor]] Origin**:
- Military issue from service
- Family heirlooms passed down
- Saved and scrimped for years to purchase
- Now your most important possessions — they stand between you and death

**Key Concept**: When creating a fighter, focus on two elements: where you got your combat training and what set you apart from ordinary warriors. Your motivation, training background, and relationship with your equipment all shape who your fighter is.
