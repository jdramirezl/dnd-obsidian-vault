# Fighter - Fighting Style

## Original Text

You adopt a particular style of fighting as your specialty. Choose one of the following options. You can't take a Fighting Style option more than once, even if you later get to choose again.

### Archery

You gain a +2 bonus to attack rolls you make with ranged weapons.

### Defense

While you are wearing armor, you gain a +1 bonus to AC.

### Dueling

When you are wielding a melee weapon in one hand and no other weapons, you gain a +2 bonus to damage rolls with that weapon.

### Great Weapon Fighting

When you roll a 1 or 2 on a damage die for an attack you make with a melee weapon that you are wielding with two hands, you can reroll the die and must use the new roll, even if the new roll is a 1 or a 2. The weapon must have the two-handed or versatile property for you to gain this benefit.

### Protection

When a creature you can see attacks a target other than you that is within 5 feet of you, you can use your reaction to impose disadvantage on the attack roll. You must be wielding a shield.

### Two-Weapon Fighting

When you engage in two-weapon fighting, you can add your ability modifier to the damage of the second attack.

## Condensed Version

**Fighting Style** (1st Level): Choose one permanent combat specialty. You can't take the same style twice, even if you later get another choice (e.g., Champion's Additional Fighting Style at 10th level).

### Archery

**Bonus**: +2 to attack rolls with ranged [[03-Weapons|weapons]]
- Applies to longbows, shortbows, crossbows, etc.
- Does NOT apply to thrown weapons (they are melee weapons with the thrown property)
- Example: Longbow attack = d20 + DEX mod + proficiency + 2

**Best For**: Ranged fighters who want consistent accuracy

### Defense

**Bonus**: +1 to AC while wearing [[02-Armor-and-Shields|armor]]
- Works with any armor (light, medium, or heavy)
- Does NOT work if unarmored
- Example: Chain mail (AC 16) + shield (+2) + Defense (+1) = AC 19

**Best For**: Any fighter who wears armor — universally useful

### Dueling

**Bonus**: +2 to damage rolls with a one-handed melee [[03-Weapons|weapons]] (no other weapons held)
- Works with sword + shield (shield is not a weapon)
- Does NOT work if holding a weapon in each hand
- Example: Longsword (1d8) + STR mod + 2 damage per hit

**Best For**: Sword-and-shield fighters who want reliable damage

### Great Weapon Fighting

**Bonus**: Reroll 1s and 2s on damage dice for two-handed melee attacks
- Must use the new roll, even if it's another 1 or 2
- [[03-Weapons|weapons]] must have the two-handed or versatile property
- Only applies to the weapon's damage dice, not extra damage (e.g., not Smite dice)
- Example: Greatsword (2d6) — reroll any die showing 1 or 2, average damage increases from 7 to ~8.33

**Best For**: Greatsword/greataxe wielders who want higher average damage

### Protection

**Bonus**: Use reaction to impose disadvantage on an attack against an ally within 5 feet
- Must be wielding a shield
- Must be able to see the attacking creature
- Uses your reaction (one per round)
- Example: Ogre attacks your wizard ally standing next to you — you impose disadvantage on the ogre's attack roll

**Best For**: Defensive fighters who protect allies in melee

### Two-Weapon Fighting

**Bonus**: Add ability modifiers to the damage of the second (bonus action) attack
- Normally, the bonus action attack does NOT add your ability modifier to damage
- Example: Without style: 1d6 + 0. With style: 1d6 + STR/DEX mod

**Best For**: Dual-wielding fighters who want full damage on both attacks

### Comparison Summary

| Style | Benefit | Best Pairing |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| Archery | +2 attack (ranged) | Longbow + DEX build |
| Defense | +1 AC (any [[02-Armor-and-Shields|armor]]) | Any build wearing armor |
| Dueling | +2 damage (one-handed) | Sword + shield |
| Great [[03-Weapons|weapons]] Fighting | Reroll 1-2 on damage dice | Greatsword/greataxe |
| Protection | Disadvantage on ally attacks | Shield + front-line tank |
| Two-Weapon Fighting | Add modifier to off-hand | Dual-wield build |

**Key Concept**: Fighting Style is a permanent 1st-level choice that defines your combat specialty. Each style boosts a specific weapon/armor configuration — Archery for ranged accuracy, Defense for universal AC, Dueling for one-handed damage, Great Weapon Fighting for two-handed damage, Protection for ally defense, and Two-Weapon Fighting for dual-wield damage.
