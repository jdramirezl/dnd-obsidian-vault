# Fighter

> **Primary Ability:** Strength or Dexterity
> **Hit Point Die:** D10 per Fighter level
> **Saving Throw Proficiencies:** Strength and Constitution
> **Skill Proficiencies:** *Choose 2:* Acrobatics, Animal Handling, Athletics, History, Insight, Intimidation, Persuasion, Perception, or Survival
> **Weapon Proficiencies:** Simple and Martial weapons
> **Armor Training:** Light, Medium, and Heavy armor and Shields
> **Starting Equipment:** *Choose A, B, or C:* (A) Chain Mail, Greatsword, Flail, 8 Javelins, Dungeoneer's Pack, and 4 GP; (B) Studded Leather Armor, Scimitar, Shortsword, Longbow, 20 Arrows, Quiver, Dungeoneer's Pack, and 11 GP; or (C) 155 GP

Fighters rule many battlefields. Questing knights, royal champions, elite soldiers, and hardened mercenaries—as Fighters, they all share an unparalleled prowess with weapons and armor. And they are well acquainted with death, both meting it out and defying it.

Fighters master various weapon techniques, and a well-equipped Fighter always has the right tool at hand for any combat situation. This combination of broad ability and extensive specialization makes Fighters superior combatants.

## Becoming a Fighter…

### As a Level 1 Character
- Gain all the traits in the Core Fighter Traits table.
- Gain the Fighter's level 1 features.

### As a Multiclass Character
- Gain Hit Point Die, proficiency with Martial weapons, and training with Light and Medium armor and Shields.
- Gain the Fighter's level 1 features.

## Fighter Class Features

### Level 1: Fighting Style
You gain a Fighting Style feat of your choice. You can replace it whenever you gain a Fighter level.

### Level 1: Second Wind
As a Bonus Action, regain HP equal to 1d10 + your Fighter level. Two uses; regain one on Short Rest, all on Long Rest.

### Level 1: Weapon Mastery
Use mastery properties of 3 Simple or Martial weapons. Change one choice on a Long Rest.

### Level 2: Action Surge
On your turn, take one additional action (except Magic). Once per Short/Long Rest. At level 17, twice per rest but only once per turn.

### Level 2: Tactical Mind
On a failed ability check, expend a Second Wind use to roll 1d10 and add it to the check. If it still fails, the use isn't expended.

### Level 3: Fighter Subclass
You gain a Fighter subclass of your choice.

### Level 4: Ability Score Improvement
ASI feat or another feat. Again at levels 6, 8, 12, 14, and 16.

### Level 5: Extra Attack
Attack twice when you take the Attack action.

### Level 5: Tactical Shift
When you activate Second Wind, move up to half your Speed without provoking Opportunity Attacks.

### Level 9: Tactical Master
Replace a weapon's mastery property with Push, Sap, or Slow for that attack.

### Level 9: Indomitable
Reroll a failed saving throw with a bonus equal to your Fighter level. Once per Long Rest (twice at 13, three times at 17).

### Level 11: Two Extra Attacks
Attack three times with the Attack action.

### Level 13: Studied Attacks
If you miss an attack roll, you have Advantage on your next attack against that creature before the end of your next turn.

### Level 19: Epic Boon
You gain an Epic Boon feat or another feat.

### Level 20: Three Extra Attacks
Attack four times with the Attack action.

## Fighter Subclass: Champion

*Pursue Physical Excellence in Combat*

### Level 3: Improved Critical
Critical Hit on 19 or 20.

### Level 3: Remarkable Athlete
Advantage on Initiative and Strength (Athletics) checks. After a Critical Hit, move up to half your Speed without provoking Opportunity Attacks.

### Level 7: Additional Fighting Style
Gain another Fighting Style feat.

### Level 10: Heroic Warrior
During combat, give yourself Heroic Inspiration at the start of your turn if you don't have it.

### Level 15: Superior Critical
Critical Hit on 18–20.

### Level 18: Survivor
- **Defy Death.** Advantage on Death Saving Throws. Roll of 18–20 counts as a 20.
- **Heroic Rally.** At the start of each turn, regain HP equal to 5 + Con modifier if Bloodied and at least 1 HP.
