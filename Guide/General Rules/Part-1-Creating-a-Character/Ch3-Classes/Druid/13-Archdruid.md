# Archdruid

*20th-level druid feature*

## Original Text

At 20th level, you can use your Wild Shape an unlimited number of times.

Additionally, you can ignore the verbal and somatic components of your druid spells, as well as any material components that lack a cost and aren't consumed by a spell. You gain this benefit in both your normal shape and your beast shape from Wild Shape.

---

## Condensed Version

### Unlimited Wild Shape

| Before (levels 2–19) | After (level 20) |
|----------------------|-----------------|
| 2 uses per short rest | **Unlimited** uses |

### Ignore Spell Components

| Component | Ignored? |
|-----------|:--------:|
| **Verbal (V)** | ✅ Always |
| **Somatic (S)** | ✅ Always |
| **Material (M) — no cost, not consumed** | ✅ Ignored |
| **Material (M) — has GP cost or consumed** | ❌ Still required |

This benefit applies in **both normal and beast shape**.

### Combined with Beast Spells (18th)

At 20th level, a druid can:
1. Wild Shape **unlimited** times (infinite HP buffer)
2. Cast spells in beast form with **no V, S, or free M components**
3. Only spells requiring **costly or consumed materials** have any restriction

> **Capstone power**: Archdruid is widely considered one of the strongest 20th-level features in the game. Unlimited Wild Shape means infinite effective HP (shift → take damage → revert → shift again), and ignoring components means near-invisible spellcasting.
