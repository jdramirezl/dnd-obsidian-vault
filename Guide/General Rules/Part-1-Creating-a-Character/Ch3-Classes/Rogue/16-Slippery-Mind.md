# Rogue - Slippery Mind

## Original Text

By 15th level, you have acquired greater mental strength. You gain proficiency in Wisdom saving throws.

---

## Condensed Version

### Slippery Mind (Level 15)

**What It Does**: Grants proficiency in Wisdom [[11-Saving-Throws|saving throws]]

### Why This Matters

**Wisdom saves protect against**:
- Charm and fear effects
- Spells like [[Hold Person|Hold Person]], [[Dominate Person|Dominate Person]], [[Command|Command]]
- Many monster abilities (dragon Frightful Presence, vampire Charm)
- Psychic and mind-control effects

### Your Saving Throw Profile (with Slippery Mind)

| Save | Proficient? | Source |
|------|------------|--------|
| Dexterity | ✅ | Class feature (level 1) |
| Intelligence | ✅ | Class feature (level 1) |
| Wisdom | ✅ | Slippery Mind (level 15) |
| Strength | ❌ | — |
| Constitution | ❌ | — |
| Charisma | ❌ | — |

**Example**: Level 15 rogue, Wis 12 (+1), Prof +5:
- Wisdom save before Slippery Mind: +1
- Wisdom save after Slippery Mind: +6

### Impact

- Rogues become proficient in **3 of 6 [[11-Saving-Throws|saving throws]]** — the "big three" mental/reflex saves
- Covers the most common save types at high levels
- Makes rogues extremely hard to control or disable

**Key Concept**: Slippery Mind adds Wisdom saving throw proficiency at level 15, giving rogues three proficient saves (Dex, Int, Wis) and making them highly resistant to the charm, fear, and mind-control effects that dominate high-level play.
