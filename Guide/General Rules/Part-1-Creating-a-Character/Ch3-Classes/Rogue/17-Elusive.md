# Rogue - Elusive

## Original Text

Beginning at 18th level, you are so evasive that attackers rarely gain the upper hand against you. No attack roll has advantage against you while you aren't incapacitated.

---

## Condensed Version

### Elusive (Level 18)

**What It Does**: No attack roll can have advantage against you (unless you're [[Incapacitated|incapacitated]])

### How It Works

- **Negates advantage** on all attack rolls against you
- Only condition: you must **not be [[Incapacitated|incapacitated]]**
- Works against **all sources** of advantage — flanking, invisibility, [[Restrained|restrained]], [[Prone|prone]], etc.

### What This Counters

- **[[Invisible|invisible]] attackers**: They normally have advantage — not against you
- **Pack Tactics**: Wolves and similar creatures lose their advantage
- **Flanking** (optional rule): Doesn't grant advantage against you
- **[[Restrained|restrained]] condition**: Attackers still don't get advantage (though you still have disadvantage on Dex saves and attacks)
- **[[Prone|prone]]**: Melee attackers don't get advantage (you still have disadvantage on your attacks while prone)

### Synergy with Other Rogue Features

- **[[12-Uncanny-Dodge|Uncanny Dodge]]**: Halve one attack's damage per round
- **[[13-Evasion|Evasion]]**: Take no/half damage from Dex save effects
- **Elusive**: Enemies can't gain advantage on attacks
- Together: Rogues become one of the hardest classes to damage at high levels

### Limitation

- Does **not** prevent advantage on [[11-Saving-Throws|saving throws]] or ability checks against you
- Only applies to **attack rolls**
- Shuts off if you're **[[Incapacitated|incapacitated]]** ([[Stunned|stunned]], [[Paralyzed|paralyzed]], [[Unconscious|unconscious]], etc.)

**Key Concept**: Elusive prevents any attack roll from having advantage against you as long as you're not incapacitated. This is a capstone-tier defensive ability that, combined with Uncanny Dodge and Evasion, makes high-level rogues extraordinarily hard to hit effectively.
