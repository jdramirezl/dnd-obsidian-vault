# Rogue - Cunning Action

## Original Text

Starting at 2nd level, your quick thinking and agility allow you to move and act quickly. You can take a bonus action on each of your turns in combat. This action can be used only to take the Dash, Disengage, or Hide action.

---

## Condensed Version

### Cunning Action (Level 2)

**What It Does**: Use a bonus action every turn for one of three tactical options

### The Three Options

**Dash** (bonus action):
- Double your movement for the turn
- Example: 30 ft speed → 60 ft total movement
- Use for: Chasing targets, escaping, repositioning

**Disengage** (bonus action):
- Your movement doesn't provoke opportunity attacks for the rest of the turn
- Use for: Hit-and-run tactics — attack then safely retreat

**Hide** (bonus action):
- Make a Dexterity (Stealth) check to become hidden
- Use for: Setting up Sneak Attack with advantage next turn
- Requires something to hide behind/in

### Tactical Combos

| Situation | Cunning Action | Result |
|-----------|---------------|--------|
| Need Sneak Attack advantage | Hide | Attack with advantage next turn |
| Hit and run | Disengage | Attack, then move away safely |
| Close distance to target | Dash | Move up to double speed |
| Escape danger | Dash or Disengage | Get away without taking hits |

**Key Concept**: Cunning Action is the rogue's signature combat mobility feature, granting a free Dash, Disengage, or Hide as a bonus action every turn. This makes rogues incredibly slippery — they can strike, retreat, hide, and reposition in ways no other class can match at level 2.
