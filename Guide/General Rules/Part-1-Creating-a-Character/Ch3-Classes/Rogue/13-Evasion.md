# Rogue - Evasion

## Original Text

Beginning at 7th level, you can nimbly dodge out of the way of certain area effects, such as a red dragon's fiery breath or an [[Ice Storm|ice storm]] spell. When you are subjected to an effect that allows you to make a Dexterity saving throw to take only half damage, you instead take no damage if you succeed on the saving throw, and only half damage if you fail.

---

## Condensed Version

### Evasion (Level 7)

**What It Does**: Upgrades your Dexterity [[11-Saving-Throws|saving throws]] against area damage

### How It Works

| Dex Save Result | Normal | With Evasion |
|----------------|--------|--------------|
| **Success** | Half damage | **No damage** |
| **Fail** | Full damage | **Half damage** |

### When It Applies

- Any effect requiring a **Dexterity [[11-Saving-Throws|saving throws]]** for half damage
- Examples: [[Fireball|fireball]], [[Lightning Bolt|Lightning Bolt]], dragon breath [[03-Weapons|weapons]], traps

### Example

A red dragon breathes fire for 56 damage (DC 17 Dex save):
- **Without Evasion**: Save = 28 damage, Fail = 56 damage
- **With Evasion**: Save = **0 damage**, Fail = **28 damage**

### Synergy

- Rogues already have **Dexterity save proficiency** — high chance of success
- Combined with high Dex, you'll often take zero damage from area effects
- Level 7 rogue with Dex 18 (+4) and Prof +3: Dex save = +7

**Key Concept**: Evasion transforms Dexterity [[11-Saving-Throws|saving throws]] so you take no damage on a success and only half on a failure. Combined with the rogue's Dexterity save proficiency and high Dex score, this makes rogues nearly immune to area-of-effect damage like [[Fireball|fireball]] and dragon breath.
