# Rogue - Uncanny Dodge

## Original Text

Starting at 5th level, when an attacker that you can see hits you with an attack, you can use your reaction to halve the attack's damage against you.

---

## Condensed Version

### Uncanny Dodge (Level 5)

**What It Does**: Use your reaction to halve the damage from one attack

### Requirements

- **Trigger**: An attacker you can **see** hits you with an attack
- **Cost**: Your **reaction** (1 per round)
- **Effect**: The attack's damage is **halved**

### Important Details

- Works against **any attack** you can see (melee, ranged, spell attacks)
- Does **not** work if you can't see the attacker ([[Blinded|blinded]], [[Invisible|invisible]] attacker)
- Uses your **reaction** — can't also use it for opportunity attacks that round
- Applies to the **entire damage** of that single attack (all damage types included)
- Only works on **one attack** per round

### Example

An ogre hits you for 18 damage:
- Use Uncanny Dodge → take only **9 damage**
- Without it → take full 18 damage

A dragon hits you with a claw for 22 damage:
- Use Uncanny Dodge → take only **11 damage**

**Key Concept**: Uncanny Dodge is a powerful defensive reaction that halves the damage of any single attack you can see, available once per round. It makes rogues surprisingly durable despite their medium hit die, especially against heavy-hitting single attacks.
