# Rogue - Thief Archetype

## Original Text

### Thief

You hone your skills in the larcenous arts. Burglars, bandits, cutpurses, and other criminals typically follow this archetype, but so do rogues who prefer to think of themselves as professional treasure seekers, explorers, delvers, and investigators. In addition to improving your agility and stealth, you learn skills useful for delving into ancient ruins, reading unfamiliar languages, and using magic items you normally couldn't employ.

### Fast Hands

Starting at 3rd level, you can use the bonus action granted by your Cunning Action to make a Dexterity (Sleight of Hand) check, use your thieves' tools to disarm a trap or open a lock, or take the Use an Object action.

### Second-Story Work

When you choose this archetype at 3rd level, you gain the ability to climb faster than normal; climbing no longer costs you extra movement.

In addition, when you make a running jump, the distance you cover increases by a number of feet equal to your Dexterity modifier.

### Supreme Sneak

Starting at 9th level, you have advantage on a Dexterity (Stealth) check if you move no more than half your speed on the same turn.

### Use Magic Device

By 13th level, you have learned enough about the workings of magic that you can improvise the use of items even when they are not intended for you. You ignore all class, race, and level requirements on the use of magic items.

### Thief's Reflexes

When you reach 17th level, you have become adept at laying ambushes and quickly escaping danger. You can take two turns during the first round of any combat. You take your first turn at your normal initiative and your second turn at your initiative minus 10. You can't use this feature when you are surprised.

## Condensed Version

**Thief Archetype**: The classic rogue specialization — larceny, agility, and eventually using any magic item in the game.

### Fast Hands (Level 3)

**What It Does**: Expands [[09-Cunning-Action|Cunning Action]] with three new bonus action options

**New Bonus Action Options**:
- **Sleight of Hand check** — pick pockets, plant items mid-combat
- **Thieves' tools** — disarm a trap or pick a lock as a bonus action
- **Use an Object** — drink a potion, throw caltrops, use items quickly

**Practical Impact**: Other rogues spend an action to use objects; you do it as a bonus action and still attack

### Second-Story Work (Level 3)

**Climbing**: No longer costs extra movement
- Normal: 30 ft speed, climbing costs double = 15 ft of climbing
- Thief: 30 ft speed = 30 ft of climbing

**Running Jump Bonus**: Add your Dexterity modifier to jump distance
- Example: Dex 18 (+4) = 4 extra feet on running jumps
- Long jump: normally Strength score in feet + 4 ft bonus

### Supreme Sneak (Level 9)

**What It Does**: Advantage on Stealth checks if you move half speed or less

**How It Works**:
- Move no more than **half your speed** on your turn
- Gain **advantage** on Dexterity (Stealth) checks
- Example: 30 ft speed → move 15 ft or less → advantage on Stealth

**Why It's Great**: Reliable way to get advantage on Stealth without needing cover or darkness

### Use Magic Device (Level 13)

**What It Does**: Ignore ALL class, race, and level requirements on magic items

**This Means You Can**:
- Use a **Holy Avenger** (normally paladin only)
- Read from a **spell scroll** of any class
- Attune to items restricted by class or race
- Use wands and staves meant for spellcasters

**Practical Impact**: The Thief becomes the most versatile magic item user in the game

### Thief's Reflexes (Level 17)

**What It Does**: Take **two full turns** in the first round of combat

**How It Works**:
- **1st turn**: At your normal initiative
- **2nd turn**: At your initiative minus 10
- Each turn is a full turn (action, bonus action, movement)
- Does **not** work when surprised

**Example**: Initiative roll of 18:
- 1st turn at initiative 18 (full turn with [[07-Sneak-Attack|Sneak Attack]])
- 2nd turn at initiative 8 (another full turn with [[07-Sneak-Attack|Sneak Attack]] — it's once per *turn*)
- That's **two Sneak Attacks** in round 1

### Thief Feature Summary

| Level | Feature | Key Benefit |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| 3 | Fast Hands | Bonus action: Sleight of Hand, thieves' tools, Use Object |
| 3 | Second-Story Work | Free climbing, bonus jump distance |
| 9 | Supreme Sneak | Advantage on Stealth at half speed |
| 13 | Use Magic Device | Ignore all magic item restrictions |
| 17 | Thief's Reflexes | Two turns in first round of combat |

**Key Concept**: The Thief archetype enhances the rogue's core identity — faster hands, better movement, superior stealth, unrestricted magic item use, and double turns in the opening round. It's the quintessential rogue subclass, excelling at everything rogues are known for.
