# Rogue - Stroke of Luck

## Original Text

At 20th level, you have an uncanny knack for succeeding when you need to. If your attack misses a target within range, you can turn the miss into a hit. Alternatively, if you fail an ability check, you can treat the d20 roll as a 20.

Once you use this feature, you can't use it again until you finish a short or long rest.

---

## Condensed Version

### Stroke of Luck (Level 20)

**What It Does**: Once per rest, guarantee a hit or turn a failed check into a natural 20

### Two Uses (choose one when you use it)

**Option 1 — Guaranteed Hit**:
- If your attack **misses** a target within range, turn it into a **hit**
- Works on any attack roll — melee, ranged, spell attack
- Guarantees [[07-Sneak-Attack|Sneak Attack]] damage lands when you need it most

**Option 2 — Perfect Ability Check**:
- If you **fail** an ability check, treat the d20 roll as a **20**
- Effectively makes the check result: 20 + all modifiers
- With Expertise: could reach 30+ on the check

### Recovery

- **Recharges on**: Short or long rest
- **Uses per rest**: 1

### When to Use It

- A critical Sneak Attack that must land (boss fight, finishing blow)
- A crucial skill check with dire consequences (disarming a trap, picking a lock under pressure)
- Any moment where failure is not an option

### Example

Level 20 rogue misses a dragon with a rapier attack:
- Activate Stroke of Luck → miss becomes a **hit**
- Add Sneak Attack (10d6) → massive guaranteed damage

Level 20 rogue fails a DC 25 Thieves' Tools check:
- Activate Stroke of Luck → treat roll as 20
- Result: 20 + 17 (Dex 5 + Expertise 12) = **37** — success

**Key Concept**: Stroke of Luck is the rogue's level 20 capstone, letting you turn one missed attack into a hit or one failed ability check into a natural 20, once per short or long rest. It's the ultimate safety net for the moments that matter most.
