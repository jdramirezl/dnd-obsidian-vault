# Rogue - Sneak Attack

## Original Text

Beginning at 1st level, you know how to strike subtly and exploit a foe's distraction. Once per turn, you can deal an extra 1d6 damage to one creature you hit with an attack if you have advantage on the attack roll. The attack must use a finesse or a ranged weapon.

You don't need advantage on the attack roll if another enemy of the target is within 5 feet of it, that enemy isn't [[Incapacitated|incapacitated]], and you don't have disadvantage on the attack roll.

The amount of the extra damage increases as you gain levels in this class, as shown in the Sneak Attack column of the Rogue table.

---

## Condensed Version

### Sneak Attack (Level 1+)

**What It Does**: Deal massive extra damage once per turn with a precise strike

### Requirements (need ALL of these)

**[[03-Weapons|weapons]]**: Must use a finesse or ranged weapon
**Frequency**: Once per turn (not once per round — can trigger on opportunity attacks too)

**Trigger** (need ONE of these):
- You have **advantage** on the attack roll, OR
- An **ally is within 5 feet** of the target, that ally isn't [[Incapacitated|incapacitated]], and you don't have disadvantage

### Sneak Attack Damage by Level

| Level | Damage | Level | Damage |
|-------|--------|-------|--------|
| 1–2 | 1d6 | 11–12 | 6d6 |
| 3–4 | 2d6 | 13–14 | 7d6 |
| 5–6 | 3d6 | 15–16 | 8d6 |
| 7–8 | 4d6 | 17–18 | 9d6 |
| 9–10 | 5d6 | 19–20 | 10d6 |

### Common Ways to Get Sneak Attack

- **Hide then attack**: Advantage from being unseen
- **Ally in melee**: Your [[01-Fighter-Overview|fighter]]/barbarian is next to the target
- **Familiar Help action**: Owl familiar grants advantage
- **[[Prone|prone]] target**: Melee attacks have advantage (but ranged have disadvantage)
- **[[Restrained|restrained]]/[[Paralyzed|paralyzed]] target**: Attacks have advantage

### Example

Level 5 rogue with rapier (1d8) and Dex 16 (+3):
- Normal hit: 1d8 + 3 = avg 7.5 damage
- With Sneak Attack: 1d8 + 3d6 + 3 = avg 18 damage

**Key Concept**: Sneak Attack is the rogue's primary damage source, adding escalating d6s to one attack per turn when you have advantage or an ally nearby. It requires finesse or ranged [[03-Weapons|weapons]] and rewards tactical positioning over multiple attacks.
