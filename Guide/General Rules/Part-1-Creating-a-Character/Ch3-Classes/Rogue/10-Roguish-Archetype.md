# Rogue - Roguish Archetype

## Original Text

At 3rd level, you choose an archetype that you emulate in the exercise of your rogue abilities. The Thief archetype is detailed here; see the Player's Handbook for other archetypes. Your choice grants you features at 3rd level and then again at 9th, 13th, and 17th level.

Rogues have many features in common, including their emphasis on perfecting their skills, their precise and deadly approach to combat, and their increasingly quick reflexes. But different rogues steer those talents in varying directions, embodied by the rogue archetypes. Your choice of archetype is a reflection of your focus—not necessarily an indication of your chosen profession, but a description of your preferred techniques.

## Condensed Version

### Roguish Archetype (Level 3)

**When You Choose**: 3rd level
**Archetype Features At**: Levels 3, 9, 13, and 17

### Available in Basic Rules

- **Thief** — Larceny, agility, stealth, and using magic items (see Thief Archetype file)

### Additional Archetypes (Player's Handbook)

- [[21-Assassin|Assassin]]
- [[22-Arcane-Trickster|Arcane Trickster]]
- *(and more in expansion books)*

### What Archetypes Represent

- A reflection of your **focus and preferred techniques**
- Not necessarily your profession — a Thief archetype doesn't mean you steal
- Steers your common rogue talents in a specific direction

**Key Concept**: At 3rd level, you choose a Roguish Archetype that defines your specialization, granting new features at levels 3, 9, 13, and 17. The Basic Rules include the Thief archetype; the Player's Handbook offers additional options like Assassin and Arcane Trickster.
