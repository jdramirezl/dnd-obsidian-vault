# Warlock - Pact Boon

## Original Text

At 3rd level, your otherworldly patron bestows a gift upon you for your loyal service. You gain one of the following features of your choice.

### Pact of the Chain

You learn the *find familiar* spell and can cast it as a ritual. The spell doesn't count against your number of spells known.

When you cast the spell, you can choose one of the normal forms for your familiar or one of the following special forms: imp, pseudodragon, quasit, or sprite.

Additionally, when you take the Attack action, you can forgo one of your own attacks to allow your familiar to make one attack of its own.

### Pact of the Blade

You can use your action to create a pact weapon in your empty hand. You can choose the form that this melee weapon takes each time you create it (see chapter 5 for weapon options). You are proficient with it while you wield it. This weapon counts as magical for the purpose of overcoming resistance and immunity to nonmagical attacks and damage.

Your pact weapon disappears if it is more than 5 feet away from you for 1 minute or more. It also disappears if you use this feature again, if you dismiss the weapon (no action required), or if you die.

You can transform one magic weapon into your pact weapon by performing a special ritual while you hold the weapon. You perform the ritual over the course of 1 hour, which can be done during a short rest. You can then dismiss the weapon, shunting it into an extradimensional space, and it appears whenever you create your pact weapon thereafter. You can't affect an artifact or a sentient weapon in this way. The weapon ceases being your pact weapon if you die, if you perform the 1-hour ritual on a different weapon, or if you use a 1-hour ritual to break your bond to it. The weapon appears at your feet if it is in the extradimensional space when the bond breaks.

### Pact of the Tome

Your patron gives you a grimoire called a Book of Shadows. When you gain this feature, choose three cantrips from any class's spell list. While the book is on your person, you can cast those cantrips at will. They don't count against your number of cantrips known.

If you lose your Book of Shadows, you can perform a 1-hour ceremony to receive a replacement from your patron. This ceremony can be performed during a short or long rest, and it destroys the previous book. The book turns to ash when you die.

### Your Pact Boon (Sidebar)

Each Pact Boon option produces a special creature or an object that reflects your patron's nature.

**Pact of the Chain**: Your familiar is more cunning than a typical familiar. Its default form can be a reflection of your patron, with sprites and pseudodragons tied to the Archfey and imps and quasits tied to the Fiend. Because the Great Old One's nature is inscrutable, any familiar form is suitable for it.

**Pact of the Blade**: If your patron is the Archfey, your weapon might be a slender blade wrapped in leafy vines. If you serve the Fiend, your weapon could be an axe made of black metal and adorned with decorative flames. If your patron is the Great Old One, your weapon might be an ancient-looking spear, with a gemstone embedded in its head, carved to look like a terrible unblinking eye.

**Pact of the Tome**: Your Book of Shadows might be a fine, gilt-edged tome with spells of enchantment and illusion, gifted to you by the lordly Archfey. It could be a weighty tome bound in demon hide studded with iron, holding spells of conjuration and a wealth of forbidden lore about the sinister regions of the cosmos, a gift of the Fiend. Or it could be the tattered diary of a lunatic driven mad by contact with the Great Old One, holding scraps of spells that only your own burgeoning insanity allows you to understand and cast.

## Condensed Version

**Pact Boon**: Your second identity-defining choice at 3rd level — a gift from your patron.

### Comparison

| Pact | Gift | Key Benefit | Related Invocations |
|------|------|-------------|---------------------|
| **Chain** | Enhanced familiar | Scouting, Help action, familiar attacks | [[08-Eldritch-Invocations\|Voice of the Chain Master]], Chains of Carceri |
| **Blade** | Conjured magical weapon | Melee combat, any weapon form | [[08-Eldritch-Invocations\|Thirsting Blade]] (Extra Attack), Lifedrinker |
| **Tome** | Book of Shadows (3 cantrips from any class) | Cantrip versatility, ritual casting | [[08-Eldritch-Invocations\|Book of Ancient Secrets]] |

### Pact of the Chain — Details

- Learn [[Find Familiar|*find familiar*]] as a ritual (doesn't count against spells known)
- **Special forms**: imp, pseudodragon, quasit, sprite (much stronger than normal familiars)
- Can forgo your attack to let familiar attack instead
- Familiar can use Help action to grant advantage on your attacks

### Pact of the Blade — Details

- Create any melee weapon as an action (proficient, counts as magical)
- Weapon disappears if 5+ feet away for 1 minute, or on dismissal/death
- Can bond a magic weapon via 1-hour ritual (stores in extradimensional space)
- Cannot bond artifacts or sentient weapons
- **Critical invocation**: Thirsting Blade (5th level) gives Extra Attack — without it, Blade pact falls behind

### Pact of the Tome — Details

- Choose 3 cantrips from **any class's spell list** (cast at will, don't count against known)
- Book of Shadows is replaceable (1-hour ceremony during rest)
- Book turns to ash on death
- **Critical invocation**: Book of Ancient Secrets turns you into the party's ritual caster (any class's ritual spells)

### Patron-Flavored Appearances

| Pact | Archfey | Fiend | Great Old One |
|------|---------|-------|---------------|
| Chain | Sprite, pseudodragon | Imp, quasit | Any form |
| Blade | Vine-wrapped blade | Black metal axe with flames | Ancient spear with eye-gem |
| Tome | Gilt-edged enchantment tome | Demon-hide iron-studded grimoire | Tattered lunatic's diary |

**Key Concept**: The Pact Boon defines your warlock's playstyle as much as your patron. **Chain** excels at scouting and utility (imp invisibility is incredible). **Blade** enables melee combat but requires Thirsting Blade at 5th level to keep up with martial classes. **Tome** is the most versatile — 3 cantrips from any class plus (with Book of Ancient Secrets) unlimited ritual casting makes you the party's Swiss army knife. Each pact unlocks exclusive invocations that grow its power over time.
