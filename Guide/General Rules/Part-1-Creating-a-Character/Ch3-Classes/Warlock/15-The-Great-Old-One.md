# Warlock - The Great Old One

## Original Text

Your patron is a mysterious entity whose nature is utterly foreign to the fabric of reality. It might come from the Far Realm, the space beyond reality, or it could be one of the elder gods known only in legends. Its motives are incomprehensible to mortals, and its knowledge so immense and ancient that even the greatest libraries pale in comparison to the vast secrets it holds. The Great Old One might be unaware of your existence or entirely indifferent to you, but the secrets you have learned allow you to draw your magic from it.

Entities of this type include Ghaunadar, called That Which Lurks; Tharizdun, the Chained God; Dendar, the Night Serpent; Zargon, the Returner; Great Cthulhu; and other unfathomable beings.

### Expanded Spell List

The Great Old One lets you choose from an expanded list of spells when you learn a warlock spell. The following spells are added to the warlock spell list for you.

| Spell Level | Spells |
|-------------|--------|
| 1st | *dissonant whispers*, *Tasha's hideous laughter* |
| 2nd | *detect thoughts*, *phantasmal force* |
| 3rd | *clairvoyance*, *sending* |
| 4th | *dominate beast*, *Evard's black tentacles* |
| 5th | *dominate person*, *telekinesis* |

### Awakened Mind (1st Level)

Starting at 1st level, your alien knowledge gives you the ability to touch the minds of other creatures. You can communicate telepathically with any creature you can see within 30 feet of you. You don't need to share a language with the creature for it to understand your telepathic utterances, but the creature must be able to understand at least one language.

### Entropic Ward (6th Level)

At 6th level, you learn to magically ward yourself against attack and to turn an enemy's failed strike into good luck for yourself. When a creature makes an attack roll against you, you can use your reaction to impose disadvantage on that roll. If the attack misses you, your next attack roll against the creature has advantage if you make it before the end of your next turn.

Once you use this feature, you can't use it again until you finish a short or long rest.

### Thought Shield (10th Level)

Starting at 10th level, your thoughts can't be read by telepathy or other means unless you allow it. You also have resistance to psychic damage, and whenever a creature deals psychic damage to you, that creature takes the same amount of damage that you do.

### Create Thrall (14th Level)

At 14th level, you gain the ability to infect a humanoid's mind with the alien magic of your patron. You can use your action to touch an incapacitated humanoid. That creature is then charmed by you until a *remove curse* spell is cast on it, the charmed condition is removed from it, or you use this feature again.

You can communicate telepathically with the charmed creature as long as the two of you are on the same plane of existence.

## Condensed Version

**The Great Old One**: Telepathy, psychic power, and mind control — the Lovecraftian horror patron.

### Patron Examples

Ghaunadar (That Which Lurks), Tharizdun (the Chained God), Dendar (the Night Serpent), Zargon (the Returner), Great Cthulhu, and other unfathomable beings.

**Unique Patron Dynamic**: The Great Old One might be **unaware of your existence** or entirely indifferent to you — you draw power from its secrets, not its favor. This is the only patron where the relationship can be entirely one-sided.

### Expanded Spells — Highlights

- [[Dissonant Whispers|*Dissonant whispers*]] (1st) — psychic damage + forced movement (triggers opportunity attacks)
- [[Phantasmal Force|*Phantasmal force*]] (2nd) — incredibly versatile illusion that deals psychic damage
- [[Evards Black Tentacles|*Evard's black tentacles*]] (4th) — area control + [[Restrained|restrained]] condition
- [[Dominate Person|*Dominate person*]] (5th) — powerful single-target mind control

### Feature Summary

| Level | Feature | Recharge | Effect |
|-------|---------|----------|--------|
| 1st | **Awakened Mind** | At will | 30-ft telepathy, no shared language needed |
| 6th | **Entropic Ward** | Short rest | Reaction: disadvantage on attack vs. you; if miss, your next attack has advantage |
| 10th | **Thought Shield** | Passive | Immune to mind reading, psychic resistance, reflect psychic damage |
| 14th | **Create Thrall** | No limit | Touch [[Incapacitated|incapacitated]] humanoid: [[Charmed|charmed]] until [[Remove Curse|*remove curse*]] + telepathic link |

### Analysis

- **Awakened Mind** is pure utility — no combat power, but incredible for roleplay (communicate with anything that understands a language, silently, at will)
- **Entropic Ward** is a solid defensive reaction — disadvantage on one attack, plus advantage on your next attack if they miss
- **Thought Shield** is niche but powerful when relevant — psychic resistance + damage reflection is strong against mind flayers and similar foes
- **Create Thrall** has no usage limit and no save — the target just has to be incapacitated (knocked out, sleeping, etc.). The charm lasts indefinitely until *remove curse*

**Key Concept**: The Great Old One is the most roleplay-heavy and least combat-focused patron. Awakened Mind provides incredible social utility from level 1 (silent communication, bypassing language barriers). The expanded spell list leans toward psychic damage and mind control. Create Thrall is uniquely powerful for its permanence — charm an NPC indefinitely with no concentration. This patron rewards creative, out-of-combat thinking more than raw combat power.
