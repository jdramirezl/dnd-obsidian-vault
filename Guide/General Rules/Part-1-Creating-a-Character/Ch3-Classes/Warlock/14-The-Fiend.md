# Warlock - The Fiend

## Original Text

You have made a pact with a fiend from the lower planes of existence, a being whose aims are evil, even if you strive against those aims. Such beings desire the corruption or destruction of all things, ultimately including you. Fiends powerful enough to forge a pact include demon lords such as Demogorgon, Orcus, Fraz'Urb-luu, and Baphomet; archdevils such as Asmodeus, Dispater, Mephistopheles, and Belial; pit fiends and balors that are especially mighty; and ultroloths and other lords of the yugoloths.

### Expanded Spell List

The Fiend lets you choose from an expanded list of spells when you learn a warlock spell. The following spells are added to the warlock spell list for you.

| Spell Level | Spells |
|-------------|--------|
| 1st | *burning hands*, *command* |
| 2nd | *blindness/deafness*, *scorching ray* |
| 3rd | *fireball*, *stinking cloud* |
| 4th | *fire shield*, *wall of fire* |
| 5th | *flame strike*, *hallow* |

### Dark One's Blessing (1st Level)

Starting at 1st level, when you reduce a hostile creature to 0 hit points, you gain temporary hit points equal to your Charisma modifier + your warlock level (minimum of 1).

### Dark One's Own Luck (6th Level)

Starting at 6th level, you can call on your patron to alter fate in your favor. When you make an ability check or a saving throw, you can use this feature to add a d10 to your roll. You can do so after seeing the initial roll but before any of the roll's effects occur.

Once you use this feature, you can't use it again until you finish a short or long rest.

### Fiendish Resilience (10th Level)

Starting at 10th level, you can choose one damage type when you finish a short or long rest. You gain resistance to that damage type until you choose a different one with this feature. Damage from magical weapons or silver weapons ignores this resistance.

### Hurl Through Hell (14th Level)

Starting at 14th level, when you hit a creature with an attack, you can use this feature to instantly transport the target through the lower planes. The creature disappears and hurtles through a nightmare landscape.

At the end of your next turn, the target returns to the space it previously occupied, or the nearest unoccupied space. If the target is not a fiend, it takes 10d10 psychic damage as it reels from its horrific experience.

Once you use this feature, you can't use it again until you finish a long rest.

## Condensed Version

**The Fiend**: Fire, durability, and raw damage — the most straightforward and combat-effective patron.

### Patron Examples

**Demon lords**: Demogorgon, Orcus, Fraz'Urb-luu, Baphomet
**Archdevils**: Asmodeus, Dispater, Mephistopheles, Belial
**Others**: Mighty pit fiends, balors, ultroloths, lords of the yugoloths

### Expanded Spells — Highlights

- [[Burning Hands|*Burning hands*]] (1st) — AoE damage from level 1
- [[Scorching Ray|*Scorching ray*]] (2nd) — multi-target ranged damage
- [[Fireball|*Fireball*]] (3rd) — the iconic blast spell, normally not on the warlock list
- [[Wall of Fire|*Wall of fire*]] (4th) — battlefield control + damage

### Feature Summary

| Level | Feature | Recharge | Effect |
|-------|---------|----------|--------|
| 1st | **Dark One's Blessing** | Per kill | Temp HP = CHA mod + warlock level on reducing hostile to 0 HP |
| 6th | **Dark One's Own Luck** | Short rest | Add d10 to one ability check or saving throw (after seeing roll) |
| 10th | **Fiendish Resilience** | Short/long rest | Choose one damage resistance (not vs. magical/silver weapons) |
| 14th | **Hurl Through Hell** | Long rest | On hit: target vanishes 1 round, non-fiends take 10d10 psychic |

### Analysis

- **Dark One's Blessing** is excellent at all levels — scales with warlock level, triggers on every kill, and temp HP stacks with your existing HP pool for effective tankiness
- **Dark One's Own Luck** is a reliable save-or-suck insurance policy — d10 after seeing the roll is very strong
- **Fiendish Resilience** provides flexible damage resistance that you can change every rest — pick fire for a dragon fight, cold for a frost giant, etc.
- **Hurl Through Hell** is devastating — 10d10 (avg 55) psychic damage plus removing the target from combat for a full round

**Key Concept**: The Fiend is the best patron for combat-focused warlocks. Dark One's Blessing gives you staying power (temp HP on every kill), the expanded spell list adds *fireball* and other blast spells the warlock normally lacks, and Hurl Through Hell is one of the highest single-target damage features in the game. Every feature is straightforward and powerful — no tricks, just raw effectiveness. The Fiend warlock is the easiest patron to play well.
