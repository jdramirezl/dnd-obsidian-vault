# Brutal Critical

## Original Text

Beginning at 9th level, you can roll one additional weapon damage die when determining the extra damage for a critical hit with a melee attack.

This increases to two additional dice at 13th level and three additional dice at 17th level.

---

## Condensed Version

**Brutal Critical** (9th Level): Roll extra weapon damage dice on critical hits with melee attacks.

### Scaling

| Barbarian Level | Additional Crit Dice | Total Extra Dice on Crit |
|-----------------|----------------------|--------------------------|
| 9th | +1 | Weapon dice ×2 + 1 |
| 13th | +2 | Weapon dice ×2 + 2 |
| 17th | +3 | Weapon dice ×2 + 3 |

### Crit Damage Examples (Greataxe = 1d12)

| Level | Normal Hit | Critical Hit |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| Pre-9th | 1d12 + STR + Rage | 2d12 + STR + Rage |
| 9th | 1d12 + STR + Rage | 3d12 + STR + Rage |
| 13th | 1d12 + STR + Rage | 4d12 + STR + Rage |
| 17th | 1d12 + STR + Rage | 5d12 + STR + Rage |

### Why Greataxe > Greatsword for Barbarians

- **Greataxe** (1d12): Each Brutal Critical die is a d12 (avg 6.5 per die)
- **Greatsword** (2d6): Each Brutal Critical die is a d6 (avg 3.5 per die)
- At 17th level, a greataxe crit adds 3d12 extra = avg 19.5 vs. greatsword 3d6 = avg 10.5

### Synergy with Reckless Attack

- [[08-Reckless-Attack|Reckless Attack]] gives advantage, roughly doubling crit chance (5% → ~9.75%)
- More crits × bigger crits = massive damage spikes

**Key Concept**: Brutal Critical adds extra weapon damage dice to critical hits, scaling from +1 die at 9th to +3 dice at 17th level. This is why the greataxe (1d12) is the optimal barbarian weapon — each extra crit die is a d12 instead of a d6. Combined with [[08-Reckless-Attack|Reckless Attack]] advantage, barbarians crit more often and harder than any other class.
