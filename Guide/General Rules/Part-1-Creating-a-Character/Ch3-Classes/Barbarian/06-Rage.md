# Rage

## Original Text

In battle, you fight with primal ferocity. On your turn, you can enter a rage as a bonus action.

While raging, you gain the following benefits if you aren't wearing heavy armor:

- You have advantage on Strength checks and Strength saving throws.
- When you make a melee weapon attack using Strength, you gain a bonus to the damage roll that increases as you gain levels as a barbarian, as shown in the Rage Damage column of the Barbarian table.
- You have resistance to bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing damage.

If you are able to cast spells, you can't cast them or concentrate on them while raging.

Your rage lasts for 1 minute. It ends early if you are knocked unconscious or if your turn ends and you haven't attacked a hostile creature since your last turn or taken damage since then. You can also end your rage on your turn as a bonus action.

Once you have raged the number of times shown for your barbarian level in the Rages column of the Barbarian table, you must finish a long rest before you can rage again.

## Condensed Version

**Rage** (1st Level): The barbarian's defining feature — a bonus action that unleashes primal fury, granting bonus damage, damage resistance, and enhanced Strength.

### How It Works

**Activation**: Bonus action on your turn
**Duration**: 1 minute (10 rounds)
**Requirement**: Cannot be wearing heavy armor

### Benefits While Raging

| Benefit | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Advantage on STR checks/saves** | Grappling, shoving, Athletics, STR saving throws |
| **Rage Damage bonus** | +2 to +4 on melee weapon attacks using Strength (see [[04b-Barbarian-Level-Table|level table]]) |
| **Damage Resistance** | Resistance to bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing damage (half damage) |

### Rage Damage Scaling

| Barbarian Level | Rage Damage Bonus |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| 1st–8th | +2 |
| 9th–15th | +3 |
| 16th+ | +4 |

### Rage Uses Per Long Rest

| Barbarian Level | Rages |
|-----------------|-------|
| 1st–2nd | 2 |
| 3rd–5th | 3 |
| 6th–8th | 4 |
| 9th–11th | 4 |
| 12th–15th | 5 |
| 16th–19th | 5–6 |
| 20th | Unlimited |

### Rage Ends Early If

- You are **knocked [[Unconscious|unconscious]]**
- Your turn ends and you **haven't attacked** a hostile creature or **taken damage** since your last turn
- You **choose** to end it (bonus action)

### Restrictions

- **No spellcasting**: Can't cast spells or concentrate on them while raging
- **No heavy armor**: Rage benefits only apply if you aren't wearing heavy armor
- **Melee only**: Rage damage bonus applies only to melee weapon attacks using Strength

### Tactical Tips

- Rage first, then attack — activation is a bonus action, so you still get your Attack action
- Take damage or attack every turn to keep rage active
- Resistance to B/P/S effectively doubles your HP against physical attacks
- At level 1 with 2 rages, be strategic — save rage for dangerous fights
- Grappling while raging is extremely effective (advantage on Athletics)

**Key Concept**: Rage is the single most important barbarian feature. It provides three massive benefits simultaneously: bonus melee damage, advantage on Strength, and resistance to the three most common damage types. The resistance alone effectively doubles your HP against physical attacks, making barbarians the toughest front-line class in the game.
