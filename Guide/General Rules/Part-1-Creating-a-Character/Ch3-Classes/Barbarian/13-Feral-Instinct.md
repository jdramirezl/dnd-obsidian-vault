# Feral Instinct

## Original Text

By 7th level, your instincts are so honed that you have advantage on initiative rolls.

Additionally, if you are surprised at the beginning of combat and aren't incapacitated, you can act normally on your first turn, but only if you enter your rage before doing anything else on that turn.

---

## Condensed Version

**Feral Instinct** (7th Level): Advantage on initiative rolls, and you can act when surprised if you [[06-Rage|rage]] first.

### Two Benefits

**1. Advantage on Initiative**
- Initiative is a Dexterity check — advantage means rolling 2d20 and taking the higher
- Going first lets you [[06-Rage|rage]] and attack before enemies can act
- Stacks with bonuses to initiative (e.g., Alert feat)

**2. Act While Surprised**
- Normally, surprised creatures can't act on their first turn
- With Feral Instinct, you CAN act — but you must enter [[06-Rage|rage]] before doing anything else
- This means you can rage, then attack, move, etc. on the surprise round

### Key Details

- **Must not be [[Incapacitated|incapacitated]]**: The anti-surprise benefit requires you to not be incapacitated
- **Rage first**: You MUST rage before taking any other action on the surprise turn
- **Always active**: Advantage on initiative is passive — no cost or activation

**Key Concept**: Feral Instinct makes barbarians among the fastest to act in combat with advantage on initiative, and lets them ignore the surprised condition by raging immediately. Going first means getting [[06-Rage|rage]] resistance up before taking any damage.
