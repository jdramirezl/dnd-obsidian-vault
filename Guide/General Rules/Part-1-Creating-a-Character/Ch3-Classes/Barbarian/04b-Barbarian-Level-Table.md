## Condensed Version

### Key Milestones

- **Level 1** — [[06-Rage|Rage]] (2 uses, +2 damage) + [[07-Unarmored-Defense|Unarmored Defense]] — core kit online from day one
- **Level 2** — [[08-Reckless-Attack|Reckless Attack]] + [[09-Danger-Sense|Danger Sense]] — offensive and defensive tools
- **Level 3** — [[10-Primal-Path|Primal Path]] chosen (Berserker, Totem Warrior, etc.)
- **Levels 4, 8, 12, 16, 19** — **ASI ×5** — standard progression
- **Level 5** — [[11-Extra-Attack|Extra Attack]] + [[12-Fast-Movement|Fast Movement]] — major power spike
- **Level 6, 10, 14** — Path features (3 subclass upgrades total)
- **Level 7** — [[13-Feral-Instinct|Feral Instinct]] — advantage on initiative, act while surprised
- **Level 9** — [[14-Brutal-Critical|Brutal Critical]] (1 die) + Rage Damage increases to +3
- **Level 11** — [[15-Relentless-Rage|Relentless Rage]] — refuse to drop to 0 HP
- **Level 13** — [[14-Brutal-Critical|Brutal Critical]] (2 dice)
- **Level 15** — [[16-Persistent-Rage|Persistent Rage]] — rage no longer ends early
- **Level 16** — Rage Damage increases to +4
- **Level 17** — [[14-Brutal-Critical|Brutal Critical]] (3 dice) — devastating crits
- **Level 18** — [[17-Indomitable-Might|Indomitable Might]] — STR checks can't roll below your score
- **Level 20** — [[18-Primal-Champion|Primal Champion]] — +4 STR and CON (max 24), unlimited rages

### Rage Scaling

| Barbarian Level | Rages per Long Rest | Rage Damage Bonus |
|-----------------|---------------------|-------------------|
| 1st–2nd | 2 | +2 |
| 3rd–5th | 3 | +2 |
| 6th–8th | 4 | +2 |
| 9th–11th | 4 | +3 |
| 12th–15th | 5 | +3 |
| 16th–19th | 5–6 | +4 |
| 20th | Unlimited | +4 |

**Key Concept**: The Barbarian is defined by escalating [[06-Rage|Rage]] — more uses, more damage, and increasingly powerful features that make rage harder to end and more devastating. [[08-Reckless-Attack|Reckless Attack]] at 2nd level and [[11-Extra-Attack|Extra Attack]] at 5th are the biggest power spikes. The capstone grants unlimited rages and ability scores up to 24.
