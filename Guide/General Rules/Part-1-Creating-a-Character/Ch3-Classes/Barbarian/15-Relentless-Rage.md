# Relentless Rage

## Original Text

Starting at 11th level, your rage can keep you fighting despite grievous wounds. If you drop to 0 hit points while you're raging and don't die outright, you can make a DC 10 Constitution saving throw. If you succeed, you drop to 1 hit point instead.

Each time you use this feature after the first, the DC increases by 5. When you finish a short or long rest, the DC resets to 10.

---

## Condensed Version

**Relentless Rage** (11th Level): When you drop to 0 HP while raging, make a CON save to stay at 1 HP instead.

### How It Works

**Trigger**: Drop to 0 HP while [[06-Rage|raging]] (and don't die outright)
**Save**: Constitution saving throw
**Success**: Drop to 1 HP instead of falling unconscious
**Failure**: Fall [[Unconscious|unconscious]] as normal

### Escalating DC

| Use (per rest) | DC |
|----------------|----|
| 1st | 10 |
| 2nd | 15 |
| 3rd | 20 |
| 4th | 25 |
| 5th+ | 30+ |

**DC resets to 10** after a short or long rest.

### Key Details

- **Must be raging**: Only works while [[06-Rage|rage]] is active
- **Not outright death**: If a single hit deals enough damage to kill you outright (remaining HP + max HP), this doesn't help
- **CON save**: Barbarians have proficiency in CON saves and high CON — DC 10 is nearly automatic
- **Keeps rage going**: Staying at 1 HP means your rage doesn't end from unconsciousness
- **Stacks with rage resistance**: You're already taking half physical damage, so reaching 0 HP is harder

**Key Concept**: Relentless Rage lets barbarians refuse to go down, making a CON save (starting at DC 10) to stay at 1 HP instead of falling unconscious. With high CON and proficiency in CON saves, the first use is nearly guaranteed. The escalating DC means it gets harder each time, but even 2-3 uses per rest dramatically increases survivability.
