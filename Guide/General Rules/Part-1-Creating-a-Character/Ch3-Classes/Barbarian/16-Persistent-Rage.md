# Persistent Rage

## Original Text

Beginning at 15th level, your rage is so fierce that it ends early only if you fall unconscious or if you choose to end it.

---

## Condensed Version

**Persistent Rage** (15th Level): Your [[06-Rage|rage]] no longer ends early from not attacking or taking damage.

### What Changes

**Before 15th level**, rage ends early if:
- ❌ You are knocked unconscious
- ❌ Your turn ends without attacking a hostile creature or taking damage
- ❌ You choose to end it

**At 15th level**, rage ends early ONLY if:
- ❌ You fall unconscious
- ❌ You choose to end it
- ✅ Not attacking or taking damage NO LONGER ends rage

### Why This Matters

- **No more rage maintenance**: You don't need to attack or take damage every turn to keep rage active
- **Exploration raging**: Rage through non-combat challenges (breaking doors, lifting gates) without it dropping
- **Tactical flexibility**: You can Dodge, Dash, or Help without worrying about losing rage
- **Ranged turns**: Throw javelins or reposition without rage ending
- **Combined with [[15-Relentless-Rage|Relentless Rage]]**: Even harder to take down — rage only ends if you actually go unconscious

**Key Concept**: Persistent Rage removes the biggest limitation on [[06-Rage|rage]] — the requirement to attack or take damage every turn. This gives barbarians complete tactical freedom while raging, and ensures rage stays active through any situation.
