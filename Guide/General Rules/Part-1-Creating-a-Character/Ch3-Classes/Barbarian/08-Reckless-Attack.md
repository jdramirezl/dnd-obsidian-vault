# Reckless Attack

## Original Text

Starting at 2nd level, you can throw aside all concern for defense to attack with fierce desperation. When you make your first attack on your turn, you can decide to attack recklessly. Doing so gives you advantage on melee weapon attack rolls using Strength during this turn, but attack rolls against you have advantage until your next turn.

---

## Condensed Version

**Reckless Attack** (2nd Level): Gain advantage on all melee Strength attacks this turn, but enemies get advantage on attacks against you until your next turn.

### How It Works

**Trigger**: Declare when making your first attack on your turn
**Benefit**: Advantage on all melee weapon attack rolls using Strength for the rest of your turn
**Cost**: All attack rolls against you have advantage until the start of your next turn
**Uses**: Unlimited — no resource cost

### Key Details

- **Strength attacks only**: Does not work with DEX-based melee attacks or ranged attacks
- **Whole turn**: Advantage applies to ALL melee Strength attacks that turn, not just the first
- **Enemies get advantage**: Every creature attacking you gets advantage, not just the one you attacked
- **Lasts until your next turn**: The downside persists through the entire enemy turn
- **No action cost**: It's a decision made as part of your first attack, not a separate action

### Synergies

- **[[06-Rage|Rage]] resistance**: Taking more hits matters less when you have resistance to B/P/S damage
- **[[11-Extra-Attack|Extra Attack]]**: Advantage on 2+ attacks per turn = more hits, more crits
- **[[14-Brutal-Critical|Brutal Critical]]**: Advantage doubles your crit chance (from 5% to ~9.75%), and Brutal Critical makes crits devastating
- **Great Weapon Master**: Advantage offsets the -5 attack penalty for +10 damage

**Key Concept**: Reckless Attack is the barbarian's signature offensive tool — free, unlimited advantage on melee attacks at the cost of enemies also getting advantage. Combined with [[06-Rage|Rage]] damage resistance, the tradeoff is heavily in the barbarian's favor, making them the most consistently accurate melee class in the game.
