# Path of the Berserker

## Original Text

For some barbarians, rage is a means to an end — that end being violence. The Path of the Berserker is a path of untrammeled fury, slick with blood. As you enter the berserker's rage, you thrill in the chaos of battle, heedless of your own health or well-being.

### Frenzy

Starting when you choose this path at 3rd level, you can go into a frenzy when you rage. If you do so, for the duration of your rage you can make a single melee weapon attack as a bonus action on each of your turns after this one. When your rage ends, you suffer one level of exhaustion (as described in appendix A).

### Mindless Rage

Beginning at 6th level, you can't be charmed or frightened while raging. If you are charmed or frightened when you enter your rage, the effect is suspended for the duration of the rage.

### Intimidating Presence

Beginning at 10th level, you can use your action to frighten someone with your menacing presence. When you do so, choose one creature that you can see within 30 feet of you. If the creature can see or hear you, it must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw (DC equal to 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Charisma modifier) or be frightened of you until the end of your next turn. On subsequent turns, you can use your action to extend the duration of this effect on the frightened creature until the end of your next turn. This effect ends if the creature ends its turn out of line of sight or more than 60 feet away from you.

If the creature succeeds on its saving throw, you can't use this feature on that creature again for 24 hours.

### Retaliation

Starting at 14th level, when you take damage from a creature that is within 5 feet of you, you can use your reaction to make a melee weapon attack against that creature.

## Condensed Version

**Path of the Berserker**: A subclass focused on maximum offensive output through reckless, exhausting fury.

### Frenzy (3rd Level)

**Effect**: While raging, make a bonus action melee weapon attack each turn (after the first turn)
**Cost**: One level of [[Exhaustion]] when rage ends

- Effectively gives you a third attack per turn while raging
- **Exhaustion is the major downside**: Level 1 = disadvantage on ability checks; Level 2 = speed halved; etc.
- Only removed by long rest (one level per long rest)
- Best used sparingly — once per day in the hardest fight

### Mindless Rage (6th Level)

**Effect**: Immune to [[Charmed|charmed]] and [[Frightened|frightened]] while [[06-Rage|raging]]
- If already charmed/frightened when you rage, the effect is suspended for the duration
- Excellent against dragons (Frightful Presence), spellcasters (charm spells), and fey

### Intimidating Presence (10th Level)

**Action**: Frighten one creature within 30 ft.
**Save**: Wisdom (DC = 8 + proficiency + CHA modifier)
**Duration**: Until end of your next turn (can extend with subsequent actions)
**Limitation**: If the creature saves, can't target it again for 24 hours

- **Costs your action**: Competes with attacking — significant opportunity cost
- **CHA-based DC**: Barbarians typically have low Charisma, making this unreliable
- Ends if creature moves out of line of sight or beyond 60 ft.

### Retaliation (14th Level)

**Trigger**: Take damage from a creature within 5 feet
**Cost**: Your reaction
**Effect**: Make a melee weapon attack against that creature

- Essentially a free attack every round if you're in melee and taking hits
- Works perfectly with the barbarian playstyle of being in the thick of combat
- Only one reaction per round, so choose wisely if multiple triggers occur

### Berserker Summary

| Level | Feature | Rating |
|-------|---------|--------|
| 3rd | Frenzy | Strong damage, costly exhaustion |
| 6th | Mindless Rage | Excellent defensive utility |
| 10th | Intimidating Presence | Weak — action cost + CHA dependency |
| 14th | Retaliation | Strong — free reaction attack |

**Key Concept**: The Path of the Berserker trades sustainability for raw damage. Frenzy adds a bonus action attack every turn while raging but inflicts exhaustion — a steep cost that limits how often you can use it. Mindless Rage and Retaliation are strong, but Intimidating Presence is hampered by its action cost and CHA dependency.
