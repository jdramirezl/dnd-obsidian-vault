# Danger Sense

## Original Text

At 2nd level, you gain an uncanny sense of when things nearby aren't as they should be, giving you an edge when you dodge away from danger.

You have advantage on Dexterity saving throws against effects that you can see, such as traps and spells. To gain this benefit, you can't be blinded, deafened, or incapacitated.

---

## Condensed Version

**Danger Sense** (2nd Level): Advantage on Dexterity saving throws against effects you can see.

### How It Works

**Benefit**: Advantage on DEX saving throws against visible effects
**Examples**: Fireball, Lightning Bolt, trap triggers, dragon breath weapons
**Conditions**: You must NOT be [[Blinded|blinded]], [[Deafened|deafened]], or [[Incapacitated|incapacitated]]

### Key Details

- **Must see the effect**: Works against fireballs and visible traps, but NOT against effects you can't see
- **DEX saves only**: Does not help with Wisdom, Constitution, or other saving throws
- **Always active**: No action or resource cost — passive benefit
- **Stacks with [[06-Rage|Rage]] resistance**: If you succeed on a DEX save for half damage, and you're raging with resistance, you take only one-quarter of the original damage

**Key Concept**: Danger Sense gives barbarians advantage on DEX saves against visible threats — a strong defensive feature that compensates for their typically moderate Dexterity. Combined with [[06-Rage|Rage]] resistance, barbarians can shrug off area damage that devastates other melee classes.
