# Hit Points and Proficiencies

## Original Text

### Class Features

As a sorcerer, you gain the following class features.

### Hit Points

Hit Dice: 1d6 per sorcerer level
Hit Points at 1st Level: 6 + your Constitution modifier
Hit Points at Higher Levels: 1d6 (or 4) + your Constitution modifier per sorcerer level after 1st

### Proficiencies

Armor: None
Weapons: Daggers, darts, slings, quarterstaffs, light crossbows
Tools: None
Saving Throws: Constitution, Charisma
Skills: Choose two from Arcana, Deception, Insight, Intimidation, Persuasion, and Religion

---

## Condensed Version

**Sorcerer Class Features**: The lowest hit dice (d6, tied with wizard) with no armor — but Constitution saving throw proficiency is a major advantage.

### Hit Points

**Hit Dice**: 1d6 per sorcerer level — **tied for lowest in the game** (with wizard)
**1st Level HP**: 6 + Constitution modifier
- Example: Con 14 (+2) = 8 HP at level 1
**Higher Levels**: 1d6 (or 4) + Constitution modifier per level
- Example: Level 5, Con 14 (+2) = 6 + 4×(4+2) = 30 HP (using average)
- [[12-Draconic-Bloodline|Draconic Bloodline]] adds +1 HP per level, effectively making it a d8

### Armor Proficiencies

**None** — sorcerers have zero armor proficiency
- Must rely on spells ([[Shield|*shield*]], [[Mage Armor|*mage armor*]]) or [[12-Draconic-Bloodline|Draconic Resilience]] for AC
- Multiclassing can grant armor proficiency

### Weapon Proficiencies

| Weapon | Type | Notes |
|--------|------|-------|
| Daggers | Simple | 1d4 piercing, finesse, light, thrown |
| Darts | Simple | 1d4 piercing, finesse, thrown |
| Slings | Simple | 1d4 bludgeoning, ranged |
| Quarterstaffs | Simple | 1d6 bludgeoning (1d8 versatile) |
| Light crossbows | Simple | 1d8 piercing, ranged |

### Tool Proficiencies

**None**

### Saving Throws

**Constitution**: Maintaining concentration on spells, resisting poison and disease — **this is the sorcerer's biggest mechanical advantage over wizards**
**Charisma**: Resisting banishment, some charm effects

### Skills (Choose 2)

| Skill | Ability | Use |
|-------|---------|-----|
| Arcana | INT | Recall magical lore, identify spells |
| Deception | CHA | Lie convincingly, disguise intentions |
| Insight | WIS | Read people, detect lies |
| Intimidation | CHA | Threaten and coerce |
| Persuasion | CHA | Influence through tact and diplomacy |
| Religion | INT | Recall lore about deities and rituals |

**Recommended**: Persuasion + Arcana (or Deception for a face role)

**Key Concept**: Sorcerers are fragile (d6 HD, no armor) but have Constitution save proficiency — the most important save for maintaining concentration spells. This is a significant edge over [[Wizard|wizards]], who must take the [[41-War-Caster|War Caster]] feat or [[30-Resilient|Resilient]] (Con) to match. Your CHA-based skill options make sorcerers natural party faces.
