# Equipment

## Original Text

You start with the following equipment, in addition to the equipment granted by your background:

- (a) a light crossbow and 20 bolts or (b) any simple weapon
- (a) a component pouch or (b) an arcane focus
- (a) a dungeoneer's pack or (b) an explorer's pack
- Two daggers

---

## Condensed Version

**Starting Equipment**: Minimal gear — sorcerers rely on magic, not weapons.

### Equipment Choices

| Choice | Option A | Option B |
|--------|----------|----------|
| **Weapon** | Light crossbow + 20 bolts | Any simple weapon |
| **Focus** | Component pouch | Arcane focus (crystal, orb, rod, staff, or wand) |
| **Pack** | Dungeoneer's pack | Explorer's pack |
| **Always** | Two daggers | — |

### Recommendations

- **Light crossbow** — best ranged option (1d8) for when you want to save spell slots; simple weapons are rarely better
- **Arcane focus** — cleaner than a component pouch; one hand, no fumbling for bat guano
- **Explorer's pack** — more generally useful than dungeoneer's pack for most campaigns
- **Two daggers** — emergency melee and thrown option (1d4, finesse)

**Key Concept**: Sorcerers get the lightest equipment loadout of any class. Your real weapons are your spells. The arcane focus replaces material components (without gold costs), and the light crossbow is your fallback when you're out of slots. After level 5, you'll almost never touch a weapon.
