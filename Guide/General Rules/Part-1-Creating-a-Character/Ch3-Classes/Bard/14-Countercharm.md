# Bard - Countercharm

## Original Text

At 6th level, you gain the ability to use musical notes or words of power to disrupt mind-influencing effects. As an action, you can start a performance that lasts until the end of your next turn. During that time, you and any friendly creatures within 30 feet of you have advantage on saving throws against being frightened or charmed. A creature must be able to hear you to gain this benefit. The performance ends early if you are incapacitated or silenced or if you voluntarily end it (no action required).

---

## Condensed Version

**Countercharm** (6th Level): Use an action to grant advantage on saves against being frightened or charmed to you and nearby allies.

### How It Works

**Action**: Action (not bonus action)
**Duration**: Until the end of your next turn
**Range**: 30 feet
**Effect**: You and friendly creatures within range have advantage on saves vs. [[Frightened|frightened]] and [[Charmed|charmed]]
**Requirement**: Creatures must be able to hear you
**Ends early if**: You are [[Incapacitated|incapacitated]], silenced, or voluntarily end it (no action)

### Limitations

- **Costs an action**: Must be used proactively, before the frightening/charming effect happens
- **Short duration**: Only lasts ~1 round (until end of your next turn)
- **Narrow scope**: Only frightened and charmed conditions
- **Requires prediction**: You need to anticipate when charm/fear effects are coming

### When to Use

- Before engaging creatures known to use fear (dragons, liches)
- Before engaging creatures known to charm (vampires, fey)
- When an ally is about to make a save against an ongoing charm/fear effect

**Key Concept**: Countercharm is situational — it costs an action and only protects against frightened and charmed conditions for one round. It's useful when you know fear or charm effects are coming, but in practice it competes with casting a spell on your turn. Considered one of the bard's weaker features.
