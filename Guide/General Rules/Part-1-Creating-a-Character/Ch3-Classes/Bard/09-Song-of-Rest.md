# Bard - Song of Rest

## Original Text

Beginning at 2nd level, you can use soothing music or oration to help revitalize your wounded allies during a short rest. If you or any friendly creatures who can hear your performance regain hit points at the end of the short rest by spending one or more Hit Dice, each of those creatures regains an extra 1d6 hit points.

The extra hit points increase when you reach certain levels in this class: to 1d8 at 9th level, to 1d10 at 13th level, and to 1d12 at 17th level.

---

## Condensed Version

**Song of Rest** (2nd Level): During a short rest, allies who spend Hit Dice to heal regain extra hit points.

### How It Works

**Trigger**: Short rest — allies must spend at least one Hit Die to heal
**Effect**: Each creature that heals gains extra hit points (rolled separately)
**Range**: Must be able to hear your performance
**Cost**: None — no action, no resource expended

### Die Progression

| Level | Extra Healing Die | Average Extra HP |
|-------|-------------------|------------------|
| 2nd   | 1d6  | +3.5 |
| 9th   | 1d8  | +4.5 |
| 13th  | 1d10 | +5.5 |
| 17th  | 1d12 | +6.5 |

### Key Details

- **Requires Hit Dice spending**: Creatures that don't spend Hit Dice during the short rest don't benefit
- **Applies to each creature**: Every ally who spends Hit Dice gets the bonus — scales with party size
- **Includes the bard**: You benefit too if you spend Hit Dice
- **Free resource**: No uses, no slots — works every short rest

**Key Concept**: Song of Rest is a passive party-wide healing boost during short rests. It's free, scales with party size, and grows from d6 to d12. Over an adventuring day with multiple short rests, it adds significant total healing without costing any resources.
