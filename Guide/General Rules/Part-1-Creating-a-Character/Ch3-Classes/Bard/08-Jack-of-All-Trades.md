# Bard - Jack of All Trades

## Original Text

Starting at 2nd level, you can add half your proficiency bonus, rounded down, to any ability check you make that doesn't already include your proficiency bonus.

---

## Condensed Version

**Jack of All Trades** (2nd Level): Add half your proficiency bonus (rounded down) to any ability check that doesn't already include your proficiency bonus.

### How It Works

- Applies to **ability checks only** (not attack rolls or saving throws)
- Only applies when you are **not already proficient** in the skill or tool
- Rounds down: +2 proficiency → +1 bonus; +3 proficiency → +1; +4 → +2; +5 → +2; +6 → +3

### Bonus by Level

| Level | Proficiency Bonus | Jack of All Trades Bonus |
|-------|-------------------|--------------------------|
| 2nd–4th | +2 | +1 |
| 5th–8th | +3 | +1 |
| 9th–12th | +4 | +2 |
| 13th–16th | +5 | +2 |
| 17th–20th | +6 | +3 |

### Notable Interactions

- **Initiative**: Initiative is a Dexterity check — Jack of All Trades applies (unless you have a feature adding proficiency to initiative)
- **Counterspell/Dispel Magic**: The ability check to counter higher-level spells benefits from this
- **Tool checks**: Applies to any tool you're not proficient with
- **Stacks with nothing**: If you're proficient, your full proficiency bonus applies instead — they don't stack

**Key Concept**: Jack of All Trades ensures bards are never truly bad at anything. The half-proficiency bonus to all non-proficient ability checks — including initiative and counterspell checks — makes bards the most consistently competent class across all skills.
