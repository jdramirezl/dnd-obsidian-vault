# Paladin - Ability Score Improvement

## Original Text

When you reach 4th level, and again at 8th, 12th, 16th, and 19th level, you can increase one ability score of your choice by 2, or you can increase two ability scores of your choice by 1. As normal, you can't increase an ability score above 20 using this feature.

---

## Condensed Version

**Ability Score Improvement** (4th, 8th, 12th, 16th, 19th Level): Increase one ability score by 2, or two scores by 1 each. Maximum 20.

### ASI Levels

| Level | Typical Priority |
|-------|-----------------|
| 4th   | STR or CHA to 18 or 20, or a feat |
| 8th   | Max STR or CHA to 20 |
| 12th  | Boost the other primary, CON, or feat |
| 16th  | Round out secondary stats or feat |
| 19th  | Final optimization |

### ASI Count

**5 total** — standard for most classes (fighters get 7)

### Paladin Priority

1. **Strength 20** — affects attack rolls, damage rolls, and Athletics checks
2. **Charisma 20** — affects spellcasting DC, spell attacks, [[02-Divine-Smite|Divine Smite]] synergy, and [[06-Aura-of-Protection|Aura of Protection]] bonus
3. **Constitution** — HP, concentration saves, and frontline durability
4. **Feats** (if using optional rule): Great Weapon Master, Polearm Master, Sentinel, War Caster

**Key Concept**: Paladins get 5 ASIs at the standard levels. They are MAD (Multiple Ability Dependent), needing both STR and CHA. Prioritize STR for melee damage or CHA for Aura of Protection and spellcasting — whichever is closer to 20. After both primary stats are strong, boost CON for frontline survivability, or take feats.
