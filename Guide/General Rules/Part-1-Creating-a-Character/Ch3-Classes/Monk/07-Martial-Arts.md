# Martial Arts

## Original Text

At 1st level, your practice of martial arts gives you mastery of combat styles that use unarmed strikes and monk weapons, which are shortswords and any simple melee weapons that don't have the two-handed or heavy property.

You gain the following benefits while you are unarmed or wielding only monk weapons and you aren't wearing armor or wielding a shield:

- You can use Dexterity instead of Strength for the attack and damage rolls of your unarmed strikes and monk weapons.
- You can roll a d4 in place of the normal damage of your unarmed strike or monk weapon. This die changes as you gain monk levels, as shown in the Martial Arts column of the Monk table.
- When you use the Attack action with an unarmed strike or a monk weapon on your turn, you can make one unarmed strike as a bonus action.

---

## Condensed Version

**Key Concept**: Three benefits that define the monk's combat style — **DEX for attacks**, **scaling damage die**, and **bonus action unarmed strike**.

### Monk Weapons

Any weapon that is:
- A **shortsword**, OR
- A **simple melee weapon** without two-handed or heavy

Examples: dagger, handaxe, javelin, mace, quarterstaff, shortsword, spear

### Three Benefits

| Benefit | Effect | Requirement |
|---------|--------|-------------|
| DEX attacks | Use DEX instead of STR for attack/damage | Unarmed or monk weapon |
| Scaling die | Replace weapon damage with Martial Arts die | Unarmed or monk weapon |
| Bonus strike | 1 unarmed strike as bonus action after Attack | Used Attack action with unarmed/monk weapon |

### Martial Arts Die Progression

| Monk Level | Die | Average |
|-----------|-----|---------|
| 1st–4th | d4 | 2.5 |
| 5th–10th | d6 | 3.5 |
| 11th–16th | d8 | 4.5 |
| 17th–20th | d10 | 5.5 |

> [!warning] Restrictions
> All three benefits require: **no armor**, **no shield**, and using **unarmed strikes or monk weapons only**.

> [!tip] DM Note
> The bonus action unarmed strike is separate from [[08-Ki|Flurry of Blows]]. At level 1, a monk can make 2 attacks per turn (Attack + bonus). With Flurry at level 2, that becomes 3 attacks. At level 5 with Extra Attack + Flurry, that's 4 attacks per turn.
