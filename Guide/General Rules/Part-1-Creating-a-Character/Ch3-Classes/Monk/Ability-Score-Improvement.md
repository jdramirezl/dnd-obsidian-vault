# Monk - Ability Score Improvement

## Original Text

When you reach 4th level, and again at 8th, 12th, 16th, and 19th level, you can increase one ability score of your choice by 2, or you can increase two ability scores of your choice by 1. As normal, you can't increase an ability score above 20 using this feature.

---

## Condensed Version

**Ability Score Improvement** (4th, 8th, 12th, 16th, 19th Level): Increase one ability score by 2, or two scores by 1 each. Maximum 20.

### ASI Levels

| Level | Typical Priority |
|-------|-----------------|
| 4th   | DEX to 18 or 20, or a feat |
| 8th   | Max DEX to 20, or WIS boost |
| 12th  | WIS, CON, or feat |
| 16th  | Round out secondary stats or feat |
| 19th  | Final optimization |

### ASI Count

**5 total** — standard for most classes (fighters get 7)

### Monk Priority

1. **Dexterity 20** — affects attack rolls, damage rolls, AC, initiative, and DEX saves
2. **Wisdom** — AC (with [[01-Unarmored-Defense|Unarmored Defense]]), ki save DC, and WIS saves
3. **Constitution** — HP and concentration saves for durability
4. **Feats** (if using optional rule): Mobile, Alert, Tough, Sentinel

**Key Concept**: Monks get 5 ASIs at the standard levels. Maximizing Dexterity is the top priority since it powers attacks, AC, and initiative. After DEX 20, boost WIS (AC/ki save DC) or CON (HP/survivability), or take feats. Mobile is an excellent feat pick — its bonus movement and disengage-on-attack synergize perfectly with the Monk's hit-and-run style. Alert is also strong, pushing an already-high initiative even further for first-round Stunning Strikes.
