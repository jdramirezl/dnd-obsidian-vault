# Wizard - Evocation: Potent Cantrip, Empowered Evocation, Overchannel

## Original Text

### Potent Cantrip

Starting at 6th level, your damaging cantrips affect even creatures that avoid the brunt of the effect. When a creature succeeds on a saving throw against your cantrip, the creature takes half the cantrip's damage (if any) but suffers no additional effect from the cantrip.

### Empowered Evocation

Beginning at 10th level, you can add your Intelligence modifier to one damage roll of any wizard evocation spell you cast.

### Overchannel

Starting at 14th level, you can increase the power of your simpler spells. When you cast a wizard spell of 1st through 5th level that deals damage, you can deal maximum damage with that spell.

The first time you do so, you suffer no adverse effect. If you use this feature again before you finish a long rest, you take 2d12 necrotic damage for each level of the spell, immediately after you cast it. Each time you use this feature again before finishing a long rest, the necrotic damage per spell level increases by 1d12. This damage ignores resistance and immunity.

## Condensed Version

### Potent Cantrip (6th Level)

**Effect**: Damaging cantrips deal half damage even when the target succeeds on its [[11-Saving-Throws|saving throws]].

**How It Works**:
- Target succeeds on save → takes **half damage** (instead of zero)
- No additional effects from the cantrip on a successful save
- Only applies to cantrips that require a **saving throw** (not attack rolls)

**Affected Cantrips** (examples):
- [[Ray of Frost|Ray of Frost]] — no, uses attack roll
- [[Fire Bolt|fire bolt]] — no, uses attack roll
- [[Acid Splash|Acid Splash]] — yes, Dex save
- [[Poison Spray|Poison Spray]] — yes, Con save

**Why It Matters**: Guarantees your cantrips always deal some damage, making them reliable even against high-save enemies.

---

### Empowered Evocation (10th Level)

**Effect**: Add your Intelligence modifier to **one damage roll** of any wizard **evocation** spell.

**Details**:
- Applies to any wizard evocation spell (not just cantrips)
- Added to **one** damage roll, not each target or each die
- With Int 20, that's +5 damage to every evocation spell

**Examples** (with Int 20, +5):
- [[Magic Missile|magic missile]]: +5 to one dart's damage (each dart is a separate roll, add to one)
- [[Fireball|fireball]]: 8d6 + 5 fire damage
- [[Fire Bolt|fire bolt]] cantrip: 2d10 + 5 fire damage (at 5th level)

**Why It Matters**: A flat +5 damage on every evocation spell adds up significantly over an adventuring day.

---

### Overchannel (14th Level)

**Effect**: When casting a wizard spell of 1st–5th level that deals damage, deal **maximum damage**.

**Usage and Cost**:
- **1st use per long rest**: No adverse effect (free!)
- **2nd use**: Take 2d12 necrotic damage per spell level
- **3rd use**: Take 3d12 necrotic damage per spell level
- **4th use**: Take 4d12 necrotic damage per spell level
- Damage **ignores resistance and immunity**

**Damage Examples** (maximum damage):

| Spell | Normal Damage | Overchanneled |
|---|---|---|
| [[Magic Missile|magic missile]] (1st) | 3d4+3 (avg 10.5) | **15** |
| [[Fireball|fireball]] (3rd) | 8d6 (avg 28) | **48** |
| [[Cone of Cold|Cone of Cold]] (5th) | 8d8 (avg 36) | **64** |

**Self-Damage on Repeated Use**:

| Use # | Necrotic per Spell Level | 3rd-Level Spell | 5th-Level Spell |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1st | None | 0 | 0 |
| 2nd | 2d12 | 6d12 (avg 39) | 10d12 (avg 65) |
| 3rd | 3d12 | 9d12 (avg 58.5) | 15d12 (avg 97.5) |

**Tips**:
- Use the free first Overchannel on your biggest damage spell
- Second use is extremely dangerous — the self-damage can kill you
- Best with 3rd-level spells (good damage boost, lower self-damage risk)
- Combines with Empowered **evocation** for even more damage

**Key Concept**: These three features define the Evocation wizard's power curve. Potent Cantrip (6th) ensures cantrips always deal damage. Empowered Evocation (10th) adds your Intelligence modifier to every evocation spell. Overchannel (14th) lets you deal maximum damage once per long rest for free — but using it again risks devastating self-harm.
