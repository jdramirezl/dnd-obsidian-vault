# Wizard - Ability Score Improvement

## Original Text

When you reach 4th level, and again at 8th, 12th, 16th, and 19th level, you can increase one ability score of your choice by 2, or you can increase two ability scores of your choice by 1. As normal, you can't increase an ability score above 20 using this feature.

---

## Condensed Version

**[[01-Ability-Scores-and-Modifiers|ability scores]] Improvement** (ASI): Increase your ability scores at key levels.

### When You Get It

- **Levels**: 4th, 8th, 12th, 16th, and 19th (5 total)

### Options Each Time

- Increase **one** [[01-Ability-Scores-and-Modifiers|ability scores]] by **+2**, OR
- Increase **two** ability scores by **+1** each
- Maximum ability score: **20**

### Recommended Priorities for Wizards

1. **Intelligence to 20** — your spellcasting ability; affects spell save DC, spell attack, and prepared spell count
2. **Constitution** — more HP (you only have d6 [[07-Damage-and-Healing|hit dice]]) and better Concentration saves
3. **Dexterity** — better AC (no [[02-Armor-and-Shields|armor]]) and initiative

### Example Progression

- **4th level**: Int 16 → 18 (+2)
- **8th level**: Int 18 → 20 (+2)
- **12th level**: Con 14 → 16 (+2) or split Dex/Con
- **16th, 19th**: Shore up remaining scores or take feats (if using optional rules)

**Key Concept**: Wizards get 5 ASIs over their career. Max Intelligence first for the strongest spells, then boost Constitution for survivability. Each +1 to Intelligence increases your spell save DC, spell attack bonus, and number of prepared spells.
