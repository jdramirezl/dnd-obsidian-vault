# Wizard - Equipment

## Original Text

You start with the following equipment, in addition to the equipment granted by your background:

- (a) a quarterstaff or (b) a dagger
- (a) a component pouch or (b) an arcane focus
- (a) a scholar's pack or (b) an explorer's pack
- A spellbook

---

## Condensed Version

**Starting Equipment**: Gear you begin with at 1st level (in addition to background equipment).

### Equipment Choices

**[[03-Weapons|weapons]]** (choose one):
- (a) Quarterstaff — 1d6 bludgeoning (1d8 versatile)
- (b) Dagger — 1d4 piercing, finesse, light, thrown (20/60)

**Spellcasting Component** (choose one):
- (a) Component pouch — holds material components
- (b) Arcane focus — crystal, orb, rod, staff, or wand; replaces non-costed material components

**Adventuring Gear** (choose one):
- (a) Scholar's pack — backpack, book of lore, ink, ink pen, 10 parchment, little bag of sand, small knife
- (b) Explorer's pack — backpack, bedroll, mess kit, tinderbox, 10 torches, 10 days rations, waterskin, 50 ft rope

**Always Included**:
- Spellbook — essential for your class; contains your known spells

### Recommendations

- **Quarterstaff** works as both a [[03-Weapons|weapons]] and an arcane focus (if using a staff focus)
- **Arcane focus** is simpler than tracking individual material components
- **Scholar's pack** fits the wizard theme; **explorer's pack** is more practical for dungeon crawling

**Key Concept**: The spellbook is your most important piece of equipment — without it, you can't prepare new spells. Protect it at all costs, and consider making a backup copy when you can afford it.
