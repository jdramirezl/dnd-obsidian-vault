# Wizard - Creating a Wizard

## Original Text

Creating a wizard character demands a backstory dominated by at least one extraordinary event. How did your character first come into contact with magic? How did you discover you had an aptitude for it? Do you have a natural talent, or did you simply study hard and practice incessantly? Did you encounter a magical creature or an ancient tome that taught you the basics of magic?

What drew you forth from your life of study? Did your first taste of magical knowledge leave you hungry for more? Have you received word of a secret repository of knowledge not yet plundered by any other wizard? Perhaps you're simply eager to put your newfound magical skills to the test in the face of danger.

---

## Condensed Version

**Creating a Wizard**: Your backstory should include at least one extraordinary event related to magic.

### Key Backstory Questions

**Discovery of Magic**:
- How did you first come into contact with magic?
- Do you have natural talent, or did you study hard?
- Did a magical creature or ancient tome teach you the basics?

**Motivation for Adventure**:
- Did your first taste of magical knowledge leave you hungry for more?
- Have you heard of a secret repository of knowledge?
- Are you eager to test your magical skills against real danger?

### Backstory Ideas

- Found an ancient spellbook in a ruined tower
- Apprenticed under a powerful wizard
- Discovered innate talent during a crisis
- Driven by curiosity about lost magical secrets
- Seeking a legendary library or artifact

**Key Concept**: A wizard's backstory should center on how they discovered magic and what drives them to seek more knowledge and power — the "extraordinary event" that set them on the path of arcane study.
