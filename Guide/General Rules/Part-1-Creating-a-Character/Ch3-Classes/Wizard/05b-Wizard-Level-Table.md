## Condensed Version

- **Level 1** — Spellcasting + Arcane Recovery (recover slots on short rest). 3 cantrips, 1st-level slots.
- **Level 2** — Choose your **Arcane Tradition** (subclass).
- **Level 3** — Unlock **2nd-level spell slots**.
- **Level 4** — First **ASI** ([[01-Ability-Scores-and-Modifiers|ability scores]] Improvement). 4th cantrip.
- **Level 5** — Proficiency +3. Unlock **3rd-level spell slots** ([[Fireball|fireball]], [[Counterspell|counterspell]]).
- **Level 6** — **Arcane Tradition feature**.
- **Level 7** — Unlock **4th-level spell slots**.
- **Level 8** — Second **ASI**.
- **Level 9** — Proficiency +4. Unlock **5th-level spell slots**.
- **Level 10** — **Arcane Tradition feature**. 5th cantrip.
- **Level 11** — Unlock **6th-level spell slots** (one per long rest).
- **Level 12** — Third **ASI**.
- **Level 13** — Proficiency +5. Unlock **7th-level spell slots**.
- **Level 14** — **Arcane Tradition feature** (final subclass feature).
- **Level 15** — Unlock **8th-level spell slots**.
- **Level 16** — Fourth **ASI**.
- **Level 17** — Proficiency +6. Unlock **9th-level spell slots** ([[Wish|Wish]]).
- **Level 18** — **Spell Mastery**: cast one 1st-level and one 2nd-level spell at will.
- **Level 19** — Fifth **ASI**.
- **Level 20** — **Signature Spells**: two 3rd-level spells you can cast once each without a slot.

**Key Concept:** Wizards are the most versatile spellcasters — they learn spells by copying them into their spellbook, giving them the largest potential spell list of any class. Their power curve is defined by new spell slot tiers (odd levels) and ASIs (every 4th level), with subclass features at 2, 6, 10, and 14. Capstones at 18–20 grant at-will and free casting of lower-level spells.
