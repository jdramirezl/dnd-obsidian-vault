# Wizard - Sculpt Spells

## Original Text

Beginning at 2nd level, you can create pockets of relative safety within the effects of your evocation spells. When you cast an evocation spell that affects other creatures that you can see, you can choose a number of them equal to 1 + the spell's level. The chosen creatures automatically succeed on their saving throws against the spell, and they take no damage if they would normally take half damage on a successful save.

---

## Condensed Version

**Sculpt Spells** (2nd Level, School of **evocation**): Protect allies from your area-of-effect evocation spells.

### How It Works

- **Trigger**: When you cast an **evocation** spell that affects other creatures you can see
- **Choose**: Up to **1 + the spell's level** creatures to protect
- **Effect**: Chosen creatures **automatically succeed** on their [[11-Saving-Throws|saving throws]]
- **Bonus**: They take **no damage** if the spell normally deals half on a save

### Protected Creatures by Spell Level

| Spell Level | Creatures Protected |
|---|---|
| 1st | 2 |
| 2nd | 3 |
| 3rd | 4 |
| 4th | 5 |
| 5th | 6 |
| 6th+ | 7+ |

### Practical Examples

**[[Fireball|fireball]] (3rd level)**: Protect up to 4 allies
- Allies take **0 damage** (normally half on a save)
- Enemies take full 8d6 fire damage (or half on their save)

**[[Thunderwave|Thunderwave]] (1st level)**: Protect up to 2 allies
- Allies take 0 damage and aren't pushed
- Enemies take 2d8 thunder and are pushed 10 ft

### Key Interactions

- Only works on ****evocation**** spells
- Must be able to **see** the creatures you protect
- Works on any creature, not just allies (but you'd typically protect allies)
- The protected creatures take **zero** damage, not half — this is better than Evasion

**Key Concept**: Sculpt Spells is what makes Evocation wizards shine — you can drop [[Fireball|fireball]] on a group of enemies mixed with allies and your allies take zero damage. This removes the biggest drawback of area spells and lets you blast freely without friendly fire.
