## Original Text

The Beast Master archetype embodies a friendship between the civilized races and the beasts of the world. United in focus, beast and ranger work as one to fight the monstrous foes that threaten civilization and the wilderness alike. Emulating the Beast Master archetype means committing yourself to this ideal, working in partnership with an animal as its companion and friend.

### Ranger's Companion (3rd level)

At 3rd level, you gain a beast companion that accompanies you on your adventures and is trained to fight alongside you. Choose a beast that is no larger than Medium and that has a challenge rating of 1/4 or lower (appendix D presents statistics for the hawk, mastiff, and panther as examples). Add your proficiency bonus to the beast's AC, attack rolls, and damage rolls, as well as to any saving throws and skills it is proficient in. Its hit point maximum equals its normal maximum or four times your ranger level, whichever is higher.

The beast obeys your commands as best as it can. It takes its turn on your initiative, though it doesn't take an action unless you command it to. On your turn, you can verbally command the beast where to move (no action required by you). You can use your action to verbally command it to take the Attack, Dash, Disengage, Dodge, or Help action. Once you have the Extra Attack feature, you can make one weapon attack yourself when you command the beast to take the Attack action.

While traveling through your favored terrain with only the beast, you can move stealthily at a normal pace.

If the beast dies, you can obtain another one by spending 8 hours magically bonding with another beast that isn't hostile to you, either the same type of beast as before or a different one.

### Exceptional Training (7th level)

Beginning at 7th level, on any of your turns when your beast companion doesn't attack, you can use a bonus action to command the beast to take the Dash, Disengage, Dodge, or Help action on its turn.

### Bestial Fury (11th level)

Starting at 11th level, your beast companion can make two attacks when you command it to use the Attack action.

### Share Spells (15th level)

Beginning at 15th level, when you cast a spell targeting yourself, you can also affect your beast companion with the spell if the beast is within 30 feet of you.

## Condensed Version

**Key Concept: Pet-class subclass with significant action-economy costs.**

### Companion Rules
- **CR 1/4 or lower**, Medium or smaller (hawk, mastiff, panther are examples).
- Add **your proficiency bonus** to beast's AC, attacks, damage, saves, and proficient skills.
- Beast HP = max of (normal HP, 4 × ranger level).
- Beast acts on **your initiative**, but needs **your action** to Attack/Dash/Disengage/Dodge/Help.
- At 5th+ (Extra Attack): when you command beast to Attack, you get **one** weapon attack.
- Beast dies → 8 hours to bond a new one.

### Subclass Progression
| Level | Feature | Effect |
|:-----:|---------|--------|
| 3rd  | **Ranger's Companion** | Gain beast; costs your action to command |
| 7th  | **Exceptional Training** | Bonus action for non-Attack commands (Dash/Disengage/Dodge/Help) |
| 11th | **Bestial Fury** | Beast makes 2 attacks per Attack command |
| 15th | **Share Spells** | Self-targeting spells also affect beast within 30 ft |

**Critical issue:** Before level 5, commanding the beast to Attack means *you* don't attack. Even at 5+, you only get one attack (not two). The action economy tax is the defining weakness of PHB Beast Master.
