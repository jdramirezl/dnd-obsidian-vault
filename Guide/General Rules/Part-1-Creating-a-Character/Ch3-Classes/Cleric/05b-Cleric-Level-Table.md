## Condensed Version

### New Spell Slot Levels
- **Level 1** → 1st-level slots
- **Level 3** → 2nd-level slots
- **Level 5** → 3rd-level slots
- **Level 7** → 4th-level slots
- **Level 9** → 5th-level slots
- **Level 11** → 6th-level slots
- **Level 13** → 7th-level slots
- **Level 15** → 8th-level slots
- **Level 17** → 9th-level slots

### Ability Score Improvements (ASIs)
- Levels **4, 8, 12, 16, 19**

### Channel Divinity Scaling
- **Level 2** → 1/rest
- **Level 6** → 2/rest
- **Level 18** → 3/rest

### Divine Domain Features
- **Level 1** → Domain chosen, domain spells
- **Level 2** → Channel Divinity option from domain
- **Level 6** → Domain feature
- **Level 8** → Domain feature (e.g., Divine Strike or Potent Spellcasting)
- **Level 17** → Domain feature

### Destroy Undead CR Scaling
- **Level 5** → CR 1/2
- **Level 8** → CR 1
- **Level 11** → CR 2
- **Level 14** → CR 3
- **Level 17** → CR 4

### Other Milestones
- **Level 10** → Divine Intervention (% chance = cleric level)
- **Level 20** → Divine Intervention improvement (automatic success)

### Cantrips Known
- 3 cantrips at levels 1–3, 4 at levels 4–9, 5 at levels 10–20

**Key Concept:** The Cleric is a full caster with access to all spell levels by 17th level. Progression is defined by domain features (1, 2, 6, 8, 17), ASIs at standard intervals, and Channel Divinity uses scaling at 2, 6, and 18. Unlike prepared casters who learn spells, Clerics prepare from their entire spell list daily — the table's spell slots are the primary resource to manage.
