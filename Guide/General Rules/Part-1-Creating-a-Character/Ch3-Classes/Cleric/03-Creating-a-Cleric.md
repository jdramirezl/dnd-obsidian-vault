# Creating a Cleric

## Original Text

As you create a cleric, the most important question to consider is which deity to serve and what principles you want your character to embody. The Player's Handbook includes lists of many of the gods of the multiverse. Check with your DM to learn which deities are in your campaign.

Once you've chosen a deity, consider your cleric's relationship to that god. Did you enter this service willingly? Or did the god choose you, impelling you into service with no regard for your wishes? How do the temple priests of your faith regard you: as a champion or a troublemaker? What are your ultimate goals? Does your deity have a special task in mind for you? Or are you striving to prove yourself worthy of a great quest?

## Condensed Version

**Creating Your Cleric**: Key questions to develop your character's divine connection.

### Primary Question: Which Deity?

**Choose a Deity**:
- See Player's Handbook for god lists
- Check with DM about campaign deities
- Deity choice affects your character significantly

**Principles to Embody**: What aspects of your deity do you represent?
- [[19-War-Domain|War]] god → warrior priest
- [[13-Life-Domain|Life]] god → healer
- [[14-Knowledge-Domain|Knowledge]] god → scholar-priest

### Relationship with Your Deity

**How Did You Enter Service?**

**Option 1 - Willing Service**:
- Chose to serve the deity
- Devoted follower
- Embraced the calling

**Option 2 - Chosen by God**:
- God selected you
- Impelled into service
- No regard for your wishes
- Reluctant champion

### Standing in Your Faith

**Temple Priests' View of You**:

**Champion**: 
- Respected and honored
- Model cleric
- Trusted representative

**Troublemaker**:
- Unconventional methods
- Questions authority
- Gets results but causes problems

### Goals and Purpose

**Ultimate Goals**: What do you hope to achieve?

**Divine Task**: Does your deity have a special mission for you?

**Proving Yourself**: Are you striving to prove worthy of a great quest?

**Key Concept**: Creating a cleric requires defining your relationship with your deity—whether you chose them or they chose you, how your faith views you, and what divine purpose drives you forward.
