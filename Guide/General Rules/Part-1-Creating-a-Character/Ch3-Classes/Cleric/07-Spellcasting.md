# Cleric - Spellcasting

## Original Text

As a conduit for divine power, you can cast cleric spells. See chapter 10 for the general rules of spellcasting and chapter 11 for a selection of cleric spells.

## Cantrips

At 1st level, you know three cantrips of your choice from the cleric spell list. You learn additional cleric cantrips of your choice at higher levels, as shown in the Cantrips Known column of the Cleric table.

## Preparing and Casting Spells

The Cleric table shows how many spell slots you have to cast your cleric spells of 1st level and higher. To cast one of these spells, you must expend a slot of the spell's level or higher. You regain all expended spell slots when you finish a long rest.

You prepare the list of cleric spells that are available for you to cast, choosing from the cleric spell list. When you do so, choose a number of cleric spells equal to your Wisdom modifier + your cleric level (minimum of one spell). The spells must be of a level for which you have spell slots.

For example, if you are a 3rd-level cleric, you have four 1st-level and two 2nd-level spell slots. With a Wisdom of 16, your list of prepared spells can include six spells of 1st or 2nd level, in any combination. If you prepare the 1st-level spell [[Cure Wounds|cure wounds]], you can cast it using a 1st-level or 2nd-level slot. Casting the spell doesn't remove it from your list of prepared spells.

You can change your list of prepared spells when you finish a long rest. Preparing a new list of cleric spells requires time spent in prayer and meditation: at least 1 minute per spell level for each spell on your list.

## Spellcasting Ability

Wisdom is your spellcasting ability for your cleric spells. The power of your spells comes from your devotion to your deity. You use your Wisdom whenever a cleric spell refers to your spellcasting ability. In addition, you use your Wisdom modifier when setting the saving throw DC for a cleric spell you cast and when making an attack roll with one.

**Spell save DC** = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Wisdom modifier

**Spell attack modifier** = your proficiency bonus + your Wisdom modifier

## Ritual Casting

You can cast a cleric spell as a ritual if that spell has the ritual tag and you have the spell prepared.

## Spellcasting Focus

You can use a holy symbol (found in chapter 5) as a spellcasting focus for your cleric spells.

## Condensed Version

**Cleric Spellcasting**: Divine magic channeled through devotion to your deity.

### Cantrips (0-Level Spells)

**At 1st Level**: Know 3 cantrips from cleric spell list
**Higher Levels**: Learn more (see Cleric table)
**Always Available**: Cast at will, no spell slots needed

### Spell Slots and Casting

**Spell Slots**: Limited uses per day (see Cleric table)
- Cast spells of 1st level and higher
- Expend slot of spell's level or higher
- Regain all slots after long rest

### Preparing Spells

**Number of Prepared Spells**: Wisdom modifier + cleric level (minimum 1)
- Example: 3rd-level cleric, Wis 16 (+3) = 6 prepared spells

**Preparation Rules**:
- Choose from cleric spell list
- Must have spell slots for that level
- Can mix spell levels (e.g., four 1st-level, two 2nd-level)

**Flexibility**:
- Prepared spells don't get "used up" when cast
- Can cast same spell multiple times (if you have slots)
- Example: Prepare [[Cure Wounds|cure wounds]] once, cast it multiple times

**Changing Prepared Spells**:
- Change list after long rest
- Requires prayer/meditation: 1 minute per spell level per spell
- Example: Preparing six 1st-level spells = 6 minutes

### Spellcasting Ability: Wisdom

**Why Wisdom**: Power comes from devotion to deity

**Spell Save DC**: 8 + [[03-Proficiency-Bonus|proficiency bonus]] + Wisdom modifier
- Enemies roll against this when resisting your spells

**Spell Attack Modifier**: Proficiency bonus + Wisdom modifier
- Add to d20 when making spell attacks

**Example** (3rd-level cleric, Wis 16, Prof +2):
- Spell save DC: 8 + 2 + 3 = 13
- Spell attack: +2 + 3 = +5

### Ritual Casting

**Can Cast as Ritual** if:
- Spell has ritual tag
- You have the spell prepared
- Takes 10 minutes longer
- Doesn't use spell slot

### Spellcasting Focus

**Holy Symbol**: Use as focus for cleric spells
- Replaces material components (unless they have a cost)
- Must hold, wear visibly, or bear on shield

**Key Concept**: Clerics prepare spells daily from their full spell list, cast using limited spell slots, and use Wisdom for all spell effects. They're flexible casters who can adapt their prepared spells each day.

*See Chapter 10 for spellcasting rules, Chapter 11 for cleric spell list*
