# Cleric - Divine Agents

## Original Text

Not every acolyte or officiant at a temple or shrine is a cleric. Some priests are called to a simple life of temple service, carrying out their gods' will through prayer and sacrifice, not by magic and strength of arms. In some cities, priesthood amounts to a political office, viewed as a stepping stone to higher positions of authority and involving no communion with a god at all. True clerics are rare in most hierarchies.

When a cleric takes up an adventuring life, it is usually because his or her god demands it. Pursuing the goals of the gods often involves braving dangers beyond the walls of civilization, smiting evil or seeking holy relics in ancient tombs. Many clerics are also expected to protect their deities' worshipers, which can mean fighting rampaging orcs, negotiating peace between warring nations, or sealing a portal that would allow a demon prince to enter the world.

Most adventuring clerics maintain some connection to established temples and orders of their faiths. A temple might ask for a cleric's aid, or a high priest might be in a position to demand it.

## Condensed Version

**Clerics vs. Priests**: Not all religious figures are clerics—true clerics are rare.

### Types of Religious Figures

**Ordinary Priests**:
- Called to simple temple service
- Carry out gods' will through prayer and sacrifice
- No magic or combat abilities
- Most common in temples

**Political Priests**:
- Priesthood as political office
- Stepping stone to higher authority
- No actual communion with gods
- Found in some cities

**True Clerics** (Rare):
- Chosen by gods for divine power
- Imbued with magic and combat ability
- Rare even within religious hierarchies

### Why Clerics Adventure

**Divine Mandate**: Usually because their god demands it

**Common Divine Goals**:
- Brave dangers beyond civilization
- Smite evil
- Seek holy relics in ancient tombs
- Protect deity's worshipers

**Protection Duties** (examples):
- Fight rampaging orcs
- Negotiate peace between warring nations
- Seal portals to prevent demon invasions

### Connection to Faith

**Maintain Ties**: Most adventuring clerics stay connected to:
- Established temples
- Religious orders of their faith

**Requests for Aid**:
- Temples may ask for help
- High priests may demand service
- Clerics answer to religious authority

**Key Concept**: Clerics are rare, divinely chosen champions distinct from ordinary priests. They adventure at their god's command to pursue divine goals, protect worshipers, and combat threats to their faith, while maintaining ties to their religious institutions.
