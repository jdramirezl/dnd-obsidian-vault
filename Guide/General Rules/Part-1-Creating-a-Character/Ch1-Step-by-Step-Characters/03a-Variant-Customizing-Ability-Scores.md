# Variant: Customizing Ability Scores

## Original Text

At your Dungeon Master's option, you can use this variant for determining your ability scores. The method described here allows you to build a character with a set of ability scores you choose individually.

You have 27 points to spend on your ability scores. The cost of each score is shown on the Ability Score Point Cost table. For example, a score of 14 costs 7 points. Using this method, 15 is the highest ability score you can end up with, before applying racial increases. You can't have a score lower than 8.

This method of determining ability scores enables you to create a set of three high numbers and three low ones (15, 15, 15, 8, 8, 8), a set of numbers that are above average and nearly equal (13, 13, 13, 12, 12, 12), or any set of numbers between those extremes.

**Ability Score Point Cost**

| Score | Cost |
|-------|------|
| 8     | 0    |
| 9     | 1    |
| 10    | 2    |
| 11    | 3    |
| 12    | 4    |
| 13    | 5    |
| 14    | 7    |
| 15    | 9    |

## Condensed Version

**Point Buy System**: An optional method for creating [[01-Ability-Scores-and-Modifiers|ability scores]] with more control (requires DM approval).

**How It Works**:
- You have **27 points** to spend
- Each ability score has a point cost (see table below)
- Minimum score: **8** (costs 0 points)
- Maximum score: **15** (costs 9 points, before racial bonuses)

### Point Cost Table

| Score | Cost | Score | Cost |
|-------|------|-------|------|
| 8     | 0    | 12    | 4    |
| 9     | 1    | 13    | 5    |
| 10    | 2    | 14    | 7    |
| 11    | 3    | 15    | 9    |

**Example Builds**:
- **Specialized**: 15, 15, 15, 8, 8, 8 (three high, three low)
- **Balanced**: 13, 13, 13, 12, 12, 12 (all above average)
- **Custom**: Any combination between these extremes

**Advantages**:
- More control over your character's strengths/weaknesses
- Can create specific character concepts
- No randomness—predictable results

**Disadvantages**:
- Less exciting than rolling
- Can't get scores above 15 before racial bonuses
- Requires more planning

**Key Concept**: Point buy trades randomness for control, letting you precisely craft your character's ability score array within defined limits.
