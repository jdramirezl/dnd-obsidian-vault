# Step 6: Come Together

## Original Text

Most D&D characters don't work alone. Each character plays a role within a party, a group of adventurers working together for a common purpose. Teamwork and cooperation greatly improve your party's chances to survive the many perils in the worlds of Dungeons & Dragons. Talk to your fellow players and your DM to decide whether your characters know one another, how they met, and what sorts of quests the group might undertake.

---

## Condensed Version

**Party Formation**: D&D characters work in groups called parties—adventurers cooperating toward common goals.

**Why Teamwork Matters**: Cooperation greatly improves survival chances against the dangers of D&D worlds.

**Questions to Discuss with Your Group**:
1. **Do your characters know each other?**
   - Old friends?
   - Just met?
   - Mixed (some know each other, some don't)?

2. **How did they meet?**
   - Shared background?
   - Chance encounter?
   - Hired for the same job?

3. **What brings them together?**
   - Common goal?
   - Shared enemy?
   - Complementary skills?

4. **What quests might the group undertake?**
   - What motivates the party as a whole?
   - What are their shared objectives?

**Collaborative Process**: Talk with fellow players and the DM to establish party dynamics and shared history.

**Key Concept**: D&D is a team game. Establishing how your characters know each other and why they work together creates stronger party cohesion and better roleplaying opportunities.
