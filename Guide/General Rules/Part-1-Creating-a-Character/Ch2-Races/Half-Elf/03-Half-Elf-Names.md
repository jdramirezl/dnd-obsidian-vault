# Half-Elf Names

## Original Text

Half-elves use either human or elven naming conventions. As if to emphasize that they don't really fit in to either society, half-elves raised among humans are often given elven names, and those raised among elves often take human names.


## Condensed Version

**Half-Elf Naming Convention**: Half-elves borrow from both human and elven naming traditions.

### Naming Practices

**General Rule**: Use either human or elven naming conventions

**The Ironic Pattern**:
- Half-elves raised among **humans** → often given **elven** names
- Half-elves raised among **elves** → often take **human** names
- Emphasizes that they don't truly fit into either society

### Name Sources

**For Elven Names**: See [[05-Elf-Names|Elf Names]]
- Child names, adult names, family names
- Elven naming traditions and conventions

**For Human Names**: See [[06-Human-Names-and-Ethnicities-Intro|Human Names and Ethnicities]]
- Names vary by human ethnicity and culture
- Wide variety of naming traditions

**Key Concept**: Half-elf naming reflects their dual identity. The tendency to receive names from the "other" parent's culture highlights their perpetual outsider status — always marked as different regardless of which society raises them.
