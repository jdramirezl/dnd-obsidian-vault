# Half-Elf - Diplomats and Wanderers

## Original Text

Half-elves have no lands of their own, though they are welcome in human cities and somewhat less welcome in elven forests. In large cities in regions where elves and humans interact often, half-elves are sometimes numerous enough to form small communities of their own. They enjoy the company of other half-elves, the only people who truly understand what it is to live between these two worlds.

In most parts of the world, though, half-elves are uncommon enough that one might live for years without meeting another. Some half-elves prefer to avoid company altogether, wandering the wilds as trappers, foresters, hunters, or adventurers and visiting civilization only rarely. Like elves, they are driven by the wanderlust that comes of their longevity. Others, in contrast, throw themselves into the thick of society, putting their charisma and social skills to great use in diplomatic roles or as swindlers, con artists, smugglers, or other such occupations.

## Excellent Ambassadors

Many half-elves learn at an early age to get along with everyone, defusing hostility and finding common ground. As a race, they have elven grace without elven aloofness and human energy without human boorishness. They often make excellent ambassadors and go-betweens (except between elves and humans, since each side suspects the half-elf of favoring the other).


## Condensed Version

**Half-Elf Society**: A people without a homeland who thrive as diplomats, wanderers, and go-betweens.

### Homeland and Community

**No Lands of Their Own**:
- Welcome in human cities
- Somewhat less welcome in elven forests
- No half-elf nation or territory

**Half-Elf Communities**:
- Form in large cities where elves and humans interact often
- Enjoy company of other half-elves
- Only people who truly understand living between two worlds

**Rarity**:
- In most of the world, half-elves are uncommon
- One might live for years without meeting another

### Two Paths

**The Wanderer**:
- Avoid company altogether
- Wander the wilds as trappers, foresters, hunters, adventurers
- Visit civilization only rarely
- Driven by wanderlust from their longevity (like elves)

**The Socialite**:
- Throw themselves into the thick of society
- Put charisma and social skills to great use
- **Legitimate roles**: Diplomats, ambassadors, go-betweens
- **Less legitimate roles**: Swindlers, con artists, smugglers

### Excellent Ambassadors

**Natural Diplomats**:
- Learn at an early age to get along with everyone
- Defuse hostility and find common ground
- **Elven grace** without elven aloofness
- **Human energy** without human boorishness

**The Ironic Exception**:
- Make excellent go-betweens for most groups
- **Except** between elves and humans
- Each side suspects the half-elf of favoring the other

**Key Concept**: Half-elves' outsider status becomes their greatest strength in social situations. They blend the best social qualities of both parents — elven grace and human energy — making them natural diplomats, though their dual heritage can also be a source of suspicion.
