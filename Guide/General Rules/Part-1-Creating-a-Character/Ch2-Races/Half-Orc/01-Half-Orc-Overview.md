# Half-Orc - Overview

![Half-Orc](https://www.dndbeyond.com/avatars/thumbnails/6/466/420/618/636274570630462055.png)

## Original Text

The warchief Mhurren roused himself from his sleeping-Loss and pulled a fur cloak tightly about his broad shoulders. Like most of the stronghold's people, he was a half-orc, born to a human slave and an orc warrior during one of the tribe's decadent victory celebrations. He had the pointed ears and prominent lower canines of his orc father, and the tall, powerful build of his human mother. He was proud of his orc blood and suspicious of his human heritage, for though he was the strongest of the tribe's warriors, a few of the orcs in the stronghold were bigger and stronger than he.
—Richard Baker, Swordmage

Whether united under the leadership of a mighty warlock or having fought to a standstill after years of conflict, orc and human tribes sometimes form alliances, joining forces into a larger horde to the terror of civilized lands nearby. When these alliances are sealed by marriages, half-orcs are born. Some half-orcs rise to become proud chiefs of orc tribes, their human blood giving them an edge over their full-blooded orc rivals. Some venture into the world to prove their worth among humans and other more civilized races. Many of these become adventurers, achieving greatness for their mighty deeds and notoriety for their barbaric customs and savage fury.

## Scarred and Strong

Half-orcs' grayish pigmentation, sloping foreheads, jutting jaws, prominent teeth, and towering builds make their orcish heritage plain for all to see. Half-orcs stand between 5 and 7 feet tall and usually weigh between 180 and 250 pounds.

Orcs regard battle scars as tokens of pride and ornamental scars as things of beauty. Other scars, though, mark an orc or half-orc as a former slave or a disgraced exile. Any half-orc who has lived among or near orcs has scars, whether they are marks of humiliation or of pride, recounting their past exploits and injuries. Such a half-orc living among humans might display these scars proudly or hide them in shame.


## Condensed Version

**Half-Orc Identity**: Born of alliances between orc and human tribes, half-orcs combine orcish strength and ferocity with human cunning and ambition.

### Origins

**How Half-Orcs Are Born**:
- Orc and human tribes sometimes form alliances
- United under a [[01-Warlock-Overview|warlock]]'s leadership or after fighting to a standstill
- Alliances sealed by marriages produce half-orcs
- Also born from conquest (human slaves and orc warriors)

**Paths in Life**:
- Some rise to become proud chiefs of orc tribes
  - Human blood gives them an edge over full-blooded orc rivals
- Some venture into the world to prove their worth among humans
- Many become adventurers
  - Achieve greatness for mighty deeds
  - Gain notoriety for barbaric customs and savage fury

### Physical Characteristics

**Build**:
- **Height**: 5 to 7 feet tall
- **Weight**: 180 to 250 pounds
- **Build**: Towering, powerful

**Appearance**:
- Grayish pigmentation
- Sloping foreheads
- Jutting jaws
- Prominent teeth (lower canines)
- Pointed ears
- Orcish heritage plain for all to see

### Scars and Culture

**Battle Scars**: Tokens of pride among orcs
**Ornamental Scars**: Considered things of beauty
**Slave/Exile Scars**: Mark of humiliation and disgrace

**The Scar Dilemma**:
- Any half-orc raised among orcs has scars
- Scars recount past exploits and injuries
- Among humans: might display proudly or hide in shame
- Scars tell the story of a half-orc's life

**Key Concept**: Half-orcs are defined by the tension between their orcish and human heritage. Their imposing physical presence and scarred bodies tell the story of lives lived between two very different cultures — one that values savage strength, another that often fears it.
