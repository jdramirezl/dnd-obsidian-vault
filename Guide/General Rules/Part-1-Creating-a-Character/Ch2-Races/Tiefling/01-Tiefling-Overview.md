# Tiefling - Overview

## Original Text

"But you do see the way people look at you, devil's child."

Those black eyes, cold as a winter storm, were staring right into her heart and the sudden seriousness in his voice jolted her.

"What is it they say?" he asked. "One's a curiosity, two's a conspiracy—"

"Three's a curse," she finished. "You think I haven't heard that rubbish before?"

"I know you have." When she glared at him, he added, "It's not as if I'm plumbing the depths of your soul. Everyone knows what Farideh is. Even walking ugly, people assume the worst of you. Ugly, and distasteful."

But ugly was distasteful. It was ugly.

"You know," she said, "most of the people who stare at me have never seen a tiefling before. You know what they think? They think I'm some kind of devil."

—Erin M. Evans, Brimstone Angels

To be greeted with stares and whispers, to suffer violence and insult on the street, to see mistrust and fear in every eye: this is the lot of the tiefling. And to twist the knife, tieflings know that this is because a pact struck generations ago infused the essence of Asmodeus—overlord of the Nine Hells—into their bloodline. Their appearance and their nature are not their fault but the result of an ancient sin, one that will haunt them and their children and their children's children for all time.

## Infernal Bloodline

Their infernal heritage has left a clear imprint on tieflings, giving them an appearance that often causes fear and mistrust. Tieflings have large horns that take any of a variety of shapes: some have curling horns like a ram, others have straight and tall horns like a gazelle's, and some spiral upward like an antelopes' horns. They have thick tails, four to five feet long, which lash or coil around their legs when they get upset or nervous. Their canine teeth are sharply pointed, and their eyes are solid colors—black, red, white, silver, or gold—with no visible sclera or pupil. Their skin tones cover the full range of human coloration, but also include various shades of red. Their hair, cascading down from behind their horns, is usually dark, from black to dark brown, though dark reds or blues are also found.


## Condensed Version

**Tiefling Identity**: Humanoids bearing the mark of an infernal pact — descendants of a bloodline touched by Asmodeus, overlord of the Nine Hells.

### The Tiefling Burden

**Social Reality**:
- Greeted with stares and whispers
- Suffer violence and insult on the street
- See mistrust and fear in every eye

**Root Cause**:
- A pact struck generations ago infused the essence of Asmodeus into their bloodline
- Their appearance and nature are not their fault
- Result of an ancient sin
- Haunts them, their children, and their children's children for all time

### Physical Characteristics

**Horns** (large, varied shapes):
- Curling like a ram
- Straight and tall like a gazelle
- Spiraling upward like an antelope

**Tail**:
- Thick, four to five feet long
- Lashes or coils around legs when upset or nervous

**Teeth**: Canine teeth sharply pointed

**Eyes**: Solid colors with no visible sclera or pupil
- Black, red, white, silver, or gold

**Skin**: Full range of human coloration, plus various shades of red

**Hair**: Cascades from behind horns
- Usually dark (black to dark brown)
- Dark reds or blues also found

**Key Concept**: Tieflings are defined by their infernal heritage — an ancient pact with Asmodeus left a visible, permanent mark on their bloodline. They bear horns, tails, and solid-colored eyes that mark them as "devil's children" in the eyes of the world, suffering prejudice for a sin that was never theirs.
