# Tiefling - Mutual Mistrust (Sidebar)

## Original Text

People tend to be suspicious of tieflings, assuming that their infernal heritage has left its mark on their personality and morality, not just their appearance. Shopkeepers keep a close eye on their goods when tieflings enter their store, the town watch might follow a tiefling around for a while, and demagogues blame tieflings for strange happenings. The reality, though, is that a tiefling's bloodline doesn't affect his or her personality to any great degree. Years of dealing with mistrust does leave its mark, however, and tieflings respond to it in different ways. Some choose to live up to the wicked stereotype, finding it the easiest path through a hostile world. Others are virtuous, working hard to prove their goodness and demonstrate that they are not defined by their ancestry. Most are simply trying to find their way in the world, neither wholly good nor wholly evil, like members of any other race.


## Condensed Version

**Mutual Mistrust**: How the world treats tieflings, and how tieflings respond.

### How Others Treat Tieflings

**Assumption**: Infernal heritage has marked their personality and morality, not just appearance

**Examples of Prejudice**:
- Shopkeepers watch their goods closely when tieflings enter
- Town watch follows tieflings around
- Demagogues blame tieflings for strange happenings

### The Reality

**Bloodline ≠ Personality**: A tiefling's infernal heritage does not meaningfully affect personality or morality.

**What Does Leave a Mark**: Years of dealing with mistrust shapes tieflings in different ways.

### Three Tiefling Responses to Prejudice

1. **Embrace the Stereotype**: Live up to the wicked reputation
   - Find it the easiest path through a hostile world
   - Why fight expectations when you can use them?

2. **Prove Their Virtue**: Work hard to demonstrate goodness
   - Actively counter the stereotype
   - Show they are not defined by ancestry

3. **Simply Live**: Find their way like anyone else
   - Neither wholly good nor wholly evil
   - Like members of any other race
   - **This is the most common response**

**Key Concept**: The prejudice tieflings face is based on a false assumption — their bloodline does not determine their character. But the experience of constant mistrust does shape them, pushing tieflings toward defiance, virtue, or simply getting by.
