# Dwarf - Gods, Gold, and Clan

## Original Text

Dwarves who take up the adventuring life might be motivated by a desire for treasure—for its own sake, for a specific purpose, or even out of an altruistic desire to help others. Other dwarves are driven by the command or inspiration of a deity, a direct calling or simply a desire to bring glory to one of the dwarf gods. Clan and ancestry are also important motivators. A dwarf might seek to restore a clan's lost honor, avenge an ancient wrong the clan suffered, or earn a new place within the clan after having been exiled. Or a dwarf might search for the axe wielded by a mighty ancestor, lost on the field of battle centuries ago.


## Condensed Version

**Why Dwarves Adventure**: Common motivations that drive dwarves to leave their kingdoms and take up the adventuring life.

### Treasure Motivations

**For Its Own Sake**: Simple desire for wealth and riches

**For a Purpose**: Seeking treasure to accomplish a specific goal

**Altruistic**: Desire to help others through wealth

### Religious Motivations

**Divine Command**: Direct calling from a deity

**Divine Inspiration**: Inspired by a deity's teachings

**Bringing Glory**: Desire to honor the dwarf gods through deeds

### Clan and Ancestry Motivations

**Restore Honor**: Seeking to restore a clan's lost honor

**Avenge Wrongs**: Avenging ancient wrongs suffered by the clan

**Earn a Place**: Proving worth after exile or seeking new standing

**Recover Heirlooms**: Searching for ancestral [[03-Weapons|weapons]] or artifacts
- Example: Finding a mighty ancestor's axe lost in battle centuries ago

**Key Concept**: Dwarven adventurers are typically motivated by treasure, divine calling, or clan-related goals. These motivations tie back to core dwarven values: wealth, faith, and family honor.
