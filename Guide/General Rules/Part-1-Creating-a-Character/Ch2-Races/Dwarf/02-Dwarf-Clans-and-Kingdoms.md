z# Dwarf - Clans and Kingdoms

## Original Text

Dwarven kingdoms stretch deep beneath the mountains where the dwarves mine gems and precious metals and forge items of wonder. They love the beauty and artistry of precious metals and fine jewelry, and in some dwarves this love festers into avarice. Whatever wealth they can't find in their mountains, they gain through trade. They dislike boats, so enterprising humans and halflings frequently handle trade in dwarven goods along water routes. Trustworthy members of other races are welcome in dwarf settlements, though some areas are off limits even to them.

The chief unit of dwarven society is the clan, and dwarves highly value social standing. Even dwarves who live far from their own kingdoms cherish their clan identities and affiliations, recognize related dwarves, and invoke their ancestors' names in oaths and curses. To be clanless is the worst fate that can befall a dwarf.

Dwarves in other lands are typically artisans, especially weaponsmiths, armorers, and jewelers. Some become mercenaries or bodyguards, highly sought after for their courage and loyalty.


## Condensed Version

**Dwarven Kingdoms**: Underground realms beneath mountains where dwarves mine, forge, and create wonders.

### Economy and Trade

**Primary Activities**:
- Mining gems and precious metals
- Forging items of wonder
- Creating fine jewelry

**Relationship with Wealth**:
- Love beauty and artistry of precious metals
- Some dwarves: Love becomes avarice (greed)
- Trade for wealth they can't mine

**Trade Practices**:
- **Dislike boats**: Avoid water travel
- **Trade partners**: Humans and halflings handle water-route trade for dwarven goods
- **Visitors**: Trustworthy outsiders welcome, but some areas restricted

### Social Structure

**The Clan System**:
- **Chief unit**: Clan is the fundamental social organization
- **Social standing**: Highly valued within clan structure
- **Clan identity**: Even dwarves living far from home:
  - Cherish clan identities
  - Recognize related dwarves
  - Invoke ancestors' names in oaths and curses

**Worst Fate**: Being clanless—the ultimate dishonor and isolation

### Dwarves Abroad

**Common Professions** (when living in other lands):
- **Artisans**: Especially weaponsmiths, armorers, jewelers
- **Mercenaries**: Hired warriors
- **Bodyguards**: Sought after for courage and loyalty

**Key Concept**: Dwarven society centers on underground kingdoms and clan identity. Their craftsmanship is legendary, and clan affiliation is so important that being clanless is considered the worst possible fate.
