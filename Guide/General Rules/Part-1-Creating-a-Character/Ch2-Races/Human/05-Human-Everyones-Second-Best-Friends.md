# Human - Everyone's Second-Best Friends (Relations with Other Races)

## Original Text

Just as readily as they mix with each other, humans mingle with members of other races. They get along with almost everyone, though they might not be close to many. Humans serve as ambassadors, diplomats, magistrates, merchants, and functionaries of all kinds.

**Dwarves.** "They're stout folk, stalwart friends, and true to their word. Their greed for gold is their downfall, though."

**Elves.** "It's best not to wander into elven woods. They don't like intruders, and you'll as likely be bewitched as peppered with arrows. Still, if an elf can get past that damned racial pride and actually treat you like an equal, you can learn a lot from them."

**Halflings.** "It's hard to beat a meal in a halfling home, as long as you don't crack your head on the ceiling—good food and good stories in front of a nice, warm fire. If halflings had a shred of ambition, they might really amount to something."


## Condensed Version

**Human Social Nature**: Mix readily with all races, get along with almost everyone (though not necessarily close to many).

**Diplomatic Roles**: Humans commonly serve as:
- Ambassadors
- Diplomats
- Magistrates
- Merchants
- Functionaries of all kinds

### Human Views on Other Races

**Dwarves** (Reliable but Greedy):

**Appreciation**:
- Stout folk
- Stalwart friends
- True to their word (trustworthy)

**Criticism**:
- "Their greed for gold is their downfall"
- Too focused on wealth

**Elves** (Dangerous but Knowledgeable):

**Warning**: "Best not to wander into elven woods"
- Don't like intruders
- Might bewitch you or shoot you with arrows
- Territorial and dangerous

**Potential**: If elves can get past their racial pride:
- Treat you as an equal
- Can learn a lot from them
- Valuable knowledge and skills

**Frustration**: "That damned racial pride" gets in the way

**Halflings** (Hospitable but Unambitious):

**Appreciation**:
- Great hospitality
- Good food and good stories
- Warm, welcoming homes
- Comfortable atmosphere

**Practical Note**: "Don't crack your head on the ceiling" (homes built for Small creatures)

**Criticism**:
- "If halflings had a shred of ambition, they might really amount to something"
- Lack drive to achieve greatness
- Content with simple lives

**Key Concept**: Humans view other races pragmatically, appreciating their strengths (dwarf reliability, elf knowledge, halfling hospitality) while noting their flaws (dwarf greed, elf pride, halfling lack of ambition). They get along with everyone but maintain some emotional distance.
