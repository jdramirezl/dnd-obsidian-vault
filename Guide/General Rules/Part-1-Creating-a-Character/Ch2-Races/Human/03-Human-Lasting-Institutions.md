# Human - Lasting Institutions

## Original Text

Where a single elf or dwarf might take on the responsibility of guarding a special location or a powerful secret, humans found sacred orders and institutions for such purposes. While dwarf clans and halfling elders pass on the ancient traditions to each new generation, human temples, governments, libraries, and codes of law fix their traditions in the bedrock of history. Humans dream of immortality, but (except for those few who seek undeath or divine ascension to escape death's clutches) they achieve it by ensuring that they will be remembered when they are gone.

Although some humans can be xenophobic, in general their societies are inclusive. Human lands welcome large numbers of nonhumans compared to the proportion of humans who live in nonhuman lands.


## Condensed Version

**Human Institutions**: Humans create organizations and structures to preserve knowledge and traditions beyond individual lifespans.

### Institutional vs. Individual Guardianship

**Other Races** (Individual Approach):
- **Elves/Dwarves**: Single individual guards locations or secrets
- **Dwarves**: Clans pass traditions person-to-person
- **Halflings**: Elders pass traditions to next generation
- Relies on long-lived individuals

**Humans** (Institutional Approach):
- Found sacred orders and institutions
- Create organizations that outlast individuals
- Examples:
  - Temples
  - Governments
  - Libraries
  - Codes of law

**Why**: Fix traditions in "bedrock of history" through written records and formal structures

### Quest for Immortality

**The Dream**: Humans dream of immortality

**Rare Paths**:
- Undeath (becoming undead)
- Divine ascension (becoming a god)
- Few achieve these

**Common Path**: Achieve immortality through legacy
- Ensure they will be remembered
- Leave lasting impact on the world
- Live on through institutions and memory

### Inclusive Societies

**General Attitude**: Human societies are inclusive (though some individuals are xenophobic)

**Evidence**: 
- Human lands welcome large numbers of nonhumans
- More nonhumans live in human lands than humans in nonhuman lands
- Proportionally more diverse than other races' territories

**Key Concept**: Humans compensate for short lifespans by creating institutions that preserve their knowledge and traditions. They seek immortality through legacy and memory rather than extended life, and their societies are notably inclusive of other races.
