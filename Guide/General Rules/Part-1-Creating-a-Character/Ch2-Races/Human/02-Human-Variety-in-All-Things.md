# Human - Variety in All Things

## Original Text

Humans are the most adaptable and ambitious people among the common races. They have widely varying tastes, morals, and customs in the many different lands where they have settled. When they settle, though, they stay: they build cities to last for the ages, and great kingdoms that can persist for long centuries. An individual human might have a relatively short life span, but a human nation or culture preserves traditions with origins far beyond the reach of any single human's memory. They live fully in the present—making them well suited to the adventuring life—but also plan for the future, striving to leave a lasting legacy. Individually and as a group, humans are adaptable opportunists, and they stay alert to changing political and social dynamics.


## Condensed Version

**Human Adaptability**: Most adaptable and ambitious of the common races.

### Cultural Diversity

**Varying Across Lands**:
- Widely varying tastes
- Different morals
- Diverse customs
- Each settlement develops unique culture

### Permanence Despite Short Lives

**When Humans Settle**:
- Build cities to last for ages
- Create great kingdoms persisting for centuries
- Construct lasting institutions

**Paradox**:
- **Individual**: Short lifespan (under 100 years)
- **Collective**: Nations and cultures preserve traditions for millennia
- Traditions outlast any single human's memory

### Temporal Perspective

**Live in the Present**:
- Fully engaged with current moment
- Makes them well-suited for adventuring
- Seize opportunities as they arise

**Plan for the Future**:
- Strive to leave lasting legacies
- Build for generations to come
- Think beyond their own lifetimes

### Adaptability

**Opportunists**:
- Individually adaptable
- Collectively adaptable
- Alert to changing political and social dynamics
- Quick to adjust to new circumstances

**Key Concept**: Humans balance living in the moment with planning for the future. Their short individual lives drive both immediate action and the creation of lasting institutions that preserve their cultures across centuries.
