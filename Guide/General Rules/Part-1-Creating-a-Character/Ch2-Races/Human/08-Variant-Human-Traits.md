# Variant Human Traits

## Original Text

If your campaign uses the optional feat rules from chapter 6 of the Player's Handbook, your Dungeon Master might allow these variant traits, all of which replace the human's Ability Score Increase trait.

**Ability Score Increase.** Two different ability scores of your choice increase by 1.

**Skills.** You gain proficiency in one skill of your choice.

**Feat.** You gain one feat of your choice.


## Condensed Version

**Variant Human**: Optional rule that trades broad ability increases for focused bonuses and a feat (requires DM approval).

### Requirements

**Campaign Must Use**: Optional feat rules from Player's Handbook Chapter 6

**DM Approval**: Required to use this variant

### Variant Traits (Replace Standard Human)

**Standard Human** (for comparison):
- All [[01-Ability-Scores-and-Modifiers|ability scores]] +1
- One extra language

**Variant Human** (replaces above):

**1. Ability Score Increase**
- Choose **two different** ability scores
- Each increases by +1
- More focused than standard human

**2. Skills**
- Gain proficiency in **one skill** of your choice
- Any skill from the skill list

**3. Feat**
- Gain **one feat** of your choice
- Feats are powerful abilities (see Chapter 6)
- Normally only available at higher levels

### Comparison

**Standard Human**:
- +1 to all six abilities (versatile)
- Extra language
- No feat

**Variant Human**:
- +1 to only two abilities (focused)
- No extra language
- Skill proficiency
- **Feat at 1st level** (major advantage)

### Why Choose Variant?

**Advantages**:
- Feat at 1st level (normally unavailable until 4th level)
- Skill proficiency adds versatility
- Can create more specialized builds

**Disadvantages**:
- Only +2 total to abilities (vs +6 for standard)
- Lose extra language
- Less well-rounded

**Key Concept**: Variant human trades the standard human's broad versatility for focused ability increases, a skill, and most importantly, a feat at 1st level—allowing unique character builds not possible with other races.
