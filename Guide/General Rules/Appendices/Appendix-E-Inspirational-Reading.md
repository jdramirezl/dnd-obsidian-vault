# Appendix E: Inspirational Reading

## Original Text

Dungeons & Dragons owes much to the writers and game designers who came before. The following list includes many of the authors whose work has inspired and shaped the game over the decades. This reading list, sometimes called "Appendix N" after its original appearance in the 1st Edition *Dungeon Master's Guide*, is a starting point for anyone interested in the fiction that helped create D&D.

## Condensed Version

The PHB's "Appendix E" (historically known as "Appendix N") lists fiction that inspired D&D. Key highlights:

**Foundational influences:** Tolkien (*Lord of the Rings*), Howard (*Conan*), Leiber (*Fafhrd & Gray Mouser*), Vance (*Dying Earth* — the basis for D&D's magic system), Lovecraft, Moorcock (*Elric*), and Anderson (*Three Hearts and Three Lions* — the basis for D&D's alignment and paladin).

**Classic fantasy/sci-fi:** LeGuin, Zelazny, Wolfe, Dunsany, Merritt, Norton, Brackett, de Camp & Pratt, Bellairs, Peake.

**Modern additions (5e):** Jemisin, Sanderson, Rothfuss, Lynch, Miéville, Bear, Ahmed, Martin — reflecting the genre's evolution since the original list.

**D&D fiction:** Salvatore (*Drizzt*), Hickman & Weis (*Dragonlance*).

The list serves as a reading guide for players and DMs who want to explore the literary roots of the game.
