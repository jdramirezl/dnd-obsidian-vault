# Appendix C: The Planes of Existence

## Original Text

The cosmos teems with a multitude of worlds as well as myriad alternate dimensions of reality, called the **planes of existence**. They encompass every possible reality — from the familiar Material Plane to the bizarre and alien Far Realm. The most well-known planes are a group of sixteen Outer Planes that correspond to the eight alignments and the shades of distinction between them.

### The Material Plane

The Material Plane is the nexus where the philosophical and elemental forces that define the other planes collide in the jumbled existence of mortal life and mundane matter. All the worlds of D&D exist within the Material Plane, making it the starting point for most campaigns and adventures. The rest of the multiverse is defined in relation to the Material Plane.

### Transitive Planes

The **Ethereal Plane** and the **Astral Plane** are called the Transitive Planes. They are mostly featureless realms that serve primarily as ways to travel from one plane to another.

- **The Ethereal Plane** is a misty, fog-bound dimension that is sometimes described as a great ocean. Its shores, called the Border Ethereal, overlap the Material Plane and the Inner Planes, so that every location on those planes has a corresponding location on the Ethereal Plane. Certain creatures can see into the Border Ethereal, and the *see invisibility* and *true seeing* spells grant that ability. Visibility in the Border Ethereal is limited to 60 feet. The plane's depths comprise a region of swirling mist and fog called the Deep Ethereal, where travelers can move from one Inner Plane to another.

- **The Astral Plane** is the realm of thought and dream, where visitors travel as disembodied souls to reach the planes of the divine and demonic. It is a great, silvery sea, the same above and below, with swirling wisps of white and gray streaking among motes of light resembling distant stars. Most of the Astral Sea is a vast, empty expanse. Visitors occasionally stumble upon the petrified corpse of a dead god or other chunks of rock drifting forever in the silvery void. Color pools — Loss of swirling color — are portals to the Outer Planes.

### Inner Planes

The Inner Planes surround and enfold the Material Plane and its echoes, providing the raw elemental substance from which all worlds were made.

**The four Elemental Planes:**
- **Plane of Air** — An open expanse of sky with constant winds, ranging from gentle breezes to raging hurricanes.
- **Plane of Earth** — A chain of mountains, caverns, and tunnels of solid stone, crushing weight, and suffocating earth.
- **Plane of Fire** — A landscape of blazing flames, searing heat, and rivers of magma.
- **Plane of Water** — An endless sea without surface or bottom, a domain of currents and crushing depths.

**The Energy Planes:**
- **Positive Plane** — A brilliant, blazing realm of radiant energy that overloads the senses. It is the source of life energy that animates all living beings.
- **Negative Plane** — A void of darkness and entropy, the source of the energy that powers undead creatures. It saps the life from anything that enters it.

### Outer Planes

The Outer Planes are realms of spirituality and thought. They are the spheres where celestials, fiends, and deities dwell. Each of the Outer Planes has a particular alignment, and a creature's alignment determines which Outer Plane its soul travels to after death.

### Feywild and Shadowfell

- **The Feywild**, also called the Plane of Faerie, is a land of soft lights and wonder, a place of music and death. It is a realm of eternal twilight, with slow lanterns bobbing in the gentle breeze and huge fireflies buzzing through groves and fields. The sky is alight with the faded colors of the setting — or perhaps rising — sun. The Feywild exists in parallel to the Material Plane as an echo of it, a twilight reflection.

- **The Shadowfell**, also called the Plane of Shadow, is a darkly lighted dimension that is a distorted reflection of the Material Plane. It is a place of darkness that hates the light, where the sky is a black vault with neither sun nor stars. The Shadowfell overlaps the Material Plane in much the same way as the Feywild.

### Other Planes

- **Demiplanes** are small extradimensional spaces with their own unique rules. They are pieces of reality that don't seem to fit anywhere else. Demiplanes come into being by a variety of means — some are created by spells, others exist naturally. A demiplane can be as small as a single room or large enough to contain an entire realm.

- **The Far Realm** is outside the known multiverse entirely. It is a realm utterly alien to everything in the known planes. The entities that abide in the Far Realm are too alien for a normal mind to accept without damage. Warlocks of the Great Old One patron might draw their power from the Far Realm.

- **Sigil, the City of Doors**, sits atop the Spire at the center of the Outlands. It is the greatest metropolis of the multiverse, a city of portals — doorways leading to every corner of the planes. Sigil is ruled by the mysterious Lady of Pain, a being of inscrutable motives.

- **The Outlands** is the plane between the Outer Planes. It is the plane of neutrality, incorporating a little of everything, keeping all in a paradoxical balance. The plane has varied terrain, with prairies, mountains, and shallow rivers. Gate-towns ring the Outlands, each built around a portal leading to one of the Outer Planes.

## Condensed Version

The D&D multiverse is organized into several categories of planes:

- **Material Plane** — The default world where adventures take place. All other planes are defined relative to it.
- **Transitive Planes** — The **Ethereal Plane** (misty realm overlapping the Material/Inner Planes, used for short-range planar travel) and the **Astral Plane** (silvery void of thought connecting to the Outer Planes via color pools).
- **Inner Planes** — Source of raw elemental matter: **Air**, **Earth**, **Fire**, **Water**, plus the **Positive** (life energy) and **Negative** (entropy/undeath) Energy Planes.
- **Outer Planes** — 16 planes aligned to the moral/ethical axes, home to gods, celestials, and fiends. Range from **Mount Celestia** (LG) through **Limbo** (CN) to **The Nine Hells** (LE) and **The Abyss** (CE).
- **Feywild** — Bright, enchanted echo of the Material Plane; land of fey creatures.
- **Shadowfell** — Dark, gloomy echo of the Material Plane; associated with undead and despair.
- **Demiplanes** — Small pocket dimensions with unique rules (e.g., Ravenloft).
- **Far Realm** — Utterly alien reality beyond the multiverse; source of aberrant horrors.
- **Sigil & the Outlands** — Sigil is the "City of Doors" atop the Spire, ruled by the Lady of Pain. The Outlands is the neutral plane at the center, with gate-towns linking to each Outer Plane.
