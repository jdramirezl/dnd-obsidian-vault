# Dexterity

## Original Text

Dexterity measures agility, reflexes, and balance.

### Dexterity Checks

A Dexterity check can model any attempt to move nimbly, quickly, or quietly, or to keep from falling on tricky footing. The Acrobatics, Sleight of Hand, and Stealth skills reflect aptitude in certain kinds of Dexterity checks.

**Acrobatics.** Your Dexterity (Acrobatics) check covers your attempt to stay on your feet in a tricky situation, such as when you're trying to run across a sheet of ice, balance on a tightrope, or stay upright on a rocking ship's deck. The DM might also call for a Dexterity (Acrobatics) check to see if you can perform acrobatic stunts, including dives, rolls, somersaults, and flips.

**Sleight of Hand.** Whenever you attempt an act of legerdemain or manual trickery, such as planting something on someone else or concealing an object on your person, make a Dexterity (Sleight of Hand) check. The DM might also call for a Dexterity (Sleight of Hand) check to determine whether you can lift a coin purse off another person or slip something out of another person's pocket.

**Stealth.** Make a Dexterity (Stealth) check when you attempt to conceal yourself from enemies, slink past guards, slip away without being noticed, or sneak up on someone without being seen or heard.

**Other Dexterity Checks.** The DM might call for a Dexterity check when you try to accomplish tasks like the following:

- Control a heavily laden cart on a steep descent
- Steer a chariot around a tight turn
- Pick a lock
- Disable a trap
- Securely tie up a prisoner
- Wriggle free of bonds
- Play a stringed instrument
- Craft a small or detailed object

### Attack Rolls and Damage

You add your Dexterity modifier to your attack roll and your damage roll when attacking with a ranged weapon, such as a sling or a longbow. You can also add your Dexterity modifier to your attack roll and your damage roll when attacking with a melee weapon that has the finesse property, such as a dagger or a rapier.

### Armor Class

Depending on the armor you wear, you might add some or all of your Dexterity modifier to your Armor Class, as described in chapter 5.

### Initiative

At the beginning of every combat, you roll initiative by making a Dexterity check. Initiative determines the order of creatures' turns in combat, as described in chapter 9.

### Hiding

The DM decides when circumstances are appropriate for hiding. When you try to hide, make a Dexterity (Stealth) check. Until you are discovered or you stop hiding, that check's total is contested by the Wisdom (Perception) check of any creature that actively searches for signs of your presence.

You can't hide from a creature that can see you clearly, and you give away your position if you make noise, such as shouting a warning or knocking over a vase. An invisible creature can't be seen, so it can always try to hide. Signs of its passage might still be noticed, however, and it still has to stay quiet.

In combat, most creatures stay alert for signs of danger all around, so if you come out of hiding and approach a creature, it usually sees you. However, under certain circumstances, the Dungeon Master might allow you to stay hidden as you approach a creature that is distracted, allowing you to gain advantage on an attack before you are seen.

**Passive Perception.** When you hide, there's a chance someone will notice you even if they aren't searching. To determine whether such a creature notices you, the DM compares your Dexterity (Stealth) check with that creature's passive Wisdom (Perception) score, which equals 10 + the creature's Wisdom modifier, as well as any other bonuses or penalties. If the creature has advantage, add 5. For disadvantage, subtract 5.

For example, if a 1st-level character (with a proficiency bonus of +2) has a Wisdom of 15 (a +2 modifier) and proficiency in Perception, he or she has a passive Wisdom (Perception) of 14.

**What Can You See?** One of the main factors in determining whether you can find a hidden creature or object is how well you can see in an area, which might be lightly or heavily obscured, as explained in chapter 8.

### Finding a Hidden Object

When your character searches for a hidden object such as a secret door or a trap, the DM typically asks you to make a Wisdom (Perception) check. Such a check can be used to find hidden details or other information and clues that you might otherwise overlook.

In most cases, you need to describe where you are looking in order for the DM to determine your chance of success. For example, a key is hidden beneath a set of folded clothes in the top drawer of a bureau. If you tell the DM that you pace around the room, looking at the walls and furniture for clues, you have no chance of finding the key, regardless of your Wisdom (Perception) check result. You would have to specify that you were opening the drawers or searching the bureau in order to have any chance of success.


## Condensed Version

**Dexterity**: Measures agility, reflexes, and balance. One of the most versatile ability scores.

### Acrobatics (Skill)

**Dexterity (Acrobatics)** — staying on your feet and performing stunts:
- Running across ice, balancing on a tightrope, staying upright on a ship
- Acrobatic stunts: dives, rolls, somersaults, flips

### Sleight of Hand (Skill)

**Dexterity (Sleight of Hand)** — legerdemain and manual trickery:
- Planting something on someone or concealing an object on your person
- Pickpocketing a coin purse or slipping something from a pocket

### Stealth (Skill)

**Dexterity (Stealth)** — concealment and sneaking:
- Hiding from enemies, slinking past guards
- Slipping away unnoticed, sneaking up on someone

### Other Dexterity Checks (No Skill)

- Control a cart on a steep descent
- Steer a chariot around a tight turn
- Pick a lock or disable a trap
- Tie up a prisoner or wriggle free of bonds
- Play a stringed instrument
- Craft a small or detailed object

### Attack Rolls and Damage

- **Ranged weapons** (sling, longbow): Add Dex modifier to attack and damage
- **Finesse melee weapons** (dagger, rapier): Can use Dex instead of Str for attack and damage

### Armor Class

- Add some or all of Dex modifier to [[02-Armor-and-Shields|AC]] depending on armor type:
  - **No armor**: 10 + Dex modifier
  - **Light armor**: Add full Dex modifier
  - **Medium armor**: Add Dex modifier (max +2)
  - **Heavy armor**: No Dex modifier added

### Initiative

- **Initiative = Dexterity check** (d20 + Dex modifier)
- Determines turn order at the start of combat (see Chapter 9)

> [!info] Rule Reminder
> Dexterity affects AC, initiative, ranged attacks, finesse weapons, and Stealth. Hiding requires a Stealth check contested by passive Perception — creatures don't need to actively search to notice you.

### Hiding Rules

**To Hide**: Make a Dexterity (Stealth) check
- **Contested by**: Wisdom (Perception) of searching creatures
- **Can't hide** from a creature that can see you clearly
- **Noise breaks hiding** (shouting, knocking things over)
- **[[Invisible|Invisible]] creatures** can always attempt to hide (but must stay quiet)

**In Combat**:
- Coming out of hiding usually means you're seen
- DM may allow staying hidden vs. distracted creatures → **advantage on attack**

**Passive Perception** (for creatures not actively searching):
- Your Stealth check vs. creature's passive Perception (10 + Wis mod + bonuses)
- Advantage on Perception: +5 to passive score
- Disadvantage on Perception: −5 to passive score

### Finding a Hidden Object (Sidebar)

- Use **Wisdom (Perception)** to search for secret doors, traps, hidden clues
- **You must describe where you're looking** — vague searching won't find specific items
- Example: A key hidden in a bureau drawer won't be found by "looking around the room" — you must say you're searching the bureau

**Key Concept**: Dexterity affects ranged attacks, finesse weapons, AC, initiative, and stealth. It's central to both combat and exploration. Hiding uses Stealth (Dex) contested by Perception (Wis), and passive Perception catches hidden creatures even without active searching.
