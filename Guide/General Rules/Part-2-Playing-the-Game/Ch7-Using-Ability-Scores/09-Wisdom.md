# Wisdom

## Original Text

Wisdom reflects how attuned you are to the world around you and represents perceptiveness and intuition.

### Wisdom Checks

A Wisdom check might reflect an effort to read body language, understand someone's feelings, notice things about the environment, or care for an injured person. The Animal Handling, Insight, Medicine, Perception, and Survival skills reflect aptitude in certain kinds of Wisdom checks.

**Animal Handling.** When there is any question whether you can calm down a domesticated animal, keep a mount from getting spooked, or intuit an animal's intentions, the DM might call for a Wisdom (Animal Handling) check. You also make a Wisdom (Animal Handling) check to control your mount when you attempt a risky maneuver.

**Insight.** Your Wisdom (Insight) check decides whether you can determine the true intentions of a creature, such as when searching out a lie or predicting someone's next move. Doing so involves gleaning clues from body language, speech habits, and changes in mannerisms.

**Medicine.** A Wisdom (Medicine) check lets you try to stabilize a dying companion or diagnose an illness.

**Perception.** Your Wisdom (Perception) check lets you spot, hear, or otherwise detect the presence of something. It measures your general awareness of your surroundings and the keenness of your senses. For example, you might try to hear a conversation through a closed door, eavesdrop under an open window, or hear monsters moving stealthily in the forest. Or you might try to spot things that are obscured or easy to miss, whether they are orcs lying in ambush on a road, thugs hiding in the shadows of an alley, or candlelight under a closed secret door.

**Survival.** The DM might ask you to make a Wisdom (Survival) check to follow tracks, hunt wild game, guide your group through frozen wastelands, identify signs that owlbears live nearby, predict the weather, or avoid quicksand and other natural hazards.

**Other Wisdom Checks.** The DM might call for a Wisdom check when you try to accomplish tasks like the following:

- Get a gut feeling about what course of action to follow
- Discern whether a seemingly dead or living creature is undead

### Spellcasting Ability

Clerics, druids, and rangers use Wisdom as their spellcasting ability, which helps determine the saving throw DCs of spells they cast.


## Condensed Version

**Wisdom**: Reflects attunement to the world around you — perceptiveness and intuition.

### Animal Handling (Skill)

**Wisdom (Animal Handling)** — interacting with animals:
- Calm a domesticated animal or keep a mount from getting spooked
- Intuit an animal's intentions
- Control your mount during a risky maneuver

### Insight (Skill)

**Wisdom (Insight)** — reading people:
- Determine the true intentions of a creature
- Search out a lie or predict someone's next move
- Glean clues from body language, speech habits, and mannerisms

### Medicine (Skill)

**Wisdom (Medicine)** — medical knowledge:
- Stabilize a dying companion
- Diagnose an illness

### Perception (Skill)

**Wisdom (Perception)** — sensory awareness:
- Spot, hear, or detect the presence of something
- Hear a conversation through a door or eavesdrop under a window
- Spot orcs in ambush, thugs in shadows, or candlelight under a secret door
- Measures general awareness and keenness of senses

> **Perception vs. Investigation**: Perception is about *noticing* with your senses. Investigation is about *deducing* from clues you've already found.

> [!tip] DM Tip
> Perception is the most commonly rolled check in the game. Use **passive Perception** (10 + modifiers) for hidden creatures and traps so players don't know a check is happening. Only call for active Perception rolls when a player specifically says they're searching.

### Survival (Skill)

**Wisdom (Survival)** — wilderness expertise:
- Follow tracks or hunt wild game
- Guide a group through harsh terrain
- Identify signs of nearby creatures (e.g., owlbear territory)
- Predict the weather
- Avoid natural hazards (quicksand, sinkholes)

### Other Wisdom Checks (No Skill)

- Get a gut feeling about what course of action to follow
- Discern whether a creature is undead

### Spellcasting Ability

- **Clerics, druids, and rangers** use Wisdom as their spellcasting ability
- Determines spell save DC and spell attack modifier

**Key Concept**: Wisdom is the "awareness" ability — five skills covering animals (Animal Handling), reading people (Insight), healing (Medicine), sensory awareness (Perception), and wilderness navigation (Survival). Clerics, druids, and rangers use Wisdom for spellcasting. Perception is one of the most commonly rolled checks in the game.
