# Constitution

## Original Text

Constitution measures health, stamina, and vital force.

### Constitution Checks

Constitution checks are uncommon, and no skills apply to Constitution checks, because the endurance this ability represents is largely passive rather than involving a specific effort on the part of a character or monster. A Constitution check can model your attempt to push beyond normal limits, however.

The DM might call for a Constitution check when you try to accomplish tasks like the following:

- Hold your breath
- March or labor for hours without rest
- Go without sleep
- Survive without food or water
- Quaff an entire stein of ale in one go

### Hit Points

Your Constitution modifier contributes to your hit points. Typically, you add your Constitution modifier to each Hit Die you roll for your hit points.

If your Constitution modifier changes, your hit point maximum changes as well, as though you had the new modifier from 1st level. For example, if you raise your Constitution score when you reach 4th level and your Constitution modifier increases from +1 to +2, you adjust your hit point maximum as though the modifier had always been +2. So you add 3 hit points for your first three levels, and then roll your hit points for 4th level using your new modifier. Or if you're 7th level and some effect lowers your Constitution score so as to reduce your Constitution modifier by 1, your hit point maximum is reduced by 7.


## Condensed Version

**Constitution**: Measures health, stamina, and vital force. Unique among abilities — it has **no associated skills**.

### Constitution Checks

**Uncommon** — Constitution represents passive endurance, not active effort. No skills apply.

**Example situations**:
- Hold your breath
- March or labor for hours without rest
- Go without sleep
- Survive without food or water
- Quaff an entire stein of ale in one go

> [!info] Rule Reminder
> Constitution has **no skills**. When a character's Con modifier changes, their HP maximum adjusts retroactively for all levels — increase or decrease.

### Hit Points

- Add **Constitution modifier to each Hit Die** rolled for HP
- This applies at **every level**

**When Constitution Changes** (retroactive adjustment):
- HP maximum adjusts as if you always had the new modifier
- **Increase**: Gain extra HP for all previous levels
- **Decrease**: Lose HP for all previous levels

**Example — Constitution Increases**:
- 4th-level character, Con modifier goes from +1 to +2
- Gain +1 HP per previous level = +3 HP (for levels 1–3)
- Roll 4th-level HP with new +2 modifier

**Example — Constitution Decreases**:
- 7th-level character, Con modifier drops by 1
- Lose 7 HP (1 per level)

**Key Concept**: Constitution is the only ability with no skills. It primarily determines your [[07-Damage-and-Healing|hit points]] (modifier added per level) and governs endurance-based checks. Changes to Constitution retroactively adjust your HP maximum for all levels.
