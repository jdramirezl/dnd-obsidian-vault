# Grappling and Shoving

## Original Text

> **Contests in Combat**
>
> Battle often involves pitting your prowess against that of your foe. Such a challenge is represented by a contest. This section includes the most common contests that require an action in combat: grappling and shoving a creature. The DM can use these contests as models for improvising others.

### Grappling

When you want to grab a creature or wrestle with it, you can use the Attack action to make a special melee attack, a grapple. If you're able to make multiple attacks with the Attack action, this attack replaces one of them.

The target of your grapple must be no more than one size larger than you, and it must be within your reach. Using at least one free hand, you try to seize the target by making a grapple check, a Strength (Athletics) check contested by the target's Strength (Athletics) or Dexterity (Acrobatics) check (the target chooses the ability to use). You succeed automatically if the target is incapacitated. If you succeed, you subject the target to the grappled condition (see appendix A). The condition specifies the things that end it, and you can release the target whenever you like (no action required).

**Escaping a Grapple.** A grappled creature can use its action to escape. To do so, it must succeed on a Strength (Athletics) or Dexterity (Acrobatics) check contested by your Strength (Athletics) check.

**Moving a Grappled Creature.** When you move, you can drag or carry the grappled creature with you, but your speed is halved, unless the creature is two or more sizes smaller than you.

### Shoving a Creature

Using the Attack action, you can make a special melee attack to shove a creature, either to knock it prone or push it away from you. If you're able to make multiple attacks with the Attack action, this attack replaces one of them.

The target of your shove must be no more than one size larger than you, and it must be within your reach. You make a Strength (Athletics) check contested by the target's Strength (Athletics) or Dexterity (Acrobatics) check (the target chooses the ability to use). You succeed automatically if the target is incapacitated. If you succeed, you either knock the target prone or push it 5 feet away from you.


## Condensed Version

**Grappling and Shoving**: Special melee attacks that replace one attack within the Attack action. Both use contested ability checks.

> [!info] Rule Reminder
> Grapple/shove **replaces one attack**, not the whole Attack action. A fighter with Extra Attack can grapple + attack in the same turn. Target must be **no more than one size larger**. Your Athletics vs. their Athletics or Acrobatics (target's choice).

> **Contests in Combat**: Grappling and shoving are the most common combat contests. The DM can use them as models for improvising other physical contests.

### Grappling

**Requirements**:
- Use the Attack action (replaces one attack if you have multiple)
- Target must be **no more than one size larger** than you
- Target must be **within your reach**
- You need **at least one free hand**

**The Check**:
- **You roll**: Strength (Athletics)
- **Target rolls**: Strength (Athletics) or Dexterity (Acrobatics) — target's choice
- **Auto-success**: If target is [[Incapacitated|incapacitated]]

**On Success**:
- Target gains the **[[Grappled|grappled]]** condition (speed becomes 0)
- You can release the target at any time (no action required)

**Escaping a Grapple**:
- [[Grappled]] creature uses its **action**
- **Target rolls**: Strength (Athletics) or Dexterity (Acrobatics)
- **Grappler rolls**: Strength (Athletics)
- On success: Grapple ends

**Moving a Grappled Creature**:
- Drag or carry the creature with you when you move
- **Speed halved** while dragging
- **Normal speed** if creature is 2+ sizes smaller than you

### Shoving a Creature

**Requirements**:
- Use the Attack action (replaces one attack if you have multiple)
- Target must be **no more than one size larger** than you
- Target must be **within your reach**

**The Check**:
- **You roll**: Strength (Athletics)
- **Target rolls**: Strength (Athletics) or Dexterity (Acrobatics) — target's choice
- **Auto-success**: If target is incapacitated

**On Success** (choose one):
- **Knock [[Prone|prone]]** — target falls to the ground
- **Push 5 feet** — shove target away from you

### Quick Reference

| Feature | Grapple | Shove |
|---|---|---|
| Replaces | One attack | One attack |
| Your check | Str (Athletics) | Str (Athletics) |
| Target's check | Str (Athletics) or Dex (Acrobatics) | Str (Athletics) or Dex (Acrobatics) |
| Size limit | One size larger max | One size larger max |
| Requires | Free hand | — |
| Result | Grappled condition | Prone or pushed 5 ft. |

**Key Concept**: Grappling and shoving replace one attack within the Attack action, not the whole action. This means a fighter with Extra Attack can grapple and still attack (or grapple and shove) in the same turn. Both use Strength (Athletics) contested by the target's choice of Athletics or Acrobatics.
