# Cover

## Original Text

Walls, trees, creatures, and other obstacles can provide cover during combat, making a target more difficult to harm. A target can benefit from cover only when an attack or other effect originates on the opposite side of the cover.

There are three degrees of cover. If a target is behind multiple sources of cover, only the most protective degree of cover applies; the degrees aren't added together. For example, if a target is behind a creature that gives half cover and a tree trunk that gives three-quarters cover, the target has three-quarters cover.

A target with half cover has a +2 bonus to AC and Dexterity saving throws. A target has half cover if an obstacle blocks at least half of its body. The obstacle might be a low wall, a large piece of furniture, a narrow tree trunk, or a creature, whether that creature is an enemy or a friend.

A target with three-quarters cover has a +5 bonus to AC and Dexterity saving throws. A target has three-quarters cover if about three-quarters of it is covered by an obstacle. The obstacle might be a portcullis, an arrow slit, or a thick tree trunk.

A target with total cover can't be targeted directly by an attack or a spell, although some spells can reach such a target by including it in an area of effect. A target has total cover if it is completely concealed by an obstacle.


## Condensed Version

**Cover**: Obstacles between you and an attacker grant bonuses to [[02-Armor-and-Shields|AC]] and Dexterity [[11-Saving-Throws|saving throws]]. Cover only applies when the attack originates from the opposite side.

### Degrees of Cover

| Cover Type | AC Bonus | Dex Save Bonus | Effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Half Cover** | +2 | +2 | Obstacle blocks at least half the body |
| **Three-Quarters Cover** | +5 | +5 | Obstacle blocks about three-quarters |
| **Total Cover** | — | — | Can't be targeted directly |

> [!info] Rule Reminder
> **Half cover** = +2 AC and Dex saves. **Three-quarters cover** = +5 AC and Dex saves. **Total cover** = can't be targeted directly. Multiple sources don't stack — only the best applies. Other creatures (friend or foe) provide half cover.

### Half Cover (+2 AC, +2 Dex saves)

- Obstacle blocks **at least half** of the target
- Examples: Low wall, large furniture, narrow tree trunk, another creature (friend or foe)

### Three-Quarters Cover (+5 AC, +5 Dex saves)

- Obstacle blocks **about three-quarters** of the target
- Examples: Portcullis, arrow slit, thick tree trunk

### Total Cover (Untargetable)

- Target is **completely concealed** by an obstacle
- **Can't be targeted** directly by attacks or spells
- **Exception**: Area-of-effect spells can still reach the target

### Important Rules

- **Multiple sources**: Only the most protective cover applies (don't stack)
  - Example: Half cover (creature) + three-quarters cover (tree) = three-quarters cover
- **Direction matters**: Cover only works against effects from the opposite side
- **Creatures as cover**: Any creature (ally or enemy) between you and the attacker provides half cover

**Key Concept**: Cover provides +2 (half) or +5 (three-quarters) to AC and Dexterity saves, while total cover prevents direct targeting entirely. Multiple sources don't stack — only the best applies.
